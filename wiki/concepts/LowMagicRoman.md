## Overview

Low Magic Roman is the magical and tonal setting of the Spartacus Tiberius campaign—a D&D 5e adaptation of the Spartacus TV series (War of the Damned) that strips away high fantasy elements in favor of gritty historical realism. The world has magic, but it is rare, subtle, and peripheral to the human drama of politics, slavery, and warfare.

## Defining Characteristics

### No High-Fantasy Spellcasting
Standard D&D spellcasting is largely removed. Tiberius's modified Paladin (Legion's Vindicator) has no spell slots—only resource-based martial abilities reskinned to fit the Roman aesthetic. No Fireball, no Lightning Bolt, no Cure Wounds—legion field medicine instead.

### Human-Scale Drama
The conflicts are political and military, not world-threatening. The stakes are personal: Tiberius's ambition, the fate of the slave rebellion, the political machinations of Roman nobles.

### Roman Authenticity
Everything feels authentic to the TV show:
- Blood-soaked gladiatorial arenas
- Decadent Roman villas as political battlegrounds
- Marching legions with realistic supply and command issues
- Rebel camps with internal tensions

### D&D Mechanics Survive
Combat remains tactical (d20 rolls, AC, HP, conditions), but magic items and spellcasting are minimized. Tiberius's Lay on Hands is field medicine. His Smite is a finisher technique.

## Campaign Fit

The Low Magic Roman setting allows players to engage with Roman history through the lens of D&D without the anachronism of fireballs and healing magic. It is historical fantasy—the aesthetics and social dynamics of Rome, with enough D&D mechanics to make combat engaging.
