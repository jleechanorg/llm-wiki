# Timeline Compression Discovery

## Definition

A thought exercise for identifying real bottlenecks in engineering workflows: take your project timeline, cut it in half, then work backwards to figure out what breaks. The resulting bottleneck list (build time, code review latency, iteration cycles, launch decisions) reveals exactly where AI tool investment will produce returns.

## Process

1. Make your normal project timeline (already too short, as usual)
2. Cut it in half
3. Work backwards: what would have to change to hit the compressed deadline?
4. The resulting list IS your AI investment priority queue

## Why It Works

Most teams adopt AI tools haphazardly ("let's pause all work and experiment"). Timeline compression forces structured discovery: you don't adopt tools because they're new, you adopt tools because they unblock your actual bottleneck.

## Common Bottlenecks Exposed

- Build time (30-60 min for large projects at Snap)
- Code review latency (2-3 days when reviewers have different incentives)
- Iteration/dogfooding cycles
- Launch decision coordination across 5+ teams
- Design doc consensus (especially at large companies where stakeholders can block progress)
