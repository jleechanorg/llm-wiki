## Overview

The Dragon Scholar is a custom D&D 5e class created for the Visenya V1 campaign. It is an INT/WIS-based full spellcaster with a Wizard subclass, designed to be a top-tier meta class with scholarly dragon lore as its thematic core.

## Class Design

### Primary Attributes
- **INT 16:** Primary spellcasting stat, class feature dependency
- **WIS 16:** Secondary stat, also feeds class features
- **DEX 14:** Dexterity for armor class and initiative
- **CHA:** Variable (remaining points)
- **CON 8:** Lowest priority (squires are not warriors)

### CHA Substitution Rule
One of Visenya's key requests: Charisma-based checks use the INT or WIS modifier instead of CHA. This reflects her scholarly nature—she persuades through knowledge and wisdom, not charm.

## Key Features

### Full Spellcaster Progression
Unlike half-casters or partial casters, the Dragon Scholar has full spell slot progression—equivalent to Wizard or Sorcerer in spell quantity.

### Subtle Spell
Enabled as a core class feature, allowing spells to be cast without verbal or somatic components. This reflects the scholarly, precise nature of dragon magic.

### Dragon Companion (Level 6)
At Level 6, Visenya will receive a dragon companion through a narrative quest. The companion grows and levels with Visenya, serving as a mount, ally, and manifestation of her Targaryen heritage.

### Wizard Subclass
The class uses a Wizard subclass framework (Order of Scribes or similar) with INT-based casting and spellbook mechanics, flavored as dragon scholarship rather than arcane academia.

## Balance Considerations

The class is designed to be "top meta" level—a powerful, high-utility spellcaster with excellent attribute spread and a companion mechanic that scales into late game.
