## Overview

The Mercy Path is one of the two fundamental approaches to the Undertale campaign (alongside the Kill Path). It represents choosing mercy in encounters with monsters—resolving conflict through dialogue, empathy, and understanding rather than violence. The Determination Adept class is specifically optimized for the Mercy Path through its high Charisma and Wisdom attributes.

## Gameplay Implications

### Dialogue-Based Resolution
Rather than rolling for attack, a Mercy Path player uses Persuasion (+5), Insight (+4), and Perception (+4) to navigate encounters. Every monster encounter has a non-combat resolution.

### No XP from Killing
The Mercy Path avoids gaining Experience Points through combat kills. Progression comes from exploration, story, and social encounters.

### Emotional Core
The Mercy Path is the thematic heart of Undertale—exploring whether violence is necessary, whether enemies can become friends, and whether empathy can triumph over fear.

## The Mercy/Kill Distinction

The Undertale campaign fundamentally distinguishes between:
- **Mercy:** Spare the monster, find another way
- **Kill:** Fight and defeat the monster, gain XP

A third option is the "Grind"—pure combat encounters without narrative weight.

The Determination Adept's attribute spread (high CHA/WIS, lower combat stats) is designed to make the Mercy Path the mechanically optimal choice, rewarding the player's narrative investment.
