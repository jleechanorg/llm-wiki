## Overview
The Fatebinder is the player archetype in the Tyranny campaign. Nocturne presents as a 16-year-old Fatebinder but carries an ancient intellect piloting the body. They are a Bard (College of Swords) flavored as a Wizard of the Order of Scribes — reciting mathematical proofs that force reality to correct itself, with their blade serving as a conductor for magic.

## Key Details
- **Category:** GameMechanic / Player Archetype
- **Campaign(s):** [[tyranny-campaign]]
- **Character:** Nocturne (Fatebinder / Ascendant Architect / Ghost in the Machine)
- **Level:** 16+ (initially requested Level 1, then set to Level 6+)
- **System:** D&D 5e

## The Two Masks
1. **The Courtly Shadow (Social Persona):** Soft-spoken, hyper-competent, unassuming. Uses Johan Liebert-style mirroring to create dependency loops.
2. **The Dispassionate Strategist (Crisis Persona):** Cold, administrative evil. Tywin Lannister + Aizen Sosuke hybrid. Intellectualizes fear into systems and probability calculations.

## Core Drives
- **The "Hogyoku" Complex:** Must be useful/essential. Weaponized belief that others who offer friendship without utility are parasites.
- **The "Itachi" Experiment:** Clinical urge to stress-test raw talents like Verse.
- **The Refactoring of Reality:** Seeks forbidden knowledge to "patch" the universe — Terratus is "full of bad code."
