## Description

Post-Absolute Baldur's Gate refers to the setting shortly after the events of Baldur's Gate 3, where the city is in a state of "chaotic recovery and opportunity" following the defeat of the Absolute and the Netherbrain.

## In This Campaign

The campaign takes place in this unstable period, where:
- The city is recovering from the crisis caused by the Absolute's influence
- Refugees still occupy temporary lodging in places like The Elfsong Tavern
- Power vacuums exist as various factions reorganize
- The Council of Four is in reformation
- The Flaming Fist is restructuring

This setting provides the backdrop for Gladius's mercenary work — a city where old rules have broken down and new ones are being established through raw power and manipulation.

## Significance

The Post-Absolute setting creates:
- Opportunity for mercenary work (instability = jobs)
- Moral ambiguity (no clear authority to enforce rules)
- Political complexity (multiple factions competing for power)
