## Description

Masked Power (also called "Smurfing") is the most ubiquitous mechanical concept across the WorldArchitect campaign universe. It describes a character who deliberately hides their true power level, maintaining a "masked level" that is significantly lower than their actual capabilities. This creates a dual-state tracking system where the player manages both real and apparent power.

## Core Mechanic

- **True Level ≠ Masked Level:** The game state tracks both values
- **The Reveal Tradeoff:** Using power above the masked level exposes the character's true nature
- **Consequences of Reveal:** Depends on campaign — demonic hunters, political paranoia, cosmic entities
- **Advantage on Deception:** Characters who mask power always gain advantage on checks to maintain the disguise

## Examples Across Campaigns

- [[Nocticula]] — Mana Veil: at level N, has spell slots as level N+2; can mask as any level ≤ current; using masked slots reveals true mana to detection
- [[SarielCrossCampaign]] (Hunting Party) — True Level 9 / Masked Level 1; narrative treats her as a prodigy novice
- [[Alexiel]] — Publicly a "Null" (no magic); secretly a magical prodigy with a Nullification Field
- [[AizenSosuke]] — The Polite Prodigy (public) vs. the Absolute Sovereign (true); maintains the "harmless student" facade
- [[VisenyaBelaerys]] — The Silver Persona; appears as a "lucky peasant" while being an Apex Monarch

## Why It Recurs

Masked Power solves a fundamental design problem: the player wants to feel overpowered (fun) while the narrative needs tension (drama). By forcing consequences for showing power, it creates strategic depth — the player must choose when to reveal, and each reveal is a narrative event.
