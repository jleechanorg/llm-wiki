# AI Adoption Spectrum

## Definition

The wide variation in AI tool proficiency and adoption willingness among engineers and engineering managers. Jeff Lee-Chan ([[JeffreyChan]]) describes this as a spectrum: some managers actively experiment with AI tools (spending 20-30 extra hours/week), while others haven't tried them at all and ask "is it really that good?"

## Key Insight

Those who haven't adopted AI tools will "fall further behind" as time goes on — the gap is not static but compounding. Managers who stay current can help their engineers; those who don't cannot effectively optimize AI-augmented workflows.

## Practical Implications

- **Managers need top-25% proficiency**: Not expert-level, but enough to debug, point engineers to tools, and evaluate tool claims
- **Double-time investment**: Engineering managers must maintain both management skills and IC-level AI tool knowledge
- **Leading by example > mandating**: Rules like "everyone must use 50M tokens/day" miss the root cause; excitement and demonstration work better
- **Critical mass of early adopters**: Teams need enough AI-curious members to create momentum before spreading practices across the org
