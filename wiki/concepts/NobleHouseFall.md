## Definition
The Noble House Fall theme explores the downfall of aristocratic families in the aftermath of major conflicts. In Baldur's Gate Post-Absolute, many noble houses whose investments were tied to ruined trade routes and devastated estates were erased by rivals who struck while the city was distracted by survival. The theme examines how economic devastation, political maneuvering, and personal betrayal combine to destroy established families.

## How It Appears in the Campaigns
- [[nocturne-bg3-after-campaign]]: House Nocturne was destroyed during the Absolute crisis. The bankruptcy stripped Nocturne and her sisters of their title and agency, leaving them to be sold to The Bloom & Shadow to settle debts

## Key Elements
- **Economic Devastation:** Investments in ruined trade routes lead to bankruptcy
- **Political Opportunism:** Rivals strike while the city is distracted
- **Loss of Agency:** Family members become currency to settle debts
- **Redemption Arc Potential:** Fallen nobles may seek vengeance or restoration
