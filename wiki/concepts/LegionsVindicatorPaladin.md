## Overview

The Legion's Vindicator is the modified Paladin class for the Spartacus Tiberius campaign—a reskinned Paladin adapted for a low-magic historical Roman setting. Rather than drawing power from divine sources, the Legion's Vindicator channels martial prowess through ancestral oaths and legion discipline.

## Key Modifications from Standard Paladin

### No Divine Spell Slots
Traditional Paladin spellcasting is removed. No Fireball, Cure Wounds, or divine magic—in this gritty Roman world, there is only grit and steel.

### Smite = Arena Finishers
Divine Smite is reskinned as "ancestral fury"—a resource-based burst (3 uses per short rest) that adds 2d8 radiant damage to melee hits. It is a finisher, not a spell.

### Lay on Hands = Field Medicine
Rather than divine healing, Lay on Hands represents legion training in battlefield medicine. A 15-point HP pool can heal or cure disease.

### Auras = Commanding Presence
Auras are reskinned as inspiring presence and terror tactics—the ability to project authority and fear through sheer martial bearing.

### Channel Divinity
- **Conquering Presence:** Frighten enemies in range
- **Guided Strike:** Gain +10 to an attack roll

## Campaign Fit

This class fits the Spartacus TV series aesthetic: blood-soaked arenas, marching legions, political intrigue—without high fantasy magic. Tiberius is a warrior-official sworn to Rome's glory, and the Legion's Vindicator captures that martial-religious spirit without actual deities.
