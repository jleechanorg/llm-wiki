## Overview
The Void Experiment is the narrative framework of Itachi Evil Campaign 2, where Itachi Uchiha performs a "grand experiment" on himself — forging himself into a being of pure, unfeeling resolve by systematically eliminating his emotional attachments (the Uchiha clan). The campaign uses a custom Ninja class built on the Destiny ruleset.

## Key Details
- **Category:** Campaign Framework / Character Arc
- **Campaign(s):** [[itachi-evil-campaign-2]]
- **Character:** Itachi Uchiha (Age 13, Evil alignment, not secretly good)
- **Ruleset:** Destiny (custom D&D adaptation for Naruto)

## Custom Ninja Class
- **Base:** Assassin Rogue + Extra Attack at L5 + all Illusion spells
- **Key Ability:** Sharingan active from Level 1
- **Mangekyo Sharingan:** Acquired — Tsukuyomi chosen as primary ability
- **Future Abilities:** Amaterasu and Susano'o unlocked through emotional catalysts or ritual

## The Three-Tier Experiment
1. **Uchiha Excision (Immediate):** The massacre itself — necessary sacrifice for power acquisition
2. **Konoha Hidden Hand (Mid-term):** Establishing Itachi as the villain who "saved" Konoha
3. **Great Unifying Order (Long-term):** Global order through the elimination of chaotic emotion

## Itachi's Philosophy
> "The Uchiha clan, in Itachi's cold, calculating view, is a cancer upon the Shinobi world... He sees himself as a surgeon, performing a necessary, brutal excision for the 'greater good' — his own meticulously planned, absolute, and unyielding order."
