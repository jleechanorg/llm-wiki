## Overview

Role-playing frameworks in multi-agent systems assign specific roles to agents that guide their behavior and interactions. CAMEL's role-playing approach is the canonical example — agents adopt roles and use those roles to guide their contributions.

## Key Properties

- **Role assignment**: Agents receive specific roles (e.g., "coder", "reviewer")
- **Role-guided behavior**: Role constrains what the agent says and does
- **Emergent cooperation**: Complex tasks emerge from simple role assignments
- **Contrast to fixed-prompt agents**: Role-playing agents adapt dynamically

## Connection to Governance

Role-playing frameworks provide implicit governance — each role has expectations and constraints. MetaGPT's SOP encoding is a more structured form of role-playing framework.
