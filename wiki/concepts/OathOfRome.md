## Overview

The Oath of Rome is the Paladin oath subclass for Tiberius in the Spartacus Tiberius campaign—a reskin of the Oath of Conquest adapted for the Roman historical setting. Where the standard Oath of Conquest centers on conquering enemies through strength and fear, the Oath of Rome channels that into patriotic devotion to the Roman Republic/Empire and its legions.

## Channel Divinity Abilities

### Conquering Presence
Tiberius can channel his presence as a weapon of terror. As an action, he can force each creature of his choice within 30 feet to make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failed save, a creature is frightened for 1 minute. This reflects the psychological warfare that Roman legions practiced—using discipline and reputation to break enemies before combat even began.

### Guided Strike
As a bonus action, Tiberius can gain advantage on one weapon attack roll. If that attack hits, he adds a +10 bonus to the damage roll. This represents the precision of a veteran Roman officer whose decades of training make every strike count.

## Role in the Campaign

The Oath of Rome fits Tiberius's character as the ruthless, ambitious son of Marcus Crassus. His oath is not to abstract justice or divine mandate—it is to Rome itself, to its legions, and to the glory of the Crassus family name. This makes him both a more relatable Roman noble and a more dangerous one: his conviction is grounded in nationalism rather than theology.
