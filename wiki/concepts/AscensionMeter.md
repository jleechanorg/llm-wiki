## Description

The Ascension Meter (also called Threat Gauge, Mana Reveal, Favor Tracker) is a recurring mechanical concept that quantifies the tension between power expression and consequence. It tracks how much of the PC's true power has been revealed, and triggers escalating negative consequences when the meter rises too high.

## Core Design Pattern

1. **Starting Value:** 0/100 (or equivalent scale)
2. **Trigger:** Using true/masked power, killing high-profile targets, or drawing attention
3. **Consequence at High Values:** Hunters dispatched, political paranoia, cosmic entities notice
4. **Consequence at Low Values:** Limited influence, must play weak, miss opportunities
5. **Strategic Tradeoff:** Player must choose when to "spend" meter on big plays vs. staying hidden

## Examples Across Campaigns

- [[VisenyaBelaerys]] — **Ascension Meter (0/100):** Using Apex Aura raises the meter; high meter attracts Architects of Silence and triggers Rhaenyra's paranoia. Each major power use adds +2 to +15.
- [[Nocticula]] — **Mana Reveal:** Binary — using masked spell slots shatters Mana Veil temporarily, alerting all magical detection in the region to her true power level.
- [[AizenSosuke]] (Thay) — **Obsidian Mirror Favor:** Performing well = Court rewards (boons, buffs); performing poorly = Court mocks, psychic backlash, Exhaustion.
- [[Alexiel]] — **Nullification Field Use:** Activating the Field is a dramatic reveal that permanently changes how allies and enemies perceive her.

## Why It Recurs

The Ascension Meter solves the "overpowered PC" problem not by nerfing the player, but by making power expression a **narrative choice with consequences**. It transforms the question from "can I do this?" to "should I do this here?" — creating genuine strategic depth.
