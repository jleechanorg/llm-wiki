## Description

The Singularity is a recurring narrative concept in the WorldArchitect campaign universe describing a character whose power is so extreme and self-accelerating that they threaten to "delete the board" rather than play the game. Singularities are not merely powerful; they are entities whose existence destabilizes the cosmic order, forcing intervention by higher powers (Ao, the Overgod, the Creator).

## Definition

A Singularity is defined by:
1. **Self-accelerating power:** Growth rate exceeds the system's ability to contain them
2. **Cosmic disruption:** Their mere existence threatens the balance of planes/realms
3. **Forced containment:** Higher powers must intervene (Geometric Firewall, exile, sealing)
4. **Incomprehensible to peers:** Even allies cannot fully grasp their trajectory

## Examples Across Campaigns

- [[NocturneSosuke]] — The original Singularity; conquered Sword Coast + Hells + Abyss in one year at age 16; Ao sealed her because she was "deleting the board"
- [[GaiaJuliaCaesar]] — Described explicitly as a "Singularity" in her campaign bible; the daughter whose power dwarfs her siblings'
- [[AizenSosuke]] — "A force that would either ascend to godhood or drag the entire House into the Abyss"
- [[VisenyaBelaerys]] — The Apex Bloodline; if she shines too bright, the Architects of Silence awaken
- [[AureliusJuliusCaesar]] — "The Devouring Maw; a singularity of Appetite"

## Narrative Function

The Singularity serves as the campaign's central escalation mechanic: the PC starts as a prodigy, the world reacts with increasing alarm, and the endgame is either apotheosis or containment. It creates a natural tension between expressing power (fun) and concealing it (survival).
