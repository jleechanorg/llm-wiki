## Description

The Uchiha Massacre is the canonical event in Naruto where Itachi Uchiha slaughtered his entire family — the Uchiha clan. In the original canon, this was done secretly to protect the village and spare his brother Sasuke. In this campaign, Itachi is evil, making the massacre a genuine act of genocide rather than a sacrifice.

## In This Campaign

The campaign begins at the Uchiha Compound during the night of the massacre. This sets the tone for the entire campaign — Itachi is not a tragic hero but a genuine villain who views the Uchiha as "a cancer upon the Shinobi world" requiring excision.

His justification is coldly logical: The Uchiha's obsession with tradition, their volatile emotions tied to their Sharingan evolution, and their clan-centric vision are fundamental flaws. Their elimination is necessary for his vision of a unified world order.

## Significance

The Uchiha Massacre serves as:
- The catalyst for Itachi's power (Mangekyō activation)
- The proof of his evil nature
- The starting point for his manipulation of Konoha
- The foundation for his ultimate goal of world domination
