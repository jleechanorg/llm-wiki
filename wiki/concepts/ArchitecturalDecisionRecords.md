## Definition
Architectural Decision Records (ADR) are documents that capture important architectural decisions made in a project, including the context, decision, and consequences.

## Purpose
- Document why specific technical choices were made
- Provide historical context for future developers
- Enable traceability of design decisions
- Support onboarding and knowledge transfer


## Typical Structure
1. **Title**: Brief decision description
2. **Status**: Proposed, Accepted, Deprecated, Superseded
3. **Context**: The situation or problem being addressed
4. **Decision**: The chosen solution
5. **Consequences**: Outcomes, trade-offs, and side effects
