## Definition
ISO 8601 is an international standard for representing dates and times in a machine-readable format. The format usesYYYY-MM-DDTHH:MM:SS.ssssssZ for UTC timestamps or YYYY-MM-DDTHH:MM:SS+HH:MM for timezone-aware timestamps.

## Relevance
The world_time module uses ISO 8601 format for parsing timestamps from API responses and converting them to internal world_time representations.

## Key Characteristics
- **UTC marker**: "Z" suffix indicates UTC timezone
- **Timezone offset**: "+02:00" or "-05:00" indicates offset from UTC
- **Microsecond precision**: ".123456" captures microsecond-level detail
- **T separator**: "T" separates date and time components
