Proof Assistant is a generic term for formal verification tools like [[Lean]], [[Coq]], and Isabelle that enable interactive theorem proving. They allow users to state theorems and prove them mechanically.

## Key Properties
- **Interactive**: Human and AI collaborate to construct proofs
- **Machine-checked**: Every step of the proof is verified by the tool
- **Expressiveness**: Can represent complex mathematical structures and program properties
- ** [[Lean]] and [[Coq]] are the most widely used in AI research contexts

## Connections
- [[FormalVerification]] — proof assistants are the tools of formal verification
- [[Lean]] — Microsoft's proof assistant, particularly popular in AI math verification
- [[Coq]] — Used for verified compilers and formal mathematics
- [[Isabelle]] — Generic proof assistant used in academia
