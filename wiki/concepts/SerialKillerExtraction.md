## Description

"Serial Killer Extraction" is the core premise of the Hunting Party Sariel campaign — following a mass escape of the nation's most dangerous serial killers, the FBI is forced to recruit Sariel, the operative they previously disowned for being "too effective."

## In This Campaign

The campaign centers on Sariel's hunt for escaped serial killers. This premise creates a dark narrative where the boundary between hunter and hunted blurs. The FBI officially denies any involvement with Sariel, creating a legal gray zone where she operates with neither full authority nor official restriction.

## Significance

This premise establishes the moral and legal ambiguity that defines the campaign. The serial killers are "the nation's most dangerous" — implying they've committed multiple murders and are extremely capable. Hunting them requires someone with Sariel's particular skills and moral flexibility.
