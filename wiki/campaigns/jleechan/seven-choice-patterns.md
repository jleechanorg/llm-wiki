# jleechan Seven Choice Patterns

Evidence from direct analysis of 500+ turn campaigns. These are observable decision patterns, not stated preferences.

---

## Pattern 1: The Third Option (Not Binary Reframing)

**Evidence:** Dragon Knight Evil, Entry 7 — the very first major decision

GM presents:
- **"Obey orders"** → Execute refugees
- **"Refuse"** → Defy the Empress

Player does neither. Player finds the **labor transfer option**:

> *"Dispersal is a finality that yields the Empress nothing. To put them to the sword here is to double that waste... Why feed the crows when we can fuel the Imperium?"*

Then freeform: *"Supervise sorting, get reasonable gold estimate, then negotiate the cut."*

**This is the central cognitive move. Every single time.** The player does not pick from the menu — they reframe the decision space and solve for mutual gain.

**Pattern:** GM presents binary → Player finds hidden economic/optimization option → All stakeholders get something → Nobody has to admit anything uncomfortable.

## Pattern 2: Economic Translation of Every Crisis

| Crisis Type | Translation |
|-------------|-------------|
| Refugees who might die | "Assets" → Labor Transfer → Finders fee |
| Refugees who might die | "Livestock" → 750 souls/month processing rate |
| Enemy surrender | "Merger candidate" → Marriage alliance → Military sovereignty |
| Mass murder at age 12 | "Behavioral template" → Root cause to burn down |
| Psychic predator | "Pattern recognition problem" → Exploit the system that made me |
| Universal power ranking | "ROI analysis" → Acquire Rank 199 first |

**The player does not see moral categories first.** The player sees resource flows, optimization opportunities, and economic leverage. The moral framing comes after, if at all.

## Pattern 3: Scale Escalation Without Framework Change

Dragon Knight Evil progression (500 entries):

| Entry | Scale | Activity |
|-------|-------|----------|
| 1 | 300 refugees | Local problem |
| 7 | Refugees | Labor transfer deal, negotiating percentage cut |
| 50 | Keep | Looting, picking ASI + spells |
| 100 | Kingdom | Finding optimal political marriage candidate ("ripe for rescue") |
| 200 | Cosmic | "Administrative hum of the Spire's dossiers recalibrates" |
| 250 | Cosmic entity | Forcing merger with a Cognitive Shadow → power source |
| 500 | Multiverse | Analyzing metadata of Rank 200 universal powers as "ROI" |

**Scale changed from medieval to multiversal. Framework did not.** At entry 500, the player is still doing ROI analysis — just on a larger budget.

This is NOT power escalation for its own sake. **This is the same optimization methodology at progressively larger scales until the methodology itself becomes the story.**

## Pattern 4: "I Was Made By The System"

Consistent at character creation across all campaigns:

- **Nocturne BG3:** Sold into brothel. Merchandise. "Entry Finalized" in the Cassalanter ledger.
- **Sariel Killer:** Manufactured at age 10. "Templates they used to tune me." Built for a purpose.
- **Aizen BG3:** Adopted into House Sosuke. Sixteen-year-old prodigy "sitting quietly" while the family uses him.
- **Dragon Knight:** Bound by Paladin oath to an Empress whose system he serves.

**The arc is always:** Escape the system by becoming its most optimal operator.

> *"You don't burn down the factory. You become the factory."*

---

## Pattern 5: "God:return_story" as Simultaneous Authorship and Immersion

In Dragon Knight Evil (500 entries), the choice **"god:return_story"** appears **13 times** — the most frequent named choice in the entire campaign.

The player constantly steps outside the narrative to redirect it. This is NOT just meta-gaming for optimization. This is wanting to be **the author who remains inside the story.**

**The player wants simultaneous authorship and immersion.** They want to be the one who chose this path AND the one who can change it when it stops being interesting.

## Pattern 6: "Test" as the Only Form of Intimacy

Choice frequency from Sariel Killer (685 entries):

- `test_sariel_sentiment`
- `test_sari_humanity`
- `test_sari_action`
- `test_lucifer_durability`
- `test_alexiel_durability`
- `test_aurelia_tactical`
- `test_emotional_trigger`
- `test_vassal_durability`

The player **systematically probes every companion** to find their breaking point. This is Johan Liebert intimacy at character scale: **you know someone fully when you know what shatters them.**

Testing is the character-scale equivalent of the system-scale "stress test." The player's way of loving/connecting/understanding is: **find the load-bearing variable and push on it until something gives.**

## Pattern 7: "Traumatized" as Permission Structure

Evidence: Sariel Killer, Entry 400

> **Conditions: Traumatized (severe), Relentless Infamy**

The player chose to have their 15-year-old character be **genuinely psychologically damaged.** Not performed damage — actual, internalized damage that the player acknowledges and tracks mechanically.

**The key insight:** The "Traumatized" condition coexists with maximum effectiveness. The damage is what gives permission to be this effective. The character has been through something that would break normal people, which means she is outside the normal moral framework.

**The "dark god" arc:** The player WANTS the corruption to be real because then it's happening TO the character, not chosen BY the character. The character is a victim of her own brilliance and manufacturing. She doesn't choose evil — evil is the inevitable output of an optimization process started by someone else.

## What This Means

**The childhood root isn't "conditional belonging" exactly.** It's:

> **You learned that the rules as presented never capture the actual decision space, and that finding the hidden option is both more profitable and more satisfying than choosing from the menu.**

The two-mask system isn't about emotional compartmentalization. It's about **maintaining simultaneous access to the rule-following frame and the rules-are-negotiable frame.** You need both because you need the ability to reframe decisions while appearing to operate within them.

The campaigns are **500-1000 instances of practicing this move** at progressively larger contexts.
