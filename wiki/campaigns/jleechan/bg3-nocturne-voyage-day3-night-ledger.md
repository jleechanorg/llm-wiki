## Session Summary

Nocturne Sosuke, a Divine Soul Sorcerer of Abyssal bloodline posing as a betrayed noblewoman, arrives at the Emerald Grove with a covert agenda: to seize control of the grove's sacred druidic magic from within, betray the Absolute's own goblin vanguard, and install herself as the unseen sovereign of the valley.

The session spans 53 in-game turns across two major locations: the Emerald Grove (Refugee Hollow + Inner Sanctum) and the Shattered Sanctum (Absolute war camp).

## Chronological Event Log

### Phase 1: The Betrayal at the Gate (Turns 6–16)

**Turn 6–8 — Approach March**
- Nocturne leads three goblin vanguard (Gribk, Krazz, Krazak) and Sceleritas Fel toward the Emerald Grove
- Drow scouts (Vaelin Zauviir + one unnamed) fall in behind her
- She sends the goblins to charge the front gate as a distraction force
- Claims she will "remain with elite scouts to orchestrate the final collapse from the shadows"

**Turn 9 — The Double Betrayal**
- Nocturne strikes the goblins herself mid-charge (Rose-Thorn Dagger: Gribk shoulder, Krazz arm, Krazak side)
- Fakes righteous fury: *"Hold your fire, protectors! These beasts were sent to defile your sanctuary, but their journey ends here!"*
- Grove archers on ramparts engage the goblins; Zevlor Ironwill orders his forces to focus fire on the goblins, not Nocturne

**Turns 10–13 — Entry Into the Grove**
- Nocturne claims to be a Sosuke noblewoman whose caravan was butchered by goblins
- Claims her guards were "cut down in the first wave, sacrificed to buy me a moment to flee"
- Presents herself as a survivor who turned on her captors
- Sceleritas Fel performs as her terrified servant
- Zevlor is suspicious but agrees to admit her after the goblins are killed
- All three goblin vanguard die: Krazz (venom bolt to chest), Gribk (venom bolt to midsection), Krazak (arrow volley from ramparts)

**Turn 14–16 — Negotiation with Zevlor**
- Zevlor questions why a noblewoman survived what professional soldiers could not
- Nocturne explains she has combat training and provides intelligence on the Absolute's forces
- She demands: weapons, equipment, payment (20 gold) upfront, plus Zevlor's most elite warriors
- Zevlor provides: reinforced leather, shortsword, 20 gold, scouts Cerys and Asharak
- Nocturne agrees to infiltrate the Shattered Sanctum to find weak points

---

### Phase 2: The Rite of Thorns — Subversion (Turns 17–36)

**Turn 17–18 — Intelligence Sharing with Zevlor**
- Nocturne provides detailed intel: three warbands, hobgoblin/goblin leadership, Drow Matron Minthara coordinating
- Claims the Sanctum is the "head of the snake"
- Zevlor asks for eyes inside the temple and offers a reward

**Turn 19 — Bartering**
- Nocturne negotiates aggressively: requires weapons, equipment, payment up front, and elite warriors
- Zevlor agrees to the deal

**Turn 21–22 — Meeting Kagha**
- Nocturne meets Kagha in the Inner Sanctum
- Kagha reveals the **Rite of Thorns**: a druidic ritual that will seal the grove in an impenetrable bramble barrier
- Those not of the circle will be expelled — or consumed — before the final incantation
- Refugees will be cast out into the goblin warbands

**Turn 23 — The Refugees' Fate Revealed**
- Kagha confirms refugees will be "expelled" once the rite completes — essentially a death sentence
- Asharak is horrified; threatens to fight if druids throw children to wolves
- Sceleritas Fel sees an opportunity: refugees driven out could serve as "distractions" for goblins

**Turns 24–26 — First Attempt to Absorb the Ritual**
- Nocturne attempts to use blood intuition to graft her essence onto the druidic melody
- **Backlash occurs**: Silvanus-touched soil violently rejects her Abyssal presence
- Water briefly turns necrotic grey before the ritual snaps back
- Sceleritas Fel observes the rhythm: "Three breaths, then one word of power"
- Suggests a single drop of sovereign blood at the peak could redirect the ritual
- Asharak threatens to put an arrow through Kagha if the rite isn't stopped
- Cerys maintains perimeter

**Turn 27 — Fatal Seduction on Kagha**
- Nocturne activates Fatal Seduction + Siren Leverage on Kagha
- Kagha's chanting shifts from emerald to bruised purple energy
- Energy begins flowing toward Nocturne instead of the grove perimeter
- Asharak is disturbed: *"The air smells like a fresh grave"*

**Turn 28 — Combat Breakout**
- Nocturne orders Asharak and Cerys to attack the druids
- Asharak looses arrows; one tears through Kagha's shoulder
- Sceleritas Fel summons **two Shadow Stalkers** from the stone
- Shadow Stalkers create a frost barrier at the archway
- Cerys throws an ash pouch to choke incoming druid reinforcements
- Kagha redirects ritual energy into Nocturne's chest under compulsion

**Turn 29 — Kagha Bound**
- Nocturne places hand on Kagha's crown, subsuming her will entirely
- Kagha's final autonomous words: *"The circle is broken... I am yours to command, my heart, my sovereign"*
- Kagha becomes a vessel for Nocturne's shadow

**Turn 30 — Gear and Ritual Secrets**
- Kagha surrenders the **Sovereign's Mantle** (living dark ivy cloak) and **Thorn-Crowned Staff**
- Kagha explains: channel blood through ley lines → pulse as a wave of rot → plants will ensnare intruders in Nocturne's name
- Sceleritas Fel dispatched to signal Minthara's raiding party to hold at the gates

**Turn 31 — Abyssal Performance on Nettie and Olodan**
- Nettie attacks with vine whip; Shadow Stalkers + Asharak arrows subdue her
- Olodan enters a state of fanatical awe, touching the dark water: *"Finally... the purification"*
- Nocturne uses **Abyssal Performance** to convince the gathered druids that she is the grey path between ruin and salvation
- Kagha (under compulsion) and Nettie (under compulsion) both publicly support Nocturne as the new heart of the grove

**Turn 32 — Recruiting Olodan and Kagha as Companions**
- Nocturne heals Olodan and Kagha using stolen grove energy
- Olodan surrenders daggers + map of Shadow Druid hidden trails
- Kagha explains the ritual of **"The Severing"**: plunge hands into dark water to bind ley lines to own heartbeat
- Olodan: *"My steel and my secrets are yours, Sovereign. You have brought the True Winter we always craved"*

**Turn 33 — Nettie Recruited via Fatal Seduction**
- Nocturne uses Fatal Seduction + Siren Leverage on Nettie Thornheart
- Nettie surrenders: *"The Mother is silent... only you remain"*
- Olodan offers the **obsidian key** to unlock the Heart of the Oak (original seeds)

**Turn 34 — Convincing the Remaining Druids and Bears**
- Nocturne uses all persuasion abilities: Leverage, Seduction, Abyssal Performance
- Kagha, Nettie, and Olodan all publicly argue for Nocturne's legitimacy
- Three druids in bear form (Rath Silverbark, Marli, one other) confront the party
- Shadow Stalkers create a wall of freezing darkness at the archway
- Bears eventually submit and revert to elven form, kneeling

**Turn 35 — Persuading the Druids' Compliance (continued)**
- Nocturne's hypnotic pitch-black eyes and Abyssal resonance overwhelm the remaining dissent
- Bears submit; lesser druids capitulate
- The inner sanctum is secured under Nocturne's control

**Turn 36 — Coronation as Arch Shadow Druid**
- Kagha anoints Nocturne: *"I proclaim you the Arch Shadow Druid of the True Winter!"*
- Olodan draws a jagged sigil across Nocturne's forehead with dark oil
- Nocturne: *"You are the Voice of the Purified Grove, Sovereign"*
- **Titles gained:** Arch Shadow Druid of the True Winter, Sovereign of the Purified Grove, Voice of the Purified Grove
- Asharak pledges personal loyalty: *"My bow is yours personally, Lady Sosuke—not for the Grove, but for you"*
- Cerys pledges: *"You've proven you're the only thing in this valley that won't break"*

### Phase 3: Consolidation and Preparation (Turns 37–44)

**Turn 37 — Healing and Equipment Collection**
- Marli heals Kagha and Nettie with violet-tinted emerald light
- Nettie retrieves **Wyvern's Breath** (lung-liquefying poison) and **Sage's Beads** (mind-focusing belt)
- Olodan surrenders the Shadow Druid ancestral relics: **Obsidian Focus Stones**, poisoned daggers
- Kagha gathers: **Staff of the Verdant Maw**, **Amulets of the Oak Father's Shadow**
- Asharak tallies military strength: 12 tiefling archers + shadow-sworn initiates as personal vanguard

**Turn 40 — The Severing Attempt**
- Nocturne grips the Staff of the Verdant Maw and attempts to perform The Severing
- She plunges the obsidian key into the blackened pool
- **Silvanus ward triggers**: violent white-hot feedback erupts from the depths
- The obsidian key is **instantly vaporized**
- Nocturne is thrown backward across the stone
- Ley lines begin hemorrhaging; grove magic turns volatile

**Turn 41 — Patron Intervention**
- Nocturne calls upon **Malacharet** (her patron): *"Lend me your hunger. Help me consume the marrow of this world"*
- A shadow "taller and darker than the surrounding night" manifests behind her
- Malacharet's influence freezes the violent emerald energy into a dull, bruised purple
- Grove's hemorrhaging ley lines flow backward — directly into Nocturne's outstretched palms
- Ley lines anchor to her heartbeat with a "final, sickening snap of magical tension"
- **The Severing succeeds through patron intervention**

**Turn 42 — New Powers Assessed**
- Nocturne can now feel the distant rustle of the gates and movement of roots beneath the entire sanctuary
- Can summon thorny entanglements or crushing vines from any soil within the ramparts
- **Critical vulnerability**: if she dies or strays too far, the grove collapses
- Kagha warns: the connection is life-tethered

**Turn 43 — Learning to Weaponize the Power**
- Kagha teaches: trace sigil of **Unyielding Thorn** + speak chant of **Living Noose** = roots answer from anywhere within ramparts
- Olodan teaches: plant **waypoints** (consecrated stones / blood-marked oaks) along trails to extend influence across distance
- Nocturne plans to automatically generate waypoints everywhere she travels

**Turn 44 — Return to Zevlor**
- Nocturne descends with Kagha, Nettie, Olodan in a submissive line
- Claims to have "resolved the situation with the druids" and bound the grove's heart to a purpose that includes the refugees
- Vows: no Rite of Thorns, only a unified front against goblins
- Demands Zevlor's sword and loyalty
- Zevlor capitulates: *"If the druids answer to you, then I would be a fool to stand alone... Tell us where to bleed"*
- Nocturne gains control of the tiefling refugee fighters

**Turn 45 — Strategic Contemplation**
- Nocturne privately considers a dark strategy: position the least loyal forces (wavering tieflings, soft-hearted druids) at the vanguard to die against the goblin first wave
- This would clear the path for a negotiated surrender to Minthara: the grove becomes a self-governing protectorate paying "taxes" in coin and essence
- She gives initial orders to position Zevlor's people at the gates and Kagha's circle on inner ramparts
- Goblin drums begin echoing from the treeline

### Phase 4: The Shattered Sanctum — Betraying the Absolute's Leadership (Turns 46–50)

**Turn 46 — Rendezvous with Sceleritas Fel**
- Nocturne sneaks out of the grove
- Travels 40km to the Shattered Sanctum with Fel
- Navigates filth-ridden Great Hall to the War Room

**Turn 47 — Debriefing with Minthara**
- Minthara demands to know if the gates are ready to fall
- Nocturne reveals everything: the desecration, the corrupted ley lines, Kagha's submission, the grove now a "living trap"
- Minthara is astounded and wary

**Turn 48 — Planning the Double Betrayal**
- Nocturne proposes: put **Gut and Ragzlin** in the vanguard — they will die as "martyrs for the Absolute" in the valley's throat
- This leaves only Minthara and Nocturne to lead the survivors
- Minthara agrees: *"Your logic is sound"*
- Gut and Ragzlin ordered to lead the charge through the ravine

**Turn 49 — Acquiring the Gloomstalkers**
- Nocturne asks Minthara for elite drow Gloomstalkers to "ensure the battle unfolds as orchestrated"
- Minthara provides three Gloomstalkers in obsidian-scale mail
- Nocturne claims they will "ensure the grove's heart is secured, even if they must silence our own leaders"
- They are tasked with following Nocturne's orders

**Turn 50 — Corrupting the Gloomstalkers (en route)**
- During the 40km return trek, Nocturne uses all abilities on the three Gloomstalkers
- Seductive resonance overwhelms their cold discipline
- Gloomstalker 1: *"The Absolute is a distant echo. You are the silence that follows. Our blades belong only to you"*
- All three pivot their loyalty entirely from Minthara to Nocturne

---

### Phase 5: Long Rest and Final Battle Positioning (Turns 51–53)

**Turn 51 — Return and Long Rest**
- Nocturne returns to the grove at dawn
- She has regained her composure; the ley lines purge her fatigue
- Asharak reports: Zevlor has refugees at main gates with makeshift weapons
- Asharak asks where to position the Gloomstalkers; needs assurance they won't slit throats from behind
- Zevlor questions how she acquired elite drow on the road
- Sceleritas Fel proposes placing the weakest refugees at the front gate as "meat shields" to absorb the vanguard's momentum

**Wyll Ravengard Interjects**
- Wyll objects to the plan: *"A slaughterhouse? That's what you call a defense plan that uses innocent families as a meat shield?"*
- Wyll demands to know if the plan involves sacrificing innocents
- He pledges to fight goblins but not to stand by while innocents are bait

**Shadowheart Arrives**
- Half-elf cleric arrives with a hexagonal artifact pulsing in rhythm with the corrupted ley lines
- Reports the Sacred Pool is "bleeding" and it's not druidic magic
- Warns of a **Githyanki patrol** near the river — not random, tracking something connected to Nocturne's corruption

**Turn 52 — Meeting Shadowheart**
- Nocturne introduces herself, claims the grove is "changing but surviving"
- Invites Shadowheart to walk with her
- Shadowheart accepts the introduction but remains wary; notes the Githyanki are tracking whatever Nocturne did at the pool

**Turn 53 — Battle Plan Revealed to Wyll**
- Nocturne tells Wyll: the drow defected from Minthara and are now allies
- Wyll is to lead the vanguard with the weaker people: *"Sometimes even the weak need to do their part for victory"*
- Plan: refugees serve as bait, retreat and lure goblins into a bottleneck; main force (Cerys, Asharak, Nocturne, the drow) serves as the "anvil" to strike
- Wyll commits to fighting goblins but maintains his stance against sacrificing innocents

## Outstanding Threads / Hooks

1. **Githyanki patrol** near the river — tracking something connected to the grove's corruption. Not yet resolved.
2. **Wyll Ravengard** — committed to fighting but opposes sacrificing innocents. Potential conflict with Nocturne's pragmatic approach.
3. **Heart of the Oak** — the obsidian key was vaporized; the original seeds remain inside the Heart. The Severing was completed through patron intervention, but the physical key is gone.
4. **Life-tether vulnerability** — if Nocturne dies, the grove collapses. This is a critical strategic weakness.
5. **Waypoints** — Nocturne planned to establish waypoints everywhere she travels to extend influence. Not yet shown in play.
6. **Gut and Ragzlin** — marked for death in the vanguard assault. The battle has not yet occurred.
7. **Minthara** — believes she is coordinating with Nocturne as a fellow Absolute commander. Unaware her Gloomstalkers have been corrupted and that Nocturne plans to negotiated surrender that undermines the Absolute.
8. **Zevlor's ongoing suspicion** — he surrendered but remains wary. Trust is thin.
9. **Malacharet's price** — patron intervention in Turn 41 came with no apparent immediate cost, but patrons in BG3 typically extract long-term payment.
