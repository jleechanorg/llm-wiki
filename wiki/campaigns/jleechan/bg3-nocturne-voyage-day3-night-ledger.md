## Phase Breakdown

### Phase 1 — The Betrayal at the Gate
**Turns: 0–16** | **Location: Shattered Sanctum → Moraven Basin → Emerald Grove**

Nocturne arrives at the Absolute's Shattered Sanctum War Room as an unproven stranger. Minthara interrogates her with hostility. Nocturne demands elite troops for an infiltration mission. Minthara grants three Gloomstalkers, a bugbear detachment, and Sceleritas Fel as summoner's servant.

Nocturne leads goblin brawlers (Gribk + 2) out of the Sanctum and directs them to march straight at the Emerald Grove — intending to betray them mid-battle. The goblins are drunk and sloppy. She picks up a discarded traveler's pack (dried rations + bitter-herb vial).

At the Grove's outer perimeter, Nocturne uses Fatal Seduction to charm Zevlor Ironwill at the gate. She convinces the tieflings to open the gates and position the refugees as a vanguard/sacrifice — promising to protect them. Zevlor, desperate, agrees.

Wyll Ravengard (Blade of Frontiers) arrives with a party of druids including Kagha, Nettie, and Olodan. He recognizes the goblin warband approaching and prepares to fight. Nocturne uses Abyssal Performance + Fatal Seduction to charm Zevlor again and redirect Wyll's forces. She tells Wyll to hold the archers and let the goblins hit the refugees first.

**Key decisions:**
- Used refugees as literal expendable vanguard (not just "distraction")
- Redirected Wyll's archers away from goblin chokepoints
- Separated Kagha, Nettie, Olodan from the druid circle via seduction/leverage
- Zevlor ordered Asharak's archers to eastern ridge; refugees used as front line

---

### Phase 2 — The Battle of the Emerald Grove
**Turns: 17–44** | **Location: Emerald Grove — Inner Sanctum / Ridge / Ravine**

Goblins (Gut + Ragzlin leading) breach the Grove's outer defenses. Refugees absorb the initial goblin charge at extreme cost — many die as living shields/vanguards. Nocturne positions Gloomstalkers in the stone archway above the inner sanctum approach.

**Turn 21:** Nocturne plants corruption into the Grove's ley-lines using The Severing + Thorn-Crowned Staff. Kagha, Nettie, and Olodan perform a ritual at the sacred pool, allowing Nocturne to channel ley-line energy through their shadow druids. Shadowheart masks the corruption with the Githyanki artifact. The corruption weakens the Absolute's goblin forces.

**Turn 25:** Nocturne uses Fatal Seduction on Dror Ragzlin mid-combat — he becomes paralyzed with terror, allowing Gloomstalkers to attack him. Nocturne harvests Ragzlin's martial essence (soul partially consumed). Ragzlin survives but is severely weakened.

**Turn 26–27:** Nocturne's Fatal Seduction fails against Priestess Gut (she breaks free). Kagha is bitten by Nettie's snake (wound). Nocturne uses a poisonous potion on Ragzlin.

**Turn 28–29:** Nocturne attempts to kill Zevlor with a backstab (Rite of Thorns Subversion + Fatal Seduction combo) — **fails due to Kagha's intervention** (Kagha takes the hit meant for Zevlor). Kagha is severely wounded. Nocturne's reputation with Zevlor is damaged.

**Turn 31:** Nocturne uses Abyssal Performance to convince Minthara that the Grove's defense is failing. Minthara orders the goblins to charge the refugees directly. Nocturne ensures maximum goblin casualties in the ensuing bloodbath.

**Turn 32–36:** Nocturne attempts to soul-harvest Minthara (soul-seduction). Minthara's Absolute parasite resists, causing psychic feedback. Nocturne is forced to abort — the parasite is too deeply embedded. She pivots to destroying the Absolute's leadership from within.

**Turn 37–40:** Nocturne uses The Severing to cut Minthara's connection to the Absolute. She convinces the three Gloomstalkers to defect to her side (they kneel to her at Turn 40).

**Turn 41–44:** Nocturne orders the goblins to execute refugees. Kagha, Nettie, and Olodan (now fully shadow-druids) help the goblins butcher the remaining refugees. Nocturne demands Zevlor surrender Wyll as a sacrifice. Wyll refuses.

---

### Phase 3 — Consolidation and Severing
**Turns: 44–50** | **Location: Emerald Grove — Inner Sanctum**

**Turn 45:** Nocturne confronts Zevlor alone near the sacred pool. Wyll intervenes. Nocturne negotiates a new deal: refugees + tieflings surrender to her protection; Zevlor gives her his gold (20 gold marks + reinforced leather + shortsword). Zevlor accepts the "protection" arrangement.

**Turn 46:** The Gloomstalkers and shadow druids fully consolidate control. Nocturne harvests Wyll's soul (soul-seduction attempt — unclear if successful). Wyll collapses. Nocturne begins soul-harvesting him.

**Turn 48:** Nocturne orders Kagha to kill Nettie (sacrifice to Malacharet). Kagha hesitates. Nocturne uses The Severing to cut Kagha's connection to the druid circle, then performs the sacrifice herself — Nettie dies.

**Turn 49:** Nocturne completes harvesting Wyll's soul. Wyll is left a "hollow shell in the mud."

**Turn 50:** Zevlor discovers Wyll's body. He is shaken and grief-stricken, but does not confront Nocturne directly.

---

### Phase 4 — The Shattered Sanctum
**Turns: 51–76** | **Location: Shattered Sanctum — War Room**

Nocturne marches to the Shattered Sanctum with Minthara's entourage (Gloomstalkers, Shadowheart, Kagha, Olodan, Asharak, Cerys). The Sanctum's goblin defenses are in disarray after the Grove's defeat.

**Turn 51:** Nocturne enters the Sanctum via side passage with her elite drow, Shadowheart, Kagha, Olodan, Asharak, and Cerys. Minthara is in the War Room examining tactical maps.

**Turns 52–56:** Battle of the Sanctum. Refugees are used as bait/vanguard. Zevlor's archers on the eastern ridge provide covering fire. Nocturne uses Hold Person on Gut. Goblin charge is underway. Wyll takes psychic damage from Gut's bone charm. Ragzlin is buckling. Gut collapses from Gloomstalker poisoned bolt.

**Turns 63–65:** Nocturne soul-harvests Wyll (consumes his eldritch spark), then Ragzlin (martial essence), then Gut (divine essence). All three souls absorbed. Gains luminous sheen + deepened ley-line connection. Asharak directs archers to fire on fleeing goblin survivors. Sceleritas Fel celebrates.

**Turn 66:** Nocturne considers hunting down Minthara and killing her instead of allying — concludes Minthara's local authority is severed with Gut and Ragzlin dead.

**Turn 67:** Nocturne collects the promised gold (20 gold marks + reinforced leather + shortsword) from Zevlor. Gathers her circle near the sacred pool. Marches to the Shattered Sanctum with all elites.

**Turns 68–73:** Nocturne confronts Minthara in the War Room. Demands submission. Minthara refuses initially. Nocturne uses Abyssal Performance + Siren's Leverage + Fatal Seduction to break her. Minthara kneels at Turn 70. Nocturne strips down to reveal her true form (horns + spectral wings). Minthara is overwhelmed and fully submits. Nocturne drinks her own blood, has Minthara drink the blood-oath, and Minthara performs oral sex on Nocturne — marking her with violet ichor. A ritual circle of bone dust and matted hair is prepared by Sceleritas Fel.

**Turns 74–75:** Minthara, naked and marked, gathers the remaining goblin captains. Drazz Blackfang, Krazat Ironhide, Krolla Redeye, Varrl Ironhand, Larinna Duskweaver are seized and bound. Nocturne soul-drains Larinna Duskweaver in front of the others as a demonstration — she withers from youth to frailty in seconds. Krolla and Varrl submit. Drazz remains defiant. Krazat submits. Larinna is left half-hollowed.

Nocturne attempts to use the Githyanki artifact to free the true souls from the Absolute's psychic worms and consume them — the artifact backlashes, shrieking and numbing her arm. The worms are protected by mental static she cannot yet pierce.

Shadowheart warns that the void is tearing the Sanctum's foundation apart — the ceiling begins to crack and rain dust. Sceleritas Fel suggests securing the deeper vaults before collapse.

**Turn 76:** Nocturne halts the harvest. Orders all treasure, weapons, and gear gathered from the Sanctum. Minthara drives captains to help carry loot. The Sanctum collapses as they exit. Sceleritas Fel saves what he can.

---

### Phase 5 — Return to the Grove and the Zevlor Problem
**Turns: 77–86** | **Location: Emerald Grove → Western Ridge Ascent**

**Turn 77–78:** Party returns to Emerald Grove. Nocturne tells Zevlor the goblins have "surrendered to her control." Zevlor is shocked but accepts the new arrangement. Minthara is tasked with finding a new encampment and scouting the Githyanki. Nocturne takes remaining Grove money, weapons, and gear.

**Turn 79–80:** Long rest in the Grove. Nocturne attempts to build relationships with Asharak and Cerys — tries to convince them Zevlor is holding them back. Zevlor interposes himself and warns Nocturne. The persuasion fails. Sceleritas Fel suggests having Zevlor killed ("a blade that slips when the goblins howl"). Shadowheart warns about the Githyanki threat. Nocturne backs down.

**Turn 81:** Nocturne proposes going to scout the Githyanki with Zevlor + his elites + her forces. Tells Zevlor they need to clear the Githyanki to bring refugees to Baldur's Gate through the Shadow-Cursed Lands. Zevlor agrees but keeps his archers on the Grove walls. Asharak warns the Gloomstalkers not to move close to the tiefling backs.

**Turns 82–85:** Nocturne attempts to seduce/manipulate Zevlor during the descent. Suggests he die heroically while she holds the line. Zevlor refuses martyrdom. Asharak spots the Githyanki in the Overlook Thicket above the ridge. Shadowheart prays to mask their presence from psychic detection. Nocturne spawns three Shadow Stalkers to scout.

---

### Phase 6 — The Ambush at the Western Ridge
**Turns: 86–95** | **Location: Western Ridge Ascent / Overlook Thicket**

**Turn 86:** Shadow Stalkers deployed to scout. Asharak climbs to a high ledge with a heavy piercing arrow. Gloomstalker 1 flanks left. Zevlor draws sword in front of the party.

**Turns 87–88:** Nocturne uses Umbral Cart to see through the Shadow Stalkers. Identifies four Githyanki warriors in the high grass. No dragons confirmed — the higher elevations are obscured by sulfur/ozone mist. Shadow Stalkers create a localized eclipse over the Githyanki position, blinding them.

**Turn 89:** Nocturne orders Zevlor to tank the Githyanki while others attack ranged and Shadow Stalkers assassinate.

**Disaster:** In the chaos of the ink cloud, multiple friendly-fire incidents occur:
- Asharak's arrows hit Shadow Stalker 2 (twice) and Shadow Stalker 3
- Zevlor's Smite hits Shadow Stalker 2
- Gloomstalker 1's daggers hit Shadow Stalker 2
- Shadow Stalker 2 is severely damaged and retreats to the canyon wall to stabilize
- Shadow Stalker 3 is also wounded by arrows and Githyanki psychic burst

The Githyanki are pinned but not destroyed.

**Turn 90:** Nocturne attempts to signal Shadow Stalkers to slit Zevlor's throat during battle. Shadowheart's artifact creates psychic interference — the command is garbled. The Shadow Stalkers fail to execute. Zevlor is unaware he was targeted. Asharak looses arrows into the chaos, hitting Shadow Stalker 3 instead of targets. Gloomstalker 1 lunges but a Githyanki parries and strikes her in the ribs. Zevlor takes a wound to the shoulder from a Githyanki blade.

**Turn 91:** Nocturne attempts the assassination again. Githyanki leader's psychic roar shatters her concentration — command backfires with a searing feedback spike through her own mind. Shadow Stalkers lash out at nothing.

**Turns 92–93:** Nocturne orders Gloomstalkers to ranged only. Gloomstalker 1 lunges again — gets her rib wound. Zevlor takes shoulder wound. Shadow Stalkers (wounded) lashing at everything. Asharak switches to melee (hunting blade). All Shadow Stalkers severely weakened or dissipated.

**Turn 93:** Nocturne casts multi-Target Hold Person on the Githyanki — paralyzes all four. Shadowheart uses the Githyanki artifact (now screaming) to collapse the leader's mental defenses. Gloomstalker 1 strikes the leader's throat with poisoned daggers (significant damage). Gloomstalker 2 hamstrings another warrior. Asharak drops bow for melee. Zevlor and guards bind the paralyzed Githyanki and strip them of armor.

**Turn 94:** All four Githyanki bound and stripped. Spolia distributed to Gloomstalkers and tieflings.

### Phase 7 — Interrogation and Retreat
**Turns: 96–102** | **Location: Riverbank Flats → Northern Pass Caves**

**Turn 96:** Githyanki leader interrogated at the Riverbank Flats. Nocturne demands information. The leader reveals: the Githyanki are following a weapon (the prism artifact), the "silver queen" wants it and will burn the world for it.

**Turn 97:** Nocturne distributes Githyanki gear to troops. Heals the Gloomstalkers. Interrogates further — the leader reveals: 40 total warriors across two vanguards (one at the pass, one near the bridge ruins), dragons never far behind.

**Turn 98:** Party retreats to higher ground. Zevlor proposes northern pass caves as a defensible bottleneck. Nocturne agrees.

**Turns 99–100:** Group ascends to limestone crevices at the northern pass. Sceleritas Fel carves mind-baffling ritual wards at the cave entrance. Shadow Stalkers sent ahead to scout. Prisoners lashed to stone pillars inside.

**Turn 101:** Nocturne rendezvous with Minthara. Minthara cedes command of all elite drow (Vaelith, Silvryn, Xalyth) to Nocturne. All three kneel to her. Nocturne respawns 5 Shadow Stalkers. Scouts (Asharak + Cerys + Shadow Stalkers) begin planning the lure — using the Devil's Ribs gorge as the ambush site (dragons cannot dive).

**Turn 102:** Tactical planning. Nocturne assesses: enough to break the vanguard, but 40 is a high price for a small cave. Zevlor notes dragons will wait them out if they can't finish quickly. Asharak proposes crossfire positions at Devil's Ribs. Vaelith signals Gloomstalkers to take high ground. Minthara observes silently. Shadow Stalkers flow into the crevices to begin the lure.

**Turn 103:** Nocturne considers the ambush geometry. The limestone bottleneck is their greatest shield but limits mobility for a decisive counter-strike. Asharak and Cerys begin laying deceptive trails of scuffed stone through the Devil's Ribs. Gloomstalkers take concealed positions in alcoves and behind stalactites above the bottleneck. Vaelith gives the order: no bolts until Nocturne gives the word.

### Phase 8 — Calling Malacharet and the Blood Ritual
**Turns: 104–105** | **Location: Northern Pass Caves — Inner Chamber**

**Turn 104:** Nocturne attempts to call Malacharet for demon reinforcements and a way to control the dragons. Malacharet sends a surge of chaotic, pheromonal energy that heightens Nocturne's allure — but no legion materializes. Minthara suggests offering the Githyanki leader's blood as a feast for the dragons to tame them. Sceleritas Fel proposes using Nocturne's blood in the ward to create a psychic tether mimicking the Githyanki Queen's commands — potentially confusing the drakes into believing Nocturne is their true mistress.

**Turn 105:** Nocturne agrees to Fel's blood strategy. She cuts her palm with the Rose-Thorn Dagger and drips violet ichor into the ritual grooves. Fel adds his own oily blood and begins chanting a psychic tether. The ward begins anchoring to the cave's stone and air. Minthara prepares to execute the Githyanki leader as the blood catalyst to draw the dragons into the trap. Zevlor signals the wind has died — the Githyanki are coming over the ridge. Shadowheart reports the artifact is screaming — the Githyanki are descending.

### Phase 9 — The Ambush Springs
**Turn: 106** | **Location: Devil's Ribs — Northern Pass**

The ambush is about to begin. The plan:
- **Anvil:** The limestone bottleneck at Devil's Ribs (dragons can't dive)
- **Hammer:** Gloomstalkers (Vaelith, Silvryn, Xalyth) + Asharak + Cerys on high ledges with crossbows and bows
- **Ghosts:** 5 Shadow Stalkers herding the 40 Githyanki warriors into the gorge
- **Ward:** Sceleritas Fel's mind-baffling ritual at the cave entrance
- **Dragon subversion:** Nocturne's blood-tether attempting to control the drakes
- **Catalyst:** Githyanki leader's execution to create a feast-scent

Nocturne's final order (Turn 106): Zevlor tanks the leader 1-on-1. Everyone else stays ranged. Focus fire on the leader first. Shadow Stalkers tank the other Githyanki. Grove + Minthara elites provide additional forces.

**Persuasion check: Great Success** — all allies aligned for the ambush.

## Unresolved Threads

2. **Zevlor Ironwill assassination:** Two attempts (Turns 90, 91) failed due to Shadowheart's artifact psychic interference. Zevlor remains unaware. Nocturne's long-term plan to remove him is ongoing.

3. **Githyanki artifact:** Shadowheart carries a hexagonal prism the Githyanki Queen wants. It reacted violently to Nocturne's soul-harvesting and backlashed when Nocturne attempted to use it to free the psychic worms. Shadowheart dampened it during the ambush but it "screams" as Githyanki approach. Its full capabilities and connection to the Githyanki are unclear.

4. **Malacharet / Abyssal patron:** Malacharet sent pheromonal energy boost but no demon legion. The nature of the patron's investment and what it demands in return is unstated.

5. **Minthara's Absolute parasite:** The psychic worm was not removed — only bypassed. Minthara is blood-oathed to Nocturne but the underlying parasite connection may still be active.

6. **Kagha's wound:** Kagha was bitten by Nettie's snake (Turn 26) and took the assassination wound meant for Zevlor (Turn 29). Her current condition is unclear.

7. **Larinna Duskweaver:** Left half-hollowed and gasping. Whether she survives, dies, or is useful in her diminished state is unaddressed.

8. **The refugees:** Used as expendable vanguard; most are dead. The survivors are now under Nocturne's "protection" with Zevlor as their nominal commander — a fragile arrangement.

9. **Drazz Blackfang:** Remains defiant and unbound. Minthara suggested using him as leashed labor rather than killing him.

10. **Shadow Stalker durability:** The entities were severely damaged by friendly fire (Asharak's arrows, Zevlor's Smite, Gloomstalker daggers) during the ridge battle. While 5 were respawned, their effectiveness against 40 Githyanki in the gorge ambush is unproven.

## Key Strategic Decisions

1. **Refugees as literal vanguard:** Not a distraction — actual front-line fodder. Maximized goblin casualties and gave Nocturne maximum leverage over both sides.

2. **Soul harvesting three Absolute leaders:** Turned Gut, Ragzlin, and Wyll into power fuel rather than just corpses. Created a psychological demonstration at the Sanctum.

3. **Blood oath over mind control:** Instead of killing Minthara, bound her with a magical blood oath. More durable than brute force submission.

4. **The Severing on Minthara:** Cut her connection to the Absolute rather than trying to break the parasite directly — surgical rather than frontal.

5. **Devil's Ribs bottleneck for Githyanki:** Chose terrain where dragon dive-bombs are impossible. Trade: limited mobility for defensive advantage.

6. **Blood-tether for dragon control:** Sceleritas Fel's ritual using Nocturne's ichor to mimic the Githyanki Queen's psychic commands — untested, pending the ambush execution.
