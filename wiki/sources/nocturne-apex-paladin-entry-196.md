Location: The Black Lily's Den, Baldur's Gate, Early Afternoon

The cool, quiet air of Nocturne's (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) hideout offered a sanctuary for thought. The external gears of House Valerius were now turning to her command, mobilizing agents and funds for the Underdark intervention. Lysandra, her intellect captivated by the chilling artifacts, was already unraveling the arcane mysteries. All was proceeding with calculated precision.

But the question of Julian (Age 32), her eldest brother, lingered. His resentment was a palpable, predictable variable. He was the heir apparent, now utterly bypassed by Father's direct decree, his incompetence laid bare. The thought of leveraging him, twisting his predictable ambition into a tool for her own ascension, was a tempting strategic exercise.

***

**Nocturne's Internal Monologue & Planning:**

*Julian. A pawn, albeit a conceited one. Father's words, "No Julian. No interference," were a clear victory, a subtle shift in the balance of power. But he will not accept irrelevance. He will scheme. He will resent. Could that resentment be... channeled?*

*Could I utilize Julian as 'cannon fodder' for the Architect mission? Pretend to ally, offer him a chance to 'redeem' himself in Father's eyes, to 'share the glory' of uncovering the conspiracy? Then, place him in a position where his predictable incompetence or arrogance would lead to his demise. A convenient, silent removal of a persistent obstacle. My current objective is the Architect and the Convergence, not to babysit an emotional liability.*

*Julian Valerius. (Level 8 Mastermind Rogue - equivalent). He fancies himself a master of courtly intrigue and manipulation, but lacks true strategic depth or combat prowess. He would avoid direct confrontation at all costs, preferring to operate from the shadows or through proxies. This is his weakness.*

*   **Pros of using him as 'cannon fodder':**
    *   **Eliminates a Rival:** If he dies, a constant source of petty interference and a contender for the family's future leadership is removed.
    *   **Conserves My Resources:** His life would be expended instead of mine or a valuable agent's.
    *   **Tests Defenses:** He could serve as a probe, testing the Architect's immediate defenses in the Underdark without risking my critical presence.
*   **Cons/Risks:**
    *   **Incompetence is a Liability:** Julian's lack of genuine tactical and combat skill makes him unreliable. His panic or blunders could compromise the entire delicate Underdark operation, alerting the Architect prematurely or creating a chaotic scene that jeopardizes my primary objective. This is an unacceptable risk for a mission of this magnitude.
    *   **Political Fallout:** Father just placed unprecedented trust in me, bypassing Julian. If Julian were to die under my command, especially so soon after my return, Father's pragmatic mind might suspect manipulation. It could undermine the very trust and authority I just secured. Father values the family's public image and internal stability, even if he is ruthless in private.
    *   **Lack of Control:** I cannot guarantee his actions under pressure. He is prone to grandstanding or cowardly retreat. I require absolute control over my assets. Julian is inherently uncontrollable in a high-stakes field operation.

*The allure of eliminating a rival is potent, but not at the cost of jeopardizing the primary mission or my newly solidified authority. Julian is too unpredictable for a critical field role. He is a piece to be managed politically, through gradual erosion of his influence, not through a messy, potentially compromising death by proxy. My intellect demands efficiency, and Julian's presence in the Underdark would be inefficient. He is a political variable, not a tactical asset.*

***

Nocturne's eyes narrowed, a decision made. The path to power was rarely direct, but always precise. Julian was not cannon fodder for this particular war; he was a political obstacle to be managed from afar, his inevitable demise orchestrated through less volatile means. Her focus remained on the Architect.
