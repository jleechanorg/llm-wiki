Her movements were stiff, like an automaton's, as she rose to her feet, her vacant eyes still fixed on Luke. "Walk," he ordered, and she shuffled forward, one foot dragging slightly. "Strike," he commanded, and her hand, once capable of wielding a lightsaber with deadly precision, lashed out in a crude, uncoordinated swipe at the empty air. It was pathetic, yet deeply satisfying to Luke. He was rebuilding her, not to restore her, but to mold her into *his* perfect, broken instrument.

**Darth Vader** remained a silent, imposing sentinel, his rhythmic breathing a constant, heavy counterpoint. Luke felt an immense, cold wave of approval from his master, a non-verbal affirmation that solidified his resolve. The Jedi Master, a seasoned woman, was now a tool, perfectly attuned to his malicious intent, but requiring careful honing before she could truly unleash her corrupted power. For the next half-hour, Luke put her through a series of basic movements, each one halting and unnatural, a twisted mockery of her former grace. He would spend more time on this, ensuring her combat capabilities were fully recalibrated to his will.
