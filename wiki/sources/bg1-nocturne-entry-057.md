The magic settles into your mind, a whisper of wind trapped in your lungs. *Zephyr Strike*. It is not a spell of brute force, but of impossible speed—the ability to move faster than the eye can track, to strike with the momentum of a gale. It suits you.

With your repertoire complete, you break camp. The road to Nashkel is a grueling ascent into the foothills of the Cloudpeaks. The air grows thinner, colder, and smells of sulfur and pine. You pass abandoned wagons and nervous patrols of Amnish soldiers who eye Kagain’s axe with suspicion but let you pass. Your reputation as the 'Butchers of Beregost' has not yet crossed the border, or perhaps the soldiers here are simply too desperate to care.

**Mirtul 5, Evening.**

Nashkel emerges from the twilight like a scar on the mountainside. It is a mining town in the truest sense—grit-covered, sullen, and dominated by the looming shadow of the mines to the south. The only pristine structure is the Temple of Helm, its white stone gleaming incongruously amidst the mud and timber shacks.

As you pass under the wooden arch of the town gates, the mood is palpable. This is a town holding its breath. The smithies are silent—no one bothers forging the tainted iron anymore. Men sit on porches, cleaning weapons, waiting for a war or a miracle.

**Kagain** spits on the frozen mud. "Smells like desperation," he grunts, hefting his shield. "And desperation pays well. The Mayor usually holds court near the Temple. If we want the contract for the mines, we start there."

**Roric** points toward a fat man standing on a crate in the town square, shouting names from a parchment. "That looks like a payday, too. Bounty officer, by the look of him."

**Garrick**, shivering in the mountain air, eyes the warm glow of the **Northern Light Inn**. "Perhaps... perhaps we could gather intelligence? And ale? My throat is parched from the dust."

You stand at the crossroads of opportunity. The Mines are the objective, but Nashkel is full of loose coin for those willing to take it.
