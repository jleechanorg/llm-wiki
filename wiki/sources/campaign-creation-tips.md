## What Makes a Compelling Campaign

A great campaign needs three things: a hook that draws players in, stakes that matter to the characters, and room for the story to breathe. The AI in WorldArchitect.AI handles the mechanics — your job is to bring the narrative soul.

## Core Campaign Elements

**The Hook** — Start with a question, not an answer. "Why is the merchant convoy missing?" beats "go kill the bandits." The AI responds to open-ended prompts far better than kill quests.

**Faction Tiers** — The World of Assiah has six major factions. Aligning with a faction unlocks storylines, special intel, and mechanical benefits. A campaign gains depth when it ties to faction politics.

**The Right Scope** — Start small, expand outward. A missing person in a small town can unravel into a continent-spanning conspiracy. The Celestial Wars created perfect soil for this: history bleeds into present events.

## Campaign Arcs

- **Personal Arc** — Your character's backstory becomes the plot. A displaced noble seeking to reclaim their house, a mercenary hunting the company that betrayed them.
- **Faction Arc** — Align with a faction and ride its rise or fall. The Tsuran Dominion, Adamant Vault, First-Spear Clans — each has its own agenda and enemies.
- **World Arc** — The Celestial Wars never truly ended. Shattered Host remnants, twin heir disputes, Nexus instability — these are time bombs waiting to explode.

## Practical Tips

- **Ground your prompts in the world** — "I ask the NPC about the Celestial Wars" gives the AI more to work with than "I talk to the NPC."
- **Set the mood** — Describe what you want the scene to feel like. "I want a tense negotiation where the NPC is hiding something" produces richer results.
- **Let the AI surprise you** — WorldArchitect.AI generates dynamic events. Roll with unexpected twists — some of the best campaign moments come from dice failures or NPC betrayals.
- **Return to your campaign** — State persists across sessions. A running campaign gets richer the more you play it.
