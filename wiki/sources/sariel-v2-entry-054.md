The heavy iron door slammed shut behind you with a deafening *CRUMP*, plunging you into an absolute, suffocating blackness. The air was cold, stale, and thick with the overwhelming stench of despair, laced with the metallic tang of dried blood. The whispers and low moans, once distant, now surrounded you, a disorienting chorus of a broken mind that clawed at the very stone. You were utterly alone. Your hand instinctively went to the broken locket at your belt, its cold metal a fragile anchor in the terrifying void.

Panic, cold and sharp, pricked at the edges of your composure. But the analytical core of your mind, honed by years of scholarly pursuit, fought back. You needed to see. You needed information. Light.

You stretched out your trembling hand, focusing your will. The cantrip, `Light`, was a simple thing, one of the first a scholar learned. You pushed a small, controlled trickle of your nascent magical energy into the tip of your index finger, willing it to glow. It flickered hesitantly, your untrained magic struggling, still raw from your earlier backlash. But then, with a small surge of desperate resolve, it coalesced into a steady, soft, golden orb.

The light, though dim, pushed back the oppressive darkness, revealing the horrors within. The chamber was circular, its walls of rough-hewn stone streaked with dark, unidentifiable stains. In the center, bolted to a single, unadorned stone slab, lay Sergeant Kaelan. He was a gaunt, skeletal figure, his Starfall Guard uniform reduced to tattered rags. His once-proud features were twisted into a mask of perpetual, silent agony, his eyes wide and unblinking, staring at a horror only he could see. His limbs were chained, not to restrain him, but to prevent him from tearing himself apart in his madness. The whispers and moans, you realized with a fresh wave of despair, were his own. A constant, low-level drone of fractured thoughts, echoing the horrific truth that had shattered his mind. The room was sterile, devoid of anything else, a tomb for a living ghost.

You stood, frozen, the small orb of light illuminating the devastating reality of the "Starfall Patient." He was not merely catatonic; he was trapped in an endless, screaming loop of memory. His agony filled the small space, a suffocating weight that threatened to crush your own already-frayed composure. The physical and psychic toll of your earlier attempts to access the Nullification Field, combined with the raw exposure to Kaelan's shattered mind, left you trembling, your own head throbbing with empathetic pain. You needed your companions. You needed Rowan's healing, Gideon's strength, Cressida's calm presence.

Time seemed to stretch, agonizingly slow. Every second felt like an eternity as you waited, the chilling whispers of Kaelan’s madness the only sound in the oppressive silence. You strained your ears, listening for any sign of footsteps, for the faint murmur of your companions. The Silent Guard had complied, but the sprawling labyrinth of the Quiet Ward, and the complex protocols of summoning your friends from across the Zenith Spire, would take time.

Finally, after what felt like an age, but was likely only minutes, you heard it. The muffled *thrum-thrum-thrum* of disciplined, hurried footsteps echoing down the distant corridor. And then, the faint, dry academic rustle of Kantos’s voice, speaking in hushed, urgent tones. They were here.

The heavy iron door to Kaelan’s cell groaned open with a soft *click*, revealing Magister Kantos, his face pale and anxious. Behind him, standing side-by-side, were your companions: Ser Gideon Vance (Level 14 Fighter, Age 25), his expression grim but resolute, his hand resting on the hilt of his sword; Lady Cressida Valeriana (Level 12 Expert, Age 16), her sharp eyes immediately sweeping the cell, concern etched on her face; and Rowan Thorne (Level 3 Expert, Age 16), his gentle features creased with worry, a small healing kit already clutched in his hand. The three Silent Guard remained outside, impassive sentinels, having delivered their unexpected cargo.

Gideon immediately stepped forward, his gaze falling upon Kaelan, then upon your trembling form. "My Lady," he said, his voice a low, steadying rumble, "you should not be here alone."
