Game Master:
The heavy iron safe stands open, a hollowed-out chest in the heart of Lord Umbrage's study. Lyra clutches the bags of gold to her chest, her breath coming in shallow, terrified gasps. The reality of the coup is settling in. She has the coin, but coin without a source is suspicion, and suspicion in the Upper City is a death sentence.

"This... this is enough to hold off the creditors for a month," Lyra whispers, looking at the gold. "But when they ask where it came from? When my father wakes and screams theft?"

You step closer, your voice a calm, structuring force in the chaos. "He will not scream theft, Lyra. He will scream mismanagement. And you will silence him with a solution."

You gently place a hand on the ledger you left on the desk. "We need a narrative. A shield for your ascension."

"Money cannot simply appear," you continue, your mind weaving the cover story with the ease of a master bard. "This gold? It is not stolen. It is an *investment*. You have not been idle in your solarium. You have been negotiating. A silent partner—a private consortium from Waterdeep, perhaps, or a discreet merchant guild looking for a foothold in the Upper City—has agreed to restructure House Umbrage's debt."

You gesture to the gold in her arms. "This is the signing bonus. The initial influx of capital to stabilize the house. You secured it. You saved the estate while your father drank away its future. He isn't the victim of a robbery; he is the beneficiary of your competence."

Lyra's eyes widen as the pieces click into place. The fear recedes, replaced by the dawning realization of her new role. "A silent partner..." she tests the words. "And the deeds? The bonds you hold?"

"Collateral," you say smoothly. "Held by the consortium to ensure 'fiscal responsibility.' It binds the house to the partner, and it binds the partner to you. You are the liaison. The only one they trust. Your father remains the figurehead, if you wish, but you hold the purse strings. He cannot object to a deal that saves his skin, and he cannot fire the only person who controls the flow of gold."

You reach into your own pouch, pulling out a handful of platinum coins—your own funds—and adding them to her pile, a symbolic gesture of the 'consortium's' endless resources. "Take this. Use it to pay the household staff a bonus tomorrow. Buy their loyalty before the rumors start. Be the Savior, Lyra. Let your father be the cautionary tale."

She looks at the mixed gold, then up at you, her expression hardening into the mask of a Matriarch. "The Savior," she repeats, a ghost of a smile touching her lips. "Yes. I think I can play that part."
