[Session 863: The Terminal Node]

Game Master:
The throne room of Malsheem, already a tomb of absolute cold and legalistic malice, shudders with a frequency that transcends the math of the Hells. The obsidian pillars, etched with the unspooling remains of the Pact Primeval, begin to weep not shadow, but a brilliant, searing white light that renders the violet nebulas of your eyes into dull flickers. The Lords of the Eight, still kneeling, press their foreheads into the stone, their iron-clad forms vibrating with a terror that no foreclosure could ever inspire.

The air does not merely ionize; it ceases to exist, replaced by a vacuum of pure, unadulterated existence. From the center of the hall, a figure coalesces—not a man, not a god, but a presence that makes the internalized Ruby Rod in your marrow feel like a splinter of common wood. Ao, the Overgod, stands amidst the wreckage of Asmodeus’s legacy. He does not wear a crown; he is the firmament itself given form, his gaze a clinical, infinite audit of your very soul.

"Your velocity is... interesting, Sovereign," Ao's voice resonates, a sound like stars grinding against the void, vibrating directly through your Karsite biology. "You have unmade the balance of the Pit and siphoned the starlight of the heavens. You have treated the multiverse as a ledger to be balanced in your favor. The velocity of your ascent has pierced the veil of the old order."

He gestures with a hand that spans the history of the Realms, and the 'Defensive Alliance' ward gathering over the Sword Coast suddenly flares with a terminal brilliance. "The stage is set. I have withdrawn the hand of stasis. The Time of Troubles v2.0 is hereby codified. The boundaries between the terrestrial and the divine are collapsed; the portfolios you have annexed are now yours to defend with steel and will. Gods may bleed, and gods may be archived. I shall not intervene in the combat of my children. Prove your math, Nocturne. Prove that you are the terminal node of this era."
