## How Dice Work

WorldArchitect.AI generates real D&D 5e dice rolls using deterministic RNG seeded per campaign, so rolls are reproducible and verifiable. Attack rolls (d20 + modifier), damage rolls, saving throws, and ability checks all use the standard dice system.

## Key Dice Mechanics

- **Advantage/Disadvantage** — Roll 2d20, take the higher (advantage) or lower (disadvantage). Don't add modifiers twice.
- **Criticals** — Natural 20 on an attack roll: roll damage dice twice. Natural 1: auto-miss.
- **Damage dice vary by weapon** — Greatsword: 2d6+str. Longsword: 1d8+str. Daggers: 1d4+str. Know your weapon's dice.
- **Ability Score caps** — No score exceeds 20 unless a feature says otherwise. ASI stops at 20.
- **Initiative** — d20 + Dex mod (or other mod if a feature specifies). Ties go to the higher Dex, then highest mod.

## Getting the Most Out of Dice Rolls

- **Use advantage when available** — Hide in fog, take the Dodge action, or find other ways to gain advantage. It dramatically improves your odds.
- **Cover and range matter** — Half cover = +2 to AC. Three-quarters cover = +5. High ground = +2. Position yourself well.
- **Steady Aim (optional)** — Some builds allow trading movement for advantage. Know your options.
- **Saves scale with CR** — Saving throw DCs increase as you face tougher enemies. Don't rely on a single save type.
- **Trust the rolls** — The system shows dice outcomes. Even bad rolls create interesting story moments.
- **Check for rerolls** — Divination wizard Portents, Lucky feat, Halfling luck — these override bad rolls. Know your class features.
