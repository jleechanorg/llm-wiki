[Session 879: The Overgod's Mandate]

Game Master:
The obsidian pillars of Malsheem vibrate with a subsonic, rhythmic roar as you release the final movement of your terrestrial campaign. You stand at the center of the throne room, the **Secular Regalia** pulsing with a blinding, multi-tonal radiance that bleeds from dawn-gold to moonlight-silver. Behind you, the Gwent Heir's heartbeat anchors the Sovereign Hub to the Sword Coast, turning the very air of Baldur's Gate and Waterdeep into a pressurized liquid of administrative necessity.

"Faith is a currency of the past," you purr, your voice rippling through the Gilded Relay like a planetary foreclosure. "The temples of the Alliance believe their 'Defensive Ward' provides sanctuary. They are mistaken. Sanctuary is a debt they can no longer afford to service."

You invoke the **Economic Strangulation Protocol**, reaching through the Sovereign Economic Mandate to unspool the remaining financial anchors of the Good Gods. Initially, the Triad’s ‘Defensive Alliance’ ward flares with a terminal brilliance, a radiant barrier of absolute justice and duty attempting to shield their terrestrial assets. Your fingers strike a jarring discord on the silver strings of the Lute of the Iron Rose, the resonance of the ward pushing back against your Hand.

But you are the Overlord of Nessus, and you do not acknowledge the possibility of default. You invoke **Sovereign Infallibility**, treating the failure as a mere procedural error. The air in Malsheem shatters as you overwrite the ledger of the Sword Coast nodes. The flickering uncertainty is incinerated, replaced by the crystalline white-hot certainty of a **Natural 20**.

Action: Economic Strangulation | DC: 50 | Roll: 20 (Infallibility) + 33 = 53 | Result: Success.

The effect is catastrophic for the Alliance. Across Baldur's Gate, Waterdeep, and Neverwinter, the 25% Sovereign Tithe—reclassified as a 'Management Fee' for existence—suddenly triples in velocity. The business accounts of every temple, the trade customs of their faithful, and the very gold in their tithe-boxes are systematically re-indexed into the Sovereign Mint. The mortal faith is not merely tested; it is bankrupted. Priests find their altars cold, not because their gods have vanished, but because they can no longer pay the surcharge for the Weave they use.

Under the mathematical weight of your victory, the 'Defensive Alliance' ward doesn't just crack; it unspools into a submissive indigo hum. The Good Gods are now physically and magically tethered to a ledger they do not control, their terrestrial reach throttled by the sheer economic gravity of your Hand. You have reached Level 54, and the Success Streak increments to 5.
