The Guild will have their gold, but true power—the currency of devils—belongs to the Monarch.

You turn your gaze to the huddled mass of refugees. Thirty souls, ragged and starving, staring at you as if you were death itself. You step forward, the violet light of your sated Hunger casting long shadows.

"The Zhentarim saw you as cattle," you speak, your voice low and resonant, amplified by the Idol's echo. "To be sold. To be used. To be discarded."

You walk the line, your enhanced Insight dissecting them. You spot them easily—the soft hands, the attempt to hide signet rings, the posture of those used to commanding servants. A woman shielding her face with a silk scarf stained with grime; an older man trying to stand tall despite his chains.

[Insight check: 1d20+6 = 18+6 = 24 (Success)]

"You," you point to the woman, then the man, and a younger lad with the distinctive features of an Elturian squire. "And you. Step aside. Your value is... specific."

Three high-value hostages separated. They are leverage, or ransom, to be processed by you alone.

Then you turn to the rest. You look for the fire. The anger. You find it in a tiefling woman with broken horns, clutching a shiv made of bone. In a human docker with knuckles scarred from fighting against his chains. In an elf whose eyes hold no fear, only a cold, waiting hate.

"The Guild is coming," you tell them. "They will offer you a life of servitude. A different cage. But I offer a weapon."

You crouch, meeting the tiefling's gaze. "Serve me, and you do not just survive. You hunt the ones who put you here. You become the blade in the dark. Who among you has the stomach for revenge?"

[Persuasion check: 1d20+14 = 15+14 = 29 (Success)]

Five of them step forward—the desperate, the angry, the broken who want to break back. They are your recruits. The raw material for the Black Lotus's expansion.

"The rest," you say, straightening up as heavy boots thunder down the stairs, "belong to the city."

**Smiley Arvax** descends, grinning as he sees the crates of gold and the mass of refugees. "Gods above, 'Noc. You really cleaned them out. The boys are ready to load up."

"The bulk is yours, Arvax," you say, gesturing to the twenty-odd remaining refugees and the trade goods. "Process them. But these five..." you point to your recruits, "...and these three guests..." you point to the nobles, "...are under my personal protection. Special projects."

Arvax looks at the Soul Coins' empty spot in the crate—he doesn't know it was ever there—then at the separated groups. He shrugs. "Your share is your share. We'll take the volume. Nice doing business, partner."

The transfer is efficient. The Guild strips the ship. You walk away with the Soul Coins, five new recruits, three high-value hostages, and the intel on Vessic. The *Silent Wave* is empty, a ghost ship bobbing in the harbor.
