You turn your back on the direct road to Nashkel, leading your company east into the jagged foothills that border the mines. The terrain here is treacherous—shale slopes that slide underfoot and box canyons that trap the heat of the midday sun. But the *Hunger* pulls you forward, sensing a different kind of prey.

**Roric** scouts ahead, his mood improved by the promise of gold. "Sculptors," he mutters, checking the edge of his scimitar. "Why is it always artists stealing things? A blacksmith steals an anvil, I get it. But stealing rocks to carve rocks?"

**Dynaheir** walks with a quiet dignity, ignoring the rogue's commentary. She uses her staff to navigate the scree, her eyes scanning the horizon for magical anomalies. **Minsc** acts as her shadow, glaring at every shifting pebble as if it might threaten his charge.

By early afternoon, you hear it: the rhythmic *chink-chink-chink* of metal on stone, echoing off the canyon walls like a heartbeat.

You crest a rise and freeze. Before you, carved directly into the sheer face of a grey cliff, is a face of breathtaking, impossible beauty. It is an elven woman, her expression one of sorrow and grace, emerging from the mountain as if waking from a deep sleep. Even **Kagain** pauses, grunting in grudging respect.

Suspended on a rickety wooden scaffold in front of the carving is a gaunt, frantic elf. He is covered in stone dust, his hands moving in a blur as he chisels at the statue's cheekbone. This is **Prism**.

He spots you instantly. He does not reach for a weapon; he clutches a heavy velvet pouch at his belt to his chest.

"No!" Prism screams, his voice thin and desperate. "Not yet! I am not finished! You cannot take them!"

He points a trembling chisel at you. "She needs her eyes! The emeralds... they are for her! Queen Ellesime must see!"

**Roric** steps up beside you, eyeing the pouch. "That's the bounty, Nocturne. 300 gold for the gems, likely more for his head. And he's cornered."

**Minsc** frowns, looking up at the statue. "But... look, Boo. It is pretty. The stone-man loves the stone-lady. Is it evil to make art?"

Before you can decide the sculptor's fate, the sound of heavy, iron-shod boots crunching on gravel draws your attention to the trail behind you. A man steps out from the shadow of a boulder.

He is a professional. You can tell by the way he stands—balanced, relaxed, dangerous. He wears heavy plate armor that gleams in the sun, and his hand rests on the hilt of a longsword that hums with a faint, magical vibration. His face is a mask of cold boredom.

"The artist dies today," the newcomer says, his voice a metallic rasp. "I am **Greywolf**. I have tracked this thief from Amn. Hand him over, along with the gems, and I will let you walk away with your lives."

He draws his sword. The blade glows with a cold, blue light (**Varscona +2**). It is a weapon worth a kingdom to a level 3 adventurer.

"Interfere," Greywolf adds, his eyes locking onto yours, "and I will carve my own statue from your bones."
