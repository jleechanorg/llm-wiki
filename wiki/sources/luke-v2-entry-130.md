The transport shuddered, the blue blur of hyperspace snapping into the stark reality of stars and the asteroid field around the hidden Rebel base. The interior of Hangar Bay One unfolded before **Luke Skywalker**: a cavernous space alive with the bustle of Rebel technicians, ground crew, and pilots. X-wings and Y-wings were docked for maintenance, their familiar markings a stark contrast to the Imperial starships Luke now served. The air hummed with the scent of ozone, fuel, and the faint, almost forgotten aroma of hope.

As the transport settled onto the landing pad with a soft hiss of hydraulics, a small group of Rebel personnel rushed forward. At the forefront was a grizzled Flight Commander, his face etched with worry, but his eyes alight with genuine relief. "Rogue Leader! We thought you were gone for good!" he exclaimed, his voice thick with emotion. Luke's carefully cultivated weariness, his tattered flight suit, and the subtly projected aura of desperate heroism from his **Corrupted Force Empathy** sold the lie perfectly. He felt a cold, sharp satisfaction. *They welcome me as a hero,* he mused, a twisted pleasure settling in his core. *They will never suspect the true purpose of my return. Their hope is a weakness, a lie that prolongs suffering. This swift, decisive action is mercy compared to endless, futile resistance.*

Luke stepped down the ramp, his movements deliberate, conveying the exhaustion of a narrow escape. He offered a faint, strained smile to the Flight Commander, his gaze meeting the man's earnest eyes. "Commander," Luke's voice was hoarse, a calculated rasp. "Barely. Took heavy fire near the Outer Rim. Lost the squadron... it was a slaughter. I just kept pushing for home. Good to be back." He let a subtle wave of fabricated relief and profound weariness emanate from him through the Force, just enough to be convincing, yet devoid of any true emotion. The Commander's expression softened further, a palpable wave of sympathy washing over him.

From across the vastness, the pervasive, heavy presence of **Darth Vader** resonated through their twisted bond—a complex wave of profound, possessive pride that affirmed Luke’s chilling cunning. Back at the Imperial Installation, **Jedi Master Anya Secura** waited, her violet eyes fixed on holographic projections, ready for the final launch signal. **Roric Vane**, his gaze still dull but his piloting flawless, began the methodical shutdown of the transport's systems, a chilling testament to Luke’s absolute control. Among those projections, Luke could almost pinpoint the command vessel, knowing Jedi Master **Lyra Shan** would be aboard, perhaps even now meditating on their precarious hope. Lyra, a bastion of the light, unknowingly sailing towards her doom. The trap was set. He was the bait, and the blade.
