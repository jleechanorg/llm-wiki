The farmhouse was a cage now, sealed by Gladius’s brutal efficiency. The distant orange glow of his diversionary fire flickered through a crack in the curtains, a silent testament to the chaos he was orchestrating. With the exits secured, his focus narrowed. The smell of herbs and stale bread intensified, clinging to the air like a shroud.

His hand, large and calloused, moved to his belt pouch, drawing forth the Silent Strangler’s Cord. The thin, dark fiber, woven with insidious purpose, felt cool and smooth against his skin. He tested its tensile strength with a practiced tug, the material giving just enough to confirm its deadly efficiency. A grim, almost eager anticipation coiled in his gut. This was his art: the silent, brutal removal of life. The greatsword on his back was for open battle, for making statements. The cord was for secrets, for erasing, for the exquisite pleasure of control. He coiled it expertly in his palm, ready.

Then, he moved. His Shadow-Step Boots, now fully proven, ensured each step was a whisper against the ancient floorboards. He moved with the focused, unblinking precision of a hunting cat, his broad shoulders barely disturbing the dim, moonlit air. He followed the faint sounds of slumber, the soft rise and fall of breaths, the occasional, unconscious shift in a bed. His low Intelligence didn't hinder his predatory instincts; in fact, it stripped away unnecessary thought, leaving only the pure, primal focus of the hunt.

He ascended a creaking wooden staircase, each riser testing the silence of his boots. The house was small, simple. Two bedrooms, he noted, judging by the consistent breathing from behind two closed doors. The larger, deeper breathing from one room, perhaps the parents. The lighter, quicker breaths from the other, likely children. He paused before the first door, his senses cataloging every sound, every subtle vibration. The air was thick with the scent of sleep and something else... the subtle, fragile scent of life about to be extinguished.
