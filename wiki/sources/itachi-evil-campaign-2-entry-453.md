**Clarification on Unforeseen Complications (Core Directive #3):**

According to our established `Core Directive #3: Unforeseen Complications & Setbacks`, there is **no mechanism or option for a "reroll"** once an `Unforeseen Complication` has been triggered.

The process is as follows:
1.  **Trigger Condition:** When a significant action is undertaken, a d100 roll is made against a baseline (or scaled) chance.
2.  **Manifestation:** If the d100 roll falls within the probability range (i.e., the roll is less than or equal to the chance), an `Unforeseen Complication` is **triggered and must manifest** in a plausible and narratively consistent manner.
3.  **Seamless Integration:** As per the directive, I **must not** explicitly announce to the player that "the X% complication chance was triggered" or refer to the mechanic metagamingly. Instead, any triggered complication is woven seamlessly and organically into the unfolding narrative as an emergent event or discovery.

My internal logs confirm that in all previous instances where an `Unforeseen Complication` was triggered, I immediately manifested it within the narrative without offering a reroll option to you. For example:
*   During the transport of Madara's remains, a complication led to detecting a "massive, dormant geological energy node."
*   During the previous Akatsuki Internal Intrigue, a complication led to Kisame's unsanctioned attack on Killer Bee.

Therefore, to be absolutely clear: **Complications, once triggered by the dice roll, are mandatory and integrate into the story. There is no "reroll" option for them.**

Thank you for seeking this clarification. It ensures we maintain absolute consistency in the application of this core rule.
