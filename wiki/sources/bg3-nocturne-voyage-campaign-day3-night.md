## Session Summary

### War Room Confrontation (Turn 0)

Nocturne Sosuke enters the War Room — a stone chamber carved into the mountainside. Minthara interrogates her about her lack of Absolute brand. Sceleritas Fel interjects reverently. Nocturne asserts dominance; Minthara's authority visibly wavers as Nocturne's Abyssal presence pushes back against the Absolute's psychic pressure in Minthara's mind.

**Nocturne's demand:** Elite troops + spy companions for infiltration before the raid.

**Minthara grants:** A vanguard of personal Drow scouts + bugbear ravagers.

### Scout & Loot Phase (Turns 1–2)

Minthara sends scouts toward the Grove. Nocturne searches the War Room:
- **Scroll of Invisibility** (side table)
- **Heavy iron key stamped with spider motif**
- Locked wooden chests (unexplored)

Outside the Sanctum, a discarded traveler's pack is found (dried rations + bitter herb vial).

### The Double Betrayal Strategy (Turns 3–6)

Nocturne's core strategic move emerges:

1. **Command the goblin brawlers to attack the Emerald Grove** — convincing them the Grove's defenses are a sham
2. **Wait for Grove defenders to engage the goblins**
3. **Betray the goblins mid-battle** — attack them from behind
4. **Present herself as Grove ally** — arriving to "save" the defenders

Gribk (lead goblin) accepts the plan enthusiastically. Sceleritas Fel endorses the deception with gleeful anticipation.

**Nocturne's tactical preference:** Direct frontal assault through the Moraven Basin — "no back door for those who seek glory."

### The March (Turns 7–9)

The goblins charge the Grove. Nocturne holds position at the ravine mouth with elite Drow scouts. At the moment of Grove engagement, Nocturne strikes — Rose-Thorn Dagger drawing shallow wounds on Gribk and Krazz — before the goblins can process the betrayal.

**Status at transcript break:** Betrayal in progress, Grove defenders engaging, Sceleritas Fel lurking to prevent retreat.

## Key Patterns

### Player Character: Nocturne Sosuke

- **Aesthetic:** porcelain skin, torn blue silk, violet-to-black eyes — marble and silk vs. the filth of the sanctum
- **Presence:** Creates ozone-scented chill; shadows elongate toward her; Abyssal influence registers as "far older and more demanding than the Absolute"
- **Social manipulation:** Never fights fair if she can fight smart — deception is the primary tool

### The Double Betrayal

The strategy follows a precise structure:
1. **Marshal the pawns** — convince disposable forces to commit to a fight
2. **Let the real fight happen** — let the third party weaken the target
3. **Eliminate the pawns mid-engagement** — arrive as savior, not attacker
4. **Present yourself as the solution** — the saved party owes you

This maps to Jeffrey's established pattern: never pick from the menu presented; create a third option that reframes the decision space. The goblins are not allies to be betrayed after the fact — they are fuel for a rescue narrative.

### Minthara's Doubt

The Absolute's voice "grows quiet, almost afraid" when Nocturne is present. This establishes Nocturne as a power that competes with — or supersedes — the Absolute's influence over its own servants. Minthara's authority wavers visibly.

### Sceleritas Fel

A fully loyal shadow creature who reads Nocturne's plans with reverent anticipation. Acts as both narrator of the cruelty and enforcer (ensures no goblin retreats). The "wet clicking" of his joints, the "giggling like dry parchment tearing" — body horror as court jester.
