Astarion's fingers brushed against the obsidian shard, its cold, residual hum a chilling presence against his skin. Another piece of this alien puzzle, secreted away. Lingering was for fools and the soon-to-be-dead. His eyes, sharp and calculating, flicked to his companions. The Githyanki stood poised, her impatience a palpable thing, while the burly man merely grunted, eager for the next obstacle to be smashed.

"Forward," Astarion commanded, his voice a low, urgent rasp, stripping away any pretense of politeness. He pushed past the mangled Sentry, its shattered carapace crunching sickeningly underfoot, and stepped deeper into the curving corridor. The passage here was narrower, the fleshy walls pulsing with a more intense, sickly yellow light that seemed to ooze from every membrane. The constant, infernal thrum of the Nautiloid intensified, vibrating through his bones, making the tadpole behind his eye squirm with a cold, insistent urgency. His high neuroticism screamed at him to find an exit, to escape this living hell. He scanned every pulsating alcove, every snaking conduit, his Darkvision cutting through the lurid dimness, searching for threats, for traps, for anything that might impede their relentless march towards freedom. He hated this place, its alien biology, its sickening purpose, but he would survive it. He would not be a thrall again. The corridor curved sharply ahead, promising another glimpse into the nightmare of this ship.
