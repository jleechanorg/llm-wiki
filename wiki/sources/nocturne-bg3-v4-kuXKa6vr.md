I. Character Profile: Nocturne Sosuke
The Physicality of a Weapon You are a creature designed for observation and devastation, not labor. At 5'7", you are willowy and terrifyingly delicate (Strength 8, Constitution 8), possessing a frame that looks as though it might snap in a strong wind—yet you move with a fluid, predatory grace (Dexterity 14) that suggests you would bend rather than break.
The Face: Your features are a seamless blend of elven ethereality and high-born human arrogance. Your skin is pale as bone china, flawless and cool to the touch, maintained by alchemical creams that cost more than a commoner earns in a year. Your cheekbones are high and sharp, capable of catching the dimmest candlelight to cast elegant shadows.
The Eyes: Your most arresting feature. They are a vivid, unnatural violet—a genetic marker of the Sosuke bloodline. They are large, framed by thick, dark lashes that you use like a veil. When you widen them, you look innocent, almost childlike; when you narrow them, you look like a judge handing down a death sentence. They are eyes that have seen everything in the dark, yet reflect nothing back but what the viewer wants to see.
The Hair: Raven-black and lustrous, falling in heavy, silken waves to your waist. It is always perfectly coiffed, smelling of night-blooming jasmine and expensive oils. Tonight, it is pinned up loosely with a silver hairpin, allowing stray tendrils to frame your neck—a calculated vulnerability.
The Attire: You wear a sheer, floor-length robe of midnight-blue silk, belted loosely at the waist. It is designed to conceal nothing and promise everything. Beneath the silk, your body is marked only by the invisible scars of two years in the trade; your skin remains pristine, as Zylra forbids any damage to the merchandise. You are a porcelain doll that has learned to hate.
You are not just beautiful; you are distracting. Your presence in a room acts as a gravitational well. Men stumble over their words; guards forget to check credentials; rivals underestimate your intelligence because they cannot look past your face. You have turned your appearance into a blinding light, hiding the dagger of your intellect (Intelligence 16) in the glare.

II. Campaign Context: The Long Defeat
The Current State
It has been 730 days since the fall of House Sosuke.
The Asset: You are not merely a worker; you are the "Black Diamond" of Madam Zylra’s collection. Zylra charges triple for your time, not just because you are a fallen noble, but because looking at you feels like a privilege. You have spent two years refining this allure into a terrifying science. You know exactly how to tilt your head to simulate vulnerability, and exactly how to smile to simulate love.
The Sisters: Elara and Lyra are dead. They did not survive the transition. You received word six months ago that they died "of illness" in the workhouses of the Outer City. You know the truth: they were used up. They lacked your cold adaptability. Their deaths were quiet, unmourned footnotes in the Cassalanter rise to power.
The Enemy: The Cassalanters are no longer just rivals; they are the uncrowned royalty of Baldur's Gate. Their wealth is limitless, their luck supernatural. They feed on the city, and the city loves them for it.
The Routine: Horgus Gwent is a regular. He pays a premium for the irony of defiling a Sosuke, but he stays because he is hopelessly captivated. He believes you actually desire him. This is your greatest masterpiece: making a monster believe he is loved by a goddess.
The Catalyst
Tonight is different. Tonight, Horgus is not just drunk; he is ecstatic. He has been invited to the Cassalanter Ascension Gala, a private celebration of the very deal that destroyed your family two years ago. And in his drunkenness, blinded by your presence, he has brought something dangerous into your room.

III. Scene 1: The Tuesday Regular
Location: Nocturne’s Chamber Sensory Details: The smell of sour wine, heavy musk, and expensive night-blooming jasmine. The sound of rain against the shuttered window. The feeling of silk sheets that feel like cobwebs against your skin.
The Setup
The act is over. Horgus Gwent lies half-dressed on your bed, face flushed with wine and a pathetic, sloppy adoration. You sit at your vanity, the candlelight catching the sharp, elegant planes of your face. Even in the dim light, you are breathtaking—a creature that belongs in a palace, not a prison. You run a brush through your hair, watching him watch you in the mirror. He looks at you with the hunger of a starving dog.
He is talking. He always talks after. It is part of the service—letting him believe he has conquered something unattainable.
Horgus: "Gods, look at you... you were worth every copper. Better than usual. Maybe it's the occasion. You shine tonight, little bird. Truly."
He fumbles with his discarded coat pile on the floor, fishing for a wine flask. A heavy, iron-bound scroll case clatters out of his pocket, along with a small, glowing object that rolls across the rug.
The Object: It is a Soul Coin. It stops near your bare foot. The metal is cold, but the center hums with a frantic, screaming heat. You look down. You don't need an Arcana check. Your Intelligence (16) connects the dots instantly. You know the face minted into the iron. It’s Lyra.
The Revelation
Horgus laughs, seeing you stare at it. He is too drunk, and too enamored with your beauty, to notice the shift in your posture—the way your spine straightens like a drawn bow.
"Oh, careful with that. Won it off Victoro’s ledger-keeper last night. High stakes table. Can you believe it? They didn't die in the workhouse, girl. The Cassalanters... they’re efficient. They bottled them. Kept them as... vintage currency."
He takes a swig of wine, gazing at your back, lost in the curve of your neck.
"He invited me, you know. To the Ascension. Tonight at midnight. They’re renewing the pact. Going to crack open a few fresh ones to keep the gold flowing. I might even spend that one there... trade it for a seat at the high table."
The Encounter Mechanics
State: You are unarmed. You are unarmored (AC 12 due to DEX). You have 1 Hit Die. Enemy: Horgus Gwent (Commoner stats, but wealthy/protected). He is currently Prone (on bed) and Poisoned (Drunk). The Edge: You have 16 Charisma. You are not just a person to him; you are an obsession. He will hesitate. He will believe any lie you tell because he wants it to be true.
Tactical Options:
The Seductive Kill (Deception/Persuasion):
Roll: Charisma (Deception) +7 (Includes Seduction Bonus).
Action: Use his obsession against him. Whisper that you want to see the coin. Crawl back into bed. Tell him he's the only one who ever understood you.
Effect: Lure him into a vulnerable position. Use an improvised weapon (hairpin, heavy glass perfume bottle) to strike with Advantage (Surprise).
The Cold Theft (Sleight of Hand):
Roll: Dexterity (Sleight of Hand) +4.
Action: He is drunk (Disadvantage on Perception). You are graceful, fluid.
Effect: Swipe the coin and the scroll case while pouring him more wine. Slip out the window before he realizes the "goddess" has flown.
The Awakening (Intimidation/Magic):
Action: This is the moment your Class manifests. You turn your devastating presence into a weapon. You don't scream; you whisper, and the room goes cold.
Spell: Cast Vicious Mockery or Charm Person.
Flavor: You use the "Perfect Hostess" mask to shatter his mind. "You think you own me, Horgus? You are just the wallet that held my sister."

IV. Scene 2: The Red Ledger
Assuming you secure the coin and the scroll case, you have a moment of respite while Horgus is either dead, unconscious, or charmed.
The Item: Lyra's Coin Holding it is agony. You can hear her screaming in your mind. It is a loop of her final moments—betrayal, fear, calling your name. Effect: You gain 1 Inspiration. The rage clarifies everything. The numbness is gone.
The Document: The Ascension Invitation
Event: "The Second Tithe."
Location: Cassalanter Villa, The Temple of the Golden Sun (Hidden Basement).
Time: Midnight (2 hours from now).
Details: The document reveals that the Cassalanters power their fortune by sacrificing "ruined nobility" to Zariel. Your family wasn't just bankrupt; they were harvested.
Key Intel: The ritual requires a "Witness." Horgus was invited to be a Witness.
The Choice
You are standing in the room. Horgus is dealt with. You have the invitation. You have your sister's soul in your hand. You catch a glimpse of yourself in the mirror again—blood (yours or his) on your hands, but your face still perfect, still beautiful. A terrifying porcelain doll coming to life.
A. Escape: Flee the city. Use the coin to bargain for safety elsewhere. (Rejects the Call to Adventure). B. The Suicide Run: Go to the Villa. Kill Victoro Cassalanter. Die trying. C. The Contract: You realize that killing them isn't enough. You need to ruin them. You need to go to that party, use your beauty to blend in as Horgus's "plus one" or a high-end entertainer, and find the source of their power. You don't want to stop the ritual; you want to steal it.

VI. The Shadow of the Absolute (BG3 Integration)
Timeline Synchronization: This campaign begins just as The Cult of the Absolute begins its final squeeze on Baldur's Gate.
Refugee Crisis: The Outer City is flooding with refugees. The Bloom Shadow’s clientele has become more desperate.
The Heroes: A band of "heroes" (The BG3 Party) has just arrived in Rivington/Lower City. They are causing chaos, destabilizing the Steel Watch, and hunting for the Netherstones.
Gortash's Coronation: Approaching in 3 days. The Cassalanters are desperate to complete their ritual tonight to secure their standing before Gortash becomes Archduke.
The Party Dynamic: The BG3 party are high-level combatants (Level 9-10) but socially clumsy in the viper pit of the Upper City. They have mud on their boots and blood on their hands. They need a Face. They need someone who knows which fork to use, which noble is sleeping with whom, and how to walk into a ballroom without drawing steel. You are that Face.
Plot Hooks & Interactions

Alignment: Chaotic Evil/Neutral.
Attraction Status: Yes (+2 Bonus Applies).
The Hook: Astarion is prowling the Upper City, hunting for the Cazador Palace but distracted by the gala. He spots you at the event.
The Dynamic: He recognizes a fellow creature of the night. You are both beautiful, damaged things that learned to use seduction as a shield.
Interaction: He needs an invitation to a private conclave of Vampire Spawn taking place within the Cassalanter Gala. You need muscle that moves silently.
Dialogue Note: "Darling, we both know the smile is just baring teeth politely. Let's not pretend with each other. I kill the guards, you charm the door. Deal?"
2. Wyll Ravengard (The Blade of Frontiers)
Alignment: Neutral Good.
Attraction Status: Yes (+2 Bonus Applies).
The Hook: Wyll is desperate to find his father, Duke Ravengard. He believes a lead exists in the Cassalanter archives regarding the "Grand Design."
The Dynamic: He remembers House Sosuke. He remembers you from childhood balls before your ruin. He feels guilty for not saving you; you can weaponize that guilt to make him your bodyguard.
Interaction: He offers to help you save your sisters (not knowing they are dead/coins) if you help him navigate the high-society politics he has been exiled from for years. Your beauty makes him stutter, reminding him of the life he lost.

XII. The Climax: The Inversion Ritual
This is not a combat encounter where you kill everyone. You are Level 1. You will die.
This is a Complex Skill Challenge (4 Successes before 3 Failures).
The Goal: You aren't stopping the ritual. You are stepping into the circle and changing the beneficiary from the Cassalanters to Nocturne.
Round 1: The Approach
Action: Sneak to the edge of the circle without being seen.
Roll: Stealth DC 13 OR Deception (pretend to be an acolyte).
Failure: The guards spot you. Initiative starts. (You need to talk fast).
Round 2: The Disruption
Action: Victoro is reading the contract. You must interrupt the flow of magic.
Roll: Intelligence (Arcana) DC 14 to spot the mathematical flaw in the summoning.
Alternative: Charisma (Performance) DC 15 to start singing a counter-melody that disrupts their chanting.
Round 3: The Offer
Action: Zariel's avatar (a burning projection) appears. She expects the Cassalanters. You step forward.
The Pitch: "They offer you coin, Zariel. I offer you competence. They are scared of the Absolute. I will eat the Absolute for you."
Roll: Charisma (Persuasion) DC 16 (This is the big one).
Bonuses: If you have Lyra's Soul Coin, you can offer it as a "down payment." (+5 to the roll).
Round 4: The Anchor
Action: The magic shifts. It tries to burn you alive. You must hold onto the power.
Roll: Constitution Saving Throw DC 12 OR Charisma Save DC 12.
Success: The ritual locks onto you. The power flows into your veins.
Failure: You take 2d6 Fire damage and are thrown back.
Outcome: Success
The Inversion: The gold in the room turns to ash. The Cassalanters are stripped of their protection. Zariel laughs.
The Reward: You gain the College of Perdition subclass instantly. You level up to Level 2 (or 3, depending on pacing).
The Escape: The Villa goes into lockdown. You must flee in the chaos.

XV. The Survivor's Stash
You have not been idle for 730 days. You are a squirrel hiding nuts for winter, except the nuts are shivs and the winter is a heist.
Location: Inside a hollowed-out section of your vanity’s leg and taped behind a loose brick in the fireplace. Method: Acquired through petty theft from drunk clients, bribes from sympathetic guards, and traded favors with the kitchen staff.
The Inventory:
The "Hairpin" (Dagger): A custom-ordered stiletto disguised as a silver hair ornament.
Stats: 1d4 Piercing, Finesse, Light. Advantage on Deception checks to conceal it.
Currency Pouch: 125 GP, 40 SP. (A small fortune for a worker, enough to buy passage on a ship or bribe a Sergeant).
Potion of Sleep (x1): Stolen from a visiting alchemist.
Use: Can be slipped into wine. DC 13 Constitution save or fall unconscious for 1 hour.
Thieves' Tools (Improvised): Lockpicks made from corset boning and filed spoons.
