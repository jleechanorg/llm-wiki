Location: Ridge Overlooking Man Tibbet's Farm, Early Evening

The complex of Man Tibbet's farm lay before Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16), a puzzle demanding elegant solution. Her gaze, sharp and analytical, swept over the farmhouse where her **Apex Senses** had pinpointed two armed guards, and the barn, pulsating with a subtle arcane ward. The primary threat was clear: these internal guards. Infiltrating directly (Option 1) was too risky; it invited chaotic confrontation. The most efficient path, her intellect confirmed, was to draw them out, isolate them, and neutralize them silently.

Her mind, an engine of strategic calculation, began to construct the perfect lure. It had to be subtle enough not to cause a full alarm, yet compelling enough to draw professional guards away from their posts. Noise was still her enemy. What would compel vigilance, without screaming "intruder"? A distressed animal, perhaps? A minor malfunction, barely audible, that demanded investigation?

She considered the layout: the farmhouse, the barn, the distant sounds of livestock. The magical ward near the barn was a complication. Luring them near it risked an alert. The ideal location for a silent takedown would be somewhere out of sight of the farmhouse windows, away from the ward, and with sufficient cover. The far side of the barn, near the old, overgrown wagon path, offered a good ambush point.

Nocturne reached into her **Bag of Holding**, her fingers brushing against the various tools of her trade. She selected a small, tarnished silver bell, originally from a child's toy, a relic from a past, softer life. It would serve her purpose.

Her plan coalesced: create a subtle, intermittent sound to lure one guard, perhaps two, to investigate. The bell, gently activated and then silenced from a distance, could mimic a distressed animal caught in the barn, or a loose latch rattling in the wind. Something that sounded like a problem, but not a threat. Her proficiency in **Anatomy & Weak Points (Combat)** and her **Great Weapon Master** feat hummed in her awareness – once lured, the takedown would be decisive.

She would approach the far side of the barn, ensuring she was out of sight of the farmhouse windows. Then, a faint, metallic jingle, followed by silence, repeated perhaps twice, just enough to pique the curiosity of the vigilant guards. Once one, or both, emerged to investigate, she would strike from the shadows.

This was the way of the Apex Predator: precision, patience, and overwhelming force applied only at the moment of maximum advantage. The night was young, and the farm's occupants awaited their inevitable harvest.
