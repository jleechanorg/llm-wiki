The alien hum of the Nautiloid's engine seemed to deepen as the massive, fleshy doors finally tore open, revealing a swirling, pulsating throat of a tunnel. Astarion's meticulous assessment had yielded little beyond the certainty of its alien nature, and his companions' impatience was a palpable thing. *Very well. If we must rush headlong into the unknown, at least I will do so with a modicum of grace.*

"After you, my dears," Astarion drawled, though his tone held no genuine deference, merely a pragmatic desire to observe their reactions first. He kept his bone blade ready, its faint psionic hum a chilling counterpoint to the ship's internal thrum. The Githyanki, needing no further invitation, plunged into the maw first, her lithe form vanishing into the swirling light with a warrior's fierce abandon. The burly man, with a grunt of raw eagerness, followed without hesitation, his broad shoulders filling the gruesome opening for a moment before he too disappeared.

Astarion hesitated only a breath, then stepped forward, the pulsating flesh of the entryway closing behind him with a wet, squelching sigh. The air within the tunnel was immediately thicker, warmer, laden with the sickeningly sweet scent of decay mixed with ozone. The walls pulsed with an internal, sickly green and violet light, contracting and expanding as if the very passage itself was breathing. Underfoot, the surface was strangely soft, almost spongy, yet surprisingly firm, with a viscous, slippery sheen. The nauseating suction he had sensed before was stronger here, a faint current pulling them deeper into the living labyrinth. Every instinct screamed for caution, yet the tadpole behind his eye throbbed with a cold, insistent urgency, driving him forward into the belly of this beast. He suppressed a shiver of disgust and a wave of profound fear. This was not a natural place, and the sounds of the ship, the distant, muffled groans and shrieks, suggested they were far from the only cargo aboard.
