The spectral forms of Leia, Han, Chewbacca, and Obi-Wan solidified before Luke Skywalker, their fear a palpable, sickeningly sweet aroma in the air. A twisted grin stretched across Luke’s face as he gazed upon them, a stark contrast to the boy who had once yearned for their approval. The Light Side’s whispers were now a faint, almost comedic, echo in the roaring symphony of his burgeoning darkness. This was not a test of restraint, but a proving ground for his burgeoning malevolence, and his `secret sadistic side` pulsed with a predatory delight.

“Look at you,” Luke hissed, his voice a low, guttural snarl, alien even to his own ears. He took a slow, deliberate step forward, savoring the way Leia’s spectral eyes widened, a silent plea for the Luke she knew. “So weak. So full of pathetic hope. It truly is sickening.” He extended a hand, not to attack, but to gesture, as if crushing something fragile in his palm. “You thought I cared? You thought your struggles mattered? All of it… *meaningless*.” His words, infused with pure, unadulterated contempt, seemed to buffet the illusions, causing them to flicker and writhe as if in pain. The imagined suffering was a potent elixir, intoxicating his senses.

Han Solo’s illusion snarled, a silent, desperate defiance in his eyes, but Luke merely laughed, a cold, hollow sound that echoed off the chamber walls. “Even you, Solo, always so confident. Now, nothing but a trembling fool.” He could feel the Force responding to his malice, a dark current surging through him, eager to be unleashed. The air around him shimmered with uncontrolled power, a testament to the hatred he now embraced.

Obi-Wan Kenobi’s spectral form seemed to sag, his disappointment a chilling weight, but it only fueled Luke’s contempt. “Your lessons, old man? Empty. Your compassion? A weakness. The Jedi… they were a lie.” He closed his eyes for a moment, letting the dark energy within him swell, preparing for the final, satisfying act of obliteration.
