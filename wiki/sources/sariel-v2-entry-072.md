You knelt beside Rowan, the full horror of Kaelan's condition laid bare before you. His moans and whispers, once just a disorienting cacophony, now felt like a direct assault on your own fragile composure. Rowan’s gentle murmurs, while a comfort, could not pierce the wall of madness that imprisoned Sergeant Kaelan. You needed a different kind of understanding, a window into the specific torment that held him captive.

Your mind, now operating with a cold, precise focus, turned to your meager magical repertoire. `Comprehend Languages`. It was a simple spell, but one that might offer the crucial insight you needed. It wouldn't heal him, but it might translate the chaos, might turn the unintelligible whispers into a horrifying, yet decipherable, truth.

You extended your free hand, palm open, hovering inches above Kaelan's forehead. You closed your eyes, pushing a controlled trickle of your nascent magical energy into the cantrip. The subtle thrum of the Weave responded, your untrained magic still struggling faintly, but holding. You felt the familiar pull, the brief disorientation, as the spell wove its way through your mind, preparing to interpret the fractured thoughts of another.

As you opened your eyes, the moans and whispers from Kaelan's lips did not cease, but they *changed*. The chaotic cacophony resolved into a torrent of fragmented, yet horrifically clear, words. You heard snippets of battle cries, the clang of steel, the whoosh of immense wings. But beneath it all, a chilling, repeating refrain: *"...silence...the field...gone...impossible...unmoved...her eyes...cold...joy...unmaking...Lucifer...gone..."*

His words, now intelligible, painted a devastating picture. He was reliving the Starfall Gambit, trapped in the moment of your mother's ultimate act. He described the sudden, impossible void of the Nullification Field, the look of disbelief on the Apostates' faces as their magic died. And then, the horrifying climax: *"...she rose...the light...wings...so many...not a Null...she was...unmoved...Lucifer...his face...broken...joy...cold...eyes..."* The last words were a raw, desperate gasp of pure, unendurable terror, the sound of a universe shattering in his mind. The image of Alexiel, triumphant and terrifyingly composed amidst the unmaking, burned in his consciousness, forever scarring him.

You gasped, tearing your hand away, the metallic taste of blood returning to your mouth as the psychic strain momentarily overwhelmed you. The direct, unfiltered torrent of Kaelan's broken memories, the raw horror of his witnessed truth, was far more devastating than any abstract scholarly text. You had glimpsed it through his eyes, the terrifying clarity of your mother's power, and the chilling, elegant ruthlessness that accompanied it.

The raw, unfiltered horror of Kaelan's memories still clawed at your mind, the metallic taste of blood a constant reminder of the psychic backlash. You had glimpsed it – the terrifying clarity of your mother's power, the chilling efficiency in unmaking – but the vision was chaotic, uncontrolled, and utterly devastating. Rowan continued his gentle murmurs beside the tormented Sergeant, while Gideon maintained his silent vigil, and Cressida watched with an intensity that missed nothing. Magister Kantos hovered by the door, pale and anxious. The air in the cell, thick with despair and the hum of Kaelan’s madness, felt heavy and suffocating.

You pushed yourself upright, swaying slightly, but your posture was rigid with a new, grim purpose. The time for individual exploration was over. You needed their minds, their skills, their unwavering loyalty. You needed a different approach.

You swept your gaze across your companions, meeting each of their eyes in turn. Your face, pale and streaked with fresh tears, was a stark testament to the horror you had just witnessed. Your voice, though raw and trembling, was firm, cutting through the spectral whispers of Kaelan’s madness. "We have a problem," you stated, your gaze fixed on Kaelan’s tormented form, then sweeping across their faces. "A problem far more complex than any of us anticipated. And a truth that will shatter everything you thought you knew about my mother."

You paused, drawing a ragged breath, the words tasting like ash. "Sergeant Kaelan is not merely mad. His mind was shattered by witnessing a truth too profound for mortal comprehension. My mother... Magister Arcanus... her public identity was a lie. She was not a Null. She possessed a terrifying, singular power: the Nullification Field. An ability that silenced all magic within its radius. And, impossibly, she could wield her *own* magic within that void."

You saw Gideon’s eyes widen behind his helm, a flicker of profound disbelief quickly replaced by a deeper, almost fearful respect. Cressida’s sharp gaze snapped to Kantos, demanding silent confirmation, which the old Magister provided with a trembling nod. Rowan, still tending to Kaelan, visibly flinched, his gentle face etched with a horror that mirrored your own.

"The Null Genesis journals," you continued, your voice dropping, "confirm this. They theorize this power, this chilling efficiency in unmaking, is a refined manifestation of the Empyrean Affliction itself—a perversion of celestial will, a lucid desire for absolute control. And it is linked to Lucifer's own essence. Kaelan witnessed her embrace this power, this... cold, terrifying precision in destruction. He saw her unmoved, while his comrades died believing she was a suffering hero sacrificing herself for them."

Your voice cracked with the raw pain of the betrayal. "He saw the ultimate lie. And his mind could not endure it. I tried to access his memories with `Comprehend Languages`. I glimpsed it. The Nullification. The sheer power. But the backlash was immense. His mind is a maelstrom of trauma and paradox. It’s walled off. We cannot simply force our way in without risking further harm to him, or shattering my own fragile composure."

You met their gazes, your voice now pleading, laced with a desperate urgency. "We need to access his memories. To understand. To find the blueprint of her power. But we cannot break him further. And I cannot risk breaking myself. How do we do this? How do we access a mind shattered by ultimate truth?"

The silence in the cell was heavy, filled with the echoes of your horrifying revelations and Kaelan's low, persistent moans. Your companions, now armed with the impossible truth of Alexiel's deception, looked from the tormented Sergeant to you, the fragile princess who had just revealed herself to be the unwitting heir to a terrifying, hidden power.
