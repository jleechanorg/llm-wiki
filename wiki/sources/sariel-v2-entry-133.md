Game Master:

Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 12:18 PM.
Location: Small Study, Great Archives, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 3 (XP: 6980/3000), Gold: 10gp, HP: 14/14

Rowan’s gentle words, Gideon’s solid anchor, and Cressida’s fierce kindness had pierced through the raw personal pain, offering a fragile lifeline. Their unwavering faith, even in the face of such horrifying revelations, was a truth you could hold onto. You were not alone. You were not a monster, unless you chose to be. The raw, personal implications of your mother's deception and your own terrifying inheritance settled into a cold, hard resolve.

You looked at Magister Kantos, his ancient face still etched with the weight of the secrets he had revealed. The Empyrean Affliction. The madness that plagued your family. The "chilling capacity for unmaking and control" that your mother had mastered. The connection was undeniable, terrifying.

"Magister," you stated, your voice steady now, cutting through the heavy silence of the study. Your Starlit Eyes, though still burning with the afterimage of Kaelan's trauma, focused on him with a scholar's relentless precision. "This 'chilling capacity for unmaking and control'—this refined Affliction—you said it was linked to Lucifer's essence. Does this new knowledge, this terrifying understanding of its true nature, change anything about the possibility of a cure for the Empyrean Affliction? Does the lore offer any new pathways? Any hope for those like my uncle Titus, or… or for any of us who carry this blood?" Your gaze swept across your own hands, a silent, desperate question.

Kantos wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting between your determined face and the towering shelves of forbidden lore, as if seeking an answer there. He sighed, a dry, papery sound, his fear momentarily overshadowed by the sheer magnitude of the question. "Princess," he rasped, his voice a low murmur, laden with centuries of grim knowledge, "the lore on the Affliction is vast, but bleak. The 'chilling capacity' is its purest, most insidious form, a constant battle of the will. It explains why so many attempts at a 'cure' have failed: they treat the symptoms, the rage, the paranoia, but not the inherent drive for absolute control."

He adjusted his spectacles, his ancient eyes fixed on you with profound sadness. "True healing would require not merely suppression, but a fundamental re-ordering of the celestial essence itself. A spiritual alchemy. A path the Seraphim never found, and one that Lucifer, in his own pursuit of ultimate control, never truly sought. The knowledge you possess is the first true understanding of the Affliction's root, but the cure, Princess... remains a distant, terrifying dream."

You absorbed his grim assessment, a cold knot tightening in your stomach. The cure, a terrifying dream. This knowledge—the Null Genesis journals, the Codex of the Unseen Thread, the truth of your mother's power, and its link to the Affliction—was far too volatile to remain exposed, even in this hidden study. It was a weapon that could shatter the Imperium, a truth Raziel had buried for centuries, a secret that now rested solely with you and your small, shaken team.

"Magister," you commanded, your voice sharp, infused with a new, chilling precision. "This knowledge. These texts. The raw data we have accessed from Kaelan's mind. It is too dangerous to remain here, or in any single location without absolute, layered security. How do we secure it? How do we ensure its preservation, but also its absolute control? We cannot risk its dissemination without our full understanding of its implications. Every copy, every whisper, must be accounted for."

Kantos's ancient eyes widened further, recognizing the chilling imperative in your voice. He wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting to the heavy, warded door of the study, then to the shelves of ordinary, innocuous-looking tomes. "Princess," he rasped, his voice a dry whisper, "such knowledge... its true security lies not in physical containment, but in absolute, layered deception. A web of lies. As your mother herself understood." He paused, a flicker of profound weariness crossing his face. "We must disperse it. Fragment it. Hide it in plain sight, or in places no one would ever think to look. And destroy any copies that are not under our direct, meticulous control."
