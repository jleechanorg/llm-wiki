Astarion's mind, a whirlwind of calculated fear and predatory instinct, whirred. The first Mind Flayer was a twitching ruin, a gory testament to their sudden, brutal efficiency. The second one, thanks to his own precise strike, was wounded, staggering, its psionic hum now a ragged, broken gasp. But the *third*. The third was stirring, its alien head turning with deliberate, dreadful slowness, its multifaceted eyes beginning to pierce the chaos. It was fresh, untouched, and its aura was already a cold whisper of retribution. The window of opportunity was not merely closing; it was slamming shut with alarming speed.

He had two furious, armed companions – a Githyanki warrior, a living blade, and a hulking brute whose rage was as formidable as his brawn. How best to use them? How best to escape this hell before the tadpole claimed him, before the remaining illithids decided to extract his brain with their disgusting tentacles? His high neuroticism gnawed at him, screaming for an escape, for control. His low agreeableness dismissed any thought of altruism beyond what served his immediate need for survival. This was a hunt for freedom, and he was both hunter and hunted.

It won't solve the problem, only delay it, and delay often means death in these situations.*

*The decision is clear. Neutralize the most immediate, uninjured threat. Keep the focus away from myself, and direct the eager, blunt instruments I’ve acquired. The third one. That’s the target.*
