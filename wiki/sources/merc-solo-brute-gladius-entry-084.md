The whimpers of the bound Stonehand couple were a pathetic symphony to Gladius’s ears, a testament to his chilling success. He had offered them an easy out, a chance to provide information, but their stubbornness, their simple defiance, remained. A cold, precise hum resonated deep within him; this was not about efficiency now, but about the exquisite, raw pleasure of total dominance. Their refusal, their clinging to petty secrets, only heightened his desire to utterly break them.

He straightened from the woman, his gaze sweeping over the two bound forms. Then, with deliberate, unhurried movements, he walked to where the unconscious child lay, a small, inert bundle on the straw mat. The parents’ eyes, wide with a despair that mirrored the child’s terror, followed his every movement. Gladius knelt, his large hands carefully picking up the child. The small body felt fragile, vulnerable. He held it aloft, not cruelly, but with an almost clinical detachment, positioning it so that the bound parents, their faces pressed against the cold stone, could see it clearly in the dim, hearth-lit room.

The man let out a strangled gasp, a sound of pure agony. His wife’s body convulsed, a silent, desperate sob racking her frame. Their eyes, glistening with tears, were fixed on their child, then on Gladius’s impassive face. He let the silent horror build, savoring the raw, immediate fear that pulsed from them. This was the true indulgence: the manipulation of their deepest affections, twisting their love into an instrument of their own torment.

"Last chance," Gladius rumbled, his voice a low, gravelly current, cutting through the thick, desperate air. He shifted his grip on the child, just slightly, a subtle, almost imperceptible adjustment that spoke volumes of the impending doom. "What else does Lord Silvanus want? What hidden paths? What secrets? Or your child... will learn what happens when you defy true power." He looked into their eyes, letting the chilling promise of the cord in his other hand fill the space. The dawn, painting vibrant oranges and purples on the eastern sky, felt like a cruel, mocking spotlight on their profound, intimate humiliation. Their struggle, their despair, was a delicious offering.
