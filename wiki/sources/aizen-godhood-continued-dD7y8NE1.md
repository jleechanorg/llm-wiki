Starting Point: A City on the Brink 

The campaign opens during a period of escalating tension in Baldur's Gate. The summer heat is oppressive, but it's not just the weather; there's a palpable sense of unease, a gnawing dread that something terrible is brewing. Whispers of strange illnesses, unexplained disappearances, and escalating cult activity fill the taverns. 

The Hook (choose one or combine elements for the PCs): 

The Player Characters (PCs) are individuals with their own reasons for being in Baldur's Gate. They could be adventurers seeking coin, disillusioned former soldiers, Guild members looking for a score, or even Flaming Fist officers trying to make a difference. They should have some connection to the city. 

1. The Dockside Massacre: 

○ One or more PCs are near the Gray Harbor, perhaps looking for work, delivering goods, or simply passing through. Suddenly, the chaotic peace is shattered. A small group of Flaming Fist guards are ambushed by a surprisingly 

well-organized group of River Reavers, but this isn't just a gang fight. The Reavers are backed by several unnaturally strong, gaunt figures bearing the chilling symbol of Bane. The fight is brutal, ending with a massacre. The cultists

are searching for a specific crate amongst the cargo – something pulsating faintly with dark magic. The PCs are eyewitnesses, or caught in the crossfire. 

2. The Blighted Outbreak: 

○ A PC is in a poorer district of the Lower City, perhaps the Outer City slums, dealing with an NPC contact or exploring. Suddenly, screams erupt. A wave of monstrous, mutated creatures – perhaps giant, blight-ridden rats or grotesque, walking fungal growths – bursts from a sewage grate or a forgotten alley. They seem to be driven by some unseen force, attacking indiscriminately. A few desperate members of the Ash Dogs gang are trying to fight them off, but they are clearly overwhelmed. The creatures have strange, almost crystalline growths on their bodies, reminiscent of corrupted flora from the Blackwoods. 

3. The Noble's Desperate Plea: 

○ A PC with connections to the Upper City or a reputation for problem-solving is approached by a frantic, minor noble of the House Vanthampur (perhaps a lesser-known cousin, not tied to the main plot of Descent into Avernus). Their younger sibling has gone missing after associating with a new, seemingly benevolent "philanthropic society" that has sprung up recently. The noble is too terrified to go to the Flaming Fist directly, as they believe this "society" (secretly House Aizen) has spies everywhere. They offer a significant sum for discretion and recovery of their sibling.
