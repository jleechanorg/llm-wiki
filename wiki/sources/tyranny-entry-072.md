The Gangboss flinches as your voice cuts through the morning air, the cold administrative weight of your demand acting as a physical barrier to his erratic energy. He gestures toward a central cluster of rusted iron cages and tattered tents. There, amidst the filth and the stench of unwashed bodies, you see him: Lantry. The Sage is huddled on a pile of damp straw, his fingers tracing patterns in the dirt, his eyes wide and intelligent behind his cracked spectacles. He looks up as you approach, his gaze darting from your Fatebinder's seal to the towering, iron-bound silhouette of Barik.

Krimp stands over him, a jagged cleaver in one hand and a half-gnawed bone in the other. He looks up, a flicker of predatory irritation crossing his face as he sees the Disfavored escort. Before he can speak, you interpose yourself between him and the prisoner, your shadow falling across the scholar like a shroud.

'Krimp,' you state, your voice a flat, clinical vibration that demands compliance. 'The logistics of this valley are currently in a state of critical failure. I am here to correct the ledger. This man, Lantry, is a Sage of the School of Ink and Quill. He is protected by the Edict's administrative protocols, and I am formally requisitioning him for immediate transfer to my custody. Your 'scavenging' rights do not extend to artifacts of the Court. Release him. Now.'

Krimp's eyes narrow, his grip on the cleaver tightening. [DICE: Intimidation (Expertise) 1d20 + 9 = 16 + 9 = 25 vs DC 15 (Success)]. He looks at the iron wall of Barik, then at the cold, geometric certainty in your eyes. He recognizes that you aren't here to haggle; you are here to inform him of a reality he cannot change. He spits on the ground, a wet, ragged sound, and shoves Lantry toward you with the flat of his blade.

'Take the old bird, Fatebinder,' Krimp rasps, his voice a low snarl. 'He's been nothing but a waste of rations anyway, mumbling about ink and old walls. But tell Tunon... tell him the Chorus doesn't like being audited in our own sprawl.'
