Astarion's lip curled, a thin line of grim determination. The crash site was a charnel house, best left to scavengers. That distant cry, however... a potential asset, or a fresh problem to be neatly disposed of. His high neuroticism demanded he know, demanded control over this new variable. "Beyond that plume of smoke," he drawled, his voice a low, sardonic rasp, "lies either salvation or a more interesting demise. Let's not dawdle." He dismissed Gale's grumbling with a scoff, focusing instead on the urgent, desperate cries ahead.

He pushed forward, leading the way, his steps light and precise on the increasingly scorched earth. His new **Gloom Stalker** abilities pulsed faintly, a cold comfort in the dim, ash-choked light filtering through the damaged canopy. He moved with the fluid grace of a true predator, his longbow held ready, its smooth surface a chilling anchor in this alien landscape. His crimson eyes, adapted to the gloom, dissected every shadow, every twisted root, for signs of threat or opportunity. The tadpole behind his eye squirmed, a constant, chilling reminder of his own precarious existence, urging him towards answers.

The acrid stench of burnt metal and ozone grew thicker, mixing with the damp earth and pine. Ancient trees, splintered and charred by the Nautiloid's impact, lay scattered like colossal bones, a testament to unimaginable violence. Lae'zel, a silent, grim shadow, followed without a word. Her golden eyes, fierce and unyielding, swept the ruined landscape with an unnerving intensity, her hand instinctively going to the empty space where her silver sword *should* be. Gale, meanwhile, continued to stumble and grumble, a discordant note in the silent approach, irritating Astarion to no end.

The cry, initially muffled, grew clearer, sharper with every step. It was unmistakably human, filled with a raw, desperate fear. Ahead, through the thinning smoke, a clearing began to emerge, and within it, a scene of immediate, brutal conflict.

As Astarion edged around the final charred tree, he peered into the clearing. A human figure, a young man, lay sprawled on the ground, desperately trying to scramble away. Towering over him was a monstrous, six-limbed creature of shadows and malice – a **Shadow Mastiff**. Its eyes glowed with malevolent red light, and its guttural snarl promised immediate death. The beast was distracted, focused entirely on its struggling prey, offering Astarion the perfect opening for an unseen strike.
