### **Clarification: Action Roll vs. Unforeseen Complication Trigger**

There was **no reroll** for the `Unforeseen Complication`. The system's dice rolls and the `Core Directive #3: Unforeseen Complications & Setbacks` were applied as intended, but the narrative flow might have obscured the precise mechanics.

Here's the breakdown of what happened:

1.  **Unforeseen Complication Roll (`d100`):**
    *   At the start of the "Akatsuki Internal Intrigue" action, an `UNFORESEEN COMPLICATION ROLL` was made: `d100 = 31`.
    *   The chance for a complication was `45%`.
    *   **Result:** `31 <= 45 — Unforeseen Complication Triggered!` This outcome means a complication *definitively occurred* and would manifest in the narrative. This roll is *never* rerolled.

2.  **Itachi's Action Roll (`d20`):**
    *   Immediately *after* the system confirmed a complication would occur, Itachi's `Action: Itachi manages Akatsuki internal intrigue` was resolved with a `d20` roll.
    *   **Itachi's Roll:** `20 (Natural 20 - Critical Success!)` on a `DC 28` task.
    *   **Result:** `Automatic Success (due to Natural 20)!` This outcome means Itachi **flawlessly succeeded at his primary goal** of managing Akatsuki internal intrigue (sowing discord, identifying Nagato's trigger, etc.).

**The Key Distinction:**

*   The `d100` roll for an `Unforeseen Complication` determines *if* an unexpected narrative twist happens, independent of the primary action's success.
*   The `d20` roll for the `Action` determines *how well* the player character's declared primary objective is achieved.

**How they interact in this instance:**

Itachi's **primary action (managing Akatsuki internal intrigue) was a Critical Success.** He excelled at sowing discord and gaining key intel on Nagato.

However, the **Unforeseen Complication** (triggered by the separate d100 roll) manifested as an *unintended side effect of that success*. The increased internal friction he *successfully* caused led Kisame to take an *unsanctioned, premature action* (attacking Killer Bee).

This aligns perfectly with `Core Directive #3.C` which states: "If an Unforeseen Complication is triggered... it does **not** automatically mean outright failure of the player's primary, immediate action... Instead, complications should introduce new challenges, unexpected consequences, or partial setbacks."

So, Itachi succeeded at his goal, but Kisame's action was an **unforeseen consequence/setback** arising from the delicate nature of his manipulation, pushing his timeline forward in an uncontrolled way.

Thank you for prompting this clarification. It ensures we maintain absolute consistency and transparency in the application of the rules.
