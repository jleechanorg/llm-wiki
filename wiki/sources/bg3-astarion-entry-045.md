Astarion's sigh was barely audible, a soft exhalation of frustration at the lack of immediate, intelligent solutions. The burly man and the Githyanki waited, their crude weapons still clutched in their hands, their eyes fixed on the massive, unyielding doors. *Fine*, he thought, *if we must resort to brutish methods, at least let me ensure we haven't overlooked any hidden advantages.* He turned back to the grotesque, pulpy forms of the defeated Mind Flayers, a flicker of cold, detached curiosity warring with his ingrained revulsion. He had extracted one parasite, a chilling discovery, but what else might these alien creatures carry? Their very biology was their strength, their wealth, their tools. He ignored the faint squelch beneath his boots as he stepped closer to the closest corpse, its tentacles now slack and lifeless.

His nimble fingers, still sticky with residual ichor, meticulously probed the folds of chitin, the slick, membranous skin, and the various orifices of the fallen monsters. He wasn't looking for coin purses or mundane trinkets; he was searching for anything that hinted at their function, their purpose, or any other hidden biological components. His mind, an urban hunter’s, instinctively sought patterns in the chaos, even in this utterly alien physiology. The stench of ozone and death was thick, cloying, and the distant, constant thrum of the Nautiloid's engine seemed to mock his efforts, a relentless reminder of their dwindling time. He could feel the parasite behind his eye, still, a faint, unsettling warmth that intensified his focus, sharpening his senses to the gruesome details before him. The Githyanki watched, a silent, almost disapproving presence, while the burly man merely grunted, impatient for action.
