The burly man’s gaze, though initially clouded by pain, sharpened with surprising speed, sweeping over Astarion and the Githyanki. He was clearly assessing threats and opportunities, not unlike Astarion himself, though with less theatrical flair. Astarion didn't wait for him to speak. Time was a luxury they couldn’t afford on this infernal craft. "Welcome back to the land of the sentient, brute," Astarion hissed, his voice low and urgent, devoid of its usual sardonic edge. He held up the jagged bone blade. "Weapons. Then an exit. Those squid-faced horrors are otherwise occupied, for now, but that won't last. Can you fight?"

The man grunted, a rough sound that seemed to vibrate with suppressed power. He rubbed his wrists, his muscles bunched. "Fight? Aye. Give me something to fight *with*." His voice was deep, gravelly, and held an undercurrent of raw, frustrated power. His eyes, fixed on the Mind Flayers, burned with an unyielding fury that mirrored the Githyanki’s. He didn't waste a glance on the whimpering, still-bound captives. He was a force of nature, untamed and immediate.

"Excellent," Astarion murmured, a sliver of genuine relief mingling with his pragmatic assessment. He glanced at the Githyanki, who offered another terse nod, her attention already drifting back to the Mind Flayers. She was clearly impatient, ready to tear into their captors even unarmed. "There's not much around," Astarion continued, gesturing vaguely around the fleshy chamber with the bone blade. "But perhaps we can improvise. Any thoughts on how to make these... *delightful* chains less effective for the others, or how to get out of this chamber without alerting our hosts?" The rhythmic pulse of the engine seemed to quicken, a subtle shift that tightened Astarion's nerves. He needed to act, and this burly man might just be the leverage they needed.
