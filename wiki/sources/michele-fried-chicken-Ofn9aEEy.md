### Campaign Logic: The Silver Lake Choice

Silver Lake was selected as your starting location because it represents the **'Hard Mode' of the Los Angeles culinary scene**, providing immediate narrative tension and mechanical depth for a restaurant-sim campaign.

Silver Lake is a real-world epicenter for the 'Old LA vs. New LA' struggle. By placing you here, the campaign gains an immediate antagonist in **The Silver Lake Shift**. This forces you to engage with social mechanics (Persuasion, Reputation) rather than just cooking. Your BOGO strategy and 'undocumented' status hit a specific nerve in this neighborhood that wouldn't exist in a more industrial or purely commercial zone.

**2. High Reward vs. High Risk (Economic Balance):**
- **Reward**: The demographic in Silver Lake (Sunset Blvd) is willing to pay premium prices for 'heirloom radishes' and 'organic poultry.' Success here means much higher gold margins than in a lower-income area.
- **Risk**: The regulatory environment is brutal. 'Red-Pen' Miller and the Type 41 Liquor License hurdles are designed to test your **Intelligence** and **Charisma** stats early on.

**3. The 'Julian' Rivalry (Competitive Parity):**
Silver Lake is 'trendy,' which justifies why a Celebrity Chef like Julian would choose it for a pop-up. This creates a direct 'David vs. Goliath' scenario where you are fighting for the same limited pool of influencers (Sunny) and press (Eater LA).

**Is it troublesome?** Yes, by design. It is a high-friction environment where every business decision has a political consequence. It was chosen to ensure that 'Crispy Dreams' isn't just a shop, but a story about survival in a changing city.
