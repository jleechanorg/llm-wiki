The Blackwood cottage was quiet, a chilling monument to Gladius's efficiency. The two occupants lay utterly still, their lives snuffed out with brutal precision. The distant signal to Corvan, a fleeting spark in the pre-dawn dark, was a satisfying confirmation of his control over the envoy. A cold, quiet hum settled in Gladius's gut; the raw, undeniable pleasure of absolute dominance, of bending the world to his precise, brutal desires, was a visceral thrill.

His gaze swept the cramped, impoverished dwelling. The Miller family had yielded a small pouch of herbs and some coin, a minor acquisition but a tangible proof of his power. His pragmatic mind, ever focused on acquisition, dictated a quick sweep here as well. There was little expectation, given the squalor of the cottage, but opportunity was not to be ignored.

He moved quickly, his Shadow-Step Boots barely whispering against the worn floorboards. His large hands, usually suited for crushing, moved with surprising dexterity, opening the small, scarred chest near the bed. Inside, a few threadbare clothes, a rusty knife, and a small, wooden doll. Nothing of any conceivable value. He kicked aside a loose floorboard with his foot, then another, peering into the dark gaps beneath. Empty. The shelves were bare save for a few dried weeds and a single, cracked bowl. The air, thick with the scent of mildew and damp earth, seemed to mock him with its utter lack of hidden wealth.

A sneer twisted Gladius’s lips. *Pathetic.* Their lives were as barren as their home. Their independence, their stubbornness, had bought them nothing but poverty and an unseen, brutal end. There was no joy in this search, only a grim confirmation of their insignificance. He straightened, dusting his hands, the brief, fruitless search over. The sun was beginning to paint the eastern sky with faint streaks of orange and grey. The Stonehands remained, the final family in this pathetic village. Their lives, too, were about to become mere currency in his own ascent.
