Astarion's jaw tightened, a thin line of vexation. Nothing. The Mind Flayers were as useless in death as they were terrifying in life, yielding no further secrets or convenient escape routes. His previous attempts at subtle investigation and intellectual solutions had met only with alien inscrutability. *Fine.* If subtlety failed, and brute force was the only language these brutes understood, then he would at least ensure he was prepared to *survive* the inevitable next wave of horrors.

He cast a quick, dismissive glance at the Githyanki and the burly man, who now stood expectantly by the massive, fleshy doors, their impatience a palpable, grating presence. They were ready to smash, to tear, to confront. Good. Let them. His own survival depended on a more refined approach.

He moved with an almost imperceptible shift, gliding away from the putrid corpses and towards a cluster of pulsating conduits near the chamber's edge. This was no grand strategic maneuver, but a hunter's instinct for positioning. His fingers, still faintly sticky with ichor, found the familiar grip of his longbow, drawing it with a whisper of taut string. The air thrummed with the engine's relentless pulse, a constant reminder of their predicament, and the tadpole behind his eye squirmed, an insidious, chilling presence.

He scanned the cavernous room, his eyes automatically tracing lines of sight, potential cover, and the most efficient angles for ranged attacks. His Archery Fighting Style was a silent promise of precision, a stark contrast to the chaotic melee his companions favored. He mentally reviewed the few spells at his command—Hunter's Mark, Ensnaring Strike—each a calculated tool for control, for isolating and dismantling a threat from a distance. The thought of more Mind Flayers, or whatever grotesque servants this ship housed, filled him with a cold dread, but also a sharpened resolve. He wouldn't be caught unawares again. He wouldn't be a thrall. Not again.
