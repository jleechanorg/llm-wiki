Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 12:05 PM.
Location: Sergeant Kaelan's Cell, Quiet Ward, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 3 (XP: 6980/3000), Gold: 10gp, HP: 14/14

You pushed yourself upright, your body still weary, but your posture infused with a cold, unwavering purpose. Kaelan's long-term care and security were paramount. He was no longer a threat, but a living archive, a silent testament to a truth too dangerous to expose.

"Sergeant Kaelan will remain here," you commanded, your voice steady, sweeping your gaze from Rowan to Kantos, then to Gideon, and finally to the heavy iron door where the Silent Guard stood sentinel. "His peace is fragile. His secret is absolute. His confinement, though no longer a torment, must be maintained."

You turned to Kantos. "Magister, you will instruct the Silent Guard on his changed condition. He is no longer a threat of madness, but a sacred trust. His containment must be secure, but his care must be humane. No physical restraints beyond what is necessary to prevent self-harm, if any." You paused, then added, "His status will be 'unaffected by the Affliction, in a state of profound meditation.' A lie, but one that aligns with their understanding of discipline."

You looked at Rowan. "Rowan, you will visit him daily. Continue your soothing touch, your poultices. Provide him with fresh water, nourishment, and whatever small comforts are allowed within this sterile environment. His physical well-being is your sole priority here."

Finally, you turned to Gideon. "Ser Gideon, you will be his guardian. The Silent Guard will ensure his isolation, but you will oversee his protection. Ensure no one, not even my brothers, gains unauthorized access. His peace, and the truth he carries, must remain undisturbed."

Gideon gave a curt, respectful nod, his hand instinctively going to the hilt of his sword. Rowan's face, though still sad, held a new, quiet determination. Kantos, while trembling, seemed to find a grim satisfaction in the meticulous planning of this profound deception. The Silent Guard, hearing your words through the door, remained motionless, processing the new directives, their internal logic resolving into acceptance. Kaelan's sanctuary was now secure, guarded by a paradox.

You tore your gaze from Kaelan, a profound weariness settling over you, but also a fierce, desperate resolve. The air in the cell felt thick with the weight of shattered truths. Your companions, wide-eyed and shaken, stared at you, at the blood on your face, at the way you commanded the Silent Guard with mere words. They had witnessed your strange power, your audacious command, but they did not yet understand the horrifying abyss you had just stared into. Now, it was time to bring them into the light.

Your voice, though raw and trembling from the psychic strain, was firm, cutting through the low moans that still occasionally escaped Kaelan's lips. You swept your gaze across their faces—Gideon's grim resolve, Cressida's sharp, intelligent concern, Rowan's gentle, empathetic worry, and Kantos's profound terror. "We have a problem," you stated, your gaze fixed on Kaelan’s tormented form, then sweeping across their faces. "A problem far more complex than any of us anticipated. And a truth that will shatter everything you thought you knew about my mother."

You paused, drawing a ragged breath, the words tasting like ash. "Sergeant Kaelan," you explained, gesturing to the tormented figure, "is not merely mad. His mind was shattered by witnessing a truth too profound for mortal comprehension. My mother... Magister Arcanus... her public identity was a lie. She was not a Null. She possessed a terrifying, singular power: the Nullification Field. An ability that silenced all magic within its radius. And, impossibly, she could wield her *own* magic within that void."

You saw Gideon’s eyes widen behind his helm, a flicker of profound disbelief quickly replaced by a deeper, almost fearful respect. Cressida’s sharp gaze snapped to Kantos, demanding silent confirmation, which the old Magister provided with a trembling nod. Rowan, still tending to Kaelan, visibly flinched, his gentle face etched with a horror that mirrored your own.

"The Null Genesis journals," you continued, your voice dropping, "confirm this. They theorize this power, this chilling capacity for unmaking and control, is a refined manifestation of the Empyrean Affliction itself—a perversion of celestial will, a lucid desire for absolute control. And it is linked to Lucifer's own essence. Kaelan witnessed her embrace this power, this... cold, terrifying precision in destruction. He saw her unmoved, while his comrades died believing she was a suffering hero sacrificing herself for them."

Your voice cracked with the raw pain of the betrayal. "He saw the ultimate lie. And his mind could not endure it." You met their gazes, your voice now pleading, laced with a desperate urgency. "I used my Mirror Magic to glimpse his memories, to see what he saw." You shivered, the psychic backlash still coursing through you. "I saw her. Six-winged. Terrifyingly serene. Unleashing a power that unmade magic itself. And then... I saw her visit Kaelan. After the battle. She mourned him. She mourned all of them. But she still *chose* to bear the perceived betrayal, to maintain the lie for the sake of the Imperium. Her grief was real. Her mask was real. And the cost was absolute."

The silence in the cell was heavy, filled with the echoes of your horrifying revelations and Kaelan's low, persistent moans. Your companions, now armed with the impossible truth of Alexiel's deception, looked from the tormented Sergeant to you, the fragile princess who had just revealed herself to be the unwitting heir to a terrifying, hidden power. The gravity of your words settled upon them, a crushing weight of cosmic secrets and devastating personal betrayal. They understood.
