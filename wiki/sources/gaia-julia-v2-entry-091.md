He recognizes the trap: by 'protecting' Glaber's assets, he becomes Glaber's legal and social master. The Praetor would not be 'saved'; he would be owned. But the terror of the purge and the desperate need for a master overwhelm his suspicion. He is no longer a Praetor; he is a man who has just been offered his life back at the cost of his soul.

"Lord Calavius... Gaia..." Glaber rasps, his voice breaking as he lowers his head, his crimson cloak pooling on the floor like a stain. "If such a thing were possible... I would be... eternally helpful."
