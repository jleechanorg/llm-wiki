Game Master:
The failure of the smelter was not a mechanical error; it was a failure of the human hardware's transparency. You descend upon the forge masters not with a wrench, but with the cold, geometric intent of a master debugger. They are brought before you in the heat-shimmer of the temple's lower courtyards, the scent of cooling slag still heavy in the air. You do not shout. You do not even look at the broken machinery. You look only at the masters, your eyes scanning the minute tremors in their hands as if reading a faulty script.

'A delay in reporting is a delay in the Spire's resonance,' you state, your voice a low-frequency vibration that seems to vibrate the very iron they work. 'You hid the structural flaw because you feared my reaction. That fear was a localized inefficiency. I am here to make the cost of concealment far higher than the cost of failure.'

[DICE: Sadistic Audit (Intimidation) 1d20 + 14 = 16 + 14 = 30 vs DC 18 (Success)]. You perform a clinical 'audit' of their lead foreman. You do not take his life—that would be a waste of a trained asset—but you systematically remove the fingernails of his primary hand while he describes the exact sequence of the smelter's failure. You perform the task with statuesque boredom, your 'Controlled Sadism' ensuring his pain remains at peak levels without causing shock. The others are forced to watch, their faces turning the color of ash. 

'In the future,' you murmur, wiping a bead of blood from your gauntlet with a silk handkerchief, 'you will report a hairline fracture as if it were a mortal wound. If a single grain of iron is lost because you chose silence over transparency, I will refactor your entire families into the labor-tier. The ledger is now clear. Return to the forge.'

The masters flee back to the iron-works, their initiative no longer paralyzed by fear of failure, but by a frantic, absolute terror of delay. The workforce is synchronized. The hardware will be repaired by nightfall.
