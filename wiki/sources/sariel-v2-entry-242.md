Sariel Arcanus sat hunched over a collection of ancient, forbidden scrolls in the hidden, warded study deep within her private wing of House Arcanus. The air hummed faintly with the suppressed magic of her Nullification Field, a silent shield against prying eyes and ears. Magister Lucian Kantos, looking perpetually on the verge of a heart attack, nervously organized a stack of obscure genealogies on a nearby table, his hands trembling slightly. Ser Gideon Vance stood like a statue at the chamber's only entrance, his gaze fixed on the heavy, oak door. Gareth Ashfeld, her personal scribe and healer, quietly brewed a calming tea in a corner, his brow furrowed with concern. Lady Cressida Valeriana, ever observant, leaned against a bookshelf, her eyes sharp, missing nothing.

The low hum of the magical ward abruptly faltered. Gideon stiffened, his hand instinctively going to the hilt of his sword, "Vow-Keeper." Lucian Kantos let out a small, terrified gasp, his trembling hands scattering a stack of scrolls. Gareth Ashfeld flinched, nearly dropping a teacup. Cressida's eyes narrowed, her mind already calculating angles of defense and counter-attack, a faint, predatory smile touching her lips.

The heavy door to the study groaned open, and her brothers, Cassian val Artorius and Darius val Artorius, stood silhouetted against the dim light of the corridor, their faces a thundercloud of raw fury and cold accusation. Cassian’s hand rested on the pommel of his greatsword, "Mother's Wrath," his eyes burning with his inherited rage. Darius, ever stoic, held a pristine data slate, his Starlit Eyes cold and assessing, a single, unadorned shield in his other hand.

Cassian's voice, usually a booming roar, was a low growl of barely contained rage, echoing the fury of his late father. "What is this, Sariel? Gideon, stand down! Darius received a report... a report that Magister Kantos has been seen smuggling forbidden texts into your wing. And that you... you've been seen visiting the Quiet Ward. Explain yourself."

Darius stepped forward, his voice devoid of emotion, cutting precisely. "And we found the source of the report, Sariel. A physician from the Quiet Ward, who spoke of a 'Starfall Patient' and 'unnatural interference.' This violates protocol, sister. It violates Imperial law. What exactly are you doing in here?"

Sariel Arcanus did not move. She felt the sudden, electric tension in the air, the weight of their combined accusations. She looked at each of them in turn: Cassian's furious challenge, Darius's cold logic, her loyal companions' desperate worry, and Lucian's palpable terror. This was the moment. They had stumbled into her web, but they saw only a trap set by a weak, grieving scholar. This perception of her "weakness" is their fatal flaw, and her sharpest blade.
