Regarding Gaia's personality and moral history:

**Was Gaia 'good' before?**
No. According to her character profile and the metaphysics of this campaign, Gaia Julia Caesar has never possessed a 'good' alignment in the traditional sense. Her starting alignment for this origin story is **Lawful Evil**, and this is rooted in several foundational factors:

**1. The Nihilistic Response to the Miasma:**
Since her childhood, the Fated Miasma has shown Gaia millions of timelines where Rome burns, where her family is slaughtered, and where all human effort is rendered meaningless. This has stripped her of the ability to believe in abstract 'goodness,' 'honor,' or an 'afterlife.' Instead of despair, she chose consumption. She is a 'Weaponized Epicurean' who believes that if the universe is chaotic, the only rational response is to devour the present moment.

**2. The 'Unbroken Colt' Nuance (Age 16):**
At the start of this prequel, her personality was raw and volatile. She wasn't a 'Gourmand' of power yet; she was a scared teenager self-medicating with adrenaline and control to drown out the psychic noise of the Miasma. She engaged in 'Testing Phases'—seducing people, stealing, and ordering cruel punishments—not because she was inherently malicious toward individuals, but because she was terrified of being 'Just a Girl' and needed to prove her own strength to herself.

**3. The Heroic Mask vs. Secret Sadism:**
Gaia's heroism is a **Tactical Mask**. She plays the part of the 'Sponsa Bellona' (Bride of War) and the 'Generous Mother' because it is the most efficient way to build a fanatical cult of personality. Internally, as documented in the DM Notes, she often enjoys the physical and psychological submission of others (like her internal reaction to the trackers' subservience) and views most people as 'useful assets' or 'cattle.'

**4. The Moral Complexity (Genuine Attachments):**
While she is evil and sadistic, she is not a caricature. She possesses a **nuanced moral code**: 
- **Genuine Attachments:** She tracks specific people (like Antony or Voros) as personal attachments rather than assets. Her feelings for them are real, even if they are often possessive.
- **Personal Honor:** She adheres to a Julian sense of honor—she will not break a formal covenant lightly, as 'The Julian Peace' requires a foundation of absolute, predictable law (even if that law is her own will).

**Summary:** Gaia began this campaign as a volatile, nihilistic teenager with a penchant for dominance. She has always been the 'Wolf at the Feast,' even before she learned how to hide her teeth behind a smile of gold.
