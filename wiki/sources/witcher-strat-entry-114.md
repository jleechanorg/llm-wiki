Game Master:
Nocturne allows Elara's words to hang in the air for a moment, her sharp eyes sweeping over the scattered ledgers on the desk. She processes the information with the cold, effortless precision of a master strategist. The Baron's consortium. Escalating premiums. Silk from the North. It is a predictable system of wartime exploitation, ripe for dismantling.

"The challenges are indeed intricate, Mistress Elara," Nocturne murmurs, her voice soft, yet resonating with an undeniable undercurrent of intellectual authority. She offers a subtle, knowing smile, perfectly calibrated to appeal to Elara's pragmatism. "A 'premium' from a consortium often implies not a scarcity of the goods themselves, but a bottleneck in their *passage*. The Baron, I suspect, is less a source and more a\... *toll-keeper*, exploiting the disrupted lines from the North."

She leans forward just slightly, her gaze sharp and intelligent, meeting Elara's. "To circumvent such predation," Nocturne continues, her words flowing with the serene logic of effortless superiority, "one must understand the true arteries of trade. There are always lesser-known routes, perhaps even more ancient ones, that avoid the major, controlled thoroughfares. Or, alternatively, one could identify the primary producers of the silk itself. To establish a discreet, direct line, entirely bypassing the Baron's inflated demands. It would require\... *nuanced investigation*, certainly. Perhaps a temporary disruption to the Baron's own supply in a localized manner, to make his existing premium untenable. But the long-term saving, the establishment of a truly *independent* supply, would be substantial. A question of strategy, not mere payment."

Elara's eyes, sharp and assessing, widen almost imperceptibly. Her ISTJ pragmatism registers the sheer, chilling logic of Nocturne's words. She had been grappling with simple cost-benefit, but Nocturne had offered a complete, strategic overhaul. The proprietary caution in her features gives way to a profound, almost desperate, respect. "Bypass the Baron?" she whispers, a new, dawning hope in her voice. "Discreet lines... A temporary disruption? Lady Lyra, such a thought... it had not occurred to me. You truly do possess a keen mind for commerce."

Beatrice, ever perceptive, watches the exchange with growing fascination, her smile now openly warm as she perceives her mother's grudging awe. Old Man Hemwick, a broken puppet, beams with pathetic pride, delighted that his new 'relative' is so readily proving her 'indispensability.'
