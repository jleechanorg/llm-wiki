Astarion's focus was absolute, a chilling blend of fear and predatory instinct. The destroyed Sentry was a forgotten mess behind them, a monument to their brutal efficiency, but the ship itself remained a living, breathing labyrinth. He pressed on, each squelching step on the spongy floor a fresh assault on his senses, the thick, metallic tang in the air growing more acrid with every breath. His high neuroticism gnawed at him, twisting every shadow, every grotesque curve of the living walls into a potential ambush. He would not be caught unawares, not here, not in this horrifying, alien gut.

"Keep up," he hissed, his voice a low, urgent rasp, barely audible over the relentless thrum of the Nautiloid's engine. He moved with a fluid, silent grace, his longbow held ready, its smooth surface a comforting anchor in this biomechanical nightmare. The sickly yellow light from the pulsating walls seemed to grow brighter, casting lurid, shifting shadows that danced with the rhythm of his own thrumming fear. His crimson eyes, sharpened by a lifetime of discerning hidden traps and the tells of his prey, meticulously dissected every detail. The Githyanki followed like a disciplined shadow, her golden eyes sweeping the passage with an unnerving, predatory intensity, her movements almost as silent as his own.

But the burly man... Astarion inwardly seethed. His heavy boots squelched loudly, a jarring cacophony that echoed through the narrow passage, obliterating any hope of subtlety. *Clumsy brute. He'll draw every horror on this ship to us.* Astarion suppressed an irritated sigh, a flicker of his usual disdain showing on his face before he forced it away. He hated this place, its alien biology, its sickening purpose, but he would survive it. He would not be a thrall again.

The corridor curved sharply to the right, obscuring whatever lay beyond. The constant, infernal thrum of the ship vibrated through his bones, and the tadpole behind his eye squirmed with an insistent, chilling urgency, a relentless reminder of the transformation drawing closer. As they rounded the bend, the pulsating yellow light intensified, bathing a new section of the corridor in a harsh, almost blinding glow. And then, he saw it. Not another Mind Flayer, not a Sentry, but something far more unsettling. A series of large, translucent pods lined the walls, each one filled with a grotesque, humanoid figure, still alive, suspended in a viscous fluid. His eyes widened, a wave of profound, cold dread washing over him. These were not tadpoles. These were... *victims*.
