Astarion's eyes widened, a rare flicker of genuine horror replacing his usual sardonic mask. The corridor curved, bathing a new section in intense, sickly yellow light, revealing not another Mind Flayer or Sentry, but something far more insidious. A series of large, translucent pods lined the walls, each one filled with a grotesque, humanoid figure, still alive, suspended in a viscous, amber fluid. These weren't tadpoles. These were... *victims*. His stomach clenched, a wave of profound, cold dread washing over him, a chilling premonition of his own potential fate.

"Gods," he choked, the word a raw, almost involuntary gasp. His high neuroticism screamed, demanding understanding, demanding control over this terrifying variable. He moved forward, not with caution, but with a desperate, almost morbid curiosity, his movements fluid but his mind a whirlwind of revulsion and calculation. The very air here felt heavier, thick with the metallic tang and a new, sickeningly sweet scent he couldn't quite place.

The Githyanki halted, her golden eyes fixed on the pods, a grim, unreadable expression on her face. The burly man, meanwhile, grunted, his face contorted in a mixture of confusion and dawning horror. They were as repulsed as he was.

Astarion peered into the nearest pod. The humanoid within was a human, young, his face contorted in a silent scream, eyes wide and unseeing. His skin was unnaturally pale, almost translucent, and a faint, sickly green light pulsed within his chest, mirroring the light of the pods. No external wounds, no visible restraints beyond the fluid itself. He was being *processed*. The tadpole behind Astarion's own eye squirmed, a chilling, insistent burrowing. This was their fate, laid bare. He pressed his hand against the pod's cool, rubbery surface, searching for any mechanism, any weakness, anything that would explain this horror, or offer a reprieve.

Your keen intellect, sharpened by desperation and morbid curiosity, cuts through the alien facade. These aren't mere prisons; they're **transformation chambers**. The viscous fluid is a potent, alchemical concoction designed to accelerate the metamorphosis of the tadpole within the host, slowly breaking down their humanoid form and reforming it into something new, something... illithid. The green light pulsing within the victims' chests is not just internal light; it's the tadpole itself, glowing, growing, consuming. You also notice a series of finer, almost invisible organic filaments connected from the pods to a central, pulsing conduit running along the wall – a shared neural network, feeding power and, perhaps, absorbing the victims' very essence. The bodies within are technically alive, but their minds are already gone, consumed by the parasite.

You also discern a faint, almost imperceptible tremor in the fluid of the pods closest to the curving corridor ahead. It's not a natural tremor, but a slight, rhythmic shudder, as if something is *moving* within the walls, further down the passage. A sound, too, now more distinct than the ship's thrum: a faint, scuttling sound, like claws on chitin, followed by a low, guttural chittering. You are not alone in this corridor.
