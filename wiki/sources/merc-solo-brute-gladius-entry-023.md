The details about the drunken sheriff were sufficient, but Gladius’s hunger for information, for leverage against the 'clever' Lord Silvanus, remained unsated. The vein in his neck continued its slow, deliberate pulse, a silent command for Corvan to yield more. The greatsword’s faint scrape against the floor had shattered Corvan’s remaining composure, leaving him a quivering mess.

"The insult was a lie," Gladius rumbled, his voice low and accusatory, cutting through the inn's silence. He didn't ask a question; he stated a fact, watching Corvan’s eyes. "Silvanus doesn't care about a 'slight.' He wants something else. The herbs?" His gaze flicked to the mention of the Miller family’s potent herbs, recalling the dockworker’s complaints. "What's his real reason for this... 'cleansing'?" He leaned closer, a predatory glint in his eyes, enjoying the exquisite agony of the man before him. He wanted to see the truth, the true rot at the heart of the noble's greed, laid bare.

Corvan flinched, his head snapping up, then down again, as if physically unable to meet Gladius's piercing stare. His breath hitched. "The herbs... yes, they're part of it," he whispered, his voice barely audible, the scent of his fear thick in the air. "The Miller family's land... it's unusually fertile. Lord Silvanus wants to expand his holdings, not just a granary. He plans to cultivate the herbs on a massive scale. They're... quite valuable in the city, for the right buyers. Healing, and other, more... potent concoctions." He shuddered, revealing a glimpse of the darker undercurrents of the trade. "The peasant's refusal was merely an inconvenience. A stubborn refusal to yield what Lord Silvanus believes is his right by station. This entire village, eventually, could be his."

Gladius straightened slowly, a grim, satisfied smile playing on his lips. *Control.* That was the true currency, and Lord Silvanus, in his arrogance, had just revealed his own weakness. He thought he was playing a grand game, when in fact, he was merely a pawn in Gladius’s own, far more brutal, ambition.
