The Blackwood cottages, grim and silent against the pre-dawn gloom, presented themselves as the next tableau for Gladius's brutal artistry. The Miller family was gone, erased, their absence a silent testament to his meticulous hand. The subtle hum of control, the intoxicating feeling of bending the world to his will, pulsed beneath his skin. The Blackwoods, with their stubborn "independence," were merely the next obstacle to be subsumed.

He moved with an almost ethereal silence, his Shadow-Step Boots sighing softly against the damp earth, effortlessly muffling the heavy clang of his splint mail. He was a force of nature, moving with the predatory grace of a hunter whose every instinct was perfectly aligned with the kill. He singled out the largest of the three cottages, its windows dark and uninviting, a perfect picture of unsuspecting vulnerability. The scent of stale wood smoke and damp earth hung in the air, oddly comforting in its mundanity, a stark contrast to the violence he carried.

Approaching the rear, Gladius noted a single, loose shutter, warped by age and neglect. It was almost an invitation. He pressed against it, and with a barely audible *groan* of rotting wood, it yielded. He slipped through the narrow gap, his large frame surprisingly fluid, landing silently on the dust-laden floor within. The interior was colder, damper than the Miller's home, smelling faintly of mildew and unwashed linen. Moonlight, a mere sliver, filtered through a grimy pane, casting long, distorted shadows that danced with the subtle movements of his armor.

He paused, a sentinel in the profound silence. No creaking boards, no rustle of fabric, no stirring breaths. The Blackwoods slept, utterly unaware of the intrusion, their fierce independence a pathetic illusion against the reality of his presence. A cold, almost aesthetic appreciation for the purity of their helplessness washed over him. This wasn't about anger; it was about the exquisite sensation of unchallenged power, the raw, experiential thrill of taking what was denied. He was inside. The hunt had begun anew.
