Corvan, his silks disheveled and his face ashen, stood trembling in the doorway, his pathetic excuse about the locked inn dying in his throat. Gladius, his hands still smeared with the grime of his grim work, straightened to his full height, his immense shadow falling over the quivering envoy. The subtle smear of dark red on Gladius's gauntlets was a stark, visual reminder of the reality Corvan was now forced to confront. "Took you long enough," Gladius had rumbled, his voice low, cutting, filled with brutal disdain. The unstated accusation of incompetence hung heavy, a delicious weight in the air.

Gladius felt a cold, exquisite hum beneath his skin. Harassing Corvan was a pleasure, yes, but physical, undeniable control was even more satisfying. With a deliberate, unhurried motion, he reached for a set of simple iron manacles from his belt pouch, the cold metal clinking softly in the stillness. Corvan’s eyes, wide with fresh terror, fixed on the cuffs. He tried to back away, a pathetic, strangled sound escaping his lips, but there was no escape. Gladius moved with a fluid, terrifying grace, closing the distance in a single stride. His large, calloused hand clamped around Corvan’s wrist, surprisingly gentle but utterly inescapable. The metal snapped shut with a sharp, decisive click. He repeated the process for the other wrist, binding the envoy's hands together, bringing them up high, forcing Corvan into an awkward, humiliating posture.

Corvan whimpered, a sound like a kicked dog, his face contorted in a mixture of fear and profound degradation. His eyes, swimming with tears, darted wildly around the grim room, then to the silent bodies. He was trapped, utterly at Gladius's mercy, his delicate hands now utterly useless. The raw, immediate pleasure of Gladius’s triumph was a potent surge, a visceral experience of absolute dominance. This was the true taste of power: to bind and break, not just the body, but the spirit of those who thought themselves superior.

"Now," Gladius commanded, his voice a low, gravelly current, stripped bare of any pretense of courtesy. He gestured with his chin towards the bodies already prepared for the marsh. "You'll drag them to the moor. You'll ensure no trace is left here. No blood, no dragged marks. You'll burn their bedding, their clothes, anything that holds a scent in the hearth. The well water will wash the surfaces clean." His eyes, cold and unblinking, fixed on Corvan's face, conveying a chilling, absolute expectation. "And any valuables you find, you'll bring directly to me. Understood? Move." He stepped back, letting the cold reality of the task, and his utter helplessness, settle over the cuffed envoy.
