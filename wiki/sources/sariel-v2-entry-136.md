Game Master:
[Mode: STORY MODE]
Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 12:25 PM.
Location: Small Study, Great Archives, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 3 (XP: 6980/3000), Gold: 10gp, HP: 14/14

You looked at the three forbidden journals resting on the dusty table, their ancient leather covers seeming to hum with suppressed power. You had absorbed their contents, every chilling detail of the Null Genesis Project and the 'chilling capacity for unmaking and control.' Kaelan's mind was now a living archive of a crucial memory. The original physical texts, however, remained a profound vulnerability. They were a single, concentrated point of failure, a direct link to the very truth Raziel had ordered purged. To leave them here, even in this hidden vault, was to risk everything.

"The originals," you stated, your voice cold and absolute, cutting through the low hum of conversation. Your gaze fixed on the forbidden journals, then swept to Magister Kantos. "Once their contents are fully copied, cataloged, and partitioned according to your methods... they must be destroyed. Utterly. Leave no trace. No one, not even Raziel's ghosts, must ever find these texts."

Kantos visibly flinched, his ancient face paling further, his hands flying to clutch his robes. "Princess!" he gasped, his voice a horrified whisper. "Destroy them? These are ancient records! The very truth of creation! To unmake such knowledge... it is a sin against scholarship! A violation of the sacred oath of the Archives!" His eyes, wide with terror, darted to the towering shelves around them, as if the very spirits of the forgotten texts were protesting. The thought of destroying even a single, irrelevant scroll was anathema to him, let alone a truth of this magnitude.

You met his horrified gaze, your Starlit Eyes holding a chilling clarity that brooked no argument. You understood his scholar’s reverence, but the survival of the Imperium, and the security of your devastating secret, outweighed any oath to parchment. You were not asking him to destroy truth, but to transform it.

"Magister," you commanded, your voice firm, cutting through his despair. "You speak of 'unmaking.' This is not unmaking. This is *preservation*. Your duty, your sacred oath as Keeper of the Archives, is to ensure knowledge endures. These texts, in their current form, are a liability. A single point of failure that threatens the very truth they contain. If they are discovered, if their contents are unleashed before we understand their full implications, the Imperium will fall. And with it, all knowledge."

You gestured to the journals, then to the vastness of the Archives beyond. "You spoke of 'semantic partitioning,' of 'disguised carriers.' This is your task, Magister. You will meticulously copy every glyph, every diagram, every chilling detail. You will fragment these truths, scatter them into a thousand innocent texts, embed them in ciphers, in star charts, in poems. You will ensure their content is preserved, not in this vulnerable form, but in a distributed network of secrets, accessible only to us." Your gaze was absolute. "You will not destroy knowledge, Magister. You will make it truly indestructible. This is your greatest work. This is the ultimate act of preservation."

Kantos stared at you, his mouth working silently. His horror did not vanish, but it warred with a dawning, terrifying understanding. He was a scholar. His life was dedicated to preserving truth. And you were asking him to transform it, to make it truly eternal, even as its physical form perished. He slowly, agonizingly, nodded. His hands, though still trembling, reached for the journals, no longer with fear, but with a new, grim, meticulous purpose. The air in the study, thick with the scent of dust and fear, now held the faint, metallic tang of a profound act of intellectual transformation.
