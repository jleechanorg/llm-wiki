The faint, chilling *click-hiss* from down the corridor snagged Astarion's attention, a sound out of place in this organic nightmare. His already heightened senses, sharpened by the constant thrum of the tadpole and his own profound neuroticism, locked onto it. He moved with a predator's silent grace, his longbow held ready, its smooth surface a comforting anchor against the alien environment. The sickly yellow light from the pulsating walls cast lurid, shifting shadows, making the curving passage feel like a living, breathing throat. The air grew thicker, heavy with that metallic tang, a promise of whatever strange, unknown mechanism lay ahead.

"Stay quiet," he murmured, his voice a low, urgent rasp, more a command than a suggestion. He pressed forward, each step precise on the spongy, viscous floor, the Githyanki a silent, disciplined shadow at his heels. The burly man's heavy boots squelched behind him, a jarring cacophony that made Astarion's teeth clench. *Clumsy oaf. If we're ambushed, I know who's drawing fire first.*

As the corridor curved sharply to the left, the source of the sound became terrifyingly clear. It wasn't a mechanism on the wall, but a hulking, grotesque construct, a fusion of obsidian-black metal and organic sinew, positioned like a sentinel. Its multi-jointed limbs ended in wickedly sharp talons, and a single, unblinking eye glowed with an eerie violet light from its central mass. It was a **Nautiloid Sentry**, a guardian of the ship's inner workings, and it had just *activated*, the click-hiss of its internal mechanisms signaling its awakening. Its head, a grotesque fusion of chitin and metal, slowly swiveled towards their approach. It hadn't seen them yet, not truly, but it was aware. And it was blocking their path forward. Astarion felt a cold dread, but also a surge of hunter's anticipation. This was a trap, but a visible one.
