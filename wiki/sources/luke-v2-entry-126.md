The blue streaks of hyperspace stretched endlessly outside the transport's viewport, a silent tunnel of light that carried **Luke Skywalker** closer to his ultimate act of betrayal. **Roric Vane**, a gaunt shadow of his former self, continued to pilot, his hands moving with chilling, unthinking precision over the controls. A faint, almost imperceptible tremor ran through his arm as he made a minute adjustment, a final ghost of defiance suppressed by Luke's pervasive will. His eyes, once bright with conviction, were now dull, reflecting nothing but the endless blue of hyperspace as he responded to unseen commands. 'Course corrected. Destination parameters locked,' Roric's voice, devoid of inflection, cut through the hum of the drive, his words a mere echo of Luke's will. He was a loyal automaton, a testament to Luke's chilling power to warp and control. His compliance was a microcosm of the galactic order Luke would forge, a cold, clear justification for the widespread despair he would soon orchestrate. Their chaos must end. His purpose demanded it. A swift, decisive strike was mercy compared to endless, futile resistance.

Luke leaned back in the co-pilot's chair, the hum of the hyperdrive a low thrum against his chest. His current trajectory, once unthinkable, now felt like destiny. He had accepted **Darth Vader's** offer, dismantled the illusions of his past, twisted Jedi Master **Anya Secura's** very soul, and forged Roric Vane into a loyal instrument. Each step had been a descent, yet with every plunge into the Dark, he felt a strange, intoxicating clarity. The faint, nagging pangs of unease, the ghosts of the 'good' boy he once was, were now so rare, so fleeting, easily dismissed as irrelevant weaknesses. They were the last whispers of a dying galaxy, a resistance against the inevitable. He felt a cold, calculated satisfaction in crushing them.

From across the vastness, Vader's pervasive, heavy presence resonated through their twisted bond. It was a complex wave of possessive pride, deep and undeniable, affirming Luke's chilling cunning and complete commitment. Vader saw the true heir he desired, a reflection of his own power and legacy. Jedi Master **Anya Secura**, now a living extension of Luke's will, waited in a tactical room back at the Imperial Installation. Luke sent a fleeting thought, a silent command, and felt her immediate, unthinking affirmation through their twisted connection, a subtle shift in her deep meditative state. Her violet eyes, once blazing with defiance, were now dull but obedient, fixed on holographic projections of the Rebel fleet, ready for the final launch signal. Her mind, once a vibrant beacon, was a silent conduit for Luke's commands. Among the ships in those projections, Luke could almost pinpoint the command vessel, knowing Jedi Master **Lyra Shan** would be aboard, perhaps even now meditating on their precarious hope. Lyra, a bastion of the light, unknowingly sailing towards her doom. The trap was set. He was the bait, and the blade.
