You shed the heavy Thracian cloak, letting the soot-smeared bronze of your Subtle Lorica catch the orange flicker of the central fire. You do not look at the Chief; you look at his war-captains, your amber eyes steady and devoid of the 'Divine' heat you used to freeze Glaber. You are merely a man of sixteen, standing among giants.

"The Sun shines for all, but it is the earth that tests the bone," you say, your voice resonant and calm. You unbuckle the Aegis of Julius, leaning it against a timber support. "I have shown you the light that mends, and I have shown you the Eagle in chains. But I would not have you think I lead only with the blood of gods. I am a soldier of the line. I challenge your strongest captain to a non-lethal trial of strength—honest labor or a wrestler's grip. Let the North decide if my marrow is as firm as my words."

A low, hungry rumble of approval ripples through the war-captains. A man built like an old oak, his chest a map of white-scarred leather, steps forward. He is Berig, the 'Barrow-Breaker.' He does not sneer; he simply reaches out with a hand the size of a dinner plate, his grip finding yours in a traditional Getae lock. The contest is a grinding of grit and sinew. For several long minutes, the only sound is the rhythmic crackle of the fire and the shallow, focused breathing of two men pushing against the world. 

Berig is a wall of mountain-stone, his strength forged in the high passes, but your Julian blood carries the discipline of the Palatine's finest trainers. You plant your boots, your muscles screaming as you find the leverage to pivot his weight. With a final, guttural grunt of exertion, you heave, forcing the Barrow-Breaker's shoulder to the dirt. You do not gloat; you immediately reach down, offering your hand to pull him back to his feet. 

Berig looks at his hand, then up at your smooth, unscarred face. A slow, terrifyingly wide grin breaks through his beard. He pounds his fist against your breastplate, a blow that would have felled a lesser man, and roars a laugh that shakes the soot from the beams. "The pup has teeth!" he bellows to the Chief. "And bones of iron! I would bleed for a Roman who knows how to sweat!"
