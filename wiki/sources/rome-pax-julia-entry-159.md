Morning (10:45:00) at Vultur Ridge finds the Samnite holdfast a hive of grounded, visceral industry. I move through the basalt courtyard, the coarse grey wool of my commoner's tunic scratching against my skin—a tactile reminder of the exquisite joke I am playing. My divine aura is a tightly coiled spring within my marrow, properly masked behind the 'Mask of the Singularity.' To the Samnite warriors sharpening their iron pila and the veterans of the Tenth cleaning their masterwork (+1) blackened plate, I am merely a shadow in the periphery, a girl of no consequence carrying a tray of watered wine. 

I find a delicious, sadistic amusement in their ignorance. I watch Decimus barking orders at his men; he treats my 'absence' with the hushed reverence of a priest waiting for an omen, never suspecting that the source of his fanatical devotion is standing close enough to see the sweat-salt on his neck. Beside him, Septimus and 'The Silent' move with that shivering, clinical grace I find so erotic—fourteen iron ghosts whose minds I have only partially returned to them. They are the perfect instruments, their surgical scars hidden by their hoods, their focus so sharp it feels like a physical blade. I savor the fragility of their returned humanity; it is a sensory dessert to know that their very existence is a miracle I have permitted.

I linger near the secondary vault, where Livia remains a broken instrument upon her pallet. Cinnaminus is there, his sparrow-eyes never leaving her face. The boy's unrequited worship is a silken leash I have already begun to tighten; every time he looks at me for hope, I feel the delicious weight of his soul being added to my collection. Sulla's 'Loom' is the prize I require, a secret hidden behind Livia's logic-locks. The peace of the holdfast is a fragile thing, a temporary sanctuary built from the ruins of a tyrant's garden, but the road to Capua beckons. The 'Solar Scourge' needs a larger stage, and the fire I have lit here is merely the first spark of a world in flames.
