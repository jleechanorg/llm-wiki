Game Master:
You do not leave the temple steps immediately. Instead, you turn to your pack, the *Hunger* guiding you to separate the useful tools from the potential liabilities.

**Strategy: Gaslight the Berserker**
You step into Minsc's line of sight, blocking his view of the Mayor's trembling form. You place a hand on the berserker's pauldron, looking up at him with a gaze of intense, martial respect.

"The Mayor pays us not for charity, Minsc, but because he recognizes a true force of nature," you say, your voice vibrating with conviction. "He knows that laws and courts have failed. He knows that the only thing that can scrub the darkness from those mines is your sword. You are not a mercenary today; you are the Hammer of Justice."

Minsc's chest swells, his earlier confusion about the extortion vanishing under the praise. "Justice! Yes! Boo says the coin is just fuel for the butt-kicking engine! We shall scrub the darkness until it sparkles!"

**Strategy: Isolate the Witch**
Before Dynaheir can interject with questions about the morality of threatening town officials, you pivot to her. You shove the map of the mines into her hands, effectively cutting off her line of sight to the others.

"Dynaheir, the Mayor mentioned Abyssal signatures," you lie smoothly, leveraging her academic curiosity. "I need you to cross-reference this layout with your knowledge of ley lines and planar breaches. Don't worry about the logistics or the gold; that is grunt work. Your mind is the only weapon sharp enough to dissect the *source* of the taint. Focus on that. Ignore everything else."

Dynaheir frowns, looking at the map, her ego stroked just enough to distract her. "Abyssal... yes. The geometry of the tunnels does seem... spiral. I will study it. Do not disturb me."

**The Investment**
With the party psychically managed, you lead them back to the **Nashkel General Store**. The shopkeeper groans as you enter, but the heavy sack of gold silences him.

You select gear with a cold, necrotizing logic: what makes them effective now, but remains valuable if they become... absent.

For **Minsc**, you purchase a suit of **Splint Mail (200 gp)**. It is heavy, durable, and expensive. It will keep him alive while he tanks for you, and if he dies, it can be stripped, cleaned, and sold—or worn by Kagain.

For **Dynaheir**, you ignore the scrolls she eyes longingly (which become useless ink once scribed) and instead purchase a **Wand of Magic Missiles (400 gp)**. It is a simple, brutal tool. It gives her burst damage now, but more importantly, it is a universal item. If the witch falls, **Garrick** can pick it up and use it instantly. It is an asset that belongs to the party, not the wizard.

"Equip yourselves," you order, tossing the gear to them. "We march on the mines tonight."

Minsc straps on the heavy armor, looking like a steel mountain. Dynaheir tests the balance of the wand, looking pleased despite herself. They are stronger. And you are ready to use them.
