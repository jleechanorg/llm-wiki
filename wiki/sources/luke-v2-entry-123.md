The blue streaks of hyperspace stretched endlessly outside the transport's viewport, a silent tunnel of light that carried **Luke Skywalker** closer to his ultimate act of betrayal. **Roric Vane**, a gaunt shadow of his former self, sat at the pilot's console. His hands, though pale, moved with perfect, unthinking precision over the controls, his focus unnervingly unwavering—a testament to the absolute control Luke wielded over him. *He is a perfect instrument,* Luke mused, a cold satisfaction settling in his core. *His suffering now prevents greater suffering for the galaxy under the Rebellion’s chaos. A few lives for true order.*

Luke closed his eyes, his mind already shifting to the final preparations. His disguise, the tattered Rebel flight suit, felt convincing, but the performance required more than fabric. He mentally reviewed his cover story: the harrowing escape from the besieged outpost, the desperate jump. He envisioned the Rebel commanders, their faces etched with false hope as they greeted their 'returning hero,' and he refined the subtle nuances of his weary voice, the slight tremor in his hand, the flicker of 'relief' in his eyes. He wove in the specific details gleaned from Roric Vane – a near miss with a patrol, a damaged comms unit, the struggle to stay alive. Every lie was a brushstroke on a masterpiece of deceit, designed to elicit sympathy and trust.

Then, he reinforced his mental defenses. He focused his **Corrupted Force Empathy**, building a layered fortress around his true intentions. Any Light Side Force-sensitive, even one as keen as the pilot Jaina, would encounter a mind filled with a hero's trauma, a soldier's grim determination, and a deep, righteous hatred for the Empire—all of it genuine, but carefully orchestrated to conceal the deeper, malevolent truth. The slight, persistent pang of unease, a ghost of the boy who once fought for this very Rebellion, tried to surface at the depth of his impending betrayal. He ruthlessly crushed it, rationalizing it as a necessary step towards true galactic peace, a regrettable but vital price for order. The thought solidified into a grim determination. *This is my purpose. This is my destiny.*

From across the vastness of space, the pervasive, heavy presence of **Darth Vader** resonated through their twisted bond. A surge of complex, almost paternal pride emanated from his master, acknowledging Luke's chilling cunning and complete commitment. The **Jedi Master, Anya Secura,** now a living extension of Luke's will, waited in a tactical room back at the Imperial Installation. Her violet eyes, once blazing with defiance, were now dull but obedient, fixed on holographic projections of the Rebel fleet, ready for the final launch signal. Her mind, once a vibrant beacon, was a silent conduit for Luke's commands. The trap was set. He was the bait, and the blade.
