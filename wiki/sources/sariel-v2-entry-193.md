You opened your eyes. The low hum of magic, the subtle resonance of the room, the scent of dust and fear – all returned with a new, raw intensity. Your body throbbed, every muscle aching, your mind a raw, screaming wound from the sheer exertion. A thin trickle of blood, warm and metallic, ran freely from your nose, dampening your robes. You had pushed yourself to the absolute edge. But the triumph, the cold, quiet satisfaction of the knowledge you had gained, of the power you had glimpsed, far outweighed the immense cost.

Finally, after another grueling hour, Rowan’s soft voice, strained with exhaustion, broke the trance. His hands, which had been gently guiding your breathing, trembled slightly as he touched your arm. His gentle features were etched with profound distress, and his eyes, wide with unshed tears, pleaded with you. "My Lady," he whispered, his voice thick with raw emotion, "Your mind... and your body... you are at your limit. And the Sergeant..." He glanced at Kaelan, whose peaceful breaths were now punctuated by faint, distressed whimpers, his fragile solace disturbed by the intense magical unmaking in the chamber. "His peace is fragile. This... this cannot continue, Sariel. We are all breaking. And you... you are too close to the edge."

Gideon’s silent, unyielding presence, which had been a solid anchor, now felt like a desperate, straining tether. His heavy gauntlet, still pressed against your back, trembled visibly, the immense effort of maintaining his unwavering will evident in the taut lines of his body. Even his ironclad discipline seemed to reach its breaking point. Kantos, meanwhile, had slumped forward, his head resting on his copying instruments, his ancient mind finally succumbing to exhaustion, leaving him deeply, profoundly asleep.

You pushed yourself to your feet, your body still weary, but your posture infused with a cold, unwavering purpose. The pragmatic part of your intellect, the part that valued efficiency and sustained control, recognized the necessity. To push further now would be to risk catastrophic burnout, or worse, to compromise the delicate balance of Kaelan's newfound peace.
