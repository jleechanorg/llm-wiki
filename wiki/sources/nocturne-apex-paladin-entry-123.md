Location: Forest Edge, Near Man Tibbet's Farm, Just Before Dawn

The cool, pre-dawn air hung still and silent around Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) as she remained a motionless shadow within her concealed observation point. The three cloaked figures of Kaelen's transport team, moving with practiced efficiency, had converged on the bundles of captives. Her decision to observe from a distance, rather than reveal herself, was a calculated one. There was no tactical advantage in direct interaction now; the objective was simply to confirm the secure transfer of her 'stock' and then to pivot to the far more valuable currency: the intelligence she held.

The transport operatives worked swiftly, their movements precise and economical. They located each of the six bundled forms, their large, empty sacks now finding their purpose. There was no fumbling, no unnecessary noise, just the soft rustle of canvas and the almost imperceptible thud as each bundle was shouldered. They were professionals, just as she had assessed. Within minutes, the pile of captives had vanished, absorbed by the anonymous figures and the encroaching gloom of the forest. The team then melted back into the trees, heading south, presumably towards a discreet rendezvous point or a hidden carriage deeper within the woods. Their departure was as seamless as their arrival.

Nocturne watched until the last rustle of leaves faded into the pre-dawn quiet. A cold satisfaction settled over her. The contract was now, mechanically, complete. Kaelen had complied. The Shadow Weavers had their 'stock,' and she had her doubled payment. But more importantly, she had secured something far more potent than gold or human lives. She possessed fragments of a truth that could reshape the political landscape of Baldur's Gate, evidence of House Durin's Folly's chilling, clandestine operations, and a direct link to a shadowy figure named "The Architect" and the sinister depths of the Underdark. The true game was about to begin. The peasants were merely the opening gambit.
