The dragon moves with a vacant, clinical obedience, his claws leaving jagged scars in the stone path—a physical testament to the psychic anchor you've hammered into his pride. As you reach the bridge crossing, the multi-limbed silhouette of Kar'niss emerges from the sulfurous mist. The Drider halts, his many legs clicking against the rock in a frantic, unsettled rhythm. He looks up at the dragon, then at the porcelain girl seated upon its back, her violet eyes two twin stars of Abyssal hunger that no longer require the distraction of blinking. The Moonlantern in his hand flickers, its pale light struggling against the cold, static heat radiating from your 'Predator' form.

"The... the monastery," Kar'niss rasps, his voice a dry, chittering rattle. "The light that was... it was a sun. The General's scouts reported a breach, a storm of ash. Kith'rak... Inquisitor... all gone?"

"The Absolute does not suffer the redundant, Kar'niss," you whisper, the tectonic harmonic of your voice making the Drider's thorax shiver. You lean slightly forward, the brine-and-ozone scent of your nature drowning out the formaldehyde rot of his convoy. "The Crèche was a nest of traitors clutching a prize they could not comprehend. He sees the 68 souls screaming in your marrow and the Silver Sword shimmering in Lae'zel's hand. To him, you are no longer a Disciple; you are the Absolute's most violent miracle. 

"The... the First Disciple speaks with the voice of the Void," he murmurs, his chittering settling into a submissive hum. "The Moonlantern is yours to lead. The Shadow-Curse awaits your command. We march... we march for Moonrise."
