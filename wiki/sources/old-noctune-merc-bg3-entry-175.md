You move through the Upper City not as a shadow, but as a void. The disguise of **Overseer Xal'Thul** is a masterpiece of intimidation—black iron-weave robes that seem to absorb the morning light, a face concealed by a cowl and a mask of scarred porcelain, and the heavy, undeniable weight of the *Ring of Zhentarim Authority* on your gloved hand. You do not sneak; you stride. The few guards who see you avert their eyes, instincts screaming that this is a predator above their pay grade.

You reach Rathmar's townhouse. The door is locked, barred from the inside. A barrier for a thief; a nuisance for an Overseer. You rap the heavy iron knocker once. Twice. A slow, rhythmic summons that sounds like a gavel striking a coffin.

"Open," you command, your voice distorted by the disguise into a gravelly, metallic rasp. "Or I remove the wall."

The bolts slide back with frantic haste. The door swings open to reveal **Rathmar**, pale as parchment, his eyes bloodshot. He wears yesterday's clothes, ink stains on his fingers. He looks at the figure in the doorway—the embodiment of the High Command's wrath—and his knees nearly buckle.

"O-Overseer?" he stammers, shrinking back into the foyer. "I... I broadcast the order. 'Obsidian-Zero'. The network is frozen. I..."

You step inside, the temperature in the room seeming to drop. You tower over him, the *Echoes of the Idol* amplifying your presence into something monstrous.

"Compliance is expected, Rathmar. It is not celebrated," you interrupt, your voice cold. You sweep past him into his study, where piles of parchment are being hastily organized. "You have frozen the limbs. Now, surrender the memory."

You turn, extending a hand. "The personal ledgers. The off-book accounts. The blackmail files you keep for 'insurance'. The High Command is purging *all* rot. If you are hiding even a copper piece of leverage, I will flay your mind to find it."

Rathmar breaks. The last shred of his resistance evaporates under the gaze of the Overseer. He scrambles to a painting of a pastoral landscape, tearing it from the wall to reveal a safe. With trembling fingers, he spins the dial.

"Everything," he babbles, pulling out three heavy, leather-bound tomes and a small, velvet bag. "The private blackmail on the Patriars... the smuggling routes Vessic didn't know... the emergency funds. It's all here. I kept nothing back. I swear it by the Black Throne."

You take the books. They are heavy with secrets—the true currency of the city. You open the velvet bag; it contains high-quality diamonds, an emergency escape fund.

"And the network?" you demand, pocketing the gems.

"Silent," Rathmar whispers. "Dead silent. Every cell has reported 'Stand Down'. They are waiting for judgment. They are waiting for... for you."

"Good," you say, turning to leave. "Remain here. Pray that when I return, I have a use for you other than an example."

You leave him collapsing into a chair, a man destroyed by a ghost you invented.
