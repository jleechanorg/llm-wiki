Astarion's fingers brushed away the last clinging soil from the collected earthroot, a grimace fighting with a thin line of satisfaction. The crude sustenance and the fresh water were a small, tangible victory in this horrifying, alien reality. He secured his waterskin, its cool weight a minor comfort. His gaze flicked to his companions. Lae'zel, the formidable Githyanki, stood like a statue of coiled aggression, her golden eyes fixed on the distant plume of smoke marking the Nautiloid's demise, a silent, burning fury in her posture. Gale, the burly man, sat heavily on a damp rock, rubbing his temples, his face etched with a scholar's bewilderment and a hint of lingering nausea.

"Now that we've established our... rather unglamorous current accommodations," Astarion drawled, his voice edged with a sardonic weariness, "and acquired what little comfort this savage landscape offers, perhaps we should address the matter of our immediate future. And by extension, each other. I've done my part, now for yours. What exactly *are* you two?" He gestured vaguely, his disdain for their brute force and lack of finesse thinly veiled. "I'm a ranger, if you must know. What charming professions do you hail from, that you found yourselves so thoroughly abused by Mind Flayers?"

Lae'zel finally tore her gaze from the horizon, her golden eyes, sharp as a dragon's claw, fixing on Astarion with an almost palpable impatience. "I am a warrior, *tav'qu'ith*," she rasped, the Githyanki word for "outsider" or "foe" a subtle barb in her clipped tone. "A soldier of Vlaakith, honed in the creche. I fight. I kill. That is my profession." Her hand instinctively went to the empty space where her silver sword would normally rest, a flicker of raw frustration crossing her alien features. She seemed less concerned with the aftermath of the crash and more with the perceived idleness. Her powerful frame, though weary, still vibrated with contained ferocity. Her class was undeniably **Fighter**.

Gale, meanwhile, finally looked up, his blue eyes still a little unfocused. He let out a long, theatrical sigh. "I, my dear... *ranger*," he began, his voice surprisingly rich despite its current gravelly quality, "am a wizard. Or rather, was. A rather *talented* one, if I do say so myself. Though I seem to be... lacking my usual connection to the Weave, at present. A minor inconvenience, I assure you." He waved a dismissive hand, attempting a confident smile that didn't quite reach his eyes. "My profession involved arcane research, the unraveling of mysteries, the wielding of potent magic." He gestured vaguely at his ruined robes. His class was clearly **Wizard**.

Astarion's lip curled, a mixture of mild surprise and lingering disdain. A fighter. A wizard. How utterly predictable. And yet, perhaps useful. "A wizard, you say? How quaint. And a brute who fights. How terribly... direct." His neuroticism pulsed behind his eye. He needed a plan, a logical next step, and this wilderness was not conducive to his refined sensibilities. He felt a desperate urge to impose order, to force these disparate elements into a cohesive, controllable whole.
