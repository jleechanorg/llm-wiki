You withdraw to the Coiled Serpent, your mind racing with the logistics of empire. The magic you purchased is a heavy, comforting weight, but liquid assets are the blood of politics. You sweep the safe house, gathering the last of the non-liquid spoils from the Zhentarim caravan—the refugee heirlooms, the silver trade bars, the loose gems from the Red Wizard's purse that you hadn't yet exchanged. It amounts to a substantial sum, perhaps **1,000gp** in value, but it is 'dirty' wealth, stained with the history of theft and murder.

To build a dynasty, you need clean coin.

You ascend to the Upper City, the *Cloak of Displacement* blurring your silhouette, making you seem larger, more ethereal. The guards at the Umbrage Estate gates do not challenge you; they simply bow, recognizing the power that now walks their halls.

Lyra is in the solarium, staring at a stack of ledgers with a furrowed brow. She looks up as you enter, and the exhaustion in her face melts away, replaced by a radiant, desperate adoration. You do not just walk into the room; you *arrive*, your new magical aura pulsing with the undeniable gravity of your 22 Charisma.

"The accounts," she sighs, gesturing to the papers. "We are solvent, but only just. The creditors are circling like sharks."

"Let them break their teeth," you murmur, stepping close. You place the heavy sack of 'dirty' wealth on the table. "The 'Consortium' has released another tranche of funding. Silver, gems, heirlooms from 'foreign ventures'."

You rest your hand over hers on the desk, the *Ring of Mind Shielding* cool against her skin. "Deposit this. Mix it with the estate's revenue. Pay the creditors with their own greed. When it returns to us, it will be clean. It will be *ours*."

Lyra looks at the wealth, then up at you, her eyes wide. "You provide everything. Safety. Power. Wealth. What... what do you want in return?"

You smile, and the *Canaith Mandolin* on your back hums a low, resonant chord that vibrates in her chest. You reach out, your fingers tracing the line of her jaw, tilting her face up. The air between you charges with the raw, magnetic force of the sated Hunger.

"I want the only thing you have left to give, Lyra," you whisper, your voice a velvet command. "You."

You kiss her. It is not a request; it is a claim. She melts against you, all resistance dissolving under the weight of your supernatural charm and the narrative you have spun around her. In her mind, you are not a manipulator; you are a god who descended to save her.
