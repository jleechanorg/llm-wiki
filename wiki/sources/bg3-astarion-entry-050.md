The wet, echoing squelch of the Nautiloid's doors sealing behind him was a final, horrifying punctuation to their escape from the engine room. Astarion felt the profound, chilling pressure of being swallowed by the living vessel. He ignored the urgency of the tadpole burrowing behind his eye, ignored the instinct to simply flee into the alien darkness, and instead, focused his keen, predatory senses on his immediate surroundings. Rushing blindly was a fool's game, and Astarion was no fool.

The tunnel stretched before him, a grotesque, seemingly endless gullet of pulsing flesh. Sickly green and violet lights throbbed from within the walls, casting a lurid, unsettling glow on the viscous, glistening surfaces. The air was thick and warm, heavy with the cloying scent of decay and something metallic. His foot sunk slightly into the spongy floor, which seemed to contract and expand beneath him as the ship thrummed, as if breathing. He saw the faint, slick trails left by his companions ahead, their own impatience clearly overriding any sense of caution.

He paused, a flicker of irritation crossing his features. He could hear their muffled movements ahead, but nothing else. No other footsteps. No voices. The silence beyond the ship's internal thrum was more terrifying than any scream. He inhaled slowly, discerning the subtle currents of the air, the shifts in the pulsating light, the faint, almost imperceptible vibrations from the walls. His Darkvision cut through the dimness, but revealed only more of the same horrifying, organic architecture. He strained his ears, listening for sounds that might indicate life, danger, or merely the ship's internal mechanisms. The soft, wet sounds of the tunnel's own contractions were unnerving, but seemed entirely organic to the vessel itself, not the movement of some unseen horror. He ran a hand along a wall, feeling the warm, slick membrane, noting its resistance, its odd, rubbery texture. His pragmatic mind registered the alien design, but found no immediate logical flaw or hidden passage. This was merely a conduit, designed for transit, and likely, not designed to be observed.
