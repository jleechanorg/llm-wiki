Reincarnation as Cain von Silford
He is reborn as Cain von Silford, the third son of Margrave Garm von Silford (ruler of the Gracia domain in the Kingdom of Esfort). His mother is Sarah (second wife), and he has older half-brothers and a doting older sister Reine. Retaining his memories and Earth knowledge, young Cain (starting as a toddler) secretly studies in the family library, learns about the world’s geography, politics, and magic system. ￼
Magic here is elemental (Fire, Water, etc., including rare types), with a visible Status Board (like a game UI) showing stats, skills, and blessings. Cain experiments secretly and discovers that applying scientific principles (e.g., combustion chemistry for fire) vastly boosts his spells.
Baptism and Overpowered Blessings
At age 5, during the standard church baptism, the crystal shatters in a massive pillar of light. Cain receives Level 10 blessings from all Seven Gods (unprecedented; normal blessings are Level 1-5, and multiple are rare). His mana, physical stats, affinities (including all elements and advanced magic like Space/Creation), and everything else are absurdly maxed out—millions in mana capacity where adults have hundreds. ￼
He tries to conceal his status but still appears as an elite prodigy. His family and the priest are stunned. The gods’ lack of restraint sets up the series’ running gag of Cain trying (and often failing) to live a “normal” life while hiding his power.
Training and Early Exploits
Garm hires tutors: swordswoman Milly (Millie) and mage Nina (both B-rank adventurers). Cain’s training is comically one-sided—he destroys practice equipment and grounds with minimal effort. They become more like companions/chaperones. ￼
Cain sneaks into the Forest of Monsters, tests Spatial and Creation magic (making items, structures, and near-unlimited-capacity Magic Bags as gifts), and causes chaos (e.g., massive explosions mistaken for stampedes). Garm scolds him after finding a dropped handkerchief with the family crest. He also begins crafting advanced magic tools.
Journey to the Capital, Bandit Rescue, and Engagements
At age 10, Cain travels to the royal capital with Garm and Reine for his noble debut. Their convoy encounters bandits attacking a luxurious carriage carrying Princess Telestia von Esfort (third princess) and Silk von Santana (daughter of Duke Eric von Santana). Cain single-handedly defeats the bandits using restrained but still overwhelming power. ￼
The grateful girls (same age as Cain) immediately crush on him. Social norms and their insistence lead to him riding with them. In the capital, King Rex and Duke Eric reward him lavishly: Baron title (independent nobility at 10), a prime mansion, wealth, etc. The girls declare their love, and the king/Duke arrange engagements to bind Cain’s power to the kingdom. Cain, a former average high schooler, is overwhelmed. ￼
Adventurer Life and Monster Stampede
Cain registers at the Adventurer’s Guild (disguising his identity somewhat) but shatters testing crystals and completes high-difficulty quests instantly via teleportation and overwhelming magic. A major monster stampede (involving a powerful Orc Lord or similar in early arcs) threatens a city. Cain ends it solo with barriers, barrage spells, and a decisive strike. ￼
The king learns more of his true power and swears him to secrecy.
Tifana and Further Engagements
Tifana von Ribelt (elven Knight Commander, daughter of a Duke, battle maniac) challenges Cain to a duel after hearing rumors. He defeats her (restrained), and she proposes on the spot per her vow to marry only one stronger than her. The king approves, adding her as the third fiancée. ￼
Royal Academy, Politics, and Hinata
At 12, Cain enters the Royal Academy, deals with jealous nobles, protects commoners, and struggles to hold back in classes (destroying facilities). A diplomatic visit by Saintess Hinata Lyra Marineford from the theocratic Holy Country of Marineford leads to assassination attempts by corrupt cardinals. Cain foils them spectacularly, revealing divine power. Hinata perceives his connection to all Seven Gods, reveres him as an Apostle, and (via oracle) joins as the fourth fiancée. ￼
Deeper Lore: The First King, Evil God Aaron, and the Greater Mission
Cain meets the First King Yuuya Terra Hirasawa Esfort (another Japanese reincarnate/summoned hero) in a special realm. Yuuya sealed the fallen Evil God Aaron (former 8th god of entertainment/chaos) centuries ago. The seals are weakening, causing monster surges, demons, and corruption. The gods reincarnated Cain (stacking him with max power) specifically to destroy Aaron permanently. Cain trains intensively (including time in a divine realm like Fabrinil, aging him temporarily before reversion), crafts artifacts, builds alliances, tames divine beasts (e.g., Fenrir Haku, dragon Gin), and purges corruption. ￼
Later arcs involve diplomacy with empires (including the Demon Empire, leading to fifth fiancée Lisabeth von Benestos), confrontations with Aaron’s fragments/avatars (e.g., possessing nobles or demons like Corzino or Agus), promotions (to Margrave), territory management, and balancing academy/noble life with world-saving duties. Cain’s harem grows to five main fiancées (Telestia, Silk, Tifana, Hinata, Lisabeth), plus others who show interest. ￼
Overall Tone and Themes
The series is a lighthearted overpowered protagonist isekai comedy with action, slice-of-life, crafting, and harem elements. Cain is kind, humble, and heroic but lacks common sense, leading to constant lectures from family, fiancées, and the king. He wants a peaceful adventurer life but keeps getting dragged into politics and divine conflicts. The manga expands on the light novel with detailed visuals of his exploits, magic, and chaotic daily life.
