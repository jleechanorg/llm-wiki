Location: House Valerius Estate, Upper City, Morning

The tension at the gates of House Valerius was a taut thread in the morning air. Julian (Age 32), her eldest brother, stood before Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16), his initial cordiality replaced by a flicker of irritation and thinly veiled concern. Her mention of House Durin's Folly and "unprecedented infiltration" had clearly struck a nerve. The guards, stiff and uncertain, watched the silent standoff between the exiled prodigy and the heir apparent.

Nocturne's gaze, sharp and analytical, remained fixed on Julian. He recognized the threat, but his petty jealousy and desire for control meant he would try to manage it, to take credit, or to deny her access. That would not do. Her mission was to assert her indispensability to her father, not to hand Julian an opportunity to sabotage or dilute her triumph.

She reached into her concealed garb, her movements deliberate and slow, pulling forth a tightly rolled piece of parchment: the sealed letter from House Durin's Folly to "Tibbet, Keeper of the Eastern March." Her aura, a chilling, unyielding presence, subtly intensified, pressing against Julian and his retainers. It was not a physical force, but a mental one, a silent promise of absolute ruthlessness if defied.

"Your 'management' has failed, Julian," Nocturne's voice was a low, even tone that cut through the silence. She unfurled the letter just enough for Julian to see the crest of House Durin's Folly, holding it so the guards could not read its contents. "This is but one fragment of the truth. Tibbet's farm is not a farm. It is a clandestine outpost of Durin's Folly, established directly on our eastern approach. Their guards were professional, their defenses arcane. Your network, and Father's, has been utterly blind to it."

Julian's eyes widened, a flash of genuine shock breaking through his composure. He snatched the letter, his gaze devouring the crest, then scanning the address. His jaw tightened, and a vein pulsed visibly in his temple. He knew the implications of such a discovery, especially one so close to the city and so thoroughly missed by his own intelligence. The cold dread of incompetence, of a rival's brazen audacity, eclipsed his personal animosity for a moment.

Nocturne pressed her advantage, her voice gaining an edge of chilling command. "This is not a matter for your discretion, Julian. It is a crisis. I require an immediate, private audience with Lord Cassian. Now. This very moment. The threat is time-sensitive, and my patience is not infinite. You will take me to him." Her aura, amplified by her direct challenge, brooked no refusal.

Julian's knuckles whitened on the parchment, his face a mask of furious, impotent frustration. He despised her, but the evidence was irrefutable, the threat undeniable, and her commanding presence overwhelming. He swallowed, then turned abruptly to the lead guard. "Open the gates. Escort my sister to Lord Cassian's private study. Immediately. And ensure no one, *no one*, interrupts them." His voice was clipped, a thinly veiled command of both urgency and resentment. He glared at Nocturne one last time, a silent promise of future reckoning in his eyes, before stalking back into the estate, leaving the way clear.

Nocturne merely inclined her head, a silent acknowledgment of his forced compliance. Her gaze swept over the now-obedient guards, then turned towards the imposing doors of House Valerius. The first hurdle cleared. The game had truly begun.

Location: House Valerius Estate, Lord Cassian's Private Study, Morning

The polished marble floors of the Valerius Estate gleamed under the morning light as Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) was escorted by two stern-faced guards through its opulent halls. The scent of beeswax and old money hung heavy in the air. Each step was deliberate, echoing in the hushed silence of the ancestral home. The air thrummed with a familiar tension—the undercurrent of power and hidden agendas that defined her family. She paid the guards no mind, her thoughts already on the imminent confrontation, the careful unfolding of her gathered intelligence.

They stopped before a massive, intricately carved oak door. The lead guard rapped twice, a soft, deferential knock. A deep, resonant voice from within bade them enter. The door swung open silently, revealing a spacious, richly appointed study. Walls lined with leather-bound tomes, a massive desk laden with scrolls and maps, and a roaring fireplace casting dancing shadows.

Lord Cassian Valerius (Age 55), Nocturne's father and the cunning patriarch of House Valerius, sat behind the desk. His silver hair was meticulously combed, his posture ramrod straight despite his age. His eyes, sharp and intelligent, fixed on Nocturne as she entered. They held no warmth, only a cold, assessing gaze that missed nothing. He dismissed the guards with a curt nod, and the door closed, leaving father and estranged daughter in a charged silence.

"Nocturne," Lord Cassian began, his voice surprisingly soft, yet carrying an immense weight of authority. "Julian informed me you have... urgent information. Information my network somehow missed." There was no accusation, only a statement of fact, a subtle probe. His gaze remained unwavering, attempting to gauge the full extent of her audacious return. He did not ask her to sit.

Nocturne met his gaze without flinching, her own features an unreadable mask. "Indeed, Father," she replied, her voice as calm and devoid of emotion as his. "Information that threatens the very foundations of House Valerius. Information your *eldest son's* 'management' was entirely blind to." She let the subtle barb land, watching for any reaction, but his face remained impassive. "It concerns House Durin's Folly. And something far more dangerous than mere commerce."

The air in the room grew heavy, anticipation hanging thick between them. This was the moment. The stage was set.
