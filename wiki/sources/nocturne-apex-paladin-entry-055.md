Location: Widow Elara's Cottage, Early Evening

The detailed internal map provided by her **Apex Senses** solidified Nocturne's (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) resolve. A single, elderly occupant, frail and unsuspecting, represented the path of least resistance. The cottage was a simple, easily navigable space. The initial disturbance from the mastiffs was a receding echo; now, it was time for the quiet, efficient acquisition. Her gaze, sharp and analytical, swept over the rear of the cottage, identifying the window closest to the creek as her entry point.

She moved with the predatory grace of an apex hunter. Her Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate, designed for such nocturnal pursuits, absorbed the last whisper of light, rendering her an almost invisible shadow against the darkening thicket. Every muscle in her body moved with precise economy, her Coordination (14) a symphony of controlled power. There was no hesitation, no wasted breath. The creek's gentle gurgle became her accomplice, muffling the faint sounds of her approach.

The window, old and slightly warped, yielded with a soft, almost imperceptible groan as Nocturne's skilled fingers found its latch. She eased it open, the sound swallowed by the creek. With a fluid motion, she slipped inside, landing silently on the worn wooden floor. The interior was exactly as her Apex Senses had revealed: a single main room, warmed by the dying embers in the hearth. The air was thick with the scent of woodsmoke, chamomile, and the subtle, comforting aroma of baked bread.

Widow Elara, a wizened old woman with a shawl draped over her head, sat by the hearth, her back to Nocturne, softly humming a forgotten tune. A single, flickering lantern cast her frail silhouette against the opposite wall. She was utterly oblivious.

Nocturne's movements were swifter than a shadow. Her longsword remained sheathed, unnecessary for such a simple task. Her hand clamped over the woman's mouth before a gasp could form, the other arm wrapping around her frail body, pinning her in place. There was no struggle, no sound. The humming cut off abruptly. The old woman's eyes, wide with sudden, uncomprehending terror, stared up at the unreadable mask of Nocturne's face, now barely visible in the dim light. No force, no malice, just cold, unyielding efficiency. The process was clean.

The 'stock' was acquired. The silence of the cottage remained unbroken, save for the crackle of the hearth and the old woman's frantic, muffled breathing. The mission continued.
