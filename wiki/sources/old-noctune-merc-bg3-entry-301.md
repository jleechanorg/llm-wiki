Game Master:
You leave Lord Caldwell to his wine and his newfound loyalty, slipping back into the glittering current of the gala. The applause for his toast has settled into a murmur of intrigued speculation. The room is warming to Lyra, but warmth is not enough. You need greed.

**Target Beta: Lady Luticia Hhune.**

You find the matriarch near the expansive windows overlooking the Chionthar River. She is a woman of sharp angles and heavy jewelry, her gaze not on the party, but on the distant, dark waters below. She holds a crystal flute like a weapon, her expression one of calculated boredom.

You approach her side, your own gaze following hers to the river. "The water is calm tonight, Lady Hhune," you murmur, your voice a soft counterpoint to the string quartet. "Unusually so. The... *obstructions*... that have plagued the shipping lanes of late seem to have dissolved."

Lady Hhune turns, her eyes narrowing as she assesses you. She sees the midnight velvet, the confidence, the lack of a familiar crest. "The river is a fickle mistress," she replies, her tone clipped. "And the Zhentarim are a persistent weed. They choke the trade routes with their tolls and their 'inspections'. My captains report delays daily."

"Report*ed*," you correct her, turning to face her fully. You let the *Echoes of the Idol* lend a weight of absolute truth to your words. "The Zhentarim presence on the river has been... liquidated. Their captain is dead. Their vessel, the *Silent Wave*, has been seized. The chokehold is broken."

Hhune's mask slips. A flash of genuine avarice lights her eyes. "Broken? By whom? The Fist?"

"By the 'Sovereign Consortium'," you smile, gesturing vaguely toward Lyra on the dais. "Regent Umbrage's new partners believe in free trade... for those who pay the proper respect. The river is open, My Lady. But the Consortium will be assigning new... *protection protocols*... for authorized vessels."

You lean in, dropping your voice to a whisper. "I am authorized to offer House Hhune an exclusive contract. Priority lane access. Reduced tariffs. And total immunity from the 'Consortium's' enforcement fleet. All we require... is your vocal support for the Regent's new security measures in the Parliament tomorrow."

Lady Hhune looks at Lyra, then back at you. She does calculations in her head—the cost of Zhentarim bribes versus the profit of unrestricted shipping. The math is simple.

"Exclusive rights?" she asks, her voice low.

"Monopolistic," you promise.

A slow, shark-like smile spreads across her face. She raises her glass to you. "Then it seems House Hhune has found a new friend. I shall make sure the Duke hears of Lyra's... *visionary* leadership."

Two pillars down. The merchant queen is bought.
