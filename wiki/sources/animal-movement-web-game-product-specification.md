## Summary
Product specification for AnimalWorld, a browser-based HTML5 game where players control various animals as image sprites with realistic movement patterns and behaviors. Targets ages 8-35 with a free-to-play model, planned for 12-month development.

## Key Claims
- 12+ diverse animals across four categories: land (Lion, Rabbit, Bear, Fox, Deer), flying (Eagle, Owl, Butterfly), aquatic (Dolphin, Fish, Turtle), and special (Chameleon, Cheetah)
- Physics-based movement reflecting real animal locomotion (quadruped gaits, flight mechanics, swimming with momentum)
- Four game modes: Free Roam, Survival, Challenge, and Multiplayer
- Three-phase monetization: animal packs ($2.99), habitat DLCs ($4.99), cosmetic items ($0.99-$1.99)
- 12-month development timeline: Phase 1 (months 1-3) core, Phase 2 (4-6) expansion, Phase 3 (7-9) advanced features, Phase 4 (10-12) launch prep
- Target metrics: 15+ min session, 70% D1 retention, 15% conversion, $5.50 ARPU

## Key Quotes
> "Create an engaging, accessible web game that allows players to embody different animals, explore diverse habitats, and experience unique movement mechanics inspired by real animal behaviors."

> "No Pay-to-Win: All gameplay advantages achievable through play"
