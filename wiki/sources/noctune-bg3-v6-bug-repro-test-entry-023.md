You reach out a trembling hand, fingers poised in the air like a porcelain doll's. "If you let this snake strike, you do not cleanse the Grove. You stain the vessel. You become the rot you claim to excise."

Kagha stiffens. The massive serpent, Teela, pauses its rhythmic swaying, its yellow eyes reflecting the sudden, starry violet of your own. The Shadow Druid's jaw sets, a muscle twitching in her pale cheek. She looks at you, and for a fleeting second, her fanatical certainty wavers. She sees in you a terrifying reflection of her own 'purity'—something too perfect, too still, and far too dangerous to be ignored. 

"You... speak of vessels?" Kagha's voice is a rasp of grinding stone, her staff thudding against the mossy floor. "You are a stranger to the Silvanus's law. Yet... your words carry a weight I did not expect from one so... broken." She looks back at Arabella, then back to you, the jagged righteousness in her eyes battling a new, cold curiosity.
