Daemon (Level 8, Age 30) turned from the window, his gaze sweeping over the assembled medical experts, Mina (Age 60) and Master Elms (Age 75), and his Castellan, Kael (Level 7, Age 25). Seraphina's (Age 20) pregnancy was a grim triumph, but the shared barrenness of Lady Isolde Blackwood (Level 3, Age ~22) and Elara Croft (Age 19) remained an irritating puzzle.

"You," Daemon rumbled, pointing at Mina and Master Elms. "Your task is not complete. Your focus now is Lady Isolde. Research her lineage. Find remedies. Discover how to make her fertile. Whatever components are required, whatever rituals, Kael will provide. Succeed, and you will be greatly rewarded. Fail, and you will face my displeasure." The experts blanched but bowed, understanding the chilling implication.

Daemon then turned his formidable attention to Kael. "The heir is being dealt with. Now, the empire. My blade hungers for new lands. Kael, what is the next conquest? Where do we strike to further my dominion, to bleed more wealth and manpower into my coffers?"

Kael, ever prepared, stepped forward, a new map unrolled before them. "My Lord, your scouts have returned with updated intelligence, especially now that Blackwood's lands are under your influence."

He pointed to a wealthy, independent trading town located north-east of Riverbend. "To the northeast lies **Havenport**. It's smaller than Silverport, but a bustling hub for overland trade, with a significant river connecting it to the interior. Its defenses are modest, mostly a reinforced palisade and a small, complacent militia. Its Mayor, a merchant prince, is known to be overly confident in his gold. It would yield significant immediate wealth and control of a vital trade artery."

Kael then moved his finger to a more isolated, heavily forested region. "Alternatively, to the far west, beyond Blackwood's borders, lies the **Fiefdom of Whisperwood**. Its lord is a reclusive ranger, ruling over scattered logging camps and a hidden silver mine. His forces are few but skilled in guerrilla warfare. Conquest would be a slow, bloody grind through dense wilderness, but the silver mine would offer a steady, strategic resource for forging coin and arms, a long-term gain."

He finished, meeting Daemon's gaze. "Both offer distinct advantages, My Lord. Havenport for immediate strategic trade control and quick wealth. Whisperwood for long-term strategic resources, but at a higher tactical cost."
