Astarion’s patience, thin as stretched membrane, finally frayed. The viewport was useless, the chamber barren of further secrets. His companions, bless their unobservant hearts, were still content to gape at the terrifying ballet of distant stars. *Fine. If this infernal vessel insists on being inscrutable, and my companions insist on being impatient, then I shall at least ensure I am ready for the next delightful surprise it undoubtedly holds.* The thought of another Mind Flayer, another ambush, sent a cold shiver down his spine, a testament to his high neuroticism. He wouldn't be caught off guard again.

He turned from the glowing consoles, his crimson eyes sweeping over the chamber one last time, not for hidden passages, but for tactical advantage. He adjusted the leather armor that clung to his slender frame, ensuring his two shortswords were loose in their sheaths, ready for a swift draw. His hand instinctively went to the longbow, a silent promise of precision and distance. He took a slow, deliberate breath, forcing the frantic thrum of the tadpole behind his eye into submission, forcing his mind to focus.

He mentally reviewed his newfound spells: *Hunter's Mark*, a predator's tool for isolating and eliminating a single threat; *Ensnaring Strike*, a vicious trick to bind a foe from afar. Both would be invaluable. He imagined them in use, the satisfying thud of an arrow carrying magic, the writhing vines of ensnared enemies. This was his art, his form of control in a world that constantly sought to strip it away. He hated the thought of what he was becoming, but he would embrace what it offered for survival. The air still hummed with the engine’s relentless pulse, a constant, sickening reminder that they were still hurtling through the cosmic void, but at least, for this fleeting moment, Astarion felt a modicum of control over his own immediate fate. He was ready.
