Detailed Explanation of Faction Claims and 'Own Time' Logic:

Your question touches on the core friction of being a double-agent in a high-scrutiny empire like Thay. Here is the breakdown of why the Black Company (and by extension, the state) would attempt to claim your current inventory, even if you are acting on your 'own time':

**1. The Legal Fiction of 'Own Time':**
In the Black Company's bylaws, you are an active-duty Sergeant. There is no such thing as 'off-the-clock' when it comes to capturing state enemies or valuable slaves. Any assets seized by a squad are technically Company property until they are 'processed.' If you return to the Obsidian Ziggurat with five collared rebels, Captain Reynolds will expect the standard 30% management fee (or the Zulkirs will expect their tax) regardless of whether you were on a specific contract or a personal stroll.

**2. The Silt-River Precedent (How you avoid the claim):**
You have already demonstrated the solution to this problem: **The Audit.** 
- In Silt-River, you harvested 40 souls and reported them as 'burned in the fire.' Because there was no physical inventory to count, the Company couldn't claim a cut of those souls. 
- You only paid the Company their cut on the 10 slaves you actually 'booked' and the Elara asset. 

**3. The Risk of the Old Mill:**
Because you are currently at the Old Mill without a specific contract, any 'inventory' (the 5 rebels) you keep alive becomes a liability to your cover. If you bring them back 'on-book,' the Company takes their cut. If you keep them 'off-book,' you must hide them or liquidate them before returning to base. This is why the planning block suggests 'Liquidating the Sentries' via The Mint—it converts physical inventory the Company can claim into DPP that only you and Nocturne can see.

**4. Why they 'come' to claim it:**
They aren't physically following you right now, but the Thayan bureaucracy is built on surveillance. If you sell these rebels to a wizard in Bezantur or try to stash them in a Company-owned cell, the ledger-keepers will notice. In Thay, 'un-audited wealth' is treated as treason against the Zulkirs.

**Summary:** You have the freedom to do whatever you want on your own time, but to keep 100% of the profit, you must ensure the 'secular' world believes there was no profit to be had. Convert them to Souls or Soul Coins, and the Black Company has nothing to claim.
