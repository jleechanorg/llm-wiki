Dunk looks down at his mismatched hauberk, his brow furrowing as if he's seeing the orange-red flakes for the first time. "It's old iron, Egg. Been through more rain than I care to remember."

"It's good iron underneath," you counter, sliding off the bench. You reach for the heavy, rusted shirt. It's a weight that would crush most ten-year-olds, but you handle it with a scholar's precision, finding the weak points where the patches meet. "My... I've seen it done. Dunk watches you, a slow, tired curiosity in his eyes. "Go on then, lad. If you've the stomach for the grime, I won't stop you. Just mind you don't rub a hole through it entirely."

You take the mail to a patch of sunlight by the door. Using a handful of gritty river sand and the remaining grease, you begin to work. It is mind-numbing labor, the kind of work the Red Keep's armorer would delegate to the lowliest apprentice, but your mind finds a rhythmic peace in it. You visualize the structural integrity of each link, using your sharp intellect to identify exactly where the friction is greatest. By the time you finish, the shoulder pieces gleam with a dull, honest grey, the structural rot halted by your meticulous care.
