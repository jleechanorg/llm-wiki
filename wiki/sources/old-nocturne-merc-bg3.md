## Summary
A D&D 5e Baldur's Gate noir psychological horror campaign set in 1492 DR during the refugee crisis following Elturel's fall. The protagonist Nocturne is a Level 6 College of Swords Bard — a philosophical evil mastermind and serial killer known as the "Serpent-King." She is the youngest bastard daughter of a powerful noble house, exiled for her cold, ritualistic sadistic practices and enjoyment of murder. The campaign begins in the Smirking Boar tavern cellar where she negotiates her first Guild contract: kidnapping a tiefling refugee family for a slaver. This is a darker, more explicitly evil campaign featuring the "Hunger" mechanic — a serial killing compulsion that provides mechanical bonuses when fed.

## Key Claims
- Nocturne is Level 6 College of Swords Bard with CHA 18, DEX 16, INT 16 — a supremacist intellectual who views people as "variables" and conversations as "conductions"
- The "Hunger" mechanic: she must kill a sapient humanoid (not children) to gain +2 to all six ability scores for one week; two weeks without a kill causes -2 to all scores
- Two primary masks: "Quiet Rat & Courteous Advisor" (default, musical voice, disarming gentility) and "Dispassionate Strategist" (win-win manipulation, puppet-master)
- The campaign is set against the backdrop of the Absolute cult's kidnappings, providing cover for Nocturne's own predatory activities
- She operates as a mercenary taking "contracts others won't touch" — a cover for her serial killing and power accumulation
