Location: Man Tibbet's Farm, Farmhouse Interior, Early Evening

The farmhouse main room was a tableau of frozen horror. Man Tibbet, his wife, and their three children stood utterly still, their faces slack with pure, uncomprehending terror, their eyes locked on Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16). Her presence, a silent, dark silhouette framed by the pantry archway, had extinguished the warmth of their domesticity, replacing it with an absolute, chilling silence. Her aura, a cold, unyielding pressure, had paralyzed them. They were not merely afraid; they were completely subdued, prime 'stock' for her lucrative contract.

Her EP was critically low, and her Channel Divinity expended, but her HP remained full. The family, simple farmers, posed no real threat. Her mind, a relentless engine of strategic thought, processed the scene with cold, precise efficiency. The objective was to secure them, swiftly and cleanly, maintaining the silence that was her hallmark. She would not waste time on intimidation, which could lead to unpredictable reactions. Direct, non-lethal action was the most reliable path.

Nocturne moved with the fluid grace of an executioner. Her Masterwork Longsword remained sheathed; it was unnecessary for this delicate task. She approached the burly Man Tibbet first, the perceived head of the household. His eyes, wide with primal fear, tracked her every movement. Before he could even begin to comprehend, Nocturne's hand shot out, a swift, precise strike to a pressure point at the base of his neck. His large frame stiffened, then crumpled, falling forward in a heavy, silent heap.

The wife let out a tiny, choked sob, but her limbs remained rigid. Nocturne pivoted instantly. Her hands, moving with unerring accuracy, found the same pressure point on the woman. She slumped unconscious to the floor beside her husband, her face still contorted in terror. The silence in the room stretched, broken only by the crackle of the hearth and the muffled, rapid breaths of the three children.

Nocturne's gaze settled on the children. The two boys huddled together by the table, their playful roughhousing replaced by a desperate, silent plea in their eyes. The small girl, clutching her wooden doll, seemed too shocked to even whimper. Her cold, pragmatic mind registered them as smaller, easier targets. One swift, non-lethal blow to each, and they too would be secured. Her objective was wealth, not cruelty; unnecessary force was inefficient.

She moved to the two boys, her movements a blur. They collapsed in quick succession, their unconscious forms resembling discarded dolls. The little girl, her eyes now brimming with silent tears, was the last. Nocturne approached her, her touch surprisingly gentle as she located the pressure point. The girl went limp, her wooden doll clattering softly to the floor beside her.

Five bodies, all silent, all unconscious. The room was utterly still, the domestic warmth now replaced by a chilling stillness. The operation was clean, precise, and efficient. The 'stock' was acquired. The harvest was complete.
