"Purity is a silent thing, Kagha," you whisper, your voice dropping into a low, harmonic reverb that seems to bypass her ears and vibrate directly against her skull. "It is not found in the chaotic pruning of branches. It is found in the deep shadows... where the roots embrace the dark to feed the tree. Why stain such a masterpiece with the blood of a child? Unless... you fear your own resolve is as fragile as she is?"

Kagha's breath hitches, a sharp, ragged sound. Her gaze darts to the Shadow Druid elders standing in the alcoves, then back to you. The subtle hint—the recognition of her secret alliance—hits her like a physical blow. The jagged righteousness in her expression cracks, revealing a flicker of raw, unadulterated panic beneath the fanatical mask. She isn't just resisting an outsider anymore; she is staring at a mirror that knows her darkest oath.
