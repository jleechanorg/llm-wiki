Location: Valerius's Study, House Arcanus Wing, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.

Valerius’s calm, detached observation felt like a blade of ice compared to Cassian’s burning accusation. It was a different kind of pain, one that bypassed the raw wound and went straight for the logical flaw. You saw the careful order of his study, the precise lines of his reports, and felt the immense weight of your own chaos. Your mother's death, your failure – it was a catastrophic anomaly in his perfectly ordered world.

"Valerius," your voice cracked, barely a breath. You felt the familiar sting of tears welling, but forced them back, knowing they would serve no purpose here. "I... I'm so sorry. About Mother. And... and about my failure." Your gaze dropped to his polished obsidian desk, unable to meet his eyes. "It was my fault. I know it. If I hadn't been so weak, if I hadn't broken under Mordan's torture, if I hadn't screamed... she would still be alive. The legend would still be here."

You gripped the edge of your mourning robes, your knuckles white. "I gave him the information he needed to trap her. My fear, my helplessness... it cost her everything. And now... now the Imperium has lost its queen. Our house... it's broken. Because of me." The admission was a familiar liturgy of self-condemnation, a truth you carried like a stone in your chest. You waited for his cold, surgical judgment, prepared to accept it as penance.

Valerius remained utterly still, his gaze fixed on your face, analyzing it with the same detached intensity he applied to a tactical map. There was no anger in his eyes, no overt grief, only a deep, almost clinical disappointment. He merely nodded slowly, a single, deliberate movement.

"Indeed, Sariel," he said, his voice flat, devoid of warmth or condemnation. "The data supports that conclusion. Your emotional response introduced an unpredictable variable. It was... inefficient." He pushed a data-slate across the desk towards you, its surface displaying complex military charts. "The cost was significant. Now, if you are quite finished with this... emotional review... perhaps you could attempt to comprehend the scale of the strategic vacuum her loss has created. Your intellect, at least, is still functional."

The words, so utterly devoid of empathy, were a fresh wound. They were not a personal attack, but a cold, factual confirmation of your deepest fear: that in his eyes, you were simply a flawed calculation, an inefficient variable that had destroyed the perfect system. You felt a chilling wave of desolation.
