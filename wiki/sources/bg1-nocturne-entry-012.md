"If the perimeter was porous enough for a hired knife to slip into the inner wards," you say, your voice cool and detached, cutting through his panic, "then the exterior is already compromised. They aren't just sending assassins, Gorion. They're laying a siege line. We aren't escaping; we're walking into a kill box."

Gorion stops, his hand gripping his staff until the knuckles turn white. He looks at you—really looks at you—and for a second, the 'fatherly' mask slips, revealing the battle-hardened Harper beneath. He nods, a grim appreciation for your assessment replacing his earlier frantic energy.

"You have your mother's mind," he murmurs, half to himself. "Calculated. Cold. Yes, you are likely right. The road will be watched. But staying here is a death sentence with no variables we can control. Out there... out there, I have power. I have prepared spells that can turn the tide of an ambush, provided we see it coming."

He straightens, his voice firming. "We move fast. We stick to the shadows. And if we meet resistance... you run. Do you understand? You do not fight. You run."

***

**Three Hours Later.**

The Lion's Way is a ribbon of mud winding through the gnarled oaks of the Coast Way. The night is thick with fog, damp and chilling. You have been walking for hours, putting Candlekeep behind you, but the feeling of being herded hasn't left you. Your enhanced senses pick up every rustle in the brush—a fox here, an owl there—but nothing unnatural. Yet.

Gorion stops abruptly, holding up a hand. The air suddenly grows heavy, static-charged with the scent of ozone and old iron.

From the shadows of the treeline, shapes emerge. Two towering figures—ogres, by the smell of them—flank the road. But they are insignificant. Your eyes lock on the figure stepping from the mist directly in your path.

He is a mountain of black steel. The armor is jagged, adorned with spikes and barbs that look like they grew from the metal rather than were forged. A helm with curved horns obscures his face, leaving only glowing yellow eyes visible in the dark slit of the visor. He radiates a pressure that makes the air hard to breathe—a malice so concentrated it feels physical.

"You can hide no longer," the armored figure booms, his voice a deep, resonant baritone that vibrates in your chest. "Hand over the ward, old man, and your death will be swift."

Gorion steps in front of you, staff glowing with blue arcane light. "You are a fool if you believe I would trust your mercy, Sarevok. Step aside."

The Armored Figure—Sarevok—laughs. It is a dry, humorless sound. "I am no fool. And you are out of time."
