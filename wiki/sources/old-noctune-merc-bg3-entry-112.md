Serpent Queen did not wait for the acknowledgment to fade. She stepped into the silence she had created, the resonance of the *Whispering Idol* still humming beneath her skin, lending her words the weight of edicts.

"The proposal is simple, Nine-Fingers," she began, her voice dropping to a register of absolute, terrifying calm. "The Guild has always struggled to ascend. You rule the shadows, the docks, the alleys. But the Upper City? That remains a fortress of stone and lineage, impenetrable to simple bribery or brutality."

She placed a single, gloved hand on the stack of deeds. "I am not offering you a heist. I am offering you a **franchise**."

Serpent Queen circled the desk slowly, her gaze never leaving the Guildmaster's. "Lyra Umbrage is mine. She is emotionally hollowed out and refilled with a desire for 'mastery' that I have defined. When her father falls—ruined by these deeds—she will step into the void. She will be the Patriar. And through her, I will rule House Umbrage."

She stopped, leaning in, her face inches from the light of the desk lamp, her beauty sharp and predatory. "Here is the partition of the world: You keep the Lower City. You keep the rackets, the smuggling, the blood on the cobblestones. That is your domain, and you are its undisputed queen. But the Upper City... the politics, the Patriars, the legitimacy... that belongs to me."

"I will be the Shadow Monarch of the highborn," she declared, the title tasting of iron and ambrosia. "I will filter their secrets to you. I will funnel their wealth to your coffers. I will ensure that when the Guild speaks, the Parliament of Peers listens. We do not serve one another, Keene. We serve the *equilibrium*. A partnership of equals. One hand in the gutter, one hand in the crown. And between us... we strangle the Zhentarim until they break."

Arvax stopped breathing. The audacity of the proposal sucked the air from the room.

Nine-Fingers Keene stared at her. For a long, agonizing minute, the only sound was the crackle of the hearth. She looked at the deeds, then at the freelancer who dared to carve up her city.

Then, she laughed. It was a dry, sharp sound, like parchment tearing.

"'Shadow Monarch'," she repeated, shaking her head with a look of disbelief that curled into a savage grin. "Gods, the arrogance. You walk in here with wet boots and stolen papers and demand half the city."

She stood up, planting her hands on the desk. "But you're right. We *can't* touch the Patriars. Not like this. And if you have the girl... if you truly have her..."

She extended a hand, missing a finger, calloused and strong. "I don't do 'partnerships' often, freelancer. They tend to end in knives. But for the Upper City? For a leash on the nobility? I'll make an exception. You run your play. You install your puppet queen. But know this: if you burn the Upper City down, I won't send water. I'll send executioners."

She gripped Serpent Queen's hand. "Deal. Welcome to the high table, 'Your Majesty'. Now get out of my office and win me a House."
