`Location: Outskirts of Greenmeadow Village, Secluded Copse`

The afternoon sun had climbed, then begun its slow descent, as Gladius emerged from his brief, intense period of rest. The quiet satisfaction of Corvan’s broken will still hummed beneath his skin, but now his focus sharpened on the next stage. A predator didn't strike blindly. He needed to see the prey in its natural habitat, observe its routines, find the cracks.

He moved with an almost ethereal silence, the Shadow-Step Boots a testament to their cost, even his heavy splint mail muted to a faint whisper against the marsh breeze. From the cover of distant trees and overgrown hedgerows, he watched Greenmeadow come alive with the mundane rhythm of peasant life. Farmers tilled fields, their movements slow and predictable. Children, small and vulnerable, chased each other near the well. He watched the Miller family’s farmhouse, noting the times they entered and exited, the small, frail woman tending her herbs, the sturdy men working the land. The Blackwood cottages seemed even more isolated, rarely showing signs of life outside a wisp of smoke from their chimneys. The Stonehands, near the village center, were loud, their laughter carrying on the breeze, their habits open and obvious.

*Simple. Weak. Predictable,* Gladius mused, a cold satisfaction settling in his gut. They were entirely unprepared for the hammer that was about to fall. The thought brought a grim smile to his lips. Their mundane existence, their petty worries, only served to highlight the vast gulf between their insignificance and his own burgeoning power. He committed their routines to memory, noting the few scattered lanterns that would flicker to life at dusk, the times the last fires died down.

As the last light bled from the sky, and the village gradually winked out its lights, Gladius retreated deeper into the shadowed copse. He waited. Hours crawled by, the world growing silent save for the rustle of leaves and the distant croaking of frogs. The initial impatience, a familiar itch in his blood, slowly transformed into a deep, primal calm. This was the hunter's wait, the patient anticipation of the kill. The air grew colder, damp with dew, and a sliver of moon offered just enough light to delineate the outlines of the sleeping village. By the time the last star was swallowed by the approaching dawn, Greenmeadow would be quiet, its families erased, and Lord Silvanus’s arrogance further punished by the unseen hand of 'The Hammer.'
