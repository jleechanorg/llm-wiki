The Blackwood cottage was utterly silent, save for the rhythmic, distant croaking of frogs from the marsh. Two more lives had been meticulously erased, their "fierce independence" no match for the chilling efficiency of Gladius's will. The Silent Strangler's Cord, now cleaned and re-coiled, was a comforting presence at his belt. A cold, quiet satisfaction settled in his gut; the raw, undeniable pleasure of absolute control, of bending the world to his precise, brutal desires. The air, thick with the scent of damp earth and stale wood, now also carried the faint, almost imperceptible tang of finality.

It was time for Corvan to earn his fear. Gladius moved to a grimy window, one that offered a clear sightline towards the distant Rusty Flagon, a dark, huddled shape against the barely brightening sky. He unlatched the wooden frame with a practiced, silent motion, just enough to create a sliver of an opening. With the utmost care, he shielded a small flint and steel in his cupped hand, ensuring no errant light escaped. A quick, sharp strike, and a single, brief spark flared, a pinprick of orange against the profound pre-dawn darkness. It lasted for only a heartbeat, then vanished as quickly as it appeared. A subtle flicker, easily dismissed by any casual observer, but a direct, undeniable command to the terrified agent waiting at the inn.

Gladius felt a subtle hum beneath his skin, a deep satisfaction. Corvan, the supposed envoy of a Lord, was now a mere extension of Gladius's will, forced to participate in the grim consequences of his master's arrogance. The thought of Corvan’s likely terror, his delicate hands now forced into the muck of cleanup, was a more potent indulgence than any fine ale or expensive food. It was the exquisite pleasure of subverting the powerful, of making them grovel in ways they never imagined. The dying embers of the shed fire still cast a faint, orange haze, a testament to the chaos he orchestrated, a silent signature.

The village was beginning to stir, the first roosters crowing, but the Blackwood cottage remained a pocket of profound, unnatural silence. Gladius's work here was done. The Stonehands awaited, the final family to be erased before the true light of dawn.
