Leaving the pristine, sun-drenched gardens of the Umbrage Estate, Shadow Monarch descended once more into the city's bowels. The transition was stark: from the perfumed air of the Patriars to the cloying humidity of the Undercellar, a subterranean labyrinth beneath the Blushing Mermaid where the Guild's true heart beat in darkness.

She navigated the maze of gambling dens and illicit parlors with the ease of a ghost, her path guided by the heavy iron key Keene had given her. It led her past the common thugs, deeper into the reinforced chambers reserved for the Guild's elite assets.

The 'Black Lotus' squad was waiting in a dimly lit ready-room that smelled of oiled steel and alchemical silence. There were four of them, stripping away the pretense of the tavern above. They didn't look like thugs; they looked like instruments.

**Vesper**, the apparent leader, was a drow female with eyes like polished flint, sharpening a dagger that seemed to absorb the light. Beside her sat **Gant**, a massive human whose stillness betrayed a monk's discipline, his hands wrapped in shadow-weave cloth. **Silas**, a half-elf with a longbow and the restless energy of a scout, paced the perimeter. The fourth, **Nyx**, was a tiefling warlock, idly manipulating a coin that vanished and reappeared between her fingers in puffs of sulfurous smoke.

They stopped the moment Shadow Monarch entered. No words were spoken. Vesper simply looked at the iron key in Shadow Monarch's hand, then up to her face. The assessment was mutual, cold, and professional.

"Keene's 'Monarch'," Vesper said, her voice a dry whisper. "We were told you'd have a target. We didn't expect..." She paused, her eyes narrowing as she sensed the palpable aura of power radiating from the sated Hunger. "...someone who looks like she could do the job herself."

Shadow Monarch stepped into the room, allowing the door to click shut. "A blade needs a hand to wield it, Vesper. And tonight, the target is the Zhentarim. The 'Wide Arc' shipment at Grey Harbor."

She walked the line, inspecting them. Their gear was masterwork, blackened and muffled. Their eyes held no fear, only a professional curiosity and a hunger for the coin that followed success. They were not loyal to her—not yet—but they were loyal to the hierarchy, and right now, she held the key.

"We are 'night-blades'," Gant rumbled, his voice like grinding stones. "We cut where we are told. But the Zhents... they travel heavy. Mages. Monsters."

"And you are the counter-measure," Shadow Monarch replied smoothly. "Tonight, you are not just killers. You are the surgical instruments of a new order. I will provide the opening. I will provide the distraction. You will provide the silence."

She gauged them. Vesper was the tactician; Gant, the muscle; Silas, the eyes; Nyx, the chaotic variable. It was a balanced unit, lethal and precise. They would serve.

"Sundown," Shadow Monarch commanded. "Grey Harbor. Western breakwater. Do not be late."

Vesper sheathed her dagger with a sharp *snick*. "We never are."
