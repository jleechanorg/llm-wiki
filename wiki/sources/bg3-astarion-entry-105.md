Astarion's lip curled, a thin line of grim determination. The crash site was a charnel house, best left to scavengers. That distant cry, however... a potential asset, or a fresh problem to be neatly disposed of. His high neuroticism demanded he know, demanded control over this new variable. "Beyond that plume of smoke," he drawled, his voice a low, sardonic rasp, "lies either salvation or a more interesting demise. Let's not dawdle."

He pushed forward, leading the way, his steps light and precise on the increasingly scorched earth. The air grew thicker with the acrid stench of burnt metal and ozone, mixing with the damp earth and pine. Ancient trees, splintered and charred by the Nautiloid's impact, lay scattered like colossal bones, a testament to unimaginable violence. His new Gloom Stalker abilities pulsed faintly, a cold comfort in the dim, ash-choked light filtering through the damaged canopy. He moved with the fluid grace of a true predator, his longbow held ready, his crimson eyes dissecting every shadow, every twisted root, for signs of threat or opportunity. The tadpole behind his eye squirmed, a constant, chilling reminder of his own precarious existence, urging him towards answers.

Lae'zel, a silent, grim shadow, followed without a word. Her golden eyes, fierce and unyielding, swept the ruined landscape with an unnerving intensity, her hand instinctively going to the empty space where her silver sword *should* be. Gale, meanwhile, let out a dramatic sigh, stumbling slightly over a piece of twisted Nautiloid debris. "Must we truly embrace this... *apocalypse*?" the wizard grumbled, his voice laced with disdain. "My head still rings with that infernal thrum, and the very air feels *wrong*." He cast a longing glance back at the relative calm of the overgrown cliffside.

But Astarion ignored them, his focus absolute. The cry, initially muffled, grew clearer, sharper with every step. It was unmistakably human, filled with a raw, desperate fear. Then, another sound joined it: a low, guttural snarl, followed by a frantic yelp. Not just a victim, then. A victim *in peril*. Ahead, through the thinning smoke, a clearing began to emerge, and within it, a scene of immediate, brutal conflict.
