Any recent changes to the perimeter, the shield grid, new patrols? The Flight Commander’s brow furrowed, his relief quickly replaced by a familiar anxiety. “That’s what everyone’s saying, Rogue Leader. It’s been… tense. Luke’s internal response was a surge of grim satisfaction. The commander’s words confirmed the precision of the Jedi Master’s intel. *They are patching holes that I will blow wide open,* Luke mused, a dark current of delight accompanying the thought. *Their struggle is futile. This pain is necessary to bring true order.* The faint, nagging pang of unease, a ghost of the boy who once clung to hope, tried to surface, but he crushed it, rationalizing it as a necessary step towards galactic peace, a regrettable but vital price for order.

Now, with a new insidious purpose, Luke leaned in. “Commander,” he began, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper, “that ion cannon you mentioned… a nightmare, you said? Even if it means stripping personnel from what seem like less critical internal sectors. Make it operational, make it a fortress. Divert all attention to it. It’s the only way to send a message.” His conviction was absolute, designed to prey on the commander’s fear and desire for security. The commander’s eyes widened, a flicker of desperate hope and new resolve taking hold.

Lyra, a bastion of the light, unknowingly sailing towards her doom. The trap was set. He was the bait, and the blade.
