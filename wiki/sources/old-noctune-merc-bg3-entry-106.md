Serpent Queen held up a hand, silencing the breathless runner. "A moment," she murmured, her voice calm despite the urgency. "Anticipation sharpens the appetite."

She retreated into the room, closing the door but leaving it unbarred. In the flickering lamplight, she withdrew *The Whispering Idol* from her pack. It was heavier than it looked, the wood warm to the touch, carved into a hooded figure whose face was a swirling void of grain. She focused her mind, enhanced by the sated Hunger, seeking to pierce the veil of its enchantment.

At first, there was only silence. Then, as her intellect probed the chaotic aura, a sound emerged—not a voice, but a resonance. It was the sound of a thousand breaths held in check, a chorus of suppressed desires. The idol did not speak; it *echoed*. It took the ambition of the holder and amplified it, projecting it outward like a psychic shockwave. It was a tool of command, meant to make the weak kneel before the strong.

She slipped the idol back into her pack, but the sensation lingered—a cold, electric aura of authority that clung to her skin. It was a useful edge for the lion's den.

Serpent Queen emerged, nodding to the runner. "Lead on."

The journey to the Guildhall was a blur of shadowed streets. As she walked, her mind touched upon the pieces she had set in motion. In the Upper City, **Lord Umbrage** slept on a foundation of sand, his wealth stripped, unaware he was already a pauper. **Lyra**, in her solarium, likely dreamt of the 'mastery' Serpent Queen had promised, her loyalty secured by a web of lies and longing. And **Rathmar**, the terrified Zhentarim, lay awake in his own bed, paralyzed by the threat of exposure, his eyes blinded to the estate he was meant to watch.

The Guildhall loomed, a fortress of stone disguised as a warehouse. The runner led her past layers of security that would have stopped an army, up stairs worn smooth by the boots of thieves and killers, until they reached the heavy oak doors of the High Office.

**Smiley Arvax** was waiting outside, pacing. He looked pale, his rictus grin twitching nervously. When he saw Serpent Queen, he visibly exhaled. "She's in a mood," he hissed, opening the door. "Don't... don't disappoint her."

The office was spacious, smelling of expensive parchment and old blood. Behind a massive desk sat **Nine-Fingers Keene**. She was smaller than the legends suggested, but her presence filled the room like a sudden drop in pressure. She was reading a document, her famous daggers nowhere in sight, which was far more terrifying than if she were brandishing them. She didn't look up as they entered.

"Arvax tells me," she said, her voice dry and sharp as a cracking whip, "that a freelancer has handed him the keys to House Umbrage. And that this freelancer has a... *proposal* regarding the succession."

She finally looked up, her gaze piercing, weighing Serpent Queen's soul on a scale of utility versus risk. "Speak. And be interesting. I have a city to run."
