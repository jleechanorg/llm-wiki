You leave the Guildhall, the pact with Nine-Fingers sealed in the smoky air of her office. The city outside is bright and oblivious, the midday sun gleaming on the white stone of the Upper City. You move towards the Umbrage Estate, not as a supplicant, but as the architect of its future.

The guards at the gate bow low. The servants scatter like leaves. You find **Lyra** in the solarium, surrounded by the trappings of her new Regency—ledgers, invitations, the heavy seal of her house. She looks up as you enter, her face lighting up with that desperate, devotional relief you have carefully cultivated.

"Monarch," she breathes, rising to meet you. "The rumors... people are whispering about a darkness on the river. A ship that never docks. Is it... is it the Zhentarim returning?"

"No, Lyra," you say, stepping close, your voice a low hum that vibrates in the quiet room. "The Zhentarim are ash. This is something new. Something useful."

You take her hands, guiding her back to the seat of power. You do not offer comfort; you offer clarity. It is time to peel back a layer of the illusion, to let her see the machinery of the world as you do—not to frighten her, but to bind her to the controls.

"There is a shipment arriving in three days," you reveal, your violet eyes locking with hers. "From Moonrise Towers. It carries a parasite. A weapon designed to enslave the minds of the Patriars, to turn the Parliament into puppets for a cult called the Absolute."

Lyra gasps, her hands tightening on yours. "We must stop it! We must tell the Fist, the Dukes..."

"We will stop it," you correct her, smoothing a stray lock of hair from her face. "But not yet."

You lean in, the *Echoes of the Idol* amplifying your presence until you are the only reality in the room. "If we destroy it quietly, Lyra, you are safe, but you are not *powerful*. The Patriars will never know how close they came to the leash. They will remain arrogant. They will remain independent."

A slow, cold understanding begins to dawn in her eyes. She is learning.

"We will let the fear take root," you whisper, tracing the line of her jaw. "We will leak the rumor of the assassination plot. We will let the other Houses tremble in their beds, wondering who can protect them when the Fist fails. And when their panic is at its peak... *you* will offer the shield."

"I... I will offer them protection?" she asks, her voice trembling with the magnitude of the ambition.

"You will offer them *us*," you confirm. "We will intercept the ship at the last moment. We will crush the threat publicly, undeniably. And you, the Regent of House Umbrage, will stand over the wreckage as the Savior of the Upper City. They will not just owe you favors, Lyra. They will owe you their souls."

You kiss her forehead, a benediction from a dark god. "This is the real plan, my queen. We do not just survive the storm. We sell the umbrellas."
