Morning (09:30:00) in the Northern Vanguard Camp—the cold mountain air bites through the wool of your cloak as you stand beside the command wagon. You look at Spartacus, then out at the jagged white teeth of the Alps, your posture shifting from the rigid discipline of a Tribune to the quiet, slightly slumped modesty of a man who carries a world he never asked for.

"We start with a bowed head, Spartacus," you say, your voice a resonant sliver of sound against the whistling wind. "The Suebi and the Cimbri do not fear Roman steel, but they loathe Roman arrogance. If we approach them with the heralds and trumpets of my father's house, they will see only a new master come to claim their forests. We must go to them as men who have bled for the same truth they cherish: that a life without chains is the only life worth living."

You gesture toward the wagon where Glaber is kept, though you do not pull the curtain. "We tell them we have captured a Legate of the Thirteenth—a man who has spent his life enforcing the brand. We tell them this not to boast, but to prove that the eagle can be plucked. But we keep him hidden, Spartacus. We do not display him like a trophy at a Triumph; that is the Roman way, and it tastes of the very vanity they despise. We let the knowledge of his capture be a secret strength—a promise that we have the power to protect the North, and the mercy to keep our vengeance in the dark."

You look up, your amber eyes catching a stray shaft of morning sun. "If the talk turns to bloodlines, I will speak of the ancient honor—the divine spark of Venus that was meant to illuminate the world, not burn it. I will offer them a pact of brothers, not a treaty of subjects. We show them the Sun, but we do not let it blind them."

Spartacus remains silent for a long moment, his dual blades resting against his thighs as he weighs the 20 on your Persuasion check. He looks at the camp, then back at you, a faint, grim respect softening the iron lines of his face. "A humble noble with a captive lord in his shadow," the Thracian muses. "It is a nuanced dance, Aurelius. But I have seen you lead the Spires through a throat of stone. If anyone can convince a Germanic king that a Caesar has come to set him free, it is the boy who shamed Glaber in his own atrium. We move at midday. Agron! Ready the scouts. We find the Suebi borders by sunset."
