**--- THINK ---**
The soft *thrum-thrum-thrum* of disciplined footsteps, growing louder, moving towards the study, resolved into the silent, armored presence of two Starfall Guard. *Only two,* I thought, my mind immediately assessing the optics. My request to Titus was for a "small, elite contingent," not a specific number. Is this the full extent of his concession? A minimal gesture of security for a disgraced princess, easily dismissed by the court? Or are more waiting outside, a silent, more substantial force, a testament to his unspoken trust?

The number matters. Two guards are symbolic. A true contingent, a foundation for my personal legion, requires more. It reflects Titus's current assessment of my "fragility," of my need for protection. It’s a political signal. If it's just two, it means I'm still perceived as weak, a liability to be contained, not an asset to be leveraged. If more are waiting, it's a greater commitment, a more valuable piece on my board. My immediate energy is better spent discerning the true scope of this new asset. My ultimate objective is unchallengeable military power, and that begins with understanding the pieces I've been given. The exquisite satisfaction of seeing my subtle manipulation bear fruit, of watching Titus's duty-bound grief lead him to assign instruments that will ultimately serve *my* will, is a potent lure.
**--- END THINK ---**
