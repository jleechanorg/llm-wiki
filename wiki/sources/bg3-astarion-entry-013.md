Your gaze tracks the Mind Flayers. They move with an unnerving, deliberate grace, their tentacles twitching as they tend to the engine. They seem utterly absorbed, their backs mostly to the captives, though their psionic presence still presses against your mind, a cold, alien hum of intellect. They aren't panicked; they are *working*. This suggests a goal beyond mere torment, a destination this vessel is relentlessly pursuing. You note their general positions, the pathways they favor, and the surprisingly open spaces between their grotesque forms and the various control panels and conduits. The Githyanki woman, still straining against her chains, seems to draw one’s peripheral attention momentarily, but it quickly returns to its task.

You mentally log the grotesque symmetry of the room, the fleshy conduits leading to unseen parts of the ship, and the odd, almost mechanical clicks that punctuate the organic groans. They aren't just torturing; they're *transporting*. And whatever they're transporting, or wherever they're going, it seems to demand their full, undivided psionic might.
