Astarion, his lips pressed into a thin line of calculated vexation, turned his back on the impatient glares of his companions. Their brutish eagerness to simply smash through walls was a testament to their lack of refined intellect. He needed *information*. The cosmic vista beyond the massive viewport was undeniably terrifying, a dizzying tapestry of swirling nebulae and distant, indifferent stars, but it was also a source of potential knowledge. If he could understand *where* this monstrous vessel was going, perhaps he could predict its next move, find a weakness, or even a way to seize control. The thought of being a helpless passenger, hurtling towards an unknown, illithid-ordained fate, clawed at his already frayed composure. The tadpole behind his eye throbbed, a cold, insistent pulse mirroring his growing desperation.

He approached the immense, crystalline membrane, his movements fluid and cautious. The light here was ethereal, bathing the grotesque, organic consoles in shifting hues of cosmic purple and blue. He ran a hand over the cool, strangely smooth surface of the viewport, feeling the faint vibrations of the ship's impossible speed. His eyes, usually discerning the subtle tells of a liar or the faint tracks of a mark, now strained to find meaning in the swirling chaos of deep space. He peered into the swirling vortex of a distant galaxy, searching for constellations he knew, for any celestial landmark that might offer a clue to their trajectory. There were no controls, no levers, no interface that spoke to his understanding of navigation. Only the sheer, overwhelming, indifferent grandeur of the cosmos.

He moved from one pulsating console to another, his fingers probing at their squelching surfaces, searching for a hidden button, a psionically locked panel, anything that hinted at a helm or a navigation system. The soft, wet sounds of the ship's internal mechanisms, the distant groans of the engine, were the only soundtrack to his desperate search. He could feel the cold hum of the Mind Flayer parasite specimen in his pack, a grim comfort. If only it offered an insight into *this* monstrous craft. He inhaled, the air still thick with the lingering scent of ozone and something disturbingly organic, pushing down the wave of nausea that threatened to rise. He needed control. He needed to understand.
