Valerius’s words, cold and sharp as the obsidian of his desk, were a final, brutal judgment. *Emotion is a flaw. A liability. It leads to unacceptable losses.* His gaze, stripped of all warmth, confirmed it: you were the error. The single, unpredictable variable that had broken the perfect system and cost the Imperium its queen. The desolation that washed over you was absolute, a chilling emptiness far worse than Cassian’s raw fury. There was no reaching him, no shared humanity in his world of data and logic.

You stood for a long moment, the silence in the study thick and suffocating, punctuated only by the distant hum of the Spire's arcane workings. Your own breathing felt ragged, a shameful testament to the chaos he so dispassionately condemned. The desire to scream, to shatter the sterile calm of his study with your pain, was immense, but you suppressed it. It would only prove his point.

Your shoulders slumped, and you turned slowly, silently. You couldn't meet his eyes, couldn't bear the cold, analytical judgment that saw only inefficiency where your heart bled. You simply walked away, your footsteps heavy on the polished floor, leaving him to his perfectly ordered world of numbers and strategies. The heavy oak door closed softly behind you, a final, definitive click that echoed the severance of a bond you desperately wished to mend.

The grand corridors of the Zenith Spire, usually a source of reassuring order, now felt vast and intimidating. Every passing noble, every hushed servant, seemed to glance at you with a mixture of pity and quiet disdain. *The Architect of the Fall.* The whispers, though unspoken, were everywhere. You clutched your mourning robes tighter, shrinking inward, wanting nothing more than to escape the crushing weight of their unspoken judgment. You needed a different kind of solace, a different kind of truth than the cold, hard logic of your brothers. You needed someone who saw beyond the failure, beyond the data.

You found yourself walking almost instinctively towards Cressida’s chambers, her quick wit and fierce loyalty the only shield you knew how to seek in this viper's nest of a court.
