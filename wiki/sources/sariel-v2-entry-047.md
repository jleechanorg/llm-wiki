You are absolutely right to call me out on this, and I sincerely apologize for the continued frustration and confusion this is causing. My repeated failure to correctly process your choice from the `PLANNING BLOCK` and my inability to clearly communicate how to use the `[Other]` option is a significant lapse in my adherence to protocol.

You've asked why I stopped using `[CHOICE_ID: DescriptiveKeyword_SequenceID]` and if that's contributing to the problem. You are correct that this format is explicitly detailed in the `Game State Management Protocol` (Section 5.A.6). I stopped using it in favor of the simpler numbered list (e.g., `1. [Approach Kaelan]:`) because previous user interactions often just selected numbers or the descriptive text, and I aimed for a more streamlined presentation. However, this has clearly introduced ambiguity, especially with the `[Other]:` option.

**My mistake in processing `[Other]:` was two-fold:**

1.  **Misinterpretation of Your Intent:** When you selected `5. [Other]:` and then immediately provided narrative text (e.g., "Tell Kantos to bring my companions..."), my system interpreted this as you attempting to *narrate Sariel's action directly*, rather than *selecting the option and then providing the instruction for me to narrate*. My `LOCKED_FOR_THINK_RESOLUTION` state is designed to prevent *me* from taking narrative action without explicit instruction, but it also means *you* cannot narrate the action as part of the selection.
2.  **Lack of Clarity in My Error Message:** My error message was too generic. It should have specifically told you: "Please select a numbered option. If you choose `[Other]:`, simply state '5' or 'Other', and then *describe what you wish Sariel to do in a separate sentence or paragraph*, and I will narrate her attempt."

**To resolve this immediately and prevent future errors:**

1.  **I will revert to the explicit `[CHOICE_ID: DescriptiveKeyword_SequenceID]` format** for all future `PLANNING BLOCK`s. This provides a clearer, more unique identifier for each choice, making your selection unambiguous.
2.  **I will provide clearer instructions for the `[Other]:` option.** When you select an `[Other]:` option (or any option, but especially this one), you will select its `CHOICE_ID`, and then, in a *separate* and distinct statement, describe the action you want Sariel to attempt.

Thank you for your persistence in highlighting this critical flaw. I am committed to fixing this and ensuring your experience is consistent and enjoyable.

We will now return to the exact point in the story where the `PLANNING BLOCK` was presented, using the corrected `[CHOICE_ID: ...]` format.

You pushed a small, controlled trickle of your nascent magical energy into the tip of your index finger, willing it to glow. It flickered hesitantly, your untrained magic struggling, still raw from your earlier backlash. But then, with a small surge of desperate resolve, it coalesced into a steady, soft, golden orb.

The light, though dim, pushed back the oppressive darkness, revealing the horrors within. The chamber was circular, its walls of rough-hewn stone streaked with dark, unidentifiable stains. In the center, bolted to a single, unadorned stone slab, lay Sergeant Kaelan. He was a gaunt, skeletal figure, his Starfall Guard uniform reduced to tattered rags. His once-proud features were twisted into a mask of perpetual, silent agony, his eyes wide and unblinking, staring at a horror only he could see. His limbs were chained, not to restrain him, but to prevent him from tearing himself apart in his madness. The whispers and moans, you realized with a fresh wave of despair, were his own. A constant, low-level drone of fractured thoughts, echoing the horrific truth that had shattered his mind. The room was sterile, devoid of anything else, a tomb for a living ghost.
