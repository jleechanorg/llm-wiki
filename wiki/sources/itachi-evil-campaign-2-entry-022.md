Let's break this down to clarify the `Destiny` ruleset's approach and Itachi's unique capabilities:

**1. Itachi's Level vs. Opponent's Levels:**

Your observation is astute and highlights a key aspect of how the `Destiny` ruleset, combined with Itachi's specific character concept, functions.

*   **"Level 1" for Itachi is not "Beginner":** In `Destiny`, a character's "Class Level" primarily represents their progression along *their own unique path of power and narrative influence*. For Itachi, who is established as a **prodigy of unparalleled talent** with **innate, extraordinary abilities (Sharingan, Mangekyō Potential)** and a **custom "Ninja" class optimized for assassination and powerful illusion/genjutsu from Level 1**, his starting "Level 1" is vastly different from a generic "Level 1" shinobi, or even a typical D&D Level 1 character.
*   **His Abilities are Front-Loaded and Potent:**
    *   His **Aptitude Scores** (Coordination 16, Intelligence 16, Wisdom 16) are exceptionally high for a starting character, giving him significant modifiers (+3 for all).
    *   His **Sharingan** grants him passive Advantage on Perception checks and makes him a master of stealth and genjutsu from the outset, providing Advantage on all Stealth rolls (via Expertise Tag).
    *   His chosen **Mangekyō ability, Tsukuyomi**, is a devastating Tier 2 equivalent ability, available at Level 1 (albeit with a Fatigue cost).
    *   His **Assassin Rogue features** are designed for silent, lethal strikes against unaware targets.
    *   "The Silent Edge" Chokutō provides a further +1 bonus to these precision strikes.
*   **Mechanics Support His Prowess:** When he takes actions like stealth, he's rolling d20 (Advantage) +3 (Coordination) + [any other bonuses], easily achieving totals in the high teens or twenties. His opponents, even Jōnin, have a base Perception modifier but may roll poorly, or their CN is simply overcome by Itachi's highly specialized and powerful early-game kit.
*   **The Narrative Goal:** The campaign premise explicitly stated that this Itachi **could solo the Uchiha Clan**. To make that narratively and mechanically plausible from the start, his character build was designed to be *exceptionally effective* at what he does best, even at his "starting point" of Level 1. His challenges will escalate rapidly to reflect his increasing renown and the ambition of his goals, as per `Destiny` Protocol 6.C (Leveling Tiers) and `Narrative` Protocol 6.B.3.c (Appropriate Challenge Level).

Essentially, Itachi is a "Level 1" on a scale that goes far beyond what a typical shinobi might ever achieve. He's already a significant threat, even to seasoned Jōnin, because of his unique, top-tier innate talent and specialized training that allows him to exploit weaknesses with surgical precision.

**2. Highest Level Shinobi in the Village:**

Given Konohagakure's structure and the `Destiny` ruleset's tiers:

*   **Jōnin (Elite Tier):** The Jōnin Itachi has been encountering (Levels 4-5) represent highly skilled, but still *standard*, elite shinobi. There would be numerous others of similar or slightly higher levels (up to perhaps Level 8-10) within the general Jōnin ranks and specialized forces.
*   **Council/Anbu Commanders:** Figures like Danzo Shimura, and the various Anbu commanders (like the one who leads the Root organization) would likely be **Tier 3 (Levels 11-16)**. They possess immense skill, strategic acumen, and significant influence.
*   **The Sannin & The Hokage:** The legendary Sannin (Jiraiya, Tsunade, Orochimaru) and the current Hokage (Hiruzen Sarutobi) would represent the absolute pinnacle of power in Konoha, falling into **Tier 4 (Levels 17-20)**. These are world-class threats/assets whose power and influence transcend the village and impact the entire shinobi world.

So, while Itachi is Level 1, he's effectively operating at a level of competency that allows him to outmaneuver and eliminate opponents who might be considered mid-tier threats (Jōnin) to a more conventional, lower-level group. As his Class Level increases, he will move into the tiers where he can genuinely challenge or manipulate the likes of Danzo, and eventually, even the Sannin and the Hokage, which aligns perfectly with his long-term goals.
