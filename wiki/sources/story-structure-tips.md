## Story Arcs for RPG Campaigns

WorldArchitect.AI generates stories dynamically, but you can steer the narrative by understanding classic story structures. Use these arcs as loose frameworks — the AI will fill in the details.

## The Five-Act Arc

Based on classic dramatic structure, adapted for RPG pacing:

1. **Setup** — Introduce the character, the world, and the initial situation. In WorldArchitect.AI, this is where you establish your character's voice and goals.
2. **Inciting Incident** — Something disrupts the status quo. A letter arrives, a town burns, an NPC is kidnapped. Give the AI a clear trigger.
3. **Rising Action** — Complications build. Sub-plots emerge. Faction allegiances get tested. The AI generates these dynamically — lean into unexpected twists.
4. **Crisis** — The climax approaches. Stakes are clear. Resources are low. In the World of Assiah, this is often where faction betrayals or celestial events intervene.
5. **Resolution** — The story concludes or pivots to a new arc. Campaigns are rarely a single story — use resolutions as launch points for the next chapter.

## Side Quest Patterns

Not every session needs a full arc. Useful side quest templates:

- **The Escort** — Protect someone or something from Point A to Point B. Simple but effective for showcasing the world.
- **The Mystery** — Something happened. Investigate, gather clues, confront the truth. Works especially well in the Nexus trade hub or Aeterna city scenes.
- **The Negotiation** — Two factions are in conflict. You are the intermediary. Diplomacy, threats, or charm determine the outcome.
- **The Heist** — Steal something from somewhere heavily guarded. Planning + improvisation + dice rolls.

## Pacing in AI Narratives

WorldArchitect.AI responds to your energy. Dense, descriptive prompts slow the pace and deepen the scene. Short, action-oriented prompts accelerate things. Mix both:

- **Slow scenes** — Use for NPC conversations, lore discovery, strategic planning. "I want to understand the full history of the Shattered Host before deciding anything."
- **Fast scenes** — Use for combat, chases, tense negotiations. "I sprint through the corridor, shoving anyone in my way."

## Using Dice Outcomes

Bad dice rolls are story gifts. A failed Persuasion check becomes an NPC who now distrusts you. A missed attack creates an opening for the enemy. The AI adapts to dice outcomes — don't hide from them.
