Game Master:
You retrieve the **Chain Mail +1** from the pile. The links are incredibly fine, shimmering with a faint, verdant light in the gloom of the armory. 

"Xan," you call out. The elf looks up from his cleaning, his face a mask of weary resignation. You toss the bundle of mail to him. "Put it on. If you're going to complain about impending doom, you might as well be dressed for it."

Xan catches the armor, his eyes widening as he runs a long finger over the weave. "Mithral," he breathes. "And woven with the enchantments of Evereska. This is Elven Chain. It breathes with the wearer... it will not hinder the somatic gestures of the Art."

He strips off his tattered grey robes and dons the mail. It fits him perfectly, clinking softly as he moves. He draws his Moonblade, the blue light reflecting off the silver links. For the first time, he looks less like a doomed scholar and more like a battle-mage of the elven courts. "I... I suppose this improves my odds of survival from 'none' to 'negligible'. Thank you."

With the party equipped, you turn your attention to the structure itself. The Armory is built of sturdy stone, defensible and dry. The rest of the camp is a smoking ruin, but this building can be held.

"We are not leaving," you announce. "We are digging in."

**Operation: Stronghold**

For the next several hours, you direct the fortification efforts with the ruthless efficiency of a siege engineer.

*   **The Walls:** **Kagain** and **Minsc** use their strength to drag heavy iron weapon racks and stone debris to the windows and the breached rear hatch, creating firing slits that offer heavy cover while blocking entry.
*   **The Perimeter:** You and **Roric** sweep the immediate area. You reset the bandits' own bear traps in the tall grass and string tripwires with bells across the approach paths. 
*   **The Wards:** **Dynaheir** uses chalk to inscribe glyphs of warning on the main threshold. "They will not pass unseen," she promises.

By sunset, the Armory has been transformed. It is no longer a loot cache; it is a Forward Operating Base. You have a secure location to rest, store supplies, and retreat to if the Cloakwood proves too deadly.

"A fortress!" Minsc declares, planting his boot on a barricade. "Boo says it needs a name! The Hamster Hold? The Butt-Kicking Bastion?"

"The Armory," you correct him flatly. "It keeps our steel dry and our enemies out."

You stand on the roof of your new stronghold, looking northwest toward the brooding canopy of the **Cloakwood**. The sun is setting behind the trees, casting long, spider-legged shadows across the valley. You have a base. You have an army. The hunt begins now.
