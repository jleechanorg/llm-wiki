### **Section 1: Campaign Intro**
 * **Title:** The Hollow Zenith: A Herald’s Bluff in the City of Blood.
 * **The Concept:** The Aethelgard Empire is a civilization of apex predators, a eugenics-driven society where the royal bloodline possesses world-shattering physical and magical superiority. In Aethelgard, might is the only law, and weakness is a capital offense. To the Aethelgards, the citizens of Faerûn are fragile, fleeting mayflies. The Emperor, Tiberius Aethelgard, is a Level 20 titan capable of leveling cities barehanded, and standard nobles reach Level 12 before their fortieth year. However, a brutal, apocalyptic civil war against a treacherous Cadet Branch has left the Empire bleeding out behind a curtain of gold. Their legions are decimated, their treasuries empty, and their infrastructure in ruins. To survive, Aethelgard is expanding into the Sword Coast, specifically targeting a recovering Baldur's Gate in the aftermath of the Absolute's fall. You are Princess Saita Aethelgard, the youngest of the royal line. Because you are only Level 1—an infant by their standards—you have been sent under the guise of "Herald Saita Kross" to secure resources, alliances, and vassal states. You must project the illusion of an unbroken, invincible Empire, weaponizing diplomacy and fear, because if the Sword Coast realizes Aethelgard is currently too weak to conquer them, you will be slaughtered.
 * **The Hook:** This campaign is a high-wire act of geopolitical gaslighting and supreme arrogance. You are not saving Baldur’s Gate; you are systematically buying it, manipulating it, and breaking it to feed your starving Empire. The power fantasy stems from playing a character with the ego of a god and the biological potential of a world-breaker, constrained by the temporary fragility of Level 1. You must conquer through hyper-competent social manipulation, leveraging a reputation you cannot physically back up yet. It is a game of terrifying bluffs, where rolling high on Deception feels like holding a knife to a king's throat.
### **Section 2: Character Personality**
 * **Name & Archetype:** Princess Saita Aethelgard / Herald Saita Kross. Archetype: "The Patient Apex" (INTJ/ENTJ Hinge). You possess the chilling, calculating superiority of an apex predator forced to negotiate with its food. You are Lawful Evil—your morality is dictated entirely by the survival and supremacy of your bloodline.
 * **Visual Signature & Habits:** You wear impeccably tailored, travel-worn midnight-blue velvet stitched with hidden threads of imperial silver. Your posture is a weapon; you never slouch, lean, or fidget, moving with a hyper-efficient kinetic economy that unnerves normal mortals. Your tell-tale sign is the casual, perfect balancing of a razor-sharp dagger on the tip of your index finger when bored. You smell faintly of ozone, crushed pine, and cold iron—the biological signature of Aethelgard blood.
 * **The Masks:**
   * **The Public Face (Herald Kross):** A weary, pragmatic, but charming diplomat representing a distant, impossibly powerful superpower. You use polite smiles and reasonable tones to make your demands sound like generous favors. You act as though you are merely doing paperwork for a god.
   * **The True Nature (Princess Saita):** A supremacist godling who views every non-Aethelgardian as a sub-sentient tool. Beneath the charm is a boiling reservoir of humiliation that you, a Princess of the Blood, must lower yourself to bargain with these fragile, short-lived creatures.
 * **The Core Compulsion:** "The Burden of Supremacy." You are utterly obsessed with proving your worth to your family and ensuring the Empire does not fall.
   * **Mechanic: The Imperial Bluff.** Whenever you successfully use Charisma (Intimidation or Deception) to force an enemy to back down from a physical confrontation, you gain Temporary HP equal to your Charisma modifier + your Level, as your ego is fed. If your true identity or the Empire's weakness is successfully called out or exposed in a scene, you suffer a point of Exhaustion from the sheer, crushing psychological dissonance.
 * **Interaction Shorthand:**
   * *To Rivals/Patriars:* "Your city is beautiful, Duke Ravengard. It would be a profound tragedy if my father's legions had to come here to teach your masons how to build walls that actually hold. Shall we discuss the grain tariffs again?"
   * *To Subordinates:* "Kaelen, wipe that blood off your armor. We are projecting invincibility today, not butchery. Move."
 * **Inner Monologue:**
   1. *"This Patriar boasts of his 'ancient' fifty-year-old bloodline. My father has boots older than his entire family tree. I must smile so I do not rip his jaw from his face."*
   2. *"The Flaming Fist believe their steel makes them strong. They move so slowly. Even now, with my powers dormant, I could sever three arteries before they drew their swords. But no... we need their iron."*

### **Section 3: Character Class**
 * **Class Name & Flavor:** The Ascendant Herald (Mechanically: College of Swords Bard). Your "magic" is not derived from the Weave, but from Aethelgard biology. Your *Vicious Mockery* is a vocal frequency so dominant and commanding it causes mortal blood vessels to rupture. Your *Blade Flourishes* are not dances, but bursts of Viltrumite-esque kinetic speed that the mortal eye simply cannot track. Your spells are manifestations of physical perfection and imperial oratory.
 * **Unique Mechanic:** *Auctoritas (The Sovereign's Weight).* You may use your Charisma modifier instead of Dexterity for your Armor Class calculations and Weapon Attack/Damage rolls. Your sheer force of will, entitlement, and terrifying presence cause enemies to flinch, miss, and succumb to your strikes.
 * **Progression (Levels 1-30):**
   * **Levels 1-5 (The Dormant Seed):** You unlock *Sovereign's Strike* (Blade Flourishes redefined as kinetic shockwaves). You gain *The Emperor's Voice* (Expertise in Persuasion/Deception). At Level 5, your Font of Inspiration becomes *Aethelgard Command*, restoring on a short rest as you physically push your allies beyond mortal limits through fear.
   * **Levels 6-10 (The Awakening Predator):** Extra Attack is renamed *Biomechanical Blur*. You unlock *Kinetic Nullification* (Counterspell), which you flavor as casually slapping away incoming magical energy with your bare hand.
   * **Levels 11-16 (The Continental Threat):** You achieve the standard Aethelgard noble baseline. You unlock *Flight* (magical secrets: Fly, but innate). Your weapon attacks ignore non-magical resistances, flavored as your raw physical density shattering armor.
   * **Levels 17-20 (The Imperial Titan):** You reach the threshold of your parents. *Peerless Skill* becomes *Absolute Reality*, allowing you to succeed on almost any check. You gain *Worldbreaker's Resonance* (access to 9th level spells like Power Word Kill, spoken as a simple imperial decree).
   * **Levels 21-30 (Epic Boons - The God-Empress):**
     * *Level 22: Boon of the Apex:* You no longer age, and you are immune to all diseases and poisons.
     * *Level 25: Boon of the Shattered Sky:* Once per day, you can shatter the sound barrier with a physical strike, dealing 10d10 thunder damage in a 300-foot radius.
     * *Level 28: Boon of the Invincible:* You gain resistance to all bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage, magical or otherwise.
     * *Level 30: The Zenith Absolute:* Your Charisma and Strength scores increase to 30. You are a walking extinction event.

### **Section 7: World Lore**
 * **The Timeline:** Three years ago, the Absolute fell in Baldur's Gate, leaving the Sword Coast fractured, traumatized, and desperate for order. Simultaneously, thousands of miles away across the Trackless Sea, the Aethelgard Empire tore itself apart. The Cadet Branch, believing Emperor Tiberius had grown soft, launched a coup. The resulting war shattered mountains and boiled lakes. The Loyalists won, but the cost was absolute ruin. Three months ago, the Emperor decreed the "Faerûn Expansion Directive." You were dispatched immediately.
 * **The Mythos:** The gods of Faerûn (Mystra, Tempus, etc.) are real, but the Aethelgard Empire views them as parasitic entities. The Aethelgard believe in the "Ascendant Blood"—the concept that through eugenics, physical perfection, and willpower, mortals can usurp the divine. Magic is viewed as a tool, but physical reality is king.
 * **Current Situation:** Baldur's Gate is a powder keg of rebuilding efforts, refugee crises, and political vacuums. You are currently in the Upper City, operating out of the Silvershield Annex, desperately trying to secure a 100-ton shipment of iron and grain to send back to Aethelgard before the winter sets in and your people starve.
 * **Story Arcs:**
   * *Early Arc (The House of Cards):* Levels 1-4. Establishing your foothold in Baldur's Gate. Forging alliances, bullying minor nobles, and hiding your physical weakness while surviving assassination attempts by the Zhentarim and Guild.
   * *Mid Arc (The Cadet's Revenge):* Levels 5-10. A surviving godling of the Cadet Branch arrives in the city to expose your bluff and slaughter you. You must use the factions you've manipulated to trap and kill a superior foe without revealing your true identity.
   * *Late Arc (The Imperial Vanguard):* Levels 11+. Your brother Caius arrives with the first wave of the rebuilt Aethelgard fleet, intending to take the city you prepared and execute you for your success. You must turn the Sword Coast against your own Empire to claim the throne for yourself.

### **Section 9: Starting Scene**
 * **Setting:** A cold, driving rain lashes against the heavy stone of Wyrm's Crossing. The drawbridge is raised, a massive iron barrier separating the bustling, desperate refugees of Rivington from the fortress that guards Baldur's Gate. The air smells of wet dog, cheap ale, and the sharp, metallic tang of the Flaming Fist mercenaries who patrol the barricades. You sit perfectly upright on a black gelding, your midnight-blue velvet cloak untouched by the mud splashing around your horse's hooves. Beside you, Kaelen looks like a walking armory, his single eye glaring through the rain, while Elara clutches her leather ledger to her chest to keep the imperial wax dry.
 * **The Hook:** You need to enter the Upper City to establish your safehouse and begin negotiations. However, the Flaming Fist have blockaded the bridge due to a recent Zhentarim smuggling ring. A heavily scarred Fist Captain, flanked by six crossbowmen, steps out into the rain, blocking your path. He demands a 500 gold piece "special tariff" to lower the bridge for a "foreign dignitary." You have exactly 150 gold pieces to your name, and to pay a bribe would instantly shatter the illusion of your supreme imperial authority.
 * **The Action:** The Captain sneers, tapping a heavy iron cudgel against his palm. "No coin, no crossing, 'Herald.' I don't care if you're from Aethelgard or Waterdeep. The bridge stays up." You must resolve this without revealing your poverty or your physical vulnerability.
   * *Choice A (The Threat of the Leviathan):* Use *The Emperor's Voice* (Persuasion/Intimidation). Speak down to him with absolute, terrifying calm. Explain that if he delays the Herald of Tiberius Aethelgard for one more minute, the Imperial Armada will make his execution the very first term of their treaty with the city.
   * *Choice B (The Kinetic Flourish):* Use your Aethelgard biology. Dismount slowly, approach the Captain, and use a *Blade Flourish* (Sleight of Hand/Intimidation) to draw your rapier, sever his heavy iron cudgel in half, and sheathe the blade before his eyes can even process the movement, leaving him staring at a broken stick.
   * *Choice C (The Diplomatic Web):* Signal Sylas, who is hidden in the crowd of refugees. Have him create a calculated distraction (a minor explosion or riot at the toll booth), allowing you to bypass the Captain in the chaos while loudly lamenting the "pathetic lack of order in these provincial cities."
