Astarion's eyes dart towards the still-occupied Mind Flayers, then back to the Githyanki. Her quiet, intense readiness is a stark contrast to the whimpering of other captives. A potential ally, however temporary. He slides off the pulsating slab, grimacing at the sticky residue clinging to his skin, and quickly closes the small distance between them. "Right, now that we're both free of our delightful hosts' 'hospitality'," he murmurs, his voice a low, sardonic rasp, "unless you fancy being a brain-snack, we need to move. Those... *things* are preoccupied, but they won't be forever. Any ideas on our grand exit? Beyond just, you know, smashing everything." He raises a brow, a flicker of his usual disdain for brute force showing.

The Githyanki woman, now fully upright, turns her head slowly, her golden eyes piercing. She assesses him for a moment, a creature of alien pride and a potent, barely contained ferocity. She doesn't waste breath on pleasantries or lengthy explanations. "Weapons," she states, her voice a low, guttural rasp, utterly devoid of emotion, yet ringing with steely resolve. Her gaze sweeps the gruesome, biomechanical chamber, fixing briefly on a discarded piece of illithid equipment nearby. "Then a way out. They bleed." Her meaning is clear: confront the Mind Flayers, if necessary. The simple declaration carries a weight of experience, a history of facing such horrors. Astarion feels a surge of unease—he prefers subtlety, but her conviction is undeniable. He also notes the slight clench of her jaw; she's eager to act.

"Bleed?" Astarion repeats, a faint, almost delighted smirk playing on his lips, though his heart thrums with a primal, terrified anticipation. "That's always a plus. Anything nearby that might facilitate said bleeding? I'd rather keep my skin intact, if possible, rather than serve as a pin cushion for their rather... *personal* attacks." He subtly gestures towards the Mind Flayers, who remain absorbed in their tasks, their tentacled heads still turned away. The hum of psionic energy from the tadpole in his head intensifies, urging him to act, to escape this hell.
