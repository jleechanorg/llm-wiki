## Summary

A D&D 5e campaign set in an alternate-history Roman Empire where Julius Caesar seized power early and became Emperor. The player character is Gaia Julia Caesar (disguised as Aurelius Varenus), the 12-year-old youngest daughter of the Emperor, posing incognito as a merchant's son. She is actually a Level 6 College of Swords Bard using the Miasma (Divine Blood of Venus) as her magic source. She has been sent to "fix" the mess created by incompetent Praetors during the Spartacus Revolt, with 50,000 gold, a squad of black-ops Praetorians, and a secret mandate.

## Key Claims

- The campaign diverged from real history when Caesar survived the Ides of March assassination attempt at 44 BCE
- Caesar is now Dictator Perpetuo (Emperor), age 60, obsessed with his own divinity
- Three sons compete for succession: Caesarion (East), Octavian (Rome administration), and Gaia (border wars)
- Gaia's public identity is Aurelius Varenus — a wealthy merchant scion dealing in grain and wine
- Her true nature is a College of Swords Bard channeling the Miasma (Divine Blood of Venus)
- Her attributes: STR 10, DEX 18, CON 14, INT 14, WIS 10, CHA 20 (maxed)
- She has five Praetorian Hand companions: Valerius (Shield), Felix (Ghost/Sniper), Titus (Butcher), Cassius (Surgeon), Brutus (Wall)
- Spartacus leads a revolt (the Third Servile War), and the campaign hook involves "fixing" Glaber's failure
