Kantos slowly closed the tome before him, the *thump* echoing loudly in the silent scriptorium. He adjusted his spectacles, his gaze seeming to peer into your very soul. "The past, Princess," he said, his voice dropping to a near-whisper, filled with a caution that was almost palpable, "is often more dangerous than the present. Some truths are... better left undisturbed."

His words were a wall, constructed of ancient fear and profound weariness. He was dismissing you, just as your brothers had, trying to send you back to the safety of ignorance. But Cressida's voice, *Your mind is the sharpest blade our house possesses. It is time to wield it,* echoed in your memory. You could not be dismissed. Not this time. You needed the truth.

Your gaze, direct and unwavering despite the tremor in your hands, met his. You leaned forward slightly, your voice dropping to match his conspiratorial whisper, adding a layer of deliberate, unsettling knowledge. "Some truths," you countered softly, "are simply hidden. Like the records of the 'Quiet Ward.' Or the files on the 'Starfall Patient.'"

The effect was instantaneous and profound. Lucian Kantos, a man who had navigated centuries of political and arcane treachery, froze. The spectacles on his nose slid down a fraction, unheeded. His eyes, usually sharp and discerning, widened behind the thick lenses, betraying a flicker of raw, unadulterated terror. A faint, almost imperceptible tremor ran through his ink-stained fingers, clutched tightly on the closed tome. He stared at you, his mouth opening slightly as if to speak, but no sound emerged. The air in the scriptorium crackled with the sudden, unspoken revelation that you, the grieving scholar, were far from as ignorant or as harmless as he had assumed.

He knew you had stumbled upon a secret, a dangerous thread connected to your mother's final, impossible victory. The old man, burdened by millennia of forbidden knowledge, looked at you not as a naive princess, but as a newly awakened threat, or perhaps, a terrifyingly new variable he had not anticipated.
