BALDUR’S GATE 3: THE AFTERMATH – A CAMPAIGN OF CHAOS & SURVIVAL
THE WORLD STATE: FATE OF THE LEGENDS
The Netherbrain is defeated, its psychic dominion shattered. But the saviors of Baldur's Gate did not liberate the city; they merely reorganized its chains. The "Triumvirate of the Absolute's Bane"—Astarion, Shadowheart, and Gale—have carved the Sword Coast into three distinct, terrifying empires of blood, shadow, and unbridled arcana. Remarkably, the apocalyptic battle against the Brain left almost all major figures alive, resulting in a city dense with legendary heroes and villains, all locked in a claustrophobic, high-stakes struggle for survival and supremacy.
The Playable Characters
Astarion (The Ascended Lord): Level 12+ Rogue (Thief) / Ranger (Gloom Stalker). Astarion completed the Rite of Profane Ascension. He rules the night from the rebuilt Szarr Palace, controlling the city’s economy, politics, and underworld. He wears the Bhaalist Armour and wields the dual blades Crimson Mischief and Bloodthirst, becoming a blur of piercing death. His vampiric spawn infest the Upper City, replacing the Parliament of Peers.
Shadowheart (Mother Superior of Loss): Level 12+ Cleric (Trickery Domain) / Paladin (Oathbreaker). She fully embraced Shar, usurping the House of Grief. She wears the Dark Justiciar Half-Plate and Viconia's Walking Fortress, wielding the Spear of Evening. She has plunged the Lower City into a brutal theocracy where memories are violently extracted in the Mirror of Loss, and dissenters are subjugated into the Sharran faith.
Gale of Waterdeep (God of Ambition): Level 12+ Wizard (Evocation) / Sorcerer (Wild Magic). Gale forged the Crown of Karsus anew, achieving a terrifying pseudo-divinity. He hovers above the city in a floating citadel carved from Ramazith’s Tower, wearing the Robe of the Weave and wielding Markoheshkir. He treats the populace as test subjects for experimental Netherese weaving, frequently mutating citizens or tearing holes in the fabric of reality.
Lae'zel (The Astral Scourge): Level 12+ Fighter (Battle Master). Having rejected Vlaakith, Lae'zel took to the Astral Sea with the surviving Orpheus. Wielding the Silver Sword of the Astral Plane and clad in the Helldusk Armour, she leads a relentless guerrilla war against the Lich-Queen. She occasionally strikes Baldur's Gate to steal Gale's magical resources to fund her rebellion.
Wyll (The Blade of the Resistance): Level 12+ Fighter (Champion) / Ranger (Hunter). Wyll broke his pact with Mizora and survived the ordeal, though stripped of his Warlock powers. Refusing to yield to the Triumvirate, he leads a desperate coalition of refugees and disbanded Flaming Fist in the Outer City. Wielding The Duke's Ravengard Longsword, he fights a grueling defensive war to protect the innocent from Astarion's blood taxes.
Karlach (The Iron Engine): Level 12+ Barbarian (Berserker) / Fighter (Champion). Karlach survived when the Gondians and Ironhand Gnomes temporarily stabilized her infernal engine using repurposed Steel Watch tech. Refusing to return to Avernus, she hides in the Undercity. Wielding Balduran's Giantslayer and wearing the Bonespike Garb, she acts as the heavy muscle for the anti-Gale resistance, burning with literal and metaphorical fury.
Minthara (The High General): Level 12+ Paladin (Oath of Vengeance). Minthara allied with Astarion, acting as his brutal military enforcer. She commands the elite "Night Watch," a heavily armored legion of spawn and Drow. She wears the Armour of Persistence and wields the Mace of the Believer, crushing any strikes, riots, or Sharran incursions that threaten Astarion’s absolute control.
Halsin (The Oak Father's Shield): Level 12+ Druid (Circle of the Moon). Halsin retreated to the Cloakwood, which is slowly being encroached upon by Shadowheart's shadow-curse. Wearing the Armour of Moonbasking, Halsin has erected a massive, permanent dome of natural magic, creating a sanctuary for those fleeing the city, though the ward drains his life force daily.
Jaheira (The Shadow General): Level 12+ Druid (Circle of the Land) / Fighter (Battle Master). Jaheira survived the political purge and reorganized the Harpers into a deeply clandestine network. Operating out of the city sewers alongside Nine-Fingers Keene, she coordinates precise, devastating strikes against all three members of the Triumvirate, refusing to let her city fall.
Minsc (The Stone-Breaker): Level 12+ Ranger (Hunter) / Barbarian (Wildheart). Minsc and Boo are the chaotic wrecking balls of the resistance. Operating in the Lower City, Minsc actively hunts Dark Justiciars and Vampire Spawn, smashing their operations with overwhelming brute force while giving loud, wildly inappropriate heroic speeches that make Jaheira's stealth operations incredibly difficult.
The Non-Playable Characters
Withers (Jergal): Disgusted by the chaotic, evil outcome of his intervention, Withers has abandoned the party and the city. He sits in a forgotten tomb, recording the inevitable collapse of the Sword Coast into the Book of the Dead, waiting for the dust to settle.
Raphael: Survived the battle in the House of Hope but lost much of his domain. He has relocated to a hidden manor in the Upper City, offering terrible, soul-binding contracts to desperate nobles and resistance fighters who need power to survive the Triumvirate.
The Emperor: Defeated but kept alive by Gale. The Emperor is trapped inside a magically reinforced Netherese jar, reduced to an organic supercomputer forced to calculate complex Weave equations to keep Gale's floating citadel airborne.
Orpheus: Survived the final battle, though deeply wounded. He remains in the Astral Plane, slowly regaining his strength under Lae'zel's protection, preparing to unify the Githyanki against Vlaakith.
Dame Aylin (The Nightsong) & Isobel: Both survived. They lead a desperate, mobile crusade of Selûnite faithful in the Lower City. Aylin's power is severely dampened by the permanent magical eclipses conjured by Shadowheart, and Isobel spends all her energy keeping Aylin hidden from Sharran hunter-killer squads.
Duke Ulder Ravengard: Survived the Iron Throne but was captured by Astarion. He is kept alive under heavy vampiric mind control, acting as a hollow puppet passing Astarion's draconian laws to maintain a veneer of political legitimacy.
Volo: Captured by Astarion and forced to become the Triumvirate's official propagandist. He is given luxurious quarters but is essentially a prisoner, writing glowing, entirely fabricated histories of their benevolence.
Zevlor: Survived the crisis and fully redeemed himself. He now fights alongside Wyll in the Outer City, commanding a hardened company of surviving Hellriders who protect the refugee camps.
Rolan: Survived and took over Sorcerous Sundries, but was forced into becoming a sycophantic apprentice to Gale. He manages the day-to-day magical extortion of the city, secretly passing information to the Harpers out of guilt.
Wulbren Bongle & Barcus Wroot: Both alive and leading the Ironhand Gnomes. Wulbren is obsessed with building a runepowder bomb large enough to vaporize Gale's citadel, while Barcus desperately tries to sabotage Wulbren's plans from the inside to prevent massive civilian casualties.
Arabella: Mastered her wild magic and grew into a formidable rogue-sorcerer. She leads a vast network of orphan thieves in the Lower City, actively sabotaging the Triumvirate and working with Jaheira.
Nine-Fingers Keene: Locked in a brutal, losing turf war with Astarion's spawn. The Guild has been decimated, but Keene survives in the deepest sewers, forming an uneasy alliance with Jaheira and Karlach.
Auntie Ethel: Thriving. The misery of the city has brought her endless desperate customers. She operates openly out of a swampy cavern beneath the city docks, trading favors for souls.
Yurgir: Broke his contract with Raphael and now works as an independent mercenary. The Orthon prowls the rooftops of the Upper City, taking high-paying assassination contracts from Astarion and Gale.
Tara the Tressym: Disgusted by Gale's descent into megalomania, Tara left him. She now lives with Arabella's street gang, providing them with magical trinkets and motherly advice.
Lorroakan: Stripped of his tower and his pride by Gale, Lorroakan survived the crisis. He is now a bitter, pathetic street-mage in the Lower City slums, desperately plotting to steal a fraction of Gale's Netherese power.
Ketheric Thorm: Stripped of Myrkul's favor after his defeat, Ketheric survived as a broken Oathbreaker Paladin. He wanders the Undercity in rusted armor, seeking to find and protect Isobel from afar, seeking a redemption she will not grant him.
Orin the Red: Survived the fall of the Absolute but lost the backing of the Bhaalist cult. She now operates as a chaotic, lone serial killer in the city, specifically targeting Astarion's spawn because their clean, bloodless kills "insult the art of murder."
Enver Gortash: Lost his political power and his absolute dominion. He escaped into the Outer City slums, operating as a grimy, black-market arms dealer. He is secretly trying to rebuild the Banite cult from the ground up using disgruntled Flaming Fist mercenaries.
Viconia DeVir: Defeated and usurped by Shadowheart, but managed to escape with her life. She is in hiding, leading a schismatic sect of Sharrans who view Shadowheart as a heretic, plotting to retake the House of Grief.
Cazador Szarr: Defeated but kept alive by Astarion. He is locked in a silver cage in his own former kennels. Astarion keeps him barely alive, draining his blood daily for personal amusement and psychological torture.
Alfira & Lakrissa: Both survived. They run the "Siren's Call," a heavily warded underground tavern in the Lower City that serves as the primary safehouse for Wyll's resistance. Alfira's songs are the only hope the refugees have left.
Omeluum & Blurg: Reside in the Underdark beneath the city. They actively smuggle medicinal supplies to Karlach and the Harpers, researching a way to dismantle Gale's Netherese machinery safely.
Roah Moonglow: Zhentarim leader, perfectly adapted to the new regime. She plays all three factions of the Triumvirate against each other, smuggling blood for Astarion, artifacts for Gale, and secrets for Shadowheart.
Lucretious: Moved her necromancy circus out of the city, finding the new regime "far too depressing and lacking in theatricality," though she occasionally returns to illegally harvest high-quality corpses.
SECTION 6: FACTIONS OF THE RUINED GATE
Noble Houses & Political Factions
House Szarr (The Ascendant Court): Leader: Ascendant Astarion. Specialty: Blood taxation, economic monopoly. The Vibe: Decadent, terrifying elegance. The Hook: They are deeply arrogant and underestimate mortal ingenuity.
The Parliament of Shadows: Leader: Minthara. Specialty: Military enforcement, martial law. The Vibe: Cold, unyielding fascist control. The Hook: Deep internal racial tensions between Drow and Vampire Spawn.
The Cloister of the Eclipse: Leader: Mother Superior Shadowheart. Specialty: Religious indoctrination, memory modification. The Vibe: Silent, oppressive despair. The Hook: Sharrans are highly susceptible to bright, sudden bursts of radiant magic which disrupt their stealth.
The Netherese Scholars: Leader: God-Emperor Gale. Specialty: Arcane experimentation, reality manipulation. The Vibe: Arrogant, detached scientific horror. The Hook: They rely entirely on magical infrastructure; anti-magic fields render them helpless.
House Ravengard (The Puppets): Leader: The mind-controlled Duke Ulder Ravengard. Specialty: Providing a veneer of legitimacy to Astarion. The Vibe: Hollow, fearful compliance. The Hook: Ulder's mind control can be temporarily broken with powerful emotional triggers or anti-magic.
The Iron Bank of Gond: Leader: A corrupted Gondian loyal to Gale. Specialty: Financing the Triumvirate's wars. The Vibe: Industrial, ruthless capitalism. The Hook: Highly vulnerable to sabotage of their physical vaults.
The Zhentarim Smugglers: Leader: Roah Moonglow. Specialty: Black market goods, poison. The Vibe: Opportunistic, cutthroat. The Hook: They can be bought. They have no loyalty.
The Guild (Remnants): Leader: Nine-Fingers Keene. Specialty: Information, assassination, sewer navigation. The Vibe: Desperate, cornered rats with teeth. The Hook: They need resources and healing; providing it earns absolute loyalty.
The Banite Syndicate: Leader: Enver Gortash. Specialty: Gun-running, extortion, structural sabotage. The Vibe: Grimy, organized mobsters. The Hook: Gortash is deeply paranoid and will turn on his own men if he suspects betrayal.
The Aristocracy of the Vine: Leader: Various terrified nobles. Specialty: Wine and agriculture production. The Vibe: Drunken denial. The Hook: They possess secret escape tunnels out of the city that they will trade for safety.
Friendly Factions (Tools for the PC)
The Outer City Resistance: Wyll and Zevlor's coalition of refugees and ex-soldiers.
Arabella’s Urchins: A massive network of child spies with wild magic talents.
The Ironhand & Gondian Alliance: Wulbren and Barcus's deeply divided engineers. Excellent for demolition.
The Selûnite Crusade: Dame Aylin and Isobel's holy warriors operating underground.
The Hidden Harpers: Jaheira's veteran spies and assassins.
The Undercity Brawlers: Karlach and Minsc's chaotic, heavy-hitting strike force.
The Cloakwood Enclave: Halsin's druids, providing a safe haven outside the city walls.
The Deep Gnome Merchants: Underdark traders (Omeluum and Blurg) who supply the resistance.
The Society of Brilliance: Blurg and Omeluum's expanded network of Underdark scholars. Excellent for translating Netherese texts and providing rare alchemical healing, though they are strictly pacifist.
The Viconian Schism: Viconia's rogue Sharrans, useful for distracting Shadowheart's forces.
The Waterdhavian Exiles: Wizards who fled Gale's madness, possessing deep knowledge of his weaknesses.

The Night Watch: Astarion’s elite spawn assassins.
The Dark Justiciars: Shadowheart’s memory-wiping inquisitors.
The Weave-Warped: Mutated citizens serving Gale as mindless elemental bombs.
The Orthon Mercenaries: Yurgir and his demonic hit squads hired by the Triumvirate.

The Shadow-Cursed Beasts: Monstrosities from the corrupted wilderness encroaching on the city.

The Sharran Infiltrators: Spies who steal memories to blackmail officials.
The Arcane Inquisitors: Gale's thought-police, led by Rolan, detecting unauthorized magic use.
The Iron Throne Remnants: Former guards and mercenaries of Gortash who refused to join his grimy street syndicate. They operate as heavily armored pirates in the Chionthar River, attacking supply lines.
SECTION 7: WORLD LORE
The Timeline: The Divergence Point occurred precisely when the Netherbrain fell. Instead of relinquishing their power, Astarion completed his ritual, Shadowheart embraced Shar completely, and Gale claimed the Crown of Karsus. The ensuing "Night of Three Thrones" saw the city's heroes turn on each other, eventually settling into a tense, three-way cold war, with the remaining survivors scattered into the shadows.
The Mythos: The Gods are largely silent, terrified by Gale's ascension. Magic is volatile; casting high-level spells risks tearing reality. The Shadowfell bleeds into the material plane at night, and vampires walk openly in the Upper City, shielded by permanent magical eclipses conjured by Shadowheart.
Current Situation: The Cold War is heating up. Astarion wants Gale's magic; Gale wants Shadowheart's control over the populace's minds; Shadowheart wants to plunge the entire city into the Shadowfell. The resistance factions are desperately trying to ignite a war between the three tyrants before the city is destroyed entirely.
Story Arcs:
Early: Surviving the streets, establishing a foothold, choosing a patron or remaining independent.
Mid: Dismantling the infrastructure of one of the Triumvirate members, triggering a full-scale turf war.
Late: Epic-level confrontation with god-like beings to claim the city or destroy it utterly.
SECTION 8: GAZETTEER & MECHANICS
Locations
The Szarr Upper City: The Vibe: Opulent, blood-soaked velvet. Hazard: The Blood Tithe (Constitution saving throws to avoid having blood forcibly extracted by localized magical wards).
The Sharran Lower City: The Vibe: Endless twilight, whispering shadows. Hazard: Memory Drain (Wisdom saving throws; failure means forgetting a spell slot or proficiency for 24 hours).
Gale’s Floating Citadel: The Vibe: Crackling ozone, geometric impossibility. Hazard: Wild Magic Zones (Every spell cast triggers a Wild Magic Surge).
The Undercity / Sewers: The Vibe: Putrid, desperate survival. Hazard: Aberrant Rot (Toxic environment requiring continuous healing or specific protective gear).
Custom Mechanics
The Triumvirate Threat Tracker: A 1-100 scale for each of the three rulers. As the PC does actions, their threat level rises. At 25, spies are sent. At 50, elite hit squads. At 75, a ruler intervenes personally via an avatar. At 100, the ruler unleashes a city-wide cataclysm to kill the PC.
The Sanity & Shadow System: Prolonged exposure to the city's warped reality causes "Shadow-Taint." For every week in the city without resting in a consecrated safehouse, the PC gains a permanent -1 to maximum HP, reversible only by Greater Restoration.
Relic Loot Table
The Caged Sire's Blood: Blood forcefully extracted from the imprisoned Cazador Szarr. Grants temporary vampiric regeneration but risks subservience to Astarion.
The Mirror Shard: A piece of the Mirror of Loss. Allows the user to erase one memory from a target's mind on a failed save.
Karsite Weave-Core: A battery of pure magic siphoned from Gale's engines. Can be consumed to cast Wish, but permanently burns out a spell slot.
The Nightsong’s Plume: A radiant feather willingly given by Dame Aylin. Sheds sunlight (damaging vampires) and prevents the user from dropping below 1 HP once per dawn.
The Emperor's Cipher: A piece of illithid technology stolen from Gale's prison jar. Grants telepathy and advantage against mind-altering magic.
Gortash’s Master Key: A mechanical device stolen from the arms dealer's stash that can temporarily override the remaining Steel Watchers.
The Shadow-Weave Cloak: Grants permanent invisibility in dim light or darkness.
The Crown's Fragment: A loose jewel from the Crown of Karsus. Grants a permanent +2 to any stat, but alerts Gale to your location.
The Astral Prism Shard: A crystalline fragment left behind by Orpheus. Once per long rest, the user can cast Mind Blank on themselves or an ally, rendering them temporarily immune to Shadowheart's memory drain and Gale's mental probing.
The Corrupted Blood of Lathander: The legendary mace, twisted by the city's dark ambient magic. It still sheds light, but the light deals 1d4 necrotic damage to anyone—friend or foe—caught within its radius, reflecting the bitter state of the divine.
PROTAGONIST OPTION 1: THE BLOOD-SCION
Tone: Lawful Evil Political / Gothic Intrigue Setting Concept: A high-society political thriller where you play a vampire spawn vying to overthrow your sire, Astarion.
Section 1: Campaign Intro
Title: The Crimson Ascendancy.
The Concept: You are Astarion’s most favored, yet most dangerous, creation. You are a true vampire, created in secret, possessing the strength to challenge the Ascendant Lord. Your goal is to navigate the cutthroat politics of the Upper City, dismantle his court, and claim the Szarr Palace for yourself.
The Hook: You are not a ragged survivor; you are apex predator royalty. The campaign is about maneuvering, backstabbing, and wielding immense wealth and dark seduction to conquer a city already ruled by monsters.
Section 2: Character Personality
Name & Archetype: Valerius Szarr (The Decadent Usurper - ENTJ).
Visual Signature & Habits: Valerius leaves a scent of expensive bergamot and coppery blood. His habit is meticulously adjusting his cuffs when someone is lying to him. He operates in dim, perfectly manicured candlelight.
The Masks: Public Face: The charming, aloof socialite who throws the best galas. True Nature: A cold, calculating sociopath who views mortals strictly as varying vintages of wine.
The Core Compulsion: "The Thirst for Dominance." Valerius must prove his superiority. Mechanic: When he forces a superior to submit (socially or physically), he gains 15 Temporary HP. If he backs down from a challenge, he suffers 1 level of Exhaustion.
Interaction Shorthand: To Rivals: Polite, venomous condescension. To Subordinates: Dismissive, transactional demands.
Inner Monologue:
"Astarion thinks he is a god because he ate a few thousand souls. He is merely a glutton. I am a connoisseur."
"The pulse in her neck is distracting, but her political connections are far more intoxicating."
"Power isn't taken by force alone; it's taken by making them beg you to hold the leash."
Section 3: Character Class
Class Name & Flavor: The Sanguine Aristocrat (Gestalt Rogue Mastermind / Bard College of Whispers). Power derived from pure vampiric bloodline and centuries of political conditioning.
Unique Mechanic: "The Blood Command." As a bonus action, command any creature with blood in its veins to halt, kneel, or attack. (Requires CHA save).
Progression (Levels 1-30):
Levels 1-5: "The Fledgling." Gains Bite attack, Shadow Stealth, Words of Terror.
Levels 6-10: "The Courtier." Gains Mantle of Whispers, Misdirection, immunity to charm.
Levels 11-16: "The Night Lord." Gains Soul of Deceit, Vampiric Flight, resistance to necrotic damage.
Levels 17-20: "The Usurper." Gains Shadow Lore, True Regeneration (10 HP/turn).
Levels 21-30: "Epic Boons." Boon of Immortality, Boon of the Night Hunter (immune to sunlight).
Section 4: Assets & Retinue
Status: High Lord of the Outer Ward.
Resources: 50,000 gp starting wealth. A heavily fortified vineyard estate outside the city walls. Blackmail ledgers detailing the illicit affairs of Minthara's generals.
The Panoply:
The Sanguine Rose: A masterwork rapier that heals the user for half the piercing damage dealt.
Cazador’s Signet: A ring stolen from the imprisoned Cazador, granting advantage on all Deception checks against other vampires.
The Velvet Shroud: A cloak that allows the user to cast Gaseous Form at will.
The Retinue:
Silas (The Bodyguard): Level 12 Fighter. A dominated half-orc. Devoted purely through magical compulsion. Stoic, brutal, silent.
Lysandra (The Spy): Level 10 Rogue. A human courtesan addicted to vampire bites. Loyal for the "high." Flirtatious, observant, deeply paranoid.
Kaelen (The Fixer): Level 11 Wizard. A necromancer who cleans up Valerius's messes. Loyal for funding and research bodies. Cynical, exhausted, brilliant.
Section 5: Family (The Viper's Nest)
The Parents: Astarion (Sire, Lvl 25 Ascendant) and Minthara (Step-Mother figure, Lvl 20 General). Astarion treats Valerius as a favored pet; Minthara views him as a tactical threat.
The Siblings:
Darius (Older Brother): The Brute. Mask: The Loyal Enforcer. Dynamic: Rival. He wants to crush Valerius to prove his physical superiority to Astarion.
Lucius (Older Brother): The Sycophant. Mask: The Devoted Son. Dynamic: Pawn. He is cowardly and can be easily manipulated to feed Astarion false information.
Seraphina (Older Sister): The Seductress. Mask: The Innocent Belle. Dynamic: Ally (for now). She controls the brothels and trades secrets with Valerius.
Vespera (Older Sister): The Zealot. Mask: The High Priestess of Blood. Dynamic: Rival. She believes Astarion is a literal god and views Valerius’s ambition as heresy.
Section 9: Starting Scene
Setting: The Grand Ballroom of the Szarr Palace. Crystal chandeliers drip with magical, cold light. The room smells of roasted meat, spilled wine, and terror. The mind-controlled Duke Ravengard sits blankly in a corner. Mortal nobles dance frantically, knowing if they stop, they become the main course.
The Hook: Valerius’s older brother, Darius, has just publicly executed one of Valerius’s key mortal spies on the dance floor, leaving the bleeding corpse as a direct insult. Astarion is watching from his throne, waiting to see how Valerius reacts.
The Action:
A: Draw The Sanguine Rose and challenge Darius to a first-blood duel in front of the court, establishing dominance.
B: Charm the terrified nobles into swarming Darius, using them as a meat shield to humiliate his brute strength.
C: Smile, step over the corpse, and loudly compliment Darius on his "blunt, unimaginative handiwork," turning the court's opinion against his lack of elegance.
PROTAGONIST OPTION 2: THE HERETIC
Tone: Grimdark High Fantasy / Theocratic Rebellion Setting Concept: You are a rogue Sharran Inquisitor who has discovered the light of Selûne, fighting a brutal shadow war against Shadowheart's regime alongside the exiled Viconia and the hunted Dame Aylin.
Section 1: Campaign Intro
Title: The Breaking of the Eclipse.
The Concept: You were Shadowheart's most trusted interrogator, but a slip of the mind allowed you to experience a true, unadulterated memory of love and light. You have turned your back on the Lady of Loss. Your goal is to shatter the Mirror of Loss, overthrow Shadowheart, and restore sunlight to the Lower City by protecting Dame Aylin.
The Hook: You are fighting a guerrilla war of the mind and soul. You possess the very secrets of the enemy, turning their own brutal inquisitorial tactics against them to liberate a hopeless populace.
Section 2: Character Personality
Name & Archetype: Elara Vane (The Penitent Executioner - ISTJ).
Visual Signature & Habits: Elara smells of ozone and crushed incense. Her tell-tale habit is rubbing a crescent-moon scar carved into her palm whenever she lies. She prefers absolute silence and operates in pitch darkness.
The Masks: Public Face: The relentless, emotionless Sharran loyalist. True Nature: A deeply guilty, desperately hopeful protector of the innocent.
The Core Compulsion: "Absolution through Action." She must save those she previously condemned. Mechanic: When she takes damage to protect an innocent, she gains Advantage on all attacks next turn. If she ignores suffering, she cannot regain hit points until she completes a long rest.
Interaction Shorthand: To Rivals: Cold, clinical, analytical. To Subordinates: Protective, stern, demanding perfection.
Inner Monologue:
"The Mother Superior preaches loss, but she hoards power like a dragon. Hypocrisy is the true religion here."
"The shadows are comfortable, but the light is necessary. I will burn my own eyes out if it means seeing the sun again."
"I remember every face I wiped from the Mirror. Their ghosts are my only companions."
Section 3: Character Class
Class Name & Flavor: The Lunar Inquisitor (Gestalt Paladin Oath of Vengeance / Cleric Light Domain). Power derived from a hidden, furious spark of Selûne's divine wrath.
Unique Mechanic: "The Moonsilver Smite." When hitting an enemy, expend a spell slot to deal Radiant damage that also strips the target of all invisibility, magical darkness, and memory-altering buffs.
Progression (Levels 1-30):
Levels 1-5: "The Awakened." Gains Divine Smite, Warding Flare, Zone of Truth.
Levels 6-10: "The Shadowbreaker." Gains Aura of Protection, Corona of Light, immunity to blindness.
Levels 11-16: "The Silver Blade." Gains Improved Divine Smite, Sunburst, resistance to psychic damage.
Levels 17-20: "The Lunar Avatar." Gains Avenging Angel, Mass Heal.
Levels 21-30: "Epic Boons." Boon of the Silver Fire (ignore radiant resistance), Boon of True Sight (permanent truesight 120ft).
Section 4: Assets & Retinue
Status: Exiled High Inquisitor.
Resources: A hidden sanctum beneath a ruined Selûnite temple shared with Isobel. A cache of smuggled holy water. The true names of ten high-ranking Sharran operatives.
The Panoply:
The Penitent's Chains: A spiked chain weapon (reach, 1d10 slashing) that grapples and silences spellcasters on a critical hit.
Armor of the Dawnbringer: Half-plate that constantly sheds dim light (can be suppressed) and heals the wearer for 1d6 when struck by necrotic damage.
The Glass Eye of Selûne: Replaces a lost eye, granting the ability to see through magical darkness perfectly.
The Retinue:
Garrick (The Heavy): Level 10 Barbarian. A former Flaming Fist driven mad by Sharran torture, healed by Elara. Loyal out of gratitude. Reckless, fiercely protective, simple.
Nia (The Hacker/Thief): Level 11 Rogue. A street urchin who specialized in stealing Sharran holy symbols. Loyal for gold and the thrill. Sarcastic, nimble, untrusting.
Brother Tomas (The Healer): Level 9 Cleric. A terrified, hidden Selûnite priest working under Isobel. Loyal out of faith. Cowardly but morally steadfast.
Section 5: Family (The Viper's Nest)
The Parents: Shadowheart (Mother Superior, Lvl 20+) and Viconia DeVir (The Exiled Matriarch, actively plotting to manipulate Elara to destroy Shadowheart).
The Siblings: (Orphans raised together in the Cloister, treated as blood siblings).
Caleb (Older Brother): The Fanatic. Mask: The Perfect Disciple. Dynamic: Rival. He genuinely believes in Shar's peace and views Elara as a cancer to be excised.
Jaxon (Older Brother): The Torturer. Mask: The Bureaucrat. Dynamic: Pawn. He enjoys inflicting pain but cares nothing for dogma. He can be bribed with rare cruelties.
Lyra (Older Sister): The Whisperer. Mask: The Confessor. Dynamic: Rival. She uses mind-reading to blackmail the entire city and wants Elara dead to secure her own power.
Maeve (Older Sister): The Broken. Mask: The Zealous Guard. Dynamic: Ally. Maeve's mind was fractured by the Mirror. Elara can use her fragmented memories to find secret paths.
Section 9: Starting Scene
Setting: The Docks of the Lower City, shrouded in a permanent magical fog. The water laps against the wood like a dying heartbeat. Three Sharran Justiciars have cornered a family of refugees who attempted to smuggle supplies to Wyll's camp.

# BALDUR’S GATE 3: THE DOLLHOUSE PARADOX
## THE CAMPAIGN SETTING & PROTAGONIST PROFILE
**Name:** Saita (A chosen designation. In the East, she was the *Hollow Crown*, the *Ash-Bringer*, the *End of Arguments*.)
**Apparent Age:** 16 (Fragile, wide-eyed, perpetually terrified)
**True Age:** 18 (Cold, apathetic, ancient in experience)
**True Level:** 20 Gestalt (Fighter 20 / Wizard 20)
**Apparent Level:** 1 (Divination Wizard)
**Alignment:** Lawful Evil (Playing Neutral Good)
### PART I: THE PSYCHOLOGY OF THE WINNER (THE ASHLANDS)
To understand Saita, one must understand the silence of the East. She was not born a god; she was born an anomaly in a world entirely unprepared for her.
**The Awakening (Age 10): The Physics of Superiority**
Magic in Faerûn is typically a language one must study—a complex negotiation with the Weave. Saita did not negotiate. At ten years old, the Weave simply deferred to her. When a local magistrate attempted to seize her family's land, Saita didn't cast *Fireball*; she looked at the magistrate and decided the kinetic energy in his blood should stop moving. He froze solid in mid-sentence.
She wasn't taught combat. She simply perceived the mathematical vectors of physical force. A master swordsman swinging a blade at her was like watching a leaf fall; it was slow, predictable, and irrelevant. By her eleventh birthday, she had accidentally killed her tutors, her local lords, and the continent’s preeminent archmage, simply because they got in her way and she didn't know her own strength.
**The Five-Year Tyrant (Age 11-15): The Consumption Algorithm**
What followed was not a war. A war implies a contest. What Saita executed was a five-year administrative restructuring of a continent via localized apocalypse.
 * **Age 11:** She incinerated the capital of the ruling empire because they sent assassins after her.
 * **Age 12:** She dominated the minds of the continent's standing armies, marching them into the sea to prevent further rebellions.
 * **Age 13:** She dismantled the economy, hoarding all magical artifacts in a single, unbreachable fortress.
 * **Age 14:** She executed anyone who remembered how the old world worked, ensuring her new regime had no ideological rivals.
 * **Age 15:** The continent was silent.
She won. Completely. The eastern continent was a perfectly optimized, frictionless machine of absolute obedience. And it was the most excruciatingly boring thing she had ever experienced.
**The Existential Void (Age 15-16)**
Saita sat on a throne carved from the melted swords of a hundred kings, looking out over a perfectly subjugated population, and felt nothing. Evil, she realized too late, is a consumption algorithm. It consumes rivals, it consumes allies, and eventually, it consumes the narrative itself. When you kill everyone who disagrees with you, you are left in an echo chamber of terrified sycophants.
She had cleared the server. There were no more boss fights. There were no more quests. There was no *game*. For a year, she ran thought experiments. *What if I had been merciful? What if I had made friends? What if the struggle is the actual point?* She didn't know. She had never struggled. So, at sixteen, the most powerful being on the material plane abdicated a continent-spanning empire simply because she was bored. She boarded a merchant vessel, suppressed her world-ending aura, and sailed west to the Sword Coast.
### PART II: THE ECOSYSTEM THEORY (THE MASQUERADE)
Saita arrived in Baldur's Gate as the Absolute crisis was reaching its climax. She watched the Netherbrain fall. She watched the Triumvirate—Gale, Astarion, Shadowheart—carve the city into pieces. She could have killed all three of them in six seconds. Instead, she sat in the mud and smiled.
This place was a mess. It was chaotic, loud, and inefficient. It was *perfect*.
**The "Good" Strategy**
Saita realized that being a tyrant ends the story. Being a "Good Hero," however, is sustainable content generation. Good characters don't salt the earth; they leave villains alive to plot revenge. They make friends who get kidnapped, generating rescue missions. They engage in complex moral dilemmas that keep the mind sharp.
She adopted the persona of "Saita," a traumatized 16-year-old orphan who just wanted to help. She chose to pretend to be a Level 1 Divination Wizard because it allowed her to "accidentally" predict ambushes or solve puzzles without revealing her raw destructive power.
**Method Acting the Victim**
This is not merely a disguise; it is an obsession. Saita needs the masquerade to be flawless, or the illusion of stakes shatters.
 * **The Saitama Defense:** Because she is Level 20, she does not wear armor. A goblin’s arrow cannot pierce her skin. Therefore, when an archer shoots at her, she must actively use her superhuman reflexes to throw herself backward into the dirt, screaming, pretending the arrow grazed her.
 * **The Fake Tears:** When a companion takes a grievous wound to protect her, she weeps real tears. Not out of empathy, but out of profound, artistic appreciation. She is watching a masterclass in human desperation, a feeling she has never genuinely possessed.
 * **The "Accidental" Magic:** When she needs to kill a monster, she pretends to panic, waving her wand randomly while subtly casting a 9th-level spell without verbal or somatic components, making it look like a wildly lucky *Fire Bolt*.
### PART III: THE FAKE EXP SYSTEM & MECHANICAL WITHDRAWAL
Saita is mechanically capped at 355,000 EXP. Nothing on the Sword Coast can provide her with "Real EXP." But the human brain craves progression. Saita is deeply, psychologically addicted to the *placebo* of leveling up.
**The EXP Ledger**
Hidden in her "wizard spellbook" is a meticulous ledger where she tracks the party's EXP. She treats this fake progression with terrifying reverence.
| Threat Encountered | Real EXP Gained | Fake EXP Gained | Saita's Reaction |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Goblin Ambush** | 0 | +200 | She claps her hands, genuinely thrilled by the "progress." |
| **Shadow-Cursed Shambling Mound** | 0 | +1,800 | She hugs the party tank, weeping with joy at the massive payout. |
| **Surviving a Triumvirate Hit Squad** | 0 | +4,500 | She spends the evening obsessively planning what "new spell" she will learn next level. |
| **Killing a Triumvirate Member** | 0 | 0 | She actively prevents this. If Astarion dies, the questline ends. They are content, not targets. |
**The Symptoms of Withdrawal**
Because her true progression is stalled, Saita exhibits manic behavior regarding her fake progression:
 * **Inventory Hoarding:** A true Level 20 warlord doesn't care about a *+1 Shortsword*. Saita treasures it. She polishes it every night. She acts like it is Excalibur because, in the "game" she is playing, it represents a hard-earned reward.
 * **Forcing Encounters:** If the party tries to stealth past a sleeping troll, Saita will "accidentally" snap a twig. She *needs* the troll's EXP. She needs the numbers to go up.
 * **The Phantom Level-Up:** When the party hits a milestone, Saita vibrates with genuine adrenaline. It is the only time her mask slips into pure, unadulterated fanaticism.
**The God-Eater Paradox (Level 21)**
There is only one way for Saita to gain Real EXP: she must consume a cosmic entity. A God. A Level 21+ being.
She knows Gale of Waterdeep is forging the Crown of Karsus. She knows he is attempting to ascend. Saita is not trying to stop him. She is watering him. She is pruning his enemies. She is farming a God, waiting for the exact moment he breaches Level 21 so she can harvest him and finally feel the rush of a true Level-Up.
### PART IV: THE DOLLHOUSE (THE COMPANIONS)
Saita views her party not as friends, but as incredibly complex, fragile toys that she must maintain to keep her game running.
**Gale of Waterdeep (The Crop)**

 * *The Choice:* Does she eat Gale, hit Level 21, and realize she is just as bored as she was three years ago? Or does Gale offer her something she hasn't considered—a partnership, an eternity of shared godhood, breaking her EXP lock through synthesis rather than consumption?
