The Fixer's eyes linger on the ledgers, but you don't offer them for inspection yet. Instead, you slide the heavy leather-bound books toward Vaximus. The scholar’s ink-stained hands are shaking, but as your gaze locks onto his, he finds a sliver of the old noble spine he used to serve. 'Vaximus,' you say, your voice like silk over stone, 'read the fine print. Ensure their "bleach" doesn't wash away our family's specific rights. I want no hidden clauses, no Zhentarim liens, and certainly no "administrative fees" tucked into the sub-text.'

Vaximus nods frantically, pulling a jeweler's loupe from his pocket to pore over the Fixer's proffered documents. The Fixer watches, his expression unreadable, as you turn your attention back to him. You don't just want the manor; you want the Network to pay for the privilege of using your tunnels.

'A clean title is a start,' you lean in, the ember-glow of your palms pulsing with a predatory light. 'But the Zhentarim are moving into a prime artery of the Upper City. That kind of real estate demands more than just legal paperwork. I require a detachment of Network "security" stationed at the perimeter—on your coin—and a monthly stipend of five hundred gold to cover the "upkeep" of the tunnels you'll be frequenting.'

The Fixer's thin lips twitch. He looks at the dead guards, then back at you. 'You have a bold tongue, Nocturne. But we are fixers, not a charity. The legal cleansing of a massacre is already a high-cost service. We will provide the security—discreetly—but the stipend is out of the question. You keep the manor and its secrets; we keep our gold. That is the only accord on the table.'

Vaximus looks up, his eyes wide. 'The deeds... they're clean, Nocturne. No hidden clauses. It’s a total restoration of the Sunder-Stone name. But we need to sign now. I can hear the Fist at the gate.'
