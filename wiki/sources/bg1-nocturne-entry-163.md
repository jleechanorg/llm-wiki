Game Master:
You sit at the quartermaster's desk, the heavy oak scarred by knife marks and spilled ale. You pull a sheet of parchment from Venkt's stolen ledger and dip a quill in ink. The *Hunger* demands expansion, and a fortress without a garrison is just a tomb waiting to be robbed.

You write with sharp, angular strokes:

*"Garrick. The Bandit Camp is ours. The leadership is dead. The Armory is secure. Take the gold I left you. Hire mercenaries—loyal ones, not trash. Buy supplies. Come to the Wood of Sharp Teeth. Occupy the Armory. Rename it. Hold it. - N."*

You roll the parchment tight and seal it with candle wax, pressing the hilt of *Varscona* into the soft red puddle to leave a mark of frost.

You ascend to the roof of the blockhouse. The night air is filled with the cawing of corvids feasting on the dead camp. You focus your will, casting **Speak with Animals**.

A large raven with intelligent, bead-black eyes lands on the parapet, tilting its head.

"Fly," you command, the magic turning your words into a series of clicks and croaks the bird understands. "South. Nashkel. The loud nest with the warm light. Find the man in the bright rags who smells of fear and lute-polish. Give him this."

You tie the scroll to the bird's leg. "Go."

The raven launches itself into the night, a black arrow aimed at the heart of Amn. The message is sent. It will take Garrick at least four days to receive the message, hire a crew, and travel here. The Fort is yours, but it is empty.

"The bird flies," **Dynaheir** observes, stepping out of the shadows. "But a raven cannot hold a wall. If we leave now, scavengers will reclaim this place before your bard arrives. If we wait... the trail to the Cloakwood grows cold."
