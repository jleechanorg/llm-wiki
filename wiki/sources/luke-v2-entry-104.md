The air in the interrogation chamber was thick with the lingering psychic residue of **Roric Vane’s** breaking. He sat restrained, utterly still now, his eyes wide but vacant, fixed on **Luke Skywalker** with a horrifying, unwavering loyalty. The pervasive scent of ozone and the subtle tang of fear filled the sterile room. Luke, his own senses sharpened by his newly embraced Dark Side, felt the palpable shift in the Rebel operative's Force presence – no longer defiant, but a pliant, empty vessel waiting to be filled.

Luke stepped closer, his gaze dissecting the man before him. Roric’s body was physically unharmed, but the mental conditioning had carved a chasm in his spirit. He probed gently with a Force tendril, confirming the absolute, unyielding nature of the new loyalty. There was no flicker of resistance, no hidden resentment, only a chilling, perfect obedience. *He is a blank slate,* Luke mused, a cold satisfaction settling in his core. *A perfect instrument. His suffering here prevents greater suffering for the galaxy under the Rebellion’s chaos. A few lives for true order.* A faint, fleeting pang of what might have once been empathy for the man's shattered mind surfaced, but Luke ruthlessly suppressed it. 

“Roric,” Luke commanded, his voice a low, resonant growl, imbued with the full, unyielding authority he now possessed. “Tell me everything about the Rebel base. Its defenses, its access points, its command structure, its personnel. **The Jedi Master** stood silently beside Luke, her own violet eyes keenly assessing Roric, a perfect mirror of Luke's strategic focus. Luke felt a wave of cold approval from **Darth Vader's** distant presence, confirming the success of his work. Roric was not just loyal; he was a living, breathing datapad, ready to be plundered.
