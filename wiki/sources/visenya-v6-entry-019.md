## Summary
The "Shattered Compass" arc — Visenya's systematic dismantling of Robb Stark's identity — reaches its logical conclusion. Robb no longer sees himself as a Stark heir but as Visenya's "Warden" — a title granted by the Dragon, not by blood. His lie to Ned about the Wolfswood hollow has become an existential commitment; every lie is another brick between him and his heritage.

## Key Events
- **The three weeks of Winterfell subversion** have transformed Robb from a boy with "unearned confidence" into a hollowed-out monument of a boy
- He moves through Winterfell "with the rigid, glazed eyes of a man walking a tightrope over an abyss"
- Every time Visenya's hands brush his in a corridor, or she offers a secret look across the high table, "the stress lines in his posture vibrate with the weight of the Wolfswood hollow"
- He is "now functionally addicted to the 'fire' you provide — the only warmth in a world he now views as a cold, judgmental cage of stone"
- **The lie**: He lies to Ned with "increasing, agonizing fluency, and each lie is a new brick in the wall between him and his heritage"

## Character Notes
- **Robb Stark**: The Shattered Compass — his Stark identity is a compass pointing only to Visenya
- The arc subverts the traditional "honorable Northern heir" — Visenya doesn't win his heart; she mathematically dismantles the "Stark Honor" that defines him
- The arc involves forcing Robb to make "increasingly 'dishonorable' choices to protect your secret, eventually making him realize he loves the 'predator' more than the 'Lady'"
