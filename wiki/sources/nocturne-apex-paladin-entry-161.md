Location: House Valerius Estate, Lord Cassian's Private Study, Morning

The study air, heavy with unspoken power, thrummed with the weight of Lord Cassian Valerius's (Age 55) challenge. His eyes, cold and demanding, bore into Nocturne's (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) own. "Prove it. Show me. Not merely your claims, but the irrefutable evidence. If your intelligence is as profound as your arrogance, then perhaps... *perhaps* House Valerius will find a use for your unique talents. But first, the data. All of it." His voice was a steel gauntlet thrown.

Nocturne's expression remained utterly unreadable. He demanded proof, and she possessed it in abundance. To delay now, to argue, or to offer more rhetoric, would be inefficient, perceived as weakness. Her objective was to assert her indispensability, not to engage in a battle of wills over a point already won. The time for subtle threats and veiled words was over; now was the time for overwhelming, undeniable facts.

She reached into her Bag of Holding, her movements deliberate, almost ceremonial. First, the worn **Deed for Tibbet Farm** and the **Letter from House Durin's Folly**. She laid them precisely on the polished desk. "This farm," she began, her voice low and even, "is not owned by Tibbet. It belongs to Durin's Folly. And it is not a farm."

Lord Cassian picked up the deed, his brow furrowing as he recognized the Valerius land it once abutted. He glanced at the Durin's Folly crest on the letter, a flicker of suspicion crossing his face.

Next, the **Man Tibbet's Ledger**, opened to its hidden entries. "Their true business," Nocturne continued, pointing to the arcane payments, "a clandestine outpost for surveillance, funded through proxies. A network that managed to escape your and Julian's notice."

His eyes widened almost imperceptibly as he scanned the detailed records, the meticulous payments for "Security Contingency" and "Arcane Oversight." His grip tightened on the ledger. The implication of such a long-term, hidden operation, so close to the city, was a palpable shock.

Then, Nocturne placed the three perfectly spherical **Orbs of Scrying** on the desk. They pulsed faintly with arcane energy. "Their tools of observation."

Lord Cassian stared at the orbs, his jaw tightening. These were expensive, potent artifacts, proof of deep-seated espionage. His gaze hardened, a grim understanding dawning.

Finally, the most damning evidence. Nocturne brought forth the lead-lined box, revealing the **Intricately Carved Obsidian Shard** and the **Ciphered Journal**. She placed the **Cipher Key** beside it. "This farm is a nexus, Father. The shard is a catalyst, the Darkwood Casket – " she produced the small, malevolent box, setting it gently beside the obsidian – "a conduit. For rituals." Her voice dropped to a chilling whisper. "Rituals tied to ancient death cults. For harvesting individuals with rare bloodlines. And for strengthening the reach of **'The Architect,'** whose whispers orchestrate this. He plans a **'Convergence'** in the **Underdark, three nights from now.** These maps," she concluded, laying down the **Topographical Maps of the Shadowfell** open to marked locations, "are their routes. This is not a political maneuver, Father. This is a threat to the very foundations of Baldur's Gate."

Lord Cassian's mask had utterly shattered. His face was pale, his eyes wide with a mixture of shock and cold fury. He looked from the chilling evidence spread across his desk to Nocturne, a profound, dangerous recognition now clear in his gaze. He understood. He understood the scale, the immediate danger, and the depth of House Durin's Folly's treacherous ambition. He understood that his exiled daughter had just delivered a truth that none of his trusted agents had even glimpsed.
