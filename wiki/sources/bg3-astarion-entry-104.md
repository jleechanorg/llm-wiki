The colossal Nautiloid, a grotesque, flaming ruin, continued to groan and collapse into the scorched earth, its unholy thrum finally fading. Astarion's senses, sharpened by a lifetime of urban cunning and his new Gloom Stalker affinity, had picked up two distinct paths: a purposeful scorched trail leading east, and a distant, muffled, *human* cry. His pragmatic mind, though repulsed by the chaos, immediately discounted the direct wreckage search. That was a job for scavengers, not survivors like himself. The cry, however... that was a different matter. A potential asset. Or a fresh danger to be dealt with. His high neuroticism demanded he know, demanded control.

"A cry," Astarion drawled, his voice a low, sardonic rasp, laced with a cynical weariness. He turned from the smoking wreckage, his gaze sweeping the faces of his weary companions. "How utterly predictable. Some poor, unfortunate soul. Or perhaps, something *else* mimicking distress." He didn't wait for their comments, his eyes already fixed on the direction of the sound, a flicker of cold calculation in their crimson depths. "It's to the east, beyond that plume of smoke. Let's see what fresh horror awaits us. Perhaps it will be useful. Or at least, provide a more interesting demise than simply starving in this wilderness."

Lae'zel, despite her bleeding arm, gave a curt, almost eager nod. "A potential threat," she rasped, her golden eyes already scanning the damaged treeline, her hand instinctively going to where her silver sword *should* be. Gale, meanwhile, let out a dramatic sigh, rubbing his temples. "Must we? My head is still quite thoroughly addled, and I much prefer my suffering to be of a more... *intellectual* nature." He grumbled, but followed, his gaze still periodically drifting to the distant, plummeting remnants of the Nautiloid.

Astarion moved with a fluid, predatory grace, his longbow held ready. The ground here was a mangled testament to the crash: scorched earth, splintered trees, and strange, pulsating organic debris from the ship. The air grew thicker with the acrid scent of ozone and burnt metal, mingling with the damp earth and pine. The faint cry, still muffled, seemed to pull at them, a siren call into the unknown. He pushed down the wave of profound fear that whispered of unseen terrors. This was a new hunt, and Astarion would lead.
