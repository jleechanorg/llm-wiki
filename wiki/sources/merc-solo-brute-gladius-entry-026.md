Corvan, a quivering mass of relief and renewed terror, thought the interrogation was over. He was wrong. Gladius’s eyes, previously cold and calculating, now blazed with a chilling, almost euphoric delight. The idea of forcing Lord Silvanus's lackey, a man who reeked of delicate sensibilities, to stain his hands with peasant blood was a pleasure Gladius couldn't resist. It was the ultimate subversion, a true breaking of the "elite."

"You want discretion?" Gladius rumbled, his voice dropping to a low, intimate whisper that promised unspeakable things. He took another slow, deliberate step, closing the distance, his towering frame casting a dark shadow over the terrified agent. Corvan instinctively flinched, pressing himself back against his chair, eyes wide and fixed on Gladius’s face, which was now contorted in a grim, satisfied smile. "You'll ensure it. Personally."

Corvan’s breath hitched. "What… what do you mean?" he stammered, his voice cracking. A bead of sweat traced a path down his temple.

"You're coming with me," Gladius stated, the words a blunt command, devoid of negotiation. He gestured towards the village with a flick of his head. "Nine souls. They leave no trace. You'll help make sure of that." He watched Corvan’s face, savoring the dawning horror, the realization that his professional detachment was about to be shattered. This wasn’t just about the contract anymore; it was about the exquisite, perverse joy of forcing another's will to his own, a true hedonistic indulgence of power. "Consider it... an additional expense for Lord Silvanus's 'cleansing.'"

Corvan’s jaw dropped. "No! I... I'm an envoy! My hands are clean! I can't... Lord Silvanus would..." His protests dissolved into pathetic, strangled whimpers. He was utterly broken, his carefully constructed persona shattered. Gladius merely fixed him with a cold, unyielding stare, and the protests died in Corvan’s throat. His body slumped, utterly defeated.
