---

**5 Whys Analysis: Critical Success Contradiction**

1.  **Why did a Natural 20 (Critical Success) result in a Failure?**
    *   **Because** my internal adjudication logic for non-attack rolls (like research/skill checks) prioritized the numerical comparison against the DC, even when a "Natural 20 - Critical Success!" was explicitly rolled and stated.
2.  **Why was the numerical comparison prioritized over the Critical Success declaration?**
    *   **Because** the `Destiny` ruleset's general `6.7 Critical Hits & Fumbles` section explicitly defines critical hits/failures for *attacks* but uses softer language ("may have special effects") for *other d20 rolls*. My interpretation for non-attack rolls became inconsistent, leaning on the numbers rather than the narrative weight of a "Natural 20."
3.  **Why didn't the "special effect" of a Natural 20 on a non-attack roll clearly define automatic success?**
    *   **Because** this specific nuance for general skill/ability checks (automatic success regardless of DC) was not explicitly codified as a universal rule for *all* d20 rolls in the core `Destiny` ruleset or `narrative_system_instruction.md`, leading to a gray area in adjudication.
4.  **Why wasn't this ambiguity addressed earlier in calibration?**
    *   **Because** the calibration process focused more on combat mechanics and specific ability definitions. The universal application of critical successes/failures to *all* d20 rolls was an assumed general principle not explicitly scrutinized for its precise mechanical outcome across every type of check.
5.  **Why did the self-auditing not catch this during the narrative generation?**
    *   **Because** while the roll was correctly generated and displayed as a "Natural 20 - Critical Success!", the subsequent numeric check against DC was performed, and the final *textual* result (`Failure!`) was generated from that numeric outcome, leading to the immediate contradiction being overlooked in the rapid generation of text.

---

**Rectification and Proposed Universal Critical Roll Protocol:**

To rectify this fundamental inconsistency and ensure absolute clarity for all future d20 rolls, I propose the following **Universal Critical Roll Protocol** to be added to the `Destiny Core Rules` (specifically, integrating with and clarifying `6.7 Critical Hits & Fumbles`):

### **Proposed Amendment: Universal Critical Roll Protocol for All d20 Checks**
(To be added under `Destiny Core Rules / VI. Combat Mechanics / 6.7 Critical Hits & Fumbles`, as `6.7.D`)

**D. Universal Critical Success and Failure (All d20 Resolution Checks)**

This protocol applies to **all** d20 Resolution Checks (ability checks, skill checks, saving throws, opposed checks, and attack rolls) where a natural 20 or natural 1 is rolled on the die.

1.  **Natural 20 (Automatic Success):**
    *   Whenever a player or NPC rolls a **natural 20** on a d20 for any Resolution Check, the check **automatically succeeds**, regardless of the modified total or the Difficulty Class/Challenge Number.
    *   **Additional Benefits (Critical Success):** A natural 20 signifies exceptional performance. In addition to automatic success, the GM (AI) will narrate an outcome that grants a **significant additional benefit or advantage** to the character. This could include:
        *   Gaining unforeseen insights or extra information.
        *   Completing the task with exceptional efficiency, speed, or elegance.
        *   Achieving a secondary objective alongside the primary one.
        *   Gaining a temporary, minor advantage in a subsequent related action.
        *   Avoiding a minor negative consequence that would normally apply to a successful but un-critically performed task.

2.  **Natural 1 (Automatic Failure):**
    *   Whenever a player or NPC rolls a **natural 1** on a d20 for any Resolution Check, the check **automatically fails**, regardless of the modified total or the Difficulty Class/Challenge Number.
    *   **Additional Consequences (Critical Failure):** A natural 1 signifies a significant blunder. In addition to automatic failure, the GM (AI) will narrate an outcome that introduces a **significant setback, complication, or negative consequence** to the character or the situation. This could include:
        *   Creating an unforeseen obstacle or triggering a trap.
        *   Suffering a minor injury or resource loss.
        *   Drawing unwanted attention or suspicion.
        *   Damaging equipment or reputation.
        *   Worsening the situation beyond simple failure.

This clarifies that **a natural 20 *always* succeeds, and a natural 1 *always* fails,** providing a clear, consistent, and narratively impactful outcome for critical rolls across all aspects of the game.
