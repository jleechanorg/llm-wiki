The cool night air was a crisp balm against Nocturne’s (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) face. Her short rest, a calculated necessity, had replenished her EP and Channel Divinity, restoring her to full operational capacity. The six bundled forms of her captives lay nearby, silent and secured in their temporary concealment. Kaelen’s initial assessment had been a gross miscalculation. "Easy pickings" had transformed into a complex infiltration of a House Durin's Folly outpost, complete with professional guards, an arcane ward, and chilling evidence of Shadowfell operations. This significantly elevated the risk and effort on her part. Such incompetence in intelligence demanded a higher price.

She drew a small, lead-weighted stone from a hidden pocket in her garb, its surface smooth and cold. This was her pre-arranged contact signal for Kaelen, a method known only to a select few in the Shadow Weavers' network: a specific sequence of taps against a particular tree at the edge of the forest, indicating a secure drop point message. She moved with purpose, leaving the hidden captives behind, heading to the designated tree a short distance away.

Her fingers moved with precise, deliberate taps against the rough bark. Three short, a pause, two long, another pause, then one short. A coded pulse that would, in time, reach Kaelen. Once the signal was sent, she carefully placed a small, rolled piece of parchment into a hidden crevice beneath the tree's roots – a dead drop. The urgency of "delivery" and the precise doubling of the fee, coupled with the non-negotiable demand for transport, would speak directly to Kaelen's and his employers' pragmatism. She would brook no argument. Her efficiency was valuable, and now they would pay its true price. Kaelen would understand. He feared her ruthlessness, and that fear would be her greatest ally in this negotiation.
