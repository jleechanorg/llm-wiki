The Hook Horror, a grotesque, multi-limbed nightmare, lay shattered and inert, its black ichor pooling on the spongy floor. The corridor, bathed in the sickly yellow glow of the living walls, settled into an unsettling silence, broken only by the relentless thrum of the Nautiloid's engine. Astarion felt the surge of newfound power, a deeper connection to his predatory instincts, and a grim satisfaction. *Level 3. Good. More tools for survival.*

His mind immediately turned to his new specialization. *Gloom Stalker.* The name itself sent a shiver of perverse delight through him. It promised mastery of shadows, an affinity for ambushes, a refinement of the very skills he’d honed fleeing Cazador's clutches. He was a creature of the night, after all, and this path embraced the darkness, the unseen strike, the silent kill. It was perfect. It resonated with the chilling hum of the tadpole behind his eye, a constant reminder of the hidden dangers he had to navigate.

The Githyanki, her chest heaving, dragged her crude blade from the Hook Horror’s carcass. Her golden eyes, fierce and unyielding, met his, a flicker of something akin to approval in their depths. "Another beast felled," she rasped, her voice rough. The burly man, meanwhile, let out a grunt of exhausted triumph, kicking at a shattered limb before turning to Astarion, his eyes burning with silent query. "What now? More to kill?"

"Patience, brute," Astarion drawled, a hint of his usual sardonic charm returning, though his hand still instinctively tightened on his longbow. "The ship still flies. And I, for one, would prefer not to die in its guts. We find the exit. And we do so *quietly*." His gaze swept the eerie corridor, now feeling more like a hunting ground than a prison. His new path offered precision in the dark, a silent promise of unseen strikes. He was ready for whatever came next, a predator in the shadows.
