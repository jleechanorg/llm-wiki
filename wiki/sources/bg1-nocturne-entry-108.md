Game Master:
You wipe the last of the kobold blood from your blade and turn to the elf. **Xan** is leaning against the damp wall of the cavern, staring mournfully at the glowing Moonblade in his hand as if it were a heavy burden rather than a legendary artifact.

"Xan," you say, your voice cutting through his reverie. "The half-orc didn't just write letters. He was a cleric, and clerics have ways of speaking across distances. Did you hear him? Did he commune with anyone?"

Xan sighs, a long, tragic sound that seems to deflate his entire frame. "Commune? If by 'commune' you mean 'groveling to the empty air,' then yes. Our captor was a man of little faith and much fear."

The elf gestures vaguely with the sword, the blue light trailing in the air. "Every night, at the witching hour, he would cast a ritual. *Sending*, I believe. Crude, but effective. He spoke to a man named **Tranzig**—the one mentioned in your letters, I presume. He reported ore yields and complained about the kobolds' incompetence."

Xan pauses, his expression darkening. "But... there was another. Two nights ago. Mulahey did not cast the spell; the spell came to *him*. A scrying sensor. I felt the magic wash over the cage like a cold draft from a grave. Mulahey fell to his knees. He did not speak to it as an equal, or even as a lieutenant. He pleaded."

"Pleaded with whom?" **Dynaheir** asks, stepping closer, her interest piqued.

"He called it 'Lord'," Xan whispers. "Not Tazok. Tazok is a bandit king, a brute. This presence was... precise. Metallic. Mulahey promised that the iron would fail on schedule. He promised that 'the coast would kneel.'"

Xan shudders. "We are all doomed, you see. Mulahey was but a pawn. The hand that moves him is vast, and it is closing into a fist. We should likely lie down and wait for the end, but I suppose you'll want to march into the teeth of it?"

**Roric** grunts. "Tranzig. That's the link. If Mulahey was reporting to him, Tranzig knows where the iron is going."

You have the confirmation. The conspiracy goes higher than a bandit leader. There is a shadow over the Sword Coast, and its name might be Sarevok, or something even older.

"We have what we came for," you state. "The head of the snake is severed. The body will thrash, but the mind is elsewhere. We return to the surface."
