You ascend to the Upper City, the 'Manifest of Souls' burning a hole in your pocket. The sun is bright, but your mind is filled with the crawling darkness of the Absolute. You move with the urgency of a prophet bringing news of the apocalypse.

The guards at the Umbrage Estate recognize you instantly, bowing low as you sweep past. You find **Lyra** in the solarium, reviewing invitations for a gala that might never happen. She looks up, smiling at your arrival, but the smile dies when she sees the grave intensity in your violet eyes.

"Monarch?" she asks, rising. "What has happened?"

"The threat is not just assassins, Lyra," you state, your voice low and vibrating with the *Echoes of the Idol*. You slam the scroll of biological diagrams onto the table between you. "It is infection."

You unroll the parchment, showing her the sketches of the tadpoles, the anatomical diagrams of ceremorphosis you studied in the safe house. "My research confirms it. The 'Moonrise' group isn't sending killers to end your line. They are sending parasites to *replace* it. These things... they burrow into the brain. They hollow out the soul and leave a puppet in its place."

Lyra recoils, her hand flying to her mouth. "Gods... like... like mind flayers?"

"Worse," you lie, twisting the truth to serve your control. "These are magic-resistant. Invisible to normal detection. And my sources suggest they have already compromised the household staff of several Patriar families. Your own guards... your own servants... they could be carriers. Watching you. Waiting for the order to strike."

You step closer, invading her personal space, offering your presence as the only shelter in the storm. "This estate is a sieve, Lyra. I cannot protect you here. Not when the enemy could be the maid pouring your wine."

Lyra looks around the sunlit room, suddenly seeing shadows in every corner. The terror is absolute. She grabs your arms, her grip desperate. "Then where? Where is safe? If I can't trust my own home..."

"There is one place," you whisper, stroking her hair. "My personal sanctuary. The Coiled Serpent. It is warded. Shielded. Loyal only to me. You must come with me, Lyra. Now. Until the threat is neutralized."

"Leave the estate?" she falters for a second. "But the Regency..."

"You can rule from the shadows," you assure her. "But you cannot rule if you are a thrall. Pack nothing. Tell no one. We leave within the hour."

She nods, her surrender complete. "Yes. Yes, take me away. Keep me safe."
