I. Character Profile: Nocturne Sosuke (The Fallen Siren)
The Vessel of Stolen Fire You are a paradox. Minutes ago, you were a Level 6 conduit of Hell, holding a contract signed by Zariel herself. Now, you are shivering on a wet beach, smelling of ozone and burning flesh.
The Physicality: You are still beautiful, but it is the beauty of a shipwreck. Your silk robe is torn, stained with Mind Flayer ichor and seawater. Your body feels heavy, the fluid grace of the dancer replaced by the tremors of shock. The tadpole behind your eye is a cold worm wriggling against the warm fire of the infernal magic you stole.
The Eyes: Your violet eyes now flare with occasional red sparks—the residue of Zariel’s power struggling against the Illithid parasite.
The Mind: You remember the ritual. You remember inverting the Cassalanter pact. You remember the surge of godlike power as you bound Zariel... and then the shadows in the sky. The Nautiloid. The theft of your agency. You are not a victim; you are a queen in exile.
The "Level 1" Justification (The Nerf) The tadpole is not just eating your brain; it is actively suppressing the immense infernal energy you absorbed during the ritual. You are a Level 6 entity contained in a Level 1 cage. The campaign is not just about removing the tadpole; it is about breaking the dam so your power can flood back.
The Age Factor (The Uncanny Valley): You are physically sixteen, a porcelain doll in a torn silk dress. Logically, battle-hardened mercenaries like Zevlor or Aradin should dismiss you. They don’t.
The Narrative Hook: People listen to you not because you are imposing, but because you are wrong. The air drops ten degrees when you make eye contact. Your beauty triggers a predator response in others—the feeling of a bird staring at a snake. When you roll Intimidation, you do not shout; you whisper, and the target feels the weight of the Abyss pressing against their skull.
New Special Trait: Malcanthet’s Favor (The Plot Armor)
Replaces Standard Death Saves Mechanic for Levels 1-3.
Effect: You are too entertaining to break this early. When you are reduced to 0 Hit Points, you do not fall Unconscious.
Instead, you drop to 1 Hit Point and immediately become Incorporeal (as per the Sanctuary spell). You cannot attack or cast spells until the start of your next turn, but you can move.
Visual: You dissolve into a cloud of violet smoke and reform in a safer spot.
Limit: Once per Long Rest. (Malcanthet will not save you twice).
I. Character Profile: Nocturne Sosuke
The Physicality of a Weapon You are a creature designed for observation and devastation, not labor. At 5'7", you are willowy and terrifyingly delicate (Strength 8, Constitution 8), possessing a frame that looks as though it might snap in a strong wind—yet you move with a fluid, predatory grace (Dexterity 14) that suggests you would bend rather than break.
The Face: Your features are a seamless blend of elven ethereality and high-born human arrogance. Your skin is pale as bone china, flawless and cool to the touch, maintained by alchemical creams that cost more than a commoner earns in a year. Your cheekbones are high and sharp, capable of catching the dimmest candlelight to cast elegant shadows.
The Eyes: Your most arresting feature. They are a vivid, unnatural violet—a genetic marker of the Sosuke bloodline. They are large, framed by thick, dark lashes that you use like a veil. When you widen them, you look innocent, almost childlike; when you narrow them, you look like a judge handing down a death sentence. They are eyes that have seen everything in the dark, yet reflect nothing back but what the viewer wants to see.
The Hair: Raven-black and lustrous, falling in heavy, silken waves to your waist. It is always perfectly coiffed, smelling of night-blooming jasmine and expensive oils. Tonight, it is pinned up loosely with a silver hairpin, allowing stray tendrils to frame your neck—a calculated vulnerability.
The Attire: You wear a sheer, floor-length robe of midnight-blue silk, belted loosely at the waist. It is designed to conceal nothing and promise everything. Beneath the silk, your body is marked only by the invisible scars of two years in the trade; your skin remains pristine, as Zylra forbids any damage to the merchandise. You are a porcelain doll that has learned to hate.
You are not just beautiful; you are distracting. Your presence in a room acts as a gravitational well. Men stumble over their words; guards forget to check credentials; rivals underestimate your intelligence because they cannot look past your face. You have turned your appearance into a blinding light, hiding the dagger of your intellect (Intelligence 16) in the glare.

The Routine: Horgus Gwent is a regular. He pays a premium for the irony of defiling a Sosuke, but he stays because he is hopelessly captivated. He believes you actually desire him. This is your greatest masterpiece: making a monster believe he is loved by a goddess.

XII. The Climax: The Inversion Ritual
This is not a combat encounter where you kill everyone. You are Level 1. You will die.
This is a Complex Skill Challenge (4 Successes before 3 Failures).
The Goal: You aren't stopping the ritual. You are stepping into the circle and changing the beneficiary from the Cassalanters to Nocturne.
Round 1: The Approach
Action: Sneak to the edge of the circle without being seen.
Roll: Stealth DC 13 OR Deception (pretend to be an acolyte).
Failure: The guards spot you. Initiative starts. (You need to talk fast).
Round 2: The Disruption
Action: Victoro is reading the contract. You must interrupt the flow of magic.
Roll: Intelligence (Arcana) DC 14 to spot the mathematical flaw in the summoning.
Alternative: Charisma (Performance) DC 15 to start singing a counter-melody that disrupts their chanting.
Round 3: The Offer
Action: Zariel's avatar (a burning projection) appears. She expects the Cassalanters. You step forward.
The Pitch: "They offer you coin, Zariel. I offer you competence. They are scared of the Absolute. I will eat the Absolute for you."
Roll: Charisma (Persuasion) DC 16 (This is the big one).
Bonuses: If you have Lyra's Soul Coin, you can offer it as a "down payment." (+5 to the roll).
Round 4: The Anchor
Action: The magic shifts. It tries to burn you alive. You must hold onto the power.
Roll: Constitution Saving Throw DC 12 OR Charisma Save DC 12.
Success: The ritual locks onto you. The power flows into your veins.
Failure: You take 2d6 Fire damage and are thrown back.
Outcome: Success
The Inversion: The gold in the room turns to ash. The Cassalanters are stripped of their protection. Zariel laughs.
The Reward: You gain the College of Perdition subclass instantly. You level up to Level 2 (or 3, depending on pacing).
The Escape: The Villa goes into lockdown. You must flee in the chaos.

XII. The Climax: The Inversion Ritual
This is not a combat encounter where you kill everyone. You are Level 1. You will die.
This is a Complex Skill Challenge (4 Successes before 3 Failures).
The Goal: You aren't stopping the ritual. You are stepping into the circle and changing the beneficiary from the Cassalanters to Nocturne.
Round 1: The Approach
Action: Sneak to the edge of the circle without being seen.
Roll: Stealth DC 13 OR Deception (pretend to be an acolyte).
Failure: The guards spot you. Initiative starts. (You need to talk fast).
Round 2: The Disruption
Action: Victoro is reading the contract. You must interrupt the flow of magic.
Roll: Intelligence (Arcana) DC 14 to spot the mathematical flaw in the summoning.
Alternative: Charisma (Performance) DC 15 to start singing a counter-melody that disrupts their chanting.
Round 3: The Offer
Action: Malcanthet’s avatar (a projection of unearthly, terrifying beauty) appears. She looks bored by the Cassalanters. You step forward.
The Pitch: "They offer you a city of bored nobles, my Queen. I offer you a crusade. I will not just corrupt this city; I will bring you the hearts of its heroes and the heads of its tyrants. They offer you food. I offer you a feast."
Roll: Charisma (Persuasion) DC 16 (This is the big one).
Bonuses: If you have Lyra's Soul Gem, you can offer it as a "down payment." (+5 to the roll).
Outcome: Success
The Inversion: The purple fire in the room turns a brilliant, blinding white. The Cassalanters are stripped of their protection. Malcanthet smiles, and for a moment, her face is yours.
The Reward: You gain the College of the Abyssal Siren subclass instantly.

Level 3: The Queen’s Curriculum
When you join this College, you master the art of weaponizing desire.

They voluntarily sleep with you.
They fail a save against your Charm or Dominate spells by 5 or more.
You reduce them to 0 HP and choose to "Spare" them, whispering a promise of service.
Active Thralls: You can maintain a number of Active Thralls equal to your Bard Level + Charisma Modifier.
Effect: Thralls are Charmed by you. They will not attack you. In combat, they act on their own initiative but will obey your verbal commands (no action required).
3. Siren’s Authority (The Legendary Breaker) You gain Expertise in Deception and Persuasion. Additionally, when you cast an Enchantment or Illusion spell that requires a Saving Throw, you can expend one use of Bardic Inspiration. Roll the die and subtract the number rolled from the target's Saving Throw total.
Tactical Note: This allows you to force Bosses to fail saves, even if they have high stats.
4. Abyssal Arts (Modified Blade Flourish) Whenever you take the Attack action, your walking speed increases by 10 feet. If you hit a creature, you can expend one use of Bardic Inspiration to deal extra damage and produce an effect:
The Lash (Slashing): The target takes extra Psychic damage equal to your Inspiration Die. You can target a second creature within 5 feet to take the same Psychic damage.
The Caress (Mobile): The target takes extra Psychic damage. You can push the target up to 10 feet towards you. If they end within 5 feet of you, they have Disadvantage on their next attack roll against anyone other than you.
The Command (Defensive): The target takes extra Psychic damage. You gain AC equal to the number rolled until the start of your next turn.

Your connection to Malcanthet allows you to treat souls as fuel.
1. Extra Attack You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the Attack action on your turn.

The Cost: The Thrall takes 3 Necrotic damage per Spell Level (e.g., Casting Hypnotic Pattern deals 30 damage to your Thrall). This damage cannot be reduced.
The Result: The spell is cast as normal. If the Thrall dies from this damage, the spell is cast at one level higher automatically.
3. Devotion’s Shield (Reaction) When you take damage, you can use your Reaction to transfer 100% of that damage to an Active Thrall within 60 feet. They take the hit; you take 0 damage.
