You wipe the grey ichor from **Varscona** and signal the descent. The second floor is a tomb. The basement is a prison. Only the main hall remains.

You lead the pack down the grand staircase, the *Pass Without Trace* spell turning your armored boots into whispers. Below, in the cavernous trading hall, three figures are pacing among the rows of desks. They wear the guise of clerks and guards, but their agitation gives them away. They hiss at each other in a language that sounds like wet stones grinding together, glancing nervously at the stairs.

They are the last of the infestation.

"Clear them," you command softly.

**The Slaughter.**

You do not give them the dignity of a challenge. You draw your bow and unleash the new lethality of your level. 

**Twang. Twang.**

Two arrows streak from the shadows of the landing. The first takes a "clerk" in the eye; the second strikes him in the throat as he falls. He dissolves into sludge before he hits the marble floor. 

**Minsc** vaults the banister, landing in the center of the room with a roar. "The cleaning is not done! Evil still lurks in the corners!" He swings his greatsword in a massive circle, catching a "guard" who tries to flank him. The heavy blade shears through the creature's disguise and deep into its torso, nearly cutting it in half.

**Kagain** and **Xan** finish the third. The dwarf catches the shapeshifter's scimitar on his shield, locking it in place, while Xan steps in with the **Moonblade**. The elf's strike is elegant and sad; a blue arc of light that separates head from shoulders.

It is over in seconds. The Seven Suns Coster is silent, save for the dripping of doppelganger remains.

You walk out the front doors into the cool evening air of Baldur's Gate. **Commander Scar** is waiting, his hand resting on his sword hilt. He looks at the blood on your armor, then at the silent building behind you.

"Done?" Scar asks, raising an eyebrow.

"Done," you reply, tossing the **Ring of Protection** (Jhampah's) and the **Letter from Sarevok** to him. "They were doppelgangers. The real merchants are in the cellar. The order came from the Iron Throne."

Scar reads the letter, his face hardening into a mask of cold fury. "Sarevok... I suspected, but I didn't think they'd be this bold. Shapeshifters in a Grand Duke's city? This is an act of war."

He pockets the evidence and hands you a heavy sack of gold. "You've done the city a service, ranger. **2,000 gold pieces**, as a token of the Flaming Fist's gratitude. But the war isn't over."

He points toward the harbor. "The **Iron Throne Headquarters**. It's a fortress at the docks. If Sarevok is planning a coup, the proof—and the leaders—will be there. Duke Eltan wants to see you, but I think you'd prefer to finish the hunt first."
