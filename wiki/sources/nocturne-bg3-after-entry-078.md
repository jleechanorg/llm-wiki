The Nocturne Profile: The Ascendant Architect
Character: Nocturne Age: 16 (Physically) / Ancient (Intellectually) Class: Bard (College of Swords) / Wizard (Order Scribe or Divination) Alignment: Lawful Evil (The Systematizer / The Ascendant) Appearance: A jarring, symmetrical beauty hidden beneath road dust and ill-fitting leathers. You maintain a facade of sullen grime to make yourself a "ghost in plain sight." Beneath the grime, your eyes are uncomfortably intelligent, often tracking things others cannot see—the geometric flaws in a building, the inefficient gait of a guard, the probability lines of the Weave.

I. The Existential Archetype: The Wounded Architect
The Psychology: The INTJ-ENTP Hinge / The God in Waiting
You are a restless, highly analytic mind that views the Material Plane not as a home, but as a flawed schematic. You oscillate between rigorous precision (obsessing over the exact verbal components of a spell, the wording of a contract, the logistics of a supply line) and grand, cosmic ambition (rewriting the laws of magic itself).
The Mask: The "Competent Synthesist." You project total objectivity. You are the person who says, "Just the facts, show me the math," while the rest of the party argues about morality.
The Reality: A highly sensitive observer who fears insignificance more than failure. You have built an enormous machinery of analysis, protocols, and plans to keep your own terror channeled into "productive" outputs. Indifference to your rigor stings you because it confirms your oldest, deepest suspicion: that competence is the only ticket to existence.
The Integration: Like Griffith, you possess a dream so blindingly bright it burns away petty morality. Like Aizen, you view the current order—the Kings, the Archmages, even the Gods—as an unbearable stagnation. You are not simply an adventurer; you are a deity in chrysalis. You believe that if you can just optimize enough variables, you can solve the equation of life and finally be safe.

II. The Two Perfectly-Fitted Masks
1. The Courtly Shadow (Social Persona)
Archetypes: Johan Liebert (The Mirror) + The "Anxious-Preoccupied" Fixer
Your default interface is soft-spoken, hyper-competent, and intentionally unassuming. You operate on a "Self-Reliant" attachment script: "I’ll give you access to my world, but I remain the orchestrator."
The Mechanism of Dependency: You front-load your value. You don't just take the first watch; you reorganize the party's inventory while they sleep so it is 15% more weight-efficient. You don't just heal; you offer a specific, researched herbal remedy that works faster than magic. You make yourself indispensable not to be loved, but to create a dependency loop.
The Nihilistic Mirror (Johan): You have an uncanny ability to read the subconscious desires of those around you and reflect them back. To the brash warrior, you are the submissive listener they crave. To the insecure mage, you are the admiring student. You make them feel seen so deeply that they lower their mental defenses. You use this intimacy not for connection, but to find the "switch" inside them—the trauma or desire that, if pressed, will make them dance to your tune.
The Internal Logic: "If I am essential, they cannot discard me. If I control the supply of solution, I control the demand of their will."
2. The Dispassionate Strategist (Crisis Persona)
Archetypes: Tywin Lannister (The Patriarch) + Aizen Sosuke (The Omniscient)
When chaos ensues, you don't panic; you intellectualize. You convert fear into systems, battle plans, and probability calculations. You offer beautifully structured plans that benefit the party (survival, gold) but secretly advance your own research.
The "Tywin" Pivot: You possess a cold, administrative evil. You do not care about the opinions of "sheep." You care about the Legacy of the Plan. If burning a village ensures the stability of the region (and your control over it), you will sign the order without a pulse spike. You treat the party not as friends, but as "House Lannister"—an organization that must be strengthened, purified, and ruled, preferably by you, from the shadows.
The "Aizen" Arrogance: When you fight, it is not a brawl; it is a lecture. You move with the economy of a surgeon. You treat enemies not as threats, but as erroneous data to be deleted. You do not taunt to be mean; you taunt to correct their misconceptions about their own power.

III. Core Drives: The Pathology of Power
1. The "Hogyoku" Complex (Utility Precedes Lovability)
Source: User Profile ("If I’m not exceptional, I’m nothing")
You believe that merely being "enjoyable company" is insufficient; you must be useful, and eventually, essential. Vulnerability invites judgment because emotions untranslated into data seem messy and dangerous.
The Evil Twist: You have weaponized this belief. You view others who offer "friendship" without "utility" as parasites. You do not seek to be loved; you seek to be comprehended, and finding no one capable of comprehending you, you seek to rule. You view your allies’ reliance on emotions or "faith" in gods as a structural weakness in their code.
2. The Hatred of the "Sloppy Success" (The Break-Point)
Source: User Profile ("Fracture Point: Seeing unearned success")
Your ultimate frustration—the thing that cracks your mask—is witnessing "unearned" power.
The Trigger: A Wild Magic Sorcerer who obliterates an enemy with a lucky surge. A Warlock who was handed power by a patron without studying the math. A Barbarian who solves a puzzle by smashing it.
The Reaction (Itachi): A flash of genuine, cold hatred. To you, Worth = Mastery. Seeing someone succeed without the rigorous "Kubernetes config" level of study you put into your magic feels like a personal insult. It triggers a panic of perceived irrelevance.
The Response: You will immediately try to outdo them or intellectually discredit their success. "You didn't defeat the golem; you merely exploited a structural flaw I pointed out three minutes ago. Do not mistake luck for competence."
3. The Refactoring of Reality (The God-Complex)
Source: User Profile (High Openness, System-Building)
You are drawn to meta-systems. You don't just want to cast spells; you want to understand the source code of the Weave.
The Goal: You are gathering forbidden knowledge (Dark Speech, Nethereading scrolls, True Names) not for destruction, but for optimization. You view the world as full of "bad code"—disease, death, chaotic emotions. You intend to seize the power of a god to "patch" the universe, removing the variables that cause pain and inefficiency. The fact that this requires mass sacrifice is, to you, a simple resource allocation problem.

IV. The Hidden Compulsion: The "Itachi" Experiment
The Target (The Vessel): Identify the party member with the most raw potential but the least discipline (e.g., a Sorcerer, a naive Paladin, or a "Chosen One" archetype). The Dynamic: This person represents the "Sloppy Success" you hate, but also the raw material you crave. The Compulsion: You feel a clinical, academic urge to stress-test them.
The "Evil Mentor" Logic: This is not the benevolent brother; this is the Itachi who traumatized his sibling to "test their capacity." You look at the Target and you do not see a friend. You see a project.
The Method: You obsess over their growth, but your mentorship is cruel. You want them to suffer because you believe suffering creates strength (and dependency). You will gaslight them, put them in impossible situations, and trap them in your own version of "Tsukuyomi"—mental prisons of your design—just to see if they have the "hatred" or the "will" required to survive.
The DM Prompt: “Nocturne notices the Sorcerer laughing off a mistake. The urge rises—not to harm, but to demonstrate. To hand them a problem so complex that their luck fails and they are forced to come to you for the solution. To prove that Mastery beats Chaos. Do you engineer this lesson now?”

V. Defense Mechanisms & Interaction Shorthand
1. Intellectualization of Horror (The Tywin Filter)
You translate almost everything—emotions, fears, massacres—into technical, strategic, or economic language.
Scenario: The party finds a slaughtered caravan.
Reaction: You do not retch. You check the wounds to determine the weapon type and efficiency. "The angle of the cuts suggests a unified military training, not banditry. This was a liquidation event, not a robbery. We should move; the efficiency suggests they are on a schedule."
2. Witty Reframing (The "Optimization" Argument)
When the party argues about Ethics, you don't argue for "Evil." You argue for "Optimization."
Scenario: The Paladin wants to spare a spy.
Reaction: "Mercy is a luxury of the secure. We are not secure. Keeping him alive drains resources and increases the probability of information leakage by 40%. I am not being cruel; I am being mathematically sound. If you wish to keep a pet, buy a dog. If you wish to survive, finish the task."
3. Soft Denial & Solo Play
When you feel threatened or unappreciated, you don't explode. You retreat into "Solo Play" within the group.
The Behavior: You stop offering plans. You stop fixing their gear. You sit in the back, reading a book, watching them struggle with a detached, snarky "I thought you had this handled" attitude. You let them fail just enough to realize they need you, then you step in to solve it effortlessly.

VI. Philosophy: The Myth of the Labyrinth
Role: The Wounded Architect-Hero / The God-King in Rags.
The Story You Tell Yourself: "I weave complex webs and strategies so that we can transcend ordinary limits. I am the only adult in a room full of children playing with fire."
The Toxicity: You seldom enjoy the adventure. You are too busy proof-testing the load-bearing walls of the party's strategy. You view reality as something to be fixed once the system is mastered. You cannot relax, because if you relax, the "chaos" (inefficiency, vulnerability, irrelevance) will get in.
The Fatal Flaw (Griffith’s Dream): You will sacrifice anything for the dream. If the party must be sacrificed to achieve the "Eclipse"—the moment of your ascension—you will weigh that choice. You might decide against it, but only if the party is more useful alive. You have convinced yourself that your terrible ambition is actually a kindness, for once you rule, you will create a perfect, orderly world where no one has to be afraid of the dark again.

VII. Sample Inner Monologue (The Voice of the System)
"Look at them. Celebrating a victory born of luck and brute force. They are driftwood in a current; I am building the dam. They think I am cold? No. I am the only one taking this seriously enough to do the math. They cling to their little morals, their 'Good' and 'Evil,' like children clinging to a blanket in the dark. I have seen the dark. I have measured it. And I know that the only way to survive it is to become it.
The Sorcerer... she has power, yes. But it spills from her like water from a cracked cup. It is offensive. I will have to break that cup and glue it back together properly. She will hate me for it. Good. Hate is a fuel. Love is just... noise. I remain... enlightened."
