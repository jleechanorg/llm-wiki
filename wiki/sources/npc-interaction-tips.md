## Getting More Out of NPC Interactions

NPCs are the main interface between you and the world. The AI runs them dynamically — you can dramatically improve the quality of those interactions by giving the AI strong material to work with, and by knowing how to read what the AI describes.

---

## The NPC Blueprint

When interacting with an important NPC, give the AI context to work with:

- **Goal** — What does this NPC want? A specific, actionable desire, not generic motivation.
- **Flaw** — What undermines their own plans? The blind spot you can exploit.
- **Secret** — What are they hiding from you? Even if it never comes up directly, it shapes their behavior.
- **Voice** — How do they speak? Accent, vocabulary, sentence length. A merchant sounds different from a warlord.

Establish these in your prompt before the interaction begins: "I approach the guild factor. He's a heavyset man who speaks in short sentences, clearly pressed for time — he's hiding something about the last shipment."

---

## Reading NPC Behavior

The AI describes micro-behaviors — a pause, an averted gaze, a clenched fist. These are signals:

- **Watch for tells.** NPC nervousness, hesitation, or sudden calm often signals hidden information. Press on it.
- **Read the room first.** "I observe the NPC carefully before speaking" gives the AI context to flesh out their demeanor.
- **Find common ground.** NPCs respond better when you offer something, not just ask. Reference shared contacts, faction relationships, or known history.

---

## Faction Alignment and How NPCs Respond

The six factions in the World of Assiah have distinct cultural dispositions, and NPCs react to your alignment:

- **Celestial Imperium** — Values order, lineage, and protocol. Respects formal channels and hierarchy.
- **Shattered Host remnants** — Desperate, fractured, unpredictable. High-risk, high-reward interactions.
- **First-Spear Clans** — Honor-bound and direct. Respects strength and keeps promises.
- **Merchant Guild / Nexus** — Neutral, profit-driven. Works with anyone who pays; responds to leverage.

Your faction alignment changes how NPCs treat you. An aligned character gets different responses than an unaffiliated outsider.

---

## Multi-Session NPCs

Build recurring NPCs by referencing past interactions. "I remind the NPC of what happened in Aeterna" keeps continuity alive and deepens the relationship over time. The AI tracks NPC memory across entries.
