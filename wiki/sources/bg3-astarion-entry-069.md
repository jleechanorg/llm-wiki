The Nautiloid Sentry lay a shattered ruin, its violet eye extinguished, its multi-jointed limbs splayed at grotesque angles on the spongy, pulsing floor. The metallic tang of its internal fluids mingled with the acrid scent of ozone and the ever-present, cloying organic smell of the ship. Astarion watched its final, shuddering collapse with a grim, almost feral satisfaction. Another obstacle, reduced to scrap. He didn't waste time on congratulations or idle chatter. His mind, sharp and cold, immediately shifted to pragmatism. These alien constructs, for all their horrifying design, might yet yield a secret.

"Don't just stand there gaping," he drawled, his voice a low, sardonic rasp, directed at his companions. The Githyanki, her golden eyes still blazing with a cold battle-fury, stood over the wreckage, her improvised blade glistening. The burly man, meanwhile, kicked at a shattered limb, a lingering surge of raw power still vibrating through his massive frame. Astarion, however, knelt with the fluid grace of a seasoned urban hunter, his crimson eyes already dissecting the shattered construct.

He ignored the sticky ichor and shattered chitin, his slender fingers probing the Sentry's obsidian shell, searching for anything that wasn't merely bone and sinew. Was there a power core? A control chip? Some piece of illithid technology that could be repurposed, or at least offer a clue to this ship's horrifying purpose? His high neuroticism gnawed at him, demanding thoroughness, demanding control. The faint, insistent squirm of the tadpole behind his eye added a chilling urgency to his search. He traced wires that pulsed with sickly green light, felt for hidden compartments, for a loose plate, for anything that might have been overlooked. The thrumming of the Nautiloid, a constant, sickening vibration, was a relentless reminder of their precarious freedom.
