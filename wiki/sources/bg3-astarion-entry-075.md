Astarion's stomach churned with profound disgust. "These aren't just victims," he rasped, his voice raw with a chilling realization. "They're being *made*. Processed. Like us. We can't... we *won't* leave them to that fate." His usual self-serving pragmatism was momentarily overridden by a deep, visceral empathy born of his own terrifying ordeal and the grim premonition of his future. The thought of becoming a Mind Flayer's grotesque plaything, of having his very essence consumed, sparked a desperate, protective rage. Even if it was futile, he would not stand by.

The distant, scuttling sound grew louder, accompanied by that low, guttural chittering, drawing closer with alarming speed. Astarion ignored it, his eyes fixed on the nearest humanoid pod. His companions, sensing his grim resolve, mirrored his immediate intent. The Githyanki, her golden eyes blazing with a fierce, alien pity, moved with predatory grace towards another pod. The burly man, a low growl rumbling in his chest, aimed his makeshift blade at a third.

Astarion didn't hesitate. He raised his jagged bone blade, its psionic hum a chilling counterpoint to the thrumming ship. He aimed for the center of the translucent membrane, where the glowing tadpole pulsed within the human's chest. "End it," he muttered, a promise to himself as much as an action. He brought the crude weapon down with a furious, visceral impact, not just to destroy, but to release. The sight of the trapped, suffering humanoid fueled his strike, transforming his usual precision into a brutal, desperate force.
