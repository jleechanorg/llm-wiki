You pushed yourself upright, swaying slightly, but your posture was now rigid with an unwavering purpose. The throbbing in your head, the metallic taste of blood – it all faded to background noise. You looked at Kantos, your Starlit Eyes burning with a fierce, almost desperate determination that left no room for argument or fear.

"Then take me to him, Magister," you commanded, your voice steady despite the lingering tremor in your body. It was not a plea, but a decree. "Now. Sergeant Kaelan. The Quiet Ward. No more delays. No more warnings. I need to see him. I need to know."

Kantos stared, his ancient face paling further. He wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting from your fierce resolve to the oppressive darkness of the vault. He let out a long, defeated sigh, the sound a dry rustle in the silent chamber. He knew you would not be deterred. "As you wish, Princess," he rasped, his voice thick with a mixture of terror and weary resignation. "But I must warn you... the Quiet Ward is not a place for the faint of heart. And the Silent Guard... they will not simply allow access."

"Then we will need more than just your knowledge, Magister," you stated, your voice firm and clear, cutting through his renewed despair. Your mind, sharp and focused despite the lingering psychic backlash, rapidly assessed the needs of the coming confrontation. "We will need a shield to protect us from the Quiet Ward's terrors, and a key to unlock its hidden doors. Send for them. All of them."

You met his gaze, the unspoken command hanging in the cold, still air. "Summon Ser Gideon Vance. His loyalty is absolute, and his strength is our shield. Summon Lady Cressida Valeriana. Her wit navigates the court's labyrinth better than any blade, and her political acumen will be our key. And summon Rowan Thorne. His kindness will be a balm in a place of madness, and his knowledge of healing may yet be vital."

Kantos’s eyes widened, his face a mask of compounded terror and disbelief. To involve so many in such a dangerous secret, and for such an audacious goal... it was almost unthinkable. But he saw the unwavering resolve in your Starlit Eyes, a reflection of the mother who had once ordered him to turn a heresy into a weapon. He nodded slowly, a profound weariness settling upon his ancient features. "As you command, Princess," he whispered, turning to activate a hidden speaking tube. "Though the Spire itself will whisper of your intentions before this day is done."

The oppressive silence of the Chamber of Whispers was broken by the faint sounds of Kantos issuing hushed, urgent instructions for messengers, his voice a dry academic rustle against the ancient stone. You sank back against the cold obsidian wall, clutching your locket, the subtle throb in your bandaged ear and the residual psychic pain in your head a constant, unwelcome reminder of your earlier attempt. The silence stretched, thick with anticipation. You were a general, preparing for an impossible siege, waiting for your small, chosen army to assemble, knowing that every tick of the clock brought you closer to a truth that might shatter you, or forge you anew.
