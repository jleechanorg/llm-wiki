Astarion's lips thinned, a testament to his profound irritation. The viewport offered no answers, his investigations no secrets. And his companions, still radiating their brutish readiness for a head-on assault, clearly had no capacity for subtlety or patience. *Fine,* he decided, a cold, hard resolve settling in his chest. *If they insist on blundering into the unknown, then I shall lead them, if only to ensure my own survival.* He would not be a mere follower, not again. The tadpole pulsed behind his eye, a chilling reminder that time was a luxury they no longer possessed.

"Very well," he drawled, his voice a low, almost imperceptible murmur, laced with a cynical weariness. He didn't wait for their affirmation, merely turned on his heel and moved with the fluid, predatory grace of a seasoned hunter towards the un-investigated passage on the far side of the chamber. This was not the same gaping maw they had emerged from; this was a more conventional-looking corridor, albeit one still lined with pulsating, organic growths that glowed with a sickly yellow light. The air here was even heavier, charged with static electricity and a faint, metallic tang.

His longbow was clutched tight, a silent promise of ranged death. He moved with heightened vigilance, his eyes sweeping for traps, for ambushes, for any hint of movement in the grotesque architecture. His companions followed, the Githyanki a swift, silent shadow, the burly man a rumbling presence at his heels. He could feel their impatient energy, their readiness to erupt into violence, and he simultaneously reviled and relied upon it. This was a deeper plunge into the heart of the monster. The Nautiloid shuddered, a deep, resonant groan that seemed to swallow all other sound. Astarion felt a shiver of profound dread – not just for what lay ahead, but for the horrifying, inevitable transformation that awaited them all.
