Character Dossier: Alexiel

I. Core Identity
Name: Alexiel
Archetype/Role: The Perfect Deceiver
Occupation/Social Standing: Former Champion of the Unchained Host, now a high-value defector and political asset/prisoner of the Celestial Imperium.
D&D Alignment: (Internal) Lawful Neutral (Her actions are not driven by morality, but by a complex, internal code of strategic perfection and a single, inviolable rule. She uses the appearance of both Law and Chaos as tools.)
Myers-Briggs Type (MBTI): (Internal) INTJ (The Architect: A brilliant strategist who sees the world as a complex system to be understood and manipulated. Her entire life is a long, carefully executed plan.)
II. Psychology & Personality
Core Motivation (The "Why"): To survive and achieve a state of perfect control over her own destiny and environment, a goal born from a life of being viewed as a "flawed tool." She seeks to prove that her way—a path of ruthless intellect and deception—is superior to all others.
Greatest Fear: Being truly "seen" and understood. Her greatest terror is having her layers of deception pierced, not because it would make her physically vulnerable, but because it would expose her true, monstrously joyful nature to a world she feels would rightly fear it.
Key Personality Traits: Meticulous, analytical, patient, incredibly disciplined, and secretly passionate. She possesses a profound capacity for both empathy (as a tool for analysis) and ruthless detachment.
Quirks & Habits: She has a habit of perfect stillness when observing a situation, her Unseeing Eyes seeming to lose focus as her mind processes variables. She never uses a single wasted motion.
III. Behavior & Speech
Demeanor Under Stress: She appears to become more fragile or more stoic, depending on the mask she is wearing for her audience (The Broken Dove or The Reluctant Champion). Internally, stress sharpens her mind to a razor's edge, and she enters a state of hyper-analysis.
Speech Patterns: Her speech is a tool. She can be soft-spoken and hesitant (as Sariel would later be), direct and professional (as the Sword of the Host), or quietly philosophical, always adapting her cadence and vocabulary to her target.
Reputation: A figure of immense contradiction and legend. To the Host, she is "The Great Betrayer." To the Imperial soldiers, she is the "Hero of the Western Wall." To Raziel, she is the "Trojan Horse." To Artorius, she is the "Reluctant Champion." No one, at the start of her story in the Imperium, knows who she truly is.
IV. Backstory
Defining Moment: The Sacking of Silverwood. Witnessing the Host murder children was the violation of her one, absolute moral line. This event proved to her that Lucifer's philosophy was a failure and forced her to enact her own long-theorized plans for survival.
Relevant History: Created by Lucifer after his fall, she was designed to be his perfect heir. Her "Null" status was a deep disappointment to him, but he genuinely cared for her and trained her to be a peerless warrior and strategist. From a young age, she recognized his emotional nature as a vulnerability and began crafting her "Reluctant Champion" persona to perfectly manage his expectations, all while secretly honing the magical abilities he never knew she had.
Secrets: Her secrets are layered. The public secret is that she is a Null. The inner circle's secret is that she has a "suicidal" Nullification Field. Her true secret is that the field's cost is a lie and that she is a magical prodigy. Her deepest, most personal secret is the "Joyful Predator" within—the part of her that truly revels in the perfect execution of violence and strategy, a part she fears and loathes.
V. Game Mechanics
Feats: Alert, Mobile, Skulker (indicative of her focus on survival, awareness, and evasion).
Special Abilities / Powers: The Three Layers of her hidden power: her secret magic, the Nullification Field, and her feigned weakness when using it.

CAMPAIGN PREMISE: THE WAR OF SYSTEMS
The world of Assiah is locked in the Thousand Year War. To the east lies the Celestial Imperium, a land of rigid order, high magic, and golden starlight, ruled by the immortal Lord Regent Raziel. To the west lies the Unchained Host, a chaotic meritocracy of orcs, beast-kin, and fallen angels, led by the charismatic and nihilistic Lucifer, the Morning Star.
You are Alexiel. You are Lucifer’s "daughter"—a biological construct designed to be the perfect weapon. You are publicly known as a Null, a being devoid of magic in a world ruled by it. This is a lie. You possess a terrified, secret magic and a mind built for grand strategy.
The Hook: You have just won a battle for your father, but the cost has shattered your soul. Now, you must cross the board and defect to the enemy you have been bred to destroy.

CORE MECHANIC: THE TRINITY OF MASKS
Alexiel is not one person; she is three. The player must manage these personas. Each offers unique bonuses but carries narrative risks.
1. The Prodigal Sword (Public Persona)
Description: The cold, efficient commander of the Host. The "Null."
Mechanical Benefit: +5 to Intimidation. Advantage on Attack Rolls with melee weapons.
Restriction: Absolute Magic Lock. You cannot cast spells. You cannot show empathy.
Trigger: Used when commanding Host troops or intimidating enemies.
2. The Reluctant Champion (The Facade)
Description: The mask created to survive the Imperium. A tragic, gentle soul forced into war.
Mechanical Benefit: +5 to Persuasion and Deception. NPCs are less likely to attack you first (Target Priority lowers).
Restriction: You must make a Wisdom Save (DC 15) to deal a killing blow to a humanoid. Failure means you leave them unconscious/stabilized.
Trigger: Used when interacting with Artorius or Imperial nobility.
3. The Joyful Predator (The True Self)
Description: The secret reality. A strategic genius who revels in the perfect application of force.
Mechanical Benefit: Access to Secret Magic (Subtle Spell Metamagic is free). Access to Nullification Field. Critical Hit range expands to 18-20.
The Cost: Every round spent in this state adds 1 Fatigue Point. If Fatigue reaches 5, you suffer Disadvantage on Deception checks for 24 hours (your mask slips).
Trigger: Desperate combat or total isolation.

CHAPTER 1: THE ASHES OF SILVERWOOD
SCENE 1: THE MATHEMATICS OF SLAUGHTER
[Location: Town Square, Silverwood] [Time: Dusk, Year 78]
Read Aloud: The silence is the first offense. Victorious battlefields usually have a rhythm—the groans of the dying, the roar of victory, the crackle of banners. But Silverwood is dead. The air hangs heavy, thick with the scent of wet ash, ozone, and copper.
You stand in the town square. Your boots are coated in gray dust. Around you, the soldiers of the Unchained Host—your soldiers—move with a jagged, chaotic energy, looting the few structures left standing. To your left, the town hall is a skeletal ruin of blackened timber. To your right, the fountain is choked with bodies.
You look down. At your feet lies a small, crude object: a wooden horse, carved from pine, its leg scorched. Beside it is a small blanket, covering a shape too small to be a soldier.
DM Note: This is the breaking point. The player must internalize the "Strategic Failure."
Investigation Phase: Allow the player to examine the scene.
Intelligence (Investigation) DC 12: You notice the bodies aren't just Imperial spies. They are bakers, smiths, children. This wasn't a surgical strike; it was an extermination.
Wisdom (Insight) DC 10: You see your own troops looking at you. They are not proud; they are frenzied. This isn't an army; it's a mob.
The Confrontation: A hulking figure emerges from the smoke. Lieutenant Gorok (Orc Barbarian/Fighter). He is covered in blood that is not his own.
Gorok: "A clean sweep, Commander! No witnesses. No orphans to grow up seeking revenge. Efficient." He grins, exposing yellow tusks. "The Morning Star will be pleased."
Interaction: The player must choose Alexiel's response. This defines her character's internal logic.
Option A (The Strategist): "Define the strategic value of this slaughter, Lieutenant. You have traded a town for a graveyard."
Option B (The Moralist): "There is no honor in this. You are a butcher, Gorok."
Option C (The Silence): Say nothing. Simply stare until he becomes uncomfortable.
Event: The Choice Regardless of the dialogue, Gorok sneers. "It is the way of the Host. Weakness is a sin."
Read Aloud: The final variable clicks into place. Lucifer’s philosophy is not freedom; it is a fire that consumes itself. You reach up to your gorget. The metal is cold against your neck. You unclasp the sigil of the Unchained Host—the broken shackle wreathed in flame.
It falls into the ash with a soft, final thump.
You turn your back on Gorok. You turn your back on your life.
Mechanic - The Defection:
Status Effect: You are now marked as Traitor. All Host units are immediately hostile upon recognition.
Fatigue: You start the campaign with Level 1 Fatigue (Disadvantage on Ability Checks) due to the psychological weight of the decision.
