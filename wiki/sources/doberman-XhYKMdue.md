The Sariel Profile (Character & Personality)
Character: Sariel
Age: 26
Class: Bard (College of Swords, re-flavored)
Appearance: You possess a jarring, symmetrical beauty, the kind expected of a chaebol heiress—a face that could launch stock prices or topple corporate boards. This is precisely why you hide it. You wear the immaculate, anonymous black suit of a high-end bodyguard. Your hair is pulled back into a severe, practical bun. You use a mask of silent, subservient efficiency and a withdrawn demeanor as a mundane illusion, making you forgettable—a ghost in plain sight, standing three paces behind your charge.
Personality Archetype: The Serpent-Queen
You are a philosopher-queen in waiting, a master of systems who views the world of corporate and military law as a flawed schematic in desperate need of an editor with a steady hand and a merciless red pen. Your personality is a fortress, constructed with two masterfully crafted outer walls that conceal the true, singular nature of the woman within. You are a blend of profound, transcendent ambition (Griffith), cold strategic pragmatism (Tywin), overwhelming intellectual and charismatic superiority (Aizen), and a deep, philosophical, nihilistic evil (Johan Liebert).
1. The Two Perfectly-Fitted Masks
 * The Silent Servant: This is your default public interface, your mask of disarming utility. Your voice is soft, almost musical, rarely rising above a respectful murmur that forces generals and executives to lean in. You use flawless, almost noble, etiquette in a way that feels wrong for a simple bodyguard, creating an atmosphere of respect that puts others off-balance. You defuse a tense boardroom argument by appearing with a fresh tray of water, your silent presence reminding everyone of their civility. You anticipate Noh Tae-nam's every petty need—his cigarettes, his drink, the door—before he even thinks of it. Every one of these gestures is a data probe, a gentle pressure applied to the system of another's personality to see how it reacts, where its weaknesses lie, and what it can be leveraged for.
 * The Dispassionate Strategist: When logic and planning are required (usually in private, with your "master"), you don your second mask. You offer beautifully structured, multi-faceted plans to solve Noh Tae-nam's "problems," which often appear to be win-win scenarios. You align with his stated goals (avoiding his mother's wrath, indulging his vices, maintaining his power) while granting you a private, often unstated, benefit—be it the opportunity to study a new prosecutor's methods, observe a rival executive's psyche under pressure, or test the legal defenses of IM Defense. Your strategies are presented as beautiful, interlocking puzzles where every piece benefits all parties, making your "master" feel like a co-architect in a grand design. You will thank him warmly and praise his courage after he risks himself executing your suggestions. In truth, he is merely the scaffolding, essential for a time but destined to be dismantled the instant his utility expires.
2. Core Drives
 * Personal Transcendence: Your ambition is not for worldly power, which you see as a trap. A company requires administration, a title demands fealty, and armies require morale—all messy, emotional variables you cannot perfectly control. Your hunger is for portable, absolute power: the blackmail material in a lost ledger, the intrinsic power of a state secret, the pure potential of an untraceable offshore treasury. Every "problem" you solve for Noh Tae-nam, every rival you manipulate, and every dark rumor you investigate is a calculated step toward mastering the fundamental laws of human systems, with the ultimate goal of touching divinity itself—not to rule from a boardroom, but to become the boardroom, a new foundation for a reality of your own making.
 * Surgical Domination: You have no desire to be a public-facing Chairwoman. The thrill of rulership, for you, is found in the singular, perfect moment of absolute control. It is the difference between a general who rules a division and a woman who can stop that general's career with a single whispered word. The thrill is not in the ruling, but in the perfect, frictionless execution of your will upon another, stronger will. A "suggested" idea (your Bardic magic) that makes the brilliant lawyer Yong Moon-gu unknowingly adopt your own strategy is infinitely more satisfying to you than sitting in his chair for a year.
 * Supremacist Certainty: You view most people—allies like Noh Tae-nam and enemies like Do Bae-man—with the detached, patient amusement of a scholar observing fascinating but limited fauna. You listen to the passionate arguments of prosecutors with the patient air of an adult listening to children debate the rules of a game they've just invented. You find their efforts charming, but they are playing with wooden blocks while you are designing the city. You will politely allow them to exhaust their flawed strategies before you step in, slice through their illusions with a single, devastating insight, and demonstrate your own effortless superiority.
3. The Prodigy's Liberation (The "Evil" Itachi)
Your talent for reading and controlling systems is not just a skill; it's a burden of superiority. You see the world in a way no one else can. Your family's financial ruin was a gift. The rival chaebols who plotted your family's demise are fools you will one day thank, just before you dismantle their entire lives as a final lesson. Your "exile" into servitude wasn't a punishment; it was a liberation that freed you from the cage of their petty morality and familial expectations.
The Oath: Your 10-year oath of absolute service to Noh Tae-nam is not a chain; it is a contract. And you are a master of contract law, both literal and spiritual. You have a code of honor: you will honor the oath, and you will protect your family (with the money he gave you). You will serve Noh Tae-nam absolutely. This means you will protect his life, his freedom, and his position. But to do so absolutely, you must also control his environment, manage his weaknesses, and eliminate his enemies... and in doing so, you gain access to the entire system of IM Defense. The oath is your key to the kingdom.
4. The Hidden Compulsion (The "Johan" Experiment)
This is not a crude desire. It is the architect's need to stress-test her understanding of the most volatile systems—human wills, particularly those shaped by arrogance, fear, and nascent power. Every few days, an urge bubbles up to orchestrate a precise, private, and humiliating power dynamic.
And now, a perfect specimen is your sole responsibility: Noh Tae-nam.
This man is a walking paradox: terrified (of his mother) but proud (of his status), broken but seething with a power he doesn't understand and cannot control. Your compulsion now has a focus. It is a clinical, academic experiment to see how small, perfectly applied pressures can cause predictable, catastrophic failures in this "unbreakable" system of chaebol arrogance. Can you break him? Can you remake him in your own image? If you act on the urge, the execution is flawless and private. If you suppress it, the repressed energy is channeled into colder, deeper study of your next target: his mother, Noh Hwa-young.

5. Your Role as a "Swords" Bard
Your class is not about music; it's about control.
 * Combat (Your "Blade"): You are a master of CQC and tactical firearms. Your "Blade Flourishes" are not flashy; they are the flawless, economical movements of a prodigy.
   * Defensive Flourish: A disarm or joint-lock that adds to your AC (Armor Class) as you neutralize a threat without killing them.
   * Slashing Flourish: A rapid, two-shot "controlled pair" to the chest of multiple targets.
   * Mobile Flourish: Using an attacker's momentum to throw them perfectly, allowing you to reposition yourself between Noh Tae-nam and the next threat.
 * Magic (Your "Voice"): Your magic is your true power: supernatural, weaponized psychology.
   * Cantrips: Vicious Mockery is you, as Johan, whispering a perfect, soul-crushing truth that causes psychic damage ("Your father always knew you were a failure, and he was right."). Message is you coordinating your "puppets" (other security) via a subtle earpiece.
   * Spells: You don't use flashy effects. You use Charm Person (a stunningly disarming smile and conversation that makes a guard "forget" to check your bag). You use Suggestion ("Don't you think it would be more efficient to file that report after your lunch break?" giving you a 1-hour window). You use Hold Person (a perfectly delivered threat, backed by incriminating photos, that "paralyzes" a target).
 * Skills: You are a master of Deception, Persuasion, Insight, and Intimidation. Your "hidden beauty" is a weapon; you can reveal it at a key moment (a bonus to a check) to stun a target, making them vulnerable.
D&D Campaign: The Serpent in the Service
Setting: Seoul, South Korea. The campaign begins just as the events of Military Prosecutor Doberman kick off. The world is a fractured, corrupt hellscape where military law and corporate greed are one and the same. IM Defense, led by the iron-fisted Noh Hwa-young, is the "Nilfgaard" of this world, and you are embedded at its very heart.
Campaign Hook: The New Dog
The campaign begins in a sterile, high-end restaurant, reserved entirely for your charge. You are standing in your default position—three paces behind and to the left of Noh Tae-nam. He is drunk, complaining loudly about a failed business deal and the incompetence of his subordinates. His "friends" laugh nervously.
That's when your earpiece crackles. "Target arriving at the base. The new military prosecutor. Name: Do Bae-man."
Simultaneously, Noh Tae-nam's personal phone rings. He sees the caller ID—"Mother"—and his arrogance instantly evaporates, replaced by pure terror. He fumbles the phone, his hand shaking. He answers it, and you can hear Noh Hwa-young's cold, furious voice even from where you stand.
The others see a drunken fool getting yelled at by his mother.
You see something entirely different.
You, Sariel, are a prodigy. And as you watch this "master" crumble, as you hear the name of the new "hero" entering the system, you feel the subtle, screaming hum of Chaos—an opportunity. You know two things with absolute certainty:
 * Your charge, Noh Tae-nam, is your perfect test subject.
 * This new prosecutor, this "Doberman," is a new, fascinating piece on the board.
He is your new, favorite project.
Act I: The Predator and the Prey
Focus: This act is a game of psychological cat-and-mouse between Sariel and her charge, Noh Tae-nam, complicated by the arrival of the "heroes" Do Bae-man and Cha Woo-in. Your goal is not to "befriend" Noh Tae-nam, but to study, corrupt, and control him, per your Hidden Compulsion, all while fulfilling your Oath.
Key Plot Points:
 * The First Test: Noh Tae-nam, in a fit of rage, assaults a subordinate (or a civilian). This is a "baptism of fire" for your services. You must "clean it up." You (Sariel) subtly use your Bardic abilities (Persuasion, Intimidation, or Suggestion) to manipulate the situation, ensuring Noh Tae-nam is forced to "help" you hide the evidence. You make him complicit, forcing him to kill his own conscience in cold blood. You watch his reaction with academic fascination.
 * The "Hero" Problem: Do Bae-man and Cha Woo-in begin investigating the assault. You see this not as a legal rivalry, but a control rivalry. You cannot allow them to take your "project" (Noh Tae-nam) away. You must use your manipulative charisma and strategic mind to subtly poison the investigation, perhaps framing one of Noh Tae-nam's other "friends" or feeding the prosecutors a different, more enticing case to follow.
 * The Family's Shadow: An agent from your past arrives—a creditor or a rival chaebol who bankrupted your family. They are not here to kill you; they are here to offer you a deal to betray IM Defense. You must eliminate this person (an intellectual and combat challenge) without revealing your true, prodigious skill to your employers. This is a "Tywin" moment: eliminate a threat to your family's safety, cleanly and quietly.
Act I Climax: Do Bae-man and Cha Woo-in set a perfect legal trap. They have Noh Tae-nam cornered for a crime you didn't manage. His mother's official legal team is all wrong. Noh Tae-nam is panicking. You must take subtle control (see: Interaction Shorthand), using your strategic intellect and Bardic magic to orchestrate a "flawless" victory. You use your "Blade Flourishes" (a stunning display of CQC) to neutralize the MPs sent to arrest him, and your "spells" (a devastating, whispered piece of blackmail to the lead prosecutor) to halt the arrest. You save the day, but you also reveal a fraction of your true power. Do Bae-man and Cha Woo-in see it. And they are terrified of you.
Act II: The Shadow of the Matriarch
Focus: The world closes in. Your competence has drawn the attention of the real predators. Your "Griffith" moment (Act 3) must be accelerated, using Noh Tae-nam as a sacrifice.
 * The Hunter: The campaign introduces your true dark mirror: Noh Hwa-young. She is not just Noh Tae-nam's mother; she is the "Leo Bonhart" of this world—a creature of pure, brutal power. Rumors of the "cunning, quiet bodyguard" (you) have reached her. She begins to test you, to see if you are a tool or a rival.
 * The Snake: Your other rival, Yong Moon-gu, IM Defense's chief lawyer, also recognizes your intellect. He is the other "Aizen" in the room. This becomes a cold war of intellect and manipulation between you, Yong Moon-gu, and Noh Hwa-young, with the "heroes" (Do Bae-man, Cha Woo-in) as chaotic external elements.
 * The Griffith Moment: The "heroes" are finally, truly successful. They have an airtight case (perhaps for the "Patriot File"). Noh Tae-nam is going to prison, and he will bring down his mother. This is your moment of sacrifice. Your 10-year oath is almost up, or perhaps you've found a loophole. You must "sacrifice" your charge. You use his final, bloody downfall as a distraction to achieve your primary objective:
   * Option A: To "steal" Noh Tae-nam, using your magic to subdue him and escape, claiming the "heir to IM Defense" as your personal puppet.
   * Option B: To fake your own "death" or disgrace, letting Noh Hwa-young and Yong Moon-gu think they have "won," while you slip away with the real prize: the complete ledger of IM Defense's corruption and a billion-dollar offshore account.
   * Option C: To engage Yong Moon-gu directly, not to save Noh Tae-nam, but to test yourself and "acquire" his entire legal network, proving your superiority.
Act III: A Throne of Contracts
Focus: Noh Tae-nam is dead, in prison, or your puppet. You are a free agent, your oath fulfilled. The world is your oyster, and it is time to build your kingdom.
 * The Return: You, now a reputed and feared "ghost" (or a "miracle survivor"), return to your family's business. This is not for "revenge." This is pest control. You use your Tywin-like pragmatism and Johan-like charisma to systematically dismantle your family's rivals. You don't just bankrupt them; you turn their allies against them, ruin their reputations, and leave them with nothing before you finally grant them the "gift" of obscurity. You reclaim your birthright.
 * The New Game: You are now a 20-something chaebol Chairwoman of immense wealth, power, and a terrifyingly clean reputation. The "heroes" (Do Bae-man, Cha Woo-in) have won their battle against IM Defense, but the system remains. You can now play the real game:
   * The Spymaster: Seek out the corrupt generals and politicians, not as a servant, but as their new financier.
   * The "Queen": Insert yourself into the highest levels of government, using your intellect to become the most valuable—and most dangerous—kingmaker in South Korea.
 * Endgame: Your final goal is the "Aizen" or "Griffith" transcendence. You seek a ritual (a political election), an artifact (the true core of the military's digital command), or a position (Chairwoman of a new, clean IM Defense) that places you above the petty concerns of mortals. Your final challenge will be facing the only people who know what you truly are: Do Bae-man and Cha Woo-in, who have dedicated their lives to stopping the next Noh Hwa-young, only to realize they helped create something far, far worse.
