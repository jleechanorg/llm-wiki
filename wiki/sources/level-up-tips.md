## How Leveling Works

As you complete scenes and adventures in WorldArchitect.AI, your character earns experience points. When you hit the XP threshold, you level up — gaining new abilities, spell slots, ASIs (Ability Score Improvements), and access to higher-tier features.

## Getting the Most Out of Level-Ups

- **Plan your build early** — Know your class's power spikes (e.g., Tier 3 spells for casters, Extra Attack upgrades for martials) and plan ahead.
- **ASIs are precious** — Ability Score Improvements are limited. Don't waste them on half-feats unless you're already capped. Prioritize your primary stat first, then con, then secondary.
- **Multiclassing tradeoffs** — Delaying your main class's spell progression or attack bonus for dip features can be powerful, but costly. Be intentional.
- **Feat selection** — Some feats (Polearm Master, Sharpshooter, War Caster) are significantly stronger than ASIs. Factor this into your build planning.
- **Spell slot management at level-ups** — Track your total spell slots carefully. Full casters get big jumps at levels 5, 9, 13, 17.
- **HP bumps matter in Tier 1-2** — Extra HP from level-ups is most impactful early. Con should be your second priority after your primary stat.
- **Check what features unlock** — Paladins get Aura of Protection at level 6, Rogues get Reliable Talent at level 7. Knowing these unlocks helps you plan.
