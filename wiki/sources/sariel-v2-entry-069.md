The raw, unfiltered horror of Kaelan's memories still clawed at your mind, the metallic taste of blood a constant reminder of the psychic backlash. You had glimpsed it – the terrifying clarity of your mother's power, the chilling efficiency in unmaking – but the vision was chaotic, uncontrolled, and utterly devastating. Rowan continued his gentle murmurs beside the tormented Sergeant, while Gideon maintained his silent vigil, and Cressida watched with an intensity that missed nothing. Magister Kantos hovered by the door, pale and anxious. The air in the cell, thick with despair and the hum of Kaelan’s madness, felt heavy and suffocating.

You pushed yourself upright, swaying slightly, but your posture was rigid with a new, grim purpose. The time for individual exploration was over. You needed their minds, their skills, their unwavering loyalty. You needed a different approach.

You swept your gaze across your companions, meeting each of their eyes in turn. Their concern was palpable, their questions unspoken. "We have a problem," you stated, your voice raw but firm, cutting through the spectral moans of Kaelan’s madness. "A problem far more complex than any of us anticipated." You then began to speak, concisely but urgently, laying bare the chilling truths you had just uncovered.

"Sergeant Kaelan," you explained, gesturing to the tormented figure, "is not merely mad. His mind was shattered by witnessing a truth too profound for mortal comprehension. My mother... her public identity was a lie. She was not a Null. She possessed a terrifying, singular power: the Nullification Field. An ability that silenced all magic within its radius." You paused, letting that impossible statement sink in. "And, impossibly, she could wield her *own* magic within that void."

You saw Gideon’s eyes widen, a flicker of profound disbelief, quickly replaced by unwavering faith. Cressida’s sharp gaze snapped to Kantos, demanding silent confirmation, which the old Magister provided with a trembling nod. Rowan, still tending to Kaelan, visibly flinched, his gentle face etched with horror.

"The Null Genesis journals," you continued, your voice dropping, "confirm this. They theorize this power, this 'Joyful Predator' nature, is a refined manifestation of the Empyrean Affliction itself—a perversion of celestial will, a lucid, chilling desire for absolute control through unmaking. And it is linked to Lucifer's own essence." You touched your own Starlit Eyes, the mark of your shared bloodline. "This terrifying potential lies dormant within our own lineage. Kaelan witnessed her embrace this power, this... cold, terrifying joy in destruction. And his mind could not endure it."

You gestured to Kaelan's moaning form. "I tried to access his memories with `Comprehend Languages`. I glimpsed it. The Nullification. The 'unmoved' power. But the backlash was immense. His mind is a maelstrom. It’s walled off. We cannot simply force our way in without risking further harm to him, or shattering my own fragile composure." You met their gazes, your voice now pleading, laced with a desperate urgency. "We need to access his memories. To understand. But we cannot break him further. And I cannot risk breaking myself. How do we do this? How do we access a mind shattered by ultimate truth?"

The silence in the cell was heavy, filled with the echoes of your horrifying revelations and Kaelan's low, persistent moans. Your companions, now armed with the impossible truth, looked from the tormented Sergeant to you, the fragile princess who had just revealed herself to be the unwitting heir to a terrifying, hidden power.
