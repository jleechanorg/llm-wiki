Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 10:35 AM.
Location: Deep Archives, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.

You sagged against the obsidian shelves, clutching your head, the cold stone a faint anchor in the storm of your internal chaos. The taste of blood was acrid in your mouth, and your body throbbed with a pain that went bone-deep, not from any physical blow, but from the sheer psychic strain. Kantos’s frantic words buzzed around you, distant and muffled, confirming the horrifying truth: you had touched a power too great for your untrained mind, a power that could, indeed, unmake you.

The vision, though fleeting, had been devastatingly real. You remembered the silent explosion, the sudden void, the sheer, unimaginable force of magic being ripped from the world. It was a terrifying beauty, a pure, destructive clarity. And for a split second, you understood the perverse "joy" the journals had spoken of – not in cruelty, but in the absolute, elegant perfection of control, of unmaking. The realization chilled you more than the vault’s cold. Your mother had walked this path, embraced that monstrous joy, and had somehow, impossibly, survived it.

Kantos’s trembling hand hovered closer, his voice laced with desperate worry. "You must rest, Princess. This knowledge, this power... it will consume you if you force it. It consumed him," he gestured vaguely toward the Quiet Ward, "and he was a Starfall Guard. A warrior of iron." His gaze met yours, filled with a deep, sorrowful warning. "You are not her, Sariel. Not yet."

His words, meant to caution, only sharpened your resolve. You had glimpsed the truth, tasted its terrifying power. It was chaotic, yes. It was painful. But it was *possible*. And the only way to tame it, to understand its raw, unbridled force, was through the one who had seen its full, terrifying manifestation. Kaelan. He was no longer just a source of information; he was your key to a power you now knew you desperately needed to control.

You pushed yourself upright, swaying slightly, but your posture was now rigid with an unwavering purpose. The throbbing in your head, the metallic taste of blood – it all faded to background noise. You looked at Kantos, your Starlit Eyes burning with a fierce, almost desperate determination that left no room for argument or fear.

"Then take me to him, Magister," you commanded, your voice steady despite the lingering tremor in your body. It was not a plea, but a decree. "Now. Sergeant Kaelan. The Quiet Ward. No more delays. No more warnings. I need to see him. I need to know."

Kantos stared, his ancient face paling further. He wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting from your fierce resolve to the oppressive darkness of the vault. He saw the cold echo of Alexiel's will in your eyes, the same unyielding demand for truth that had led your mother to her impossible victories. He let out a long, defeated sigh, the sound a dry rustle in the silent chamber. He knew you would not be deterred. "As you wish, Princess," he rasped, his voice thick with a mixture of terror and weary resignation. "But I must warn you... the Quiet Ward is not a place for the faint of heart. And the Silent Guard... they will not simply allow access."
