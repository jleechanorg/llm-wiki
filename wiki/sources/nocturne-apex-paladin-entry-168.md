Location: House Valerius Estate, Lord Cassian's Private Study, Morning

The study air, usually a sanctuary of quiet authority, now thrummed with the raw shock radiating from Lord Cassian Valerius (Age 55). The assembled evidence—the deed, the ledger, the Orbs of Scrying, the Shadowfell maps, the malevolent obsidian shard, the chilling Darkwood Casket, and the decoded journal detailing the Architect's "Convergence" in three nights—lay spread across his polished desk, a damning testament to a conspiracy far grander and more terrifying than he had conceived. His usual impassive mask was utterly shattered, replaced by a pallor and eyes wide with a mixture of profound shock and cold, dangerous fury. He looked from the artifacts to Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16), a grim recognition dawning in his gaze.

Nocturne allowed the silence to stretch, a powerful weapon in itself. She offered no further words, no elaboration, no demand. Her presence was absolute, unwavering, devoid of any weakness or emotional appeal. She simply watched him, a silent observer of his internal struggle, her own features an unreadable mask. He understood. He understood the betrayal, the scale of the threat, and the chilling truth that his exiled daughter had just revealed a vulnerability that his vast network had entirely missed. She was not begging for his attention; she was demanding his recognition, forcing his hand through irrefutable, devastating facts. The weight of his realization filled the room.

Lord Cassian's gaze returned to the artifacts, his fingers slowly tracing the ancient runes on the Darkwood Casket, then the marked locations on the Shadowfell map. His breath came in a slow, controlled exhalation, a sign of a powerful mind rapidly processing and adapting to a new, terrifying reality. The initial shock receded, replaced by a cold, strategic fury. His eyes, when they finally met Nocturne's again, held a dangerous glint, a flicker of the ruthless pragmatism she so deeply admired and emulated.

"Unprecedented," he murmured, the word a rasp. "And in three nights." He rose slowly from his desk, his gaze sweeping over the evidence one last time, committing every detail to memory. He then fixed his eyes on Nocturne, a silent challenge, a profound acknowledgment of her chilling capabilities. The battle of wills had ended, not in capitulation, but in a grudging, absolute acceptance of her undeniable value.

Lord Cassian took a slow, measured breath, his eyes never leaving hers. The strategic implications of her intelligence were immense, far outweighing his personal feelings or Julian’s incompetence. "A clandestine outpost. Shadowfell incursions. A Convergence orchestrated by some 'Architect' in the Underdark. And my network, my son... blind." His voice was low, each word a hammer blow of realization. He stepped from behind his desk, moving closer to the scattered evidence, then finally, to Nocturne.

His hand, surprisingly, rested briefly on her shoulder, a gesture devoid of affection but heavy with the weight of acknowledgment. "You have uncovered a viper in our own garden, Nocturne. A threat of cosmic scale. My network... my resources... they are yours. You will manage this. You will lead the counter-operation. No Julian. No interference. Report directly to me." His eyes held a flicker of grim satisfaction, perhaps even pride, that his own blood possessed such chilling competence. "Take what you need. Agents, funds, arcane specialists from the Watchful Order – whatever is necessary to stop this 'Convergence' and dissect Durin's Folly's involvement. The honor of House Valerius, and perhaps the fate of Baldur's Gate, rests on your shoulders, youngest daughter." The slight emphasis on 'youngest daughter' was a subtle, almost imperceptible jab at Julian, a grudging admission of her superiority.

Nocturne felt a cold, deep satisfaction. No overt triumph, merely the quiet affirmation of power grasped. The game was truly hers to play.
