You approach the huddle of miners gathered near the slag heaps. They are a sorry sight—men and women with dust-choked lungs and hollow eyes, clutching pickaxes like weapons of war rather than tools of industry. They watch your approach with a mix of fear and desperate hope, eyeing the heavy plate on Minsc and the glowing rune on Kagain's new shield.

A burly man with a soot-stained beard, likely the foreman, steps forward. He spits a glob of black phlegm onto the frozen mud.

"More mercenaries?" he rasps. "Ghastkill keeps sending 'em. The mountain keeps eating 'em."

You stop a few paces away, letting the silence stretch just long enough to be uncomfortable. The *Hunger* sharpens your gaze, dissecting their terror. They aren't scared of a cave-in. They are scared of something hunting them.

"The Mayor paid us a great deal of gold to stop the eating," you say, your voice calm and professional. "But to do that, I need to know what's on the menu. You speak of demons. Be specific. Are we talking about fiends from the Abyss, or just something with sharp teeth?"

**Persuasion Check: 21 (Success)**

The foreman looks at your gear—the enchanted sword at your hip, the confident set of your shoulders—and decides you might actually survive the first hour.

"Not demons in the holy sense, mistress," the foreman admits, lowering his voice. "But devils all the same. **Kobolds**. Hundreds of 'em. But not like any kobolds we've seen. They don't scatter when you yell. They hold the line. They set traps—tripwires, poison darts, fire bottles. And they yip... they yip like laughing hyenas while they stick you."

He shudders. "But that ain't the worst of it. Deep down? Third level and below? The kobolds don't go there. We hear... shuffling. Clanking. Like metal dragging on stone. And the smell... smells like a grave opened up in the summer heat. **Undead**. Skeletons, mostly, but armored. And they guard the iron."

**Insight Check: 15 (Success)**

You read the truth in his fear. The kobolds are the infantry—organized, trapped, dangerous in numbers. The undead are the heavy guard, protecting the source of the taint deep in the earth. This confirms Dynaheir's theory of a higher intelligence directing them; kobolds and skeletons do not naturally ally.

**Minsc** growls low in his throat. "Kobolds? Little dog-men who fight with traps? Boo says this is cowardly! We shall stomp them like bugs!"

**Kagain** grunts. "Traps mean we watch our feet. Undead mean we bring blunt instruments. Skeletons don't bleed, but they shatter."

**Dynaheir** steps forward, her eyes scanning the dark tunnel entrance. "The air... it tastes metallic. And necrotic. The taint is strong here. We must be cautious."

You have the intel: **Kobolds (Traps/Ambush)** in the upper levels, **Undead** in the depths. The entrance looms before you, a dark maw breathing cold, foul air.
