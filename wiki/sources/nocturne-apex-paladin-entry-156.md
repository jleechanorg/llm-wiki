Location: House Valerius Estate, Lord Cassian's Private Study, Morning

The heavy oak door of Lord Cassian Valerius's study clicked shut, sealing Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) and her father in a profound, charged silence. Lord Cassian (Age 55) remained motionless behind his vast desk, his gaze, sharp and intelligent, fixed on his youngest daughter. His stillness was unnerving, a strategic tool he wielded as deftly as any blade, designed to compel a premature revelation. He sought to dissect her intentions, to ascertain the depth and veracity of her audacious claim.

Nocturne met his unwavering gaze without a hint of concession. Her own presence, a chilling force, filled the space between them. She was not a petitioner; she was a bearer of truth, a deliverer of vital, terrifying intelligence. The silent test concluded.

Nocturne broke the silence, her voice a low, even tone, each word carefully chosen to provoke a precise reaction. "House Durin's Folly, Father, has established a clandestine outpost on our eastern approaches. It is not a farm. It is a highly defended surveillance and acquisition hub, equipped with professional guards, arcane wards, and direct ties to something far more insidious." She paused, allowing the gravity of "clandestine outpost" and "arcane wards" to sink in. "Their activities extend beyond mere territorial gain. They are actively involved in operations within the **Shadowfell**, orchestrated by a figure known only as **'The Architect.'** And a critical **'Convergence'** is planned for three nights from now, beneath Baldur's Gate."

Lord Cassian's impassive features remained unbroken, yet his eyes, those cold pools of intellect, became impossibly sharp. His fingers, resting lightly on his desk, tightened almost imperceptibly. The mention of "Shadowfell" and "The Architect," coupled with the precise urgency of "three nights," registered with a chilling impact. He did not speak, but his strategic mind was visibly churning, rapidly re-evaluating his understanding of House Durin's Folly and the true threats to his empire. The implied scale of the conspiracy, the direct link to extraplanar forces, and the proximity of the "Convergence" were clearly not something he could dismiss. A flicker of something akin to grim recognition, or perhaps grudging respect for her chilling capabilities, passed across his face before his mask of composure solidified once more. He understood the profound implications of her words.
