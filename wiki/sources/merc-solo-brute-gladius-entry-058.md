The Stonehand dwelling, solid and defiant, stood before Gladius, its chimney already curling a lazy wisp of smoke into the pre-dawn air. The sounds of activity from within – a clatter, a gruff voice – meant subtlety was nearly impossible. His contempt for their boisterous, unprotected lives solidified. The "no witnesses" clause was a challenge here, not a guarantee. The silent precision of the cord might be harder to maintain. This called for something loud. Something to make them look away, even for a moment.

His eyes, cold and calculating, swept the immediate vicinity. The houses were too close, but between the Stonehand home and the next, a small, neglected shed sagged under the weight of years. Its roof was patched with decaying thatch, its wooden door half-rotted off its hinges. *Perfect.* It was close enough to be heard, but far enough not to immediately expose his position.

He moved with a hunter’s silent grace, his Shadow-Step Boots absorbing the slight crunch of frosted grass, the metallic whisper of his splint mail muted to an almost imperceptible rustle. He reached the shed, pulling a heavy length of discarded timber from a nearby pile. With a low, guttural grunt, a surge of raw Physique, he slammed the timber against the shed's flimsy supports, then twisted and pulled, tearing away a portion of the rotting wall. The sound was a sudden, violent **CRACK!** followed by the groaning, splintering collapse of timbers and the rustle of debris. It was a loud, undeniable sound of damage, of something falling apart in the quiet pre-dawn.

A triumphant, almost primal satisfaction pulsed beneath Gladius's skin. This was tangible, immediate. The raw, unfiltered pleasure of creating chaos, of bending the environment to his will. He backed away, melting into the deeper shadows of a nearby hedgerow. From the Stonehand dwelling, the low voices instantly ceased. A beat of silence, then a confused, muffled shout. "What in the blazes was that?" The distraction worked. Their simple curiosity would now draw them out, away from their warm beds, away from their precious home. The first streaks of orange were already painting the eastern sky, and the game was about to shift from silent predation to something more visceral.
