Corvan's words, "You have my word. And Lord Silvanus's," grated on Gladius. A "word" was air. "Lord Silvanus's word" was less than air, a puff of arrogance from a worm he intended to crush. Gladius’s eyes remained fixed on Corvan, piercing through the man’s forced calm. The vein in his neck pulsed more noticeably now, a silent, almost predatory throb against his skin. He took a single, slow step closer to the table, his greatsword scraping faintly against the floorboards as he shifted, a sound like a grave being dug.

"Your word is nothing," Gladius rumbled, his voice a low, gravelly current, cold as the marsh mist. "Silvanus's word is less. I need details. A name. A place. How was he 'accounted for'? A bottle of ale and a few coppers, or does he bleed when pricked?" Gladius’s lip curled into a sneer, a flash of contempt for the petty corruption that dared to call itself an arrangement. He wanted a weak point, a specific contact, a piece of leverage, or a piece of evidence. He wanted to know the exact nature of the sheriff's compromise, to ensure no surprises. His posture was an unyielding wall of muscle and menace, pressing Corvan.

Corvan recoiled slightly, his carefully maintained composure finally shattering. His face paled, and his eyes darted to the empty doorway, then back to Gladius, a desperate animal trapped. He visibly swallowed, his throat working. "Borin... he's at his home, near the village well," Corvan stammered, abandoning all pretense of calm. "He's... quite drunk, and will remain so. He expects no callers. Corvan's voice dropped to a near-whisper, laced with frantic urgency. "The gold was enough to blind him for a week. He'll sleep through anything. He has a weakness for... a certain vintage of elven wine. It's potent. He'll be out until midday, and confused long after." He gestured vaguely towards the village with a trembling hand, as if trying to physically push Gladius towards the task and away from himself. "No one else is on patrol tonight."

Gladius simply stared, absorbing the details. A drunkard sheriff, incapacitated by his own vice. Simple. Predictable. *Weak.* The thought brought a grim satisfaction. This was the kind of 'control' he understood.
