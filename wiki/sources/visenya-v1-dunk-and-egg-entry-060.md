The dust of the road seems to part before the white banners of the approaching riders. They sit their horses with the easy arrogance of men who know they are the law in these parts, their surcoats bearing the orange sun and white chevron of House Ashford. Dunk pulls Thunder to a halt, his massive hand shielding his eyes as he squinted at the shimmer of steel.

"Outriders," he grunted, the old suspicion clouding his face. "They'll be looking for trouble, or looking to make some. Keep your head down, Silver."

You didn't lower your head. Instead, you nudged your pony closer, your voice ringing with the quiet, effortless authority of the schoolroom. "There is no need for worry, Ser. Those are Lord Ashford's own men. Look at the chevron—it's centered, not broken, which means they are of the main line's household guard, not some minor cousin's retinue. The sun has thirteen rays; that marks them as the Ashford Outriders, the sworn protectors of the meadow."

Dunk looked from the banners to you, his jaw working as he tried to count the rays on a snapping piece of cloth from fifty yards away. "Thirteen? I can barely see the sun, let alone its whiskers."

"Thirteen for the thirteen daughters of the first Lord Ashford," you added, the historical trivia sliding off your tongue with practiced ease. "You must address the leader as 'Captain' or 'Master Outrider'. If they ask our business, we tell them we are here for the tourney. They are expected to be firm, Ser, but they are also bound by the laws of hospitality while the sun is up. Just speak clearly. I will handle the specifics of the lineage if they prove inquisitive."

Dunk let out a low whistle, a slow grin spreading across his weathered face. "Thirteen daughters. I'd rather face thirteen lances. But you've the right of it, lad. If you know their history, they'll think twice before treating us like common brigands."
