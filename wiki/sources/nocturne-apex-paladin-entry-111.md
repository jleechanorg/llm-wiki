Location: Man Tibbet's Farm, Farmhouse Interior, Early Evening

The farmhouse was utterly silent, its warmth a strange counterpoint to the chilling stillness within. The Tibbet family lay inert, secured and silent, mere bundles on the floor. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) stood amidst her completed work, her gaze sharp and analytical. The farm's external and internal security, including the road patrol and the barn's arcane ward, had been systematically dismantled or bypassed. Her short rest had replenished her EP and Channel Divinity, restoring her to full capacity.

Her mind, a relentless engine of strategic thought, processed the confluence of acquired intelligence: the deed, the ledger detailing shadowy payments, the Orbs of Scrying, and most significantly, the obsidian shard, the ciphered journal, and the topographical maps of the Shadowfell. This farm was a clandestine outpost of House Durin's Folly, engaged in operations far more sinister and politically explosive than mere farming. This intel was the true prize, a powerful lever in the intricate game of Baldur's Gate politics, and particularly against her own House Valerius's rivals.

Her meticulous nature, that intrinsic need for absolute control and complete information, demanded one final, deeper sweep. The locked study drawers had resisted her earlier, and the farmhouse might hold other, deeper secrets. The family lay secured; the immediate threats were gone. The time for further investigation was now.

Nocturne returned to Man Tibbet's study, her focus absolute. She knelt before the stubborn drawers, producing her **Masterwork Lockpicks**. Her earlier attempt had failed, but that was a consequence of a less focused effort amidst other objectives. Now, her intellect (Intelligence 18) and coordination (14) were entirely dedicated to this singular task. She probed the mechanisms with a dancer's precision, feeling for the subtle resistance, mapping the tumblers in her mind.

A series of soft, precise clicks echoed in the quiet room as the first drawer yielded. Inside, beneath a false bottom, Nocturne found not just the lead-lined box she'd spotted before, but a hidden compartment within *that* box. Within this deeper recess, nestled on a bed of velvet, lay a single, perfectly crafted **Darkwood Casket**. It was no larger than her palm, but radiated an ancient, malevolent chill. Its surface was inscribed with faint, almost imperceptible runes that seemed to writhe in the dim light. She recognized the style of inscription from her **Ancient Lore (Forgotten Realms)** expertise – these were symbols associated with the cult of Myrkul, god of the dead, or perhaps even a more ancient, primordial death entity.

The second drawer opened with a simpler click, revealing not just the pouch of gold, blank parchments, and Shadowfell maps she'd previously scanned, but also a tightly rolled scroll. Unfurling it, Nocturne found it to be a **Cipher Key** for the ciphered journal she already possessed, along with a note in the same spidery hand as the ledger, referencing "The Architect's Whispers" and a rendezvous point in a secluded area of the **Underdark** beneath Baldur's Gate, set for three nights from now.

The implications were staggering. House Durin's Folly was not just observing; they were actively involved with ancient, malevolent powers, possibly in the Underdark itself, and the Architect was clearly a key figure. The Shadowfell maps, the obsidian shard, the ciphered journal – all pieces of a terrifyingly complex puzzle. This wasn't merely local intrigue; it was a conspiracy reaching into the darkest corners of existence, potentially impacting Baldur's Gate on a scale even her own ruthlessly pragmatic family might not comprehend.

Nocturne's gaze hardened, a cold, almost imperceptible smirk touching her lips. This was a true game, a canvas worthy of her intellect. The 'stock' was secured, but the true prize was the revelation of this profound, chilling secret. The night's work had yielded far more than she could have anticipated. Power, and the pathway to even greater power, lay before her.
