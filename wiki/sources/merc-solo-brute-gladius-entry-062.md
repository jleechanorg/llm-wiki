The Stonehand dwelling was now a sealed chamber, the muffled shouts of the distracted couple outside fading as they investigated Gladius’s diversion. The warmth from the dying hearth, the scent of cooking oats, all seemed to intensify, creating a false sense of security that Gladius found profoundly satisfying. This was the pleasure of the hunt: the unsuspecting prey, the perfectly set trap.

His hand, large and calloused, moved to his belt pouch, drawing forth the Silent Strangler’s Cord. The thin, dark fiber, woven with insidious purpose, felt cool and smooth against his skin. He tested its tensile strength with a practiced tug, the material giving just enough to confirm its deadly efficiency. A grim, almost eager anticipation settled in his gut. This was his art: the silent, brutal removal of life. The greatsword on his back was for open battle, for making statements. The cord was for secrets, for erasing, for the exquisite sensation of absolute control. He coiled it expertly in his palm, ready, its black threads seeming to absorb the dim light.

Then, he moved. His Shadow-Step Boots, true to their name, ensured each step was a whisper against the worn stone floor. He moved with the focused, unblinking precision of a hunting cat, his broad shoulders barely disturbing the dim, hearth-lit air. He followed the faint sounds of slumber, the soft rise and fall of breaths, the occasional, unconscious shift in a bed. His senses, unburdened by excessive introspection, zeroed in on these primal cues, guiding him effortlessly. This was the raw, immediate experience of power, the thrill of the predator closing in.

He located them in a small, partitioned off section of the common room, separated by a hanging cloth. A few lighter, quicker breaths, like the Miller children, suggested younger occupants. Another, distinct and softer, indicated a third, perhaps an older child or relative. Three more souls. All utterly unaware. Gladius’s lips thinned into a grim line. Their simple existence, their pathetic slumber, was a testament to his own burgeoning power. The final act was approaching.
