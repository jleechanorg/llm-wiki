The air at the gate is thick with the scent of damp earth and the sharp, metallic tang of fear. You stumble slightly as you reach the base of the ramparts, your midnight-blue silks torn and clinging to your marble-pale skin like a second, ruined skin. You do not shiver—your body has long since abandoned such mortal inefficiencies—but you tilt your head at just the right angle to simulate a neck too weak to carry the weight of its own exhaustion. 

Zevlor looks down, his brow-ridges furrowed. Above him, the tiefling guards shift their weight, the tension in their crossbow arms visibly deflating as they process the image of a sixteen-year-old girl, porcelain-perfect and seemingly broken by the road. The 'Black Diamond' has never played a victim so convincingly. 

"Lower the gate!" Zevlor’s voice booms, though it lacks its usual edge of command, softened by a misplaced paternal instinct. "She’s barely more than a child. And she's... she's not one of them. Open the way!"

As the heavy wooden gate groans upward, you catch Gale's eye. He looks troubled, his gaze lingering on the tiefling children huddling near the inner fire. You try to read the depth of his hesitation, but the wizard has masked his thoughts behind a wall of scholarly detachment. Wyll is even more opaque, his hand white-knuckled on his rapier, refusing to meet your gaze. You have planted the seeds of the 'greater good' logic, but as you step into the sanctuary of the Grove, you realize the harvest of their loyalty will be more difficult than the subversion of the Druids.

You pass through the threshold, the 'Rite of Thorns' humming with a honeyed, iron-rich vibration that makes the luminescence in your eyes pulse beneath your eyelids. You are inside.
