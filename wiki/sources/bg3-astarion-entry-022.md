With the burly man now armed, his eyes still burning with an almost animalistic rage, Astarion's attention snaps back to the grotesque Mind Flayers. A direct confrontation, while seemingly inevitable given his companions' dispositions, was not his preferred method. *Efficiency*, that was the key. Find the weakness, exploit the blind spot, then vanish. He held the jagged bone blade, its psionic hum a faint echo of the Mind Flayers' own oppressive presence, as he subtly shifted his weight, preparing to dart if necessary. His heart hammered with a blend of terror and a thrill for the hunt.

He watched their every tentacle twitch, every subtle shift of their alien forms as they attended to the pulsating engine. They were masters of psionics, but they still occupied physical space, still interacted with this hideous machinery. There had to be a rhythm, a pattern, a moment of distraction, or a vital point in their grotesque operation that he could disrupt. His ranger's mind, usually dissecting urban patterns, now turned its analytical focus to these otherworldly horrors. He noticed how one Mind Flayer repeatedly adjusted a specific fleshy conduit, its tentacles brushing against a glowing node. Another seemed to be channeling a continuous stream of psionic energy into a swirling vortex, its attention singular. The hum in his head, the tadpole's presence, felt almost... *attuned* to their work, a horrifying intimacy he hated but might exploit. He pushed past the nausea, dissecting their actions, their very being, for a flaw.
