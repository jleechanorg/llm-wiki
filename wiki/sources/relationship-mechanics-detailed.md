## Summary
Comprehensive game design document establishing relationship mechanics as active gameplay systems rather than passive flavor. Defines trust levels (-10 to +10), disposition types (hostile through bonded), mandatory check/update triggers, behavior modifiers, memory protocols, and cascading relationship effects.

## Key Claims
- **Active Mechanics** — Relationships are required game mechanics that modify NPC behavior, not passive flavor text
- **Trust Scale** — -10 (Hostile) to +10 (Bonded) with 8 disposition tiers affecting pricing, information sharing, and combat behavior
- **Mandatory Triggers** — NPCs must check relationship data before interactions and update after significant actions
- **Memory Protocol** — NPCs track first impressions, promises, favors, insults, and treatment of allies
- **Cascading Effects** — Harming one NPC damages relationships with their allies

## Key Quotes
> "CRITICAL: Relationships are ACTIVE game mechanics, not passive flavor text."

> "BEFORE any NPC interaction, CHECK their relationship data"

> "NPCs REMEMBER: First impressions, Promises made, Favors owed, Insults and slights, How player treated NPC's friends/faction"
