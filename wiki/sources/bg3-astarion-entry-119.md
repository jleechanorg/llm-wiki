The human, a pathetic, trembling mess, babbled incoherently, offering nothing but raw, animal terror. Astarion's lip curled in disgust. *Useless. Utterly, predictably useless.* He straightened up, his longbow still held loosely, his gaze sweeping over the charred clearing. The acrid scent of ozone and burnt metal mingled with the cloying stench of the human's fear, a smell that, despite his revulsion, sent a faint, insidious thrum through his own starving body. He hadn't fed on sentient blood in two centuries, a cruel deprivation inflicted by Cazador. Now, here was a fresh, terrified morsel, ripe for the taking. The thought made his fangs ache, a primal urge he fought to suppress, yet it was a temptation as old as his curse.

He glanced at his companions. Lae'zel, grim and unyielding, stood like a statue of coiled aggression, her golden eyes sweeping the clearing, her disdain for weakness palpable. Gale, clutching his head, still looked rather green, his scholarly sensibilities clearly offended by the raw violence they'd just witnessed. Astarion's high neuroticism pulsed behind his eye, warring with the insistent craving. This was a precarious moment. A choice to be made.

His crimson eyes fixed on Lae'zel, then Gale, a flicker of his usual charm attempting to mask the desperate hunger. His earlier, blatant request had failed spectacularly, igniting their predictable moral outrage. But Astarion was nothing if not adaptable. He needed a different approach.

"Right," Astarion drawled, his voice a low, sardonic purr, stripped of its previous desperation, "now that we've established our... *benefactor* here is utterly useless for immediate information, we face a rather pressing issue. This area." He gestured vaguely at the scorched earth and splintered trees. "It's a testament to the Nautiloid's rather *crude* landing. There could be any manner of horrors lurking just beyond our sight, creatures drawn by the crash, or perhaps even more of those illithid's abominations. We are currently... exposed."

He met Lae'zel's impatient gaze. "One of us should scout the immediate perimeter. Ensure our rear isn't exposed. Perhaps locate a more defensible position. And search for any salvage that isn't... *organic.*" He glanced pointedly at Gale. "While I, with my rather refined senses, can remain here to 'tend' to our trembling friend and perhaps glean some coherence from his terror. And keep watch over him, of course. A valuable asset, this one, for drawing attention." He raised a sardonic brow, his heart hammering with a desperate hope for their pragmatic acceptance. The words were a thinly veiled lie, but plausible enough. Lae'zel's tactical mind would surely see the logic in reconnaissance. Gale, though still disgusted, might follow.

Lae'zel's golden eyes narrowed, assessing his words. Her gaze flicked from Astarion to the human, then to the dense, charred treeline. Her initial sneer of disgust softened marginally into a grim, thoughtful expression. "Tactical sense," she rasped, a clipped acknowledgment. "Very well. The perimeter must be secured. Gale, with me. The faster we assess the threats, the sooner we can move." She didn't wait for Gale's reply, already turning towards the eastern edge of the clearing.

Gale, looking utterly miserable, finally pushed himself to his feet. He cast a wary, still-disgusted glance at Astarion, then a pitying one at the trembling farmer. "Just... don't be a monster, Astarion," he muttered, his voice low and pleading, before reluctantly following Lae'zel into the smoke-tinged shadows of the charred trees. Astarion felt a surge of triumph, quickly masked by a cool composure. They were gone. For now. The farmer, eyes wide with terror, remained.

Astarion's crimson eyes gleamed with a predatory satisfaction as Lae'zel and Gale disappeared into the smoke-tinged shadows. *Fools. As if 'tend' was ever in my vocabulary.* The human, still a quivering mess, whimpered, his eyes wide and unfocused. The scent of his fear was a raw, intoxicating perfume to Astarion's starving senses, sharpening his fangs to an unbearable ache. Two centuries of forced abstinence. The memory of Cazador's cruel deprivation fueled a cold, desperate hunger that overrode all other considerations. This was not about morality; it was about survival.

He moved with a fluid, silent grace that belied the frantic thrum of his heart. One slender hand, quick as a viper, clamped over the farmer's mouth, stifling the inevitable shriek. The other, surprisingly strong, pinned the man's flailing limbs to the scorched earth. The farmer convulsed, his eyes wide with a terror that Astarion, for once, didn't entirely savor. He merely saw sustenance. His fangs, long and sharp, extended with a soft click, and with a single, precise movement, he plunged them into the farmer's neck.

A jolt of pure, exquisite pleasure, shockingly intense, coursed through Astarion's body as the warm, vital blood filled his mouth. It was a torrent of life, banishing the gnawing emptiness, the constant thrum of starvation that had plagued him for so long. He drank, deeply, desperately, yet with a practiced efficiency, careful not to drain too much, not to leave an obvious, irreversible mark. He felt a surge of vitality, a warmth spreading through his limbs, his muscles relaxing, his mind clearing. The tadpole behind his eye, which had been squirming so insistently, seemed to quiet, lulled by the sudden influx of raw life energy. It was a fleeting, but profound, satiation. After a few precious moments, he pulled back, wiping his lips with the back of his hand, a grim satisfaction touching his features. The farmer lay limp, unconscious, but still breathing, a faint, rhythmic pulse in his throat.

Astarion felt undeniably stronger, the edge of his neurotic fear blunted by the renewed vigor. He glanced towards the direction Lae'zel and Gale had taken. No sign of them. No shouts. His discretion had held. For now.
