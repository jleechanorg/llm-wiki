Game Master:
You look at the rogue, then at the empty throne of the wizard. The *Hunger* calculates the logistics of empire.

"Roric," you say. "Sit."

The rogue raises an eyebrow but drops into Davaeorn's high-backed chair, propping his boots on the map table. "Comfortable. But lonely."

"Temporary," you correct him. "You hold the mine. You manage the slaves. Keep the ore flowing, but stockpile it. Do not sell to the Throne. I am going to fetch you a relief force."

You turn to the party. "We march. Back to the Bandit Camp."

**Operation: Garrison Swap**

**Mirtul 12-14: The Return Trek**
You leave Roric with the keys to the kingdom and a warning not to get stabbed by the slaves. The march back to the **Bandit Camp** is swift; you know the terrain now, and the spiders are burned out. You arrive at the **Fortified Armory** on the evening of the 14th.

**The Rendezvous**
Your timing is impeccable. **Garrick** has just arrived from Nashkel, looking exhausted but triumphant. Trailing behind him are a dozen hard-looking mercenaries—unemployed caravan guards and Flaming Fist deserters looking for coin. He has followed your instructions to the letter.

"Mistress!" Garrick cries, dropping his pack. "The fortress is... rustic. But defensible! I have brought the swords you asked for!"

"Change of plan," you announce, not breaking stride. "This camp is a husk. The real prize is north. Pack up. We move the garrison to the Cloakwood."

Garrick's face falls. "But... my feet..."

"Walk," **Kagain** growls.

**Mirtul 15-17: The Deployment**
You lead the mercenary column back through the forest. With a dozen swords at your back, the few remaining beasts of the Cloakwood give you a wide berth. You arrive back at the **Cloakwood Mines** on the 17th.

**The Handoff**
You descend to Level 4. Roric is waiting, looking bored, tossing a dagger at a target drawn on a tapestry. He sees Garrick and the soldiers and grins.

"Relief!" Roric cheers, springing from the chair. "And not a moment too soon. The damp down here plays hell with my leathers."

You install **Garrick** as the Warden of the Mines. You give him simple orders: Hold the facility. Protect the workers. Stockpile the iron. Wait for your word to flood the market.

Garrick looks at the opulent sanctum, then at the army at his command. His chest swells. "Warden Garrick... I like the sound of that. I shall compose an anthem for our new domain!"

**Roric** rejoins the pack, equipping his gear. "Good to be back on the road, boss. Running a business is harder work than killing people."

You stand at the mine entrance. The asset is secured. The income is inevitable. Your party is whole again. The road to **Baldur's Gate** lies north, and the *Hunger* is beginning to stir again.
