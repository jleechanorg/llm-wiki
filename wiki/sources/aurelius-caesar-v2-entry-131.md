The sun hangs at its zenith, a cruel, mocking eye above the jagged peaks of Vesuvius. You stand amidst the grit of the western barricade, your fingers raw and weeping as you heave a jagged limestone block into place. The weight is honest; it does not care for your lineage. Nasir watches you from a distance, his gaze unreadable, while the other rebels whisper as they pass the 'noble' who bleeds like a man.

Away from the sweat of the line, in the cool, echoing shadows of the Command Grotto, the air is thick with a different kind of tension. Spartacus, Crixus, and Aurelia stand in a triangle of silence, the name 'Caesar' still vibrating in the stagnant air.

"The son of the monster," Crixus spits, his voice a low, jagged rasp that sounds like grinding stone. He paces the narrow alcove, his hand never leaving the hilt of his gladius. "We have a wolf in the fold, Spartacus. A wolf who wears the skin of a lamb and speaks of justice while his father builds crosses from our bones!"

Spartacus remains motionless, his silhouette a sharp shadow against the dripping stalactites. His eyes are fixed on the Aegis of Julia, abandoned in the dust. "He saved Naevia, Crixus. He stood alone against three hundred when he could have simply identified himself and been returned to a palace in Capua. Does a wolf offer his own throat to the pack?"

"It is a game!" Crixus roars, turning on him. "The Julians do not do anything for 'justice'. They do it for ownership! He didn't save Naevia for her life; he saved her to own our gratitude. To own *you*. To own this rebellion before it even begins! He is a Caesar! Every breath he takes is an insult to every man we lost in the pits!"

"He is a man," Aurelia interrupts, her voice trembling but carrying a resonant, fierce clarity. She steps between the two leaders, her silver scars catching the dim light. "I felt his light. I saw his eyes when he thought he was dying on the Spires. There was no 'ownership' there, Gaul. There was only a boy who wanted to be better than the world that birthed him. If you kill him for his father's name, you prove that Rome has already won—you've become the very thing you hate."

Crixus snarls, a sound of pure, concentrated agony. "He is a brand, girl! A mark upon us! If Glaber finds out who he is, he will burn this mountain to ash just to retrieve the Emperor's vanity!"

"Then we ensure Glaber never finds out," Spartacus says, his voice dropping to a gravelly, final whisper. He looks at Crixus, then at the entrance where the rhythmic thud of stone-labor echoes from the ridge. "He stays in the trenches. He bleeds in the salt. If he is a Caesar, he will break. If he is a brother... he will be the steel that helps us break Rome. But keep your eyes sharp, Crixus. If he so much as whispers a command to the men, his life is forfeit."

Outside, you lift another stone. The mountain groans, and for a heartbeat, the weight of the Julian line feels lighter than the rock in your hands.
