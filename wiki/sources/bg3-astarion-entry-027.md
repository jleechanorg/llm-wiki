Astarion's decision crystallizes in a shard of cold, desperate logic. The first Mind Flayer was as good as dead under the furious assault of the Githyanki and the burly man. His attention, however, was already on the next problem: the Mind Flayer nearest the exit, its alien head slowly turning, its colossal intellect beginning to unspool its focus from the engine. *No.* He wouldn't allow them to be surrounded, not after clawing their way to this precarious advantage.

Without a word, a phantom of his usual theatricality, Astarion became pure, focused movement. He darted away from the struggling, gurgling Mind Flayer and its two tormentors, his leather armor a muted whisper against the sickening pulse of the ship. His slender frame, accustomed to the shadowed alleys of Baldur's Gate, wove through the writhing conduits and discarded organic matter with practiced ease. The jagged bone blade, still glistening with the ichor of its first victim, felt strangely at home in his hand, a conduit for his cold, predatory intent.

The second Mind Flayer, its attention still split between the furious commotion and its vital task, was caught off-guard. Its tentacles twitched, a psionic hum growing in intensity as it attempted to reorient its focus, but Astarion was already there. He lunged, not with a clumsy roar, but with the silent, decisive precision of a hunter striking at a vital organ. His target was the vulnerable nexus where its tentacles met its head, a point that screamed "brain-snack" even to his vampire instincts. A chill, detached satisfaction ran through him. This was the dance he knew, the one where he controlled the tempo of another's demise.
