In this campaign, 'better gear' is split into two categories: standard equipment (bought with Gold) and Sovereign Requisitions (bought with DPP).

**1. Standard Equipment (Gold):**
You currently have 500 Gold. You can use this during downtime in Bezantur to buy mundane or common magical items from the Thayan markets. However, truly powerful artifacts are rarely sold for mere coin in Thay; they are awarded for service or stolen from the weak.

**2. Sovereign Requisitions (DPP):**
Your 5 Dominion Power Points (DPP) represent your requisition budget for Mission 001. As per the 'Requisition Menu' in your campaign codex, you can spend these points to acquire high-tier Thayan assets that function as gear or squad force-multipliers. 

**REQUISITION OPTIONS FOR GEAR/ASSETS:**
- **1 DPP (Local Intel):** Provides tactical maps (Advantage on initiative/planning).
- **2 DPP (Squad Upgrades):** Equips Korbin with Masterwork Plate (AC +1) or grants Needles alchemical toxins (DC 14 Paralysis).
- **2 DPP (Smokepowder Sapper):** Provides high-explosive assets for the mission.
- **3 DPP (Red Wizard Adept):** Provides a Level 4 controller mage to facilitate your Soul Thief harvests.

**Why you can't just 'buy' a Vorpal Sword right now:**
Nocturne expects you to *earn* your arsenal. You acquire powerful magical traits and unique gear by using **Soul Thief** on 'Elite' or 'Boss' targets. For example, harvesting the Shadow Monk Kaelen is the primary way to 'buy' (through blood) the Shadow Step ability or other Tier 3 gear equivalents.
