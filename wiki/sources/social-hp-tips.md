## What is Social HP?

Social HP is a meta-concept for how much an NPC is willing to engage with you in a given interaction. Unlike combat HP, social HP isn't a visible number — it's a hidden budget of patience, trust, and interest that depletes as you push, offend, or waste an NPC's time, and regenerates as you build rapport, offer value, or entertain them.

## How It Works in Practice

- **First impressions matter** — Walking up and demanding information starts you at a deficit. A courteous introduction sets you up for success.
- **Each attempt costs something** — Persuasion, Deception, or Intimidation checks that fail chip away at social HP. Succeed and you preserve it.
- **NPC disposition modifies difficulty** — A friendly NPC might give you 2 free passes. A hostile one might start with social HP nearly empty.
- **Different approaches have different costs** — Intimidation is efficient but damages long-term reputation. Persuasion takes more time but builds rapport. Deception risks a crash when caught.
- **Social HP regenerates slowly** — You can't grind social encounters by resting. One bad interaction can close a door for a session or more.

## Getting the Most Out of Social Interactions

- **Read the room** — Watch for NPC mood cues. An impatient NPC has low social HP. Adjust your approach.
- **Offer something in return** — Information trades for information. Gold trades for services. Favor trading preserves social HP.
- **Use Deception sparingly and strategically** — A well-crafted lie that serves the story beats a failed Intimidation check.
- **Investigate before negotiating** — Intel on an NPC's motivations, allegiances, and fears lets you approach them with precision.
- **Build faction reputation** — Faction standing affects NPC social HP across entire groups. High standing = easier social encounters.
- **Leverage your background and skills** — A Noble background opens doors a Criminal's can't. Choose your social approach based on who your character is.
- **Know when to leave** — If social HP is depleted, back off gracefully. You can return later with new leverage or information.
