Kantos, meanwhile, had slumped forward, his head resting on his copying instruments, his ancient mind finally succumbing to exhaustion, leaving him deeply, profoundly asleep.

You pushed yourself to your feet, your body still weary, but your posture infused with a cold, unwavering purpose. The pragmatic part of your intellect, the part that valued efficiency and sustained control, recognized the necessity. To push further now would be to risk catastrophic burnout, or worse, to compromise the delicate balance of Kaelan's newfound peace.

"We have pushed ourselves to the limit," you stated, your voice firm, addressing the entire room. "Rest is now a strategic imperative." You turned to Cressida, her sharp eyes gleaming. "Cressida, prioritize the Dream-Weaver cactus distillate. Use your Nexus contacts. This must be handled with the utmost subtlety and speed. Acquire it, but draw no undue attention. Understood?" Cressida nodded, a subtle, almost predatory gleam in her eyes. "Understood, My Lady. It will be done."

You then swept your gaze across Rowan, Gideon, and Kantos. "We will take a long rest. Gideon, ensure the integrity of the Quiet Ward's perimeter is absolute. Rowan, remain with Sergeant Kaelan. Kantos, continue the copying process of the journals, but no further until morning. No one is to disturb us."

You walked to a secluded corner of the study, the faint light of your `Light` cantrip still glowing on your finger. You sank to the floor, leaning against a towering bookshelf, the dust and the scent of ancient parchment a familiar comfort. You closed your eyes, forcing your mind to surrender to the overwhelming fatigue, to the deep, healing darkness that beckoned. Sleep, a strategic necessity, finally claimed you.
