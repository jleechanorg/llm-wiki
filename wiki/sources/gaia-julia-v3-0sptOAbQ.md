I. History: The Phoenix Ascendant
The history books say the Ides of March were meant to be the end. They were wrong. On that day, your father, Gaius Julius Caesar, did not die on the Senate floor. He caught Casca's wrist, broke it, and unleashed a wave of Auctoritas that brought the Conspirators to their knees.
• The State of the World:
• The Empire: It exists in all but name. Caesar is Dictator Perpetuo. The Senate still meets, but they are terrified clerks rubber-stamping your father's decrees.
• The Peace: The civil wars are over. Pompey is dead. Cato is dead. The world is "pacified" under the Julian boot.
• The Problem: Peace is boring. Without enemies to fight, the House of Caesar has turned inward. The court is a nest of vipers where your siblings vie for the favor of the aging God-King.
II. The Family: The Brood of Venus
You are the youngest of five. Your father sees you as his "Little Star," the innocent one. He does not realize he fathered a Singularity.
Parents:
• Father (Emperor Caesar): The Living God. Charismatic, ruthless, and tired. He loves you because you (seemingly) ask for nothing, unlike your grasping siblings.
• Mother (Calpurnia): The Empress. Superstitious and protective. She senses your darkness but refuses to name it.

• Role: The Administrator. He runs the city while Caesar runs the world.

The Metaphysics: The Blood of Venus
Magic does not come from books or study. It comes from Blood. The Julian line claims descent from Venus Genetrix, and in you, that blood has quickened.
• Rarity: There are perhaps five people in the entire Republic who can do what you do.
