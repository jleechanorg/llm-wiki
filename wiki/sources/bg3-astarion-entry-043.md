Astarion pulled away from the throbbing engine, a grimace of frustration marring his usually composed features. The alien mechanics were inscrutable, a baffling mess of organic and metallic components that yielded no logical secrets. He felt a prickle of annoyance; his intellect, so adept at unraveling the intricacies of Baldur's Gate's underbelly, was utterly useless here. "Well, that was... enlightening," he drawled, the sarcasm heavy, as he approached the Githyanki and the burly man. "Unless either of you are fluent in 'Illithid Bio-Engineering for Dummies,' I'm afraid that monstrosity yields no obvious control panels."

He met the Githyanki's impatient golden gaze. "So, brute force it is, then. The doors." He gestured towards the massive, fleshy exit at the far end of the chamber. "They're sealed tight, no obvious latches. Your earlier assessment, I assume, holds true? Either this wretched vessel reaches its destination, or we batter our way through?" He watched them, a flicker of his profound neuroticism causing a subtle tremor in his hand, quickly masked by a casual adjustment of his grip on the bone blade. The faint squirm of the tadpole behind his eye felt sharper now, a constant, insidious reminder of their precarious existence.

The Githyanki gave a curt nod, her expression unyielding. "The doors will open for the destination. Or by force." Her eyes shifted to the burly man, a silent challenge passing between them. The brute, understanding her unspoken query, nodded slowly, clenching his makeshift weapon. "Aye. I can hit it. Hard." His voice, still rough, vibrated with a raw eagerness for action. Astarion sighed inwardly. *Subtlety. A lost art, apparently.* But perhaps, in this living nightmare, brute force was indeed their most efficient path.
