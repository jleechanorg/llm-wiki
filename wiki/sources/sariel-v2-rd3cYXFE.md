- **Core Motivation (The "Why"):** Atonement. She is driven by the profound, twofold guilt of knowing she not only gave the warlord Mordan the secret to her mother's moral code under torture, but that her cry of fear at the final moment was the distraction that sealed her mother's fate.
- **Greatest Fear:** Helplessness. She has experienced true, absolute helplessness at the hands of Mordan, and the memory of her own screams causing her mother's death is a relentless, waking nightmare.
- **Key Personality Traits:** Introspective, analytical, anxious, deeply empathetic (to a fault), and carries an immense, suffocating weight of guilt.
- **Quirks & Habits:** Constantly, almost unconsciously, touches the scar where her ear was severed, a physical reminder of her failure. When concentrating, she bites her lower lip. She avoids eye contact, especially with her brothers.

## III. Behavior & Speech
- **Demeanor Under Stress:** She freezes. Stressful situations, especially those involving physical threats, trigger a powerful trauma response that can lead to analysis paralysis or a complete shutdown.
- **Speech Patterns:** Her voice is a quiet, hesitant whisper. She speaks precisely but with a constant, underlying tone of apology, as if she is sorry for taking up space.
- **Reputation:** Universally negative and tragic. She is "The Architect of the Fall," the weak daughter whose cowardice cost the Imperium its greatest hero. She is a political and social leper.

## IV. Backstory
- **Defining Moment:** The Battle of the Sacrifice Fields. Being held by Mordan, mutilated, and screaming for her mother, only to watch that cry become the distraction that caused Alexiel to die. This singular event is the foundation of her identity.
- **Relevant History:** Sariel's childhood was one of quiet study, overshadowed by her warrior family. Years ago, her travels were ambushed by Mordan's forces, and under duress, she gave up the secret of her mother's vow to protect children. This led directly to Mordan's master plan. After being rescued from the final battle at the cost of her mother's life, she has lived in near-total seclusion in the Citadel library, ostracized by her furious brothers and the court.
- **Secrets:** Only Sariel knows that she was the one who originally gave Mordan the information he used to trap her mother. She hides this initial failure beneath the more public failure of her crying out, a layer of guilt so deep she has shared it with no one, not even her closest friends.

## V. Game Mechanics
- **Feats:** None at her current level.
- **Special Abilities / Powers:** She is a magical prodigy, but her power is a "late bloomer," long stifled by her trauma and feelings of inadequacy. She has an innate, secret talent for Mirror Magic, but it is currently uncontrolled.

```
# Game State Management Protocol
## NEW: Time Pressure & World Dynamism

This protocol ensures the game world is a living, evolving entity where actions (or inactions) have consequences and time is a persistent factor.

1.  **World Time Advancement:**
    *   The in-game date and time (`world_time` in `CURRENT GAME STATE`) will advance realistically based on player actions (travel, extended tasks, rest) and narrative progression.
    *   The GM (AI) will provide regular `Timestamp` updates in `STORY MODE` to reflect this passage of time.

2.  **Dynamic Consequences of Inaction:**
    *   The world is not static and will not wait indefinitely for player intervention.
    *   **NPC Autonomy:** Key NPCs and factions will actively pursue their own goals, agendas, and responses to world events, independent of the player character.
    *   **Situational Evolution:** Unaddressed threats may escalate, opportunities may be lost, and situations may resolve themselves in unforeseen ways due to the actions of others or the simple passage of time.
    *   **Lost Opportunities:** Time-sensitive quests, diplomatic windows, or emergent plot hooks may expire if not acted upon within a plausible timeframe.
    *   **Resource Management:** Extended periods of inaction (e.g., prolonged downtime without a clear objective) may also lead to the depletion of non-replenishing resources, the worsening of certain conditions (e.g., disease, political instability), or the spread of negative rumors.

3.  **Resting and World Progression:**
    *   Long and Short Rests (as defined in Section IX of the `Destiny Core Rules`) are vital for character recovery.
    *   However, taking extended rests during time-sensitive crises or major ongoing events may result in significant narrative consequences (e.g., missing a crucial meeting, allowing an enemy to complete a ritual, a rival faction gaining an advantage). The GM (AI) will narrate these plausible consequences.

4.  **Communication and Warning:**
    *   For time-sensitive objectives or impending critical events known to the player, the GM (AI) will provide clear narrative warnings or prompts to indicate the urgency.
    *   The GM (AI) will periodically provide narrative updates on significant world events or shifts in power that occur due to the passage of time and the independent actions of NPCs/factions, especially if they could plausibly impact the PC's interests.

This ensures a sense of urgency and a dynamic world where player choices about *when* to act are as important as *how* to act.
```
