The heavy tread of boots on the stairs heralds the arrival of the Guild. Smiley Arvax descends into the hold, followed by a crew of rough-looking stevedores. His eyes light up at the sight of the stacked crates, the sheer volume of the haul.

"Gods below," Arvax breathes, running a hand over a crate stamped with the Moonrise sigil. "Look at this. Iron, spices... and these reinforced ones? What's in here? Gold?"

He reaches for the lid of a crate containing the dormant tadpoles.

"Do not touch that," you say. Your voice is not loud, but it cuts through the damp air like a razor.

Arvax freezes, his hand hovering inches from the latch. He looks at you, confused. "Monarch? We're here to load up. The Guild takes its cut, remember?"

You step between him and the cargo, the *Echoes of the Idol* amplifying your presence until the shadows of the hold seem to lengthen and bow towards you. The violet light of your eyes burns in the gloom.

"The bulk goods are yours, Arvax. The iron, the silk, the timber. Take them," you command, gesturing dismissively to the mundane supplies. "But these crates... they contain biological assets of a nature far too volatile for your warehouses."

You rest your hand on the iron-bound lid, claiming it. "These are not goods. They are hazards. They require containment, arcane suppression, and a handler who understands the risks. If you open this box, you unleash a plague that the Guild cannot fight. These remain under my personal jurisdiction."

Arvax stares at you, then at the crate. He swallows hard, the greed in his eyes warring with the primal fear your presence now instills. He remembers the Zhentarim captain. He remembers the coup.

"Biological hazards," he repeats, stepping back slowly. "Right. Messy stuff. We... we stick to the trade goods."

He turns to his men, his voice barking a little too loudly. "You heard her! Leave the iron boxes! Load the rest! Move!"

The Guild crew gives you a wide berth, terrified, as they begin stripping the hold of everything else. The tadpoles—the seeds of an army—are yours.
