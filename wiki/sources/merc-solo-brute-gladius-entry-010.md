Gladius’s thoughts, clear and cold, settled on the most satisfying figure. He watched the lackey, whose face was now slick with a thin sheen of sweat, his eyes still frantically searching for an escape. Gladius had him. He was broken. The time for subtle prodding was over; now came the crushing blow.

"Five hundred," Gladius stated, the single word a blunt hammer striking the silence between them. His voice was low, flat, and utterly devoid of negotiation. He didn't raise his voice, didn't need to. The casualness of the demand, the sheer audacity of it, was the real weapon. He picked up the first purse, felt its weight, then let it drop with a heavy thud back onto the table. It was a paltry sum, not even worth the effort of lifting it. He glanced pointedly at the second, smaller purse, then back to the lackey's wide, terrified eyes. "And that. For expenses."

The man visibly flinched. His jaw worked, trying to form words, but nothing came out. Five hundred gold was an exorbitant sum for a routine 'cleanup' job, even for a noble. It was a price that Lord Silvanus himself would balk at, a brazen act of financial brigandage. Gladius watched the internal battle play out on the lackey's face – the desperate calculations, the fear of his master's wrath, battling against the raw, immediate terror of Gladius sitting across from him.

"F-five hundred?" the lackey finally managed to squeak, his voice barely a whisper. He looked like he might actually weep. "Lord Silvanus... he would never..."

You cut him off with a slow, deliberate shake of your head, a sneer twisting your lips. "Then find someone else to clean up his mess," you rumbled, leaning back slightly, as if the conversation was already concluded. The implication was clear: refuse, and you wouldn't just lose a mercenary; you'd gain a new, much more personal problem. Your gaze dropped to the two purses on the table, then back to his face, a silent, chilling promise that the price of refusal would be far, far higher than any coin.

He broke. His shoulders slumped, defeat washing over him like a cold wave. He reached into his silks, his movements jerky, pulling out a small, ornate pouch, then another, spilling coins onto the table until a pile of gold pieces, along with the two original purses, sat before you. He counted it out, his fingers trembling, until the sum was met.

"Five hundred," he repeated, his voice hollow. "It's all here. And the smaller purse... for the expenses, as you say. Lord Silvanus will be... informed." He looked utterly miserable, his gaudy silks now seeming more like a shroud.

You finally reached out, your hand closing around the first heavy purse, then the second, and swept the scattered gold into them with a deliberate, unhurried motion. The metallic chime was a pleasant sound. "Good," you grunted, the single word a dismissal. "Greenmeadow. Nine souls. No witnesses." You met his gaze one last time, a cold, satisfied glint in your eyes, letting him see the predator beneath the grime, just for a moment, before you pulled your hood deeper.
