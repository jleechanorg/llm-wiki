## Summary
A post-Baldur's Gate 3 solo D&D 5e campaign set in 1492 DR. Nocturne, the Shadow Monarch -- a Level 8 College of Swords Bard -- has built a criminal empire in post-Elturel Baldur's Gate. The campaign resumes after Operation Scalpel: capturing the Moonrise ship, seizing thousands of Absolute tadpoles, and psychically overriding a True Soul leader. Core theme: structural conquest through terror, debt, and biological leverage.

## Key Claims
- Nocturne wields Omni-Attunement (5 items attuned simultaneously) and The Hunger mechanic (ritual killings grant +2 all ability scores)
- The Absolute's tadpoles are held in Netherese-shadow stasis, designed to subvert the Parliament of Peers by infecting them at a gala
- Nocturne controls: the Shadow Bank (consuming the city's debt), House Umbrage (puppet regency), Vice-Marshal Rokas (suborned law), and Black Lotus Squad (Vesper, Gant, Silas, Nyx)
- The Manifest of Souls reveals all harvested Elturel refugee names; this is used as blackmail leverage
- Phase Two of the Absolute is scheduled for Tarsakh 7: mass Parliament infection via wine supply
