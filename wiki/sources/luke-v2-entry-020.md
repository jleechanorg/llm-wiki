The grainy image of Leia, Han, and Chewbacca flickered on the screen, their faces etched with familiar fear and defiance. A jolt, sharp and electric, ran through Luke Skywalker. It wasn't pain, not truly, but a perverse recognition. The 'Light Side' version of him, the farm boy who yearned for connection and fought for his friends, withered under the burgeoning surge of the Dark. His `secret sadistic side`, that simmering, hidden core, finally found its voice. A slow, chilling smile touched his lips, unseen by Vader's mask, but felt profoundly within his own soul.

He met Vader's gaze, a cold, calculating glint in his eyes that would have horrified his former self. His voice, when it came, was low, almost a confession, yet laced with a frightening, almost gleeful satisfaction. "Worthless, Lord Vader," Luke stated, the words tasting of ash and triumph. "They are nothing to me now. In truth... I've always secretly dreamed of betraying them. Of watching the hope drain from their eyes as I crush their spirit, then their lives. It's a vision I've savored, hidden deep within me. This 'purge of sentimentality' is merely the final step in making that dream a reality." The last sentence was almost a whisper, a dark vow.

Vader remained impassive, a monolith of power, but Luke felt the subtle shift in the Force around them—a profound affirmation. There was no praise, no visible reaction, only a deepening of the oppressive, yet now strangely comforting, dark presence. The Sith Lord's respirator cycled, a sound that seemed to rumble not just in the chamber, but in the very depths of Luke's newly awakened dark soul. The implicit challenge had been met, the test passed with chilling conviction. This was no longer simply acceptance; this was a hungry embrace.

"Good," Vader rumbled, the single word resonating with absolute, unyielding approval. "Now, you are ready for the next lesson. Come. The true path to power awaits within the depths of this installation."
