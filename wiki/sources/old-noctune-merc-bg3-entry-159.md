You leave the Umbrage Estate behind, a silent monolith of shifting power. Lyra is playing her part; now, you must ensure the audience remains seated.

You navigate the opulent, gas-lit streets of the Upper City to a discreet, narrow townhouse nestled between two larger manors. This is where Rathmar sleeps—or tries to. You bypass the front door, scaling the garden wall with the ease of a shadow, and slip through a balcony window left unlatched.

Rathmar is in his study, nursing a glass of amber liquid. He jumps as you step from the curtains, the liquid sloshing over his hand. His face is pale, the strain of the last twenty-four hours etched deep.

"You," he breathes, setting the glass down with a tremble. "Is it done? The diversion... the guards are returning soon. I need to know if—"

"The estate has been... reordered," you interrupt, your voice a cool breeze in the stuffy room. You move to his desk, picking up a quill, inspecting the nib. "Lord Umbrage has suffered a financial collapse. A tragedy. But his daughter... Lyra has stepped forward to save the House."

Rathmar blinks, his mind racing to catch up. "Lyra? But she's... she's soft. She's *my* leverage."

You snap the quill. The sound is sharp, final.

"She was," you correct him, turning your violet gaze upon him. "Now, she is the 'Savior'. She holds the purse strings. She holds the power. And she holds a deep, abiding gratitude for the *Silent Partner* who made it possible. That partner is me, Rathmar. Not you."

You lean in, invading his space until you can smell the fear on him. "Your hook is gone. The fish has grown teeth. If you attempt to contact her, if you attempt to use her against her father, she will not crumble. She will destroy you. Because I have taught her how."

Rathmar slumps back into his chair, the fight draining out of him. He realizes the trap has closed completely. He has no leverage on Umbrage, no hold on Lyra, and his own reputation hangs by the thread you hold.

"I... I understand," he whispers, staring at the broken quill. "I am silent. I am... yours."

"Sleep well, Rathmar," you soothe, patting his cheek. "The city is safer when you know your place."

You leave him in the dark, a broken king on a chessboard he no longer understands. The Upper City is secured. The night is yours.
