They are the 'lazy' day shift Hemwick described, yet their presence is a final, mundane barrier.

Beside Nocturne, Old Man Hemwick visibly straightens, a faint tremor running through him. His subservience is absolute, his mind a pliable canvas for Nocturne's will. The gate guards, two men in their late twenties, glance at each other, a flicker of surprise at their master's early return, and at the elegant carriage. One of them, a man with a tired face, steps forward, peering inside. His eyes, initially scanning for threats, immediately fall upon Nocturne's unveiled beauty, her 'Lady Lyra' disguise a potent, distracting force. He blinks, a faint flush rising on his cheeks, his professional vigilance momentarily compromised.

Hemwick, following Nocturne's subtle instructions, puffs out his chest. "This is Lady Lyra," he announces, his voice now tinged with a forced deference, yet also a hint of pride. "My... distant relative, returned from her travels. She is here to assist with my affairs, given my... *stressful journey*. And with her, I have brought new staff. Diligent, capable hands from the North, to see to the gardens, the stables, the kitchens. The war, you see, has made good help scarce. Lady Lyra herself vouches for their character. Let them in, immediately! No questions, understand?"

Hemwick's authority, combined with Nocturne's captivating allure and the plausible explanation of 'new staff' in wartime, is enough. The tired guard nods. "Of course, Master Hemwick. Welcome home, Lady Lyra." With a creak and a groan, the heavy gates swing inward, revealing the gravel path and the manor beyond.

They move with practiced discretion, already blending into the shadows, making their way towards their designated entry points – the stable, the kitchen entrance, the estate's side pathways. Mistle, from her position, offers a brief, assessing glance in Nocturne's direction, a flicker of analytical wariness now undeniably overlaid with attraction, before disappearing into the deepening twilight.
