The pulsating hum of the Nautiloid's engine was the only sound now, a deep, unsettling thrum that seemed to fill the void left by the silenced Mind Flayers. Astarion, having satisfied his meticulous, if fearful, internal scan of the chamber, turned to his two unlikely companions. The Githyanki stood like a statue of coiled aggression, her golden eyes fixed on the distant, heavy doors, her patience as thin as a new moon. The burly man, still breathing heavily, ran a hand over his bruised forehead, his gaze lingering on the fallen illithids with a mix of awe and lingering rage.

"Well," Astarion drawled, a touch of his usual sardonic theatricality returning, though his voice was still low, edged with the raw adrenaline of recent combat. "Our delightful hosts won't be joining us for tea, but we're still quite thoroughly trapped. I've had a quick look – and by 'quick,' I mean a desperate, soul-shaking assessment – and there's no subtle escape hatch, no convenient ventilation shaft to crawl through." He gestured vaguely towards the grotesque, fleshy walls. "Just that charming set of doors at the far end."

He met the Githyanki's piercing gaze. "They appear to be the only way forward, but they're not precisely... inviting. And there's a certain *lack* of obvious mechanisms." He paused, a flicker of his profound neuroticism showing through. "Unless you, my dear, are in the habit of simply *willing* such things open?" His tone was laced with a thinly veiled plea for information. He needed a way out, and this creature, with her alien wisdom, might possess it.

The Githyanki merely grunted, a sound of dismissive irritation. "Only fools rely on magic for every lock," she rasped, her voice like grinding stone. "They are sealed. Not by magic, but by purpose. This vessel, it seeks a destination. The doors will open when it reaches. Or when enough force is applied." Her gaze, sharp and unwavering, shifted to the burly man, a clear challenge in her eyes. The brute, meanwhile, clenched his chitinous blade, his jaw set, clearly ready to apply *all* the force. Astarion felt a ripple of frustration. Brute force was so inelegant. And so very dangerous.
