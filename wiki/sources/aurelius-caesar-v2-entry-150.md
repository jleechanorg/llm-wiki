The map on the stone table is a mess of charcoal and shadow, but in your mind, the Roman machine is as clear as a Forum sunrise.

"The net is not just closing; it is tightening with a specific, lethal rhythm," you begin, your voice resonant and clinical, stripping away the boy to reveal the officer. You trace a line along the eastern slopes. "Glaber is using the 'Hammer and Anvil'. The two centuries in the east are the beaters—they will advance in broad, noisy lines to drive us toward the south. The southern pass is the anvil. That is where the heavy infantry is massed, locked in testudo, waiting to grind us into the shale."

You unroll the Centurion's parchment, tapping the ventilation schematics. "From the prisoner and this map, I know their timing. They expect the all-clear signal at midnight. When it doesn't come, they won't charge blindly. They will deploy 'Fire-Baskets'—incendiary pots designed to burn away the fog and treeline simultaneously. In three hours, the mountain will be as bright as midday, and our concealment will be ash."

Spartacus leans over the map, his eyes tracing the paths you've described. Crixus stands behind him, his massive arms crossed, his gaze fixed on you with a new, somber respect. The reveal of your father's identity hasn't just changed the mood; it has changed the stakes. They are no longer listening to a noble's whim, but to a Caesar's autopsy of his own legion.

"The Sacred Grove path is our only ghost-road," you state, looking at Aurelia, who stands closer to you now, her hand never far from your sleeve. "If we move within the hour, we can slip between the southern anvil and the eastern beaters while the fog still holds. But we must abandon the heavy loot. We move light, or we die heavy."

Spartacus looks at Crixus, then back to the map. The Thracian's jaw sets. "The boy knows the Roman heart because he shares the blood. If Glaber is lighting the mountain in three hours, we have no time for debate. Agron! Tell the men to douse the fires. We leave through the Grove. Aurelius, Aurelia—you take the point. Your light guided us through the salt; guide us through the trees."

Crixus offers a grunt of approval, his hand resting on the hilt of his sword. "If the noble says the path is there, we find it. But if we meet a Roman in the dark, I want your steel to be the first he feels. Prove to the mountain that you've truly buried the name."
