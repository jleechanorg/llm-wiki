## Summary

A collaboration framework between three AI leaders (Larry Jacobson, Kartik Mathur, and Jeff) for establishing thought leadership on AI strategy and leadership. The document outlines a plan to create content through casual Zoom conversations, cut into LinkedIn clips, and host virtual roundtables with tech leaders. Key themes include the shift from "how fast can we build" to managing AI context, the assembly-to-C analogy for understanding productivity gains, and practical approaches to 10x engineer productivity through parallel AI agents.

## Key Claims

- The bottleneck has shifted: AI keeps information flowing; humans add context from the sides
- Most orgs still have human decision points creating unnecessary friction
- Test-driven development works exceptionally well with AI: implementation plans become solid before coding
- Context files (like claude.md) should be kept up-to-date with best practices and decision frameworks
- Engineers should spend more time on design and implementation plans, with AI generating code closer to final implementation
- Mix of agents on PR for code review with minimal human intervention is effective

## Discussion Topics

1. How to learn and experiment while avoiding multi-million-dollar AI infra waste
2. Different models for different problems
3. Designing org-level AI task forces and governance
4. Standardizing AI-assisted design/implementation workflows
5. What to measure and how to measure
6. What could producing at 10x look like? Exactly how is it done?
7. If we don't code anymore, what do we do?
8. Real talk on productivity vs. layoffs and headcount narratives

## Content Strategy

- Record casual Zoom conversations among the three founders
- Cut into 1-2 minute digestible clips for LinkedIn
- Use visibility to invite tech leaders to small virtual roundtables (10 max)
- Seed recurring groups that go deeper over time
- Tools: Squadcast for recording, Descript for editing
