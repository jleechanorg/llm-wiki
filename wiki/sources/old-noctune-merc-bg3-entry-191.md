You leave the perfumed air of the Upper City behind, descending once more into the vibrant, spice-laden chaos of Little Calimshan. The contrast is stark: where House Umbrage suffocates under the weight of silent tension, The Gilded Cage hums with the nervous energy of a machine running too hot.

The shop is open for business. Vessic stands behind the counter, haggling with a tiefling merchant over a set of silver cutlery. When the bell chimes and your shadow falls across the floor, the color drains from his face. He hastily concludes the deal, shooing the customer out with a trembling hand.

"Monarch," he breathes, locking the door and flipping the sign to 'Closed'. "I... I didn't expect you until nightfall."

"Efficiency, Vessic," you reply, moving through the shop with the ease of an owner. "Report."

Vessic wipes sweat from his brow. "The Zhentarim are... confused. The *Silent Wave* didn't report in at the midnight check. They don't know it's gone; they think it's delayed, or the captain is holding out for a better bribe. But the silence is making them twitchy."

He pulls a ledger from beneath the counter—not the black book you stole, but his daily fence log. "Because of the silence, High Command is paranoid. Rathmar's intel about the caravan holds up, but there's a change. They've added an outrider detail. A Red Wizard mercenary, **Thayian**, has been contracted to escort the wagons from the checkpoint to the warehouse. They're scared of ghosts, so they hired a ghost-hunter."

He slides a small pouch across the glass. "The day's take. The fence cut from a shipment of stolen spices. It's not much, but it's... yours."

You weigh the pouch. It's light, but his fear is heavy enough to serve as collateral. He isn't holding back gold; he's holding back panic. The addition of a Red Wizard to the caravan changes the tactical calculus significantly.

"You have done well, Vessic," you say, pocketing the coin. "Keep the shop open. Keep the Zhentarim confused. If anyone asks, the *Silent Wave* is likely just... detained by the tide."

You step back out into the heat of the afternoon. The political board is set. The martial board, however, has just gained a new piece. A Red Wizard. The ambush at Wyrm's Rock will need to be perfect.
