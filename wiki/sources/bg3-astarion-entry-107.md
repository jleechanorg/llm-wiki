The clearing opened before them, a brutal tableau lit by the sickly, unnatural glow of the distant crash site. A human, young and utterly terrified, lay sprawled on the scorched earth, desperately scrambling away from a monstrous, six-limbed creature of shadows and malice – a **Shadow Mastiff**. Its eyes glowed with malevolent red light, and its guttural snarl promised immediate death. The beast was distracted, focused entirely on its struggling prey, offering Astarion the perfect opening for an unseen strike. His high neuroticism screamed, not just at the sudden danger, but at the sheer, unrefined *mess* of it all. He had no desire to be the hero, but a live asset was always better than a dead one.

"Not another one," Astarion snarled, the words a low, guttural rasp, utterly devoid of his usual melodic lilt. He moved with the fluid, silent grace of a true predator, a shadow among the charred pines. His new **Gloom Stalker** abilities sang to him, urging him into the unseen. The Shadow Mastiff was a vile creature, but it was *predictable* in its predatory focus. He would make it regret its insolence. His longbow was already drawn, an arrow nocked with practiced efficiency, its tip gleaming faintly in the dim, smoky light. His crimson eyes, narrowed in cold concentration, scanned the beast for a vital point – a gap in its shadowy hide, a vulnerable joint in its grotesque anatomy. His Archery Fighting Style promised precision, and he would deliver it.

Lae'zel, seeing his intent, tensed, her golden eyes blazing with a fierce, almost eager readiness. She moved to position herself to flank, her makeshift blade held high, a silent promise of brutal follow-up. Gale, however, let out a soft gasp of horror at the sight of the beast, clutching his head. "Gods above! What *is* that thing?" he muttered, his wizardly sensibilities clearly affronted by the raw, monstrous reality. Astarion ignored him. He was a hunter. And the hunt had just begun.
