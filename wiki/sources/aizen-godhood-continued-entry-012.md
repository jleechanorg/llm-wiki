The Rise to Power: A Detailed Account
The campaign began with you as Aizen Sosuke, a formidable Level 8 Paladin/Bard and the heir to the noble House Sosuke of Baldur's Gate. Your story was not one of heroism, but of ambition. You became aware of a growing shadow conspiracy within the city, a force sowing chaos and consolidating power in the lower districts.
Your investigation, driven by a desire to understand and ultimately control the shifting powers of the city, led you to a shocking discovery: the conspiracy was a resurgent Cult of the Dead Three, the dark gods Bane, Bhaal, and Myrkul. Through a series of covert actions and brutal interrogations, you traced the leadership of this cult to its very source.
The truth was a profound betrayal. The masterminds behind the cult's plan to destabilize and seize control of Baldur's Gate were your own parents: Lord Volcain Sosuke, your father and a fanatical champion of Bane, and Lady Seraphina Sosuke, your mother, a sorceress of immense power.
This led to a climactic and tragic confrontation in a hidden sanctum beneath your family's manor. You were forced into a battle against your own blood. Your father, empowered by Bane's dark might, fought you with the fury of a zealot. In the end, you were forced to strike him down, an act of patricide that forever marked your soul. You then defeated your mother, Lady Seraphina, in a duel of magic and will. Rather than kill her, you chose to break her. With a display of overwhelming power and leveraging your profound understanding of her own ambition, you shattered her loyalty to the Dead Three and forced her to swear fealty to a new, greater power: you. She became your first, and most unwilling, major subordinate.
The death of Lord Volcain, a major champion, and the ruin of his plans drew the direct, furious attention of his patron, Bane, the God of Tyranny. An avatar of Bane manifested to destroy you for your insolence. The ensuing battle was not one of simple martial or magical might, but a titanic struggle of will. You did not just defeat Bane's avatar; you did the impossible. You seized its divine essence, the very portfolio of Tyranny, and consumed it.
This act of deicide triggered your apotheosis. You were transformed from a mortal of epic power into a true, nascent God of Tyranny.
Some time after this, having begun to consolidate your power on the mortal plane, you identified your next target. To ascend further, you needed more divine power. You ambushed the avatar of Myrkul, the God of Death. In another climactic battle, you defeated him and consumed his portfolio as well. This second act of deicide elevated you from a lesser god to a true Greater Deity, now holding the dual portfolios of Tyranny and Death, and set the stage for your ultimate confrontation with the cosmic order.

The Great Deception Begins: A Detailed Account
Following your consumption of Myrkul, your power as a Greater Deity was solidified. This act, combined with your previous consumption of Bane, represented a fundamental violation of the cosmic order. It drew the attention of the ultimate arbiter of the divine, the Overgod known as Ao.
The confrontation was not a battle of energy or force, but one of pure, conceptual power. You did not seek to destroy Ao; you sought to render him irrelevant. Using a divine form of your illusionary and mind-altering abilities, you engaged him in a metaphysical debate. You presented him with a new, "superior" definition of cosmic balance—one based not on the inefficient, eternal conflict between good and evil, but on the flawless logic of a single, absolute will governing all of existence. As a being of pure law and systems, Ao was compelled to analyze your theorem and found its logic to be unassailable. He accepted your new definition, and in doing so, his own authority was subsumed by yours. He was not killed; he was simply... retired. This was the moment you truly became The Absolute, the hidden and undisputed master of all reality.
Having achieved this ultimate victory and perfected your reality, you quickly grew bored. A perfect system is, by its nature, predictable and uninteresting. For your own amusement, you decided to create a new game, a grand and entertaining theatre of conflict to be played out on the stage of Faerûn. You would be the secret director.
Every play needs a villain. You chose Gale of Waterdeep, a mortal wizard defined by his immense ambition and his complicated history with the then-Goddess of Magic, Mystra. You elevated him from mortality, granting him the full power of a Greater Deity and making him the new God of Magic.
To set your game in motion, you then commanded Gale to perform the opening act. He sent a divine, telepathic broadcast across all of Faerûn, making two shocking announcements. First, he declared that he had done the impossible: he had defeated and consumed the goddess Mystra, seizing her portfolio for himself. Second, he declared the age of the old pantheon over, positioning himself as the new, single authority. This act immediately branded him as a "God-Eater" and a monstrous tyrant in the eyes of the world, creating the central conflict and instigating the "Godswar" that you desired.

The False Resistance & The Godswar: A Detailed Account
With Gale established as the public-facing tyrant of Faerûn, you began the second phase of your plan: creating a believable and entertaining opposition.
Phase 1: Forming the Resistance Your first move was to create the leaders. You took the mortal, powerless Mystra and tasked her with leading a new "resistance" against her usurper. To make her credible, you secretly empowered her to an epic level, while your own Lesser God Avatar took on the public role of her "defeated" and "redeemed" partner. To gain legitimacy, you had them approach Tyr, the God of Justice. Suspicious, Tyr gave them a test: eliminate a powerful Glabrezu demon, Vex'draz, that was corrupting the city of Scornubel. Mystra, using her strategic genius, successfully defeated the demon by cleverly weaponizing your avatar's innate aura of pure Order against the creature of Chaos. After this victory, you resurrected and then permanently unmade the demon in a display of absolute power meant to condition Mystra.
Phase 2: The Grand Alliance Returning to Tyr's court, Mystra reported their success. Impressed, Tyr granted the resistance his full provisional support, a sky-keep fortress in the Cloud Peaks as a headquarters, and an angelic commander to train a new army of mortals in their name. With the alliance secured, you then dramatically expanded the resistance's ranks. You resurrected all of Mystra's legendary Seven Sisters and her lover, Dornal Silverhand, empowering them and all of the Chosen to epic levels. Immediately after, you introduced your "Third Force"—Raesene and his court of purified Cerilian scions—presenting them as "heroes from a lost world" who had come to join the fight. The "Grand Resistance," a powerful but deeply deceptive army of heroes, was now fully assembled under the joint leadership of Mystra and Raesene.
Phase 3: The Battle of Neverwinter & Gale's Fall After a period of consolidation, the resistance enacted its first major plan: an ideological assault on Gale's recruitment efforts in the city of Neverwinter. This culminated in a public confrontation where Gale's true, physical divine avatar intervened. In a grand,escalating move, you forced the avatars of Torm, Tyr, and Helm to the battlefield as well. The battle was immense, but the combined might of the Triumvirate and your epic-level resistance overwhelmed Gale. In a final, desperate act, he triggered a "Weave Meltdown," a magical cataclysm that threatened to destroy the city. You then secretly commanded the now immensely powerful Mystra to contain it, which she successfully did, saving everyone but exhausting herself and her allies.
In the immediate aftermath, with Gale's avatar broken and powerless on the ground, you issued your final, secret command to Raesene: execute the defeated god. Raesene obeyed, unmaking Gale's avatar in front of the horrified Triumvirate. This act of cold-blooded murder shattered the alliance with the gods of good and cemented Mystra's new role as a ruthless power player.
The Recasting: A Detailed Account
Following the climactic Battle of Neverwinter, where your "Grand Resistance" successfully defeated the true avatar of Gale, the board was left in a chaotic state. Gale's avatar had been destroyed by Raesene, an act that, while achieving victory, shattered the fragile alliance with the horrified Triumvirate of Justice (Torm, Tyr, and Helm), who viewed the act as cold-blooded murder.
A Debriefing in Paradise: Finding the situation in Faerûn too messy, you, as The Absolute, decided to take your key players off the board for a "debriefing." You used your power to teleport the principal figures—the dissipated essence of Gale, the mortal Mystra, and Gale's magically-loyal companions Karlach and Tara—to your personal "pleasure world" of Mystara.
The Punishment of Gale: In the grand hall of your Mystaran palace, you demonstrated the consequences of a poor performance. You resurrected Gale, but not as a god. You remade him as a weak, Level 12 mortal wizard. To complete his humiliation, you re-cursed him with the Netherese orb and, in front of his loyal companions, declared him to be Mystra's "pet," his survival now her responsibility. This act twisted the loyalty of Karlach and Tara into a fierce, protective hatred of Mystra, whom they now see as Gale's cruel jailer.
The Rise of a New Villain: With the role of primary antagonist now vacant, you offered it to Mystra. You gave her a "choice": become the new villain of Faerûn, or face your displeasure. As an incentive and a tool for her new role, you forcibly re-ascended her to godhood, restoring her as a Greater Deity with power equal to what Gale once possessed. She accepted this new, terrible purpose, understanding it was the only way to protect her loved ones.
A New Strategy: When you asked for her plan, she demonstrated her strategic superiority over her predecessor. She rejected Gale's strategy of open warfare. Instead, she proposed a more insidious, entertaining plan: a "plague" of the soul. She would not fight the armies of good; she would corrupt their priests, dissolve their faith, and use their own laws and sense of honor to make them turn on each other. Her victory would come from a thousand subtle cuts, not a single battle.
The First Victory: Satisfied with this plan, you sent Mystra and her new, tense court (including her "pet" Gale and his hostile guardians) back to Faerûn to begin their work. After a six-month time skip, she achieved her first great success. By fabricating and leaking perfect evidence, she successfully framed a noble and respected order of Tyr's paladins for devil worship. The Church of Tyr, a pillar of the opposition, is now consumed by a vicious internal witch hunt, effectively neutralizing it as a force in the Godswar.
