Serpent Queen's quiet smile remained, unwavering, as she swept the liquid wealth—the heavy bags of platinum, the glittering gems, the antique silver, and the thrumming magical idol—into a separate, concealed compartment of her pack. These were the spoils of her own private war, the fuel for her transcendence. They vanished from sight, leaving only the 'Shadow's Tithe' on the table: the heavy, wax-sealed deeds, the bearer bonds, and the grim painting of the Undercity.

She wrapped the political assets with the same reverence one might accord a holy relic. This was not loot; it was a leash.

The journey back to the Smirking Boar was a swift, shadowed transit through the cooling night. The cellar was as she had left it, though the air seemed heavier, charged with the unspoken anticipation of Arvax's greed. The Lieutenant sat at his table, a knife idly carving a groove into the wood.

"You're back," he grunted, his dead eyes flicking to her pack. "And? Did you teach his lordship a lesson?"

Serpent Queen placed the bundle on the table. She did not dump it; she presented it. "A lesson is forgotten, Arvax. A foreclosure is eternal."

She unfolded the deeds and bonds, spreading them out like a winning hand of cards. "I did not merely rob Lord Umbrage. I evicted him. These are the deeds to his Lower City holdings. These are the bonds that secure his standing with the Counting House. And this," she revealed the *Despair of the Undercity* painting, placing it atop the pile, "is the art you requested. A piece with... significant provenance regarding our mutual friends."

Arvax stared at the documents, his rictus grin faltering into genuine, stunned silence. He picked up a deed, reading the seal, then looked at the bonds. The realization of the value—not in gold, but in power—washed over him.

"You... you gutted him," Arvax whispered, a mixture of awe and terror in his voice. "You didn't just take his toys. You took his foundation. With these... the Guild doesn't just tax him. We *own* him."

He looked up, his eyes gleaming with a new, dangerous light. "I asked for a punishment. You brought me a kingdom. The liquid coin? The jewelry?"

"Operational expenses," Serpent Queen said smoothly, her voice a soft, final barrier against inquiry. "And dust in the wind compared to what lies on this table. Use the deeds, Arvax. Expand the growth sector. And remember who handed you the keys to the Upper City."

Arvax laughed, a harsh, barking sound. "Operational expenses. Right. Keep your secrets, 'Noc.' You've earned them. This... this is a game changer."
