Astarion’s lip curled, a mixture of mild surprise and lingering disdain for the "wizard" and "fighter." How utterly predictable. Yet, the raw power of Gale, and the ruthless efficiency of Lae'zel, were undeniably useful. He secured his newly acquired earthroot and fresh waterskin, the mundane act a stark contrast to the cosmic horror they’d just endured. The taste of clean water was a fleeting moment of normalcy, quickly overshadowed by the persistent squirm of the tadpole behind his eye. He was free, but irrevocably tainted.

His cold, calculating gaze swept towards the horizon, where a massive plume of black smoke billowed into the pale sky. The Nautiloid. A fiery ruin, now a grotesque monument to their terrifying escape. He knew, with the chilling certainty of a predator, that the wreckage would hold secrets. Perhaps clues about the tadpoles, or even something useful to further their desperate survival. His high neuroticism gnawed at him, demanding action, demanding control over this chaotic reality. The wilderness was an untamed beast, but the wreckage, for all its danger, was a known entity, a lingering piece of the nightmare he could still dissect.

"Hardly a scenic detour, but likely informative," Astarion drawled, pushing off the damp earth. "Unless you prefer to simply stare at the clouds, I suggest we investigate the... *landing site* of our former accommodations. It might hold answers. Or more things to kill." He began moving, his footsteps light and precise on the uneven ground, tracing the path towards the distant smoke, his longbow held loosely in one hand, ready. The air grew thicker with the scent of scorched metal and ozone as they approached, mingling with the damp earth and pine. The ground beneath their feet became increasingly scarred, trees snapped like twigs, and the earth was churned and scorched, a testament to the colossal impact.

Lae'zel followed without a word, her golden eyes fixed on the smoke, her hand instinctively going to her empty hip. Her jaw was set, a grim determination etched on her features. Gale sighed dramatically, pushing himself to his feet. "Oh, joy. More wreckage. As if the last few hours weren't sufficiently... *dismembering*." He grumbled, but followed, his gaze already scanning the ground, perhaps for lost arcane trinkets amidst the destruction.
