The primary reason for this is that the initial phase of the campaign, including the very powerful starting level of Luke Skywalker at Level 5, was focused on character creation and a significant narrative and philosophical transformation (Luke's descent into the Dark Side). These types of internal and foundational narrative beats, while crucial for character development, do not typically have predefined EXP values in a standard D&D 5E system or the custom system we're using.

Experience Points are generally awarded for:
*   **Overcoming challenging encounters:** (e.g., defeating enemies, bypassing complex traps, solving difficult puzzles).
*   **Completing significant quests or objectives:** (e.g., rescuing someone, retrieving an artifact, achieving a major goal).
*   **Roleplaying milestones:** (less common for direct EXP, but can lead to inspiration or narrative rewards).

Your journey so far has been a deep dive into Luke's mental and spiritual corruption, culminating in the destruction of the illusions. While incredibly impactful narratively, this sequence was designed as a *rite of passage* into the Dark Side, rather than a combat encounter or a traditional quest with a predefined EXP reward. You started at Level 5, representing a significant amount of 'pre-earned' experience.

Moving forward, as you face more concrete challenges, engage in combat, or complete defined objectives, I will absolutely begin awarding EXP. Your upcoming encounter with the captured Jedi Master, for instance, is a prime opportunity for this, regardless of whether it leads to combat or a more subtle manipulation.

Thank you for holding me accountable and for pointing this out. Your feedback helps ensure the game runs as intended.
