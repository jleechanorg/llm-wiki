You closed the first journal, its ancient leather suddenly feeling hot in your trembling hands. The weight of this inheritance, the potential for that terrifying 'joy' within your own blood, was a burden far heavier than any guilt you had carried before. The words "Joyful Predator" echoed in your mind, no longer an abstract concept, but a chilling, personal truth.

Without a word, you reached for the second journal on the shelf, your fingers moving with a grim determination. Magister Kantos remained silent, his gaze fixed on you, a profound sadness etched on his ancient face. The transition from one volume to the next felt less like continuing a research project and more like descending deeper into a hidden abyss.

This second journal plunged into the psychological profiles of the "successful" Nulls—those rare few who had manifested the full "Joyful Predator" trait. It detailed how their power wasn't just about silencing magic or shattering constructs. It was about *control*. The entries spoke of individuals who found profound satisfaction in subtly dismantling rival scholars' careers through fabricated evidence, or crippling noble houses through meticulously orchestrated financial ruin, or turning loyal retainers against their masters with whispers perfectly placed to exploit their deepest insecurities. The "joy" was in the intellectual elegance of completely dominating another's will or destroying their life without ever resorting to overt violence, leaving their victims broken and bewildered, unable to comprehend the unseen hand that crushed them.

You felt a cold knot in your stomach. This was a different kind of war, fought with unseen blades and psychological precision. This was the terrifying brilliance your mother had wielded, not just on the battlefield, but perhaps in the polished halls of the court. The idea that such a subtle, insidious power could bring an almost ecstatic satisfaction, a joy in personal unmaking, was deeply unsettling. The journal even theorized that this "Joyful Predator" trait was, in its purest form, a terrifyingly potent version of the Empyrean Affliction itself—a perversion of celestial power, warped not into madness or rage, but into a chilling, absolute control.
