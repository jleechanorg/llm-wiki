Serpent Queen sat in the dim light of the safe house, the heavy canvas sack open before her. The contents spilled out onto the rough wooden table, a glittering, chaotic heap of a dynasty's lifeblood. The silence of the room was heavy, broken only by the faint, rhythmic clinking as she began to sort the spoils with the dispassionate precision of a coroner dissecting a corpse.

The 'Total Acquisition' had been absolute. Lord Umbrage was not merely robbed; he was hollowed out. But raw value was not the same as realized gain. She separated the items into two distinct piles, her mind weighing not just their gold weight, but their political mass.

In the first pile: heavy bags of platinum and gold coinage, a box of loose, uncut gems, and the intricate, heavy silver candlesticks. This was silent wealth. Untraceable, liquid, and immediately useful. It disappeared into a purse as easily as water into sand.

In the second pile: the heavy, wax-sealed deeds to Lower City commercial properties, the bearer bonds drawn on the Counting House, and the most recognizable of the family heirlooms—a necklace of heavy emeralds bearing the Umbrage crest. This was 'loud' wealth. To sell the necklace was to invite questions. To cash the bonds was to leave a signature. To hold the deeds was useless without the legal standing to enforce them.

Arvax expected a punishment. He expected art. He did not know she had taken the foundation stones of the estate itself. The question was not just what to keep, but how to use what she gave away to buy something far more valuable than coin.
