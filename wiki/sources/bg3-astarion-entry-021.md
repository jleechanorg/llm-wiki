"Then let's arm you, shall we?" Astarion responds to the burly man, a subtle, almost predatory gleam in his crimson eyes. A well-armed brute was infinitely more useful than an unarmed one. His disdain for the raw, unrefined power the man exuded was tempered by a practical acknowledgment of its potential. The Mind Flayers continued their unnerving, silent work, their backs still to the trio, a fragile window of opportunity. The guttural hum of the infernal engine intensified slightly, vibrating through the fleshy floor.

Astarion moves quickly, gliding between the pulsating slabs and the grotesque bio-machinery. His eyes, accustomed to discerning patterns in urban chaos, now rapidly scan this alien environment. He isn't looking for a common axe or sword; he's looking for anything that could be repurposed, anything sharp or heavy, a discarded piece of illithid technology that might serve as a crude weapon. The air is thick with the scent of fear and something acrid, almost metallic. He can feel the tadpole squirming behind his eye, a constant, unsettling reminder of the ticking clock. The Githyanki, her patience clearly wearing thin, watches him with an intensity that borders on irritation, her hand twitching towards her empty hip. The burly man, meanwhile, flexes his impressive physique, his gaze fixed on the Mind Flayers, eager for confrontation.

Astarion's fingers probe at various conduits and discarded, calcified growths on the walls, seeking a loose point, a fracture, anything that might break off. This place was a living engine, but even living things could be broken and repurposed. He ignores the other captives, their moans and whimpers a dull backdrop to his focused search.
