He finds himself able to *catch* her, to hold her, only for her to dissolve in his arms with a look not of terror, but of profound, logical indifference.

The constant, predictable cycle of torment that feeds Strahd's obsession is being meticulously, surgically dismantled. His pursuit, once fueled by desperate, irrational hope, begins to reveal its true nature: a pointless, repetitive action. His despair is not being intensified; it is being drained of its meaning, its *logic*. The foundation of his very existence, the engine of his domain, starts to stutter and cough.

The first domain begins its conquest, not with a roar, but with a quiet, insidious erosion of its very essence. The entertainment promises to be exquisite.
