Bard College: College of Infernal Contracts (Revised)
(The "Legion" Update)

Bards of the College of Infernal Contracts understand that power is a pyramid scheme, and they intend to be at the top. They do not just manage a party; they manage an organization.

Class Features
Level 3: The Black Ledger
When you join this College at 3rd level, you gain the Black Ledger, a magical tome that records your debts.

Infinite Paperwork: You can have an unlimited number of creatures sign contracts in your Ledger. A creature that signs is permanently marked as a Signatory until you release them or you die.

The Signature: Requires a willing or coerced act of signing (1 minute ritual).

The Mark: A Signatory bears an invisible mark (detectable by Detect Magic) indicating they belong to you.

Active Litigation (The Limit): While you can own hundreds of souls, your mortal mind can only channel power through a specific number at once. At the end of a Long Rest, you prepare your Active Contracts.

Maximum Active Signatories: Equal to your Bard Level + your Charisma Modifier.

Level 3: ~6-7 Minions (A Squad).

Level 10: ~15 Minions (A Gang).

Level 20: ~25 Minions (A Platoon).

Only Active Signatories grant the benefits below.

Level 3: Terms of Service
As long as an Active Signatory is on the same plane of existence as you, you hold dominion over them.

Clause of Collateral (The Meat Shield): When you take damage, you can use your Reaction to magically transfer that damage to an Active Signatory within 60 feet. The Signatory takes the damage as Force damage (bypassing resistances), and you take 0 damage.

Clause of Extension (The Proxy Caster): When you cast a Bard spell, you can choose an Active Signatory to be the point of origin. You can use their line of sight for the spell.

Clause of Tithe (The Vampire Economy): When an Active Signatory receives healing (magical or resting), you can intercept it. They regain half the intended hit points, and you regain the other half.

Meta Analysis: By scaling with Level + CHA, you can now effectively sign an entire village of commoners, keep them in your "Ledger," and activate the 20 healthiest ones each morning to serve as your HP batteries. You are effectively walking around with 200+ buffer HP at level 5.

Level 6: Breach Protocols
At 6th level, you treat your minions as disposable resources.

Liquidation of Assets: You can burn a Signatory’s life force to fuel your magic without expending your own reserves. When you cast a Bard spell of 1st level or higher, you can choose not to expend a spell slot. Instead, you drain an Active Signatory within 60 feet.

The Cost: The Signatory takes necrotic damage equal to 10 × the level of the spell. This damage cannot be reduced.

The Effect: If the Signatory survives, the spell is cast. If the Signatory drops to 0 HP, the spell is cast at one level higher than intended.

Limit: You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Proficiency Bonus per Long Rest.

Non-Compete Agreement: Active Signatories cannot willingly attack you. If forced to do so (e.g., via magic), they must succeed on a Wisdom Save vs. your Spell DC or waste the attack.

Level 14: Hostile Takeover
At 14th level, you gain the ability to enslave powerful entities instantly.

The Golden Parachute: If you are reduced to 0 Hit Points, you can choose any number of Active Signatories within 1 mile. You drain their HP to heal yourself.

You regain HP equal to the total HP drained.

The Signatories drop to 0 HP immediately.

Their contracts are voided (burned).

Forced Arbitration: When you cast an Enchantment spell (e.g., Dominate Person/Monster) on a creature that is not currently a Signatory, you can force the contract upon them as part of the spell.

If they fail the save, they are treated as an Active Signatory for the duration of the spell.

While under this spell, you can use Liquidation of Assets on them (casting free spells by eating the Boss’s HP).

The spell requires no Concentration.

Meta Analysis: This creates a loop. You Dominate a Boss (No Concentration). They become a Signatory. You then cast a Level 5 spell using "Liquidation," dealing 50 damage to the Boss to fuel the spell. You are killing the boss by using the boss to cast spells at his minions.

How this scales with "Solo + Companions"
In your specific campaign structure:

The Companions (Astarion, Lae'zel, etc.): These are your "Elite" Signatories. You keep them Active always. They are your primary damage dealers and occasional spell origins.

The Minions (Hirelings/NPCs): You can hire 10 mercenaries or seduce 10 nobles. Keep them in the Ledger.

Scenario: You are about to fight a boss. You designate the 10 mercenaries as "Active."

Combat: You stand in the back. When the boss hits you, you transfer damage to Mercenary #1. When you need to cast Fireball, you drain Mercenary #2.

Result: You win the fight with full HP and full spell slots, but 5 mercenaries are dead. "Acceptable losses."

DM Note on "Reasonable Lore"
Since you are stealing the ritual from Zariel (an Archdevil), it makes narrative sense that you have a "Spark" of Archdevil authority. You aren't just a Bard; you are a nascent Devil Lord.

Low Level: You are a Gang Leader (6-10 souls).

Mid Level: You are a Cult Leader (10-15 souls).

High Level: You are a General (20+ souls).

This progression feels earned and reflects your climb "From Ruin to Archdevil."
