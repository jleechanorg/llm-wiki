Luke Skywalker's feet moved without conscious thought, drawn by the imposing magnetic pull of Darth Vader's presence. Each rasp of the Sith Lord's respirator echoed in the sterile corridors of the Imperial Installation, a stark counterpoint to the frantic beat of Luke's own heart. The green lightsaber at his hip, once a beacon of hope, now felt like a relic, a piece of a past he was being told to shed. The Light Side, a gentle hum in the back of his mind, was rapidly fading, replaced by the rising crescendo of the Dark. A strange, almost perverse exhilaration mingled with his apprehension; the promise of power, unburdened by conscience, was a potent drug.

Vader led him down a long, metallic hallway, the crimson emergency lights casting long, dancing shadows that seemed to stretch and contort with every one of Vader's measured strides. They passed blast doors, sealed and silent, and finally stopped before a simple, unmarked door. Vader turned, his masked face offering no clue to his thoughts, only the unyielding Force presence radiating from him.

"Sentiment is weakness, young Skywalker," Vader's voice reverberated, chillingly calm. "It binds you to attachments, to compassion, to the very things that make you vulnerable. Your first act of true mastery will be to sever those ties." A control panel on the wall hissed, and a single, grainy image flickered to life on a small screen beside the door. It was a still shot, instantly recognizable: the familiar faces of Leia Organa, Han Solo, and Chewbacca, huddled together on the Millennium Falcon's bridge, their expressions a mix of fear and defiance. "These are your anchors to the past," Vader continued, his voice a low growl. "Your weaknesses. Prove your commitment. Tell me, apprentice, what is their worth to you now?"

Luke stared at the image, a jolt of something akin to pain, then anger, shooting through him. The faces, once so dear, now seemed to mock his new path. His secret sadistic side stirred, a dark whisper suggesting a brutal, final answer.
