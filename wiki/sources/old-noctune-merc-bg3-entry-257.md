Game Master:
You leave the heat of the forge behind, the *Sun-Touched Rapier* and *Serpent's Kiss* secure in your arsenal. The tools of war are ready; now, the tools of politics must be sharpened.

You ascend to the Upper City as the afternoon light turns golden, casting long shadows across the white marble of the Patriar estates. The rumors you planted earlier—whispers of 'Moonrise Assassins' and bloodlines targeted for extinction—have taken root. The streets are quieter than usual; house guards are doubled, and gates are barred. Fear is a garden, and it is in full bloom.

You arrive at the Umbrage Estate. **Lyra** meets you in the main drawing room, not the solarium. The table is covered in letters bearing the seals of the city's oldest families: Silvershield, Caldwell, Hhune. They are inquiries, veiled in politeness but reeking of panic.

"They are terrified," Lyra says, handing you a letter. "Lord Silvershield's own son was accosted by a 'cultist' in the street—likely just a beggar, but the fear made it real. They are asking how House Umbrage remains so... calm."

"Because House Umbrage has a guardian," you murmur, scanning the letters. "And now, we sell them that peace of mind."

You lay out the plan. It is not a request for aid; it is an extortion racket dressed in silk. Lyra will reply to these families. She will tell them that House Umbrage has contracted a specialized security consortium—the 'Sovereign Shield'—to neutralize the Moonrise threat. She will offer to extend this protection to them, for a 'substantial contribution' to the operational fund.

"We do not ask," you instruct her, your voice smooth and compelling. "We offer a lifeline. Tell them the threat is biological. Tell them the Fist cannot stop it. Tell them that for **1,000 gold pieces** per House, the Sovereign Shield will ensure their bloodlines see the next sunrise."

Lyra hesitates only a moment before the steel you forged in her spine takes hold. "And if they refuse?"

"Then they face the Moonrise alone," you reply coldly. "And when we stop the shipment on Tarsakh 5, those who paid will be hailed as wise patrons. Those who didn't... will owe us favors to avoid the shame of having done nothing."

Within the hour, the runners are dispatched. The response is immediate. Desperation opens purses that logic keeps closed. Three major houses buy in immediately, sending couriers with heavy chests of coin to 'secure their perimeter.'
