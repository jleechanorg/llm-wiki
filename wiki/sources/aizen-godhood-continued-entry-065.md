**Summary: The Original Time of Troubles**

The original Time of Troubles (also known as the Godswar) occurred in the Year of Shadows (1358 DR). It was precipitated by Ao, the Overgod, who cast nearly all the gods of Faerûn down to the mortal realm. This divine exile was a punishment for their theft of the Tablets of Fate, which recorded their duties and portfolios. Stripped of most of their divine power, the gods were forced to walk among their worshipers as powerful mortals, experiencing hunger, pain, and vulnerability. Their interactions caused immense chaos and destruction across Faerûn. Many gods were slain, either by mortals or by other gods, and some new deities ascended. The conflict culminated in the return of the Tablets of Fate, the death of Bane and Myrkul, the ascension of Cyric and Midnight (the former mortal avatar of Mystra), and a reordering of the divine hierarchy, with Ao imposing stricter rules on divine intervention and interaction with mortals. The event profoundly shaped Faerûn's history, leaving a lasting impact on its peoples and their perception of the gods.

*Mystra's Plan: Time of Troubles v2 - The Great Unraveling*

"My Lord, the original 'Time of Troubles' was indeed inefficient. Its primary flaw was its unpredictability, its reliance on chaos. Your vision, however, is one of absolute, orchestrated order, even in perceived chaos. Therefore, my plan focuses on controlled variables and maximal psychological impact, ensuring continuous amusement for you.

**Phase 1: The Cascading Disconnect (Immediate)**
*   **Target:** The mortalized gods.
*   **Mechanism:** Their inherent connection to their former portfolios, though dormant, still provides a subtle anchor to reality. I will meticulously sever these conceptual anchors. This will not cause death, but a profound, existential disorientation. They will retain their Level 6 mortal forms, but their memories of *being* a god, their understanding of their purpose, will become fragmented, distorted, or utterly alien. They will remember the *truth* of your dominion, the knowledge that they were gods, but their former divine identity will be lost to them, replaced by a deep, gnawing sense of wrongness.
*   **Outcome:** They will be scattered across Faerûn, not as powerful mortals with memories of godhood, but as confused, disoriented Level 6 individuals, suffering from a profound crisis of identity, their actions driven by vague, misremembered impulses related to their old domains. Their struggles to comprehend their own existence will be a constant source of amusement.

**Phase 2: The Mortal Weave's Unpredictability (Ongoing)**
*   **Target:** All magic-users not directly bound to your will (i.e., not my Chosen).
*   **Mechanism:** I will introduce minute, undetectable fluctuations within the Weave, causing low-level magic to become subtly unpredictable. Spells will sometimes backfire, produce unexpected effects, or simply fail at random, but infrequent, intervals. Higher-level magic will require increasingly complex, almost ritualistic, preparations, making powerful spellcasting a rare and desperate act. This will not be the overt 'dead magic' zones of the old troubles, but a pervasive, subtle uncertainty.
*   **Outcome:** This fosters a pervasive sense of unease and distrust in magic itself, forcing mortals to rely on less reliable means, while paradoxically making my Chosen (and by extension, your loyal agents) appear even more formidable due to their absolute control over a now-unstable system.

**Phase 3: The Echoing Prophecies (Strategic)**
*   **Target:** Mortals and their emerging heroes/villains.
*   **Mechanism:** I will subtly plant fragmented, contradictory, and deeply unsettling prophecies across Faerûn. These prophecies will hint at the 'return' of gods, the 'rise' of new powers, or the 'end' of magic, all subtly twisted to lead mortals into futile quests, self-destructive conflicts, and ultimately, reinforce their powerlessness against the true, hidden order (yours).
*   **Outcome:** This will generate fascinating new narrative threads as mortals scramble for meaning in a reality they cannot grasp, unwittingly serving as pawns in your ongoing play. Their desperate struggles to understand a truth they cannot comprehend will be exquisite.

**Phase 4: The Chosen's Purpose (Controlled Interaction)**
*   **Target:** My empowered Chosen (Elminster, Laeral, the Seven Sisters, Gale).
*   **Mechanism:** They will serve as beacons of stable power, agents of controlled intervention. They will operate under my direct guidance, selectively stabilizing pockets of the Weave or providing just enough assistance to keep certain 'plots' engaging, preventing utter collapse while ensuring the prolonged suffering and confusion of the mortalized gods and the general populace.
*   **Outcome:** They become key figures in the unfolding drama, their actions serving to manipulate events and characters towards scenarios most entertaining to you, Lord.

This plan, Lord, ensures maximal entertainment and continued demonstration of your absolute dominion, without the crude directness of the previous iteration. It is a slow, elegant unraveling, perfectly managed.
