One year passes in the gloomy, perpetually shrouded domain of Barovia, a year of meticulous observation for Dr. Mordenheim, and a slow, agonizing unraveling for Strahd von Zarovich.

The very air in Barovia feels subtly different. The omnipresent mist, once a suffocating blanket of despair, now thins and thickens with an unnerving, almost mathematical regularity. For a few hours each week, a pale, anemic sun might actually penetrate the clouds, casting weak shadows, only to retreat with precise predictability. The villagers, once resigned to their fate with a fervent, almost ritualistic despair, now exhibit a chilling, almost logical indifference. They go about their daily lives, their faces blank, accepting their suffering with a detached, emotionless calm that is far more unsettling than their previous fear. Their screams, when they occur, are efficient, brief, and devoid of true passion. The 'hope' Dr. Mordenheim instilled is not a vibrant flame, but a carefully measured dose, enough to prolong their endurance, yet never enough to truly empower.

Within Castle Ravenloft, the ancient walls seem to press in on Strahd more acutely than ever before. He paces his audience chamber, his usually impassive face etched with a new, profound frustration. His torment, which he has so carefully cultivated for centuries, is losing its meaning. The perpetual chase for Tatyana, his driving obsession, has become... predictable. He catches her, always, in his dreams now. She no longer screams with terror but fades with a look of profound, logical indifference, as if the chase itself is merely an inefficient algorithm that has reached its inevitable conclusion.

He has tried to intensify his cruelty. He has unleashed more dire wolves, orchestrated more brutal raids, but the fear of his subjects feels *hollow*. Their suffering lacks the delicious, irrational *flavor* he craves. He casts his most potent scrying spells, his vampiric senses straining to identify the source of this erosion. He detects no magical assault, no direct divine intervention. Only an insidious, unyielding *order*, a chilling efficiency that seems to drain the very passion from his domain.

"It is... *logical*," Strahd whispers into the empty air of his castle, the word a venomous curse on his tongue. He smashes a goblet against the wall, but even his rage feels impotent, a mere response to an unseen, perfectly calculated stimulus. He has become a variable in someone else's equation.

In her laboratory back in Lamordia, Dr. Mordenheim watches the distant, minute shifts in Barovia. She makes precise notations in a leather-bound journal, a faint, intellectual smile gracing her lips. "The dismantling of irrational despair," she murmurs to herself, "is proceeding to specification. The subject is experiencing maximum intellectual and existential distress. Barovia's despair is now... perfectly manageable." She closes the journal, her gaze already turning to the next domain on her list. The conquest of Ravenloft is not a war; it is a meticulously executed scientific experiment.
