Cressida’s words, firm and unwavering, were like cool water on the burning wound of your guilt. *No. I do not blame you. And neither would your mother.* The absolute conviction in her voice, so unlike your brothers' cold judgment, allowed a fragile hope to bloom in your chest. The sobs that had wracked your body quieted, leaving behind only the profound ache of loss, now less crushing and more a dull, persistent throb. You squeezed her hand, a silent testament to the lifeline she had just thrown you.

You pulled back slightly, your gaze still fixed on her, your eyes wide and searching. "Then... how can I honor her, Cressida?" Your voice was still thin, raw from weeping, but it held a new, dawning flicker of purpose. The question was a desperate plea for direction, a need to transform your overwhelming grief into something active, something that might justify your continued existence. "How can I... can I prove myself worthy of her sacrifice? She was a legend. I am... nothing."

Cressida’s expression, usually so sharp with political calculation, held only a profound tenderness. She understood. Her hand gently squeezed yours. "You are her daughter, Sariel. That is everything. And you are not 'nothing.' You have her mind." Her gaze swept the meticulously organized scrolls on her desk, then returned to your face, her eyes thoughtful. "Your mother died for the truth she believed in. She died protecting the innocent. And she died fighting a war she alone truly understood."

She paused, a subtle shift in her posture. "If you truly wish to honor her, Sariel, then you must seek the truth she carried. Not the public legend, but the deeper, more complex one. Your mother was a scholar of war, a keeper of secrets. Perhaps," Cressida mused, her voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper, "the answers to her final battle, and the true meaning of her sacrifice, lie not in the glory of the battlefield, but in the quiet, forgotten corners of the Imperium's past. Your mind is the sharpest blade our house possesses. It is time to wield it."
