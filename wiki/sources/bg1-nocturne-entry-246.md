**Operation: Garrison Swap**

**Mirtul 12-15: The Return Trek**
You leave Roric with the keys to the kingdom and a warning not to get stabbed by the slaves. The march back to the **Bandit Camp** is swift; you know the terrain now, and the spiders are burned out. You arrive at the **Fortified Armory** on the evening of the 15th.

**The Rendezvous**
Your timing is impeccable. **Garrick** has just arrived from Nashkel, looking exhausted but triumphant. Trailing behind him are a dozen hard-looking mercenaries—unemployed caravan guards and Flaming Fist deserters looking for coin. He has followed your instructions to the letter.

"Mistress!" Garrick cries, dropping his pack. "The fortress is... rustic. But defensible! I have brought the swords you asked for!"

"Change of plan," you announce, not breaking stride. "This camp is a husk. The real prize is north. Pack up. We move the garrison to the Cloakwood."

Garrick's face falls. "But... my feet..."

"Walk," **Kagain** growls.

**Mirtul 15-18: The Deployment**
You lead the mercenary column back through the forest. With a dozen swords at your back, the few remaining beasts of the Cloakwood give you a wide berth. You arrive back at the **Cloakwood Mines** on the 18th.

**The Handoff**
You descend to Level 4. Roric is waiting, looking bored, tossing a dagger at a target drawn on a tapestry. He sees Garrick and the soldiers and grins.

"Relief!" Roric cheers, springing from the chair. "And not a moment too soon. The damp down here plays hell with my leathers."

You lean in close to **Garrick**, your hand resting heavily on his shoulder. The new Warden of the Mines flinches slightly, expecting a threat, but you offer him a conspiracy instead.

"Listen closely," you whisper, your voice a thread of sound beneath the noise of the mercenaries settling in. "This isn't just a guard post. It's a business. We aren't shutting it down; we're under new management. Keep the slaves working. Stockpile the iron. When the price peaks in Baldur's Gate, I will send word. You sell. You keep ten percent. You send the rest to me."

Garrick's eyes widen. He looks at the opulent sanctum, then at the ledger on the desk. The terror in his face is slowly replaced by greed. "A... a business. Yes. Warden Garrick, the Iron Lord. I like the sound of that. I will keep the coin flowing, Mistress. Count on it."

**Garrick Loyalty Secured.** (Passive Income Unlocked: Pending).

With the asset secured, **Roric** rejoins the pack, equipping his gear. "Good to be back on the road, boss. Running a business is harder work than killing people."

**The March North.**

You leave the Cloakwood behind. By the evening of **Mirtul 19**, the stone walls of the **Friendly Arm Inn** rise from the dusk. It is a sanctuary of warmth and light. You pass the guards without incident and enter the courtyard.

**Bentley Mirrorshade** greets you at the bar. "You look like you've walked through the Hells and back," the gnome says, wiping a glass. "And you look like you have coin to spend."

"We do," you state. "But I want to see the good stock. No rust. No junk."

Bentley nods and unlocks the heavy iron cabinet behind the bar. He lays out a selection of high-quality goods.
