## Summary

Tyranny is a D&D 5e campaign set in the world of Terratus (the Tiers), based on the Obsidian Entertainment game Tyranny. The player controls Nocturne—a Fatebinder who is secretly an ancient intellect piloting a 16-year-old body—as a Level 16 Bard (College of Swords) with a wizard's soul. The campaign explores the "Architecture of Ascension": the pathological optimization of reality, the burden of competence, and the conquest of a world ruled by the oppressive Edicts of Kyros.

## Key Details

- **Player Character:** Nocturne (The Ascendant Architect / The Ghost in the Machine)
- **Starting Level:** 16 (initially requested Level 1, then set to Level 6+)

- **Calendar:** Terratus Reckoning (TR)

## Player Character

**Nocturne** is a Fatebinder who presents as a 16-year-old but carries an ancient intellect. They are a Bard (College of Swords) flavored as a Wizard of the Order of Scribes. Rather than singing, Nocturne recites mathematical proofs that force reality to correct itself; their blade serves as a conductor for magic.

**Visual Aesthetic:** Jarring symmetrical beauty deliberately obscured by road dust and scavenged leathers—a "ghost in plain sight." When the mask slips, posture shifts to "terrifying, statuesque perfection."

**Two Masks:**
1. **The Courtly Shadow (Social Persona):** Soft-spoken, hyper-competent, unassuming. Uses Johan Liebert-style mirroring to create dependency loops.
2. **The Dispassionate Strategist (Crisis Persona):** Cold, administrative evil. Tywin Lannister + Aizen Sosuke hybrid. Intellectualizes fear into systems and probability calculations.

**Core Drives:**
- **The "Hogyoku" Complex:** Must be useful/essential. Weaponized belief that others who offer friendship without utility are parasites.
- **The "Itachi" Experiment:** Clinical urge to stress-test raw talents like Verse.
- **The Refactoring of Reality:** Seeks forbidden knowledge to "patch" the universe—Terratus is "full of bad code."

**Combat Bark:** "Your stance is off by three degrees. You were dead before you swung."

## Major Themes

- **The Pathology of Power:** Competence as existential ticket; optimization as deity-making
- **Refactoring Reality:** Viewing the world as a flawed schematic to be patched
- **Dual Masking:** Two perfectly-fitted psychological personas for social survival
- **Psychological Horror:** The "Wounded Architect" archetype—INTJ-ENTP hinge
- **Conquest as Science:** Pre-game history treated as scientific methodology
