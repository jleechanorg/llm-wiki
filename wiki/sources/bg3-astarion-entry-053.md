The gruesome tunnel finally opened into a vast, unsettling chamber, but Astarion's breath hitched, not from disgust, but from a profound, terrifying awe. Before them, a massive, crystalline membrane stretched across the far wall, revealing an impossible, dizzying expanse of the cosmic void. Stars, nebulae, and distant, swirling galaxies spun outside in a horrifyingly beautiful panorama, painting the chamber in shifting hues of purple, blue, and black. The Nautiloid was truly soaring through the depths of space, a fact that was both mesmerizing and utterly terrifying.

The Githyanki and the burly man, who had been so eager for confrontation moments before, froze, their raw fury momentarily replaced by stunned silence. They gazed out at the impossible vista, transfixed by the sheer, overwhelming scale of it all. "Gods above," the burly man muttered, his voice uncharacteristically hushed, the raw power draining from him for a moment.

Astarion, however, allowed himself only a fleeting second of similar astonishment. His eyes, trained to find danger even in beauty, quickly snapped back to the chamber itself. Scattered amidst pulsating, fleshy consoles and conduits that writhed like living snakes, were several smaller, ovoid pods. They pulsed with a sickly green light, clearly biological, and within their translucent membranes, he could discern the unsettling, familiar forms of **larval Mind Flayers**, still dormant, but undeniably present. His stomach curdled. *More of them. Of course.*

"A charming view, isn't it?" Astarion's voice, though sharp with its usual sarcasm, held a tremor of genuine revulsion. He gestured with his bone blade towards the pods. "But I suggest we refrain from admiring the scenery. Our hosts are incubating more unpleasantness. And I, for one, would prefer not to contribute to their growing collection. We need a plan, *now*." His eyes flicked between his awe-struck companions, a mixture of exasperation and desperate urgency in their crimson depths. The tadpole behind his eye throbbed, a cold, insistent reminder that their time was fleeting.
