1.  Whether Itachi will gain access to his other Mangekyō Sharingan abilities (Amaterasu, Susano'o) later in the campaign.
2.  To give Itachi a stronger narrative reason for the Uchiha clan massacre.

Let's address these in turn:

---

**1. Future Mangekyō Sharingan Abilities:**

Currently, Itachi has chosen **Tsukuyomi** as his unique Mangekyō ability, as per the initial setup. In the Naruto canon, Itachi's Mangekyō allowed him access to both Tsukuyomi (left eye) and Amaterasu (right eye), with Susano'o being the ultimate ability unlocked by possessing both.

**My Proposal for Future Access:**
Given Itachi's immense ambition and his "evil" nature, it is entirely plausible that he would seek to unlock or acquire the full spectrum of Mangekyō powers. I propose that Itachi *can* gain access to **Amaterasu** and subsequently **Susano'o** through specific, narratively significant actions.

*   **Amaterasu Acquisition:** Itachi could unlock Amaterasu through one of the following:
    *   **Intense Emotional Catalyst (Evil Aligned):** Experiencing a profound moment of extreme hatred, rage, or cold determination directly related to a major setback or obstacle to his grand vision. This is the canonical method for Mangekyō awakening, but recontextualized for his evil alignment.
    *   **Ritualistic Acquisition:** Discovering and performing a forbidden Uchiha ritual or a dark magical practice that forcibly awakens the latent ability in his other eye. This might require rare components or sacrifices.
    *   **Eye Transplantation (From a specific, powerful Uchiha victim):** If he encounters another Uchiha who possessed the potential for Amaterasu (perhaps a unique survivor or a reanimated corpse), he could forcibly extract and transplant their eye, though this would be a high-risk procedure with potential side effects.
*   **Susano'o Acquisition:** Once Itachi has awakened or acquired both Tsukuyomi and Amaterasu, he will then have the *potential* to manifest **Susano'o**. Manifesting Susano'o would likely require a significant mastery milestone, and perhaps an additional, greater cost (beyond just Fatigue per round, possibly a permanent strain on his vision or even his body over time, as per canon). This would be a Tier 3 or 4 ability.

Do you agree with this approach for Itachi to gain more Mangekyō abilities, or do you have a different preference?

---

**2. Stronger Narrative Reason for the Uchiha Clan Massacre:**

Your current evil justification is: "The Uchiha clan, in Itachi's cold, calculating view, is a cancer upon the Shinobi world. Their obsession with tradition, their festering resentment, their volatile emotions... are a fundamental flaw... He sees himself as a surgeon, performing a necessary, brutal excision for the "greater good"—his own meticulously planned, absolute, and unyielding order."

This is already quite strong and sets a clear, ruthless tone. To make it "stronger," we can either make it more personal to Itachi, more philosophical, or more immediately provoked.

**My Proposed Refinements/Additions (Choose one, or suggest your own):**

1.  **The Uchiha as an "Uncontrollable Variable" (Exacerbated Threat):** The clan's planned coup wasn't just typical defiance; Itachi had observed their internal factions, their hidden power struggles, and their dangerous, almost self-destructive tendency to escalate conflicts. He foresaw that their rebellion, even if initially successful, would inevitably plunge Konoha into a protracted civil war, spilling out into wider regional chaos that would permanently cripple his own plans for a unified, hidden order. The massacre wasn't just prevention; it was the *only* way to contain a volatile chemical reaction before it exploded and irrevocably contaminated his entire grand experiment. He didn't just *think* they were a threat; he had *proof* (internal or external) they were an **unmanageable, immediate, and irredeemable impediment to his ultimate vision of global control.**
2.  **The Sharingan's "Corrupted Potential" (Ideological Purity):** Itachi believes the Uchiha's emotional, selfish use of the Sharingan—a power born of intense trauma and often fueling cycles of hatred and revenge—is a perversion of its true, ultimate potential. He sees the clan itself as a "factory" for this flawed, emotionally driven power. By eradicating them, he is not just removing an obstacle; he is performing a **purification ritual for the Sharingan itself**, believing that once freed from the "curse of hatred" that defines the clan, its true, absolute power (which he intends to master) can be fully realized for his ultimate, cold, and logical order. The massacre is a necessary act to cleanse the bloodline of its inherent emotional weakness, making him the sole, pure wielder.
3.  **A "Necessary Sacrifice" for a Grand Experiment (Utter Detachment):** The Uchiha Clan Massacre is not merely a means to an end, but also a **foundational act, a grand experiment in control and transformation.** Itachi isn't just eliminating a threat; he is actively testing the limits of societal manipulation, psychological warfare, and the very nature of power. The sheer scale of the atrocity, the trauma inflicted, and the subsequent cover-up will serve as a **crucible through which he himself will forge a greater, more ruthless resolve**, hardening his soul for the even grander sacrifices and manipulations required for his ultimate "Great Unifying Order." He views the clan's death as a necessary (and perhaps even fascinating) data point, a foundational "sin" that will paradoxically enable a "pure" future under his absolute control.

Please choose which of these refinements resonates most with your vision for Evil Itachi's motivation, or propose your own. Once confirmed, I will integrate it into his character.
