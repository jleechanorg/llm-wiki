## 1. Campaign premise and through‑line

The campaign is a solo, evil‑aligned Baldur’s Gate 3–style game set in 1492 DR, immediately after Elturel’s fall. Baldur’s Gate is flooded with refugees, the Flaming Fist is stretched thin, the Guild is exploiting the chaos, and a new cult called the Absolute is quietly weaponizing mind‑flayer tadpoles.

You play **Nocturne** – also called **Serpent Queen** and, later, **the Shadow Monarch** – a 16‑year‑old, exiled bastard of a noble house. She’s a Level 6→7→8 **College of Swords Bard**, designed as a cold, system‑obsessed predator with a custom mechanic: **The Hunger**, which is satisfied through ritual, intentional killings and, when sated, grants **+2 to all ability scores** for a time.

The core of the campaign is not dungeon‑delving but **structural conquest**:

* Turn **contracts** (kidnapping, heists, assassinations) into *levers* on the city’s power systems.
* Build a network: **Smiley Arvax → Nine‑Fingers Keene → Black Lotus squad → Shadow Bank → House Umbrage → Flaming Fist → Patriars**.
* Simultaneously dissect and weaponize the **Absolute’s tadpoles** (Absolute Anatomy + Idol’s Malice) instead of just destroying them.

By the current point in the log:

* You have **broken the local Zhentarim**, captured key officers (Kaelen D’Vray, ship captains, a Red Wizard’s cargo), and converted their logistics into your own infrastructure.
* You control a **private kill‑team** (**Black Lotus**), a **Shadow Bank** consuming the city’s debt, **House Umbrage** as a noble puppet, and **Vice‑Marshal Rokas** as a suborned arm of the law.
* You have **captured an Absolute shipment and a True Soul leader**, giving you live tadpoles, a testbed ship, and a high‑value prisoner.

The big strategic arc: turn Baldur’s Gate into a *machine* that runs on terror, debt, and biology, with you as the unseen sovereign.

4. **Limit Break: Omni‑Attunement**

## 4. Chronological campaign summary (important elements)

### 4.1. Opening: The tiefling acquisition (Rivington)

1. **First contract from Smiley Arvax**

   * In the Smirking Boar’s cellar, Arvax offers you a “simple acquisition”: a family of four **Asmodeus‑spawn tieflings** in the Rivington shantytown. Parents and two children. Good money; low profile; perfect for someone “not squeamish.”
   * You accept, viewing it as the first step in building capital and demonstrating that you can see “the whole board.”

2. **Reconnaissance in Rivington**

   * You stealth into the refugee camp, observe the tent’s routines (father out fishing, mother doing short errands, children staying inside) and map patrols of Flaming Fist and Guild enforcers. Vulnerability window: roughly 22:00–05:00.

3. **Execution and feeding the Hunger**

   * You infiltrate at night, use enchantments to quietly wake and control the parents one at a time, then kill each with a silent thrust of your rapier, deliberately feeding the Hunger. The children are gagged, bound, and moved non‑lethally.
   * Adults’ bodies are weighted and dumped in the Chionthar; the kids are deposited, still alive and packaged, at a pre‑arranged Guild dead‑drop. Stealth and logistics are perfect; the camp never realizes what happened.

4. **Payment and positioning**

   * You return to Arvax, collect your money, and subtly demonstrate you understand markets beyond “grab bodies for coin.” This flags you as more than a thug: a potential strategist.

Result: **The Hunger mechanic** is established, Arvax is hooked, and the tone of the campaign (evil, surgical, system‑focused) is set.

### 4.2. From contract killer to strategic asset (Kaelen D’Vray, covert ops, the Idol)

5. **Basilisk Gate kill box: Kaelen D’Vray**

   * You position yourself inside the gatehouse recess at dawn as Kaelen and two knights ride in. Hypnotic Pattern drops all three where they sit, and Black Lotus appears from their hiding places to execute the ambush.
   * You kill one knight to refresh the Hunger, strip and bind Kaelen and the remaining knight, and ride them to the Coiled Serpent posing as Zhent riders with prisoners.

6. **Interrogation and intelligence**

   * In the safehouse cellar, stripped of status, Kaelen becomes your first truly high‑value interrogatee. Using Idol’s Malice aura plus psychological pressure, you extract:

     * contract terms for the Red Wizard Thayian,
     * the **lead‑wagon protection doctrine**,
     * and the green‑flare “all clear” signal critical for Wyrm’s Rock ambush design.

7. **The Whispering Idol**

   * Around this period you secure the **Whispering Idol** and, after multiple failed checks and mental assaults, finally break it open and unlock **Idol’s Malice**, gaining a potent per‑day debuff on saves against your Enchantments/Illusions.

Result: You now have **Kaelen as a resource**, a **powerful artifact**, and the doctrinal blueprint for tearing the Zhentarim caravan apart.

### 4.3. The Silent Wave heist (Grey Harbor) – proof of concept

8. **Formation and briefing of Black Lotus**

   * In the Undercellar, you’re given command of the Guild’s elite squad: Vesper, Gant, Silas, Nyx. You brief them on the **Silent Wave**, a Zhent trawler trafficking refugees and contraband. Doctrine: capture the ship and its humans intact rather than simply torpedoing the operation – seize value, don’t just deny it.

9. **Doctrine upgrade: Operation Seizure + Invisible Guillotine**

   * You redesign the attack: you and Vesper both go Invisible; Vesper alpha‑strikes the captain to break her invisibility and trigger surprise, while you remain unseen and kill key deck figures with Greater Invisibility. The squad’s role shifts from pure slaughter to jailors taking control of the ship and its cargo.

10. **Assault and capture**

    * At sundown, you infiltrate the ship. Vesper crits the captain from invisibility; you follow up with Slashing Flourish to simultaneously finish the captain and maim the quartermaster. Silas and Gant secure the deck; Nyx holds the gangplank. The *Silent Wave* leadership is annihilated, the crew is broken, and the ship is seized.
    * You deliver ship and cargo to the Guild instead of just killing everyone, proving you can **convert enemy infrastructure into Guild assets**.

Result: This job cements your reputation with Arvax and sets up your later audience with Nine‑Fingers Keene.

### 4.4. House Umbrage, Lyra, and the Shadow Bank

11. **The Umbrage problem and Lyra as lever**

    * Arvax’s higher‑level assignment: House Umbrage is entangled with Zhents and not paying the Guild. You propose using **Lyra**, the daughter, not as a hostage but as a *future queen* – a vessel for your power and a wedge against both her father and the Zhents.

12. **Rathmar and the painting**

    * You track Rathmar, the frightened Zhentarim banker, and confront him with the painting **“Despair of the Undercity”** – a visual symbol of his failures and his role in letting the Lower City rot. Combined with knowledge of his clandestine Zhent dealings, you psychologically break him, forcing him to yank Zhent eyes off Umbrage and later to cede control of debt markers to your Shadow Bank.

13. **Total Acquisition heist**

    * With Zhent surveillance pulled back, you infiltrate the Umbrage estate, loot the safe (cash, property deeds, bearer bonds), strip the master bedroom of jewelry and art, and leave the house physically untouched but financially hollowed out. This is **Scene #154** (“Total Acquisition”). Lyra is deliberately kept unaware so her trust remains intact.

14. **Installing Lyra as regent**

    * You use the stolen wealth under a front (“Sovereign Consortium”) to present Lyra as the savior of her family’s finances, arrange her **regency** position, and position yourself as the invisible power behind the house.
    * Lyra becomes the public face of the Upper City arm of your empire, while her father becomes a ghost.

Result: House Umbrage becomes a **puppet noble house**, and Rathmar and his books become the skeleton key for turning the city’s financial system into your tool.

### 4.5. Nine‑Fingers Keene and the Guild partnership

15. **Arvax as shield, Keene as gateway**

    * You propose a scheme that uses Arvax as a “risk firewall”: he presents your radical plan to Nine‑Fingers; if it fails, he takes the blame, if it succeeds, he’s the kingmaker. He accepts, and later a runner arrives: “the heavy door is open” – Keene is willing to meet.

16. **Whispering Idol as social weapon**

    * Before going into the “lion’s den,” you attune more deeply to the Whis­pering Idol, realizing it doesn’t speak but **echoes ambition**, amplifying your presence into an aura that makes the weak kneel. You carry that resonance into Keene’s office.

17. **Establishing the Shadow Monarch**

    * In Keene’s office, backed by Arvax, you effectively present:

      * the Umbrage solution,
      * your capture of Kaelen and Silent Wave,
      * and your broader philosophy of “surgical domination.” Keene comes to view you not as a disposable freelancer but as a **strategic partner** and green‑lights further high‑risk operations.

Result: You gain **formal recognition** within the Guild ecosystem; Black Lotus and major ops are now routinely under your design.

### 4.6. Vessic and the Gilded Cage (Zhent fence network)

18. **From Silent Wave to Vessic**

    * The prisoners and documents from the Silent Wave point toward **Vessic**, the Zhentarim fence in Little Calimshan, as the key node in the local Zhent logistics network.

19. **Set‑up outside the Gilded Cage**

    * You and Black Lotus stage outside Vessic’s shop (the Gilded Cage) with a plan for **silent breach**: Nyx casts a 4th‑level Invisibility to cloak Vesper, Gant, and Silas; you cloak yourself with Greater Invisibility. Enemy mercs expect thieves, not an execution squad.

20. **Assault, subversion, and loot**

    * You execute a clean surprise round, wipe Vessic’s security, and confront him amid the corpses. Through intimidation, you flip him: he keeps his life and shop, but becomes **your parasite on the Black Network**, feeding all coin, shipment data, and rumors upstream to you first.
    * You strip the shop and safe (gold, gems, magic items including **Ring of Mind Shielding**, plus ledgers detailing caravan routes and guard rosters). These ledgers later expose debts like Vice‑Marshal Rokas’s.

Result: The **Zhent fence network is not only broken but inverted**; Vessic becomes the beating heart of your Shadow Bank.

### 4.7. The Wyrm’s Rock ambush and the Soul Coins

21. **Black Lotus briefing for Wyrm’s Rock**

* Using Kaelen’s intel (Wizard protects wagon only; green flare as all‑clear), plus caravan routes from Vessic’s ledger, you design a sniper/anti‑mage doctrine:

  * Silas must alpha‑strike the Red Wizard;
  * Nyx holds Counterspell as a fail‑safe;
  * Vesper & Gant hit wagons while you maintain hard control over the battlefield.

22. **Approach and ambush**

* On Ches 25, at dawn, you ride from the city to Wyrm’s Rock, meet Black Lotus at the ridge, and set up the killbox. Silas kills the Wizard with a single arrow as soon as the caravan enters, and you signal the attack with the green flare – impersonating their own “road clear” sign.

23. **Lead wagon and discovery**

* After the guards are annihilated, you unlock the **lead wagon**, which is lead‑lined and contains:

  * an infernal‑iron chest of **10 Soul Coins**,
  * crates of refugee “personal effects” labeled *PROCESSED*,
  * and the **Manifest of Souls** enumerating harvested Elturian refugees.
* You understand: the Zhents and Red Wizards are trading souls as a currency and power source for an infernal war machine, linked to the Absolute’s broader plan.

Result: You secure massive magical capital (Soul Coins), blackmail material (Manifest), and the first hard evidence of the Absolute’s logistics chain.

24. **Deep research: Absolute Anatomy**

* Back at the Coiled Serpent, you spend a day and night studying the Manifest, infernal notes, and tadpole biology. You conclude:

  * these parasites are tuned for **remote control via hive mind**,
  * netherese shadow magic holds them in stasis,
  * and discordant psychic resonance (Dissonant Whispers, Idol’s hum) can disrupt the control channel.
