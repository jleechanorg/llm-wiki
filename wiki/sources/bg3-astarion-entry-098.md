The monster had fallen. The nightmare ship, a fiery ruin, plummeted towards the distant horizon, its unholy thrum finally fading into the raw, indifferent sounds of the wilderness. Astarion's skin still crawled with the phantom sensation of the transponder's teleportation, and the tadpole behind his eye squirmed with an insistent, chilling urgency. *Free,* he thought, a raw, desperate gasp of relief escaping his lips, quickly replaced by a grimace. This was not the refined escape he'd once dreamed of, but a fall into another, equally alien, hell.

"Lae'zel," he'd heard. "Gale." Utterly mundane, utterly predictable. And utterly irrelevant compared to the gaping maw of the wilderness now surrounding them. His initial assessment confirmed his worst fears: an overgrown cliffside, the ground uneven and treacherous, clinging to ancient pines that reeked of damp earth and something wild. The vast, crashing waves of the sea below offered no solace, only the chilling reminder of their desperate plight. His high neuroticism gnawed at him, screaming that the open air meant new, unknown dangers, and he had no desire to face them alone, especially not in such a primitive, *natural* setting. The city, with its predictable shadows and manageable vermin, felt like a distant, lost paradise.

"Splendid," Astarion drawled, his voice laced with a heavy dose of sarcasm as he turned from his briefly-named companions. "We've traded a flying brain-ship for... a particularly fragrant pile of dirt. No doubt teeming with even more unspeakable horrors." He didn't wait for their comments, his pragmatic mind already shifting gears. If they were to survive this undignified crash-landing, he needed supplies. The wilderness was an uncouth beast, but even it must yield resources. His crimson eyes, sharp with their own brand of predatory cunning, began to meticulously scan the immediate area around their rough landing spot. He wasn't looking for beauty; he was looking for usefulness: fresh water, edible plants, anything that might stave off starvation or, more importantly, infection. He crouched, prodding at a patch of unfamiliar moss, his fastidious nature battling with the desperate need to find *something*.
