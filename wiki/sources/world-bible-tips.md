# The World Bible — Managing Long Campaigns

The World Bible is your living summary of everything the AI needs to remember but will forget. It solves the core problem of AI RPGs: limited context window, unlimited narrative ambition.

---

## What Goes in the World Bible

**Current plot threads.** Active quests, mysteries being investigated, promises made to NPCs. Update at the end of every session.

**NPC master list.** Key NPCs, their allegiances, what they know, what they've done. Especially important for NPCs who have been manipulated, deceived, or whose trust has shifted.

**World state changes.** Cities that changed hands, wars that started, plagues that spread, kings that died. The AI needs to know what it didn't generate.

**Character status.** Companion loyalty levels, PC reputation changes, mechanical status (injuries, conditions, resource levels).

**Outstanding debts and promises.** "Lord Sarn owes us a favor from the negotiation in Session 3." These are plot gold — track them.

---

## How to Use It

**Session opening:** Before starting a new session, paste the relevant World Bible entries into your first prompt. "DM, recap: we're in the city of Valdris, Lord Sarn has been secretly funding the rebellion, and our companion Mira was revealed to be a spy for the Merchant's Guild last session."

**Mid-session correction:** If the AI generates something contradictory, reference the Bible. "Wait — Lord Sarn was confirmed dead in Session 4. This messenger claiming to be him can't be real."

**End of session update:** Write down what changed. New NPCs, shifted allegiances, completed quests, new mysteries. This is the single highest-leverage habit for long campaigns.

---

## Common Mistakes

**Not updating after sessions.** The Bible goes stale. Stale Bible is worse than no Bible — it gives you false confidence in AI consistency.

**Too detailed.** If your Bible is 5,000 words, you can't use it effectively. Keep each entry to 1-3 sentences. Link to longer notes if needed.

**Keeping it private.** Some AI RPG platforms share context with the AI through specific commands or modes. Learn how your platform handles context injection and use it.

**Forgetting the emotional texture.** Facts aren't enough. Note the tone of relationships, what's unspoken between characters, what everyone's afraid to admit. This is what makes the AI output feel consistent at a human level.

---

## The Bible and God Mode

Update your World Bible before and after using God Mode. God Mode changes world state — if you don't record those changes, the campaign engine doesn't know about them.

After a God Mode session: what changed? Update the Bible. What NPC moved? What event happened? What plan is now in motion?

This discipline — Bible discipline — is what separates campaigns that feel coherent across 200 entries from campaigns that feel like 200 separate one-shots.
