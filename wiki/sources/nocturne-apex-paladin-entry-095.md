Location: Man Tibbet's Farm, Outside Farmhouse, Early Evening

The small, silent pile of bundled forms, the Tibbet family and Widow Elara, lay at Nocturne's (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) feet. Six lives, transformed into silent commodities. The cool evening air was still, broken only by the distant chirping of crickets. Her gaze, sharp and analytical, swept the surrounding landscape. The farm's immediate perimeter was clear, its professional guards neutralized and concealed. But leaving such valuable 'stock' exposed, even for a short time, was an unacceptable risk. She needed a secure, temporary holding spot, somewhere deep and secluded, before proceeding with the larger game of power and influence.

Her mind, ever focused on ruthless efficiency, quickly identified a section of dense, overgrown forest bordering the far eastern edge of the farm, away from the main road and the barn's arcane ward. It was thick with ancient trees and thorny undergrowth, offering natural concealment. Perfect.

She began the laborious task of moving the captives. Each bound form, wrapped in its canvas sack, was a considerable weight, but her formidable physique made the process manageable. She returned to the Bag of Holding once, retrieving a few extra lengths of rope, which she used to lash the bundles together, three at a time. This would make them easier to drag and less likely to separate.

It was a grim, methodical process, a silent procession of bundles disappearing into the deepening gloom. She dragged them into the designated section of the woods, pushing them deep under the thickest bushes and arranging them amongst fallen logs and dense foliage, meticulously ensuring they were completely hidden from any casual glance, from ground or air. The faint, muffled sounds of breathing from within the sacks were quickly swallowed by the natural sounds of the night.

The task completed, Nocturne stepped back, her breath even, her Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate merging with the shadows. Six lives, now silent and hidden, awaited their fate. The immediate logistical challenge was met. Her EP was low, and her Channel Divinity remained expended, but the sense of meticulous control, of variables managed, was a cold satisfaction. The night's harvest was secured, for now. Her mind was already racing, planning the next, more significant moves in the intricate game of power that the acquired intelligence had revealed.
