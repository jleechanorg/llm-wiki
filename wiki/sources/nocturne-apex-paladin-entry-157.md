Location: House Valerius Estate, Lord Cassian's Private Study, Morning

The study air, usually a sanctuary of quiet authority, now thrummed with a palpable tension. Lord Cassian Valerius (Age 55) remained motionless behind his desk, his fingers a fraction tighter on the polished wood. His gaze, sharp and analytical, was a mirror of Nocturne's (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) own, betraying nothing but intense mental effort. He processed her words – "Shadowfell," "The Architect," "Convergence in three nights" – rapidly re-evaluating the geopolitical landscape of Baldur's Gate, the complex web of allegiances and rivalries he had so meticulously woven. The sheer audacity of House Durin's Folly, and the cosmic scale of their apparent treachery, were clearly profound.

Nocturne allowed the silence to stretch, a strategic weapon in itself. She met his unwavering gaze, her own features an unreadable mask. She offered no further words, no additional details, no hint of emotion. She was not begging for his attention; she was demanding his engagement. She knew his mind, understood his preference for directness, and anticipated his need for clarity amidst such shocking revelations. Her unyielding presence, devoid of any weakness or emotional appeal, compelled him. He might have exiled her, but he recognized true power, and she was demonstrating it now.

A muscle ticked imperceptibly in Lord Cassian's jaw. The silence had served its purpose. He broke first, his voice still low, but now laced with a chilling undertone of steel. "You speak of Shadowfell incursions and Architects. Unprecedented. If this is true, the implications are... vast." He paused, his gaze boring into hers. "You returned with a fragment. Where is the rest? What evidence do you possess, beyond your word, that substantiates such a claim?" His question was direct, demanding, stripped of all pleasantries. He was not asking for her loyalty, but for data. This was the language she understood.
