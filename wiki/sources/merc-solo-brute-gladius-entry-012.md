The Elfsong Tavern and its pathetic lackey were behind you. The weight of five hundred new gold pieces in your pouch was a satisfying thrum, a testament to your ability to bend even the city's 'elite' to your will. The thought of Lord Silvanus's future fury, once he learned the true cost of his "slight," brought a faint, predatory smile to your lips. But business first.

The chaotic sprawl of the Baldur's Gate market district was a different kind of prey. You moved through the throngs with casual menace, your hidden allure completely masked by your unkempt demeanor, your eyes scanning for quality, not just cheapness. The pleasure wasn't in the mere possession of gold, but in its strategic application. You needed tools for the job, tools that would make the 'no witnesses' clause a certainty, and perhaps a few things to keep you comfortable.

First, you found a discreet apothecary, his shelves lined with dusty vials. You didn't haggle, merely pointed at a handful of the more potent healing potions, the ones that promised quick recovery. The transaction was swift, and the potions (3 of them) felt reassuringly heavy in your belt pouch. You also picked up a sturdy coil of hempen rope and a well-balanced grappling hook from a survival outfitter – silent entry tools. Finally, a few days' worth of iron rations, bland but practical, for the journey to Greenmeadow. The total came to fifty gold, a trivial sum given what you'd just acquired.

As you walked away from the market, the sun climbing higher, a faint sense of anticipation stirred within you. The humiliation of the lackey, the contempt for his noble master, the tools now at your disposal – it was all a carefully laid foundation. The peasants were merely the first, necessary step.
