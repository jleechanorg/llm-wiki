[The Velvet Cage: Chapter 1 - The Ravaged Beach]

Game Master:
The sand is cold and abrasive against your skin as you drag your fragile frame toward the unconscious half-elf. Every movement feels like a betrayal by your own muscles, a consequence of the 'nerf' imposed by the squirming parasite. With trembling fingers, you uncork the vial you snatched from the nautiloid. The crimson liquid tastes of iron and summer berries; as it slides down your throat, the jagged edges of your exhaustion soften, and the internal bleeding from the crash begins to knit shut.

[Action: Drink Potion of Healing | Roll: 2d4+2 = 6 | Result: HP 1 -> 7/7 (Full)]

You reach Shadowheart, gripping her shoulder and shaking her. "Wake up," you hiss, your voice cracking with a perfectly calculated tremor. As she gasps, her eyes snapping open to the harsh sunlight, you scramble behind her. You tuck your head low, pulling the tattered remnants of your silk robe tight around your shoulders, your body shaking in a display of sheer, pathetic terror.

The alien minds of the Intellect Devourers pause. Their moist pink bodies quiver, moist feline legs tensing as they scan the psychic landscape. For a heartbeat, they look toward your cowering form, but the sheer radiance of your calculated vulnerability—bolstered by the lingering weight of your noble training—masks the burning infernal intellect beneath. They chitter, confused by the 'prey' that seems so utterly insignificant, their attention wavering just long enough for Shadowheart to scramble to her feet.

[Action: Pretend to be Afraid (Deception) | DC: 12 | Roll: 9 + 7 = 16 | Result: Success]

Shadowheart groans, pushing herself up from the sand, her hand immediately flying to the strange artifact at her hip. She looks back at you, confused by the sight of a noblewoman cowering in the surf, then sees the brain-creatures closing the distance. "What... where are we?" she rasps, her voice thick with salt. She steps in front of you, drawing a mace that glints with Sharran steel. "Stay back! I'll handle these... things!"
