You step past the hissing quench-buckets, your shadow falling across the iron-bound behemoth that is Barik. He does not turn; his world is restricted to the visor of his helm and the meticulous tension of the bronze plates that have become his skin. The four veterans Erenyos assigned to you stand several paces back, their posture shifting into a crisp, military salute as you arrive, but Barik remains hunched over his gauntlet, the rasp of a file against a burred edge the only sound of his focus.

"Barik," you state, your voice a flat, clinical vibration that cuts through the percussive rhythm of the forge. "The Iron Marshal has transferred your operational oversight to the Court. Specifically, to me. You are no longer a static guard on a failing perimeter. You are the structural anchor for a high-priority tactical unit."

At the sound of your voice, the metal man finally stills. The groan of his armor—a sound of stressed hinges and ancient leather—fills the silence as he slowly straightens. He towers over you, a mountain of bronze and iron seared by the very fires of the conquest, yet his movements are jerky, inhibited by the suit's rigid geometry. He turns his head, the dark slit of his visor meeting your gaze. 

"The Marshal's orders are absolute," Barik rumbles, his voice echoing within the hollow chamber of his chest-plate. It is a dry, metallic sound, devoid of heat. "If I am to walk in the shadow of a Fatebinder to resolve this stalemate, then my iron is yours. But do not expect the fluidity of your Scarlet shadow, Fatebinder. I am the Wall. I do not bend, and I do not 'recalibrate' for the sake of efficiency. I hold. Tell me, do you have a target that requires a wall, or are we simply rearranging the camp's furniture?"

You scan the four veterans behind him. They are 'Unbroken'—men who have survived the siege of the Bastard City and the horrors of the Oldwalls. Their armor is dull, utilitarian, and perfectly maintained. They watch you with a mixture of professional wariness and the deep, ingrained respect for anyone who can command their Marshal's concession. They are the secondary processors; Barik is the core.
