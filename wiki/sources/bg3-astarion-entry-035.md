With the second Mind Flayer a lifeless heap, its psionic hum abruptly silenced, Astarion didn't linger. The creature shrieked, a sound of alien alarm, its tentacles flailing as it tried to bring its formidable psychic powers to bear against two assailants who simply refused to be mentally dominated. It was wounded, bleeding black ichor, but far from defeated.

"Finish it!" Astarion snarled, his voice a low, guttural rasp, stripped of its usual charm and laced with a brutal satisfaction. He moved with the speed of a serpent, gliding past the pulsating conduits and fleshy growths, his jagged bone blade, slick with illithid blood, a chilling extension of his will. The air thrummed with raw psionic energy, a chaotic symphony of fear, rage, and the desperate struggle of the creature.

The Githyanki, her blade a blur of golden light and dark chitin, danced around the Mind Flayer, striking at its exposed limbs while the burly man, a roaring, unstoppable force, battered its chitinous shell. Astarion found his opening, a precise, swift thrust, aiming for the vulnerable underbelly where its massive head met its body. He felt a perverse thrill, the hunter's instinct in full bloom, overriding the lingering dread of the tadpole. This was his world now, a world of survival by tooth and claw, and he would master it.
