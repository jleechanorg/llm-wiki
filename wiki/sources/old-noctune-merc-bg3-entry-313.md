**Target Gamma: Lord Caldwell.** You spot him near the refreshments, his face flushed, hands trembling as he reaches for a goblet of wine. He is a man eroding from the inside, his eyes darting around the room, looking for creditors in the shadows. He does not know that the creditor is walking straight towards him.

You intercept him with a fluid grace, steering him deftly towards a secluded alcove draped in heavy silk. He stiffens, spilling a drop of wine.

"My Lord Caldwell," you purr, your voice a low, intimate hum that vibrates in his chest. "A pleasure. I believe we have mutual... interests."

"I... I don't believe we've met," he stammers, trying to step back.

You weave the magic of *Charm Person*, a subtle thread of enchantment meant to bind his will to yours. But Caldwell is a creature of paranoia. He stiffens, his hand flying to a heavy iron amulet beneath his tunic—a ward against mental intrusion. He blinks, shaking his head, the spell sliding off his psyche.

"What... what was that?" he hisses, backing away. "Sorcery? You dare?"

It is a moment that would end a lesser operative. You do not flinch. You simply step closer, invading his space, and drop the pretense of magic for the crushing weight of reality.

"I dared to offer you a gentle way out, Caldwell," you whisper, your voice turning to ice. You produce a small, folded parchment from your sleeve—a copy of his latest promissory note, acquired by the Shadow Bank. "But if you prefer the truth... here it is."

He looks at the paper. His eyes bulge. The indignation vanishes, replaced by the hollow terror of a man realizing he is already dead.

"I own your debt," you state, your violet eyes boring into his. "I own the markers you signed at the Wyrm's Crossing. I own the loans you took to cover your losses at the racetrack. I own *you*."

"Five thousand gold pieces," you whisper. "A sum that would see you stripped of your title and your lands. Unless..."

"Unless?" he chokes out.

"Unless you demonstrate that House Caldwell is a friend to the new order. Tonight, when the toasts begin, you will stand. You will raise your glass. And you will declare that House Caldwell stands with the Regent Lyra. You will urge the Parliament to grant her emergency powers to deal with the 'Moonrise Threat'."

Caldwell trembles, sweat beading on his brow. He looks at the debt marker, then at the ballroom. "A... a toast?"

"A ringing endorsement," you correct him. "Do this, and the interest on your debt pauses for a month. Refuse... and I foreclose by morning."

He nods, a jerky, broken motion. "Yes. Yes, of course. Lyra. The Regent. I... I will do it."

You step back, allowing him to pass. He stumbles back into the crowd, calling for wine, preparing to sell his soul for your silence. One pillar of the Parliament has fallen.
