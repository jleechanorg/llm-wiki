Game Master:
Serpent Queen’s smile remained fixed, a mask of serene command amidst the high stakes of the Guildhall. The alliance was struck, but the architecture of its execution required one final, precise draft. She moved to the large map of Baldur’s Gate spread across Keene’s desk, her finger tracing two distinct lines through the city’s districts.

"We proceed on two fronts, Guildmaster," she began, her voice cool and authoritative, the *Idol’s* resonance lending weight to every syllable. "The **Patriar Path** and the **Zhentarim Path**."

She tapped the Upper City. "The Patriar Path is a war of whispers. I return to Lyra. I guide her through the wreckage of her father's ruin. I position her as the savior of House Umbrage, with the Guild as her silent benefactor. We do not seize the house; we *inherit* it. This grants us the political legitimacy to shield our operations from the Flaming Fist and the Parliament."

Her finger slid down to the Grey Harbor. "The Zhentarim Path is a war of knives. We do not coexist; we exterminate. We begin tomorrow night with the 'Wide Arc' shipment. We intercept it, claim their goods, and leave no survivors. We frame it as internal Zhentarim incompetence, fueled by Rathmar's 'misdirection'. We dismantle them piece by piece, using their own confusion against them."

She looked up, meeting Keene's gaze. "To execute the Zhentarim purge while I manage the delicate politics of the Upper City, I cannot work alone. I require a specialized team. Not thugs, but professionals. Infiltrators and enforcers who answer to me, for the duration of this campaign. A scalpel to cut out the rot."

Keene’s eyes narrowed slightly at the request for command over Guild assets. The silence stretched, taut as a bowstring. Serpent Queen did not flinch, but she knew the moment required a counterbalance—a gesture that proved she was building the Guild, not just her own empire.

She turned slightly, gesturing to Arvax, who stood stiffly by the door, waiting for the hammer to fall. 

"And as for the oversight of this operation..." Serpent Queen said, her voice softening with deliberate respect. "None of this—the Umbrage acquisition, the blinding of the Zhentarim, the very concept of the Shadow Monarch—would have been possible without the exceptional foresight of Lieutenant Arvax. He identified the potential where others saw only risk. He brokered the initial entry. He is the architect of this opportunity."

She looked back to Keene. "He fulfills the 'Kingmaker' role perfectly. I propose he acts as my direct liaison and second-in-command for this joint venture. His loyalty to the Guild is absolute, and his understanding of the operational realities is... indispensable."

Arvax let out a breath he seemed to have been holding for ten minutes. His chest swelled, the terror in his eyes replaced by a blinding rush of pride and relief. He had been shielded, and now, he was being crowned.

Nine-Fingers looked from Serpent Queen to the beaming Arvax. A dry, knowing amusement curled her lip. She saw the manipulation, the shielding, the brilliant distribution of glory. And she approved.

"You play the game well, 'Shadow Monarch'," Keene said, her voice low. "Arvax gets the credit, you get the power, and I get the city. Very well."

She pulled a heavy iron key from her desk and tossed it to Serpent Queen. "You have your team. The 'Black Lotus' squad—four of my best night-blades. They are currently stationed in the Undercellar. Show them this key, and they are yours. Use them to break the Zhentarim. But remember... if you break my toys, you pay for them."

She leaned back, dismissing them with a wave. "Go. Reshape my city."

As they exited the office, Arvax was practically vibrating. "You... you actually did it," he whispered, looking at Serpent Queen with something bordering on religious adoration. "The Black Lotus... and you gave me the credit..."

"We are partners, Arvax," Serpent Queen said, her voice smooth as silk. "The Kingmaker and the Queen. Now... let us go to work."
