The Githyanki and the burly man stood ready, their eyes on him, waiting for the next command, their impatience a palpable thing in the blood-soaked air.

His mind, sharp and calculating even through the phantom squirm of the tadpole behind his eye, raced. This was a moment of precarious advantage. The immediate threats were neutralized, but the grand escape remained. He needed a clear path, and more importantly, he needed *control*. The parasite... a morbid trophy, or something more?
