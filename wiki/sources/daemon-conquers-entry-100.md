Daemon (Level 6, Age 30) listened to Kael's (Level 5, Age 25) assessment, his gaze sharp. "The Shadowed Approach," he rumbled, his voice low. "Can we first send scouts to *find* such a path? Not a full force, but men to confirm if a weakness exists, before we commit?"

Kael nodded swiftly, eagerly. "Yes, my Lord. That is indeed the first step. The scouts who just returned, or even others from our veteran ranks, could focus solely on finding such an entry. It would increase the success rate of that option significantly, from the initial estimate of 50% to perhaps 80-90% for finding a viable breach, if one truly exists. It would remove much of the risk from the initial strike force."

Daemon then turned to the other plan. "The Grinding Siege. Do we have any siege equipment? Rams? Ladders? Catapults?"

Kael hesitated for a fraction of a second, a flicker of apprehension. "No, my Lord," he admitted, his voice a shade softer. "Our rapid conquests have been through terror and direct force. We have no dedicated siege engines. We could improvise ladders, perhaps, from timber in the surrounding woods, but building a ram or catapults would require time – weeks, perhaps even a month or two – and resources. Skilled laborers, iron, wood… it would be slow, and it would announce our intentions to all nearby lords."

The hall fell silent again, save for the distant, rhythmic grunts from the training yard. Kael had given him more to consider.
