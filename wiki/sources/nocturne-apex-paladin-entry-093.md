Location: Man Tibbet's Farm, Farmhouse Interior, Early Evening

The farmhouse was utterly silent, a tableau of unconscious, bound forms amidst the comforting domesticity of a roaring hearth. The Tibbet family lay inert, secured and silent, ready for transport. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) stood amidst her 'stock', her movements economical, her gaze sharp and analytical. Her EP was low, her Channel Divinity expended, but her HP remained full. The primary phase of acquisition was complete. The intel on House Durin's Folly, a strategic outpost and Shadowfell operations, pulsed in her mind, a far greater prize than the gold or the captives. But the captives were a commodity, and a secured commodity was a fulfilled contract.

She moved with a chilling efficiency, her focus solely on the task of extraction. Five bodies, ranging from the burly farmer to the slight form of the youngest child, presented a logistical challenge. Her Bag of Holding, while capable of swallowing the entire family, would be conspicuous if she were to deposit them directly. Manual transport was required for the initial leg. Her formidable physique was up to the task.

She started with Man Tibbet, slinging the heavy, bundled form over her shoulder. His weight was considerable, but her strength made it manageable. She moved towards the back door of the farmhouse, the path she had used for entry. Each step was calculated, silent, ensuring no new sounds alerted the outside world. The silent farm was a temporary haven, and she needed to move these assets to a more secure, discreet location.

Once outside, in the deepening twilight, she lowered Tibbet to the ground beside the root cellar, where the second guard's body lay concealed. She returned for the wife, then the three children, carrying them out one by one, a methodical, grim procession. Each bundle was placed carefully on the damp earth, silent and still. It was a tedious process, demanding patience and physical exertion, but it was necessary to maintain the integrity of the 'stock'.

The air outside was cool and still, carrying the scent of damp earth and distant livestock. The farmhouse remained quiet, its windows glowing faintly, a false beacon of peaceful domesticity. The arcane ward near the barn pulsed faintly, a silent, useless sentinel. The presence of the Tibbet family, now a small, inert pile of bundled forms near the root cellar, presented a new, albeit temporary, vulnerability. They were outside, exposed, awaiting the next stage of the operation. She needed a more secure location for them before considering further action.
