Aurelius stands at the edge of the firelight, his soot-smeared armor catching the dying embers. As Spartacus offers him the title of 'General of the Spires,' the young Caesar does not puff his chest or radiate the heat of his Julian line. Instead, his shoulders slump, his amber eyes casting downward toward the damp limestone floor. He looks not like a titan of the ridges, but like a boy caught in a draft he wasn't prepared for.

"General?" Aurelius whispers, his voice thin and cracking with an uncertainty that feels alien in this room. "Spartacus... I am sixteen. I have spent my life in libraries and courtyards, or following the orders of men far wiser than I. What I did on the Spires... it wasn't leadership. It was... it was just a mistake that happened to work."

He glances at Crixus, then at the Doctore, his hands fidgeting with the silver wire on his gladius hilt. "You are giants. You have led men through the pits and against the lions. I just want to stay quiet, to help where I can, to maybe... keep the watch or tend the fires. I am not worthy to stand beside you as an equal, let alone lead. Please, give the rank to someone who knows the weight of a life. I am just... Aurelius."

The silence that follows is absolute, punctuated only by the rhythmic dripping of the cave. Gannicus pauses with his flask halfway to his lips, his smirk frozen in a look of sheer, incredulous bafflement. Crixus blinks, his massive jaw hanging slightly open as he looks at the warrior who broke a century with a word and is now essentially asking to be a camp-hand. Oenomaus's brow furrows so deeply his eyes nearly vanish, while Spartacus simply stares, his dual blades catching the firelight as he tries to reconcile the 'Sun' he saw on the peaks with the stuttering teenager standing before him. The modesty is so extreme, so completely at odds with the carnage still fresh on his blade, that it borders on the surreal.
