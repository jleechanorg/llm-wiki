Your land for me is very off way more than 50k acres Great Britain as a whole is about 51.7 million acres, and the Roman Empire at its maximum size was roughly 1.24 billion acres.[1][2][3][4][5][6][7]

### Britain in acres

- Great Britain’s area is about 209,331 square kilometers (80,823 square miles).[2][8]
- Using 1 square kilometer ≈ 247.1 acres, this equals about \(209{,}331 \times 247.1 \approx 51.7\) million acres.[4][6][7]

### Roman Empire (whole and “northern part”)

- At its greatest extent (under Trajan, 117 CE), the Roman Empire covered about 5 million square kilometers (around 1.93 million square miles).[3][5][9][1]
- In acres, 5 million square kilometers ≈ \(5{,}000{,}000 \times 247.1 \approx 1.24\) billion acres.[6][7][4]
- If by the “northern part” you mean the European half (from Britain across Gaul and the Danube frontier), that is not given as a separate number in sources; a rough rule of thumb is that Europe made up well under half of the total imperial area, so on the order of a few hundred million acres, but this is an estimate rather than a precise measured figure.[5][9][1][3]
