The lackey's hurried explanation of the target families and the bribed sheriff was exactly the kind of detail Gladius required, but the ease with which it came also bred suspicion. "Borin is 'amenable'," you repeated, your voice flat, a low growl of disbelief. You didn't take the purses yet; they remained on the table, a test. "And 'taken care of.' How exactly? Does he have a taste for blood or coin? And how do I confirm this... amenability?"

You leaned forward, your shadow falling over the lackey's nervous face. Your eyes, usually veiled, now pinned him, demanding absolute honesty. The subtle vein in your neck began to pulse again, a tiny, almost imperceptible tremor that, to the terrified man, likely felt like a seismic shift. Gladius didn't tolerate loose ends, especially not ones that could lead to unexpected interference. He wanted concrete assurances, not vague promises from a man who clearly held no real power. The idea of any official with a spine, no matter how small their jurisdiction, challenging his work was an irritation he preferred to nip in the bud.

The lackey stammered, pulling at his collar. "He... he has a taste for coin, sir. A good deal of it. Lord Silvanus's envoy, a man named Corvan, met with him just yesterday. He's at the Rusty Flagon, a small inn on the outskirts of Greenmeadow itself. Corvan will verify the arrangement. He expects you, as 'Lord Silvanus's discreet agent.' Just mention the Lord's 'unfortunate incident with the granary request,' and Corvan will understand." He was practically sweating now, desperate to satisfy your demand for control.
