Astarion's heightened senses, now thrumming with renewed vitality, registered the faint sound of Lae'zel and Gale's footsteps receding into the smoky distance. *Fools. So easily managed.* A grim satisfaction touched his lips, quickly wiped away by the lingering taste of blood – sweet, metallic, utterly intoxicating. The gnawing hunger, a constant companion for two centuries, was finally, gloriously, sated. The tadpole behind his eye, usually a cold, insistent squirm, felt strangely quiescent, lulled by the influx of fresh life. He felt strong, his muscles humming, his mind clearer than it had been since waking on that monstrous ship.

But the moment of indulgence was over. The crumpled form of the farmer lay at his feet, unconscious, a faint but steady pulse in his throat. Astarion's high neuroticism flared; the evidence of his "necessary measure" was far too blatant. "A messy business, isn't it?" he muttered to the still form, a flicker of his usual fastidiousness returning despite the lingering warmth in his belly. He couldn't leave him exposed. Not with Lae'zel's moralistic fury and Gale's dramatic sensibilities waiting to erupt upon their return. His pragmatic mind, sharp and cold, assessed the scorched clearing.

He moved with swift, predatory grace, his Gloom Stalker instincts guiding him. The ground here was a chaotic mess of splintered trees and churned earth. He spotted a shallow crater, likely from a smaller piece of Nautiloid debris, partially obscured by the broken trunk of a charred pine. It wasn't perfect, but it would do. With a grunt of effort, he dragged the unconscious farmer into the depression, wincing at the grimy contact. He then swiftly covered him with loose soil, charred branches, and a thin layer of debris, camouflaging the makeshift grave. It wouldn't hold up to a thorough search, but it would be enough to escape a casual glance. The scent of fresh blood, thankfully, was masked by the acrid tang of burnt metal and pine. He stood back, surveying his work with a critical eye, then wiped his hands on his ruined trousers, a grim satisfaction settling in his chest.

The clearing was silent once more, save for the distant, dying groans of the Nautiloid. He was stronger now, ready for whatever fresh horror awaited. And his companions remained blissfully unaware of his true nature, for now.
