The impact rattled Astarion's bones, but the sudden, raw relief of solid ground beneath his feet was intoxicating. He pushed himself upright, his gaze sweeping the overgrown cliffside. Pines, gnarled and ancient, clung to rocky outcrops, their needles releasing a damp, earthy scent that filled his lungs – a stark contrast to the acrid stench of the Nautiloid. Below, the colossal alien vessel was a fiery ruin, a black smudge against the distant dawn sky, its unholy thrumming finally silenced. They were free. A genuine, if fleeting, smile touched his lips, quickly replaced by a grimace as the tadpole behind his eye gave a chilling, insistent squirm. Freedom, yes, but at what cost? And with whom?

His eyes, sharp with their own brand of calculating pragmatism, snapped to his companions. The Githyanki, a warrior carved from cold steel, pushed herself to her feet with a grunt of pain, her golden eyes already scanning the unfamiliar landscape with fierce, unyielding intensity. A deep gash on her arm still oozed slowly, but she seemed to ignore it. The burly man, meanwhile, sat up with a pained groan, clutching his head, his broad face a mask of exhaustion and lingering shock. His labored breathing was a stark contrast to his usual roaring ferocity.

"Well, that was quite the spectacle, wasn't it?" Astarion drawled, his voice a little shaky, but quickly regaining its sardonic lilt. He strode towards the burly man first, a flicker of pragmatic concern overriding his usual aloofness. "Are you quite finished with your theatrics, brute? We survived, apparently. Though I'm not entirely certain *how*. And you," he turned his gaze to the Githyanki, a subtle nod of grim respect in his eyes. "You seem to attract more wounds than answers. Can you still fight, or are you quite done with the heroics?" He needed them functional. He needed them *now*. His high neuroticism gnawed at him, screaming that the open air meant new, unknown dangers, and he had no desire to face them alone.

"Before we decide whether to *actually* cooperate, or simply watch each other die in a ditch," Astarion continued, a thin, impatient edge to his voice, "perhaps we could address the glaring omission in our introductions. I am Astarion Ancunín. And you two?" He raised a sardonic brow, his crimson eyes fixing first on the Githyanki, then the burly man, awaiting their response with a mixture of disdain and desperate curiosity.
