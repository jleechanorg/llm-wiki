Location: Widow Elara's Cottage, Interior, Early Evening

The cottage air, thick with woodsmoke and the scent of quiet domesticity, was now permeated by a subtle chill. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) held Widow Elara, the old woman's frail body trembling in her grasp, a silent testament to sheer terror. The humming had ceased, replaced by faint, ragged gasps against Nocturne's palm. The initial acquisition was complete, executed with the chilling precision of a predator.

With her other hand, Nocturne reached into her **Bag of Holding**, retrieving the pre-prepared restraints: thick, oiled leather straps and a canvas sack, large enough to contain the slight form of the widow. Her movements were fluid and efficient, each action a calculated step in a meticulously planned sequence. Her conscientiousness demanded order, even in abduction.

She lowered the whimpering woman to the floor, her expression utterly devoid of emotion as she swiftly bound Elara's wrists and ankles. The straps were tight enough to prevent escape, but not so tight as to cause immediate injury – unnecessary damage was inefficient. The sack was then pulled over the old woman's head, muffling any further sounds, obscuring her terrified gaze from the world. The process was swift, silent, and without complication. The 'stock' was now secured.

Nocturne's gaze then swept the small, single room. Her Apex Senses had provided a basic layout, but now her keen intellect (Intelligence 18) and investigative skills sought tangible details. Her purpose was not wanton destruction or petty theft, but an efficient sweep for anything of value that could augment her wealth or provide unexpected utility. The cottage was humble, almost spartan, yet a hidden gem might still exist, a forgotten family heirloom.

She moved methodically, her eyes missing nothing. The small wooden table held a half-eaten bowl of gruel and a simple, hand-carved wooden spoon. A worn bible lay on a bedside table, its pages yellowed with age, a cheap iron cross hanging above it. In the pantry, a few meager stores of dried meat, root vegetables, and a small barrel of ale. Nothing of immediate value.

Then, behind a loose stone in the hearth, a glint. Nocturne's fingers, quick and precise, probed the crevice. They closed around a small, leather-bound journal and a tarnished silver locket. The locket was old, its silver dull, bearing a faded, almost indistinguishable engraving of a stylized flower. The journal felt more promising. Its cover was worn, but the pages were filled with a spidery, precise script, unlike the rough hands of a farmer. This suggested a past perhaps more complex than her current humble existence implied. A fleeting thought of the road patrol resurfaced; perhaps this tied into *their* presence. The locket she dismissed as sentimental, but the journal could hold information. Knowledge was power, and therefore, wealth. She tucked the journal and locket into her Bag of Holding.

The cottage was now silent once more, save for the crackle of the hearth and the muffled, shuddering breaths from the secured bundle on the floor. Nocturne stood over her captive, her face unreadable. The next phase of the operation awaited.
