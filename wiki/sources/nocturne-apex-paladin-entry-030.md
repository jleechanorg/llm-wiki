Location: Outskirts of Oakhaven, Near the Barn, Early Evening

The soft thuds of the fallen mastiffs were swallowed by the encroaching silence, but the echoes of their barks had already traveled. The distant calls grew louder, punctuated by the crunch of hurried footsteps on the dry earth. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) felt the cold, familiar thrill of anticipation. The stealth portion of her infiltration had been compromised, but the direct approach offered its own brutal elegance. She would not hide; she would dominate.

She stepped deliberately from the concealing shadows of the copse, revealing herself in the dim twilight. Her Fine Plate Armor, though no longer magically enhancing her stealth, lent her an imposing silhouette. Her Noble's Garb billowed slightly around her, obscuring her physical form but doing nothing to diminish the chilling aura of her presence. The Masterwork Longsword was still in her hand, its polished surface catching the last glimmers of light.

Three figures burst through the tree line, led by a burly man gripping a pitchfork like a spear, flanked by a younger woman holding a lantern and an older, gaunt man with a rusty axe. They froze, eyes wide, as they spotted the two unmoving forms of the mastiffs, then swept up to land on Nocturne. A gasp escaped the woman, the lantern light trembling in her hand. Fear, raw and unrefined, blossomed on their faces.

Nocturne's gaze, sharp and analytical (Intelligence 18), swept over them. Farmers. Unarmed, or poorly armed, and clearly terrified. Perfect. Her Aura of Unyielding Dominance (Level 5 class feature) began to subtly emanate, a silent pressure designed to crush resistance, making her appear impossibly large, impossibly dangerous. Her low Agreeableness (1/5) meant there would be no softening of her approach.

"Silence," Nocturne's voice cut through the air, a low, even tone that carried an almost unnatural weight. "The dogs made too much noise. You will answer my questions, and you will do so swiftly and truthfully. Fail, and you will join them." She gestured with the tip of her longsword towards the fallen beasts, the implication stark.

The burly man swallowed hard, his grip on the pitchfork whitening. The woman whimpered, pulling back. The older man's eyes darted wildly, searching for an escape.

The burly man's knees buckled, and the pitchfork clattered to the ground. "Wh-what do you want?" he stammered, his eyes fixed on her. The woman, near tears, huddled behind him. Even the older man, despite his trembling resolve, seemed utterly broken. They were utterly subjugated by her presence.

"Oakhaven," Nocturne stated, her voice devoid of inflection. "Tell me its layout. Any defenses, any Watch? Where are the largest families? The most isolated homes? Speak."

The man, under the chilling pressure of her aura, began to babble, his words tumbling out in a desperate torrent. He described the hamlet's single winding path, the clusters of cottages, the few older, larger farmhouses on the outskirts. He spoke of old Man Tibbet's place, almost a mile north, with a large family and no close neighbors, and Widow Elara's cottage to the west, secluded by a small stream. He confirmed there was no organized 'Watch,' just a few older men with rusty tools for self-defense, easily overwhelmed. His information was fragmented but invaluable, confirming the hamlet's vulnerability and highlighting potential targets.

With the information extracted, the villagers stood frozen, awaiting her next command. The immediate crisis was averted, and valuable intelligence gained. The efficiency of the operation was, for now, back on track.
