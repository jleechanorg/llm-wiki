You drift through the gala like a shark in a koi pond. The *Glamoured Studded Leather* is a masterpiece of deception, flowing around you in waves of midnight silk that whisper of foreign courts and dangerous secrets. The Upper City's elite part before you, their eyes drawn to the *Canaith Mandolin* on your back and the confident, predatory grace of your movement.

Your target is **Lord Caldwell**. You spot him near the pazaak tables, his face flushed, hands trembling as he reaches for a goblet of wine. He is a man trying to drown a mountain of debt in a river of vintage red.

You intercept him. A subtle gesture, a touch on the elbow, and you guide him away from the noise, steering him towards a secluded alcove draped in heavy velvet curtains. The air here is cooler, quieter.

"Lord Caldwell," you purr, your voice laced with the *Echoes of the Idol*. "You look like a man who is betting on a losing hand."

You weave the magic of *Charm Person*, a subtle thread of enchantment meant to bind his will to yours. 

But Caldwell is a creature of paranoia. He stiffens, his eyes clearing for a fraction of a second as the magic washes over him. He blinks, shaking his head. "What... what was that? A hex? You dare?"

[Wisdom Save: 18 (Success vs DC 17) - He resists!]

The spell fails to take hold. He opens his mouth to shout, to call the guards. It is a moment that would end a lesser operative. 

You do not flinch. You simply step closer, invading his space, and drop the pretense of magic for the crushing weight of reality.

"I dared to offer you a gentle way out, Caldwell," you whisper, your voice turning to ice. You produce a small, folded parchment from your sleeve—a copy of his latest promissory note, acquired by the Shadow Bank. "But if you prefer the truth... here it is."

He looks at the paper. His eyes bulge. The indignation vanishes, replaced by the hollow terror of a man realizing he is already dead.

"I own your debt," you state, your violet eyes boring into his. "I own the markers you signed at the Wyrm's Crossing. I own the loans you took to cover your losses at the racetrack. I own *you*."

[Intimidation/Persuasion Check: 26 (Success)]

"You can leave this alcove a ruined man, exposed to your peers and stripped of your title," you continue, smoothing the lapel of his jacket. "Or... you can walk out there and toast the Regent. You can tell them that House Umbrage is the future. You can tell them that Lyra is the only thing holding the Upper City together."

Caldwell trembles, sweat beading on his brow. He looks at the debt marker, then at the ballroom. "A... a toast?"

"A ringing endorsement," you correct him. "Do this, and the interest on your debt pauses for a month. Refuse, and I foreclose before the dessert is served."

He nods, a jerky, broken motion. "Yes. Yes, of course. Lyra. The Regent. I... I will do it."

You step back, allowing him to pass. He stumbles out of the alcove, calling for silence. The room turns. Lord Caldwell, a pillar of the old guard, raises his glass. His voice shakes, but the words are clear.

"To... to Lady Lyra!" he calls out. "The savior of our traditions! The strength we need in these dark times! House Caldwell stands with Umbrage!"

The room erupts in polite applause. Lyra, standing on the dais, catches your eye. She smiles, a look of pure, triumphant adoration. The first domino has fallen.
