Astarion's meticulous eye had done its work. The wilderness, for all its uncouth wildness, had grudgingly yielded something useful. He wasted no time in idle chatter. "Don't just stand there," he murmured, his voice laced with his usual sardonic edge, though his expression was grimly focused. He knelt amongst the gnarled roots of the ancient pine, the scent of damp earth and pine needles prickling his nostrils. His slender fingers, typically accustomed to more delicate pursuits, carefully extracted the cluster of **earthroot**. He brushed away the clinging soil with a grimace, the thought of its mild restorative properties overriding his innate fastidiousness. It was a crude remedy, but a remedy nonetheless.

Then, he moved towards the weeping fissure, its clear water a small, inviting gleam in the pale light. He produced his waterskin from his pack, its leather surprisingly intact after their violent escape. The trickling sound of the water against the moss-covered stones was almost soothing amidst the pervasive thrum of the tadpole behind his eye. He filled the waterskin with quick, efficient movements, every instinct screaming at him to prepare for the inevitable unknown.

Lae'zel watched his actions with an unreadable expression, her golden eyes fixed on the raw, unkempt cliffside, ever vigilant for a threat. She offered no help, but no hindrance either, her warrior's pragmatism aligning with his own. Gale, however, let out a soft sigh, running a hand through his perpetually disheveled hair. "Hardly the five-star accommodations I'm accustomed to," he grumbled, his gaze sweeping the wild landscape with a hint of scholarly disdain. Astarion merely scoffed. *As if any of us are in a position to complain.* He secured the resources, a small measure of control in a chaotic, terrifying reality. The fresh water was cool and surprisingly clean against his tongue, a fleeting moment of normalcy in the heart of the absurd.
