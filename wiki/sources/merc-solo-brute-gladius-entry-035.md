The cold, calculating clarity Gladius sought had arrived. The distant, fading glow of the burning shed was a faint memory now, the true darkness within the Miller farmhouse his domain. The whimpering child, still half-asleep, barely stirred as Gladius moved. The Silent Strangler's Cord was a whisper in his hand, a thing of purpose and finality.

He knelt by the first pallet, his shadow engulfing the small form. There was no hesitation, no flicker of emotion in his eyes, only the grim, surgical focus of a predator. The cord slid over the child's neck, thin and precise. A single, swift, powerful pull, a silent gasp, a momentary struggle that barely disturbed the straw. The whimpers ceased. The small body went limp. He held it for a moment, ensuring absolute stillness, then gently lowered it back onto the pallet, the face now slack, eyes closed. No sound, no struggle. A clean kill.

He rose and moved to the second pallet. The air, thick with the scent of herbs, seemed to hold its breath. This child, older, stirred more purposefully as Gladius approached. A soft murmur, a sleepy shift. Before a sound could fully form, before consciousness could truly grasp the terror, the cord was in place. The process was identical: swift, silent, efficient. The brief, desperate flutter of life against his will, then the sudden, complete cessation. He felt the subtle shudder, the last warmth drain, and then the utter stillness.

Gladius straightened, the cord still coiled in his hand. Two souls. Done. The room was silent once more, broken only by the rhythmic, deeper breathing from the adjacent room—the parents, still oblivious. A grim satisfaction settled in Gladius's gut. The "no witnesses" clause was being honored with chilling perfection. The pleasure wasn't in the kill itself, but in the absolute control, the effortless erasure of lives, the demonstration of his own unyielding will. He had sealed the house, and now he had silenced its youngest occupants. The rest would follow.
