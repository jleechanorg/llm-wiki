Detailed Explanation of Gear & DPP Economy:

In the 'Century of Silence' campaign, your progression is governed by three distinct economic systems. You cannot simply 'buy' high-tier gear with Gold because the Red Wizards of Thay maintain a monopoly on power; gold is for the mundane, while your Sovereign's favor is for the exceptional.

**1. The Gold Economy (Market Gear):**
- **Usage:** Used in the markets of Bezantur for common items, rations, and basic services.
- **Constraint:** Red Wizards do not sell 'Sovereign-tier' artifacts to mercenaries for gold. You currently have 500gp, enough for mundane upgrades or basic scrolls, but not for the artifacts you crave.

**2. The DPP Economy (Mission Assets):**
- **Usage:** Dominion Power Points (DPP) represent your operational budget from Nocturne. They are spent in the 'Prep Phase' (where you are now) to requisition specialized Thayan assets or squad upgrades.
- **Current Options:** You have 5 DPP. You can spend them on the 'Requisition Menu' items (Sappers, Red Wizard Adepts, Intel, or Squad Masterwork Gear). 
- **Constraint:** DPP is used for *requisitions*, not permanent personal gear. It buys the tools to complete the mission successfully.

**3. The Soul Thief Economy (True Power):**
- **Usage:** This is your primary method for 'buying' better personal gear and abilities. 
- **The Trophy System:** When you use Soul Thief on a 'Named NPC' or 'Elite' target (like the Shadow Monk Kaelen), you absorb a **Fragment** of their essence. This is how you permanently acquire new feats, abilities, and unique traits. 
- **The Mint:** You can choose to 'Mint' souls into Soul Coins (1 Soul = 1 DPP) to increase your permanent requisition power.

**Summary:** To get 'better gear' like the Shadow Step ability or magical enchantments, you must find high-value targets in the field and harvest them. Your 5 DPP are for mission-specific assets to ensure those harvests are successful.
