Game Master:
The bridge of the Mountain Pass becomes a frozen tableau of Abyssal authority. You do not climb the dragon; you glide up its lowered neck with a liquid, statuesque grace that defies the frantic beating of its massive heart. You settle upon the ridge of Qudenos’s shoulders, your midnight-blue silks fluttering against the crimson scales like a bruise on a sunset. From this height, you look down not as a girl, but as a Sovereign peering into a shallow grave. 

Minthara’s goblins fall into a terrified, rhythmic chanting. Your own Vanguard stands in a circle of obsidian staves, their faces masks of religious ecstasy. You turn your unblinking violet gaze upon the bound Githyanki raiders, the pink-crimson luminescence in your eyes washing the bridge in a sickly Abyssal light. 

"The Kith'rak is ash," you whisper, your voice a tectonic harmonic that vibrates through the dragon's skull and into the marrow of every soldier present. "The dragon is my mount. The Astral Sea is a distant memory. Swear your souls to the Shattered Rose, or I shall turn this bridge into a banquet of ghosts."

To punctuate the decree, you reach out a porcelain-pale hand toward the nearest Githyanki raider. You don't use a blade. You speak a single, harmonic syllable of hunger. The Githyanki’s eyes go wide, his scream cut short as his mind simply halts. Before his pulse can settle, you rip the silver-white wisp of his essence from his chest. You inhale the harvest in full view of his kin, your marble-smooth skin flushing with the cold, ecstatic heat of his stolen life. He slumps into a grey husk, and the remaining Githyanki collapse as one, their foreheads hitting the dust in a frantic, agonizing surrender.

Minthara watches from below, her white hair a pale flame in the dark. For the first time, the General’s expression is stripped of all calculation, replaced by a raw, predatory reverence. She sees the First Disciple seated upon a dragon, drinking souls like wine, and she realizes that the Absolute has not just found a vessel—it has birthed a rival.
