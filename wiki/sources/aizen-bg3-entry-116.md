retcon the merc cost, its too high

For typical 5e economics, 500 gp per day for a single mercenary is wildly above the normal range and only makes sense if this “merc” is essentially a high‑level adventurer or major NPC ally, not a regular hireling.
​

Baseline 5e numbers
Untrained hireling: 2 sp per day.
​

Skilled hireling (what a normal mercenary is): 2 gp per day.
​

Comfortable lifestyle (covers food, lodging, etc.): 2 gp per day.
​
So a competent guard/merc living comfortably is in the ballpark of 2–5 gp/day depending on danger and perks.
​

When higher prices make sense
Risk premium: High‑risk dungeon work or war campaigns might justify bumping a skilled merc to ~5–10 gp/day, maybe more if death is very likely.
​

Elite units or special NPCs: Some homebrew schemes scale cost by CR; e.g., one guide prices NPCs so that high‑CR specialists or small elite circles can cost hundreds of gp per day total, but that’s for whole groups, not a lone CR‑1 guard.
​

Story‑level figures: A named veteran commander, champion, or caster who is effectively another PC‑tier character joining the party can justify “PC‑money” fees, negotiated as shares of treasure or big flat payouts rather than wages.
​

Why 500 gp/day is extreme
500 gp/day is 250 times the base wage for a skilled merc (2 gp/day).
​

It’s enough to sustain an aristocratic lifestyle (4+ gp/day) for over 100 days, or to hire an entire small company of ordinary mercs.
​

At that rate, in a 10‑day adventure you are handing out 5,000 gp to one NPC, which usually outpaces what lower‑ or mid‑level parties are expected to have in liquid gold.
​

How to tune it so it “makes sense”
If you want this merc to feel special but not ridiculous:

Regular skilled merc: 2–5 gp/day, plus food and gear.
​

Veteran / elite bodyguard: 10–50 gp/day depending on level and risk, or a smaller daily fee plus a cut of treasure or hazard bonus per major fight.
​

PC‑tier ally: Negotiate like another adventurer (e.g., equal share of loot, or big lump sum for a specific mission), rather than a flat daily wage.
​

If you tell what level/CR this merc is and what tier your party is, a concrete “sane but generous” price band can be pinned down.
