Lord Cassian Valerius (Age 55) stood before Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16), his initial shock now a cold, determined fury. "My network... my resources... they are yours. You will manage this. You will lead the counter-operation. No Julian. No interference. Report directly to me." His eyes, sharp and intelligent, held a dangerous glint, a mirror of her own ruthless pragmatism. "The honor of House Valerius, and perhaps the fate of Baldur's Gate, rests on your shoulders, youngest daughter."

Nocturne felt a cold, deep satisfaction. The words, the authority, the implicit acknowledgment of her superiority – it was precisely what she had engineered. Her father, the calculating patriarch who had exiled her, now handed her the reins of power. The irony was palpable, a subtle amusement that only she would appreciate.

***

**Nocturne's Internal Monologue & Planning:**

*The path is clear. Father has granted me direct authority, bypassing Julian. The resources of House Valerius are now at my disposal. This crisis, this profound betrayal by Durin's Folly, is my crucible. But it is also a direct ascent to the power I crave.*

*My mother. A ghost in the Valerius halls, a forgotten whisper in the grand narrative of this House. Her identity, a closely guarded secret of Father's, was never a matter of sentiment for me, but of data. She was merely the vessel through which I entered this world, a means to an end. Julian's petty manipulations, the 'exposure' of my evil nature leading to my exile, were tied to the public perception of my existence. Her absence, her lack of official standing, made it easier for him to cast me out, to sever my ties.*

*Yet, Father's subtle acknowledgement, the "youngest daughter" remark, always carried a peculiar weight. It was a concession, a hint of something beyond mere political calculus. What was her power? What was her influence, if not publicly recognized? Did she possess a hidden talent, a secret network, a lineage that Father sought to protect, or perhaps control? Was her bloodline the source of my own unique aptitude, my chilling intellect, my detachment from conventional morality?*

*Could she be an untapped resource? A hidden ally in the intricate game of power I now play within House Valerius? If she is alive, and possesses a unique utility, her existence could be leveraged. Information is power. Does she hold knowledge of the House's ancient secrets, its hidden pathways, or perhaps even its vulnerabilities? Could she have insight into Father's methods, his true motivations, beyond his calculating exterior? Or is she merely a liability, a weakness to be exploited by rivals?*

*The immediate objective is the Architect and the Convergence. My mother is a variable. A potential asset, or a lingering loose end. For now, her utility is unknown, her influence speculative. My focus must remain on the immediate threat to consolidate my new authority. But her potential... a thread to be pulled, perhaps, when this crisis is averted. Every piece of the Valerius puzzle must eventually be understood, and controlled.*

***

Nocturne's gaze remained fixed on her father, acknowledging his words with a faint, almost imperceptible nod. The conversation had yielded precisely the results she sought. The game was truly hers to play.
