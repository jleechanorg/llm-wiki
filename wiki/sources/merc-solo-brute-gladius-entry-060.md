The splintering crash from the shed, Gladius’s deliberate diversion, had echoed precisely as intended. He watched from the cover of the hedgerow as the burly Stonehand man and his wife lumbered towards the noise, their faces etched with sleepy confusion and burgeoning alarm. Their simple curiosity was a lever, and Gladius had just pulled it. A cold, precise satisfaction settled in his gut; the raw, immediate pleasure of manipulating others, even these simpletons, was a potent surge.

With the Stonehand couple preoccupied, their backs to their home, Gladius moved. His Shadow-Step Boots, already proving their worth, silenced the heavy clang of his splint mail to a mere rustle against the dew-soaked grass. He crossed the short distance to the dwelling with unnerving speed, his large frame melting into the deeper shadows clinging to the stone walls. The faint, persistent scent of wood smoke and something savory, like cooking oats, drifted from the open door – a domestic intimacy about to be violated.

The sturdy wooden door, left ajar in their haste, was an open invitation. Gladius slipped through the threshold like a ghost, landing silently on the worn stone floor within. The interior was dim, the flickering light of a single hearth illuminating a common room that smelled of hard work, simple meals, and deep slumber. A child's small, wooden toy lay abandoned on the floor, a testament to innocent lives soon to be irrevocably shattered.

Gladius paused, absorbing the profound silence that had fallen over the house, broken only by the crackle of the hearth fire and the muffled, distant voices of the distracted Stonehands. The thrill was a subtle hum beneath his skin, the exquisite sensation of unchallenged entry, of taking possession of their most private space. He felt no malice, only the cold, unyielding pleasure of his power, manifested in the perfect execution of his plan. Their home was now his domain, their lives entirely at his mercy. The sun, a faint promise on the eastern horizon, would soon illuminate the full extent of his chilling work.
