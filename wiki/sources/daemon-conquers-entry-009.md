Daemon felt no fear, only a cold anticipation. He tightened his grip on the hilt of his heavy greatsword, the familiar weight a comfort against the unknown depths. He was not a fool; he knew a young wyrm could still tear him limb from limb. But he was also Daemon, and his will was an unyielding force. Stonehaven would fall, one way or another, and the dragon would be its instrument, or its grave. He would make an example, and the lands would know a new, harsher master.
