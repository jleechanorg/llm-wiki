The subtle shift in the study's dim light marked the new morning. You sat up, body fully restored, mind humming with a terrifying, renewed clarity. The long rest had replenished your reserves, and the hunger for absolute control, for the exquisite precision of unmaking, burned bright within you. Your companions still slept, exhausted from the intensity of the previous day, but the threads of your complex plans were already weaving themselves.

Your gaze swept the study. Kantos was slumped over his copying instruments, fast asleep, a half-copied page of archaic script clutched in his ink-stained hand. Rowan was curled on a mat beside Kaelan, a gentle presence amidst the profound silence of the Sergeant's peace. Gideon remained by the door, a silent, unmoving sentinel, his posture still rigid even in sleep. Cressida, her sharp eyes gleaming faintly, was awake, reviewing the data-slate filled with observations of your training.

The thought of acquiring a personal guard from Titus, of binding more of the Starfall Guard to your will, brought a cold satisfaction. But that was a long-term play, already set in motion. The immediate, tactical imperative was the security of the terrifying knowledge now residing in these journals and in Kaelan's mind.

You rose, moving with a silent, graceful efficiency. You approached Kantos, gently nudging his shoulder. The old Magister stirred, blinking owlishly, his ancient eyes widening in fear and recognition. "Princess?" he rasped, fumbling with his spectacles.

"Magister," you commanded, your voice low and precise, "the copying. Its progress. Its security. Detail it."

Kantos, though weary, immediately began to comply. He pointed to the half-copied pages, explaining how he had begun "semantic partitioning," breaking down the Null Genesis Project's chilling history into conceptual fragments. He showed you several blank scrolls, subtly prepared with hidden runes for "disguised carriers," ready to embed fragments of the truth into seemingly innocuous Imperial records – a trade manifest here, a historical annotation there. The meticulousness of his deception, his scholar's dedication to preserving truth by transforming it into a thousand lies, was chillingly impressive.

You moved then to Gideon, who stirred, his masked face turning to you, his loyalty a silent, unyielding presence. He would oversee the ultimate erasure of the originals. He would ensure no trace remained. His rigid discipline, re-ordered by your will, was a perfect instrument for this final act of unmaking.

A profound, quiet satisfaction unfurled within you. The core of your power, this 'chilling capacity for unmaking and control,' resonated with every successful step of your plan. The methodical dismantling of old orders, the precise imposition of your will – it was exquisite. You were not merely securing knowledge; you were perfecting the art of control.
