**World Background:** Assiah is a world defined by the Thousand Year War, a relentless struggle between the Celestial Imperium and the Unchained Host. The Imperium, led by the ancient Lord Regent Raziel, champions absolute order through divine bloodlines. Opposing them is the Host, a meritocracy of liberated slaves and revolutionaries led by Lucifer, the Morning Star, who seeks to shatter the divine hierarchy at any cost.

**Character History:** You are Alexiel, the 'daughter' of Lucifer—a biological construct designed to be the ultimate weapon of the rebellion. To the world, you are a 'Null,' devoid of magic but possessed of peerless physical prowess. This is your first and greatest lie. Beneath the mask of the Host's greatest champion, you hide a blossoming sorcerous power and a strategic mind that views the war as a complex mathematical equation.

**Current Context:** The campaign begins at the pivotal moment of your life: the Sacking of Silverwood. You have just led your legions to a decisive victory over the Imperial garrison, but the cost has revealed a flaw in your father's philosophy that you can no longer ignore. As the smoke of the burning town chokes the air, your path to defection begins.

--- THE ASHES OF SILVERWOOD ---

The silence of Silverwood is more offensive than the screams that preceded it. The air is thick with the copper tang of blood and the acrid stench of magical discharge. You stand in the center of the town square, the 'Prodigal Sword' mask firmly in place—cold, efficient, and untouchable. Around you, the soldiers of the Unchained Host are no longer an army; they have devolved into a frenzied mob, celebrating victory with the indiscriminate slaughter of those who could not fight back.

You look down. At your boots lies a wooden horse, its pine surface scorched. Beside it, a small blanket covers a shape far too small to be a combatant. The 'mathematics of slaughter' no longer balance. This wasn't a tactical necessity; it was an act of pure, chaotic malice. 

Lieutenant Gorok, a massive orc dripping with gore, stomps through the haze toward you. He grins, his yellow tusks bared in a hideous expression of pride. "A clean sweep, Commander! No witnesses, no orphans. The Morning Star will be pleased with our efficiency."
