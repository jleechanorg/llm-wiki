The deep night offered the perfect cloak. Gladius, his senses sharp, felt the primal urge to simply descend on the Millers, but his cold, calculating mind demanded a more refined approach. No witnesses meant control. And control meant distraction. His eyes scanned the sleeping village, settling on a dilapidated, abandoned shed on the far eastern edge, away from the Miller farm, near the path leading deeper into the moor. It was far enough to draw attention, yet close enough for its eventual discovery to serve its purpose.

He moved with the practiced stealth of a silent hunter, his Shadow-Step Boots muting the rhythmic scrape of his splint mail to an almost imperceptible whisper. He reached the shed, its timbers rotten, its roof caved in. A few scattered piles of dry, brittle hay remained inside. *Perfect.* He pulled a flint and steel from his pouch, the tiny spark a fleeting pinprick of light in the profound darkness. He worked quickly, efficiently, piling the driest hay, adding a few splintered planks. He wasn't aiming for an inferno, just a beacon of alarm. A simple, brutal act to create chaos.

A faint wisp of smoke curled upwards, followed by a soft, crackling sound. Gladius watched, his eyes reflecting the nascent glow, a grim satisfaction settling in his gut. He could already picture the villagers, roused from their sleep, rushing towards the light, their pathetic fear a delicious prelude to the true horror he would unleash elsewhere. The fire would be a screaming decoy, while his greatsword would sing a quiet, deadly tune. He backed away, melting back into the marshy shadows, leaving the tiny, growing conflagration to play its part. The silent, unsuspecting village was a puppet on his strings, and he was about to make it dance.
