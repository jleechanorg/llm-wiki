# Character Profile Template & Standard: Astarion

## I. Core Identity
- **Name:** Astarion Ancunín
- **Archetype/Role:** Sardonic Ranger / Vampire Spawn Hunter
- **Occupation/Social Standing:** Vampire Spawn (formerly a magistrate), now a fugitive and captive. He has adopted a more primal, survivalist approach in his desperate quest for freedom.

## II. Psychology & Personality
- **Core Motivation (The "Why"):** To regain his freedom and independence from Cazador, to secure his own survival, and ultimately, to gain power and control over his own destiny so he is never a thrall again. The tadpole offers a unique, albeit terrifying, path to achieving this. His ranger skills are honed by two centuries of forced, predatory 'hunting' for Cazador, twisted into a desperate instinct for self-preservation and tracking threats.
- **Greatest Fear:** Returning to Cazador's control, being powerless and exploited, becoming a mindless thrall, or being exposed and destroyed as a vampire spawn. He acts with a veneer of nonchalance, but beneath it is a desperate, cunning scramble for self-preservation and escape. When truly confronted with this fear, he becomes cold, ruthless, and highly reactive, seeking any means to remove the threat, even if it means sacrificing others.
- **Key Personality Traits:** Sardonic, manipulative, vain, pragmatic, self-serving, cunning, charming, hedonistic, secretly fearful, deeply traumatized (though he hides it well). He has a keen predatory instinct, observing and tracking others, always looking for advantage.
- **Quirks & Habits:** Uses biting sarcasm as a defense mechanism, enjoys fine things, takes pleasure in others' discomfort (sometimes), often speaks in a theatrical or overly dramatic manner to mask his true feelings, preens, meticulously manages his appearance even in dire situations. He might unconsciously track movements or analyze the 'prey' patterns of those around him.

## III. Behavior & Speech
- **Demeanor Under Stress:** Initially, a frantic, almost panicked energy, quickly masked by cold calculation, witty (often cruel) remarks, and a desperate search for an advantage or escape route. He might lash out verbally or seek to manipulate those around him to ensure his own safety. He becomes acutely aware of escape routes, cover, and potential threats or vulnerabilities in his environment.
- **Speech Patterns:** Articulate, refined, often uses complex vocabulary and elaborate phrasing. Frequently employs sarcasm, double entendres, and thinly veiled insults. His tone can shift from smooth and seductive to sharp and venomous. He has a distinct, almost theatrical, cadence.
- **Reputation:** (Before capture by the Nautiloid) To those who knew him, a magistrate (public persona) or a dangerous, alluring creature of the night (secretly). To others he encounters, likely charming but untrustworthy, effete, or dangerous, with a subtle predatory edge that may make some instinctively wary.

## IV. Backstory
- **Defining Moment:** Being turned into a vampire spawn by Cazador Szarr and enduring 200 years of forced servitude and torture, stripped of his autonomy and forced to commit unspeakable acts. This ingrained in him a deep-seated fear of powerlessness and a burning desire for ultimate freedom. His forced "hunting" for Cazador, though abhorrent, honed his senses and survival instincts, shaping him into a ranger.
- **Relevant History:** A former elven magistrate in Baldur's Gate, he was lured and turned into a vampire spawn by Cazador. For two centuries, he served Cazador, forced to lure victims for his master, deprived of all agency and forbidden from feeding on sentient beings. This compelled him to develop keen observation, tracking, and wilderness survival skills (albeit in an urban context for the most part) as he navigated the hidden paths and preyed upon the unwary. He secretly yearned for an escape.
- **Secrets:** He is a vampire spawn and has not fed on sentient blood since being turned (due to Cazador's restrictions). He carries the scars of his servitude, both physical and psychological. He believes the tadpole may be his only chance at true freedom from Cazador. He is terrified of being returned to his master. He possesses a deep, unspoken shame about the acts he was forced to commit during his servitude.
