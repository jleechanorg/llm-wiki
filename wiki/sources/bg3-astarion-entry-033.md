Astarion's eyes, sharp with predatory calculation, flick from the reeling, wounded Mind Flayer before him to the third, untouched illithid across the chamber. His internal monologue, a cold symphony of survival, screamed at him: *The fresh threat must be dealt with first. Engage the most dangerous one, and let the others finish off the cripple if they must.* His voice, usually laced with a theatrical flourish, was now a low, urgent snarl, stripped bare of any pretense. "You two! The one by the exit! Now!" He pointed his jagged bone blade, a crude but effective extension of his will, at the untouched Mind Flayer. "Pin it down. Don't let it think!"

The Githyanki, already a coiled spring of violence, responded instantly. With a guttural battle-cry that echoed strangely in the fleshy chamber, she launched herself across the pulsating floor, her makeshift weapon held high. Her golden eyes, normally piercing and cold, now blazed with a focused, almost ecstatic fury as she zeroed in on the designated Mind Flayer. She moved with a speed that belied her heavily muscled frame, her intent brutally clear.

The burly man, meanwhile, let out another savage roar that threatened to tear his throat, his face a mask of raw, unbridled rage. He didn't question, didn't hesitate. His powerful legs drove him forward, a charging bull with a chitinous blade, thundering towards the third Mind Flayer. His raw strength alone seemed to vibrate through the deck, a palpable force.

The third Mind Flayer, whose tentacles had just begun to unfurl with malevolent intent, was caught completely off guard by this sudden, concerted assault from two fresh combatants. Its psionic hum, which had been building to a terrifying crescendo, wavered, then spiked in pure, alien alarm as the Githyanki's blade sliced across its arm, drawing black ichor, and the burly man's crude weapon slammed against its carapace with a sickening crack. It reeled, its focus shattered by the unexpected, brutal physicality of the attack. Its multi-faceted eyes, now filled with something akin to confusion and a dawning, terrible anger, flickered between its two new assailants and then, with chilling recognition, to Astarion, who stood poised, watching the chaos he had unleashed.
