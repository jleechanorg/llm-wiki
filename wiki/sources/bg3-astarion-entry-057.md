Astarion's jaw tightened, a thin line of vexation. The chamber was empty of new secrets, a frustrating dead end for his meticulous search. He turned from the pulsating consoles, his gaze sweeping over the dizzying, impossible panorama of the cosmic void outside the massive viewport. It was beautiful, in a terrifying, indifferent way, but its beauty offered no solace, no escape. His companions, meanwhile, remained fixed on the spectacle, seemingly unburdened by the constant gnawing dread that pulsed behind Astarion's own eye. The recent victory over the Mind Flayer larvae offered only a temporary reprieve; the ship still hurtled through space, and the tadpole still writhed, a chilling, insistent reminder of their impending doom.

"Right," Astarion drawled, his voice edged with a forced lightness that barely concealed his growing impatience. He strode towards the Githyanki and the burly man, who still stood transfixed by the swirling galaxies. "Unless either of you are planning to hitch a ride on a passing comet, I suggest we focus on the immediate, rather than the infinite. We've cleared this... delightful nursery. Now, where to?" He gestured vaguely around the vast, alien chamber. "Any profound insights, my dear warrior, into the purpose of this cosmic goldfish bowl? Or are we simply to assume the next 'door' leads to more of the same?" He raised a sardonic brow at the Githyanki, whose golden eyes finally tore themselves from the vista, fixing on him with an almost palpable irritation.

"This is an observation deck," the Githyanki rasped, her voice a low, guttural growl, her impatience a barely contained fire. "They observe. They prepare. The path is forward." She pointed a clawed finger towards the glowing tunnel they had just emerged from, then swept it towards the far side of the chamber, where another, still-sealed organic passage could be faintly discerned. "This vessel seeks a destination. We must find its helm, or its end." Her logic was brutal, direct, and utterly devoid of Astarion's preferred subtlety.

The burly man grunted in agreement, his attention now also snapped back to the immediate surroundings. He looked at the sealed passage. "Aye. No point gawkin' at stars when there's ground to walk on. Or... fleshy tunnels. Let's find the way out." His face was grim, but determined. Astarion suppressed a sigh. It seemed the path forward would be, as always, through sheer, unrefined momentum.
