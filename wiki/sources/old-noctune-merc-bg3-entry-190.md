Game Master:
You slip through the garden shadows, the heavy velvet of the night your only cloak. The estate is in turmoil, a quiet panic vibrating through the stone walls. The discovery of the 'Total Acquisition'—the empty safe, the missing bonds—has struck. Lord Umbrage is not raging; he is silent, a man whose foundation has simply vanished.

You find **Lyra** in the solarium. She is not weeping. She is pacing, her face pale, her hands clutching a silk shawl as if it were armor. When you step from the darkness, she gasps, rushing to you not as a conspirator, but as a drowning woman seeing a raft.

"It's gone," she whispers, her voice trembling. "Everything. The safe... the deeds... he says we are ruined. He's in his study, drinking the last of the vintage. He won't even look at me."

"He is looking at his past, Lyra," you soothe, taking her cold hands in yours. "And he sees only ash. But you... you must look to the future."

You guide her to the bench, the moonlight casting long shadows across the floor. You reach into your pack, bypassing the stolen deeds (which remain hidden) and withdrawing a heavy, velvet pouch filled with platinum and gold—a portion of the very wealth you stripped from the house, now repurposed as salvation.

"The 'ruin' was inevitable," you lie smoothly, the words tasting of sweet venom. "Your father's mismanagement invited predators. But I... I have found you a shield."

You place the heavy pouch in her hands. The weight of it shocks her.

"What is this?" she asks, wide-eyed.

"Investment," you declare. "I have spoken to my associates. A private consortium—discreet, powerful, and interested in stability. They are willing to restructure the House's debt. They are willing to back *you*."

You lean in, your violet eyes locking with hers. "This gold stabilizes the accounts for the month. It buys the silence of the servants and the patience of the creditors. But it comes with a condition. The consortium does not trust your father. They trust *you*."

"Me?" Lyra breathes, looking from the gold to you.

"You must be the signatory," you command softly. "You must be the Regent. Go to him now. Show him the gold. Tell him you have found a solution where he found only despair. Tell him he can retire to the country with his dignity intact, or he can stay and face the debtors' prison alone. He is weak, Lyra. He will take the exit."

[Persuasion check: 1d20+14 = 15+14 = 29 (Success)]

Lyra straightens, her fingers tightening around the gold. The fear in her eyes hardens into something colder, something regal. She stands, the transformation you orchestrated finally taking hold. She is no longer the daughter; she is the Matriarch.

"He will listen," she vows, her voice steady. "He has no choice."

She turns and walks toward the study, the gold heavy in her arms, to depose her father with the very coin you stole from him.
