The weight of the new greatsword on Gladius’s back was a comforting presence, a promise of brutal efficiency. The silent strangler's cord and Shadow-Step Boots, tucked away, were surgical instruments for the 'no witnesses' clause. He stood amidst the fading light of the city, the chaos of the market giving way to the growing quiet of dusk. The mission was clear: Greenmeadow, nine souls, no interference.

His mind, cold and analytical, turned to the means of approach. *Stealth.* The word itself tasted bitter. He was a hammer, a battering ram, not a shadow. Yet, the client had specified "no witnesses," and that meant getting close unseen, ensuring no one raised an alarm before the work was done. He ran a hand over the scuffed plates of his splint mail. It was good armor, AC 18, a solid shell that shrugged off blows. But it was loud. Every step a metallic rasp, every shift a groan of steel.

*Should I wear more stealthy armor?*

He pictured himself without the mail, relying solely on leather and muscle. *Naked.* The thought was repulsive, a flicker of the old fear of vulnerability. His raw physique was formidable, his coordination swift, but relying only on that for defense felt like a surrender of control, a foolish gamble. The armor was him, an extension of his unyielding strength. Besides, he didn't *have* any other armor, not really. What was a flimsy leather jerkin against a pitchfork, or a lucky shot?

He tightened the straps of his gauntlets, the leather creaking. *The boots.* He remembered the half-elf's words: "walk among ghosts." He had paid a hundred gold for them, a sum that should guarantee some effect. Could they mute the clang of his mail? Enough, perhaps? He wasn’t a shadow, no, but he didn't need to be. Just quiet enough to get close, to surprise. Once the first blow fell, it would be a swift, brutal ending. His confidence surged; his current gear was *enough*. He always made it enough. The true power was his own will.
