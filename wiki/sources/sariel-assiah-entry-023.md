Kantos remained frozen, his eyes wide and unblinking behind the thick lenses of his spectacles. The only sound in the vast scriptorium was the frantic hammering of your own heart. He was an archivist who had seen everything, but this secret, this name, had shattered his composure. His ink-stained fingers, still clutched around the tome, trembled visibly. The fear radiating from him was palpable, a cold wave in the still air.

You saw his terror, and a cold, sharp resolve solidified in your chest. This was it. This was the thread. "The 'Starfall Patient'," you repeated, your voice a low, insistent whisper, each word a hammer blow against his shattered composure. "The sole survivor of the Starfall Gambit. The one Raziel hid away in the 'Quiet Ward.' What did he see, Magister? What did *she* do? What secret did my mother carry that was so terrible it broke his mind and had to be erased from history?" You stepped closer, your eyes boring into his, a desperate hunger for truth burning in your Starlit Gaze. "Tell me everything. Now."

Kantos finally broke his rigid stillness. A dry, rasping gasp escaped his lips, and he shook his head slowly, frantically. "No, Princess," he whispered, his voice thin and desperate. "You do not understand. That knowledge... it is a poison. It is meant to be buried. Forever." He looked around the scriptorium, his eyes darting to the towering shelves of forbidden texts as if they might physically collapse upon him for speaking their names. The weight of centuries of enforced silence seemed to crush him. He feared the truth more than any physical threat, because the truth could unravel his entire carefully constructed world.
