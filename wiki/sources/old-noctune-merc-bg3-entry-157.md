You choose the path of the poison, not the blade. To rob Lord Umbrage is to injure him; to turn his daughter is to unmake him.

You slip through the Western Service Entrance, the aged lock yielding silently to your touch. The estate is quiet, the servants' quarters dim. You move through the shadows of the hallways, a ghost in the house of a dying dynasty, until you reach the soft glow of the solarium.

**Lyra** is there, pacing amongst the night-blooming jasmine. She turns as you enter, her eyes widening not in fear, but in desperate relief. She sees the Shadow Monarch, her confidante, her guide.

"You returned," she breathes. "The house... it feels like a tomb tonight. My father is in his study, drinking away his 'burdens'."

"The burden is about to be lifted, Lyra," you say, stepping into the light. "But not by him. By you."

You take her hands. "Rathmar has blinded the Zhentarim. The eyes on this house are gone. But they will return, and when they do, they will expect weakness. We must show them a new strength."

You guide her towards the door. "Your father is losing his grip. His debts, his 'blindness'... it is a rot that will consume this house unless you cut it out. You must seize the reins, Lyra. Not tomorrow. Now."

"Seize... the reins?" she falters.

"The finances," you whisper, your voice hard and compelling. "The deeds. The bonds. If they remain in his safe, they will be squandered or stolen. If *you* hold them... you hold the power. You become the Patriar in all but name. You save the legacy by taking it from him."

The hesitation in her eyes shatters, replaced by a cold, crystalline resolve. It is the look of a woman realizing the cage door was never locked.

"He keeps the key... around his neck," she says, her voice steady. "He is drunk. He won't feel a thing."

"Then let us go," you say. "And secure your kingdom."

Together, you move to Lord Umbrage's study. The old man is slumped over his desk, snoring softly, a goblet of spilled wine pooling near his hand. Lyra moves with a ghost's grace—a talent she perhaps learned from you. She lifts the key chain from his neck.

The safe clicks open. 

Lyra pulls out the heavy bags of gold, the wax-sealed deeds, the bearer bonds. She looks at them, then at her sleeping father, and finally at you. There is no guilt in her face, only the terrifying clarity of survival.

"It is done," she whispers, handing the heavy satchel of deeds to you, keeping the gold for the household's 'immediate restructuring'. "Hold these safe for me. Until the transition is complete."

Lord Umbrage sleeps on, a pauper in his own chair. His daughter stands over him, the new mistress of the house. And you... you hold the papers that own them both.
