Baldur's Gate, in the year 1492 DR, teeters on the brink. The recent fall of Elturel has flooded the Outer City with a tide of desperate refugees, creating squalid shantytowns and stretching the Flaming Fist's authority to a brutal, arbitrary limit. Within this chaos, the city's criminal Guild sees opportunity, and darker whispers of a cult known as "The Absolute" are dismissed by the Patriars while disappearances continue. This volatile landscape is a predator's dream, a distraction for the unwary, and a canvas for those with the intellect to manipulate its forces.

You are Nocturne, youngest bastard daughter of a powerful, unnamed noble house. A prodigy in combat and intellect, your very nature—a cold, philosophical evil, coupled with a sadistic, ritualistic hunger for murder—was deemed a catastrophic threat to your family's reputation. Exiled under the guise of banishment, your father, a pragmatist to his core, subtly ensured your escape was well-provisioned. You carry his hidden signet ring, a finely balanced masterwork rapier, enchanted +1 studded leather armor, and a significant purse. Your goals are absolute: amass portable power, gather wealth, and, inevitably, feed the gnawing Hunger that defines your existence.

Your story begins now, in the shadow-drenched underbelly of Baldur's Gate, where you operate as a mercenary. You seek out contracts that others deem too distasteful or risky, seeing them as calculated steps towards your ultimate goal of personal transcendence and surgical domination. This is a time of immense opportunity, where the suffering of many can be meticulously reordered to serve the will of one.

***

The air in the Smirking Boar's cellar is a cloying blend of sour ale, mildew, and the river's briny tang. Water drips a steady cadence, marking the passage of time as muffled laughter from above serves as a stark counterpoint to the grim business at hand. Across the rickety table, "Smiley" Arvax, a Guild lieutenant, outlines your first contract.

His scarred face, perpetually locked in a rictus grin that never quite reaches his dead eyes, leans forward. "It's simple, 'Noc.' That's what you call yourself, right? The market's hot for new stock. The Zhents are busy with their own... politics... and the slavers from the south are paying top coin for anything that can work a field or warm a bed. They don't care about the paperwork."

He shoves a greasy pouch containing a 50-gold-piece down payment across the table. "The job is an acquisition. A family of tiefling refugees in the Rivington shantytown. Just arrived from Elturel, no connections, no one to miss 'em. A mother, a father, and their two brats. Easy."

He slides a crude map of the shantytown, a specific tent circled in red, toward you. "They're... different," Arvax adds, a sneer momentarily replacing his usual grimace. "Asmodeus-spawn. Some folks think they're bad luck. I think they'll fetch a bonus from a specialized collector. Someone who... appreciates... their 'heritage.' You understand. The kind of client who pays double for 'exotic stock' and doesn't ask questions."

The contract is clear: a simple kidnapping. Four souls for a criminal syndicate. The two adults are, of course, viable targets for your Hunger.

Arvax leans back, observing you, mistaking you for a quiet, grimy urchin. "Do this clean, and there's more. A lot more. The Guild is... expanding... its interests. We need talent. We need people who see the whole board, not just their next meal. The city is full of... complications. Complications create opportunities. Kidnapping is just the start. The Zhents are getting soft, focused on their little shadow war. We're in the growth sector. Enslavement, assassination... acquisitions. Big coin for those who aren't squeamish."

You are the Serpent-King, the philosopher-queen in waiting. Your quiet, musical voice, when you choose to use it, cuts through the damp air.
