With the jagged bone blade clutched in his hand, its alien hum a bizarre comfort, Astarion’s gaze sweeps across the other captives. He wasn’t looking for allies, not truly, but for assets. Each writhing form, each face contorted in agony or mindless despair, was a potential distraction, a meat shield, or a surprising hidden talent. His cynical nature, sharpened by two centuries of servitude, allowed no room for sentimentality here; only utility mattered.

The pale, sallow man chained nearest to him was weeping, utterly broken. Useless. Another, a hulking figure, thrashed violently, but his movements were uncoordinated, fueled by pure terror. A liability. Astarion’s lip curled. The Githyanki woman, however, was a different story. She hadn't whimpered once. Her golden eyes tracked his assessment, and he caught a fleeting expression that might have been... impatience. She was clearly of a warrior caste, disciplined and dangerous.

"Any other useful toys out there, or just more fodder for the Flayers?" Astarion mutters, not quite to the Githyanki, but letting the words hang in the heavy air. His own neuroticism hums beneath his composure, a constant reminder of the tadpole behind his eye, the very real threat of transformation. He needed a cleaner exit than a frontal assault. He tried to ignore the subtle shift in the ship's groaning, a barely perceptible change in pitch that spoke of accelerating towards a grim destination.
