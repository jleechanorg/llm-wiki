The sickening lurch of the transponder, the blinding flash of blue-white light, and then the gut-wrenching sensation of being torn apart and reassembled. Astarion hit the ground hard, the impact jolting every bone in his body, the air knocked from his lungs. He lay there for a moment, blinking, the stench of ozone and burnt demon-flesh fading, replaced by the damp, earthy scent of pine needles and wet soil. Above him, the monstrous Nautiloid, a broken, flaming ruin, careened towards the distant horizon, a fiery comet crashing into the earth, its unholy thrum finally dying away. They were free. *Free.* A raw, desperate gasp of relief escaped his lips, quickly followed by a grimace as the tadpole behind his eye gave a chilling, insistent squirm. The nightmare was far from over.

He pushed himself up, his muscles protesting but his body miraculously intact. His eyes, cold and calculating, swept his immediate surroundings, then snapped to his companions, scattered and disoriented like discarded toys. The Githyanki landed with a pained grunt, quickly pushing herself to her feet, her golden eyes already scanning the unfamiliar landscape with fierce, unyielding intensity, though a deep gash on her arm still oozed slowly. The burly man, meanwhile, let out a loud groan, clutched at his head, and pushed himself up onto an elbow, his face a mask of exhaustion and lingering shock. His labored breathing was a stark contrast to his usual roaring ferocity. They were alive, but clearly wounded and disoriented.

"Well, that was quite the spectacle, wasn't it?" Astarion drawled, his voice a little shaky, but quickly regaining its sardonic lilt. He strode towards the burly man first, a flicker of pragmatic concern overriding his usual aloofness. "Are you quite finished with your theatrics, brute? We survived, apparently. Though I'm not entirely certain *how*. And you," he turned his gaze to the Githyanki, a subtle nod of grim respect in his eyes. "You seem to attract more wounds than answers. Can you still fight, or are you quite done with the heroics?" He needed them functional. He needed them *now*. His high neuroticism gnawed at him, screaming that the open air meant new, unknown dangers.
