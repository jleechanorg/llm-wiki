Kagain takes a long drag from his pipe, the smoke curling around his horns. He looks at the gold, then at his axe, and finally at you. He grunts, a sound like shifting tectonic plates.

"The Fist is bad for business," he admits, scooping the gold into a drawer. "And my caravan contracts have dried up since the iron started snapping like kindling. A man could rot in this shop waiting for the world to fix itself."

He stands, grabbing the massive battleaxe. "Nashkel, eh? Kobolds and demons. Sounds messy. I like messy. I want twenty-five percent of the bounty, and first pick of any heavy armor we find. And if we die, I'm taking the gold off your corpse."

"Agreed," you say.

Kagain kicks his chair aside. "Then you've got yourself a shield. Now..." He points a thick finger at Garrick, who is trembling in the corner. "What are we doing with the squealer? If he talks to the Fist, we won't make it to the city limits."
