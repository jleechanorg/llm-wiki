This complication may manifest as a new obstacle, a partial failure of a secondary objective, the emergence of an unexpected consequence, or the appearance of a rival force, regardless of the success of the immediate action roll or plan. This rule does not dictate outright failure of the primary goal but ensures the path is never entirely frictionless. The GM will not explicitly announce when this check is made or its outcome; the narrative will simply reflect the emerging complication."

**Understanding & Execution:**
You are instructing me to refine and clarify the "Daemon's Fickle Fate Protocol" ([Change ID: 20240529-I]) to make its application more precise. This revised wording emphasizes that the 20% chance applies to *significant, high-stake, player-initiated actions or long-term missions*, and that the outcome is a *complication, setback, or rival interference*, rather than outright failure of the primary goal. It also reiterates that the check and its outcome are hidden within the narrative.

This is an amendment to an existing GM Protocol, so I will follow the **Finalization Protocol**.

**3. Differential Draft & Justification:**

**Differential Report - Change ID: 20240530-B (Amendment to 20240529-I)**

*   **Source Text (Current "Daemon's Fickle Fate Protocol" as per [Change ID: 20240529-I]):**
    *   **New Protocol Addition: "Daemon's Fickle Fate Protocol"**
        *   **Invocation:** This protocol is invoked for all "important missions or actions" undertaken by Daemon (the Player Character) where the outcome is not explicitly guaranteed by the narrative or by a successful `Resolution Check` that achieves a "critical success" (e.g., natural 20 on an important roll, or a roll exceeding DC by 10+). An "important mission or action" is defined as any endeavor with significant strategic, political, or military consequences (e.g., major conquests, critical diplomatic overtures, complex internal reforms, attempts to sway powerful NPCs, large-scale training initiatives with specific goals). Minor, routine actions are generally exempt.
        *   **Failure Chance:** Before determining the final outcome of such an action, the GM will internally roll a d100. On a result of 1-20 (20% chance), an unforeseen complication, setback, or partial failure will occur, even if Daemon's rolls or planning would otherwise indicate success.
        *   **Nature of Failure:** This "fickle fate" failure will manifest as:
            *   **Partial Success:** The action achieves its primary goal, but with significant unintended negative consequences, reduced gains, or unforeseen costs.
            *   **Setback:** The action is delayed, requires additional resources/effort, or encounters a new, unexpected obstacle.
            *   **Unforeseen Complication:** A seemingly unrelated event occurs that negatively impacts the current objective or creates a new problem for Daemon.
        *   **Narrative Integration:** The GM will narrate the outcome, incorporating the setback or partial failure organically into the story, ensuring it feels like a natural (albeit unlucky) development rather than an arbitrary punishment. Daemon will not be explicitly told "you failed due to the 20% chance"; rather, the narrative will present the unforeseen obstacle or consequence.
        *   **Player Agency:** The player will still have agency to react to these setbacks and attempt to mitigate their effects.

*   **Proposed New Text (Revised "Daemon's Fickle Fate Protocol"):**
    *   **New Protocol Addition: "Daemon's Fickle Fate Protocol"**
        *   **Invocation:** For any significant, player-initiated action or long-term mission that carries a high stake (e.g., establishing a spy network, assassinating a major figure, negotiating a complex alliance, infiltrating a secured location), this protocol is invoked.
        *   **Complication Chance:** There is a hidden **20% chance** that an unforeseen complication, partial setback, or rival interference will occur. This complication may manifest as a new obstacle, a partial failure of a secondary objective, the emergence of an unexpected consequence, or the appearance of a rival force, regardless of the success of the immediate action roll or plan. This rule does not dictate outright failure of the primary goal but ensures the path is never entirely frictionless.
        *   **Narrative Integration:** The GM will not explicitly announce when this check is made or its outcome; the narrative will simply reflect the emerging complication. The player will still have agency to react to these setbacks and attempt to mitigate their effects.

*   **Justification:** This amendment refines the application of the "Fickle Fate" rule, making it clearer, more concise, and emphasizing the *nature* of the setback (complication/interference) rather than outright failure. It ensures that the mechanic introduces dynamic challenges into high-stake actions, enhancing strategic depth and immersion without being punitive. The examples clarify the scope of "significant, high-stake" actions.
