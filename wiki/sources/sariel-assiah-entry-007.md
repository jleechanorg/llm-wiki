Cassian's accusation hung in the air, a physical weight that pressed down on you. Your head, already throbbing from the bandage over your ear, felt heavy. You didn't meet his gaze, instead fixing your eyes on the intricate pattern of the carpet, as if the answers lay hidden there. His words were a direct hit, echoing the relentless prosecutor in your own mind. There was no defense you could offer yourself, no argument you truly believed.

Your voice, when it came, was barely a whisper, thin and fragile. "You're right, Cassian." The words felt like ash in your mouth, but they were true. "It *is* my fault. I… I broke. I told him." You forced yourself to look at the bandage on your ear, a stark, painful testament. "He... he knew how to make me talk. And then… then I screamed. I know my scream distracted her. I killed her."

The admission was a raw, aching wound laid bare. You felt a perverse, masochistic comfort in giving voice to the guilt that suffocated you. Your shoulders slumped, shrinking further into the heavy mourning robes, wishing they could swallow you whole.

Cassian stared at you, his fury momentarily disarmed by your complete surrender. The raw anger in his eyes flickered, replaced by a flicker of something close to pity, quickly stifled. He seemed to deflate slightly, as if your immediate self-condemnation left him with no one to fight. He took a slow, deep breath, his chest still heaving with the remnants of his rage. He didn't move closer, didn't offer comfort. His silence, now, was almost as heavy as his accusations. He simply turned, his gaze distant, lost in his own grief.
