# Stellar Work EP57 — Engineering Management & AI with Jeff from Snap

## Summary

Stellar Work podcast EP57 features **Jeff Lee-Chan** ([[JeffreyChan]]), Engineering Manager at Snap, in conversation with host **Ben** ([[BenStellarWork]]). The episode covers engineering management evolution at big tech, AI tool adoption, organizational velocity, career path disruption, and specific tool shoutouts to [[cmux]] and [[AgentOrchestrator]].

Jeff spent 10 years at Google (YouTube, Staff level) and ~7 years at Snap, transitioning from IC to management. The episode captures his personal AI awakening ("30 minutes to a working website hitting an API"), his philosophy on AI-augmented management, and candid advice on the power dynamics of manager–report relationships.

## Key Claims

1. **AI velocity gap is real but organizational**: Teams using AI well see ~2x speed today; 10x is theoretically possible if every SDLC stage is optimized, but 100x won't happen because human decision-making remains a bottleneck
2. **Managers must stay technically current**: Engineering managers need to be "top 25%" in AI tool proficiency — you don't have to be the expert but you must be able to help engineers debug and point them to tools
3. **Timeline compression as discovery tool**: Cut project timelines in half and work backwards — the resulting bottleneck list (build time, code review latency, etc.) reveals where AI investment actually matters
4. **Junior roles shrinking, top-5% fine**: AI is reducing entry-level hiring; top 5% of new grads (those who build and ship real things) will always be employable; the rest face a challenging career path
5. **Teams should be flatter and smaller**: If starting a company today, Jeff would staff 2-3 people per team, not 10; AI makes cross-functional work feasible for individuals
6. **Multiple code review bots > one**: 4-5 different review bots including a custom one is better than relying on a single bot
7. **Hackathon-mode as default**: Permanent hackathon (same people, same incentives, same urgency) should be the work style, not the exception
8. **Micromanagement by engagement**: Jeff's early management mistake was being so technically engaged that every interaction implicitly checked in on reports — power dynamics make casual engagement feel like scrutiny

## Key Quotes

> "In 30 minutes I had a website and it actually hit an API… I was astounded. That would have taken me like a week, maybe full time." — Jeff, on his first AI side project (~mid 2025)

> "Every part where human is involved is a bottleneck." — Jeff, on why 10x hasn't arrived yet

> "You are truly no longer a peer — there is a formal relationship, there is a power dynamic, and if you're not aware of it, your actions do have a good amount of emotional impact." — Jeff, advice to his younger manager self

> "I think it actually should be a lot flatter and maybe even a lot less people." — Jeff, on team structure with AI

> "cmux, definitely try it out… it's actually the best terminal I've ever used, especially for AI." — Jeff, shoutout at ~33:16
