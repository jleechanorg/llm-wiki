Location: House Valerius Estate, Upper City, Morning

The sun, now fully risen, painted the Upper City in a golden, indifferent light. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) moved through its pristine streets, her Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate concealed beneath a new set of formal, yet unassuming, noble's garb. The city's waking bustle was a mere background hum to her focused intent. The Valerius Estate, a sprawling edifice of polished stone and ancient lineage, loomed ahead—a gilded cage she had willingly escaped, now a fortress she intended to breach with a new weapon: intelligence.

She approached the main gates, her head held with a quiet, undeniable authority that belied her youth. The guards, stern-faced men in House Valerius livery, recognized the family sigil on her concealed ring, and a flicker of unease crossed their features. News of her exile was widespread, yet her presence, unexpected and unannounced, commanded their attention.

"I require an immediate audience with Lord Cassian Valerius," Nocturne stated, her voice devoid of emotion, a low, even tone that carried an almost unnatural weight. Her gaze, direct and unyielding, fixed on the lead guard. "The matter is of critical importance to House Valerius. It concerns immediate threats that your current security protocols have failed to detect." She offered no further explanation, letting the implied gravitas of her words hang in the air.

Before the guards could fully process her audacity, a familiar voice, smooth as polished steel, cut through the morning air. "Nocturne. What a... surprise." Julian Valerius (Age 32), her eldest brother and the architect of her exile, emerged from the estate's main doors, flanked by two armed retainers. His smile was cordial, but his eyes, sharp and calculating, held a cold, predatory glint. "I was under the impression your presence here was... explicitly forbidden. Have you forgotten your place, little sister?" His tone was laced with thinly veiled contempt and a subtle warning.

Nocturne's gaze flickered to Julian, utterly dismissive of his attempt at dominance. She felt no anger, only a cold assessment of his predictable obstruction. "My place, Julian, is where House Valerius requires the most astute strategic mind. A mind capable of seeing threats that your 'management' has clearly overlooked." She let the implication of his incompetence hang in the air. "The matter is for Father's ears, and his alone. It is a matter of unprecedented infiltration and a looming threat, directly from House Durin's Folly. A threat that will reshape Baldur's Gate." Her voice dropped slightly, taking on a chillingly urgent undertone. "I require an audience, and resources. My knowledge is invaluable. His authority, and mine, will be necessary to contain this."

Julian's cordial facade wavered, a flicker of genuine concern, quickly masked, crossing his features at the mention of Durin's Folly and "unprecedented infiltration." His eyes narrowed, assessing her with a new, grudging intensity. He clearly recognized the weight of her words, even if he despised their source.
