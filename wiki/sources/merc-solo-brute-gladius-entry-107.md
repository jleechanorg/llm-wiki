The two massive Giant Spiders lay still, their segmented bodies rent and lifeless, their multiple eyes glazed over in death. The raw, visceral pleasure of the double kill, the absolute finality of it, surged through Gladius, a powerful, almost euphoric rush. The sharp tang of green ichor and the cloying sweetness of spider silk filled the air, a fitting perfume for his triumph. He stood amidst the quiet carnage, his chest heaving faintly, his greatsword still dripping, his senses sharp, taking in the full, immediate reality of his overwhelming power. This abandoned farmstead, once a hunting ground for spiders, was now his domain, its threats utterly silenced.

His pragmatic mind, ever focused on acquisition and the leveraging of power, immediately turned to the defeated creatures. They were beasts, yes, but even beasts held value. He recalled the note in his mind: "venomous fangs or silk glands *might* be useful components." The thought brought a cold hum beneath his skin. This wasn't about the thrill of the kill anymore; it was about the experience of extraction, the grim utility of what remained.

He knelt beside the first spider, its enormous form still twitching faintly in death. The task was messy, unpleasant, but Gladius felt no revulsion, only a detached, clinical efficiency. His large, calloused hands, usually reserved for the greatsword or the cord, now worked with surprising precision. He used a sharp utility knife from his pack, cutting through the tough chitin of its mandibles, carefully extracting the venom sacs. The viscous, yellowish fluid dripped, thick and potent. Then, he moved to the abdomen, severing the silk glands, the material surprisingly tough and elastic even after death. The air grew heavier with the cloying, sweet scent. He repeated the process with the second spider, his movements becoming more fluid, more practiced, each extraction a tangible acquisition, a new form of power to wield. The very act, though gruesome, was a raw, immediate experience, confirming his mastery over even the dead.

He wiped the green ichor and sticky venom from his hands on a clump of marsh grass, then carefully sealed the extracted components in a small, oiled leather pouch. It was a crude trophy, yet a valuable one. He pictured some alchemist in Baldur's Gate, desperate for such rare components, forced to deal with *him*, the unwashed mercenary, to acquire them. The thought brought a grim, satisfied smile to his lips. This wasn't just about gold; it was about the subtle, insidious leverage, another thread in the web he was weaving around those who thought themselves untouchable. The night was cold, but a profound internal warmth settled in Gladius's gut.
