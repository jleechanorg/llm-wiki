# God Mode — When to Use It and When Not To

God Mode is your override. It lets you direct world events, control NPCs, and set plot state outside the normal AI output flow. Use it well and the campaign has consistent long-term structure. Overuse it and you've written the story yourself.

---

## When to Use God Mode

**Long-term plot arcs.** The AI can lose track of multi-session conspiracy threads. Use God Mode to maintain the antagonist's secret plan across 50 entries when the AI might accidentally reveal it early or forget it entirely.

**World-state changes.** Need a city to fall, a faction to betray the PCs, a war to start? Set it in God Mode. The campaign engine records it; future sessions build on it.

**NPC behavior control.** A recurring villain who must act in specific ways for the plot to work. Set their key decisions in God Mode so they're consistent across every scene.

**Fixing AI drift.** When the AI's emergent narrative has drifted from your intended direction, God Mode lets you reset state without re-running the session.

**Season finale setups.** The night before a major battle, use God Mode to position all pieces on the board — the armies, the betrayals, the weather, the assassinations. Then run the battle itself in campaign mode.

---

## When Not to Use God Mode

**Every scene.** If you're controlling every NPC's dialogue and every event, you're writing a book with extra steps.

**Discovery moments.** Let the AI surprise you. A key NPC betrayal that emerges from AI-generated behavior is more powerful than one you scripted.

**Routine encounters.** A tavern fight doesn't need God Mode. Let the AI generate it. The player experience is in the collaboration, not the control.

**Relationships and tone.** Let the AI build the texture of NPC interactions. You set the structural pins; the AI generates the fabric.

---

## The God Mode Sweet Spot

Use God Mode for **structure**. Use Campaign Mode for **content**.

Structure: This city is controlled by the Merchant's Guild, Lord Sarn is secretly funding the rebellion, the plague starts in three sessions.

Content: The AI generates how that plays out in scenes, what the Merchant's Guild lords say, how the plague manifests, what the rebellion does with the gold.

This division — your structure, AI content — produces the best campaigns.

---

## God Mode Anti-Patterns

**Setting too much detail.** If your God Mode entry reads like a complete scene, you've pre-written the campaign. Let the AI generate the scene.

**Forgetting to use it.** The most common mistake. You needed to set a world-state change three sessions ago and didn't. Now the AI has generated contradictory state. Solution: maintain a World Bible and update it with God Mode entries at the end of each session.

**Overriding player agency.** If the players make a choice in campaign mode that contradicts your God Mode plan, the player choice wins. God Mode is scaffolding, not rails. The players are the story; you and the AI are the world.
