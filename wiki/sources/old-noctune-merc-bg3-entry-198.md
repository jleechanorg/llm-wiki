Serpent Queen's quiet smile remained, unwavering, as she accepted the brutal logic of the 'Total Acquisition.' Financial ruin was a weapon as potent as any blade, a systemic dismantling of Lord Umbrage's power that would leave him desperate and vulnerable. The absence of the Zhentarim, orchestrated by Rathmar's terrified compliance, had left the estate naked. Now, it was time to strip it to the bone.

But the operation required a singular, critical constraint: Lyra. The girl was a seed planted for a future harvest, her trust a fragile thing that would shatter if she saw her 'confidante' sacking her home. Serpent Queen would move like a phantom, a void in the estate's security, ensuring her involvement remained an unseen variable.

She slipped through the Western Service Entrance, the aged lock yielding once more to her touch. The interior was silent, the household settling into the evening's rhythm. Lyra, she knew, would be in the solarium or her chambers, nursing her melancholy. Serpent Queen moved in the opposite direction, towards the master wing and Lord Umbrage's private study.

The hallways were shadows and silence. She bypassed the servant's quarters and the kitchen, her enhanced senses mapping the estate's heartbeat. Lord Umbrage was likely out, or perhaps dining; his study was dark.

Inside the study, the air smelled of old paper and expensive tobacco. Serpent Queen didn't waste time with the art on the walls—she had already taken the choice pieces. Her target now was the lifeblood of the house. She moved to the heavy oak desk, her fingers tracing the wood until she found the false panel she knew, by the logic of such men, must exist.

A click, and the panel slid back to reveal a reinforced iron safe. A blunt instrument, indeed, but effective. She knelt, her tools working with fluid precision, listening to the tumblers click into submission.

The safe swung open. Inside lay the true weight of the Umbrage legacy: heavy bags of platinum and gold, deeds to properties in the Lower City, and bearer bonds drawn on the Counting House. She swept it all into her pack, the weight significant but manageable. She stripped the room of silver candlesticks, a jeweled letter opener, a box of rare gems. She moved to the adjoining master bedroom, emptying jewelry boxes of heirlooms—pearls, diamonds, gold chains.

It was a hollowing out. When Lord Umbrage returned, he would find his walls standing but his foundation gone. He would be a lord in name only, desperate for funds, perhaps desperate enough to turn to the Guild... or to a daughter who suddenly seemed to have found a new, powerful friend.

As she exfiltrated, laden with the spoils of a dynasty, she paused near the corridor leading to the solarium. A faint light spilled from beneath the door. Lyra was there, safe in her ignorance, dreaming of freedom while Serpent Queen stole the chains that bound her father. Arvax would be pleased with the chaos; Rathmar would be terrified by the reach; and Lyra... Lyra would remain the pristine vessel for the Serpent Queen's ultimate design.
