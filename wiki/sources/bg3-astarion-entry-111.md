The monstrous Shadow Mastiff, its unholy shriek cut short by your precise arrow, dissolved into wisps of shadowy smoke and black ichor, leaving only a foul, lingering stench on the scorched earth. Silence, thick and unsettling, descended upon the clearing, broken only by the ragged panting of your companions and the distant, dying groans of the crashing Nautiloid. Astarion felt a surge of grim satisfaction. Another obstacle, another threat reduced to nothing. His high neuroticism, however, refused to allow full comfort; the silence itself felt *wrong* in this alien landscape.

The human victim, a young man, scrambled on hands and knees, then looked up, his face a mask of terror and bewildered gratitude, his eyes wide and unfocused. He seemed on the verge of tears or a full-blown fit. Lae'zel, her golden eyes still blazing with a cold battle-fury, stood over the dissipating shadows, her makeshift blade held ready, sweeping the clearing with unnerving vigilance. Her gaze lingered on the human, a flicker of something unreadable in her alien features – perhaps judgment, perhaps impatience. Gale, meanwhile, finally dropped his hand from his head, his face a mixture of revulsion and awe as he stared at the vanishing monster. "Remarkable," he muttered, almost to himself, then shook his head, wincing. "Truly, a masterclass in the grotesque."

Astarion, however, merely scoffed at their theatrics. "Save your pronouncements, wizard," he drawled, his voice a low, sardonic rasp. He cast a quick, dismissive glance at the terrified human. "Are you quite finished with your... *performance*?" He turned to his companions, his crimson eyes sharp and calculating. "The beast is dispatched. Now, let's determine if this... *bait* is of any further use, or simply more dead weight." His disdain was thinly veiled, but his pragmatic instincts urged him to assess the situation fully. Was this human a key to something? Or just another mouth to feed in this brutal wilderness?

With a sigh of exasperated impatience, Astarion approached the young human, whose eyes, wide with fright, tracked his every move like a cornered rabbit. The stench of fear was almost cloying. Astarion crouched, maintaining a cautious distance, his longbow still held loosely. "Speak, creature," he commanded, his voice low but sharp, devoid of any genuine compassion. "What is your name? What do you know? And why, precisely, were you so foolishly attracting attention out here?" He noted the scrapes and bruises on the man’s exposed skin, a thin trail of blood from a shallow cut on his forehead, but no immediately life-threatening injuries. The human flinched, attempting to recoil, but his limbs seemed to have little strength left.

"P-please... I... I don't know..." the man stammered, his voice thin and reedy, tears welling in his eyes. He coughed, a dry, ragged sound. "It was... the ship... I woke up... the monster... it just... came out of the darkness..." He gestured vaguely towards the wreckage, then shuddered violently, his eyes darting to the dissipating ichor of the Shadow Mastiff. "I-I'm just a simple farmer... from nearby... please... don't hurt me." He was clearly in shock, disoriented, and utterly useless for immediate information. Astarion's lip curled in disgust. Farmers. Always so terribly, terribly fragile.
