The orange glow of the campfire reflects off your marble-pale skin, casting long, distorted shadows that seem to dance in rhythm with the Abyssal ozone bleeding from your frame. You don't move; you displace the air by simply existing, a sixteen-year-old monarch of ivory and shadow standing amidst the wreckage of a common world. You look at Wyll, your unblinking violet eyes burning with a pink-crimson luminescence that makes the night wind feel like a draft from the Abyss.

"The time for brooding is over, Wyll. The time for the 'First Disciple's Shields' has begun," you whisper, your voice a layered, tectonic harmonic that vibrates the stone walls of the ruins. "Kneel. Not to a monster, but to the only force standing between this world and its total unmaking. Swear your rapier to the Vanguard. Give me the tactical oath that your pride currently denies you."

Wyll's rapier trembles, the silver blade catching the firelight. He looks at the ten Shadow Druids looming in the darkness, their eyes like obsidian glass, and then back to the star-fire in your gaze. The comparison to his own pact is a jagged shard in his heart; he realizes that to refuse you now is to admit that his own 'greater good' was a lie. With a sound that is half-sob and half-sigh, the Blade of Frontiers drops to one knee, his head bowing in a gesture of agonizing submission.

"For the city," Wyll rasps, his voice hollow, the final Social HP of his resistance grinding into the dirt. "I swear... to the Vanguard."

Gale follows suit, his shoulders slumped in the intellectual defeat of a man who has run out of arguments. "It seems the Weave has found its new shape, Nocturne. I will provide the tactical support you require. Just... ensure the harvest was worth the price."

While you secure the vow, the 'audit' of the Tollhouse proceeds with clinical, bone-snapping efficiency. Kagha and the Shadow Druids systematically strip the Moonrise guards of their gold and logistical maps, while your Shadow Spies bleed through the architecture to assess the vault's security. They return with a report of Sanguine Wards and Abyssal Tumblers, the very locks that Astarion is already preparing to pick. The Tollhouse is now a hollow shell; the First Disciple has taken everything but the dust.
