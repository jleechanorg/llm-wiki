"But tell me, Ilithyia," I trill, my voice a melodic needle of curiosity that masks the cold strategic tally I am performing. "My father's stewards always grumbled about the cost of maintaining a proper Roman guard in the East. He spoke of Glaber's... 'Eastern simplification' with such envy. "Specialists? My dear Gaia, Glaber has no need for 'ghosts' when he has the iron of the legions," she purrs, the Palatine needle of her voice sharp with condescension. "But he is not a man to waste a useful tool. He has indeed repurposed several Thracian cohorts—auxiliaries who know the terrain better than their own names. It is a messy business, but as you say... efficient. Though I suspect they are more like dogs on a short leash than wolves."

Internally, I savor the disclosure as a sensory dessert. The geometry of the East is finally clear: Glaber is using Thracian blood to shield his Roman iron, and he has stationed them at the very throat of the region. I offer Ilithyia a look of profound, saccharine admiration, yet for a microsecond, a 'Regal Frost' flickers in my gaze as I look down at Spartacus in the pit. The Thracian's brothers are being used as a shield for the man who stole his wife. It is a delicious irony, and I find the prospect of turning those 'dogs' against their master to be the most exquisite joke of the evening.
