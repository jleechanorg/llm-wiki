The revelation of Lord Silvanus’s true, avaricious ambition for Greenmeadow was a satisfying confirmation of Gladius’s cynical worldview. Nobles were all the same: greedy, self-serving, and ultimately, weak. Corvan’s pathetic display of fear was a pleasing complement to the information. Gladius straightened, allowing the weight of his greatsword to shift, a silent punctuation mark to the interrogation. The pre-dawn light filtering through the inn’s grimy windows seemed to illuminate Corvan’s misery with stark clarity.

"Nine souls," Gladius stated, his voice now devoid of its prior menace, instead taking on a tone of cold, brutal professionalism, "the Millers, Blackwoods, Stonehands. And 'no witnesses.'" He let his gaze sweep across Corvan’s face, then to the silent Strangler’s Cord secured to his belt, then to the new Shadow-Step Boots. "That means *no one*. No alarms. No survivors. Nothing but a quiet, empty farm and two other cottages by dawn. Understood?" His words were a final, chilling confirmation of the terms, a reminder that he was not just a mercenary, but a force of nature, utterly committed to the parameters of the contract, especially the brutal ones.

Corvan nodded frantically, his eyes wide and pleading. "Yes! Yes, understood. Complete silence. Clean. Lord Silvanus desires... utmost discretion. He merely wishes the land to be available. Nothing to trace back." He practically pushed himself back against his chair, as if to physically distance himself from the grim reality of the task and Gladius's chilling embodiment of it. His relief at Gladius's apparent shift from interrogation to confirmation was palpable, though still tinged with deep-seated terror.

Gladius gave a short, dismissive grunt, a sign of his complete satisfaction with the man’s terror and his confirmation. The job was clear. The preparations were complete. The anticipation for the work, the thrill of the hunt, now pulsed through him.
