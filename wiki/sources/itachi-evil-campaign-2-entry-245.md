Zetsu was indeed incapacitated and a valuable source of intel, a living asset to be secured. However, he was a Level 15 entity, a full-sized, plant-like humanoid currently half-merged with the solid rock. Extracting his substantial form from the stone would be a physical and chakra-intensive task, far from silent, and certainly not quick. Attempting to un-merge him and then carry his considerable bulk (even unconscious) would immediately compromise Itachi's **Master Stealth** and **Greater Invisibility**, drastically reducing his movement speed and making him an obvious target. Obito's return was imminent; the window for a clean, stealthy departure was closing rapidly. A forced extraction of Zetsu in this manner would transform a planned retreat into a certain, high-risk confrontation with an alerted Obito within the unstable vault, before Itachi could even activate **Susano'o**.

Therefore, while securing Zetsu alive for later interrogation was a tempting proposition for ultimate control, attempting to physically extract and carry him *now, while retreating from Obito's imminent return*, was **not feasible** without sacrificing stealth, speed, and almost certainly forcing a direct, unprepared combat with Obito that Itachi did not choose.

The choice remained between leveraging the current advantage against Obito, or retreating with the artifact, perhaps leaving Zetsu to be dealt with by the returning Obito.
