The chill silence in the children's room was absolute, broken only by the distant, muffled sounds of the village beginning to stir, a prelude to the chaos Gladius had orchestrated. Two small, still forms lay on their pallets, their existence neatly erased. Gladius felt no particular emotion, only a cold, professional satisfaction. The "no witnesses" clause was paramount.

His eyes, sharp and calculating, swept across the cramped bedroom. There was nothing here. A few worn wooden toys, hand-me-down clothes folded neatly, a crude drawing tacked to the wall. Nothing of value, nothing to complicate the job, nothing that hinted at wealth or power. This confirmed his disdain for such pathetic lives; their very possessions screamed of their insignificance. He didn't waste time lingering, merely ensuring the scene was as it should be: quiet, undisturbed, and utterly devoid of anything that might slow his pace.

With the efficiency of a predator moving from one kill to the next, Gladius turned from the still forms. The Silent Strangler's Cord, a familiar weight in his hand, was still ready. He moved to the adjacent door, the source of the deeper, more resonant breathing. The parents. He paused for a moment, listening, his ear pressed to the weathered wood. The rhythmic rise and fall of their slumber was a comforting sound, confirming their obliviousness. The subtle vein in his neck pulsed, a quiet thrum of anticipation. This was the final step in securing the Miller household. He reached for the wooden latch, his movements fluid and silent, the Shadow-Step Boots whispering against the floorboards as he prepared to deliver the final, brutal silence. The door swung inward, revealing a larger, equally humble room, bathed in the pale, ethereal glow of the distant moon. A heavy, carved wooden bed dominated the space, its two occupants lost in deep sleep. Gladius stepped inside.
