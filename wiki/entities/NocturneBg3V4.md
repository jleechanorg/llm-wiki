## Description

Nocturne BG3 V4 is the longest-running Nocturne campaign, spanning 1,207 scenes from Mirtul 1492 DR to beyond. It is a noir psychological horror campaign set in Baldur's Gate during the Absolute crisis, integrated with BG3 events. Nocturne Sosuke begins as a Level 1 Bard (College of Swords) and ends as a Level 60 Nascent Overgod presiding over the Sovereign Citadel of Chronias in the Seventh Heaven.

**The campaign's signature pattern:** Administrative control as a form of power. Nocturne never abandons her lute or her Bard class — her magic is social and sonic, her power comes from being the one who holds the pen in a world full of people who only know how to swing swords.

## Story

Two years prior to Scene 1, House Sosuke was systematically dismantled by the Cassalanter Family. Nocturne and her sisters were sold to The Bloom Shadow — an Upper City brothel run by Madam Zylra — to settle debts they never incurred. Nocturne was the youngest and most physically beautiful. She survived by studying the men who came to use her: their weaknesses, their vanities, their hunger for power.

Scene 1 opens with Horgus Gwent — an older, wealthy nobleman — arriving at her chamber with a Soul Coin bearing her sister Lyra's face and an invitation to the Cassalanter Ascension Gala. Nocturne engineers a marriage to Horgus within six scenes, legally entering the Gwent name without ever consummating the marriage as a subservient wife.

The campaign's first arc is the Cassalanter heist: intercepting their soul-tithe operation, hijacking the Gilded Dirge ritual in the Cassalanter Temple, and redirecting the entire infrastructure of their infernal soul trade. By Scene 400, she is filing reports with the Absolute as if she is a loyal node in their network, and the Elder Brain — auditing every soul in the city — grants her 100,000 gold, 20 rangers, 20 specialists, 50 soldiers, and 50 spies as reinforcements. She has turned the city's own surveillance apparatus into her personal army supplier.

The middle arc is the Gala and Moonrise: attending Orin the Red's bloodsport event, securing the Gala of Flesh access, confronting Ketheric Thorm at Moonrise Towers docks while pregnant, surviving the Shadow-Cursed Verdict, and activating the Arros Silencer against her own registration. She is named Grand Duke and commands 1,624 personnel.

The Hell arc begins around Scene 700: she descends into Avernus, internalizes the Ruby Rod, and treats the Nine Hells as a corporate acquisition. At Scene 900 she becomes the Archdevil of Souls (Level 30), with 2.5M gold pieces and 3,120 souls. At Scene 1100 — "The Sovereign Tithe of Nessus" — she stands atop the obsidian dais of Malsheem, the Lords of the Eight kneeling before her, and imposes a 25% management surcharge on all soul harvest from the Nine Layers. Her daily income increases by 55,000 gold pieces. Ao declares Time of Troubles v2.

The final arc is the Sovereign Citadel: Scene 1200 places her on the obsidian throne of Chronias in the Seventh Heaven, Level 60 Nascent Overgod, with 81 deities as processing nodes in her Overledger, the Secular Regalia pulsing as the subsonic heartbeat of the multiverse's new nervous system. Scene 1207 — the final recorded entry — shows her renaming the Gwent bloodline to Sosuke and rewriting reality.
