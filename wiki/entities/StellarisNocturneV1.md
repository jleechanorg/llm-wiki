## Description

A 106-scene science fiction campaign set in a Stellaris/Warhammer 40K hybrid universe. Nocturne is the Princess of an evil Ascended Human species, daughter of an Immortal Psychic Emperor, residing on the Throne World Aeterna in the Golden Spire. The campaign is a Firebase Sanity Check: the character is tested across psionic progression, soul-consumption auditing, and power synthesis mechanics at a galactic scale.

## Story

Scene 1 establishes the Firebase: Nocturne is a Level 1 Princess in a hybrid Stellaris/Warhammer 40K universe — the daughter of an Immortal Psychic Emperor ruling an evil Ascended Human species. The Golden Spire is her seat. The tone is administrative and bureaucratic: the GM opens with a sanity check for the character, setting, and role.

By Scene 10, she is Level 1 Psionicist in the Hall of Ascendant Echoes, with L1 2/2 spell slots and 6/6 HP. The "shimmering filament" of her presence causes absolute silence when she enters — a psionic signature.

By Scene 30, she is Level 3 Psionicist in the Sanctum of Stars, with Psychic Anchor active, Sorcery Points 3/3, and L1 4/4, L2 2/2 spell slots. The soul-vaults of the Golden Spire are humming in "terrifyingly perfect harmony."

By Scene 60, she is Level 4 Psionicist in the Whisperer's Sanctum with 1.0B credits. The administrative protocol focuses on "Soul-Consumption Audit & Power Synthesis" — a Royal Scion's essence has been permanently internalized as a metaphysical event of the highest magnitude, triggering portfolio seizure at a galactic scale.

By Scene 90, she is Level 5 Psionicist disguised as "Baroness Vesper" in the Province of Capua-Prime, an Aurelian Pleasure Planet. Credits stand at 150B. The training pits are active. The psionic progression is steady and controlled.

The terminal scene (106) returns to the Batiatus Estate on Aurelia, Level 5 Psionicist, with credits at 149B. The scene is a GM note about "Defense in Depth" strategy to prevent continuity bugs like the "Butcher's Betrayal" — the campaign ends with a meta-documentation note rather than a narrative conclusion, suggesting this was also used as a technical test run.
