## Description

Gaia Julia Caesar is a 12-year-old noblewoman and the youngest daughter of Emperor Julius Caesar in this alternate history D&D 5e campaign set in 71 BCE during the Spartacus revolt. Despite her tender age, she possesses exceptional intellect (INT 20) and charisma (CHA 18), making her a formidable force in the political landscape of the Roman Republic.

What makes Gaia unique is her **Miasma** — the divine blood of Venus flowing through her veins. This inherited divine power marks her as special among the Caesar line, though the full extent of its capabilities remains to be explored throughout the campaign.

## In This Campaign

Gaia operates under a cover of humility — presenting herself as merely a talented young servant accompanying a band of rugged mercenaries. In reality, she is an Imperial agent tasked with a critical mission: stabilize the chaos of the Spartacus revolt while protecting Rome's public image. She must prove herself to her "cold, distant father" that she is his truest heir.

Her role as a "Glass Cannon" reflects her nature: high damage output and charisma but low physical defenses (HP 19, CON 8). She relies on her Praetorian Hand guards for protection while she uses her wits and social skills to manipulate the situation.
