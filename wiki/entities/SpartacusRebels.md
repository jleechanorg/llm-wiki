The Spartacus Rebels are the slave army faction that rose under Spartacus's leadership during the Third Servile War (73-71 BCE). This rebel army, consisting of escaped slaves, gladiators, and disaffected poor, posed an existential threat to the Roman Republic. Though ultimately defeated by Crassus, the rebellion left a legacy that inspires anti-slavery crusaders like Aurelius Julia Caesar V2. The rebel faction represents the possibility of collective resistance against systemic oppression and serves as a symbol of hope for those still enslaved.
