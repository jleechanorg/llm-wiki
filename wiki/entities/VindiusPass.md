Vindius Pass is a key strategic location in northern Spain that serves as a mountain chokepoint between the Roman-controlled territories and the independent tribal lands beyond. This pass provides the crucial link between the Ebro river valley and the Cantabrian coast, making control of it essential forRoman expansion efforts. Aurelius seeks to control this pass as part of his conquest objectives in Northern Hispania. The pass is guarded by local tribes who use the terrain to their advantage in resisting Roman occupation.
