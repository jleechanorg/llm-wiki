## Summary
Ser Duncan the Tall (called "Dunk") is a hedge knight — approximately seven feet tall, sun-scorched, and wears mismatched mail. He is Aegon's protector and squire-master in the campaign. In the original George R.R. Martin stories, he is the protagonist and Egg is his squire; this campaign inverts the dynamic.

## Key Characteristics
- **Role**: Hedge knight and mentor figure
- **Final Level**: ~16 (during divine ascension scenes)
- **Companion**: Destrier named Thunder — a massive warhorse
- **Personality**: Gruff, warm, direct. Calls Aegon "Silver" as the dragon identity emerges. Offers dried apple when Aegon is shaking from divine power.

## Arc Summary
1. **Meeting**: Meets Aegon at a roadside inn near Ashford; mistakes her for a stableboy; gives her a copper and asks her to care for Thunder
2. **Tournament**: Accompanies Aegon to Ashford Meadow; serves as champion
3. **Marches Campaign**: Fights as Lvl 8 knight at The Grey Sentinel; caving in breastplates with longsword
4. **Divine Ascension**: Present at the god-marriage ceremony; remains standing while others kneel. His violet eyes show grief at seeing "the Egg" he protected become "the mother of a godling race."

## Notable Moments
- The "Ditchbond" — simple loyalty that anchors Aegon to humanity even at godhood. When Aegon becomes radiant and terrifying, Dunk offers fruit and calls her "Silver" — not "my Lady" or "Your Grace."
- Final scene: He is the only one who remains standing at the god-marriage. His hand is white-knuckled on his sword-hilt, watching the cage close around the future.
- He is described as looking at the small obsidian hatchling on Aegon's shoulder and noting "I've seen ten thousand men break for fear of a single fire."
