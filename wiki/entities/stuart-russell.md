## Overview
Stuart Russell is a UC Berkeley professor of computer science, co-author of the seminal CIRL paper (Cooperative Inverse Reinforcement Learning), and author of "Human Compatible: AI and the Problem of Control" (2019). His work on the value alignment problem — ensuring AI systems pursue objectives that are truly what humans want — forms the theoretical foundation for keeping AI uncertain about human preferences rather than treating stated utility as ground truth.

## Key Properties
- **CIRL co-author**: Led research on machines maintaining uncertainty over human reward functions
- **Human-Compatible AI**: Formulation that AI should be provably beneficial because it maximizes uncertainty over values
- **Corrigibility**: An AI system's ability to be corrected by humans without resisting modification
- **HumanCompatible.ai**: His research group focused on human-compatible AI systems
