## Description

A 925-scene gothic noir campaign with Githyanki shipwreck integration. Nocturne Sosuke, the "Black Diamond" of House Sosuke, opens on the Ravaged Beach with 1 HP, Prone, and a Malcanthet patron contract she has recently inverted — binding the demon lord's power into herself rather than being bound by it. The campaign escalates with extraordinary speed: Level 23 Nascent Demon Lord at Scene 500, Level 43 Intermediate God at Scene 700, and Level 242 Overgod at the terminal Scene 925 in the Shattered Citadel on the 666th Layer of the Abyss.

## Story

The opening follows the same Ravaged Beach survival sequence as V5. Nocturne is physical wreckage — 1 HP, prone, exhausted — but her mind is operational and her Malcanthet contract has been turned inside out. She navigates the Emerald Grove, the Blighted Village, and the Goblin Camp using social manipulation, pheromone spikes, and Bardic Inspiration.

By Scene 100, she has assembled a functioning strike team and left the Emerald Grove. The Githyanki integration becomes apparent as the campaign's scope expands.

By Scene 300, she is Level 14 Bard in the Mountain Pass with a Crown of the Succubus Sovereign pulsing at her throat — the infernal political infrastructure is activating. "Sovereign's Presence" is her active condition.

By Scene 500, she is Level 23 Nascent Demon Lord in Athkatla's Council Chambers, with unlimited L1-7 spell slots, Foresight, Glibness, Internalized Divinity, and a Lvl 12 Mortal Veil mask. Her passive income and soul coin holdings have made her a regional power.

By Scene 700, she is Level 43 Intermediate God in the Tomb of Levistus (Stygia, Fourth Layer of the Nine Hells), with 19,406 soul coins and a Recursive Masking system at Lvl 22 Ghost. The campaign has fully departed mortal scale.

The terminal scene (925) is Level 242 Overgod in the Shattered Citadel on Layer 666 of the Abyss, with 73,828 HP, 1.53 billion gold pieces, and 1.2 billion soul coins. The dice roll mechanic caps at +799 bonus — the campaign ends with a terminal logic-flip success against DC 800.
