## Description

A 278-scene survival horror campaign with infernal politics. Nocturne Sosuke opens on the Ravaged Beach at 1 HP, Prone, with Malcanthet's Favor available — a demon patron contract that has been inverted. The campaign covers the full BG3 Act 1 experience from the beach through the Goblin Camp, the Emerald Grove, and the Shattered Sanctum, ending at Level 13 Nascent Demon Lord in the Shattered Sanctum Main Hall.

## Key Details

- **Race:** Half-Elf
- **Class:** Bard | Level 1 → Final Level 13 (Nascent Demon Lord)
- **Background:** 730 days at The Bloom Shadow brothel; Malcanthet patron (inverted)
- **Signature resources:** Pheromone Spike (3/3 → 5/5), Malcanthet's Favor, Thralls (6/6)
- **Scenes:** 278

## Story

Scene 1 follows the same Ravaged Beach setup as V5 and V5 Fixed V2: 1 HP, Prone, Intellect Devourers nearby, Shadowheart unconscious. The Malcanthet contract has been inverted — Nocturne has bound Malcanthet's power rather than being bound by it. Her background as the "Black Diamond" of The Bloom Shadow is the same.

By Scene 50, she is Level 2 Bard on the Cliffside Road, navigating early survival challenges. Her leadership persuasion fails against companion egos (roll 11 vs DC 15), establishing interpersonal friction.

By Scene 100, she has reached the Emerald Grove as Level 4 Bard, having recruited Arron and established a base of operations. Persuasion checks are succeeding (roll 26 vs DC 12).

By Scene 150, she is Level 5 Bard in the Emerald Grove Inner Sanctum, performing the Gilded Tether ritual on Kagha (roll 31 vs DC 22), and modifying Olodan's ritual — the Grove's druidic politics are being restructured through her intervention.

By Scene 200, she is Level 7 Bard at the Blighted Village Windmill, cloaked in invisibility, running Stealth checks against high-alert camps (roll 30 vs DC 16), and harvesting XP from the Grove's aftermath.

The terminal scene (278) places her as Level 13 Nascent Demon Lord in the Shattered Sanctum Main Hall with 1M gold pieces, the Aura of the Sovereign active, True Sight engaged, and a successful Religious Conversion roll (35 vs DC 20) demonstrating high-tier social dominance.
