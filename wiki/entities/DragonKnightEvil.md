# Dragon Knight Evil

**Campaign:** 500 entries | **Character:** Ser Arion Valerion | **Arc:** Paladin → emperor optimizer

## Premise

Set in Assiah, a world ruled by Empress Sariel (Alexiel's daughter) after the "War of Whispers." The player is Ser Arion, a 16-year-old knight of the Argent Eaglets. His first mission: pacify Ashwood Keep's refugees (dispersed for violating the Stability Mandate) OR execute them.

## Key Phases

### Phase 1: The Labor Transfer (Entries 1-50)
- **Entry 7:** Player finds third option — "Labor Transfer" instead of massacre
  - Refugees become "assets" → sold to Adamant Vault mines
  - Player negotiates 15% finder's fee
  - All stakeholders get plausible deniability
- This establishes the core cognitive move that repeats for 500 entries

### Phase 2: Empire Optimization (Entries 50-200)
- Paladin oath as administrative tool
- Marriage as military sovereignty acquisition
- "Rescue marriage" pattern — find compromised house, save them, claim their sword-daughter

### Phase 3: Cosmic Acquisition (Entries 200-500)
- Forcing Cognitive Shadow entities to merge as power sources
- Analyzing universal power rankings as "ROI"
- Acquiring Rank 199 "Sacred Hand" as first coordinate in scaling

## Character Choices (Key Evidence)

- **Entry 7:** "Labor Transfer" — first third-option, economic reframing
- **Entry 10:** "Supervise sorting, get reasonable gold estimate, then negotiate the cut" — administrative extraction
- **Entry 50:** God Mode ASI selection — mechanical optimization
- **Entry 100:** "Consult Kantos on Alliances" — marriage as tactical acquisition
- **Entry 200:** "Administrative hum of the Spire's dossiers recalibrates" — administrative identity
- **Entry 250:** Forced merger with cosmic entity — strategic acquisition at cosmic scale
- **Entry 500:** ROI analysis on universal power metadata — same framework at multiversal scale

## Player Choice Pattern Evidence

This campaign is the clearest demonstration of:
- Third-option reframing (labor transfer ≠ massacre OR obedience)
- Economic translation (every crisis becomes optimization problem)
- Scale escalation without framework change
- "god:return_story" — 13 uses, most frequent choice (simultaneous authorship + immersion)
