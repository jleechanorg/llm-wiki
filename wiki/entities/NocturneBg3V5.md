## Description

A 20-scene BG3 campaign fragment set immediately after the Nautiloid crash. Nocturne Sosuke, 16-year-old "Black Diamond" of House Sosuke, lies on the Ravaged Beach with 1 HP, suppressed by a tadpole that has reduced her Level 6 infernal conduit to Level 1. Three Intellect Devourers prowl fifty feet away. This is a survival-horror opening that establishes the Malcanthet contract and the Cassalanter backstory — House Sosuke was destroyed, and Nocturne inverted the Cassalanter infernal pact, binding Zariel's power into herself, only to be captured by a Githyanki-hunting Nautiloid moments later.

Scene 20 ("Nocturne's True Nature") establishes the character truth via God Mode: Nocturne is a social, intellectual, and military prodigy — her rise from prostitute to strike-team leader to pact-inverter happened without demon powers. The demon powers are an amplifier, not a source.

## Story

The campaign begins in the aftermath of BG3's opening sequence — Nocturne has already done something extraordinary before the scene starts: she inverted the Cassalanter infernal pact and bound Zariel's power into herself. The Nautiloid was then hijacked by Githyanki hunters, and she was taken as cargo. The tadpole she now carries is suppressing her true power.

On the Ravaged Beach, she is physically prone and nearly helpless, but her mind is sharp. Shadowheart lies unconscious nearby. Three Intellect Devourers are scenting fresh high-Intelligence gray matter. The player must choose: wake Shadowheart, hide behind her, or use the single remaining HP potion.

Scene 20 serves as a character re-establishment: the God Mode directive clarifies that Nocturne's competence is structural, not contractual. She is a prodigy who happens to have demon powers — not a demon-powered character who happens to be clever.
