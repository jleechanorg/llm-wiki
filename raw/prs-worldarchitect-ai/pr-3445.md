## Raw Body
### Motivation

- Prevent loss of non-reward system corrections when persisting `pending_system_corrections` after LLM/state updates. 
- Ensure reward-specific correction helpers are actually used end-to-end so reward-only messages are filtered/deduped while other corrections are preserved. 
- Remove duplicated scenario entries produced by legacy `steps` → `scenarios` conversion in evidence bundles. 
- Harden evidence conversion against unexpected `steps`/`step_data` types to avoid runtime exceptions.

### Description

- In `mvp_site/world_logic.py` preserved non-reward corrections by splitting `pending_system_corrections` into reward and non-reward lists, applying `_filter_reward_corrections()` for reward-only filtering, and persisting a combined, order-preserving deduplicated list to `pending_system_corrections`. 
- Normalized and hardened handling for `pending_system_corrections` inputs (None / non-list) when reading and cleaning combat state to avoid injecting invalid values like `[None]`. 
- In `testing_mcp/lib/evidence_utils.py` removed a duplicate `scenarios.append()` call, added a `isinstance(steps_dict, dict)` guard, and improved extraction of error messages from `step_data` (preserve `errors`/`error_message` when present, and provide a clear message for invalid step types). 
- Confirmed reward-correction helpers (`_is_reward_correction`, `_filter_reward_corrections`) are now used at persistence and response-filtering call sites so the new helpers participate in the full flow.
