## Summary

## Why
The "Skeptic Gate" CI workflow does **zero LLM analysis**. It:
1. Checks gates 1–5 mechanically (CI status, CR approval, conflicts, bugbot, resolved comments)
2. Checks gate 6 (evidence regex)
3. Posts a trigger comment and **polls for a VERDICT comment** from the AO lifecycle-manager worker

The actual LLM skeptic lives in the AO repo (`skeptic-reviewer.ts` / `ao skeptic verify`). Calling the CI gate "Skeptic Gate" implies it runs LLM evaluation, which is misleading and confusing.

**Green Gate** accurately describes it: a checklist gate that must be green before the LLM skeptic (Skeptic Cron) runs.

## Notes
- `skeptic-gate-trigger` SHA markers in the workflow body are **unchanged** — they are protocol handshake strings used by the AO worker and skeptic-cron to bind verdicts to specific commits. Renaming them would break the handshake.
- `skeptic-cron.yml` (the actual LLM orchestrator) is unchanged.
- Branch protection rule **"Skeptic Gate"** will need to be updated to **"Green Gate"** in repo settings after merge.
