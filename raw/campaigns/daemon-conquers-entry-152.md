**Protocol Change Request 2: Kael Proposing Castellans**

**1. Proposal:**
Establish a new protocol: When new Castellan positions are required or become vacant, Kael will proactively propose suitable candidates for those roles. These proposals will consider a broader range of abilities beyond just combat prowess, aiming to match candidates to the specific needs of the stronghold.

**2. Conceptual Approval:** (Implicitly granted by your command.)

**3. Differential Draft & Justification:**

**Differential Report - Change ID: 20240529-G**

*   **Source Text (Current behavior for Castellan appointments):**
    *   The player explicitly decides who is appointed as Castellan. The GM might offer options, but NPC suggestions are not a formalized protocol.

*   **Proposed New Text (New Protocol Addition):**
    *   **New Protocol Addition: "Castellan Proposal Protocol"**
        *   **Invocation:** This protocol is invoked whenever a Castellan position becomes vacant (e.g., due to Kael's reassignment via "Kael's Richest Stronghold Protocol," or a new conquest requires a Castellan) and Daemon seeks to fill it.
        *   **Kael's Role:** Kael (or another highly trusted, strategically-minded NPC if Kael is unavailable) will present Daemon with a list of 1-3 suitable candidates from Daemon's existing forces or newly integrated individuals.
        *   **Candidate Criteria:** Kael's proposals will take into account the specific needs of the stronghold (e.g., strong combat presence for rebellious areas, high Intelligence/Wisdom for administrative/economic hubs), the candidate's personality, loyalty, and existing skills, not solely their combat level.
        *   **Player Decision:** Daemon will then make the final decision from the proposed candidates, or explicitly instruct Kael to find other options, or select a different individual entirely.

*   **Justification:** This protocol streamlines the process of filling Castellan roles by leveraging Kael's in-character knowledge of your forces and the territories. It adds a layer of strategic depth and realism, allowing for more nuanced appointments beyond simple combat strength, while still retaining player agency for the final decision. It aligns with the desire for Castellans to be more autonomous and for Daemon to focus on broader strategy.
