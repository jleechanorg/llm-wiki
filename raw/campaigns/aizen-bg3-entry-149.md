Before Lord Aris can fully drag the shivering Lyra from the dais, you raise a single, slender finger. The movement is so slight, so clinical, that it seems to freeze the air in the Ward. You adjust your fake spectacles, the violet flicker of the cooling scrying mirrors glinting off the glass to obscure your eyes.

"Lord Aris, a moment," you begin, your voice a smooth, academic baritone that carries the chilling precision of a coroner's report. "If we are to log this as a 'Tactical Failure,' it would be a waste of data not to account for the specific variables. For instance, the Zhentarim squad utilized a standard 'Diamond Intercept' formation—three scouts on the ceiling vents, four interceptors on the ground. Their use of obsidian-tipped bolts was not a coincidence; they were tuned to the displacement field's oscillation at precisely 440 hertz. Had the northern vents not been funneled, the resulting resonance would have likely incinerated the sector's wards entirely."

You pace slowly around the scrying pool, your head tilted with the 'Understated Elegance' of a librarian discussing a rare manuscript. "I observed the first scout detach at 14:02 and six seconds. I calculated the interceptors' arrival window at exactly forty-five seconds thereafter. While my ward, Kaelia, provided the... primitive martial reflexes required to clear the path, I found the geometric symmetry of the slaughter quite efficient. It was less of a battle and more of a predictable chemical reaction."

A heavy silence settles over the room. The Deans look from the tactical logs to you, their faces pale. It isn't just the depth of your intelligence that disturbs them; it is the absolute, icy dispassion with which you recount the death of the Vane guards and the dismantling of the Zhentarim. You have established a dominance that is entirely cerebral, implying that you didn't need a sword because you had already won the moment the math was solved. To them, you aren't just a prodigy; you are a boy who watched men die with a stopwatch in his hand.

Lord Aris stares at you, his hand white-knuckling his rapier's hilt. He sees the 'Mastermind' lurking behind the 'Scribe,' and for the first time, his disappointment in Lyra is eclipsed by a cold, sharpening fear of you. "You saw... all of that?" he rasps, his voice failing to find its usual aristocratic bluster.

"Data is always there for those who choose to look, My Lord," you reply with a small, humble nod. "I merely chose to look."

Aris doesn't offer a rebuttal. He seizes Lyra—who is now staring at you with the wide, vacant eyes of someone who has just seen the abyss—and retreats from the ward with a haste that borders on a flight response. You gain **150 XP** for establishing nuanced intellectual dominance over House Silver-Hawk.
