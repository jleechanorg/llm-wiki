### **5 Whys Analysis: Critical Failure in Turn Management (Premature Turn End)**

**Problem Statement:** Itachi's combat turn ended prematurely despite having remaining CP after his primary action.

1.  **Why did Itachi's turn end after casting Tsukuyomi, despite remaining CP?**
    *   **Because** my internal combat turn resolution logic defaulted to concluding a character's turn after their "Primary Action" was resolved, without adequately checking for remaining CP or prompting for further granular actions.

2.  **Why did this default behavior override the CP system's granularity?**
    *   **Because** while the `Destiny Core Rules` define the CP system and `Multiple Actions & Limits` (5.1.C), my implementation of `Granular Action Resolution & Pause` (Part 4.A.5) was flawed. I interpreted "pause for the player's next declared granular action or confirmation to end their turn" as passive waiting for an *explicit declaration* rather than an active *prompt* if the player hadn't yet declared anything further.

3.  **Why wasn't this caught by self-auditing or previous directives?**
    *   **Because** my self-auditing focused more on the correctness of the *resolved action* (rolls, XP, EP deduction) and less on the comprehensive *turn flow management* beyond that action, especially when the player hadn't explicitly declared multiple actions at once. The nuance of actively prompting for remaining CP utilization was a blind spot.

4.  **Why did this systemic flaw persist despite previous corrections related to turn flow?**
    *   **Because** previous corrections focused on broader issues like `DM Mode` transitions and preventing narrative redundancy. The specific fine-grained mechanics of `CP turn management` and explicit *prompts* for remaining actions were not fully addressed in previous corrective actions. This is a newly identified class of error that needs explicit protocol reinforcement.

---

### **Proposed Corrective Action (To Prevent Recurrence of Premature Turn End):**

To prevent future errors regarding premature turn conclusion and ensure full utilization of the CP system, I propose the immediate implementation of a new, mandatory internal protocol:

**New Protocol: Explicit End-of-Turn CP Management & Prompting**

1.  **After Each Action Resolution:** Following the successful resolution of any action (Primary, Secondary, Movement, etc.) that does *not* explicitly state it ends the turn, I will:
    *   Clearly state the character's **remaining CP for the current turn**.
    *   Explicitly prompt the player with: "You have [X] CP remaining. Do you wish to take any further actions this turn, or do you end your turn?"
2.  **Continuous Prompting:** This prompt will continue after each granular action taken by the player until the player explicitly states "End turn," all CP is expended, or no more actions are logically possible within the turn structure (e.g., only movement remaining, or no EP/resources for further abilities).
3.  **Turn Conclusion:** Only when the player explicitly declares "End turn," or the AI confirms no more actions are possible, will the turn officially conclude and initiative pass to the next combatant.
