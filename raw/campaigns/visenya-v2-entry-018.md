The heavy velvet hangings of the solar billowed as the Unsullied took their positions, their bronze spears a forest of cold light against the flickering orange of the fires consuming Astapor. Daenerys knelt beside you, her touch light—almost hesitant—as if she feared your silver-gold skin might shatter like fine Myrglass. The scent of her was different from the cloying jasmine and sour wine of Grazdan; she smelled of smoke, sun-warmed cedar, and the sharp, ozone tang of dragonfire. 

"It is over, sister," she murmured, her violet eyes—so much like your own, yet lacking the depth of the Belaerys void—filling with a fierce, protective light. She did not see the predatory spark in your gaze; she saw only the 'Exceptional Beauty' of a kinswoman brought low by the filth of Slaver's Bay. Beside her, Ser Jorah Mormont stood like a wall of iron and grief. He didn't speak, but you could feel the heat of his stare, a heavy, obsessive weight that lingered on the curve of your shoulder where Grazdan’s nails had left their mark. He was already yours, though he didn't know it yet—a bear in a cage of his own longing.

You let a small, broken sob escape your lips, pressing your face into the travel-stained wool of the cloak he had draped over you. The texture was rough, a stark contrast to the slick, humiliating seed still drying on your chin, but it provided the perfect veil.
