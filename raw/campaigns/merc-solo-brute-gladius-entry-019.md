`Location: Outskirts of Greenmeadow Village, Shadowfen Moor`

The faint lights of Greenmeadow beckoned, a fragile beacon in the pre-dawn gloom. Gladius ignored the impulse to simply charge, the brute force approach appealing but inefficient for a "no witnesses" contract. He needed a clear map in his mind, the vulnerabilities laid bare. He veered off the muddy track, melting into the deeper shadows of the sparse trees bordering the village. His Shadow-Step Boots felt like a second skin, each footfall muffled, nearly silent, a stark contrast to the heavy clang his splint mail usually emitted. He moved with a predatory grace that belied his size, a silent promise of swift, unseen violence.

He circled the village slowly, the mist clinging to his worn cloak. The houses were mostly simple, sturdy peasant dwellings, huddled together. The Miller family’s home was easy to spot – a larger, well-kept farmhouse with an attached herb garden, even now a faint, sweet aroma drifted on the damp air. The Blackwoods lived in a cluster of smaller, older cottages near the edge of a shallow stream, their windows dark and uninviting. The Stonehands, true to their name, occupied a stout, stone-built dwelling closer to the village center, its single chimney offering no smoke.

No watchmen. No dogs barking. The village felt utterly, blissfully oblivious. A few late-night lanterns flickered from a couple of windows, but otherwise, a profound silence reigned, broken only by the distant croaking of frogs and the soft rustle of marsh grass. It confirmed the lackey's words: these were simple folk, unprepared for anything beyond the whims of weather or petty squabbles. Their "stubbornness" was merely ignorance of true power, their defiance a pathetic squeak against the hammer that was about to fall. A grim satisfaction settled in Gladius's gut. It would be easy. Too easy.
