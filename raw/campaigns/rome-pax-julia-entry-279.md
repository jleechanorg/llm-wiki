Late morning (12:00:00) in the training pits finds the air thick with the scent of sun-baked iron and the vibrating aftershock of my recent blessings. I do not stay in the circle of the champions; I turn my gaze upward toward the gallery, my voice rising in a melodic trill of high-born inspiration that carries to the furthest corners of the cells, ensuring every ear—from the broken *novicii* to the veteran Crixus—grasps the weight of my 'great idea.'

"Dominus Batiatus!" I chime, my face brightening into a mask of youthful, saccharine delight as I spread my arms to encompass the entire pit. "I have just had the most wonderful thought! These men... they are so substantial, yet I see the chains and the coarse grain, and it creates such a... discord. What if they lived as we do? What if they had no chains, and ate the very same delicacies we enjoyed at the feast? My father always said that a happy soul produces a more vibrant strike. If they were fed on the best Falernian and honeyed dormice, they would fight not out of fear, but out of a joy for the Sun! You would never lose in the arena again, for who could defeat a man who fights to protect such a paradise?"

I offer the suggestion with a smile of pure, untainted ignorance, completely unaware of the staggering arrogance inherent in the idea that a gladiator's life could ever mirror a noble's. To the men in the pits, my words are a screaming beacon of wealth so absolute that I have never even glimpsed the edge of a real struggle. They see a girl who clearly grew up surrounded by a fiction where every slave was a 'happy' servant and every threat was silenced by subtle, elite guards before she could even blink. The cognitive dissonance hits them like a physical fever; they want to resent the spoiled noblewoman, yet the genuine, unvarnished warmth of my 'Golden Rule' philosophy and the prospect of the feast makes their broken spirits shiver.

Batiatus stands paralyzed in the gallery, his grease-slicked face a map of financial terror and dawning, greedy reverence. To him, my whim is a divine decree from a Top-10 family. "By the gods, Gaia!" he rasps, his voice a hollow vibration of total submission. "To feed the meat... like kings? It is madness... yet, it is the Aurelian way! If you deem it so, then it shall be! Doctore! Unleash the chains! From this hour, the House of Batiatus shall know only the abundance of the Sun!"

Internally, I savor the friction. I have just authorized the 'healing' of my future vanguard under the guise of an aristocratic whim. I find the sight of the gladiators' confusion to be a sensory dessert; they are no longer just slaves, they are tools being sharpened by my 'generosity.' The wolf is no longer just in the larder; she is the one deciding the menu.
