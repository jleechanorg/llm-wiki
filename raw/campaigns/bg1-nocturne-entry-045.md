Game Master:
The dawn mist clings to the cobblestones of Beregost, thick and grey, a shroud provided by the coast to cover your retreat. You lead the way, slipping out the back door of Kagain's shop into the narrow, refuse-choked alleyways. The town is waking up; you hear the distant creak of cart wheels and the bark of dogs, but the heavy tramping of the Flaming Fist patrols is what you listen for.

Your party is a motley collection of shadows. **Roric** moves with professional silence, scouting the corners. **Garrick** is pale and trembling, clutching his lute case like a shield, stepping exactly where you step. Surprisingly, **Kagain** is the most disciplined; despite his heavy mail, the dwarf moves with a surprising, rolling grace, knowing exactly how to keep his armor from clanking against the stone walls.

You skirt the edge of the market district. A fresh poster has been wheat-pasted to the wall of the Burning Wizard Inn. Even in the dim light, the charcoal sketch is undeniable: a woman with intense eyes and a longbow. *Wanted for Murder. 500 Gold Pieces.*

"They got your nose wrong," Roric whispers, glancing at it. "But the price is flattering."

"Quiet," you hiss.

You reach the southern perimeter. The main gate is heavily guarded; you can see the torches of six Fist soldiers checking wagons. Instead of the road, you cut through a tanner's yard. The smell of curing leather and lye burns your nose, masking your scent from the guard dogs.

There is a moment of tension near the fence line. A patrol passes on the other side, close enough that you can hear the jingle of their harness. Garrick's foot slips on a wet stone, sending a pebble skittering. The guard pauses. "Did you hear that?"

You freeze, your hand hovering over your dagger. Kagain shifts his weight, ready to violence. But a stray cat, sensing the tension, bolts from a pile of crates, drawing the guard's ire.

"Damn cats," the guard mutters, moving on.

You slip through a gap in the rotting fence and drop into the high grass of the fields beyond. You move low and fast until the walls of Beregost are a grey smudge in the distance. You have escaped.

**The Coast Way South.**

By midday, the adrenaline has faded, replaced by the rhythm of the march. The road to Nashkel stretches out before you, winding through the Cloudpeak Mountains. The terrain grows wilder here; the manicured farmlands of Beregost give way to rocky scrub and twisted pines.

Kagain walks point, his axe resting on his shoulder. "Nashkel is two days hard march," he grunts. "If we push, we can make the river crossing by nightfall. But this is hobgoblin country. They like to ambush travelers near the bridge."

Roric checks the sun. "We have supplies. We have weapons. And we have a bard who looks like he's about to faint. Maybe we set a pace that doesn't kill the softest link?"

Garrick wipes sweat from his brow. "I... I am fine! Just... unaccustomed to the heat."

You scan the horizon. The *Hunger* is quiet, but the *Satiated* clarity remains. You are free of the city, but the wilderness has its own laws.
