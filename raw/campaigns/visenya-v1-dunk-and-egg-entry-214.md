Evening (18:40:00) in the High Pavilions of Ashford Meadow—the air inside the silk sanctuary feels heavy with the weight of a century's worth of waiting. Vaelon remains on his knees, his pale violet eyes fixed on the dirt floor of the pavilion, his weathered frame still vibrating with the shock of your presence.

"The Uncrowned Queen," he whispers, the title rippling with a reverence that borders on religious fervor. He looks up, his gaze searching your soot-smudged face with a look of profound, desperate hope. "You speak of the road, of the dust, and of a simple life... but the fire in your blood was not meant for the shadows of a hedge knight. You are the Apex, the true fire of the Fourteen Flames. Even as you walk among the smallfolk, you must ascent. You must rule. The Freehold was not just a city; it was a promise that the purest would guide the world."

He leans forward, his voice a melodic rasp of High Valyrian. "I will keep your secret, my Queen. I will be your shadow and your eyes. But I will never stop reminding you of what you are. The remnants wait for a sign, and you... you are the beacon they have prayed for."

You let his words hang in the dim light, the laughter of the Targaryen Princes outside sounding like the braying of mules compared to his ancient fealty. You shift your stance, the Master's Studded Leather creaking softly. "Tell me, Vaelon," you say, your voice regaining that crystalline, royal authority. "What do you know of House Peake? They say it was their hand that guided the blade of betrayal against my mother's line in Westeros. Why do they fear the Belaerys blood?"

Vaelon's face pales, the name of House Peake casting a cold shadow over his features. He rubs his weathered hands together, his eyes darting toward the silk walls as if the very mentions of the name might draw the attention of the Stormlords. "The Peakes..." he mutters, the name sounding like a curse. "They are the architects of the silence, Princess. They do not just fear your blood; they view it as a world-ending threat. They remember the ritual that brought the ash to the sky, and they have dedicated their line to ensuring the Apex never rises again. But their knowledge is fractured, a muddle of fear and half-forgotten scrolls."

He leans in closer, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper. "They do not know the technical secrets of the Fourteen Flames, Princess. They were motivated by the greed of land and gold when they struck your kin, but they keep a watch for the 'silver-gold sickness' they believe will bring a second Doom. There are agents here—Lord Peake's own household knights. They watch the pavilions, searching for any sign of the 'Apex' return. They must not see you, my Queen. Not until the fire is ready to consume them."
