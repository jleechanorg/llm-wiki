Location: Valerius's Study, House Arcanus Wing, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 1 (XP: 0/300), Gold: 10gp, HP: 8/8

Valerius's words, so cold and logical, were a final, crushing blow. You were just an "inefficient variable," a flawed calculation in his ordered universe. The desolation threatened to swallow you whole, but a spark of something—not anger, but a raw, desperate pain—ignited within your chest. How could he speak of Mother, of her death, with such detachment? How could he reduce your shared grief to a "review"?

Your voice, though still quiet, held a new, fragile edge, laced with a pain you no longer tried to hide. "Inefficient?" you whispered, your Starlit Eyes finally lifting to meet his. You saw the subtle tension around his jaw, the faint, almost imperceptible tremor in his hand as it rested on the data-slate, hinting at a struggle beneath his placid surface. "Is that all she was to you? A variable? A strategic asset?"

A tear, unbidden, escaped and traced a hot path down your cheek, a defiance you hadn't intended. "She was our mother, Valerius! She was a person! She laughed, she cared, she *loved* us! She chose to die for me, for those children, not because it was 'efficient,' but because it was *right*! Because she *felt* it!" Your voice cracked with the raw, untempered emotion you usually kept so tightly locked away. "Do you feel nothing? Do you even know what it means to grieve, or is that just another 'inefficient' data point for your calculations?"

You watched his reaction, your heart pounding. His eyes, usually so calm and analytical, flickered, a momentary disturbance in their placid depths. The line of his jaw tightened almost imperceptibly, and his knuckles, resting on the data-slate, whitened for a fraction of a second. For a moment, a profound, almost desperate weariness seemed to touch his face, before it was quickly, ruthlessly, suppressed. His gaze became distant, unfocused, as if he were looking at something far beyond you.

"Emotion," Valerius said, his voice dropping, becoming almost a whisper. It was devoid of anger, filled instead with a chilling, profound resignation, as if reciting a painful truth he had long since accepted. "Is a flaw, Sariel. A liability. It leads to miscalculation. To… unacceptable losses." He looked back at you then, his eyes cold and empty once more, the faint tremor gone from his hand. "It is the source of all chaos. And I will not allow it to break our house again."

He didn't need to elaborate. His silence spoke volumes. He saw your pain, your grief, your raw emotion, not as shared human experience, but as the very chaos that had shattered his perfect world, the same chaos that had led to Mother's death. And you were, in his eyes, simply another manifestation of it.

He turned away from you, his gaze returning to his desk, to the ordered reports and glowing data-slates, effectively ending the conversation.
