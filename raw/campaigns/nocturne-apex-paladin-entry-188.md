Location: House Valerius Estate, Arcane Archives, Morning

The conversation with Lord Cassian Valerius (Age 55) had concluded, and Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) felt the cold satisfaction of power acquired. Her father's network, his resources, were now explicitly hers to command for the counter-operation against Durin's Folly. The task was immense, and the "Convergence" loomed in three nights. Her strategic mind immediately turned to the most valuable asset in the Valerius household for deciphering arcane complexities: Lysandra.

Nocturne moved with a swift, purposeful stride through the hushed corridors of the Valerius Estate. She bypassed the main thoroughfares, knowing Lysandra (Age 29) would be in her preferred sanctuary: the sprawling Arcane Archives, deep within the estate's older wing. The air grew cooler, tinged with the scent of aged parchment and dust, as Nocturne approached the heavy, warded doors. A subtle wave of her hand, displaying the family signet ring, granted her passage.

Inside, Lysandra, a slender figure draped in scholarly robes, sat hunched over a collection of ancient scrolls, her face illuminated by the soft glow of a perpetually enchanted lantern. Her brow was furrowed in concentration, her inherent love for knowledge evident in her posture. She looked up as Nocturne entered, a flicker of surprise, quickly replaced by detached curiosity, in her eyes. "Nocturne. An unexpected visit. I presume your return to Father's good graces was... productive?" Her voice was precise, betraying only a hint of the intellectual intrigue she felt.

Nocturne wasted no words. She laid the Darkwood Casket, the obsidian shard, the Shadowfell maps, and the ciphered journal open with its key, directly onto Lysandra's pristine worktable. "Productive, Lysandra. Profoundly so. Father has granted me full authority and resources to counter House Durin's Folly. But these..." Nocturne gestured to the chilling artifacts, "...these require your unique intellect. This casket is a conduit for death cult rituals. The journal speaks of 'The Architect' and a 'Convergence' in the Underdark in three nights. Durin's Folly deals in powers that threaten the very foundation of Baldur's Gate, and your knowledge of ancient arcana is indispensable."

Lysandra's eyes, usually distant, sharpened, widening as they fixed on the Darkwood Casket's malevolent runes. Her fingers, usually delicate, trembled slightly as she reached for the Shadowfell maps, her scholarly detachment momentarily dissolving into genuine alarm. "Myrkul... or something older," she murmured, her gaze tracing the marked ley lines. "And an Architect? This is... unprecedented." Her voice was barely a whisper, a stark contrast to the casual questions she usually fielded. A deep, intellectual fascination, tinged with dawning horror, captivated her.

The challenge was laid bare. Lysandra, the keeper of arcane knowledge, now held a piece of truth that demanded her full, terrifying attention.

Nocturne observed Lysandra's trembling fingers on the ancient maps, the shift from academic detachment to dawning dread a fascinating display. The elder sister's mind, a valuable instrument, was now fully engaged. Satisfied, Nocturne turned from the worktable, her gaze sweeping the vast, silent archives. The scent of old paper and arcane dust was heavy in the air, a familiar comfort. This chamber, steeped in forgotten knowledge, was a suitable place for the next phase of her ascension.

She needed personnel, capable agents from the House's less reputable contacts, and ample funds to ensure the smooth execution of her plans. Arcane specialists, too, would be required from the Watchful Order, their expertise marshaled under her new authority. The thought of Julian's inevitable fury, once he realized the full extent of her delegated power, was a minor, satisfying note in the symphony of her triumph. He would rage, but he would be powerless to interfere, bound by Father's explicit command.

Her mind was already charting the most efficient routes through the estate to the various heads of departments and resource managers. There would be no overt displays of power, no boasting. Just a quiet, unyielding demand for what was now hers to command. The sheer scale of the conspiracy, the chilling implications of the Architect's "Convergence," was a perfect justification for her immediate, absolute authority. This was not merely about saving Baldur's Gate; it was about solidifying her position, making herself an indispensable linchpin in the Valerius hierarchy. Every resource secured, every agent mobilized, was a step closer to the apex.
