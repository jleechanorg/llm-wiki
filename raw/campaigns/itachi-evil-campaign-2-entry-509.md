*If we loosen up Infinite Tsukuyomi, will it reduce attention? We can give everyone back free will and only stop them from breaking laws or doing something chaotic.*

Itachi Uchiha's (Level 23 Ninja, Age 17) cold intellect processed the thought, dissecting it with chilling precision. The Cosmic Weaver, a fundamental force of equilibrium, reacted to *extreme* deviations from natural chaos and order. His Infinite Tsukuyomi, a state of absolute, imposed order, was the very definition of such an extreme. On the surface, introducing a measure of "free will" and "limited chaos" might indeed reduce Earth's anomalous signature in the cosmic tapestry, potentially appeasing the Weaver.

His Rinnegan, unblinking, turned to Sasuke, who stood a silent, unwavering shadow. Sasuke's youthful face, usually a mirror of Itachi's own grim resolve, was currently lost in the placid, eternal dream, a consequence of their recent triumph. Itachi then turned his gaze inward, observing the billions of minds he held within the Tsukuyomi's embrace. To reintroduce independent thought, even constrained, was to reintroduce variables. To define "laws" and "chaos" across such a vast, diverse populace would necessitate constant, active adjudication—a perpetual drain on his strategic resources, an inherent inefficiency. It risked the very emotional volatility and unpredictable impulses he had so ruthlessly purged.

The risk of compromising the absolute perfection of his "Great Unifying Order," even subtly, outweighed the tactical benefit of merely appeasing a cosmic force. He had already discerned the Weaver's "Achilles' Heel"—a direct, surgical solution. Why compromise the masterpiece when he could simply neutralize the interfering element? The Weaver's interference was a test of his Order's ultimate resilience, not a demand for compromise. His vision demanded absolute control, not a negotiation with chaos. The path forward remained clear: direct application of his knowledge to the Weaver itself.
