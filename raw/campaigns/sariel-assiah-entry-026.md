Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 9:55 AM.
Location: The Chamber of Whispers, Great Archives, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 1 (XP: 0/300), Gold: 10gp, HP: 8/8

Kantos led you into the absolute darkness of the Chamber of Whispers. The heavy stone door groaned shut behind you, sealing out the world with a final, echoing thud. The small lantern he lit cast dancing shadows, barely pushing back the oppressive gloom that seemed to swallow all sound. The air was cold, dry, and thick with the weight of centuries of buried truths. He gestured to a worn wooden table in the center, its surface thick with dust. "Here, Princess, the truth will be safe. For now."

The silence was deafening, a perfect canvas for the terrifying secrets he was about to reveal. You felt the chill of it, but your resolve, sharpened by your desperate need for answers, held firm. You knew the "Starfall Patient" was the key, the living witness to the ultimate mystery of your mother's life and death.

"The 'Quiet Ward'," you whispered, the name itself a chilling echo in the silent chamber. "Where is it, Magister? And what is its true purpose? Is it a prison for the mad, or a vault for secrets?"

Kantos’s ancient eyes, magnified by his thick spectacles, fixed on you. He hesitated, his gaze briefly flicking to your own Starlit Eyes, the mark of your shared Empyrean blood, so unlike your mother's "flawed" porcelain-fractured gaze. He had spent his life among the subtle hierarchies of the Imperium, where the perceived purity of one's celestial lineage, and the strength of their magic, determined everything. To him, your mother, despite her legendary status, was always the "Null"—a celebrated anomaly, yes, but fundamentally limited. You, her daughter, were different, but still touched by that strange lineage. The question about the Quiet Ward, coming from *you*, of all people, was a profound and unsettling anomaly in his carefully cataloged universe.

He sighed, a dry, rasping sound. "The Quiet Ward, Princess," he began, his voice dropping to a low, conspiratorial murmur, "is a place of last resort. It is a hidden annex of the Grand Sanitarium, deep beneath the Spire. Its official purpose is to house those Empyrean nobles whose minds have been... irrevocably broken by the Affliction, individuals whose madness is too dangerous or too public to be contained by conventional means."

He paused, running a trembling, ink-stained finger across the dusty tabletop. "But for a very select few, Princess, it serves a different purpose entirely. It is a place where certain... inconvenient truths... are put away with the mad. And the 'Starfall Patient' is one such truth. He is the last living witness to the Starfall Gambit. The battle that ended the war. And the battle that took your mother's sanity."

Kantos looked up, his eyes filled with a deep, sorrowful conviction. "He is catatonic, Princess. Utterly broken by what he witnessed. He speaks only in fragmented visions and terrified whispers. He cannot tell you what you seek directly. But he carries a memory, a terrible truth, that Raziel wished to keep buried forever. A truth about your mother. And about the battle that made her a legend, and broke him."
