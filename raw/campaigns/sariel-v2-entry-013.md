Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 10:38 AM.
Location: The Chamber of Whispers, Deep Archives, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 2 (XP: 1000/2700), Gold: 10gp, HP: 10/10

You sagged against the rough-hewn stone wall, clutching your bandaged ear, the relentless throb a dull counterpoint to the searing ache behind your eyes. The metallic taste of blood still lingered in your mouth, a visceral reminder of your uncontrolled power, a terrifying echo of the monster your mother had become. The memory of the Nullification Field, the glimpse of its raw, unbridled force, burned like an afterimage in your mind. You had touched the truth, and it had almost unmade you.

Magister Kantos, seeing your lingering distress, hovered anxiously. His ancient face was etched with a profound weariness, the burden of his shared secrets heavy upon him. You pushed yourself upright, swaying slightly, but your posture was rigid with a new, grim purpose. You needed to understand the path ahead, the precise obstacles that lay between you and Sergeant Kaelan.

"The Quiet Ward," you stated, your voice still a little strained, but firm. "You said it was a fortress. Guarded by the Silent Guard. Tell me, Magister, what are its defenses? What do we face? And the Silent Guard... how do we get past them? They were utterly loyal to Raziel. They saw my mother as a Null. They would never have suspected."

Kantos winced, as if the very words caused him pain. He wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting to the massive, warded door that sealed the vault. "The Quiet Ward, Princess, is designed for permanent containment. Its entrance is warded against all known forms of magic, and its corridors are a labyrinth, designed to disorient even the keenest mind. And the Silent Guard..." He shuddered, a visible tremor running through his frail body. "They are Raziel's personal phantoms. They are Apex Half-Celestials, trained for centuries in absolute loyalty, silence, and an unwavering adherence to protocol. They are incorruptible."

He looked at you, his eyes filled with a desperate warning. "They viewed your mother as the pinnacle of martial prowess, yes, a testament to what a 'Null' could achieve through sheer will. They respected her, even revered her, for her victories. But they never saw her true power. They would perceive any attempt to access the Quiet Ward, especially from you, as a direct threat to the Lord Regent's most sacred trust. They will not speak. They will not warn. They will simply engage with lethal force."
