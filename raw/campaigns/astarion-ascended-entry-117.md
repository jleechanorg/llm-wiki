You watched Lord Xykon, his formidable mind now a captive jewel, bound and silent in his warded chamber. His very presence, even in subjugation, pulsed with immense psionic knowledge. The thought of transforming him into a spawn had always been the most appealing path to absolute, unwavering loyalty, but the risk of shattering his intellect, of turning a formidable weapon into a blunt instrument, was a concern. You understood the nature of spawnhood intimately, the raw, primal essence that subsumed the former self. Your own transformation had been a different path entirely, one that had forged you into something greater than a mere thrall.

*A full vampire. Like myself.* The thought lingered, tantalizing. *Imagine Xykon, or even Morwen, not merely as loyal spawn, but as true, self-willed vampires, bound by shared purpose rather than absolute thralldom.
