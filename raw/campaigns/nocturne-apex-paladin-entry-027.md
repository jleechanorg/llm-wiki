Location: Outskirts of Oakhaven, Near the Barn, Early Evening

The sharp, insistent barks of the Oakhaven Mastiffs echoed through the twilight, slicing through the quiet serenity of the countryside. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) felt a cold flicker of irritation. The initial discreet entry had been compromised, a minor imperfection in an otherwise meticulously planned operation. This was not a failure of her ability, but a minor miscalculation of the hamlet's defenses, or perhaps a deliberate omission by Kaelen. Irrelevant. The present reality was noise, and noise meant attention.

Her mind, a rapid-fire processor of tactical information (Intelligence 18, Expertise: Military Strategy), instantly re-evaluated the situation. The primary goal of *discreet* scouting was now in jeopardy. The barking would inevitably draw someone out, if not already alerting the entire hamlet. The solution had to be swift, efficient, and above all, silent *from this point forward*. Lingering combat would exacerbate the problem.

***

**Nocturne's Internal Monologue & Planning:**

*The hounds have alerted. Discretion is no longer absolute, but damage control is paramount. Further noise cannot be tolerated. The objective requires an immediate, decisive resolution to this 'complication'.*

*   **Option 1: The Apex Silence (Lethal Force with Perfected Strike)**
    *   *Approach:* Engage the mastiffs with overwhelming, precise force. My expertise in anatomy and weak points (Expertise: Anatomy & Weak Points (Combat)) will guide the blow, coupled with a Perfected Strike to ensure immediate incapacitation of the first dog. The second will follow swiftly.
    *   *Pros:* Guaranteed silence. Eliminates the threat permanently and quickly, allowing me to resume scouting without further interruption from these creatures. This is the most efficient and controlled option. My EP pool is full, so resources are abundant.
    *   *Cons:* Consumes Energy Points. Risks attracting more attention if the first strike is not immediately lethal or if the second dog manages to get another bark out before being neutralized. But the risk is acceptable if the execution is flawless.
    *   *Confidence:* High. My skills are tailored for this. This feels like the most direct path to re-establishing control.

*   **Option 2: The Tactical Bind (Incapacitation without Killing)**
    *   *Approach:* Utilize a non-lethal method to subdue the mastiffs. Perhaps a targeted shove to knock one prone, followed by an attempt to grapple and silence it. Or a minor spell to incapacitate.
    *   *Pros:* Preserves the creatures' lives, if that were a concern (it is not). Might conserve some EP.
    *   *Cons:* Less certain and less efficient. Grappling a barking dog will be noisy and protracted. Spells might be too flashy or cause too much secondary noise. The goal is silence, not mercy. This option feels inefficient and carries too much risk of prolonged struggle.
    *   *Confidence:* Low. This is not my optimal method of engagement.

*   **Option 3: The Deceptive Retreat (Draw & Divert)**
    *   *Approach:* Withdraw from their immediate detection, allowing them to pursue in a direction away from the hamlet. Then, circle back to a different entry point.
    *   *Pros:* Avoids direct combat, preserving HP and EP.
    *   *Cons:* The barking has already occurred, so a full retreat means the hamlet is likely already alerted to *something*. It's a reactive, not a proactive, solution. This delays the operation and wastes valuable time. It’s an admission that I didn't plan for this contingency well enough, a mark of inefficiency.
    *   *Confidence:* Very low. This is an unnecessary complication to an already-compromised initial phase.

*The barks continued, each one a grating reminder of the lost element of surprise. No, a clean sweep was required. The most efficient route forward was to remove the obstacle decisively and immediately. My objective demands perfection, even if the prelude was marred.*

***

Nocturne's eyes, sharp and calculating, fixed on the lead mastiff. Her grip tightened on her longsword, a low hum of power beginning to resonate within her.
