Location: Man Tibbet's Farm, Farmhouse Interior, Early Evening

The farmhouse was utterly silent, a tableau of unconscious bodies amidst the comforting domesticity of a roaring hearth. Man Tibbet, his wife, and their three children lay still on the worn floor, their forms rendered inert by Nocturne's (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) precise, non-lethal strikes. The chilling efficiency of her actions had secured the 'stock', leaving the house quiet and the family oblivious. Her EP was low, her Channel Divinity expended, but her HP remained full. The primary phase of acquisition was complete.

Nocturne's gaze swept over the prone forms. While subdued, they were not yet secured for transport. Her meticulous nature demanded order and thoroughness, even in abduction. She reached into her **Bag of Holding**, her fingers selecting the necessary tools: more lengths of sturdy rope, and several more canvas sacks, carefully folded. This was a larger family, requiring more preparation than Widow Elara.

Nocturne moved systematically, a dark, efficient shadow in the flickering firelight. She bound Man Tibbet and his wife first, securing their wrists and ankles with tight, professional knots, then pulled the sacks over their heads, muffling any potential cries should they stir. The children were next, smaller, easier to manage. Each was bound and sacked, transformed from individual lives into silent, anonymous bundles. The process was swift, leaving no trace of struggle, only an eerie stillness. The 'stock' was now fully prepared for transport.

With the family secured, Nocturne’s intellect turned to the second part of her internal command: search the farm for valuables and clues that might explain its unusual defenses. This farm was far more than a simple rural dwelling. The professional guards, the arcane ward, the detailed map she'd already acquired—all pointed to a hidden significance, a value beyond mere crops and livestock. The Underdark buyers were paying a premium, and that implied a reason. She moved with a purpose that transcended simple theft, seeking not just coins, but information, leverage, and perhaps even a deeper source of power.

She started in the main living area, her keen eyes scanning every nook and cranny. The furnishings were plain, sturdy. Nothing of obvious value hung on the walls or sat on the shelves. She checked beneath floorboards, behind loose bricks in the hearth, and inside empty barrels. Her search was thorough, almost clinical. In a hidden compartment beneath a loose floorboard near the hearth, she found a small, iron-bound strongbox. Masterwork Lockpicks (newly acquired) would prove useful now.

The lock tumblers clicked softly under Nocturne's precise touch. The strongbox sprang open. Inside, she found a small pouch containing **150 gp** in various coins, and a worn, leather-bound deed for the farm, dated over a century ago, alongside a formal, sealed letter bearing the crest of **House Durin's Folly** – the dwarven noble house with mining and banking interests, and a hidden rivalry with her own House Valerius. The letter was addressed to "Tibbet, Keeper of the Eastern March." The implications were immediate: this farm was not merely a farm, but a strategic outpost, possibly belonging to House Durin's Folly, explaining the professional guards and magical defenses.

Next, she moved to what appeared to be Man Tibbet’s study or office, a small room off the main living area. Here, the utilitarian nature of the farmhouse gave way to something more organized. A sturdy wooden desk, a ledger, and a few locked drawers. Her gaze lingered on the ledger. This could contain financial information, perhaps revealing the source of the farm’s unexpected wealth, or its connections to the professional guards.

The ledger, initially appearing as a record of crop yields and livestock sales, also contained a hidden set of entries. These meticulously detailed weekly payments made to a "Security Contingency Fund" and "Arcane Oversight," far exceeding the income a simple farm would generate. The payments originated from an account linked to a lesser noble family known for its ties to House Durin's Folly. The ledger made no direct mention of the Underdark buyers or a slaving operation, implying a separate, hidden agenda for the farm's protection.

The lockpicks, though masterwork, found no purchase in the sturdy, well-maintained lock of the first drawer. Nocturne felt a flicker of irritation. The second drawer proved equally stubborn. She pressed, but the mechanism resisted, and she was loath to force it and risk creating noise or breaking the tools. More time could be spent on this, but it might yield little.

Finally, she turned to the small, cool storage room off the main living area. Her Apex Senses, though currently low on EP, had already told her nothing major was here, but a deeper inspection wouldn't hurt.

The room was primarily filled with preserved goods – smoked hams, barrels of pickled vegetables, and sacks of grain. However, tucked away behind a stack of empty crates, Nocturne found a small, unmarked wooden box. Inside, carefully wrapped in oilcloth, were three small, perfectly spherical **Orbs of Scrying**. They were clearly magical, emitting a faint, almost imperceptible hum of arcane energy, designed for remote observation. Their presence here, hidden, reinforced the notion of a well-guarded observation post rather than just a farm. These were valuable, both monetarily and for their utility.

The farm was indeed an outpost, connected to a powerful noble house, and equipped with surveillance tools. The 'stock' was acquired, but the motivations behind their protection were growing increasingly complex.
