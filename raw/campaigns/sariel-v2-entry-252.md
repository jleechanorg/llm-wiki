And by the heavy, iron-barred door of the catatonic **Starfall survivor's** cell, **Magister Lucian Kantos** hovered, his ancient hands trembling slightly as he clutched a sheaf of medical records.

Your humble explanation had momentarily disarmed your brothers, but their gazes—Cassian's hot with rage, Darius's cold with logic—were still fixed on you, demanding more. You knew this was the moment to weave the next layer of your deception, to transform their suspicion into a tool for your own purpose.

You took a ragged, sorrowful breath, allowing your gaze to drift towards the heavy iron door. “He is… a ghost,” you whispered, your voice cracking with a flawless performance of grief and empathy. “He is trapped in the last moments of the Starfall Gambit, reliving the horror over and over.” You looked back at your brothers, your Starlit Eyes wide with feigned scholarly desperation. “The official reports speak of Mother’s victory, of her impossible martial prowess. But they do not explain *how*. They do not explain what broke the mind of a veteran like the Sergeant.” Your gaze flickered briefly to Lucian, who offered a subtle, almost imperceptible nod of confirmation.

You gestured towards the scrolls on the table, a silent testament to your "research." "There are inconsistencies, contradictions in the accounts," you continued, your voice gaining a quiet, desperate conviction. "I believe Mother discovered something, a vulnerability, a new kind of Host tactic that was so terrifying, so profound, that witnessing it shattered his mind." You met their gazes, your own eyes burning with a desperate, convincing sincerity. "I believe that understanding what happened to him is the key to understanding her final sacrifice, and perhaps, to uncovering a hidden threat to the Imperium that she alone perceived."

Your explanation was a masterpiece, a perfect blend of familial grief, scholarly duty, and strategic necessity. It reframed your secret, forbidden research as a loyal daughter's desperate quest to honor her mother and protect the Imperium. A quiet, almost imperceptible satisfaction bloomed within you, a cold, exquisite pleasure in the flawless execution of your lie. The look on Cassian’s face, his raw fury now warring with his deep love for your mother, was exquisitely gratifying. Darius remained stoic, but the subtle shift in his posture indicated his analytical mind was recalculating, finding your explanation logically sound, even if emotionally troubling. Cressida allowed herself a faint, almost imperceptible smile, her eyes briefly meeting yours in a silent acknowledgment of a scheme well played. Gareth, still clutching his journal, simply looked relieved.
