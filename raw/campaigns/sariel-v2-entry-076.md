Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 11:25 AM.
Location: Sergeant Kaelan's Cell, Quiet Ward, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 2 (XP: 4730/2700), Gold: 10gp, HP: 10/10

The raw, unfiltered torrent of Kaelan’s memories had left you trembling, the metallic taste of blood a stark reminder of the psychic backlash. You had glimpsed it – the terrifying clarity of your mother's power, the chilling efficiency in unmaking – but the vision was chaotic, uncontrolled, and utterly devastating. Rowan continued his gentle murmurs beside the tormented Sergeant, while Gideon maintained his silent vigil, and Cressida watched with an intensity that missed nothing. Magister Kantos hovered by the door, pale and anxious. The cell was heavy with despair and the hum of Kaelan’s madness.

You pushed yourself upright, your posture rigid with a grim purpose. The raw power of Kaelan's torment, the sheer magnitude of the secret etched into his mind, demanded a methodical, multi-pronged approach. You needed to understand the "dangerous methods" Kantos spoke of, even as you prioritized Kaelan's fragile state.

You turned to Kantos, your voice raw but steady, your Starlit Eyes burning with a desperate need for knowledge. "Magister," you began, "these 'methods' you speak of – these dangerous rituals for accessing shattered minds. What are they? What are their costs? And what does your lore say about mitigating them?" You gestured vaguely between the profound horror of Kaelan and your own lingering psychic ache. "We must know. The truth is paramount, but so is... control."

Simultaneously, you turned to Rowan, your voice softening, yet still carrying the quiet authority of command. "Rowan," you instructed, "Sergeant Kaelan's physical and mental state is precarious. You must focus all your efforts on stabilizing him. Ease his torment, soothe the edges of his madness, create any measure of peace you can. Use your poultices, your teas, any of your folk remedies. Magister Kantos," you added, looking back at the archivist, "your knowledge of Empyrean Affliction and forbidden healing arts might be vital here. Assist Rowan. Every moment of calm we can give him is a window for us. We cannot risk breaking him further."

Kantos, though still visibly afraid, nodded, his scholarly instincts stirring. He moved to join Rowan, his ancient face etched with a mix of dread and professional curiosity as he began to confer quietly with the young healer. Rowan, ever empathetic, was already gently applying a soothing poultice to Kaelan's brow, his touch surprisingly firm and comforting amidst the chaos of the Sergeant's mind. The air in the cell, thick with despair, now held a faint, new scent of Rowan's calming herbs, a fragile hope in a tomb of shattered truths.
