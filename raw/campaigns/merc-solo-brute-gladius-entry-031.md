A faint, orange glow began to bloom on the eastern horizon of Greenmeadow, a silent testament to the shed Gladius had ignited. Wisps of smoke, carried on the damp morning air, drifted lazily over the sleeping village. Soon, it would be a beacon, drawing the attention of the simple folk away from their homes. *Distraction accomplished,* Gladius thought, a grim satisfaction settling in his gut. The Miller family's farmhouse, larger and more inviting than its neighbors, was his first target. Its herb garden, even in the pre-dawn chill, released a faint, sweet aroma that mixed oddly with the distant scent of burning wood.

He moved with an unnerving grace that belied his size, the Shadow-Step Boots ensuring his heavy splint mail remained a near-silent whisper against the dew-soaked ground. His senses, honed to a predatory edge, noted the creak of old timber, the faint outline of a closed window, the rhythmic, soft snores from within. The 'no witnesses' clause echoed in his mind, a personal challenge he embraced. He didn't bother with the front door, too obvious, too direct. Instead, he circled to the rear, where a small, unlatched window offered a more discreet entry.

With a fluid motion, Gladius produced his grappling hook. He flicked it upward, the rope a silent serpent unwinding from his hand, and it caught on the sill above the window with a barely audible *thunk*. A quick tug confirmed its hold. He ascended with effortless strength, his muscles coiling and uncoiling beneath his armor. He slipped through the window like a ghost, landing silently on the wooden floor within. The interior was dim, thick with the scent of dried herbs and baked bread. Moonlight, filtered through a grimy pane, cast long, distorted shadows across the humble common room. He was inside. The village was stirring, drawn by the distant fire, oblivious to the true, chilling silence about to descend upon the Miller household. The hunter had entered the den.
