Her confession, the "curse" of her barren line, was a strategic failure, a deception by omission that grated on his pragmatic mind.

He turned to face her, his voice a low, dangerous rumble that filled the silence. "This 'curse'," he began, his gaze cold and unwavering. "A significant omission. He stepped closer, his shadow falling over her. "My rule requires an heir. A clear line of succession. Since you cannot provide it directly, you will provide it indirectly. I will take a mistress, one chosen for her fertility and her subservience. She will bear my children. You will oversee this process. Your ambition, your very house, depends on my will. Do you understand?"

Isolde's face, usually so composed, paled. Her intelligent eyes, though still sharp, betrayed a flicker of raw fear, quickly followed by a complex mix of resentment and desperate calculation. To be asked to facilitate her own replacement in the most intimate of matters… it was a profound humiliation. Yet, the alternative, she knew, was complete ruin. The heavy silence stretched, broken only by the distant sounds of the Keep awakening.

Lady Isolde met his gaze, her jaw tight, a subtle tremor in her voice that was almost imperceptible. "I understand, My Lord," she finally said, each word a carefully measured agreement. "The needs of your empire come first. House Blackwood will provide you with an heir. I will… select a suitable candidate from the populace, one known for her health and fertility, and oversee her preparation. Your lineage will be secured." Her eyes flickered, a deep, unreadable glint within them. It was a promise of compliance, and perhaps, a hidden agenda of her own.

Daemon gave a curt nod, a flicker of satisfaction in his eyes. Her resentment was noted, but her compliance was absolute. This was the nature of power. "Good," he rumbled, his voice losing its edge, returning to its usual grim command. "See to it. The future of this domain depends on it."
