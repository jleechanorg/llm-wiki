Location: House Valerius Estate, Lord Cassian's Private Study, Morning

Lord Cassian Valerius (Age 55) stood before Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16), his initial shock now a cold, determined fury. "My network... my resources... they are yours. You will manage this. You will lead the counter-operation. No Julian. No interference. Report directly to me." His eyes, sharp and intelligent, held a dangerous glint, a mirror of her own ruthless pragmatism. "The honor of House Valerius, and perhaps the fate of Baldur's Gate, rests on your shoulders, youngest daughter."

Nocturne felt a cold, deep satisfaction. The words, the authority, the implicit acknowledgment of her superiority – it was precisely what she had engineered. Her father, the calculating patriarch who had exiled her, now handed her the reins of power. The irony was palpable, a subtle amusement that only she would appreciate.

***

**Nocturne's Internal Monologue & Planning:**

*The power dynamic with Father is established. The weaknesses of my siblings are clear. But there is another variable in this House, a less direct but potentially influential one: Father's wife. My stepmother.*

*She holds a formal position, a figurehead in the social circles of the Upper City. Her influence is largely confined to matters of decorum, charity, and alliance through marriage. She is not a strategist, nor an arcane scholar. Her mind is occupied with superficial concerns, the intricate dance of courtly manners and social standing. She has no direct power, no personal network that could aid me in a venture of this magnitude.*

*However, she is Father's chosen consort. Her proximity grants her indirect influence, a subtle ear to his inner thoughts, perhaps. Could she be leveraged as a source of information regarding his temperament, his long-term plans, or hidden vulnerabilities? Or is she merely a distraction, a liability that Julian or other rivals could exploit to undermine Father, and by extension, my new authority? Her reactions to my exile were largely superficial, dictated by social propriety, not genuine concern.*

*My objective is power and wealth. Her value to me is currently low, her potential as an asset even lower. Her primary function is as a part of the Valerius facade. Involvement would introduce unnecessary complexity, and her lack of strategic acumen would only hinder me. She is not a tool for infiltration or counter-espionage. She is a decorative piece, best left undisturbed.*

*My focus must remain sharp. The Architect. The Convergence. The resources Father has granted are immediate and tangible. The distractions of minor family dynamics are a waste of precious time.*

***

Nocturne's gaze remained fixed on her father, acknowledging his words with a faint, almost imperceptible nod. The conversation had yielded precisely the results she sought. The game was truly hers to play.
