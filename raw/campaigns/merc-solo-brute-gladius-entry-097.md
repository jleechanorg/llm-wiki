The sun had long dipped below the horizon, and the cool, damp air of Shadowfen Moor now fully embraced Gladius. The full recovery of his body and the recalibration of his predatory mind felt complete, a visceral hum beneath his skin. The subtle echoes of the Greenmeadow contract – the terror of Corvan, the shattered defiance of the Stonehands – were potent reminders of the exquisite pleasure of absolute dominance. But now, the immediate thrill was gone, replaced by the itch for new experience, new challenges to bend to his will.

He unslung his greatsword, the heavy blade a familiar, comforting weight in his calloused hands. In the seclusion of the copse, he began to move. This wasn't training in the academic sense; it was a physical meditation, a visceral affirmation of his own power. He swung the massive blade through the air, feeling the perfect balance, the sheer force of its arc. Each powerful rotation of his shoulders, each precise snap of his wrists, was a raw, immediate sensation, an extension of his own physical mastery. He envisioned the impact, the cleaving of bone and flesh, the utter finality of it. Then, he exchanged it for the Silent Strangler's Cord, practicing the swift, silent movements, the precise application of force, the chilling, efficient cessation of life. He focused on the feel of the cord against his fingers, the subtle give of the material. His body was a weapon, and he reveled in its lethal precision, perfecting its every deadly motion.

With his physical form humming with renewed readiness, the moor beckoned. It wasn't the kind of place where grand contracts were signed, but Gladius craved the raw, immediate experience of the hunt, the tangible proof of his burgeoning power. He moved deeper into the fen, his Shadow-Step Boots whispering against the damp earth, effortlessly muffling the occasional scrape of his splint mail against low-hanging branches. The air grew thicker with mist, the ground softer underfoot, the silence broken only by the distant croaking of frogs and the unseen scurry of marsh creatures. His senses were sharp, attuned to every subtle shift, every potential vulnerability in this desolate landscape.

The moon, a pale sliver through the swirling mist, offered little guidance, but Gladius didn't need it. He moved by instinct, drawn by an almost primal curiosity. After what felt like hours, a faint, metallic tang pricked the air, overriding the damp earth and stagnant water. It was the smell of decay, faint but distinct. As he pressed forward, the mist began to thin, revealing a skeletal outline against the moonlit gloom: an abandoned farmstead. Its barn roof sagged, its farmhouse windows were dark, and its fences lay in splintered ruin. But what caught Gladius's eye, what sent a cold, anticipatory hum through him, were the webs. Thick, glistening strands, impossibly strong, stretched between the dilapidated structures, clinging to gnarled trees, reflecting the faint moonlight like sinister silver threads. This was no ordinary abandonment. This was a nest. A hunting ground. The thrill of the immediate, visceral hunt was a potent lure.
