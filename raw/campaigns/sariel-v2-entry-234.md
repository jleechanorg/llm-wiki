The small study in the Great Archives was a place of quiet contemplation, but today, it hummed with a tension thick enough to cut with a blade. Prince **Cassian val Artorius**, his broad shoulders stiff and his jaw clenched, stood near the door, his raw fury barely contained. Across from him, **Valerius val Artorius**, ever the cold disciplinarian, watched with narrowed, analytical eyes, his arms crossed over his chest. Behind you, **Ser Gideon Vance** stood a silent, watchful sentinel, his hand resting on the hilt of his sword, while **Lady Cressida Valeriana** stood slightly to your left, her posture outwardly demure but her gaze sharp. **Rowan Thorne**, your personal scribe, hovered quietly near a towering bookshelf, a half-used quill in his hand, his usually calm demeanor strained by the palpable familial conflict.

Your explanation, a masterful performance of grief-stricken inquiry, hung in the heavy silence of the study. Cassian’s raw fury was visibly blunted, his accusations faltering in the face of your fragile, sorrowful demeanor. Valerius, his cold eyes narrowed, was processing the new information, his analytical mind grappling with a narrative that, while unexpected, was not illogical. The immediate threat of a confrontation had been de-escalated, but a tense, unspoken judgment still filled the air.

You saw Cassian’s gaze flicker to Ser Gideon, then to the closed door of the small, warded cell where the catatonic **Kaelan** was kept, his suspicion still simmering beneath the surface. You knew you had to solidify your position, to build a wall of unassailable authority around your actions. You let out a small, weary sigh, a subtle gesture of exhaustion that drew their attention back to you.

“I know this is irregular,” you whispered, your voice still laced with a convincing fragility. You looked at your brothers, your Starlit Eyes wide with a carefully crafted innocence. “But Uncle Titus… he was concerned. After the… incident.” You subtly touched the scar on your ear, a physical testament to your trauma, and a silent reminder of the sacrifice your mother made to save you, and to save **Kaelan**, whose presence here was a dark testament to that cost. “He felt I required a more dedicated security detail, even within the Archives. Ser Gideon and the others… they are here under his direct authority.”

The effect was profound. You had invoked the name of their guardian, the High Commander, the one figure whose authority they could not easily question. Cassian’s jaw tightened, his rage now impotent, trapped behind a wall of protocol. He couldn't challenge an order from Titus, especially one framed around your protection. Valerius’s expression shifted, a subtle flicker of recalculation in his cold eyes. The presence of Gideon, Cressida, and even the implied presence of Kaelan as a "security concern" was no longer an unauthorized anomaly; it was a sanctioned, logical security measure. It satisfied his need for order, even as the true purpose of your research remained a disquieting mystery.

Behind you, you felt a subtle shift in the room's atmosphere. Gideon stood a little taller, his presence now imbued with the weight of official duty. Cressida’s lips curved into a faint, almost imperceptible smile of pure, unadulterated admiration. Rowan, from his quiet corner, offered a barely perceptible nod, his kind eyes reflecting a silent approval of your resilience. You had not just deflected their attack; you had masterfully turned their own hierarchy, their own system of command, into a shield for your secrets. The exquisite satisfaction of this subtle, intellectual domination was a potent, private thrill.
