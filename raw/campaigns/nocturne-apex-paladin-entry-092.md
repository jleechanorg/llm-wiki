Location: Man Tibbet's Farm, Farmhouse Interior, Early Evening

The farmhouse was utterly silent, a tableau of unconscious, bound forms amidst the comforting domesticity of a roaring hearth. The Tibbet family, secured and silent, lay as bundles on the floor. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) stood over her secured 'stock', her movements economical, her gaze sharp and analytical. Her EP was low, her Channel Divinity expended, but her HP remained full. The primary phase of acquisition was complete.

The strongbox had already yielded its coin, the deed, and the letter from House Durin's Folly. The ledger, with its meticulous records of "Security Contingency Fund" and "Arcane Oversight" payments, confirmed the farm's true nature as a strategic outpost. The Orbs of Scrying were tangible proof of its surveillance capabilities. This mission, initially a crude abduction for coin, had unexpectedly revealed a critical piece of political intelligence.

Her mind, a relentless engine of strategic thought, processed the implications. This information was leverage, a tool to demonstrate her worth to House Valerius, to her father. The peasants were commodities, but this intel was power. And power was the ultimate currency. Her drive for thoroughness, her intrinsic need to master every variable, insisted on one last, deeper dive. The locked study drawers, which had resisted her earlier, and the general structure of the farmhouse might still conceal more.

Nocturne moved with a purpose that transcended simple theft. She sought not just coins, but deeper understanding, further leverage against rivals, and perhaps even a path to a greater source of influence.

Action: [Nocturne attempts to break into the locked study drawers again.]
Nocturne returned to Man Tibbet's study. Her previous attempt on the sturdy, well-maintained locks had failed, but now her focus was absolute, informed by the broader context of the farm's true purpose. She produced her **Masterwork Lockpicks**, their slender tools glinting faintly in the hearth light. Her fingers, usually gripping a sword, now moved with a dancer's precision, probing the intricate mechanisms. Her Coordination (14) guided her touch, her intellect mapping the tumblers within.

A soft, gratifying click echoed in the quiet room as the first drawer yielded. Inside, beneath a false bottom, Nocturne found a small, heavy, lead-lined box. Her fingers brushed against its cold surface, detecting faint residual arcane energy. This was no ordinary box. Within it, she found a single, intricately carved obsidian shard, pulsating with a faint, malevolent energy, and a ciphered journal, its pages filled with symbols unlike any common tongue. The obsidian shard felt vaguely familiar, perhaps related to the arcane pulse of the barn's ward, or something far older and darker.

The second drawer proved easier, its lock simpler. Inside, she found a small pouch with **75 gp** and a collection of several high-quality, blank parchments, along with a sealed tube containing what appeared to be maps. These were not local area maps, but detailed topographical charts of the **Shadowfell**, with several specific locations marked with tiny, glowing glyphs. The sheer implications of this discovery sent a cold thrill through her. House Durin's Folly was not just an observation post; they had direct, clandestine operations in the Shadowfell.

Nocturne's gaze hardened. This was more than mere rivalry. This was a direct, substantial threat. This was the kind of intelligence that could shatter alliances, destroy reputations, and rewrite the political landscape of Baldur's Gate. This was the true prize.
