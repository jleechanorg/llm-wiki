The cool evening air brushed against Nocturne's (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) face as she stood amidst the bundled forms of the Tibbet family. Five silent, unconscious shapes lay at her feet, secured and ready. Her Bag of Holding, now heavy with purloined gold and invaluable intelligence – the deed, the ledger, the Orbs of Scrying, and most tantalizingly, the Shadowfell maps and obsidian shard – confirmed the true, deeper prize of this operation. The peasants were commodities, but this intel was power.

Her initial acquisition, Widow Elara, remained secured in her secluded cottage to the west. Her methodical nature demanded that all assets be consolidated, all variables controlled. The logical next step was to gather all the 'stock' before moving them to a more secure temporary location away from the now-compromised farm.

She moved with a chilling grace, leaving the secured Tibbet family momentarily. Her Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate was a whisper against the tall grass as she glided back towards Widow Elara's cottage. The journey, a silent ballet of calculated movements through the moon-dappled fields, was swift. The quiet gurgle of the creek guided her back to the solitary dwelling.

Inside, Widow Elara lay exactly as she had been left, a silent, bound bundle on the worn floor. Nocturne secured the old woman’s form, the weight adding to her growing burden. With Elara secured under one arm, Nocturne moved back towards Tibbet's farm, her steps even and precise despite the load. She was not a beast of burden, but a strategist, understanding that consolidation now meant efficiency later.

She returned to the dark cluster of bundled forms near the root cellar. Six 'units' now lay together, a silent testament to her ruthless efficiency. The night was still relatively young, but the risks of delay were growing. The road patrol would eventually be missed, the farm's occupants would eventually be discovered. She needed a temporary holding point that was truly secure, allowing her to rest, plan, and then initiate the final phase of transport.
