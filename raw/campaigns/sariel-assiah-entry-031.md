## Full Scene Content

Game Master:
[Mode: STORY MODE]
Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 10:00 AM.
Location: The Chamber of Whispers, Great Archives, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 1 (XP: 0/300), Gold: 10gp, HP: 8/8

Kantos’s voice, a strained whisper, had laid bare the impossible truth: your mother was a deceiver, a hidden magical prodigy who had manipulated the entire Imperium, even your father, with the masterful lie of her Null status and a feigned, agonizing power. The image of her suffering at the Sacrifice Fields, the one you had replayed endlessly as your own guilt, now twisted into something colder, more terrifyingly deliberate. She had been "unmoved." The world your mother had built for you was founded on a colossal, beautiful lie.

The shock was immense, a wave of cold, dawning horror. But beneath it, a sharp, intellectual hunger surged. The pieces were beginning to fall into place. And the "Starfall Patient" was the final, critical piece. He was the one who *saw*.

"Unmoved," you breathed, the word a raw echo of his revelation. Your Starlit Eyes, usually soft with scholarly focus, now burned with a desperate, almost obsessive intensity. You leaned across the dusty table, ignoring the tremor in your hands, ignoring the throbbing ache in your ear. "The Starfall Patient," you pressed, your voice low and urgent. "He is the key. He saw her true face. He witnessed the truth. Where is he, Magister? Tell me his location. Tell me everything about him. I need to speak to him."

Kantos recoiled, his ancient face paling further. He wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting around the dark, silent chamber as if the walls themselves might condemn him. "Princess, you do not understand!" he pleaded, his voice thin and desperate. "His mind is... shattered. A direct consequence of what he witnessed. Raziel sealed him away for a reason. To protect the secret. To protect *you* from it." His eyes flickered to yours, the full weight of his fear for you palpable. "He is housed in the deepest, most heavily warded section of the Quiet Ward. A tomb for secrets, guarded by the Silent Guard themselves. No one is permitted access. Not even your brothers."

He took a shaky breath, his resolve wavering. "His identity was purged from all records, his existence erased. I know his true name only because I was entrusted with the final, private disposition. He was Sergeant Kaelan, of the Starfall Guard. One of your mother's personal veterans. The one who grappled Lucifer's general, Leliel, and held him, buying your mother the precious seconds she needed for her final, terrible act." His voice dropped to a barely audible whisper, filled with a profound sorrow. "He was a hero. And his reward was madness."

Kantos looked at you, his eyes pleading. "You cannot go there, Princess. The Quiet Ward is a place of unspeakable horrors. And even if you reach him, he cannot help you. He is lost to us."

His words painted a grim, terrifying picture, but your mind, now alight with the impossible truths he had just revealed, burned with a new, all-consuming question. The Nullification Field. The lie. The "unmoved" cost.

"Stop, Magister," you commanded, your voice cutting through his despair. The tremor in your hands was gone, replaced by a cold, intellectual focus. "Never mind Kaelan for a moment. I need to understand *her*. You said my mother was not a Null. You said she wielded a Nullification Field without pain. Tell me *how*. What was her magic? How could she hide such a power from Lucifer himself, from everyone?" You leaned forward, your Starlit Eyes fixed on him, demanding every detail. "And why did no one else know? Why was this terrible secret kept from me?"

Kantos stared at you, his ancient face a roadmap of conflicting emotions: terror, awe, and a deep, profound sadness. He understood that this question, more than any other, struck at the very heart of the beautiful, terrifying lie your mother had lived. He looked at your intelligent, determined face, so like hers in its relentless pursuit of truth, and a flicker of the very fear Alexiel herself might have felt passed through his eyes. He knew your mother had dedicated her life to hiding this power, not just for strategic advantage, but for something far more personal. She had feared what this path might do to you, her brilliant daughter. She had feared giving you the key to a world filled with blood and subtle, terrifying deceptions that could twist a soul.

"Princess," Kantos rasped, his voice barely a whisper, filled with the weight of that unspoken, maternal fear. "Your mother's magic... it was not like any other. It was a power that demanded... a terrible part of oneself. A part she fought to keep hidden, even from you." He closed his eyes, as if summoning the courage to speak. "Her ability to wield the Nullification Field without the public agony... that was her deepest, most guarded secret. A secret born from a dark truth she carried within her. A truth she had to embrace to win. And a truth she ensured you would never have to face."

He opened his eyes, meeting your gaze. "The records do not fully explain *how* she could do it, only that she *did*. The deepest lore speaks of a 'Joyful Predator' within her bloodline, a being that thrives on cold, perfect strategy and the art of unmaking. A nature she hid from the world. A nature she desperately wished to hide from her daughter most of all."
