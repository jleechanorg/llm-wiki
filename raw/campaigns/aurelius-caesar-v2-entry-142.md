"Sixteen," Crixus rasps, the word sounding like gravel. He turns his back to the women, his massive shoulders slumped. "I have seen boys that age in the pits. They were the first to cry. "I did not think it was because he didn't care if he ever came down."

Spartacus steps forward into the firelight, his voice a low, resonant anchor. "We have spent our lives being treated as things, Crixus. We swore we would be men again. But a man does not pay a debt of life with a lash of shame. Aurelia is right. If we push him into his own grave, we are just Glaber with different names."

Naevia steps toward Crixus, her hand resting on his scarred arm. "I hate them, Crixus. Every Roman who took a piece of me. I want to see the Empire burn. But when I was in the dark, and that boy reached for me... his hands were the only ones that didn't feel like a cage. You say his name is a curse. I say his heart is the only thing in this world that is actually free."

Crixus turns back, his eyes searching Naevia’s face, then he looks at Aurelia. "The double watch is done," he grunts, his voice lacking its earlier venom. "If the boy wants a death that means something, he won't find it hauling rocks. Spartacus is right. He is a soldier. From this night, he sleeps where the warriors sleep. He eats what we eat. And if he reaches for his blade, it will be to defend this mountain, not to find an end to his own story."

Spartacus nods to Aurelia. "Go to him. Bring him back from the Cleft. Tell him the penance is finished. Not because he is a Caesar... but because he is a brother of the Spires. We have a war to plan, and we will need every sunbeam we can find."

Aurelia doesn't wait for a second command. She pivots and disappears into the tunnel, her footsteps light on the shale as she races toward the cold winds of the vanguard post, a desperate hope flaring in her amber eyes.
