Game Master:
**--- THINK ---**
Kantos's words, "A cage one must be born into," echoed like a death knell in the silent vault. The Nullification Field, her mother's ultimate weapon, was not a skill to be learned, but a monstrous birthright, a unique perversion of celestial power. My own magic, my Starlit Eyes, are different. I can't simply "become" that. The hope of directly wielding her ultimate power, of being the weapon that saves the Imperium, felt crushed. My familiar helplessness threatened to consume me.

But then, the chilling truth about the "Joyful Predator" resurfaced. *The profound intellectual satisfaction of solving a complex problem through absolute control and overwhelming force... in subtle dismantling, in crippling ruin, in turning loyalty against masters.* That felt... familiar. A dark, insidious hum beneath my own intellect. The raw power of unmaking, not just physically, but personally. It was terrifying, a path filled with blood and subtle, terrifying deceptions that could twist a soul. Is *this* the power Mother truly wielded? And if so, is it the only way to be strong? To not break? The thought was a raw, terrifying wound. I recoiled from it, but my mind, ever analytical, couldn't deny its dreadful effectiveness.

No, I will not embrace that darkness. Not directly. But the knowledge... the knowledge itself is power. Kantos spoke of Kaelan, the Starfall Patient, who saw her "unmoved" performance. My Mirror Magic, still latent, is about replication from observation, from memory. If I cannot be born into the Null state, perhaps I can *reflect* it. Kaelan's shattered memories might hold the key not to *her* power, but to my own *unique* way of wielding *its effect*.

And beyond that, the very existence of these journals, this truth about Nulls and the Affliction... this is leverage. Against Raziel, against my brothers, against the court that calls me "the Architect of the Fall." This is a different kind of power, a power that reshapes perception and controls narratives. A subtle, intellectual weapon. I have the knowledge. Now I need the proof. And Kaelan is the proof.
**--- END THINK ---**

Kantos’s words, heavy with ancient sorrow, had distilled the truth into a terrifying, singular point: your mother, the legendary Null, was a unique horror, a "one-of-a-kind" manifestation of the Empyrean Affliction, forged from Lucifer’s very essence. The potential for that chilling, lucid "Joyful Predator" now thrummed beneath your own skin, an unwelcome inheritance from both your celestial bloodline and the father you never knew. The vault, once a sanctuary of knowledge, felt like a tomb for your own innocence.

You looked down at your trembling hands, at the faint, shimmering lines of your Starlit Eyes. The guilt for your mother's death, for your perceived weakness, twisted into a new, complex knot of fear. Had her lies, her distant love, her constant efforts to keep you separate from the horrors of her world, been a desperate attempt to protect you from this—from yourself? From the bloodlust your great-uncle Lucifer embodied, and your mother refined? The answer, now terrifyingly clear, weighed heavier than any scholarly tome.

Your mind, reeling from the personal implications, instinctively sought a point of comparison, a way to categorize this monstrous truth. Your mother had been known as a Null, the flawed clone. The journals spoke of Nulls and Joyful Predators as distinct, yet linked phenomena. Where did the truth lie? Could this terrifying capacity for unmaking exist in others?

You looked at Kantos, whose ancient face was still etched with the sorrow and fear of his revelations. "Nulls," you whispered, the word feeling colder, more ominous than before. Your voice was a low, strained question, filled with a desperate need to understand the social landscape your mother had so masterfully navigated. "You said my mother was not a Null as the world understood it. But the project... the 'Null Genesis Project'... it created others. Are there other Nulls, Magister? Real ones, like the world believes my mother to have been? And how are they treated in the Imperium, now that you understand her lie?"

Kantos visibly flinched at the word "Null," his gaze briefly flicking to your own Starlit Eyes, a subtle, almost imperceptible reminder of the prejudice Alexiel faced. Most Imperial nobles, obsessed with magical prowess, viewed true Nulls as pitiable, a genetic dead end, incapable of touching the Weave, and therefore, incapable of true power. Alexiel had used this perception as her most potent camouflage.

He sighed, a dry, papery sound. "Yes, Princess," he rasped, his voice filled with weariness. "True Nulls exist. They are rare, a distinct, barren branch of the Empyrean bloodline. They are indeed magic-inert, utterly incapable of wielding or sensing the Weave. They are... harmless, in that regard. Often scorned for their lack of magical talent, they are generally relegated to quiet, academic pursuits or administrative roles where their lack of power is not a liability. They are, at best, tolerated. At worst, seen as a genetic stain upon their lineage." He paused, his gaze meeting yours, the full weight of his sadness palpable. "Your mother's public identity, her celebrated 'flaw,' was the perfect shield for her true power. No one, absolutely no one, suspected she was anything more than a glorified sword, a blunt instrument of will in a world of magic."
