Location: House Valerius Estate, Lord Cassian's Private Study, Morning

Lord Cassian Valerius (Age 55) stood before Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16), his initial shock now a cold, determined fury. "My network... my resources... they are yours. You will manage this. You will lead the counter-operation. No Julian. No interference. Report directly to me." His eyes, sharp and intelligent, held a dangerous glint, a mirror of her own ruthless pragmatism. "The honor of House Valerius, and perhaps the fate of Baldur's Gate, rests on your shoulders, youngest daughter."

Nocturne felt a cold, deep satisfaction. The words, the authority, the implicit acknowledgment of her superiority – it was precisely what she had engineered. Her father, the calculating patriarch who had exiled her, now handed her the reins of power. The irony was palpable, a subtle amusement that only she would appreciate.

**Nocturne's Internal Monologue & Planning:**

*The path is clear. Father has granted me direct authority, bypassing Julian. The resources of House Valerius are now at my disposal. This crisis, this profound betrayal by Durin's Folly, is my crucible. But it is also a direct ascent to the power I crave.*

*My siblings. Five older obstacles, each with their own pathetic ambitions or useless distractions. How do they fit into this new chessboard? Can any of them be leveraged? Are any of them threats to my new, hard-won authority?*

*   **Julian (Heir Apparent):** A rival, but now a compelled tool. His jealousy is a constant, predictable variable. He will attempt to undermine me, but his fear of my capabilities, and of Father's new mandate, will keep him in check for now. He is a piece to be managed, not directly utilized in the field. His strategic mind is inferior to mine.
*   **Lysandra (Head Librarian/Archivist):** Her intellect is undeniable, particularly in arcane matters. She values knowledge and order. The Darkwood Casket, the obsidian shard, the Shadowfell maps – these are precisely her domain. With Father's direct backing, she would be compelled to assist. She is a valuable asset for research, a mind capable of grasping the arcane complexities that Father (and Julian) might struggle with. She is a tool to be utilized.
*   **Marcus (Hedonist/Gambler):** Utterly useless. His focus is on pleasure and avoiding debt. He has no strategic value for this operation, and his gambling debts make him a liability, a weakness that rivals could exploit. His existence is merely a reminder of the family's lesser elements. He offers no aid, only potential complication.
*   **Seraphina (High Priestess of Helm):** A zealous, righteous fanatic. Her "loathing" for my nature makes her an active enemy. She would attempt to obstruct my path if she knew the full truth of my methods or my goals. She is a variable to be monitored, perhaps neutralized if she becomes too disruptive, but never trusted. Her faith makes her predictable in her opposition.
*   **Theron (Squire):** Young, naive, and impressionable. His loyalty is fierce but unrefined. He admired my talent but was horrified by my methods. He might be swayed by a direct appeal, but he lacks the experience, the ruthlessness, and the strategic mind for an operation of this magnitude. He is a pawn, not a knight. His utility is low, his risk of being a liability is high.

*My primary focus must remain on the Architect and the Convergence. Lysandra is the only immediate, valuable asset among them for this specific crisis. The others are either irrelevant or active liabilities. I will leverage what I can, and disregard or neutralize the rest.*

*The stepmother is a decorative piece, of no tactical value. My mother's identity is an unknown variable, a potential asset or liability to be investigated at a later, more opportune time.*
