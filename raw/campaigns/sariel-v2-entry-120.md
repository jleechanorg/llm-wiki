A slow, steady calm had spread through your mind, pushing back against the throbbing ache. The psychic storm had passed, leaving you with a cool, clear focus. The truth of your mother, terrifying and beautiful, burned within you. Kaelan lay still, his moans silenced, a profound peace settling over his tormented features. He had found his answer.

You tore your gaze from his face, your eyes sweeping across your companions. Rowan Thorne (Level 3 Expert, Age 16) still knelt beside Kaelan, his gentle hands hovering, his own face etched with a mix of awe and profound concern. Magister Kantos (Level 16 Runesmith, Age 200+) hovered anxiously by the doorway, his ancient eyes wide with wonder and fear. Ser Gideon Vance (Level 5 Fighter, Age 25) stood sentinel, his hand resting on his sword, his expression grim but filled with a new, profound respect for your power. Lady Cressida Valeriana (Level 4 Expert, Age 16) watched the scene, her sharp gaze taking in every detail, her usual political mask momentarily dropped, revealing genuine shock and a dawning, almost predatory admiration for the chilling power you had just demonstrated.

You pushed yourself upright, your body still weary, but your posture infused with a cold, unwavering purpose. The long-term fate of Sergeant Kaelan, the living monument to your mother's truth, needed to be addressed.

"Rowan," you commanded, your voice steady despite the lingering tremor. "Magister Kantos. Come closer." You gestured to a clear space on the floor near Kaelan's slab. "Sergeant Kaelan is no longer tormented. His mind has found a measure of peace. But he is still broken. His memories are clear to me, but not to him."

You met their gazes, demanding their full intellectual focus. "What is his prognosis now? What is the long-term plan for his care? He cannot remain chained like this, a forgotten secret. And his memories… while no longer a source of torment, can they ever be truly accessible to him? Can he ever be made whole?" You watched them, seeking not platitudes, but the stark, unvarnished truth their knowledge and experience could provide.

Rowan, his gentle features creased with profound thought, carefully touched Kaelan's wrist, assessing his pulse. "My Lady," he began, his voice soft, "the violent psychic loops have indeed ceased. He is at peace, truly. I believe his physical decline will halt. But to 'make him whole'... as the Magister said earlier, his mind's pathways are profoundly warped. He holds the truth, yes, but he cannot articulate it, nor fully engage with it himself. He is... a silent, living archive."

Kantos, adjusting his spectacles, nodded grimly. "Rowan is correct, Princess. The lore is clear on this. A mind shattered by such a profound paradox, especially one involving the unmaking of celestial reality... the damage is, for all intents and purposes, irreversible by conventional means. Your intervention has given him solace, a miracle in its own right. But to reclaim his sanity, to reconnect the broken fragments of his consciousness... it would require a degree of healing that verges on divine intervention. He will remain a vessel of peace, holding a truth he cannot speak." He wrung his hands. "His memories are accessible to *you*, Princess, but to him, they are a static image. A profound, permanent silence."
