The splintering crash of the shed Gladius had just decimated echoed briefly in the pre-dawn stillness, a stark, violent punctuation mark in the quiet village. From his hiding spot within the hedgerow, Gladius watched, his eyes gleaming with a cold, predatory satisfaction. The Stonehands, so loud and predictable, were now reacting precisely as he intended.

From the Stonehand dwelling, the previous murmurs and clatter died. A heavy silence, then a gruff, deep voice, unmistakably a man’s, boomed, "What in the blazes was that?" Footfalls, heavy and deliberate, approached the door. A moment later, the stout wooden door groaned open, revealing a burly man, his silhouette outlined against the faint, flickering light of a lantern he carried. Behind him, a second, slightly smaller figure, likely a woman, peered out, her face a mask of sleepy confusion.

The man, clearly the head of the household, stepped out, lantern held high, his gaze sweeping the immediate area. He was thick-necked, with broad shoulders, clearly a man of the earth. His eyes narrowed, then fixed on the crumpled, broken remains of the shed. "By the gods! What's happened here?" he muttered, a note of genuine alarm now in his voice. He began to lumber towards the ruined shed, his wife following cautiously behind him, her gaze flicking nervously around the shadows.

Gladius observed their movements with clinical precision. Their predictable fear, their simple curiosity drawing them towards the obvious, was a raw, immediate pleasure. They were so easily manipulated, so utterly unprepared for the true threat lurking just beyond their sight. The rising sun, now casting faint orange and purple hues across the eastern sky, illuminated their vulnerability, their concern for a mere shed obscuring the far more immediate danger to their lives. A faint, almost imperceptible hum resonated in Gladius's gut. The pleasure wasn't in their fear itself, but in the effortless, efficient control he exerted, guiding them like sheep to the slaughter.
