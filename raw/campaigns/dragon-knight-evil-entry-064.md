'Captain,' you rumble, the resonance of your Gryphon Helm vibrating in the small space. You reach out, not with a blow, but with a firm, gauntleted hand on his shoulder. 'Look at your men out there. You lean in, your visor reflecting the guttering torchlight. 'Gratian sees them as stock, but I see them as a vanguard. If you want them to survive the reach of the Covenant, they need a reason to lift their chins. Tell them that their service to me isn't a sentence—it's an evolution. He straightens his back, the iron of his mismatched plate rasping. 'They've been treated like dogs for so long, they've forgotten they were wolves, Lord Valerion,' he rasps. He gives a single, sharp nod. 'I'll talk to them. I'll tell them the Imperium doesn't just take—it rewards those with the stomach to survive.'

He steps out into the courtyard, his voice—once a broken growl—now a booming command that cuts through the moans of the refugees. The veterans look up, their dead eyes narrowing as their captain begins to speak of pardons, of glory, and of the 'Imperial Anvil.' The muttering stops. The dead weight of their posture shifts into a grim, professional readiness. They are still ghosts, perhaps, but they are ghosts with a scent of blood and a promise of home.
