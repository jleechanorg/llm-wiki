You do not reach for your coin purse. Instead, you step further into the dim light of the shop, letting the door click shut behind you. You gesture to Roric, who dumps the sobbing Garrick onto a pile of grain sacks, then you turn your gaze to the dwarf.

"One hundred gold buys me a cellar and a sleepless night," you say, your voice smooth and reasonable. "But you didn't build a mercenary charter by renting out rooms, Kagain. You built it by recognizing opportunity."

You lean against his desk, resting your hand near the hilt of your weapon—not as a threat, but as a promise of capability. "The Harpers are dead because they let morality get in the way of efficiency. I don't have that problem. And neither do you."

Kagain's eyes narrow beneath his bushy brows, smoke curling from his pipe. He waits.

"We're heading south to Nashkel," you continue. "The mines are rotting. Everyone thinks it's demons or bad luck. I think it's profit waiting to be seized. I need a tank—someone who can hold a line while I dismantle the threats. You join us. You get an equal share of the loot, a cut of whatever we bleed from the mine owners, and the protection of the most efficient killers in Beregost."

Kagain stares at you for a long moment, chewing on the stem of his pipe. Then, a low, rumbling chuckle erupts from his chest. He leans forward, the wood of his chair creaking.

"You've got stones, girl. I'll give you that," the dwarf grunts. "Harpers are bad for business. Self-righteous meddlers. Killing them... heh. That sends a message."

He stands, hefting his battleaxe with practiced ease. "Truth is, I've got business south anyway. Some noble brat from Baldur's Gate—Silvershield's boy—went missing with a caravan near the Coast Way. I took the contract to find him, but my last crew... well, they retired early. Kobolds got 'em."

He spits on the floor. "I need bodies to clear the road. You need a shield. It's a deal. No room fee. We split the loot. But the Silvershield bounty is mine."

Roric grins from the corner, checking the edge of his scimitar. "Look at us. A regular company of heroes."

Kagain kicks a heavy iron shield toward you. "Don't get weepy, thief. We leave at first light. Until then, the cellar is safe. No one breaches my door unless I say so."

He glances at Garrick, who is curled up on the sacks. "What about the bard? He looks soft. We killing him or selling him?"
