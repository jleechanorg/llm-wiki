You have also provided a `think` command about "Who are the Triumvirate?" This is a crucial question for understanding the current political landscape.

**Regarding the Triumvirate:**
According to our established lore from the `Celestial Wars Alexiel Book` and its `Arc 3` timeline, specifically the aftermath of the "Starfall Gambit" and the "Great Autumn Offensive," the Imperium suffered catastrophic losses.

*   **Lord Regent Raziel is dead.**
*   **Prince Artorius is dead.**
*   **Commander Kyra Vian is dead.**
*   **Lord Marshal Torvath Kane is dead.**
*   **Legatus Orin Zeth is dead.**
*   **Lord Jaxom Vale is dead.**

This means that the concept of a stable, three-person "Triumvirate" as described in the later `World of Assiah` lore document (which refers to events *after* Sariel's current point in the timeline) is **not yet in place**.

**Immediately after Alexiel's death, the Imperium's leadership would be in profound crisis.**

*   **Prince Titus val Raziel** is the highest-ranking surviving Empyrean noble and Apex Celestial. He would be the de facto leader, struggling to maintain order in the immediate aftermath of the war and the immense losses.
*   The Imperium would be scrambling to appoint new Apex Generals and re-establish a governing structure. Any "council" or "triumvirate" at this moment would be ad-hoc and highly unstable, with factions vying for power in the vacuum left by so many fallen legends.

Therefore, from Sariel's current perspective, the Imperium is **not** currently led by a stable Triumvirate, but rather by a grieving and likely overwhelmed Prince Titus, attempting to rebuild a shattered command. The political situation, as noted in the game state, is "a viper's nest of factions seeking to exploit the vacuum."

This understanding of the Imperium's leadership crisis is now fully integrated into the canon.

Now, returning to the story. You are in the Chamber of Whispers, and you have just learned critical insights into the Silent Guard's protocols and their psychological vulnerabilities from Magister Kantos.

Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 10:45 AM.
Location: The Chamber of Whispers, Deep Archives, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.

Your mind, now operating with chilling clarity despite the psychic pain, disregarded his warning as irrelevant. *Ultimate folly* to a frightened scholar, perhaps, but to you, it was a complex problem, a system to be understood and exploited. You considered his words: "Only direct command from the Lord Regent – or one of his highest-ranking personal agents – can open it." Raziel was dead, and Titus, consumed by his grief and the punitive war, would never risk exposing such a secret, or sanctioning a "disgraced pariah" to access it. Official channels were useless, a direct assault was suicide. The path lay in the subtle, the unseen, the unexpected.

"Their predictability is our weapon," you murmured, mostly to yourself, your eyes tracing invisible patterns on the dusty table. "Their adherence to expected threats. Their blindness to the mundane." You looked at Kantos, your gaze sharp and analytical. "Tell me more about the Silent Guard themselves. Their specific training. Their doctrine. Are there any known rituals or social cues they respond to that are outside their combat protocols? Any specific triggers that might cause them to hesitate, even for a moment?" You thought of your mother, how she had moved through the world, a phantom in plain sight. "They saw my mother as a Null. They respected her martial strength. But how did they react to her *presence*? To her persona?" Your pursuit of truth, of ultimate understanding, was now cold and relentless.

Kantos stared at you, his ancient face a roadmap of conflicting emotions: terror, awe, and a deep, profound weariness. He had hoped to shield you from the depths of this knowledge, but your questions, so precise and demanding, left him no escape. He wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting to the unseen depths of the vault. "Princess, such texts... they are not merely forbidden. They delve into the very nature of celestial beings. Their weaknesses are not physical, but philosophical. They are... dangerous to comprehend."

Despite his warning, he shuffled deeper into the obsidian-lined aisles, his small lantern casting long, dancing shadows. He stopped before a series of narrow, almost invisible alcoves, their entrances sealed with intricate, glowing runes of absolute secrecy. He murmured a string of ancient invocations, and with a soft *thrum*, the wards dissolved. He retrieved a single, slender tome bound in white leather, its cover unadorned, its pages filled with elegant, precise script. "This is a record of their foundational conditioning. The 'Doctrine of Absolute Order.' It details their mental and spiritual training, how they are taught to suppress all deviation, all chaos." He paused, his gaze meeting yours. "And it records their observations on anomalies. Those who defy simple categorization."

You took the book, its surface cool and smooth beneath your trembling fingers. You quickly scanned the pages. The language was even more archaic than the Null Genesis journals, filled with abstract philosophical concepts mixed with cold, psychological profiling. It described the Silent Guard's training in absolute adherence to Raziel's will, their suppression of all personal emotion, and their programmed response to threats that fit predefined categories: magical, physical, or political. Anomalies, it noted, caused a "system perturbation"—a momentary hesitation, a logical disconnect that could create a micro-second of vulnerability. It detailed their ingrained reverence for raw, untamed strength, particularly that which defied explanation or categorization. Your mother, the "Null" who wielded impossible martial prowess, had been the ultimate anomaly.

You turned a page, and a particular passage caught your eye: a chillingly clinical analysis of their observations of Alexiel during the Host Wars. It spoke of her "unnerving stillness" in battle, her "coldly precise movements," and her ability to "command chaos with a look." It detailed their profound professional respect for her, seeing her as the purest embodiment of will—a being who had carved strength from nothingness. Her perceived Null status, the texts confirmed, was not a weakness to them, but a testament to an absolute, unshakeable inner discipline. They revered her not for her magic, but for her *lack* of it, believing it forced her to rely on a pure, unblemished will that transcended the Weave. You realized, with a cold, unsettling thrill, that your mother had not only hidden her magic, she had weaponized their very *prejudice* against it.
