Ashur freezes. The pat on his shoulder hits him with the force of a physical blow. He looks at your hand, then back to your liquid amber eyes, his earlier mocking facade completely erased by a soul-deep shock. In a lifetime of service to the Roman 'elite,' he has known only the back of the hand or the cold disdain of the master. He sinks into a deep, genuine bow, his voice a low, tremulous rasp. "As the Sun decrees, Dominus. My eyes are your instruments."

Later, as the night deepens and the guests retire to the peristyle, Ashur finds Praetor Glaber in the shadow of a marble colonnade. The Syrian is trembling, his analytical mind unable to reconcile the boy he just served with the atmosphere of the room.

"Praetor," Ashur whispers, his flinty eyes wide with a frantic, jagged curiosity. "Who is he? Truly? No Tribune of the equestrian order carries the sun in his voice. No merchant's son makes a Praetor of Rome walk in his shadow like a lictor. Tell me whose name I am truly serving before the weight of it crushes me."

You serve the Tribune. You serve the Sun. And if you even think of prying behind the mask he has chosen, I shall personally ensure your death is a story that terrifies the pits for a generation! Begone!"
