Gladius’s mind, though simple in its outward expressions, worked with brutal efficiency when it came to profit and power. The lackey had coughed up everything he likely knew, a pathetic display of a so-called "superior" bending to Gladius's will. The two purses sat on the table: the first, heavy, probably a hundred gold pieces, and the second, smaller, perhaps fifty. A decent sum for nine peasants, but nothing that truly reflected the satisfaction of seeing Lord Silvanus's envoy tremble. Wealth was a weapon, and Gladius aimed to wield it with the same force he used his longsword.

His inner thoughts were a slow, deliberate grind of contempt and calculation. *He's broken,* Gladius mused, watching the lackey’s clammy face. *This one, and his master too, they think they can buy anything. They think they can buy me. But they'll pay more than they planned.* The flicker of excitement was barely discernible in his eyes; this was the true pleasure, bending these supposed elites to his financial will. The peasants were just a means to an end, a conduit to drain the coffers of the arrogant.

He considered his options, the low hum of the tavern fading as his focus sharpened.
