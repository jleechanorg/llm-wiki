Game Master:
[Mode: DM MODE]
Understood. You have provided a `GOD MODE: DM note:` with a crucial question: "this doesn't make sense. even if alexiel is so powerful why would that scar him?"

You are absolutely right to question this, and it's a vital point for ensuring the logical consistency and impact of Kaelan's trauma. My apologies if the previous narrative did not make the source of his profound psychological damage sufficiently clear.

Here is the clarification, integrating established lore, to explain why witnessing Alexiel's power would shatter Kaelan's mind:

1.  **The Violation of Reality (The Nullification Field):**
    *   Kaelan is an Imperial soldier, trained in a world where magic is a fundamental force, wielded by their divine rulers (the Empyreans, including Alexiel, though she was perceived Null) and even his own Apex Celestial allies (the Silent Guard).
    *   Witnessing the Nullification Field activate—a force that *unmakes* magic, silencing the Weave, stripping Apex Celestials of their wings and powers—is not merely a tactical disadvantage. It is a profound, existential violation of the very laws of reality as he understands them. It's like seeing the sky fall or the ground turn to water. This is the "silence," the "gone," the "impossible" he whispers. For a disciplined mind like Kaelan's, this utter breakdown of fundamental order would be devastatingly disorienting and terrifying.

2.  **The Paradoxical Nature of Alexiel's Magic (The "Not a Null" Revelation):**
    *   For years, Kaelan revered Alexiel as the "Null" hero, the one who overcame her supposed "flaw" through sheer will. He fought alongside her, believing her strength was purely physical.
    *   Then, in the heat of battle, he witnesses her activate the Nullification Field, an utterly unique magical effect. And then, horrifyingly, he sees her **unleash her own, raw, devastating magic *inside* that very field, annihilating Lucifer's Apostates.** This is an impossible paradox—a "Null" wielding magic where no magic should exist. This defies all his training, all his understanding of the universe, and shatters his perception of reality itself. This is the "not a Null," "she was... unmoved," "her eyes... cold... joy..." he relives.

3.  **The "Joyful Predator" Manifestation (The Outward Horror):**
    *   While Alexiel's "joy" was internal, its outward manifestation would be chilling for an observer. Kaelan would have seen her fight with a terrifying, almost ecstatic precision, utterly devoid of the normal fear, rage, or even grim determination common in such a brutal battle. He saw her engage in the *act of unmaking*, of destroying beings, with a cold, almost detached intellectual satisfaction, a chilling grace. This perceived absence of human emotion, combined with her monstrous power, would be deeply disturbing. It's the "cold eyes" and "joy" in unmaking that would haunt him.

4.  **The Sheer Scale of Trauma & Survivor's Guilt:**
    *   Kaelan was one of the *thirty* Starfall Guard in a suicidal mission against *seven* Apex Celestials. He saw all his comrades, his brothers and sisters-in-arms, die heroically, grappling demigods and sacrificing themselves to buy Alexiel seconds. He was the *only* survivor. The immense carnage, the direct witnessing of unprecedented power, and the profound survivor's guilt of being the sole witness to such a world-altering, horrifying truth, would compound his psychic trauma exponentially.

In essence, Kaelan's mind was shattered not just by the *power* he witnessed, but by the *paradox* of that power, the *violation of fundamental reality*, and the *inhumanity* of its wielder's perceived emotional state in that moment of ultimate unmaking, all amidst the horrific loss of his entire unit. He saw a truth too vast and too contradictory for a disciplined, but ultimately mortal, mind to process.

This explanation reinforces the source of Kaelan's trauma and adds greater depth to Alexiel's terrifying nature. I will ensure this is consistently reflected in the narrative.

We will now return to the story. You have just finished casting `Comprehend Languages` and are overwhelmed by Kaelan's intelligible whispers.

You knelt beside Rowan, the full horror of Kaelan's condition laid bare before you. His moans and whispers, once just a disorienting cacophony, now felt like a direct assault on your own fragile composure. Rowan’s gentle murmurs, while a comfort, could not pierce the wall of madness that imprisoned Sergeant Kaelan. You needed a different kind of understanding, a window into the specific torment that held him captive.

Your mind, now operating with a cold, precise focus, turned to your meager magical repertoire. `Comprehend Languages`. It was a simple spell, but one that might offer the crucial insight you needed. It wouldn't heal him, but it might translate the chaos, might turn the unintelligible whispers into a horrifying, yet decipherable, truth.

You extended your free hand, palm open, hovering inches above Kaelan's forehead. You closed your eyes, pushing a controlled trickle of your nascent magical energy into the cantrip. The subtle thrum of the Weave responded, your untrained magic still struggling faintly, but holding. You felt the familiar pull, the brief disorientation, as the spell wove its way through your mind, preparing to interpret the fractured thoughts of another.

As you opened your eyes, the moans and whispers from Kaelan's lips did not cease, but they *changed*. The chaotic cacophony resolved into a torrent of fragmented, yet horrifically clear, words. You heard snippets of battle cries, the clang of steel, the whoosh of immense wings. But beneath it all, a chilling, repeating refrain: *"...silence...the field...gone...impossible...unmoved...her eyes...cold...joy...unmaking...Lucifer...gone..."*

His words, now intelligible, painted a devastating picture. He was reliving the Starfall Gambit, trapped in the moment of your mother's ultimate act. He described the sudden, impossible void of the Nullification Field, the look of disbelief on the Apostates' faces as their magic died. And then, the horrifying climax: *"...she rose...the light...wings...so many...not a Null...she was...unmoved...Lucifer...his face...broken...joy...cold...eyes..."* The last words were a raw, desperate gasp of pure, unendurable terror, the sound of a universe shattering in his mind. The image of Alexiel, triumphant and terrifyingly composed amidst the unmaking, burned in his consciousness, forever scarring him.

You gasped, tearing your hand away, the metallic taste of blood returning to your mouth as the psychic strain momentarily overwhelmed you. The direct, unfiltered torrent of Kaelan's broken memories, the raw horror of his witnessed truth, was far more devastating than any abstract scholarly text. You had glimpsed it through his eyes, the terrifying clarity of your mother's power, and the chilling, elegant ruthlessness that accompanied it.
