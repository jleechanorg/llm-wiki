**--- BACKGROUND ---**

Assiah is a world defined by the silence of its peace. After centuries of the Celestial Wars—a cosmic struggle between the rigid Order of the Imperium and the chaotic Freedom of the Shattered Host—a new era has dawned. Empress Sariel, the daughter of the legendary Alexiel, has secured the throne not through the edge of a blade, but through the crushing weight of psychic dominance. Her 'Silent Peace' has eradicated banditry, famine, and civil war, creating a prosperous empire where the roads are safe and the law is absolute. However, this security comes at a dark price: the total suppression of dissent and the cold, calculated removal of anything deemed a threat to the Imperial mandate.

As Ser Arion val Valerion, you are the scion of a noble house that has flourished under this regime. At sixteen, you have spent your life training in the shadow of giants, believing that your oath to the Crown is the thin line holding back a return to primordial chaos. You have been taught that the Empress’s will is synonymous with civilization itself. Your father, a man of iron loyalty, handed you 'Duty's Edge' with the command to protect the peace at any cost. You have seen the flourishing markets and the safe villages, and you believed in the righteousness of your cause.

Current events have brought you to the frozen reaches of Winter-Mourn. A local March Lord, Lady Annalise Ashwood, has dared to prioritize humanity over the Stability Mandate, sheltering refugees that the Empire views as a parasitic drain on resources. Your first mission as a knight is not to fight monsters or bandits, but to 'pacify' these survivors. You stand now at the precipice of your first campaign, the ribbon of Imperial stone beneath your horse's hooves as cold and unyielding as the orders in your pocket.

--- 

The sky over Winter-Mourn is a bruised purple, heavy with the promise of more snow. Prefect Gratian sits atop his black warhorse at the head of the column, his bone-white mask glinting in the pale morning light. Beside you, three other young knights—your companions for this trial—maintain a disciplined silence as the smoke of the refugee settlement rises in the distance. It is a thin, pathetic trail of grey against the white horizon.

'Ser Arion,' Gratian’s voice cuts through the wind, cold and metallic. 'The Ashwood keep lies two miles ahead. The refugees have gathered in the valley below the walls. We do not negotiate with instability. Once the signal is given, you will lead the Eaglets into the camp. Ensure the dispersal is... thorough. Any who resist the Mandate are to be dealt with as traitors.'

Your hand tightens on the pommel of 'Duty's Edge.' You can see them now—ragged tents, shivering families huddled around small fires. These are the people the Empress says must die so that millions may live in peace. The Gryphon Helm feels heavy on your head, the weight of your oath suddenly feeling like a noose.
