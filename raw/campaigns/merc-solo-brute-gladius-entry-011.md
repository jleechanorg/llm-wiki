`Location: The Winking Siren, Lower City, Baldur's Gate`

The lackey, now stripped of his bravado and most of his coin, scurried away like a frightened rat, leaving Gladius with the satisfying weight of five hundred gold and the lingering scent of fear. The Elfsong was too clean, too predictable. For real information, Gladius sought out the grimy underbelly of the docks, eventually settling into a shadowed corner of The Winking Siren. Here, the air hung heavy with the reek of fish, cheap spirits, and desperation – a far more honest smell than noble pomade.

He ordered a tankard of the Siren's notorious 'swill,' a concoction so foul it could strip paint, and let his gaze drift across the rough-and-tumble clientele. He didn't ask questions directly. Instead, he listened to the low hum of gossip, the drunken boasts, and the casual complaints. His posture remained relaxed, but his presence was a heavy, silent anchor in the room. A few stray glances from the more observant patrons quickly veered away, sensing the coiled power beneath his worn cloak.

Eventually, a grizzled dockworker, three sheets to the wind, began loudly lamenting the price of healing draughts, muttering about "Old Man Miller's good herbs being harder to find these days." Gladius's eyes, cold and calculating, fixed on the man. He let a low, rumbling grunt escape his throat, enough to signal interest without breaking the man's drunken reverie. The dockworker, encouraged by the perceived sympathy (or perhaps just intimidated into continuing his rant), grumbled on about how "the Miller family down Greenmeadow way" had the best strain, and how some folk, even in the city, relied on it. *So, not 'nobody of consequence', then,* Gladius mused, a flicker of dark amusement in his eyes. Lord Silvanus's short-sightedness was becoming clearer.

A quick, pointed word to the tavern keeper, accompanied by the clink of a few newly acquired coins, loosened his tongue further on the topic of Lord Silvanus himself. "Oh, that one," the keeper scoffed, wiping down a greasy counter. "Always trying to expand his holdings, buying up land, even if it's over the heads of the locals. Thinks he's clever, pulling strings in the Council and such. Probably thinks he's got everyone fooled." The keeper shrugged, uncaring. Gladius allowed a faint, predatory smile to touch his lips. *Overestimates his cleverness.* That was a weakness. A flaw he could exploit. The information was enough.
