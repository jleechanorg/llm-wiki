The Blackwood cottage was quiet, save for the heavy, rhythmic breathing from the rough-hewn bed where the older couple lay. Gladius stood over them, the Silent Strangler's Cord a cool, familiar presence in his hand. The disdain for their pathetic trust in isolation was a subtle hum beneath his skin, replaced by the sharpening focus of the imminent act. This was the moment of tangible power, the direct imposition of his will.

He moved with a fluid, almost graceful precision that belied his size. His Shadow-Step Boots ensured his approach was utterly silent, the heavy splint mail a mere whisper against the profound stillness of the room. He knelt beside the man first, a large shadow eclipsing the sleeping face. There was no hesitation, no wasted motion. The cord slid over the throat, thin and precise. A swift, powerful pull, a barely perceptible gasp, and the brief, futile struggle beneath his hand. The body stiffened, then went limp, the breath halting, life extinguished. He held it for a moment, the warmth fading, ensuring absolute stillness before gently lowering it back onto the rough mattress. A clean kill.

He rose and moved to the woman. Her eyes, half-lidded, flickered open, a soft, confused murmur escaping her lips just as the cord tightened. The instant of dawning terror in her eyes was a fleeting, raw sensory input—a moment of pure, unadulterated control. Her hands clawed weakly at the cord, a desperate, futile thrashing that barely registered against his strength. Then, silence. Her body sagged, the last spark of life snuffed out with chilling efficiency.

Gladius straightened, the cord still coiled in his hand, its recent use a warm impression. Two more souls. Erased. The room was utterly silent once more, broken only by the distant, dying embers of the shed fire and the sounds of the waking village. A cold, precise satisfaction settled in his gut, a subtle tremor of power. The "no witnesses" clause was being honored with chilling perfection, each death a testament to his burgeoning dominance. The Blackwood family, so fiercely independent, had simply been too weak to resist. He had sealed the house, and now he had silenced its occupants. The Stonehands awaited.
