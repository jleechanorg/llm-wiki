Decimus wipes a smear of ancient soot from his scarred cheek, his expression darkening as he considers your question. The veterans of the Tenth lean in, the bioluminescent moss casting a ghostly green pallor over their new, blackened Sullan plate. 

"The 'High Command' is a shattered mirror, Domina," Decimus rasps, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper that resonates within the consecrated silence of the Favissa. "Most of the old **Legati** (deputy commanders) are either fertilizing the fields of Etruria or have taken the 'Gilded Lie' to save their villas. But there are still those who remember the Bull."

He gestures vaguely toward the north, toward the heart of the city. "**Marcus Aemilius Lepidus**. He is a man of the **Populares** by blood, though currently he plays the part of a Sullan lackey to keep his head. He has wealth, and more importantly, he has the ear of the disgruntled 'silent majority' in the Senate. If you can secure his **Amicitia** (political alliance), you gain a legal shield that even Sulla's dogs might hesitate to bite."

Decimus then turns his gaze toward the shadows of the southern conduits. "And then there are the **Samnites**. They are the iron of Italy, and they hate Sulla with a fervor that matches your Soul Burn. They are currently regrouping in the hills near Neapolis. They don't care for Roman law, but they worship strength. If the daughter of Caesar appears at their camp with the Tenth at her back, you won't just have allies; you'll have an army of wolves."

He pauses, his eyes locking onto yours with a mix of warning and hope. "But remember, Gaia: in this Rome, a 'strong Roman' is often just a butcher waiting for a better price. Lepidus is a snake who knows how to dance; the Samnites are a fire that might consume us as easily as our enemies. Which thread shall we pull first?"
