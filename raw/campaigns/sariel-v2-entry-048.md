Game Master:
[Mode: STORY MODE]
Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 11:05 AM.
Location: Sergeant Kaelan's Cell, Quiet Ward, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 2 (XP: 1490/2700), Gold: 10gp, HP: 10/10

You stood, frozen, the small orb of `Light` illuminating the devastating reality of the "Starfall Patient." Sergeant Kaelan, a gaunt, skeletal figure, lay bolted to his slab, his features twisted into a mask of perpetual agony, his eyes wide and unblinking, staring at a horror only he could see. The whispers and moans, you realized with a fresh wave of despair, were his own. He was trapped in an endless, screaming loop of memory.

The sight of him, so utterly broken, clenched your gut. You needed Rowan. His gentle empathy, his knowledge of healing, might be the only balm for a mind so shattered. And you needed Gideon's strength, Cressida's cunning – you would need all of them to navigate this treacherous path. Your authority over the Silent Guard was fragile, a delicate balance of paradox and perceived higher duty. You had to leverage it, to expand it, to make them accustomed to your direct command.

You turned your back on the unsettling sight of Kaelan, facing the heavy iron door that sealed you in. You raised your voice, forcing it steady despite the tremor in your body, projecting it to penetrate the thick stone. "Magister Kantos!" you commanded, the words echoing in the oppressive silence of the cell. "And you, Silent Guard!"

You paused, allowing your authority to settle, then issued your orders with the same cold, absolute precision that had opened the door. "My companions, Ser Gideon Vance, Lady Cressida Valeriana, and Rowan Thorne, are awaiting summons from you, Magister. Bring them here. Immediately." You then added, a subtle assertion of your priority, "And I require Rowan Thorne to be brought here first, with all haste. His healing expertise is crucial. On the double." You emphasized the "on the double," a direct military cadence, a test of their obedience to your new authority, to subtly gauge how much control you truly held over beings once loyal only to Raziel himself.

Silence. No sound from beyond the door. No distant footsteps. The heavy stone remained impassive. You could almost feel their internal processing, the clash of your new command against their standing orders to "hold this perimeter" and "allow no one to enter or leave." The taller Silent Guard’s masked head, visible through a narrow crack in the door, tilted almost imperceptibly, a phantom of inquiry. They were processing, yes, but not complying. Not yet.
