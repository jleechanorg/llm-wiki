Horgus Gwent exhales a heavy, wine-soaked breath, his eyes glazed as they track the movement of your brush through your silken, raven-black hair. He doesn't notice the way your hand tightens on the silver handle, nor the predatory stillness that has settled into your spine.

The Soul Coin lies on the rug near your bare foot, the metal pulsing with a faint, sickly heat that makes your skin crawl. Beside it, the iron-bound scroll case—his invitation—rests like a discarded bone.

Horgus fumbles with his half-empty wine flask, his movements sloppy and uncoordinated. He is drifting in that dangerous space between ecstatic adoration and a drunken stupor. You have perhaps three minutes before he either passes out or demands your attention back in the sheets.
