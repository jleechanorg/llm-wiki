Game Master:
[Mode: STORY MODE]
Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 11:55 AM.
Location: Sergeant Kaelan's Cell, Quiet Ward, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 2 (XP: 4730/2700), Gold: 10gp, HP: 10/10

You leaned closer, your heart pounding, your gaze searching his tormented features. His gibbering was faster now, a frantic litany of disjointed words, but woven through it, you distinctly heard: *"…again…the silence…her…unmoved…why?…why did she…?"* The betrayal, raw and re-triggered, pulsed in his fragmented thoughts. His eyes, wide with a terror that seemed newly kindled, darted around the cell as if he expected your mother’s six-winged form to materialize before him. He was reacting to the *paradox* you had just created, the re-introduction of the unmaking, not just its memory. A desperate energy, a flicker of something almost like a plea for understanding, emanated from him. The wound in his mind was open, raw, and perhaps, for the first time, accessible in a new way.

You took a deep, shuddering breath, the metallic taste of blood acrid in your mouth. Your own body still trembled with the aftershocks of the replicated Nullification, but your mind, now cold and sharp, pushed past the pain. Kaelan’s re-triggered agony was a raw, open wound, and you had to act now, while the wound was exposed. You locked your Starlit Eyes onto his, trying to meet his unseeing gaze, to penetrate the chaos with your words.

"Sergeant Kaelan," you commanded, your voice raw, but clear and unwavering, cutting through the gibbering. "Remain calm. What you saw… it was real. But what you *thought* you saw… that was a lie."

You paused, letting your words sink into the churning maelstrom of his mind, trying to provide a new anchor, a different truth. "My mother, Alexiel, was more powerful than you could have imagined. She was not a Null. She wielded magic, yes, an impossible power to silence it. And she was a different person than you thought. Not just a hero, but a force of absolute, chilling clarity. A being who could move with impossible precision, who could unmake anything, anyone, with a terrible, elegant satisfaction."

Your voice softened, yet held an unyielding conviction. "She needed to be that. She needed to be *unmoved*. She needed to be that to win. Without her, without that power, that ruthlessness… you all would have died for nothing. Lucifer would have won. You understand? She did what was necessary to preserve the Imperium. To preserve you." You leaned in closer, your voice a desperate, pleading whisper, trying to re-contextualize the betrayal he had witnessed. "You must accept it, Sergeant. That was the cost. That was the truth."

Kaelan’s thrashing subsided, his body still rigid, but the frantic gibbering quieted to a low, guttural murmur. His eyes, wide and unblinking, seemed to flicker, not with the previous terror, but with a new, profound confusion, a desperate attempt to reconcile your words with the horrifying truth burned into his mind. The psychic pressure in the room intensified, as his shattered consciousness grappled with the paradox you had just presented: his beloved commander, the ultimate hero, was also a monster, a deceiver, yet one who had acted with cold, necessary purpose. The contradiction was immense, almost unbearable.

Rowan, who had been watching with wide, horrified eyes, gasped, his hand flying to his mouth. Gideon, grim and stoic, visibly tensed, his hand on his sword, his gaze fixed on Kaelan, ready for any outburst. Cressida stared, her sharp mind processing the sheer audacity of your attempt, the terrifying psychological gamble you had just taken.

You pressed your hand onto his trembling, chained one, a desperate attempt to anchor your words. "It was not a betrayal, Sergeant," you insisted, your voice raw with conviction, pushing past your own pain. "She mourned you. She mourned the Starfall Guard. I saw her after the battle, on the silent plaza, surrounded by the bodies. She was not untouched. She was in agony. She grieved for the cost. She grieved for all of you who died."

You leaned in closer, your voice dropping to a near-whisper, trying to plant a new, healing seed in his shattered mind. "Perhaps… perhaps she even visited you here. In this very ward. Before she became the 'Mortal Queen,' before the legend was set. She might have come here to mourn the last witness to her truth." You met his unseeing gaze, trying to penetrate the chaos with a desperate hope. "Try to remember, Sergeant. Try to remember if she visited you. Try to remember if Alexiel, your commander, truly abandoned you."

Kaelan’s eyes, wide and unblinking, suddenly darted, not with terror, but with a flicker of something… distant. The frantic gibbering slowed, replaced by a low, almost normal groan. His body, previously rigid, seemed to relax by a fraction. A single, silent tear, hot and thick, traced a path down his gaunt cheek. He didn't speak, but the subtle tremor in his hand, clasped in yours, intensified. He was searching. For the first time, his tormented mind seemed to be trying to remember something *other* than the horror.

"Rowan!" you commanded, your voice sharp with urgency, cutting through the profound silence that had fallen over the cell. You didn't look away from Kaelan, your Starlit Eyes fixed on his, willing him to remember, to find that fragile piece of hope. "Now! He's searching. He's vulnerable. Focus your healing. Not just his body, Rowan, but his mind. Soothe the chaos, but keep the pathway open. Ease his pain, guide his thoughts. He needs to find that memory. We need him to remember."

Rowan Thorne (Level 3 Expert, Age 16), his gentle face etched with a mix of awe and profound concern, immediately knelt closer to Kaelan. His hands, already practiced at soothing physical wounds, now moved with an almost spiritual grace over the Sergeant's brow. He murmured softly, a low, comforting chant of simple kindness and reassurance, mixing his herbal poultices with whispered promises of peace. His touch was a gentle balm on Kaelan's exposed psyche, a counterpoint to the sharp, cold precision of your words. Gideon (Level 5 Fighter, Age 25) stood sentinel, his hand now hovering over his sword, his gaze fixed intently on Kaelan. He was ready for any sudden shift, but his own presence was an anchor, a silent promise of protection that permeated the cell. Cressida (Level 4 Expert, Age 16) watched, her sharp eyes taking in every detail, her expression a mix of awe at your audacity and a dawning understanding of the immense, subtle power you wielded. Magister Kantos, pale and trembling by the door, simply stared, witnessing a profound violation of both scholarly and natural law, a truth more terrifying than any he had read in his forbidden texts.

You maintained your unwavering gaze on Kaelan. The tear on his cheek was still warm. His breathing, though shallow, was more even now. He was still lost in his madness, but the agonizing loop of terror had been momentarily disrupted. A fragile new pattern was emerging, one born from your words, from the new paradox you had presented him: the hero who grieved, the monster who loved. You pressed your mental will against his, not to command, but to encourage, to guide, to insist he find that sliver of human connection in the vast, cold void of his shattered mind. This was a battle fought on the most intimate of fields, a desperate gamble to reconstruct a memory from the raw fragments of grief and betrayal.
