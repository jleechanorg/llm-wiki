Marcus and Quintus offer stiff, military bows, their teenage faces a map of genuine awe and a deep-seated, instinctual fear. They see the goddess beneath the silk, yet they move toward you with a tentative warmth, remembering the kindness you've shown them during your previous 'visits.' Tullia stands slightly behind them, her fingers twisting the hem of her palla as she watches you with a reverence that borders on the religious. They know the name you do not speak; they know the fire you carry. 

"Oh, look at you all!" you chirp, your voice a melodic trill that seems to pull the fear right out of their lungs. You reach out with a gold-ringed hand, patting Quintus's cheek with a doting, 'provincial' affection that masks the predator's touch. "You've all grown so much since the last time father sent me gifts for you! Marcus, you're looking like a real centurion already. And Tullia, that blue is simply divine on you. Do come and sit! We were just about to see what kind of dreams the poppy brings today!"

You draw Lady Licinia into a private orbit away from the others, the aromatic fog providing a perfect, hazy screen. "You know, Licinia," you murmur, your voice dropping to a silken, conspiratorial whisper that carries the weight of a decree. "Capua is so full of surprises. Just last night, I found a little ghost in my garden. One of Marcus Crassus's shadows, trying to count my jewelry." You let out a soft, silvery laugh that doesn't reach your eyes. "I spent the most delightful hour stripping his mind bare. It's fascinating, really, how quickly a man's professional discipline dissolves when he realizes the 'law' has forgotten his name."

You lean in closer, the radiant heat of your Julian perfection making the air between you vibrate as you allow the mask to fracture for her eyes alone. "Help me have my fun, Licinia. Play the sophisticated cousin. Keep the Praetor blind. If you do, the Julian sun will find whatever you desire—gold, names, or safety. But if my play is spoiled..." You offer a smile that is as beautiful as it is lethal. "I should be very, very upset. And I think you've seen what happens to ghosts who upset me."

Licinia’s face is the color of bleached bone as she accepts the pipe with a hand that has gone completely numb, her gaze locked on yours with the shattered compliance of a woman who has just seen her own executioner and realized she is in love with the blade.
