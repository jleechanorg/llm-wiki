Kantos stared at you, his mouth working silently. His gaze darted from your unwavering Starlit Eyes to the accusing silent shelves around him. The conflict in him was immense: the physical terror of the secret against the intellectual horror of its loss. He was trapped between the very principles he had devoted his life to. A long, shuddering breath escaped him, and slowly, agonizingly, his rigid posture began to crumble. He closed his eyes, a profound, weary resignation settling upon his ancient face. The fight had gone out of him. He simply nodded, a single, almost imperceptible dip of his head. He had made his choice.

You felt a surge of cold, grim satisfaction. The threat had worked. He would speak. But the vast, open expanse of the scriptorium, with its endless shelves and occasional passing scholars, was no place for a truth of this magnitude. This knowledge was too dangerous to be overheard, too fragile to be exposed.

"Not here, Magister," you whispered, your voice firm and decisive, cutting through his resignation. You swept your hand, indicating the towering, silent witnesses around them. "This is not a place for such... delicate history. Where can we speak that is truly private? A place where walls have ears that are truly deaf?"

Kantos opened his eyes, a flicker of renewed fear, mixed with a hint of professional understanding, crossing his features. He glanced around the scriptorium, then nodded slowly, a new, almost conspiratorial glint in his ancient eyes. "Follow me, Princess," he rasped, his voice barely audible. "There are... deeper archives. Vaults where the silence is absolute."

He turned and, with a surprising spryness for his age, led you away from the central scriptorium, deeper into the shadowy labyrinth of the Archives. He navigated narrow, forgotten passages, his hand tracing ancient runes on unseen walls. The air grew colder, drier, smelling not just of old paper, but of dust and contained magic. Finally, he stopped before a massive, unadorned stone door, almost invisible in the gloom, its surface humming with faint, ancient wards. He murmured a sequence of guttural, forgotten phrases, and the door groaned open, revealing a circular chamber plunged in absolute darkness.

"The Chamber of Whispers," Kantos said, his voice hushed with a familiar reverence. "Designed to hold truths that would crack the very foundation of the Imperium. No sound, no light, no magic can pass its walls. It is a tomb for secrets." He lit a small, steady lantern with a snap of his fingers, the light barely making a dent in the oppressive darkness. "Here, Princess, the truth will be safe. For now." He gestured to a worn wooden table in the center, its surface thick with dust. "What is it you wish to know?"
