You sank onto a dusty wooden chair, the lingering psychic strain a dull throb behind your eyes. The metallic taste of blood still acrid in your mouth. Gideon immediately moved to stand guard by the door, his posture rigid, his gaze fixed on the shadows beyond. Rowan, his gentle features etched with profound concern, approached, offering a calming herbal tea from his kit. Cressida, her sharp eyes missing nothing, leaned against a towering bookshelf, her usual courtly composure replaced by a raw, intelligent awe. Even Magister Kantos, pale and trembling, found a stool and sat, his eyes wide with a mix of fear and profound, scholarly fascination.

The air in the small study was heavy with the unspoken. They had seen your command over the Silent Guard, witnessed your struggle in Kaelan’s cell, and heard your raw, fragmented revelations. But the full, terrifying scope of the truth remained unarticulated.

You took a deep, shuddering breath, the tea warm in your hands, a small anchor in the storm. "We have accessed the truth," you began, your voice raw but steady, cutting through the silence. "Sergeant Kaelan's mind is at peace, for now. But what he saw... what *I* saw... it changes everything. My mother, Alexiel, was not merely a hero. She was the architect of the greatest deception in Imperial history." You met each of their gazes in turn, conveying the immense weight of the revelation. "She was not a Null. She wielded magic, a power to unmake it, born from Lucifer's own essence. And she found... a chilling satisfaction in its perfect use. A power linked to the Empyrean Affliction itself. This knowledge... this secret... it is a weapon that could unravel the court, destroy our house, or, if wielded with ruthless precision, perhaps even save the Imperium."

You swept your gaze over their faces, seeing the dawning horror, the stunned disbelief, the flashes of strategic understanding. "The legends are lies. The very foundation of our world is built on a deception. We now hold the blueprint to that lie, and to a terrifying power. The question is... what do we do now? What are the implications of this truth for our position? For the Imperium itself? And how do we wield such a dangerous, two-edged sword?"

You paused, the collective shock palpable in the dim light of the study. You felt their minds grappling with the enormity of it, the betrayal of their most cherished legend. But before diving into the political maelstrom, a more pressing, chilling truth demanded articulation.

"Beyond the Imperium's foundation," you continued, your voice dropping to a low, raw whisper, "this truth changes everything about *her*. About my mother. About what she truly was. And about what that means for... for me." You pressed your trembling fingers to your temples, the metallic taste of blood a fresh reminder of the psychic backlash. "The journals spoke of a 'chilling capacity for unmaking and control.' A profound intellectual satisfaction in dismantling, in orchestrating ruin. Kaelan's memories... they confirmed it. She was... unmoved. She saw the perfect execution, while her comrades died believing she was suffering."

You met their gazes, your own eyes burning with a desperate, terrifying honesty. "Was her grief for Artorius real? Was her love for us, for me, real? Or was it all part of the mask? A necessary variable in her grand deception? And this 'chilling capacity'... this refined Affliction... it's in my blood. Is it my inheritance? Am I destined to become a monster, too? A tool of terrifying precision, devoid of genuine human connection?" Your voice cracked, the raw personal pain of this discovery, so long suppressed, finally breaking through.

Rowan (Level 3 Expert, Age 16) immediately moved closer, his gentle hand hovering, his face a mask of profound empathy. Gideon (Level 5 Fighter, Age 25) clenched his jaw, his grim resolve hardening, his gaze fixed on you with an intensity that spoke of a desperate need to protect you from this terrifying self-doubt. Cressida (Level 4 Expert, Age 16) watched, her sharp eyes narrowed, processing the profound personal implications, her usual predatory gleam now replaced by a deep, almost horrified concern for her friend.
