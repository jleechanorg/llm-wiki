Gladius watched the man squirm, the silent pressure far more effective than any shouted threat. The lackey’s face was a mottled red, his eyes darting frantically between Gladius and the two purses. It was a pleasant sight, the supposed superior brought low by simple, unyielding presence. Gladius had already established who held the real power in this interaction. Now, it was time to extract the details.

"Price comes after details," Gladius stated, his voice flat, cutting through the lackey’s anxious silence. He leaned forward again, just enough to convey renewed, focused menace. "What's the insult? Who are these families? And are there any local constables with a spine in this 'Greenmeadow'?" His tone made it clear that any attempt at deception would be met with swift, unpleasant consequences.

The lackey swallowed hard, his Adam's apple bobbing. He finally relinquished the second purse, pushing it across the table with a trembling hand, a clear sign of surrender. "The insult... they refused Lord Silvanus's request to build a new granary on their land. Said it would 'ruin their harvest' and 'spoil the village well.' Impudent wretches! And they spoke ill of his generosity to passing traders!" He spat the words, a flash of his master’s arrogance peeking through his fear.

"The families are the Millers, the Blackwoods, and the Stonehands. Stubborn, self-sufficient types. They believe they're untouchable, always have. As for the watch... Greenmeadow is a quiet place. The local sheriff, a man named Borin, is... amenable. A few gold pieces, and he looks the other way. He's been taken care of, so there will be no interference from the law." The man practically babbled, desperate to unload the information, to make himself useful and thereby less of a target. He seemed to shrink in his expensive silks, his earlier bravado completely forgotten.
