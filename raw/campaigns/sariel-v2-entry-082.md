He spoke of rare herbs for mental clarity, of specific breathing exercises from forbidden ascetic texts, of low-frequency hums that might quiet psychic static. He worked with a meticulous, desperate focus, outlining a series of mental and physical preparations.

You listened, absorbing every detail, even as your mind began its own rigorous preparations. You closed your eyes, focusing on the calm, steady rhythm of your breath. You pushed back against the lingering psychic ache, trying to enforce stillness on the internal storm. You envisioned your mind as a fortress, its walls strengthened, its gates secured, preparing to admit only the precise memories you sought. This was not a test of strength, but of control. You would attune yourself to Kaelan's trauma, yes. But you would not allow it to consume you. The silence of the cell was punctuated by Rowan's soft murmurs, Kantos's hushed instructions, and the faint, low moans of Sergeant Kaelan, a macabre symphony of preparation.

After several minutes, you opened your eyes. You turned to Kantos, your gaze sweeping over the strange, arcane components. Your voice was steady, infused with a cold, almost surgical precision. "Magister," you stated, cutting through the low hum of preparation. "Are your preparations complete? Are we ready to begin? Kantos looked up, his ancient face etched with a mix of concentration and profound trepidation. He glanced at the delicate components, then at the tormented Sergeant, and finally, at your own pale, determined face. He swallowed hard. "Princess," he rasped, his voice barely audible, "the preparations are... sufficient.
