Game Master:

Understood. You have provided a `GOD MODE` directive: "DM note: wait forget the cover. I want people to know Itachi did it."

I confirm my understanding: You wish to explicitly override the narrative decision Itachi just made (to create a subtle cover story implying a chaotic internal struggle) and instead have the Uchiha Clan Massacre be **publicly known as Itachi's sole act.** This means Itachi will ensure his culpability is undeniable when Konoha discovers the scene.

This is a significant shift from the implication of Itachi operating "from the deepest shadows" (his mid-term goal) and influencing a "clean" narrative. It drastically alters his public persona and the immediate trajectory of his relationship with Konoha.

**Implications of this Change:**

1.  **Shift in Mid-Term Goal: "Konoha's Hidden Hand":** If Itachi is publicly known as the perpetrator, his goal of "manipulat[ing] Konoha's power structures from the deepest shadows" becomes far more challenging, if not impossible, in its current form. He would be an overt, known rogue ninja, hunted by Konoha, rather than an unseen architect pulling strings. His influence would primarily be through terror, disruption, or direct, overt attacks, rather than subtle guidance.
2.  **Impact on Long-Term Goal: "The Great Unifying Order":** His ultimate ambition might also need re-contextualization. Achieving a "unified order under a single, hidden authority—his own" is harder when he is the world's most infamous criminal. It suggests his path to control would be through overt force and fear, rather than insidious manipulation.
3.  **Sasuke's Path:** This would solidify Sasuke's hatred and single-minded vengeance even more directly, as the target of his wrath would be undeniably clear from the outset.
4.  **Konoha's Reaction:** Instead of a contained investigation leading to a carefully constructed lie (which Danzo might have assisted with for his own ends), Konoha's immediate reaction would be one of profound shock, betrayal, and a widespread, public hunt for Itachi Uchiha. He would be branded a S-rank traitor instantly.

**Narrative Integration & Itachi's Motivation for this Decision:**

To make this consistent with Itachi's "evil" nature and his "grand experiment" of self-forging (as established in his revised justification), this choice could signify:
*   **Ultimate Severance:** He desires no ambiguity; the break from Konoha, from his past, must be absolute and undeniable. He *wants* to be seen as the ultimate monster to fully shed any lingering emotional ties or potential for return.
*   **Provocation:** He intends for this open act of terror to provoke specific reactions from Konoha, forcing their evolution (or decay) in a way he can still subtly guide from afar, albeit through different means (e.g., creating power vacuums, testing their resolve, inciting fear).
*   **Public Forging:** He views being publicly reviled as the ultimate act in forging his unfeeling resolve. He *chooses* to embody absolute villainy in the eyes of the world, rather than hide in the shadows.

Do you wish to proceed with Itachi being publicly known as the perpetrator, accepting these narrative implications and the re-contextualization of his mid-term and long-term goals as discussed above? And which of the suggested motivations (Ultimate Severance, Provocation, Public Forging) best reflects Itachi's specific intent for this choice, or do you have an alternative?

Once confirmed, I will narrate Itachi's actions accordingly and continue the story.
