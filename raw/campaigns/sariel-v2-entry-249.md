The air in the private council chamber of the Zenith Spire was thick with unspoken tension. The last of the twenty parchment strips, each bearing a numerical result, settled onto the polished obsidian table. **Sariel Arcanus** sat at its head, her small frame dwarfed by the ornate Empyrean chair. Her eyes, usually so distant, were fixed on the numbers, a slight tremor in her hands. The rolls represented the cumulative outcomes of the subtle influence checks her small faction had been making across the Imperial court for their proposed archival reforms.

**Ser Gideon Vance** stood as still as a statue behind Sariel, his hand resting on the pommel of his longsword, his gaze sweeping the room. His presence was a silent, unyielding shield against the court’s judgment.

**Lady Cressida Valeriana**, ever observant, leaned forward, her sharp eyes missing nothing. "House Aurelian responds with a seventeen," she murmured, tapping the first parchment. "Neutral, perhaps cautious support. Acceptable, for them."

**Gareth Ashfeld**, Sariel’s personal scribe and constant shadow, quietly moved to her side, placing a warm mug of herbal tea beside her. His kind eyes conveyed a silent, unwavering support. "Take a breath, Sariel," he whispered, his voice a gentle comfort.

Across the table, **Darius val Artorius**, immaculate in his unadorned uniform, picked up another strip. "A four for House Solara," he stated, his voice devoid of emotion, "meaning outright dismissal. As expected. Their traditionalist stance on archival access is well-documented." He placed it back down with a precise, almost clinical movement.

**Cassian Arcanus**, Darius's twin, scoffed, leaning back in his chair with a scowl. "Meaningless. They’re all cowards, too afraid to stand for anything. This 'subtle influence' is pointless." He gestured dismissively at the rolls. "What about a true commitment? What about action?"

**Magister Lucian Kantos**, frail and stooped but with eyes that burned with an ancient knowledge, adjusted his thick spectacles. He picked up the strip for Roll 4. "Ah, but look here, Prince Cassian," he rasped, his voice a dry rustle. "Roll four. A critical success. Twenty. The Collegium of Divination has pledged their full, albeit cautious, support to Lady Sariel's reform. This is unprecedented."

Cressida's eyes widened. "The Collegium? They are fiercely neutral. This changes everything."

Sariel, however, had gone utterly still. Her gaze was not on the Collegium's success, but on Roll 7. Her finger trembled as she reached for it. "And Roll Seven," she whispered, her voice barely audible. "A one. A critical failure."

Before she could speak the name, **Sergeant Roric**, a gruff, heavily scarred Starfall Guard veteran standing near the chamber door, stepped forward slightly, his face grim. "That one was for the Lords of the Crimson March, My Lady," he stated, his voice a low rumble. "Our last report from their envoy indicated their final decision." His jaw tightened. "They've formally denounced all Arcanus reforms as a 'heresy against the Imperium's sacred traditions' and have severed all diplomatic ties. It's a complete rejection."

A heavy silence descended. The highest high, followed by the lowest low. The court was a fickle beast, and its loyalties shifted like sand. Sariel closed her eyes, the weight of her guilt and the magnitude of the political backlash settling upon her. She had won a powerful ally, but at the cost of alienating a formidable opponent.
