The simple breathing of the Stonehand family, masked by the hanging cloth, was a fragile thing against the profound silence Gladius now commanded within their home. Outside, the muffled voices of the distracted couple were barely audible, their unwitting absence a testament to his cunning. The thought of the exquisite, drawn-out torment he could inflict, the sheer experiential pleasure of breaking their simple, strong wills, sent a cold hum through Gladius. This was the true art, a performance of dominance.

He moved behind the cloth, his Shadow-Step Boots whispering against the stone. Three figures lay in the dim light cast by the hearth. He singled out two larger forms, likely adults or older children, their sleep deep and unsuspecting. The Silent Strangler’s Cord, a familiar extension of his will, glided into his hands. There was no hesitation, only precise, brutal efficiency. One swift, powerful pull, a silent gasp, a momentary spasm of life against his unyielding force. Then stillness. He held the cord, feeling the subtle shudder, the warmth draining, before gently lowering the body. He moved to the second, the act a chilling echo of the first: swift, clean, utterly silent. Two more lives erased, no sound, no struggle.

His gaze then fixed on the third figure, smaller, curled tightly under a thin blanket – a child. A cold, anticipatory pleasure tightened in his gut. This one would live, for now. He knelt, his large hand clamping over the child’s mouth before a sound could escape. The child’s eyes snapped open, wide with immediate, primal terror, struggling weakly against his overwhelming grip. He looked into those eyes, seeing the dawning horror, the absolute helplessness. It was a potent, raw sensation of control, the truest form of power. He lifted the child, a small, fragile bundle of life, and held it tightly against his armored chest, silencing any further attempts at a cry. The child’s heart hammered against him, a frantic drumbeat of despair.

Now, to bring the others home. The rising sun, just a faint sliver on the horizon, promised a new day. But for the Stonehands, this dawn would bring only a controlled, intimate slaughter.
