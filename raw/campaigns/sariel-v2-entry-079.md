Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 11:28 AM.
Location: Sergeant Kaelan's Cell, Quiet Ward, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 2 (XP: 4730/2700), Gold: 10gp, HP: 10/10

The air in the cell was thick with the weight of shattered truths. Rowan’s gentle murmurs continued to soothe Sergeant Kaelan, a fragile melody against the cacophony of the tormented man’s mind. Gideon, a silent, watchful sentinel, maintained his grim vigil. Magister Kantos, pale and anxious, hovered by the door. You had just experienced another devastating glimpse into your mother’s terrifying power, and the psychic backlash left you trembling, the metallic tang of blood a bitter reminder.

You turned, your gaze sweeping past Rowan and Gideon, to meet Cressida’s sharp, intelligent eyes. Her face, usually a mask of courtly composure, was etched with profound concern, but beneath it, you saw a subtle, almost imperceptible gleam of awe—a testament to her observation of your unprecedented command over the Silent Guard just moments ago.

"Cressida," you stated, your voice raw but steady, cutting through the spectral moans. The tremor in your hands was almost gone, replaced by a cold, intellectual focus. "You have heard the truth. The official legend of my mother is a meticulously crafted lie. She was not a Null, but a wielder of impossible magic, born from Lucifer's essence, and she used her perceived 'flaw' as her ultimate deception. This 'chilling capacity for unmaking and control'... it's a refined manifestation of the Empyrean Affliction itself."

You paused, drawing a ragged breath, the sheer weight of the revelation settling heavily in the confined space. "Kaelan witnessed it. He saw her unmoved as his comrades died for a lie. That is what broke him." You met Cressida's gaze, your own eyes burning with a desperate urgency. "This changes everything. The very foundation of the Imperium. Its power. Its lineage. Its future. What are your thoughts? What are the implications of this... this truth... for our position? For the court? For the Imperium itself?"

Cressida’s sharp eyes, already narrowed in thought, flickered between your pale, determined face and the tormented figure of Kaelan. She took a slow, deliberate breath, her usual quick wit momentarily subdued by the sheer magnitude of the truth. "My Lady," she began, her voice a low, thoughtful murmur, "if this is true... and I confess, the thought chills me to the bone... then your mother was not merely a general. She was the architect of the greatest deception in Imperial history." A subtle, almost predatory smile, devoid of mirth, touched her lips. "Her genius was... unparalleled."

She then turned her gaze to Kaelan, her expression hardening. "The implications are immense. This secret alone... could unravel the court. The power vacuum left by Raziel and your father's deaths is already unstable. If the noble houses, especially those like Valerius and Cassian, were to learn this truth..." Her voice trailed off, leaving the unspoken chaos hanging in the air. "It would not merely be a scandal, Sariel. It would be a cataclysm."

You nodded, the chilling clarity of her strategic assessment mirroring your own. This truth was a weapon, yes, but a weapon of such destructive potential that it threatened to obliterate the very Imperium it might save. The weight of it settled upon your shoulders, a burden far heavier than any scholarly tome. Kantos’s words about "dangerous methods" and "attuning one's mind to the frequency of the trauma" echoed in your thoughts. You had to delve into Kaelan’s mind, but not blindly. You needed to understand the exact nature of the plunge.

You turned to Kantos, your voice raw but steady, your Starlit Eyes burning with a desperate need for knowledge, for *answers*. "Magister," you began, "you have read these forbidden texts. You have seen the depths of madness in this ward. What do we do now? What else does your lore say about accessing a mind so profoundly shattered by such... impossible truths? What is the next step to truly *see* what he saw, without destroying him, or myself?"

Kantos, who had been listening with a mixture of terror and scholarly fascination, visibly flinched at your direct question. He wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting from your trembling form to the source of the subtle psychic distortion – Sergeant Kaelan. "Princess," he rasped, his voice a dry whisper, "the texts... they speak of methods. Of rituals. But they are desperate measures. Dangerous. They involve... attuning one's own mind to the frequency of the trauma." He paused, a profound weariness in his voice. "And such methods are never without cost. They can blur the line between observer and observed. Between sanity and madness."

You pressed your lips into a thin line, the chilling words painting a vivid picture of the ordeal ahead. "Attuning... blurring the line... sanity and madness." The metallic tang of blood still lingered in your mouth, a stark reminder of your previous, uncontrolled attempt to access the Nullification Field from your own memory. You had to prepare. To impose order on the chaos within before attempting to navigate Kaelan's shattered psyche.

"Magister," you commanded, your voice firm, "prepare for these methods. What rituals? What preparations are required? I need to understand the precise steps, the components, the incantations. How do I attune myself to his trauma without succumbing to it? And Rowan," you added, glancing at your gentle friend, "continue your efforts to stabilize Sergeant Kaelan. Ease his torment. We need him as calm as possible."

Kantos, though pale, nodded slowly, his scholarly instincts now fully engaged. He began to murmur to himself, sifting through ancient knowledge, occasionally gesturing for Rowan to fetch specific tools from his healing kit or to adjust Kaelan's position. He spoke of rare herbs for mental clarity, of specific breathing exercises from forbidden ascetic texts, of low-frequency hums that might quiet psychic static. He worked with a meticulous, desperate focus, outlining a series of mental and physical preparations.

You listened, absorbing every detail, even as your mind began its own rigorous preparations. You closed your eyes, focusing on the calm, steady rhythm of your breath. You pushed back against the lingering psychic ache, trying to enforce stillness on the internal storm. You envisioned your mind as a fortress, its walls strengthened, its gates secured, preparing to admit only the precise memories you sought. This was not a test of strength, but of control. You would attune yourself to Kaelan's trauma, yes. But you would not allow it to consume you. The silence of the cell was punctuated by Rowan's soft murmurs, Kantos's hushed instructions, and the faint, low moans of Sergeant Kaelan, a macabre symphony of preparation.
