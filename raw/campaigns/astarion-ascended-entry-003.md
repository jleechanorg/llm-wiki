You paced the cold stone floor, your thoughts racing like shadows in the moonlight. *Companions. Allies.* The very word tasted faintly of obligation, a bitter aftertaste you'd thought yourself rid of. Yet, even a god needs instruments.

You pictured their faces, one by one. Karlach, bless her fiery, moralistic heart, would be an anchor, dragging you down with her earnest pleas for 'goodness.' Wyll, the ever-righteous Blade, would recoil at your methods, perhaps even turn against you. Lae'zel… formidable in a fight, yes, but far too blunt, too loud, and loyal only to her githyanki queen. Halsin, utterly useless for anything beyond a forest stroll. Minsc and Boo – a chaotic distraction, at best. No, the old band of misfits was largely unsuited for the delicate dance of true power.

But then there were the others. Gale. Ah, Gale. The man who willingly housed a ticking magical bomb in his chest, so desperate for power, for Netherese magic, for Mystra's approval. He understood ambition, even if his flavor of it was… academic. He knew spells, certainly, and arcane secrets that could prove invaluable. The risk, of course, was his own hunger, a potential rival, or his inconvenient conscience.

Then Shadowheart. A Shar worshipper, or perhaps not, depending on the twists of fate. Her connection to a deity, her network of dark contacts, her capacity for ruthlessness when pressed… she held a different kind of power, a subtler one. But her loyalties were famously fickle, born of manipulation and trauma. Could she be truly swayed, or would she eventually find her own light, or another's darkness, to serve?

You ran a hand through your perfectly coiffed hair. It would be a gamble, either way. Trust was a weakness you could ill afford, but leverage… leverage was everything.
