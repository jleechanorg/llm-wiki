In the adjacent chamber, **Sergeant Kaelan** (Level 4 Fighter, Age 28) remained peacefully catatonic, oblivious to the new storm.

"Sariel? What in the Creator's name is this?" Cassian growled, his gaze sweeping accusingly over Kantos and Cressida. "What twisted games are you playing now?"

Darius stepped forward, his voice flat. "Sariel. Explain. Immediately. Every deviation from protocol will be noted."

You met their furious, accusatory gazes, but a cold, quiet satisfaction bloomed within you. They were stepping directly into your web. You took a slow, deliberate breath, allowing the mask of the grieving, fragile scholar to settle over your features. You let your shoulders slump, your voice becoming a soft, hesitant whisper, laced with a convincing, weary sorrow.

“Brothers,” you began, not meeting their eyes, instead looking towards the door of Kaelan’s cell. “I… I know this seems irregular. I apologize for the alarm.” You subtly touched the scar on your ear, a physical reminder of the trauma they both knew you had endured. “I couldn’t rest. The official reports… they didn’t make sense. Mother’s final battle. The Starfall Gambit. There were inconsistencies.”

You looked at them then, your Starlit Eyes wide and filled with a carefully crafted mix of grief and scholarly desperation. “I came here, to the Archives, seeking answers. Magister Kantos, in his kindness, granted me access to older records. We found… this.” You gestured vaguely towards Kaelan’s cell, and Lucian Kantos, startled anew by the gesture, visibly paled, sinking back onto his stool. “Sergeant Kaelan. The only other survivor. He is… unwell. Broken by what he witnessed.” Your voice cracked, a flawless performance of empathy. “Rowan is here to ease his torment. Gideon and Cressida… they are here to support me. I am… not as strong as I once was.”

Lady Cressida Valeriana, sensing the shift, offered a sympathetic, almost fragile nod, her own eyes conveying a shared sense of trauma, subtly reinforcing your narrative. Cassian’s fiery gaze softened almost imperceptibly, his rage struggling with the genuine sorrow on your face. Darius, however, remained utterly still, his cold eyes fixed on the door to Kaelan’s cell, his analytical mind processing your words, searching for a flaw in the emotional narrative.

Your explanation was a masterpiece of misdirection. It acknowledged their concerns while framing your actions as a desperate, grief-stricken search for truth, a narrative that perfectly aligned with their perception of you as a weak, traumatized scholar. You had turned your greatest secret into your most potent shield.
