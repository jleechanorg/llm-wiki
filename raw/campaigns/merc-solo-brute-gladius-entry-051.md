The air within the Blackwood cottage was thick with the scent of age and neglect, a stark contrast to the cloying sweetness of the Miller's home. Gladius stood inside, having slipped through the loose shutter like a phantom, his Shadow-Step Boots a silent testament to their grim purpose. The profound stillness of the sleeping house was a canvas for his efficiency.

He moved with a methodical grace, his broad shoulders barely disturbing the dim, moonlit air. First, to the flimsy wooden door that served as the main entry. It was unbarred, a pathetic testament to the Blackwoods' misplaced trust in their isolation. Gladius pulled a length of his hemp rope from his pack, securing the door tightly with a series of quick, efficient knots, rendering it impassable without significant effort and noise. No escape, no unexpected entry. He moved to the single, grimy window, confirming its latch was secure. A subtle hum settled in his gut; the feeling of absolute containment, of the trap being set, was a familiar, satisfying sensation. These 'fiercely independent' folk were now as helpless as caged rats.

With the cottage sealed, Gladius's hand moved to his belt, drawing forth the Silent Strangler's Cord. The thin, dark fiber, woven for silent death, felt cool and smooth against his skin, a precision tool for a precise task. He tested its tensile strength with a practiced tug, the material giving just enough to confirm its deadly efficiency. His eyes, in the faint moonlight, held a chilling intensity, devoid of any sentiment. This was the moment before the kill, the calm before the swift, brutal erasure. The greatsword on his back was for making statements; the cord was for secrets, for erasing, for the exquisite pleasure of control. He coiled it expertly in his palm, ready. The subtle sounds of slumber, faint and rhythmic from deeper within the cottage, were now merely a countdown.
