Game Master:
**--- BACKGROUND ---**

The world of Assiah exists in the shadow of the Great Vanishing, a cosmic abandonment that left the divine Empyrean bloodline to rule a fracturing reality. After centuries of civil war between the brothers Raziel and Lucifer—Order versus Freedom—a new era has dawned. The legendary warrior Alexiel, daughter of Lucifer, eventually brokered a fragile peace through her own sacrifice, but it was her daughter, Empress Sariel, who truly forged the modern world. Through the 'War of Whispers' and her terrifying psychic mastery, Sariel established the Silent Throne, a regime of absolute security where dissent is a thought-crime and prosperity is the ultimate law.

You are Ser Arion val Valerion, a sixteen-year-old knight of the Argent Eaglets. Raised in the warmth of the 'Silent Peace,' you have never seen a road plagued by bandits or a village starving in winter. To you, the Empress is not just a ruler, but the architect of civilization itself. Your house, Valerion, has long stood as a pillar of this order, and your father's parting words—that loyalty to the crown is loyalty to civilization—are the foundation of your identity.

Now, you stand on the precipice of your first deployment. The wind of Winter-Mourn bites through your cloak as you look down upon a cluster of makeshift tents huddled against the walls of Ashwood Keep. These are the refugees Lady Annalise Ashwood sheltered in defiance of the Stability Mandate. To your commander, Prefect Gratian, they are an infection in the Imperial body; to you, as you see the smoke rising from their small cookfires, they look like people. The order has been given: pacify the keep, disperse the threat. The peace you love is about to be bought with the blood of those you swore to protect.

--- 

The iron-shod hooves of your warhorse, *Cinder*, ring out against the frozen Imperial stone, a rhythmic, cold sound that matches the beating of your heart. Beside you, Prefect Gratian sits atop his massive black destrier, his face a mask of scarred granite. He doesn't look at the settlement with hatred, but with the clinical detachment of a gardener preparing to pull a weed. 

"The Mandate is absolute, Ser Arion," Gratian says, his voice cutting through the whistling wind. "Lady Ashwood's sentimentality has invited chaos. If we allow this unregistered settlement to persist, it becomes a beacon for the Shattered Host and a drain on the winter stores. Mercy for the few is a death sentence for the many. Draw your blade. We move at dawn."

In the distance, you see a young girl among the tents, pulling a thin shawl tighter around her shoulders as she watches the sunlight glint off your Valerion Plate. The 'Duty's Edge' longsword feels heavy at your hip—not with weight, but with the crushing gravity of your oath.
