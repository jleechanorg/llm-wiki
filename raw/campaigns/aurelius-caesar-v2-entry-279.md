Midday (13:45:00) in the Getae High Seat Central Square—the winter sun catches the silver frost on the timber longhouses as a heavy, expectant silence replaces the thunder of the duel. You stand over the unconscious Chieftain Wulfric, your Subtle Lorica splattered with the red proof of your victory, your amber eyes sweeping over the hundreds of fur-clad Suebi warriors who look upon their fallen king with a mix of dazed grief and religious terror.

"The duel is ended!" your voice roars, not as a shout of triumph, but as a Sovereign's decree that vibrates through the very basalt of the square. You do not raise your sword in boast; you point it toward the horizon where the Roman shadow still looms. "Look upon your king! He fought with the pride of the forest, and he fell to the truth of the Sun. But I do not seek his head, nor do I seek your blood. I seek your oath!"

You take a slow, measured step toward the Suebi elders, the honey-colored warmth of your Julian blood radiating outward to push back the biting Alpine wind. "The storm is coming from the South—a storm of chains and salt-mines. Will you meet it alone, as fractured branches in a gale? Or will you take the Oath of the Spires and become the iron that breaks the Eagle's beak? Choose now! Serve a Rome that remembers its heart, or serve the silence of the grave!"

The Suebi elders look at Wulfric, then at the Getae and Cimbri chiefs who stand in a disciplined circle around you. The sight of their 'unbreakable' wolf-king silenced by a boy's pommel-strike has incinerated the last of their stubborn resistance. One by one, they drop their bone-tipped spears. The lead elder, his hair white as the peaks, sinks to his knees in the dirt. "The wolf yields to the Sun," he rasps, his voice a dry whisper that is carried by the wind. "The Suebi take the oath. We are your steel, Sovereign."
