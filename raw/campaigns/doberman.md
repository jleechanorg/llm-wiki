## Summary

A D&D 5e adaptation of the K-drama "Military Prosecutor Doberman," set in a corrupt Seoul where military law and corporate greed are intertwined. The player controls Sariel — a Level 1 Bard (College of Swords) disguised as Noh Tae-nam's bodyguard. In truth, she is a disgraced chaebol heiress with a genius-level intellect, secretly working to build untraceable power and personal transcendence over a 10-year plan. The campaign follows her manipulation of Noh Tae-nam while navigating the arrival of "hero" prosecutor Do Bae-man.

## Key Claims

- Sariel is a prodigy who sees the world as a system to be edited — a blend of Griffith, Tywin, Aizen, and Johan Liebert
- She has two masks: The Silent Servant (bodyguard persona) and The Dispassionate Strategist (behind closed doors)
- Her class is College of Swords Bard, reflavored as control through combat (Blade Flourishes) and psychology (Bardic spells)
- Her secret compulsion is to orchestrate humiliating power dynamics — she tests systems and people
- She made a 10-year oath of service to Noh Tae-nam for money to save her family, but plans to transcend this
- Her ultimate goal is "becoming the boardroom" — personal transcendence into pure systemic influence
- Noh Tae-nam has a humiliation dynamic connected to his mother Noh Hwa-young's domination of him
- Do Bae-man is the new military prosecutor who will be a "hero" obstacle
