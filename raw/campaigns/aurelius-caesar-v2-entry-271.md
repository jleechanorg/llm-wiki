Game Master:
Two months have passed since you brought Legate Glaber into the smoke-stained halls of the Getae. In that time, the Northern Storm has transitioned from a desperate flight into a continental uprising. You have lived the life of a soldier-saint, sleeping on the frozen earth and rising with the sun to heal the broken and counsel the giants.

**Month 1: The Gathering of Kings**
**Week 1:** You spent your first days among the Getae solidifying the bond. You healed their sick, blessed their steel, and turned the dazed awe of the common warrior into a permanent devotion. The Getae now look to you as much as they look to their own Chief.
**Week 2:** You led a small party to meet the Suebi. The negotiation was a disaster; the Germanic lords saw your youth and your blood as a Roman trap. Only a desperate rearguard defense by Nasir allowed you to retreat without bloodshed, leaving the Suebi borders cold and hostile.
**Week 3:** You pivoted to the Cimbri. Using the captured Glaber as proof of Roman fragility, you won their war-council. They were moved not by your divinity, but by your humility—a Caesar who ate their coarse bread and refused a chair while their elders stood.
**Week 4:** You attempted to bring the Bastarnae into the fold, but their shaman-kings demanded a sign you were unwilling to give. The talks stalled, leaving you with a fractured alliance that relied more on your presence than a shared oath.

**Month 2: The Eagle's Attrition**
**Week 5:** You coordinated a series of raids against the northern supply lines. The Roman machine is resilient; while you burned their fodder, the Audit Teams utilized scorched-earth tactics to deny your tribes the valley's grain. The hunger began to bite.
**Week 6:** You attempted to turn the northern border garrisons. The 'Amnesty Protocol' failed against the veterans of the Tenth; they saw your mercy as a sign of weakness and slaughtered the messengers you sent. The war became personal, written in the blood of those you tried to save.
**Week 7:** You unleashed the 'Sun' propaganda. You stood atop the basalt peaks during a blizzard, your skin radiating a Julian heat that could be seen for miles. The myth of the 'Golden General' spread through the Roman ranks like a plague, causing mass desertions among the auxiliary units who feared the sacrilege of your presence.
**Week 8:** The culmination of your labor. The Getae, Cimbri, and a dozen smaller clans met at the High Seat. You stood before them, stripped of your noble finery, wearing only the salt-stained Lorica of the Spires. You spoke of a Rome that serves its people, and the North finally answered. As one, the kings knelt. You are no longer a fugitive; you are the Sovereign of a Northern Storm that is finally ready to break the Eagle's wings.
