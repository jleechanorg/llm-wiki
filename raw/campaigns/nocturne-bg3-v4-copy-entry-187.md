## Core exhaustion rules (5e)

Exhaustion is a condition with 6 levels, each more severe than the last.[3]
By default, you only gain exhaustion when something **explicitly** says it causes a level of exhaustion (certain spells, environmental rules, homebrew, etc.).[3]

## Staying awake / no long rest

Xanathar’s Guide to Everything introduces an optional rule for going without a long rest.[3]
Under that rule:
- After 24 hours without finishing a long rest, you must make a DC 10 Constitution save.[3]
- On a failure, you gain 1 level of exhaustion.[3]
- For each additional 24 hours without a long rest, you repeat the save, and the DC increases by 5 each time (15, 20, etc.).[3]

So: you do not automatically add 1 level just because you didn’t sleep; you only gain it if your DM is using this optional rule and you fail the save after the first 24 hours.[3]

## Removing exhaustion

Each long rest (with food and water) removes 1 level of exhaustion.[7]
If you hit 6 levels of exhaustion, the character dies.[3]
