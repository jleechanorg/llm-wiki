I understand. You have provided a `DM Note:` command: "are you making sure to account for Daemon's low intelligence when he makes plans?"

**Understanding & Execution:**

You are asking for confirmation and an explanation of how Daemon's Intelligence score (8, Modifier -1) is factored into his decision-making and planning processes, especially when I generate his internal thoughts or reactions to strategic information.

**My Thought Process & Adherence to Directives:**

Yes, I am actively ensuring Daemon's low Intelligence is accounted for, alongside his higher Wisdom, in generating his plans and thought processes. This is directly guided by **Part 5.A.5 (Plan Quality Scaling)** of the GM Protocols, which mandates that "The quality, complexity, and insight of the plans you generate must vary based on the character’s intelligence and wisdom scores."

Here's how I've been applying it for Daemon:

1.  **Simplicity and Directness:** Daemon's plans are consistently direct, brutal, and pragmatic, rather than intricate or subtle. His strategy for Stonehaven, while effective, was based on overwhelming force and terror (the trap with barricades, archers, and human shields was a brutal application of direct advantages, not complex feints). Similarly, his approach to Oakhaven was a direct march and intimidation.
2.  **Focus on Tangible Threats:** His "thinking" prioritizes immediate, physical threats and opportunities he can directly influence through force or intimidation. He focuses on what he can break, kill, or seize.
3.  **Limited Nuance and Abstract Thought:** His internal monologues, as generated by the `think/plan` command (e.g., Sequence ID 40 and 54), reflect a mind that processes information in a straightforward, almost primal way. He identifies pros and cons in terms of direct combat advantage or resource gain, not complex logistical challenges, long-term political ramifications, or the ethical implications of his actions.
4.  **Reliance on Practical Input (Wisdom vs. Intelligence):** While his Intelligence is low, his Wisdom (13, Modifier +1) is relatively high. This means he has good practical judgment and can assess immediate threats or opportunities, and he can discern actionable information from his subordinates (like Borin's and Kael's tactical suggestions for barricades). However, he doesn't *generate* highly complex or abstract solutions himself. His rejection of mercenaries (Sequence ID 54) was based on practical considerations of time, cost, and immediate force reliability (Wisdom), not a deep economic or diplomatic analysis (Intelligence).
5.  **Brutal Efficiency:** Daemon's decision-making prioritizes brutal efficiency and swiftness over elaborate schemes that might be beyond his intellectual grasp or patience. He trusts his own raw power and the fear he instills.

In essence, Daemon's plans are effective because they leverage his overwhelming personal strength and the terror he inspires, combined with a practical (Wisdom-driven) assessment of immediate threats and a ruthless disregard for anything that doesn't directly serve his immediate goal of conquest. His low Intelligence means he's not prone to overthinking, but rather to brute-forcing solutions or demanding simple, actionable intelligence from others.

Please confirm if this explanation is satisfactory.
