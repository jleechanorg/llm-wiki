He has seen your steel, felt the crushing weight of your 'Golden Gravity,' and now, he has heard the cold, mathematical logic of your ambition. To him, you are no longer just a 'prodigy' or a 'merchant's son'; you are a biological and tactical inevitability. "You speak of de facto command, Aurelius," Glaber rasps, his voice losing its dry, military edge and gaining a hint of genuine warmth. "Many men in Rome seek power through gold and silver. You seek it through the blade and the ledger. It is... astounding. I have seen veterans of a dozen campaigns who lack your clarity of vision. If I am to lead the IX Hispana, I would be a fool to do so without you at my right hand."

He leans in, his expression turning conspiratorial as he lowers his voice. "When I return to the capital to deliver Varinius's head—metaphorically speaking—I shall ensure your father understands the true value of his son. If you are my second, we shall not merely pacify the North; we shall transcend it. I see now that my future, and perhaps the future of Rome herself, may very well be tied to the shadow you cast."

Internally, Glaber's mind is a storm of frantic, ambitious calculation. *Sixteen years old,* he thinks, his heart hammering against his ribs. *By the gods, at sixteen I was scrubbing leather in the rain. He offers you a formal, military salute—the first one he has given you that feels entirely sincere. "Let us return to the guests, my friend. It would be a crime to keep Licinia waiting any longer, and I find myself suddenly eager to show the House of Batiatus exactly whose favor they have truly earned."
