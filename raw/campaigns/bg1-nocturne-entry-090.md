Game Master:
You leave the party at the mine entrance, a silent command keeping them back. The air grows colder as you step across the threshold, the light of dawn dying within twenty feet. To a human, this is blindness. To you, it is home.

Your **Umbral Sight** activates. The pitch-black tunnels shift into a monochrome landscape of greys and sharp contrasts. You see the rough-hewn walls, the rusted tracks of the ore carts, and the scurrying shapes of rats. More importantly, you see the **Kobolds**.

They are everywhere. Small, dog-like humanoids moving in efficient packs, carrying baskets of tainted ore. They chatter in yipping Draconic, their eyes glowing faintly red in the dark. But they do not see you. To their darkvision, you are nothing but empty air. You walk right past a sentry post, the guard sniffing the air suspiciously but seeing nothing.

**Infiltration Report:**
*   **The Layout:** The first level is a maze of support beams and narrow chokepoints. The central shaft is guarded by a heavy patrol.
*   **The Traps:** Your enhanced perception picks them out easily. A tripwire across the main thoroughfare (you step over it). A pressure plate hidden under loose shale (you mark it with a scrape of your boot). Without thieves' tools, you cannot dismantle the complex mechanisms, but you jam the triggers of the primitive traps with stones and cut the tension cords of the crossbow rigs.
*   **The Enemy:** You count at least three dozen kobolds on this level alone. They are armed with shortswords and light crossbows, utilizing "murder holes" drilled into the rock to fire on intruders from safety.

You reach a junction overlooking the main descent shaft. The path is clear of immediate mechanical danger. You backtrack silently to the entrance, emerging from the darkness like a wraith.

"The first level is a kill box," you report to the waiting party. "But I have jammed the triggers. The kobolds are reliant on darkvision; they won't see us until we are in melee range if we stick to the shadows. We can clear the upper tunnels quickly."
