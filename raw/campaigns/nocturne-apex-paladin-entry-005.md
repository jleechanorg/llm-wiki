Character Attributes (specifically 2.2 Aptitude System) and Section III. Character Progression System (specifically 3.5 Aptitude Score Improvement & Feats) of the Destiny Core Rules.

As per the **Finalization Protocol**, I must present these proposed changes for your explicit review and approval before integrating them into the active ruleset.

### Differential Report for Proposed Ruleset Changes

**[Change ID: 20240702-A]**

**Proposed Additions/Modifications to Destiny Core Rules:**

1.  **Modification to II. Character Attributes, 2.2 Aptitude System, 2.2.D Aptitude Potential:**
    *   **Current Text (Excerpt from 2.2.D):**
        *   "Each Aptitude also has an associated "Aptitude Potential," a hidden numerical value representing a character's innate talent and ultimate capacity for development in that Aptitude."
        *   *(This implied values like 18, 20, 22)*
    *   **Proposed New Text (Replacing the core definition of 2.2.D and establishing a new 2.2.A.1):**
        *   **II. Character Attributes**
            *   **2.2 Aptitude System:**
                *   **A. Core Aptitudes:** ... (existing text for Physique, Coordination, Health, Intelligence, Wisdom)
                *   **A.1. Aptitude Potential (Coefficient):** Each character has an innate "Aptitude Potential" coefficient (rated 1-5) for each Aptitude, representing their natural talent. This coefficient primarily influences the rate of yearly progression and the ultimate ceiling for an Aptitude.
                    *   *GM Note: This replaces the previous numerical "Potential" score, meaning existing potential scores (e.g., Nocturne's 20, 18) will need to be mapped to this new 1-5 coefficient.*

2.  **New Section: II. Character Attributes, 2.2.B The Yearly Progression Model:**
    *   **Current Text:** No such rule exists. Aptitudes typically increase only via level-based "Aptitude Score Improvement & Feats" (3.5).
    *   **Proposed New Text:**
        *   **B. The Yearly Progression Model:** A character's Aptitude scores change fractionally each year based on their age and Aptitude Potential coefficient.
            *   **Growth Years (Ages 16-25):** All Aptitudes gain `+ (Potential Coefficient / 10)` points per year.
            *   **Plateau Years (Ages 26-35):** Physical Aptitudes (Physique, Coordination, Health) change by `+ ((3 - Potential Coefficient) * 0.1)` points per year. Intelligence changes by `-0.1` points per year. Wisdom changes by `+0.2` points per year.
            *   **Decline Years (Ages 36+):** Physical Aptitudes change by `+ ((2 - Potential Coefficient) * 0.2)` points per year. Intelligence changes by `-0.2` points per year. Wisdom changes by `+0.1` points per year.
            *   *(Note: Ages 16-25 are assumed to be "Growth Years" as the player character is 16 and this is where the Growth rule would apply first, the prompt provided 26-35 as 'Growth Years' which would imply a lull in growth before 26 - please confirm or clarify this age grouping if different from the standard assumption of growth until early 20s)*

3.  **New Section: II. Character Attributes, 2.2.C Special Growth: The "Lesson Learned" Check:**
    *   **Current Text:** No such rule exists.
    *   **Proposed New Text:**
        *   **C. Special Growth: The "Lesson Learned" Check:** Wisdom can also be actively developed outside of normal progression by successfully making an Intelligence check to learn from significant life failures. The Challenge Number (CN) for this check is between 12-18, determined by the GM (AI) based on the severity and complexity of the failure from which the character is attempting to learn. Success grants a minor, immediate increase to Wisdom (e.g., +0.1 to +0.5 points).

**Justification for Changes:**
These changes introduce a more dynamic and age-dependent attribute progression system, allowing for continuous character evolution beyond fixed level milestones and providing a new method for Wisdom development tied to narrative experience. The new "Aptitude Potential coefficient" offers a more qualitative representation of a character's innate talent that ties directly into this new progression model.
