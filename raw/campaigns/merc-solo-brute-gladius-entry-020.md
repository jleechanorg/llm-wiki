`Location: The Rusty Flagon Inn, Greenmeadow Village`

The mist of Shadowfen Moor still clung to the low-lying fields as dawn reluctantly broke over Greenmeadow. The village was stirring, a few plumes of smoke curling from chimneys, but the Rusty Flagon, a squat, uninviting inn on the village's western edge, remained mostly dark. Gladius, having concluded his silent scout, approached the inn with an economical stride. His Shadow-Step Boots continued to impress, muffling the heavy clang of his splint mail to a mere whisper as he passed the inn's creaking sign.

The door, unlatched, yielded to a gentle push, revealing a dim common room. A single lantern flickered on the counter, casting dancing shadows. The air was thick with the scent of stale ale, cold ashes, and something unpleasantly sweet, like fermented apples. No one else was present, save for a lone, slender figure hunched over a half-eaten plate of what looked like dry bread and cheese at a corner table. He was dressed in plain, practical traveling clothes, a stark contrast to the gaudy silks of Lord Silvanus's lackey back in Baldur's Gate. This must be Corvan.

The man looked up sharply as Gladius entered, his eyes, though tired, instantly assessing the imposing figure framed by the doorway. He didn't flinch, but a wary tension entered his posture, a professional caution. He was clearly expecting someone.

Gladius closed the door behind him, the soft click echoing in the stillness. He moved with a deliberate, unhurried pace towards Corvan's table, his greatsword a dark silhouette against his back. He didn't bother with pleasantries. "Lord Silvanus," Gladius began, his voice a low, gravelly rumble, "had an unfortunate incident with a granary request."

Corvan’s eyes flickered, a flicker of recognition, then a deepening of caution. He set down his bread, slowly. He clearly understood the code phrase. He remained seated, his gaze fixed on Gladius, measuring. A faint bead of sweat formed on his brow, despite the cool morning air.
