Game Master:
The sun beats down on the Coast Way, baking the mud into hard ruts that threaten to twist ankles with every step. The landscape here is starker than the gentle hills of the Friendly Arm; the trees are gnarled, wind-bent things, and the Cloudpeak Mountains loom like a bruised wall on the southern horizon.

Kagain marches at the vanguard, his heavy dwarven constitution ignoring the heat, while Roric drifts at the rear, eyes scanning their backtrail for the glint of Flaming Fist armor. You walk in the center, setting a pace that keeps **Garrick** breathless but moving.

"Water," the bard gasps, fumbling with his flask. "Just... a sip."

You grab the flask from his hand, stopping him. "Talk first. Drink second."

You shove him toward a rocky outcrop shielded from the wind. "Silke didn't hire you for your lute playing, Garrick. She hired you because you're a local who knows who to bribe and who to watch. Tell me about the contract. Who owns Silke Rosena?"

Garrick licks his cracked lips, glancing nervously at Kagain's axe. "She... she never gave a name. Just 'The Throne'. She met with men in iron-grey cloaks at the Burning Wizard two nights ago. They didn't speak like locals. They spoke like... soldiers."

**Insight Check: 22 (Success)**

The *Hunger* sharpens your perception, dissecting his micro-expressions. He is telling the truth, but he's holding back a detail he thinks is irrelevant.

"The gems," you press, leaning closer. "The merchants weren't just competitors, were they?"

"No!" Garrick yelps. "I mean... I saw the appraisal note! They weren't carrying normal gemstones. They were carrying *Beljurils* from the Nashkel mines. Flash stones. But they were wrong... cloudy. Silke said they were 'proof of the contamination.' She said if those stones reached Baldur's Gate, the Grand Dukes would know the iron rot wasn't natural."

Roric steps up, frowning. "Proof? You mean the iron isn't just bad ore?"

"It's alchemy," Garrick whispers, his eyes wide. "Or poison. The Iron Throne... they're poisoning the mountain itself."

Kagain spits a glob of tobacco juice onto the road. "Poisoning a mountain? That takes deep pockets and deeper magic. If Silke was cleaning up loose ends for the Iron Throne, then we just killed their cleanup crew. They won't like that."

"We didn't just kill the crew," you correct him, looking south. "We took their job. If the Iron Throne wants the mines shut down or controlled, and we go in there and break their operation... we don't just get the bounty. We get the leverage."

You toss the water flask back to Garrick. "Drink. We have a river to cross."

By late afternoon, the roar of the river reaches your ears. The bridge ahead is a narrow stone span, ancient and moss-slick. Kagain stops, raising a fist. "Hobgoblin sign," he grunts, pointing to a smear of dark blood on a milestone. "Fresh."
