The clamor of the market faded to a dull roar in Gladius’s mind as his thoughts turned inward, focusing on the cold, hard logic of power. He now had the contract, the gold, and the basic tools for the Greenmeadow job. The Great Weapon Master feat, a brutal extension of his own physical philosophy, demanded a weapon worthy of its name. A greatsword. It was a simple, direct answer to the question of maximum force.

*Is there anything better?* The thought rumbled, devoid of curiosity, steeped instead in a pragmatic desire for efficiency. He dismissed the idea of plate armor; his splint mail, reinforced by his own Battle-Hardened focus, was already as good as plate in terms of raw protection (AC 18). More gold there would be wasted, tying up funds that could be better deployed elsewhere. Potions of speed? Magical. Rare. Expensive, far beyond what he'd just extorted from Silvanus's sniveling lackey. And unreliable, fleeting. His power was in his own flesh, his own will, not in fragile liquids.

No, the "better" wasn't in some flimsy magic. It was in leverage. The greatsword would maximize his immediate, raw destructive power. It was the blunt instrument he needed for the job at hand. But with 400 gold remaining after its purchase, what else could amplify his power, his *dominance*? Information? The sniveling lackey had given him all he could. Perhaps more specific tools for the "no witnesses" clause? Or was it time to turn his attention, even briefly, to the true target: the fragile arrogance of Lord Silvanus himself? The joy of the current contract was already fading, replaced by the itch for a grander game, a more powerful person to break. The remaining gold could be saved, or it could be used to set a trap, to gather more specific leverage against the 'clever' noble who thought he could buy Gladius like a common dog.
