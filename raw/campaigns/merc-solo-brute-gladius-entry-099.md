The abandoned farmstead, draped in its sinister web-shroud, hummed with an unseen life. Gladius, crouched at the edge of the copse, had observed enough. The scent of decay, the vast, intricate webs, all spoke of a hunt. His pragmatic mind dismissed complex plans; this was a raw, visceral contest, a chance to feel the immediate thrill of the predator. He would draw them out.

He unslung his greatsword, its immense weight a comforting presence in his hand. From his pack, he pulled a handful of iron rations, bland but practical. With fluid, silent movements, his Shadow-Step Boots whispering against the damp earth, he moved to the nearest dilapidated outbuilding. He found a broken window, its shards like jagged teeth, and with a grunt of effort, he hurled a small chunk of the iron rations into the dark interior. The dull *thud* as it landed was surprisingly loud in the profound silence of the night, a direct challenge. Then, he used the flat of his blade to scrape a piece of metal against the broken wood of the window frame, a rhythmic, grating sound, mimicking something struggling, something trapped. He wasn't subtle; he was *loud*, direct, undeniable.

He backed away, melting back into the shadows of the copse, his eyes fixed on the outbuilding. The metallic clang of his splint mail, usually a rigid confinement, now felt like a second skin, a vessel for the raw power thrumming beneath. The air grew colder, thick with mist, but Gladius felt a heat build within him. The silence after his lure was profound, almost oppressive, amplifying the subtle tension. He watched the glistening webs, anticipating the slightest tremor. This was the moment of anticipation, the exhilarating calm before the storm. The raw, experiential pleasure of being the hunter, of luring his prey into the open, was a potent surge, a visceral rush that sharpened his senses. He could almost taste the fear of the unseen creatures. This wasn't about strategy; it was about the exquisite, immediate performance of dominance.
