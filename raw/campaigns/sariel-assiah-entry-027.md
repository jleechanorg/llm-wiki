Location: The Chamber of Whispers, Great Archives, Aeterna.

Kantos’s ancient eyes, magnified by his thick spectacles, fixed on you. He had just revealed the existence of a man whose mind had been shattered by the truth, and he feared that same truth would poison you. You felt the chill of it, the weight of centuries of buried knowledge pressing down, but it only sharpened your resolve. This was not a moment for caution. This was a moment for the truth.

"A broken mind," you whispered, your voice low and intense, "is often the only one capable of holding a forbidden truth. But a complete truth, Magister, is sometimes the only antidote. My mother died in that battle. Her legend is absolute, but the way she died... it defies logic. Thirty Starfall Guard against seven Apostates, including Lucifer himself. And she, a Null, walked out alone?" Your gaze was unwavering, piercing through his practiced composure. "How? What truly happened at the Starfall Gambit? I need the unedited details. All of them."

Kantos winced, as if the words themselves were physical blows. He wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting around the dark, silent chamber as if expecting Raziel's Silent Guard to materialize and spirit him away. He was a man caught between the terror of a lie and the horror of the truth. He closed his eyes, a deep, shuddering sigh escaping him, and when they opened, a profound weariness had settled over his ancient features.

"It is a story of impossible brilliance, Princess," he rasped, his voice barely audible, "and a cost no one should bear. The official account states your mother's peerless martial skill, honed by her perceived 'flaw' as a Null, allowed her to overcome Lucifer's magic through sheer physical prowess. It claims the Starfall Guard, without recourse to their own celestial gifts once within the anti-magic field your mother created, simply grappled and held the Apostates through impossible bravery, buying her the seconds she needed to strike." He paused, his gaze meeting yours, and a flicker of deep, sorrowful doubt crossed his face. "It is a beautiful lie, Sariel. One the Imperium desperately needed to believe."
