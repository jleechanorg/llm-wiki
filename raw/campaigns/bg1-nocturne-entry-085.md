You do not take the map. Instead, you step closer to the altar, your shadow stretching long over the holy symbols of Helm. The *Hunger* sharpens your presence, projecting an aura of lethal competence that makes the temple guards shift uneasily.

"The future of the Sword Coast is a heavy burden, Mayor," you say, your voice smooth and cold. "And heavy burdens require heavy payment. You want us to walk into a hole that has swallowed your best soldiers. You want us to kill demons that turn iron to glass."

You lean on the altar, staring directly into Ghastkill's tired eyes. "Three thousand gold pieces. Fifteen hundred now, to equip us for the slaughter. Fifteen hundred when we bring you the head of the leader."

The Priest of Helm gasps. "Blasphemy! This is a house of—"

"This is a house of war," you cut him off, not breaking eye contact with the Mayor. "And war is expensive. Look at my crew, Berrun. A Zhentarim mercenary, a Rashemaar berserker, a Witch, a thief, and a bard who has seen more blood in three days than you have in a lifetime. We are not heroes. We are the solution. Do you want the mines cleared, or do you want to save your coin for your funeral?"

**Intimidation Check: 21 (Success)**

Mayor Ghastkill pales, looking from your cold gaze to the savage grin of Kagain and the massive form of Minsc. He wipes sweat from his brow with a trembling hand. He knows he has no other option. The Amnish soldiers are useless; the Flaming Fist is overstretched. You are the only weapon left.

"Three... three thousand," Ghastkill whispers, swallowing hard. "Gods above. That is the entire garrison budget for the season."

He turns to the priest. "Open the vault. Give them what we have."

"But sir!" the priest protests.

"DO IT!" Ghastkill roars, his desperation cracking his composure. "If the mines don't open, we all starve anyway!"

The priest hurries away and returns with a heavy, iron-bound chest. He counts out the coins with shaking hands. 

"Nine hundred gold pieces," Ghastkill says, pushing the heavy sacks across the altar. "It is every coin we have on hand. The temple funds, the tax levy... everything. Take it. The remaining two thousand..." He grimaces. "I will have to draft a promissory note against the Council of Amn. You will get it when the mines are clear. I swear it on the Vigilant One."

He shoves the map into your chest. "Now go. And kill everything down there."

You take the gold (**+900 gp**) and the map. You have squeezed the town dry, but you are now funded like a small army.

**Roric** whistles as you exit the temple, hefting one of the sacks. "Remind me never to owe you money, Nocturne. You scare me."

**Kagain** laughs, a sound like grinding stones. "That's not fear, thief. That's respect. Finally, a leader who knows what blood is worth."

You stand on the steps of the Temple. The town is dark. The mines await to the south. You have **1,021 gold**, a full party, and a contract. But your spell slots are nearly empty, and the march has been long.
