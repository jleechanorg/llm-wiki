The scent of stale ale and the rhythmic snores of Sheriff Borin were a final, pathetic testament to the sheer, effortless control Gladius had exerted over Greenmeadow. He paused for a moment at the sheriff's doorway, his immense shadow falling over the incapacitated figure, then turned without a word. The sun was now fully risen, painting the village in a stark, unforgiving light, illuminating the mundane routines of its waking inhabitants. Farmers led oxen to fields, women hung laundry, children played near the well. They were utterly oblivious, their lives a fragile, predictable rhythm that Gladius had so brutally, and silently, shattered.

He moved through the village with a predatory calm, his Shadow-Step Boots muting his heavy armor to a mere whisper. No one paid him undue attention, seeing only another large sellsword passing through. His face, obscured by his hood and his cultivated scowl, revealed nothing of the cold, precise satisfaction that settled deep within him. The memory of Corvan, cuffed and broken, then released to wallow in his master's grim work, was a potent, exquisite aftertaste. That was the true indulgence: forcing the "powerful" to debase themselves, to become complicit in the filth they thought themselves above. The peasants were merely a means, a canvas for his brutal artistry.

As he reached the muddy track leading back to Baldur's Gate, the fresh air of Shadowfen Moor replaced the lingering scents of hearth smoke and fear. He felt the familiar weight of his greatsword on his back, the Silent Strangler's Cord a comforting presence at his belt, and the stolen gold and herbs a pleasing jingle in his pouch. The mission was complete, flawlessly executed. Eleven souls erased, their defiance, their very existence, utterly subsumed by his will. The thrill of the hunt, the raw, experiential pleasure of imposing his absolute dominance, was a visceral hum beneath his skin. Greenmeadow was behind him, a quiet victory. Baldur's Gate, with its labyrinthine power structures and arrogant elite, awaited. The true game, the grander performance of his rising power, was just beginning.
