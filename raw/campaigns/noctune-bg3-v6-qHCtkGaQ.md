I. Character Profile: Nocturne Sosuke (The Fallen Siren)
I. Character Description: Nocturne Sosuke (The Ascendant)
The Vessel of Stolen Fire. You retain the delicate, half-elven frame of your origin, but it has been perfected into something unsettlingly flawless. Your skin possesses the cool, polished texture of living marble—pale as bone china, smooth to the touch, and utterly devoid of mortal imperfections like sweat or pores. You are breathtakingly beautiful, but it is the beauty of a statue brought to life by a dark miracle.
The Face: Your features are sharp and aristocratic, framed by raven-black hair that falls in heavy, silken waves. It looks perpetually damp, as if you have just emerged from the sea or a birthing pool.
The Eyes: Normally, they are a deep, captivating violet. However, when you channel your power—casting a spell, commanding a thrall, or stealing a soul—they ignite. A blinding pink and crimson luminescence floods your irises, removing the whites of your eyes and turning your gaze into two twin stars of Abyssal hunger.
The Attire: You favor sheer, midnight-blue silks that cling to your form like water. At campaign start the fabric is often torn or stained with the evidence of your violence—blood, ichor, or ash—yet on you, the ruin looks like high fashion. Later on I’ll have unmarred clothes.
The Hidden Markings: You can manifest small, elegant horns and retractable Abyssal wings at will. When hidden, your silhouette is indistinguishable from a mortal noblewoman. When revealed, they are wreathed in shadow and starlight, marking you as the Sovereign of the Shattered Rose.
You are a paradox: A fragile girl in a torn dress who commands the authority of a god. When you walk into a room, the air grows heavy with the scent of brine and ozone, and even the shadows seem to lean toward you in submission.
The Narrative Hook: "You are a quiet dissonance. Visually, you are a shipwreck—a porcelain girl in ruined silk, fragile enough to break in a strong wind. Yet, your presence displaces the air in a room like a rising tide. There is no heavy musk of sulfur, only a faint, almost imagined sharpness—like the smell of the sea just before a storm breaks. People do not bow because you demand it; they step back because your stillness feels louder than their noise. The shadows don't bend, but they seem to deepen around you, lending your silhouette a weight that your small frame should not possess."
The Physical Evolution: Level 0–20
Theme: You do not grow monstrous; you grow perfect. The transformation is not about adding claws or scales, but about refining your half-elven form until it hits the "Uncanny Valley"—too symmetrical, too smooth, and too still to be entirely human.

Class Features (Levels 3–20)
These abilities are balanced for standard D&D play, requiring resource management and risk.
Level 3: Siren’s Skin
Your unearthly perfection deflects harm. While you are not wearing armor, your Armor Class equals 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Charisma modifier. You can use a shield and still gain this benefit.
Level 3: Siren’s Gaze (Social HP)
You can attack a creature's resolve to prepare them for thrallhood. As an Action, make a Charisma (Persuasion or Intimidation) check against the target's Passive Insight (10 + Wisdom Modifier).
Hit: You deal "Social Damage" equal to the difference (e.g., Roll 20 vs DC 15 = 5 Damage).
Broken: When a target reaches 0 Social HP, they are Vulnerable to your Gilded Tether (Disadvantage on the save).
Note: Standard enemies have ~10 Social HP. Bosses have ~5x their CR.
Level 3: The Gilded Tether (Thralls)
You can bind the weak-willed to your service.
Bind: As an Action, expend one Bardic Inspiration to force a Broken (0 Social HP) humanoid to make a Wisdom saving throw. On a failure, they are Charmed and obey your verbal commands.
Limit: You can maintain a number of Thralls equal to your Proficiency Bonus.
The Cost (Soul Strain): Your Maximum Hit Points are reduced by 1 for every level/CR of your active Thralls. (e.g., Binding a CR 5 Gladiator reduces your Max HP by 5). If you drop to 0 HP, all Thralls are freed.
Level 3: Abyssal Arts (Combat)
Restored Feature. Whenever you take the Attack action, you can expend one use of Bardic Inspiration to execute a maneuver:
The Lash: Deal extra Psychic Damage equal to the die roll. Hits adjacent enemies for the same damage.
The Caress: Deal extra Psychic Damage. Push target 10ft. They have Disadvantage on their next attack.
The Command: Deal extra Psychic Damage. Add the number rolled to your AC until the start of your next turn.
Level 3: Sacrificial Shield
When you take damage, you can use your Reaction to transfer that damage to an Active Thrall within 30 feet of you. The Thrall takes the damage instead. You cannot use this if no Thralls are in range.
Level 5: Pheromone Spike
Expend a Bardic Inspiration die to cast Command as a bonus action. This ties it to a limited resource she already manages and forces a tactical choice between inspiring allies/debuffing enemies and commanding them.
Level 6: Soul Siphon
When a humanoid (CR 1+) dies within 60 feet, you can use your Reaction to rip the soul from their body.
The Feast: You gain +20% XP for the encounter.
The Battery: You regain one expended Spell Slot. The slot level equals half the target's CR (Max 5th Level).
The Trophy: You attempt a Charisma Check (DC 10 + Target CR - Your Level). On a success, you steal one Trait (e.g., Parry, Pack Tactics, Magic Resistance). You can store a limited number of Trophies (see table).
Restriction: You can swap your active Soul Trophies only upon finishing a Long Rest.
The Siphon (Passive): Whenever an Active Thrall deals damage to a hostile creature, you gain Temporary Hit Points equal to your Charisma Modifier (min 1).
