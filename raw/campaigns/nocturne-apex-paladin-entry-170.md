Location: House Valerius Estate, Lord Cassian's Private Study, Morning

Lord Cassian Valerius (Age 55) stood before Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16), his initial shock now a cold, determined fury. "My network... my resources... they are yours. You will manage this. You will lead the counter-operation. No Julian. No interference. Report directly to me." His eyes, sharp and intelligent, held a dangerous glint, a mirror of her own ruthless pragmatism. "The honor of House Valerius, and perhaps the fate of Baldur's Gate, rests on your shoulders, youngest daughter."

Nocturne felt a cold, deep satisfaction. The words, the authority, the implicit acknowledgment of her superiority – it was precisely what she had engineered. Her father, the calculating patriarch who had exiled her, now handed her the reins of power. The irony was palpable, a subtle amusement that only she would appreciate.

***

**Nocturne's Internal Monologue & Planning:**

*Exile. A necessary inconvenience, perhaps even a crucible. Julian's petty jealousy, his pathetic need to assert dominance over a talent he could never comprehend, provided the catalyst. He believed he was severing a threat. He was merely sharpening a blade and sending it to gather intelligence from the darkest corners. His fear of my raw, evil nature was a convenient excuse. He cared not for the murder, only for the challenge to his perceived authority.*

*The victim... a minor noble, a distant cousin who had made the grave mistake of underestimating my capabilities, of viewing me as merely a child to be dismissed. The precision of the act, the cold efficiency of it, was almost beautiful. It was not born of passion or anger, but of a detached curiosity about the limits of human fragility, and a desire to remove an inconvenient obstruction. Julian merely observed the aftermath, and seized upon it, twisting the exposure of my 'true nature' into a political weapon for my removal. He wanted me gone, and my enjoyment of the process, my lack of remorse, made me an easy target for his machinations.*

*Father... he always understood the true currency: power. He recognized the ruthlessness required to maintain it. He orchestrated my exile because I was 'uncontrolled,' a variable he could not easily place within his meticulously ordered web. He admired my capabilities even as he feared their wildness. His 'soft spot' was not affection, but a recognition of potential, a curiosity about the depths of my aptitude. He saw a weapon, unrefined. Now, that weapon has returned, honed and laden with undeniable proof of his network's blindness.*

*They cast me out for enjoying murder, for my evil. Yet, that very 'evil,' that lack of moral constraint, allowed me to operate where their 'civilized' agents could not. It enabled the efficiency of the Oakhaven operation, the brutal precision of neutralizing the guards, the detached acquisition of the family. It allowed me to see the true nature of Durin's Folly's threat, to decipher the sinister tapestry they wove with death cults and Shadowfell rituals. My exile, orchestrated by Julian, was ultimately a testament to my superiority. It freed me to learn, to grow, to acquire the very knowledge that now renders me indispensable.*

*The game is changing. I am not merely re-entering the fold; I am carving my own path to the very apex of its power. This crisis is my opportunity. Father understands. He has yielded. Now, it is time to act.*

***

Nocturne's gaze remained fixed on her father, acknowledging his words with a faint, almost imperceptible nod. The conversation had yielded precisely the results she sought. The game was truly hers to play.
