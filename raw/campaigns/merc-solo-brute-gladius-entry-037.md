`Location: Miller Family Farmhouse, Parents' Bedroom, Greenmeadow Village`

The door to the parents' bedroom swung inward silently, revealing a modest chamber bathed in the pale, ethereal glow of the distant moon. A heavy, carved wooden bed dominated the space, its two occupants lost in deep sleep. Gladius stepped inside, his Shadow-Step Boots whispering against the floorboards, rendering his heavy splint mail a near-silent presence. The air in here was heavier than in the children's room, thick with the scent of bodies in repose and the faint, sweet musk of old wood and worn fabric.

His eyes, sharp and unblinking, fixed on the two figures. The man, larger and more robust, lay snoring softly. The woman, smaller, nestled beside him. No hesitation. The Silent Strangler's Cord, a familiar, chilling extension of his will, was already in his hand. He moved to the man first, his shadow falling over the sleeping face. The cord was swift, precise. A single, powerful pull, a silent, desperate struggle, quickly quelled. No sound escaped, only the sudden, stark cessation of life. He held it, ensuring absolute stillness, then gently lowered the body back onto the bed. Done.

He moved to the woman, her eyes fluttering open just as the cord tightened. A flicker of terror, of dawning comprehension, in the moonlight. But it was too late. The brief, choked gasp, a desperate clawing at the air, then stillness. Gladius withdrew the cord, his face impassive, his eyes cold and unfeeling. Four souls. The Miller family, utterly erased. A grim satisfaction settled in his gut; the "no witnesses" clause was being fulfilled with chilling perfection.

With the silence of death now absolute in the room, Gladius turned to the second part of his task. His gaze swept the humble space. There were no obvious caches of wealth, no gleaming treasures. This was a peasant's room. But he remembered the information: *rare herbs, valuable in the city.* He moved with methodical efficiency, his long, powerful fingers probing along the rough-hewn floorboards, behind loose wall panels, under the worn mattress. His disdain for their carelessness warred with the primal urge to acquire.

Behind a loose stone in the hearth, his fingers brushed against a small, leather-bound pouch. He pulled it free, and a faint, sweet aroma, far more potent than the general smell of the house, wafted from it. Herbs. The Miller's unique strain. He also found a small, wrapped bundle of coins, twenty gold pieces, clearly their meager life savings, hidden with a pathetic hope for safety. *Fools,* he thought, a sneer twisting his lips. He pocketed the gold and the pouch. It was nothing substantial, but it was *theirs*, and now it was *his*. Another small piece of their defiance, now subsumed into his own growing power.
