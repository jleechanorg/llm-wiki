Game Master:
The cellar is damp and smells of old wine and rust, a fitting barracks for a company of fugitives. Kagain has retreated upstairs to 'guard the door' (and likely count his own gold), leaving the three of you alone in the dark underbelly of the shop.

Garrick has curled himself into a ball on the grain sacks, exhaustion finally overtaking his terror. His soft snoring is the only rhythm in the room besides the slow drip of water from a pipe.

You find **Roric** sitting on a crate near the single shuttered window, sharpening his scimitar by the light of a dying candle. He looks up as you approach, his eyes glinting in the gloom. He doesn't look frightened. He looks *wired*.

"That was..." Roric pauses, searching for the word, then grins, a flash of white teeth in the dark. "...spectacular. Messy, sure. But spectacular."

You sit opposite him, resting your back against a barrel. The *Hunger* is quiet now, sated by the violence of the square, leaving you with a cool, intellectual clarity. You discuss the 'heist'—the ambush of the merchants, the turning of the Harpers, the escape. It wasn't a heist in the traditional sense, but Roric views it through the lens of a professional criminal: risk versus reward.

"I've cut purses in the market," Roric says, wiping his blade with an oil rag. "I've raided caravans. But I've never seen a Harper druid drop before she could finish a chant. You don't hesitate, Nocturne. Most people... they have a pause button. A moment where they ask 'should I?' You just have 'do'."

He flicks a gemstone—one of the agates he swiped from the dead merchant—over his knuckles. "I like it. It's terrifying, but I like it. Sarevok, the Iron Throne, the Flaming Fist... they're all playing big games. If we're going to survive Nashkel, we can't afford to pause."

He leans forward, his expression sobering. "But Kagain... he's useful, but he's a mercenary. He'll turn if the coin is better on the other side. And the bard? He's a liability waiting to scream. You and me? We're the only ones who walked out of that square with blood on our hands and didn't blink. That counts for something."

He offers you the gemstone—a small, token offering of loyalty. "To the new management."

Bonding with the rogue settles something in the air between you. It isn't friendship—predators don't have friends—but it is a Pack. You sleep in shifts, but for the first time since Candlekeep, you sleep soundly.

***

**Mirtul 4, Morning.**

You wake before dawn. The cellar is cold. Kagain is already awake, kicking Garrick awake with a grunt. "Up, squealer. We march."

The streets of Beregost are quiet, but the tension is palpable. Wanted posters will be going up soon. You need to be gone before the sun hits the cobblestones.
