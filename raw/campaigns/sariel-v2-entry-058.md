Game Master:

Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 11:05 AM.
Location: Sergeant Kaelan's Cell, Quiet Ward, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 2 (XP: 4730/2700), Gold: 10gp, HP: 10/10

The heavy iron door to Kaelan’s cell groaned open, revealing the worried faces of your companions. Ser Gideon Vance (Level 14 Fighter, Age 25) immediately stepped forward, his hand resting on the hilt of his sword, his gaze falling upon the gaunt, tormented figure of Sergeant Kaelan, then upon your own trembling form. Lady Cressida Valeriana (Level 12 Expert, Age 16), her sharp eyes sweeping the cell with professional assessment, and Rowan Thorne (Level 3 Expert, Age 16), his gentle features creased with profound worry, stood ready behind him. Magister Kantos, pale and anxious, hovered by the doorway.

"My Lady," Gideon said, his voice a low, steadying rumble, "you should not be here alone."

You met his gaze, then swept your eyes across the faces of Cressida and Rowan. Their concern was a balm, but this was not a moment for comfort. Your mind, sharp and cold despite the lingering psychic ache, immediately seized control.

You took a deep, steadying breath, forcing your voice to be clear and authoritative, cutting through the spectral whispers of Kaelan’s madness. "You are right, Ser Gideon. I am not. And we are not." You gestured to Sergeant Kaelan, the small orb of `Light` on your finger illuminating his tortured features. "This is Sergeant Kaelan, the Starfall Patient. The sole living witness to the true nature of the Starfall Gambit. His mind holds the final truth of my mother's power, a truth Raziel sealed away for a reason."

You then explained, concisely, the immediate situation, focusing on the tactical imperatives rather than the full, horrifying philosophical implications. "My recent efforts to access this truth have caused a psychic backlash, leaving me vulnerable. Sergeant Kaelan's condition is unstable, and his memories are volatile." You looked at each of them in turn, assigning roles with chilling precision.

"Rowan," you commanded, your voice firm, "to Sergeant Kaelan. Assess his physical and mental state. His madness is born of truth, not illness, but your kindness and healing knowledge may create a bridge to his mind. Prepare whatever you can to ease his torment."

Rowan, his face etched with grim determination, nodded immediately and moved to the slab where Kaelan lay, his healing kit already open in his hands.

"Ser Gideon," you continued, your gaze fixing on your sworn shield. "Secure this chamber. The Silent Guard outside are compromised by a logical paradox, but their programming will eventually resolve. Be prepared for any physical or magical breach. No one enters without my express command. And ensure Kaelan's containment is not jeopardized by his outburst or any unforeseen event."

Gideon gave a single, curt nod, his hand already on his sword. He began a quiet, methodical sweep of the cell, checking the chains, the walls, his posture radiating grim readiness.

"Cressida," you turned to your closest friend, your eyes now holding a different kind of intensity. "Your intellect and observation are paramount. Observe Kaelan's reactions to Rowan's touch, to the light, to our presence. Every flicker. Every sound. His subconscious mind may react to the truths we seek. And should the Silent Guard outside attempt to reassert control, your political acumen will be our first line of defense. Remind them of the higher imperative, of the Imperium's stability."

Cressida, her sharp eyes already taking in the disturbing scene, gave a crisp, decisive nod. "Understood, Sariel."

Magister Kantos, still trembling by the doorway, seemed to shrink further into himself, watching the young princess of fifteen orchestrate this terrifying scene with the chilling authority of a seasoned general. The silence of the cell was now broken by Rowan's gentle murmurs, Gideon's soft, methodical steps, and the faint, low moans of Sergeant Kaelan. The team was assembled. The final confrontation with the truth was about to begin.
