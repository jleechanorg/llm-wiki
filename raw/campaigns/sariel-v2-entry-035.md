Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 10:55 AM.
Location: The Quiet Ward, Deep Archives, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 2 (XP: 1490/2700), Gold: 10gp, HP: 10/10

You followed the Silent Guard, their perfect, silent strides setting an unnerving pace. The heavy stone door of the Chamber of Whispers groaned shut behind you, sealing out the last vestiges of the world you knew. The air in these deeper passages grew heavier, colder, thick with the scent of damp stone and something else... something sterile and unsettling, like fear itself. The labyrinthine corridors twisted and turned, each archway warded with faint, shimmering runes that seemed to hum with suppressed magic.

Your mind, now a relentless engine of cold calculation, observed their every movement. They did not deviate. Their turns were precise, their pace unwavering. Their internal logic, though perturbed, was now fixed on its new directive: *escort*. This was a test. You had forced their compliance, but true control required more.

As they approached a junction where three identical, unlit tunnels diverged, the leading Silent Guard paused, their masked head tilting almost imperceptibly. It was a subtle query to their internal protocols, a moment of processing the next logical turn. You took a deliberate step, your gaze fixed on the central tunnel, and gave a faint, almost imperceptible nod. The Silent Guard's head snapped back forward, and they immediately resumed their path down the central tunnel, as if your unspoken confirmation had been the only input they required. A chilling satisfaction, cold and precise, unfurled within your mind.

The corridors grew narrower, colder, punctuated by heavy iron doors, each one sealed with complex, almost invisible wards. You felt the subtle thrum of their suppressed magic as you passed, a constant reminder of the volatile forces contained within. Kantos, clutching his glowing map, shuffled along behind you, his terrified gasps echoing in the oppressive silence. He watched you and the Silent Guard with an awestruck horror, a scholar witnessing a profound violation of natural law.

The deeper you went, the more the air seemed to hum with a muffled, distant sound – a soft, disorienting chorus of whispers and low moans that seemed to emanate from the very stone. The Quiet Ward. You were almost there.

You felt your body thrumming with a nervous energy, the residual psychic pain a dull counterpoint to the sharp clarity of your mind. Sergeant Kaelan. The Starfall Patient. The living witness to your mother's terrifying truth. The key to unlocking a power you now knew lay dormant in your own blood. You took a slow, deep breath, trying to calm the frantic hammering of your heart, to prepare for the sight of a mind shattered by knowledge. This was not just a historical inquiry; it was a confrontation with the very essence of your mother, a terrifying echo of the monstrous clarity she had found in unmaking. You thought of your own helplessness, your screams, the shame of your failure. You would use that memory, yes. But you would also use Kaelan's eyes, his trauma, to understand the truth of what she became, to truly grasp the nature of the "Joyful Predator" and how to control it without being consumed.

The Silent Guard, leading the way, paused before a particularly heavy, black iron door, its surface devoid of any ornamentation, sealed by a single, pulsing green ward. The whispers from within were louder here, a cacophony of fragmented thoughts and soft, desperate moans. This was it. Kaelan's cell.
