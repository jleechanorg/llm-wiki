Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 11:35 AM.

You pressed your lips into a thin line, the chilling words painting a vivid picture of the ordeal ahead. "Attuning... blurring the line... sanity and madness." The metallic tang of blood still lingered in your mouth, a stark reminder of your previous, uncontrolled attempt to access the Nullification Field from your own memory. You had to prepare. To impose order on the chaos within before attempting to navigate Kaelan's shattered psyche.

"Magister," you commanded, your voice firm, "prepare for these methods. What rituals? What preparations are required? I need to understand the precise steps, the components, the incantations. How do I attune myself to his trauma without succumbing to it? And Rowan," you added, glancing at your gentle friend, "continue your efforts to stabilize Sergeant Kaelan. Ease his torment. We need him as calm as possible."

Kantos, though pale, nodded slowly, his scholarly instincts now fully engaged. He began to murmur to himself, sifting through ancient knowledge, occasionally gesturing for Rowan to fetch specific tools from his healing kit or to adjust Kaelan's position. He spoke of rare herbs for mental clarity, of specific breathing exercises from forbidden ascetic texts, of low-frequency hums that might quiet psychic static. He worked with a meticulous, desperate focus, outlining a series of mental and physical preparations.

You listened, absorbing every detail, even as your mind began its own rigorous preparations. You closed your eyes, focusing on the calm, steady rhythm of your breath. You pushed back against the lingering psychic ache, trying to enforce stillness on the internal storm. You envisioned your mind as a fortress, its walls strengthened, its gates secured, preparing to admit only the precise memories you sought. This was not a test of strength, but of control. You would attune yourself to Kaelan's trauma, yes. But you would not allow it to consume you. The silence of the cell was punctuated by Rowan's soft murmurs, Kantos's hushed instructions, and the faint, low moans of Sergeant Kaelan, a macabre symphony of preparation.

After several minutes, you opened your eyes. Rowan had managed to dim the harshness of Kaelan's moans, replacing them with a softer, almost rhythmic whisper, though the agony remained etched on his features. Kantos, meanwhile, had laid out a small array of crystalline tuning forks, delicate glass vials containing glowing fluids, and a piece of intricately carved bone—tools for a ritual that felt utterly alien to your scholarly pursuits.

You turned to Kantos, your gaze sweeping over the strange, arcane components. Your voice was steady, infused with a cold, almost surgical precision. "Magister," you stated, cutting through the low hum of preparation. "Are your preparations complete? Are we ready to begin? Is there anything further we need to ensure this... attunement... is controlled?"

Kantos looked up, his ancient face etched with a mix of concentration and profound trepidation. He glanced at the delicate components, then at the tormented Sergeant, and finally, at your own pale, determined face. He swallowed hard. "Princess," he rasped, his voice barely audible, "the preparations are... sufficient. But to be truly 'controlled,' such a dangerous ritual demands absolute focus, and a mind unburdened by prior strain." His gaze flickered to your still-bandaged ear, and the subtle tremor in your hand clutching the locket. "Your recent... attempt... it has left you vulnerable. Despite the presence of your loyal companions here, your own reserves are... diminished."

You closed your eyes, the Magister’s words a stark confirmation of your internal state. The previous attempt to access the Nullification Field from your own memory had indeed left you raw, your mind a screaming wound, prone to psychic backlashes. You had forced a glimpse of terrifying power, but paid a heavy price. To attempt a dangerous ritual now, with your reserves diminished, was to invite disaster. You needed to shore up your own defenses, to regain a measure of control over the internal chaos before attempting to navigate Kaelan's.

You pressed your trembling fingers to your temples, attempting to impose stillness on the throbbing chaos within your skull. You focused on the rhythmic *drip, drip, drip* of condensation from the ceiling, trying to empty your mind, to let the static of pain and fear drain away. It was a scholar's meditation, an attempt to bring order to the internal landscape, to prepare the ground for the delicate, dangerous work ahead. You envisioned your mind as a fortress, its walls strengthened, its gates secured, preparing to admit only the precise memories you sought. This was not a test of strength, but of control.
