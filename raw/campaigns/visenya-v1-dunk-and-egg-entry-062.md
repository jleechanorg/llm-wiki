Tell them how Ser Arlan felt the cold seep into his bones, and how he called you to his side while the thunder rolled. It has to be the truth, Dunk—or at least, the part of the truth they can't disprove."

Dunk's jaw tightened, the leather of his reins creaking in his grip. "It *is* the truth, Silver. He dubbed me. He touched my shoulders with his sword and..."

"I know," you interrupted softly, seeing the hesitation in his eyes. "But when you tell them, you mustn't stumble. You must say: 'He dubbed me on his deathbed, for ten years of faithful service, that his sword might continue to serve the King's peace.' Don't mention the ditches or the lack of silver. Speak of the service. Speak of the vow."

Dunk looked out over the golden Reach, his brow furrowed into deep, troubled ruts. "'Faithful service,'" he repeated, the words sounding wooden and heavy. "'Sword... King's peace.' Seven hells, Silver, it sounds like I'm reading from a scroll. I'm a hedge knight, not a mummer. If I try to say it like that, they'll see the lie in my eyes before I'm halfway through."

He shook his head, a frustrated huff escaping his lips. The more you tried to refine the tale, the more Dunk seemed to shrink into himself, his natural honesty rebelling against the polished narrative. He wasn't a man for 'exactly how'—he was a man of the moment, and your attempt to script his past was only making him look more like a man with something to hide.
