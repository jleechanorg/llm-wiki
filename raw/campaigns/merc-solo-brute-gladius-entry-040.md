The cold, still forms of the Miller parents lay on their bed, their lives snuffed out with silent precision. Gladius stood over them, the Silent Strangler’s Cord still in his hand, a chilling extension of his will. He ran a hand over the rough-hewn wooden bedframe, his gaze sharp, taking in the mundane details of their lives – a worn blanket, a chipped ceramic mug on a bedside table, a small, faded painting of a distant mountain scene on the wall. These insignificant objects, once filled with their simple lives, now merely bore witness to their brutal end.

He tightened his grip on the cord, then carefully re-coiled it, returning it to his belt. The greatsword on his back felt like a loyal, silent companion, ready for the next phase. The thought of Corvan, waiting at the inn, a terrified puppet, registered in his mind. A cold hum settled in Gladius's gut; the orchestration of such absolute control, bending others to his will, was a profound satisfaction. The murder of these peasants was merely the first move in a grander game, a game he intended to win. The distant, faint orange glow of the burning shed was a silent promise of the havoc he was creating, a subtle signature in the darkness.

He took one last look at the extinguished lives, a final, dismissive glance. There was nothing more here. Only the quiet evidence of his passage, waiting for Corvan to erase. The night was still young, and there were seven more souls to claim, two more families to silence, before the dawn truly broke over Greenmeadow. The Blackwoods and the Stonehands. They would learn, just as the Millers had, that defying the will of those who wield true power was a fatal mistake.
