The Silent Guard, your silent escorts, paused before a heavy, black iron door, its surface devoid of ornamentation, sealed by a single, pulsing green ward. The whispers from within were louder here, a cacophony of fragmented thoughts and soft, desperate moans that seemed to claw at the very stone. This was it. Kaelan's cell. The air thrummed with a suppressed, disquieting energy.

Despite your mental preparation, a cold knot of dread tightened in your stomach. This was the precipice. You felt the subtle tremor of your body, a visceral response to the truth you were about to face. But your mind, sharp and clear despite the lingering ache behind your eyes, refused to cede control. You needed absolute certainty.

"Magister," you commanded, your voice steady, cutting through the spectral whispers from within the door. You turned to Kantos, whose frail form was hunched over his glowing data-slate map, his face pale with fear. "Confirm our precise location. Point to it on your schematic. Ensure this is indeed Sergeant Kaelan's containment chamber, and that we have followed the correct, warded path." Your Starlit Eyes, burning with a cold, analytical fire, demanded absolute precision.

Kantos fumbled with the data-slate, his ink-stained fingers trembling as he adjusted the glowing schematic. He pointed to a single, pulsing red dot, deep within the intricate labyrinth of the Quiet Ward. "Yes, Princess," he rasped, his voice barely audible above the muffled moans. "This is it. The deepest chamber. Warded to contain... its resident's fractured mind. And to preserve the secret." His gaze met yours, filled with a profound, sorrowful warning. "Sergeant Kaelan is within."

The confirmation resonated with a chilling finality. You were precisely where you needed to be.
