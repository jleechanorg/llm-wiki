The darkness is absolute, a suffocating blanket that amplifies the chilling whispers of Kaelan’s madness. I'm alone, psychically drained, and physically vulnerable. To stumble blindly in this void, to call out without knowing what or who I might provoke, is pure folly. My first priority must be to control the environment. Light. I need light.

I am a scholar, and even a rudimentary understanding of the Weave includes cantrips. I have `Light` as a known spell, a simple, non-aggressive illumination. It's a low-cost, low-risk action. It won't reveal anything hidden by magic, but it will banish the physical darkness, allowing me to assess the chamber, locate Kaelan, and brace myself for what I truly face. It's the most logical first step, a way to impose a sliver of order on this terrifying chaos.
**--- END THINK ---**

The heavy iron door slammed shut behind you with a deafening *CRUMP*, plunging you into an absolute, suffocating blackness. The air was cold, stale, and thick with the overwhelming stench of despair, laced with the metallic tang of dried blood. The whispers and low moans, once distant, now surrounded you, a disorienting chorus of a broken mind that clawed at the very stone. You were utterly alone. Your hand instinctively went to the broken locket at your belt, its cold metal a fragile anchor in the terrifying void.

Panic, cold and sharp, pricked at the edges of your composure. But the analytical core of your mind, honed by years of scholarly pursuit, fought back. You needed to see. You needed information. Light.

You stretched out your trembling hand, focusing your will. The cantrip, `Light`, was a simple thing, one of the first a scholar learned. You pushed a small, controlled trickle of your nascent magical energy into the tip of your index finger, willing it to glow. It flickered hesitantly, your untrained magic struggling, still raw from your earlier backlash. But then, with a small surge of desperate resolve, it coalesced into a steady, soft, golden orb.

The light, though dim, pushed back the oppressive darkness, revealing the horrors within. The chamber was circular, its walls of rough-hewn stone streaked with dark, unidentifiable stains. In the center, bolted to a single, unadorned stone slab, lay Sergeant Kaelan. He was a gaunt, skeletal figure, his Starfall Guard uniform reduced to tattered rags. His once-proud features were twisted into a mask of perpetual, silent agony, his eyes wide and unblinking, staring at a horror only he could see. His limbs were chained, not to restrain him, but to prevent him from tearing himself apart in his madness. The whispers and moans, you realized with a fresh wave of despair, were his own. A constant, low-level drone of fractured thoughts, echoing the horrific truth that had shattered his mind. The room was sterile, devoid of anything else, a tomb for a living ghost.

You stood, frozen, the small orb of light illuminating the devastating reality of the "Starfall Patient." He was not merely catatonic; he was trapped in an endless, screaming loop of memory.
