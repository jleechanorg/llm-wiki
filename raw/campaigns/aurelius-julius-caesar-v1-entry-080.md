Morning (08:40:00) in the Villa of Licinia finds the private solar thick with the scent of spiced honey and the heavy, unsaid weight of political ambition. Legatus Glaber releases Ilithyia’s hand and returns to the table, his face a landscape of veteran caution that contrasts sharply with the youthful radiance of your profile. He looks at you, Aurelius, then at Licinia, who watches him with a stillness that suggests she is already cataloging his fears for your benefit.

"It is a dangerous game we play, Aurelius," Glaber begins, his voice dropping into a low, gravelly rasp that barely carries over the tinkle of the garden fountain outside. "Varinius is a fool, yes, but he is a fool with the Senate's mandate and the ear of men who view a merchant’s success as a personal affront. The disfavor of the powerful in Rome is not a social slight; it is a lethal condition. One poorly placed word in a report, one audit that finds a Julian coin in a 'modest' merchant's pocket, and the ravens will be feasting on our reputations before the week is out."

He leans forward, his eyes feverish as they lock onto yours, the 'Golden Gravity' of your presence seemingly pulling the truth from his lips. "We must stay on his good side—or rather, we must ensure his 'good side' remains a facade that we control. If we strike at him, it cannot be a slap; it must be a surgical removal that leaves the wound clean and the Senate thanking us for the blade. Varinius must believe we are his most loyal supporters even as we prepare the ledger of his ruin. You have the gold, and I have the military authority, but if we lack the subtlety to keep him blind, we are merely building our own funeral pyre."

Ilithyia returns to the couch, her expression mirrored in its calculated devotion to the path her husband has laid out. "My husband is right, Aurelius," she murmurs, her voice silk and steel as she pours a splash of wine for you. "Rome does not forgive those who reach for the sun and stumble. We must be the shadows that ensure your light is never questioned."
