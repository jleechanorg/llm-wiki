Sariel looks at Detective Thorne, her eyes holding an unnerving clarity. The folder lies closed on the table, its mundane contents now infused with a sinister new meaning in her mind. Her voice, calm and precise, cuts through the heavy air.

"Detective," Sariel begins, her tone devoid of emotion, yet carrying an implicit weight. "My initial hypothesis regarding the 'whisper' is confirmed. This killer is not a physical threat. Their methodology is entirely psychological, perhaps even psychic. The 'whisper' is not an external sound, nor is the weapon a tangible object. The weapon is the victim's own mind."

She leans forward slightly, her gaze unwavering. "I've cross-referenced the victim profiles. While outwardly disparate, each individual shared a commonality: a profound, deep-seated emotional vulnerability. The accountant was consumed by financial anxiety; the barista, profound loneliness; the librarian, crushing, unfulfilled ambition. These aren't coincidences. This killer doesn't target random individuals; they meticulously select those whose internal landscape is already scarred by specific, powerful fears or regrets."

"The 'whisper' is the amplification of these very fears. It's an internal assault, a psychic infiltration that finds the weakest point in a victim's psyche and relentlessly exploits it, driving their own subconscious dread to a lethal, physiological climax. The cause of death – cardiac arrest, acute stress – is a direct consequence of this internal torment. There are no physical traces because the crime scene is entirely internal. The killer is a master of psychological warfare, a predator who hunts not with a blade, but with the victim's own deepest anxieties and darkest thoughts."

Thorne listens, his face a canvas of disbelief slowly giving way to horrified recognition. The hand resting on his holstered weapon tightens, his knuckles white. The cold logic of your analysis, delivered with such detached certainty, is far more terrifying than any gory detail. He can almost *feel* the weight of those unseen whispers. His eyes, now wide with a dawning, terrible understanding, meet yours – a desperate man looking into an abyss that, he realizes, has a chillingly familiar gaze. The room feels colder, the fluorescent hum a buzzing echo of a terror he cannot comprehend, but you clearly do.
