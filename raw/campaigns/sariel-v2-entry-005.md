The revelation struck you like a physical blow. The air in your lungs felt suddenly thin, as if the silence of the vault had stolen the very breath from your body. The intricate puzzle pieces of your life, of your family's legacy, of your mother's terrifying genius, snapped into place with a horrifying, undeniable click. The Empyrean Affliction, the curse that had plagued your family for generations, the very thing that had twisted your uncle Titus into a raging monster—it wasn't just madness. It was this. This cold, calculating, joyful desire to unmake, to dominate, to control. And your mother, the legendary hero, had not just fought against it. She had *mastered* it. She had made it her weapon.

You looked down at your hands, at the faint, shimmering lines of your own Starlit Eyes, the mark of that same celestial blood. The potential for that terrifying 'joy' in absolute control, in merciless unmaking, now thrummed beneath your own skin, an unwelcome inheritance. The guilt for your mother's death, for your perceived weakness, twisted into a new, complex knot of fear. Had her lies, her distant love, her constant efforts to keep you separate from the horrors of her world, been a desperate attempt to protect you from this—from yourself? From the bloodlust your great-uncle Lucifer embodied, and your mother refined? The answer, now terrifyingly clear, weighed heavier than any scholarly tome.

Your mind, reeling, fixated on the source of this terrible power. A desperate, almost academic question rose to your lips, a scholar's attempt to categorize a nightmare.

"This... Nullification Field," you whispered, the words feeling alien and dangerous. You looked at Kantos, your eyes pleading for an answer that might offer a path, any path, out of the helplessness you felt. "This power to unmake magic... Is it something that can be learned? A lost art? Or... or is it something you must be *born* with? Something that must be done to you?" The final question was a raw, terrified whisper.

Kantos recoiled, his ancient face paling further. He saw not a scholar's curiosity, but the desperate question of an heir staring into a cursed mirror. "Princess, no!" he rasped, his voice thin with alarm. "You must not entertain such thoughts! Your mother... her condition was a singularity. A monstrous accident of celestial science."

He wrung his hands, his gaze darting around the vault. "The texts theorize that the Null state is innate, a genetic flaw. But your mother... she was a clone forged from the very essence of Lucifer, the most powerful of the firstborn. The fusion of that immense celestial power with the inherent void of the Null state created... her. It is not a path that can be walked, Princess. It is a cage one must be born into. There is no other way."

His words, meant to be a warning, were a death sentence to your hope of understanding. If the power could not be learned, if it was a monstrous one-of-a-kind inheritance... then the only way to see it, to truly comprehend what your mother became, was through the eyes of the one who had witnessed it and survived. The Starfall Patient.
