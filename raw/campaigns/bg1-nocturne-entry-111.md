Game Master:
You lean over the altar, your shadow falling across the holy symbol of Helm like a stain. The Mayor recoils, clutching the pouch of five hundred gold pieces he had prepared, but you do not reach for it. Instead, you rest your hand on the hilt of *Varscona*, the frost from the blade misting in the warm temple air.

"Five hundred gold," you repeat, your voice low and devoid of gratitude. "For the head of a Cyricist cleric. For the lives of your soldiers. For the iron that keeps this town from starving."

You step closer, invading his personal space. The *Hunger* sharpens your gaze, drilling into his fear. "That covers the expenses, Mayor. It does not cover the *risk*. And it certainly doesn't cover the silence of my company regarding the... vulnerabilities we found in your defenses."

**Intimidation Check: 16 (Success)**

Ghastkill swallows hard, sweat beading on his bald pate. He looks at the grim faces of your mercenaries—Kagain's avarice, Roric's cold smile, and the terrifying, blank stare of Xan. He realizes he is not negotiating with heroes; he is negotiating with a force of nature.

"I... I have no more garrison funds!" he stammers.

"Then open the box," you command, pointing to the heavy iron poor box bolted to the altar. "The gods have been paid enough in prayer. Today, they pay in gold."

With a trembling hand, the Mayor unlocks the temple coffers. He pours the contents onto the map—a cascade of silver, copper, and gold, along with a small, velvet bag containing a **Star Sapphire**. He adds the original reward pouch to the pile.

"And the letter," you add, sliding a piece of parchment and a quill across the altar. "To the Dukes of Baldur's Gate. You will write that my company saved Nashkel when Amn could not. You will commend us as agents of order. And you will sign it with the seal of Helm."

Ghastkill writes. He has no choice.

**The Road to Beregost.**

You leave Nashkel heavier in coin and reputation. The march north is swift, fueled by victory. You bypass the trade roads, cutting through the foothills to avoid the Flaming Fist patrols that might still be looking for the 'Butchers of Beregost', though with the Mayor's letter in your pocket, you now have a shield against their law.

**Mirtul 9, Morning.**

Beregost is waking up as you arrive. You enter the town not as fugitives, but as predators returning to a hunting ground. You head straight for the **Feldepost Inn**, the location mentioned in Mulahey's letters.

The inn is upscale, a place for wealthy merchants and travelers who can afford to ignore the iron crisis. The common room is quiet, smelling of beeswax and roast boar. In the corner, sitting alone at a table near the hearth, is a man in grey robes. He is reading a spellbook, a glass of wine untouched at his elbow. He looks bored, arrogant, and entirely too clean for a mercenary.

**Tranzig.**

**Roric** slides into the shadows near the door, his hand drifting to his new gauntlets. **Xan** shudders. "That is him," the elf whispers. "The voice from the dark."

Tranzig turns a page, oblivious to the six killers who have just walked through the door. You have the element of surprise.
