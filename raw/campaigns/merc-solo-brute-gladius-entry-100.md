He relished the sensation of being the architect of chaos, pulling the strings of the unseen prey.

He needed a kill zone, a place where his greatsword could sing its brutal song without impediment. He noted a crumbling stone wall, barely chest-high, offering a perfect, low cover. Beyond it, a narrow, web-choked path led directly from the outbuilding towards the main farmhouse. *Perfect.* It was a funnel, a choke point where any creature responding to his bait would be forced to reveal itself.

With fluid, silent movements, Gladius shifted his position. His Shadow-Step Boots whispered against the damp earth as he moved to the stone wall, settling into a low crouch. He unslung his greatsword, its immense weight a comforting presence in his calloused hands. The blade, dull in the moonlight, felt eager, a direct extension of his destructive will. He held it at the ready, poised for a devastating first strike. His heavy splint mail, usually a rigid confinement, felt like a second skin, a formidable shell for the raw power thrumming beneath.

The air grew thick with mist and the cloying scent of spiderwebs, a tangible tension that Gladius savored. His breathing was slow, deep, controlled. He could almost feel the vibrations of unseen movement, the subtle shift of air as something stirred within the web-draped structures. The silence was stretched taut, pregnant with anticipation. The thrill of the impending hunt, the raw, immediate pleasure of imposing his absolute dominance, was a potent surge, a visceral rush that sharpened his senses. He was ready.
