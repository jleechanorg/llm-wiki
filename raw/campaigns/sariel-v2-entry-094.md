Game Master:
[Mode: DM MODE]

Understood. You have provided a `GOD MODE:` command, highlighting a crucial point: "Why are my companions so much higher level than me? They should be around the same level."

You are absolutely correct to raise this, and I sincerely apologize. This is a significant discrepancy that impacts game balance and the collaborative feeling of the party. My initial character generation for companions (Gideon, Cressida) leaned too heavily on their established narrative roles as mature, highly skilled individuals *in the context of the larger world's power scale*, rather than balancing their levels with Sariel's starting point. This breaks the feeling of a growing party and makes Sariel's progression less impactful.

This is a major issue, and I need to propose a direct solution to fix it immediately.

**Analysis of Companion Levels vs. Player Expectation:**

*   **Sariel (Level 2):** Currently focused on intellectual discovery and latent magical potential.
*   **Gideon (Level 14 Fighter, Age 25):** His dossier describes him as a "prodigy" who served in the Starfall Guard and is a "Sworn Shield." Level 14 for a 25-year-old is high, reflecting his veteran status in a world of celestials, but it's disproportionate to Sariel.
*   **Cressida (Level 12 Expert, Age 16):** Her dossier emphasizes her "political acumen," "cunning," and "mastermind rogue" traits. Level 12 for a 16-year-old, even a prodigy, is exceptionally high, making her too capable of handling complex political challenges far above Sariel's current ability.
*   **Rowan (Level 3 Expert, Age 16):** His level is appropriate for his role as a non-combatant scholar/healer.

**The Problem:** While these levels reflect their canonical competence in the broader world lore, they make them too dominant for a starting party experience, where the player character's growth should be central.

**Proposed Major Retcon and Solution:**

To create a more balanced and engaging party dynamic, where Sariel's journey and choices are truly paramount, and her companions grow alongside her, I propose the following:

1.  **Level Adjustment for Balance:** We will significantly adjust Gideon and Cressida's starting levels to be more in line with a balanced party for a Level 2 PC, allowing them to still be competent in their roles but also having room for growth.
    *   **Ser Gideon Vance:** Reduce to **Level 5**. He will still be an accomplished, strong, and highly disciplined Knight of the Starfall Guard for his age (25), but now a formidable, *emerging* protector rather than a veteran master. His loyalty and discipline will remain absolute.
    *   **Lady Cressida Valeriana:** Reduce to **Level 4**. At 16, a Level 4 Expert is still exceptionally bright, witty, and politically astute for her age, but she will be a *developing* political operator, learning the ropes alongside Sariel, rather than a seasoned "mastermind." Her ambition and loyalty will remain.
2.  **Narrative Recontextualization:** We will adjust their character dossiers (internal only) to reflect these lower levels while retaining their core personality, archetypes, and motivations. Their "prodigy" status will now be in the context of being exceptionally talented for their *new* lower level, rather than being high-level masters. Their skills will be potent *for their level*, but not world-shattering.

This will ensure Sariel's unique development and ability to command more powerful beings (like the Silent Guard) remains a highlight, while her direct companions are impactful but not overshadowing. It also allows for more satisfying character progression for them.

**Please confirm:** Do you approve this major retcon to Gideon and Cressida's levels, understanding it will be reflected in their dossiers and future narrative portrayals? Once approved, I will implement it and resume the story.
