The shouts of the Flaming Fist echo off the stone walls of the alley, but they are growing distant. The chaos you unleashed in the square acts as a smoke screen, pulling the eyes of the law toward the carnage while you slip through the veins of the city.

You shove Garrick into the shadows of a rotting crate, pinning him with a look that promises death if he makes a sound. Then you turn to Roric. The rogue is breathing hard, wiping blood—not his own—from his cheek, but his eyes are bright with the thrill of the heist.

"We can't stay on the street," you state, your voice flat and composed. "The gates will be locked down within the hour. I need a bolt-hole, Roric. A fence, a safe house, a cellar—somewhere the Fist doesn't look. Do you have a contact, or do we need to kill a homeowner for their basement?"

Roric grins, though he glances nervously at the mouth of the alley. "No need to evict any widows yet. I know a guy. **Kagain**. Runs a mercenary charter out of a shop near the Feldepost Inn. He's a dwarf, mean as a badger and half as charming, but he hates the Flaming Fist and loves gold. If we can pay his 'entry fee', he won't care who we killed."

"Lead the way," you command.

***

**Kagain's Shop.**

The sun has set by the time you reach the unassuming storefront in the southern district. The sign above the door depicts a battered shield and a bag of gold. Roric picks the lock on the back door—a professional courtesy to avoid the street—and you slip inside, dragging the sobbing Garrick with you.

The interior smells of pipe tobacco, stale beer, and gun oil. Sitting behind a heavy desk littered with wanted posters is a dwarf with a beard like iron wire and eyes like flint. He has a heavy axe leaning against his chair and a loaded crossbow on the desk.

**Kagain** doesn't flinch as three bloodied fugitives break into his shop. He simply takes a drag from his pipe and exhales a cloud of blue smoke.

"You're bleeding on my floor," Kagain rumbles, his voice like gravel grinding together. "And judging by the noise uptown, you're the reason the Fist is running around like headless chickens."

He eyes the heavy purse at your belt, then the Harper cloak pin dangling from your pocket. A slow, greedy smile spreads across his face.

"I charge for privacy," the dwarf says, tapping the crossbow. "And I charge extra for Harper-killers. **100 gold** for the night. No questions asked. You can sleep in the cellar with the rats... and the rest of my inventory."
