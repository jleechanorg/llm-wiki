V. Integrated Game Mechanics: The "Prodigy" Scaling
As you consume more Force users, your "Vaylin" side grows stronger, making the "Nocturne" mask harder to maintain.
The Instability Meter (0 - 100)
0-20 (Stable): You are Nocturne. High Charisma. Subtle manipulation.
21-50 (Fraying): Your eyes occasionally flash violet. You get +2 to Damage but -2 to Persuasion.
51-80 (Volatile): Electronics break near you. You hear voices. You gain access to "Overload" (Maximum damage on Force powers) but take damage when you use it.
81-100 (Unbound): You are Vaylin. You cannot use Stealth or Deception. You radiate an aura of Terror (Wisdom Save or be Frightened). The Emperor knows exactly where you are.
Managing Instability:
Meditation: Lowers instability.
Killing with Lightsaber (Physical): Keeps you grounded.
Using Force Powers: Increases instability.
Consuming a Force User: Resets instability to 0 (The hunger is sated), but raises your permanent "Base Power Level."

Forget the stories of the "Eternal Fleet" or the "Outlander." Those futures have been burned away.
In this timeline, the Sith Emperor (Vitiate) never vanished. The "silence" that plagued the Dark Council was not an abandonment—it was a gestation period. Now, the Emperor has returned to the Citadel on Dromund Kaas in full power. He is not a distant figurehead; he is an active, terrifying God-King who walks the halls of the Sanctum.
The Dark Council is no longer a bickering oligarchy; they are terrified servants. When the Emperor speaks, planets die. He has dispensed with the subtlety of the Cold War. He is preparing for something massive—a ritual of such scale that it requires the resources of the entire galaxy. He is stripping the Empire for parts to feed his own ascension.
The Atmosphere: Dromund Kaas is no longer just rainy; it is suffocating. The Dark Side is so potent in the capital city that non-Force users suffer from chronic migraines and nightmares. The air tastes of copper and ozone. The populace walks with their heads down, terrified that He might be watching. To be noticed by the Emperor is to be consumed.

You operate in his shadow. While the Hand of the Emperor (his personal executioners) hunts down his enemies, you move through the aristocracy, fixing the cracks in his unstable regime. You feel his presence constantly—a cold, heavy weight at the back of your skull. He is the static in your mind.

Captain Valerius Thorne, a man of honor and logic, has seen the data. He knows this order isn't strategy; it's suicide. It’s genocide of their own people. He is quietly building a coalition of officers to refuse the order—a mutiny born of loyalty to the people of the Empire, not the Sith.
The Conflict: You have been tasked by the Hand of the Emperor to "fix" Thorne. You are expected to enter his inner circle, expose his co-conspirators, and publicly execute him to terrify the military into submission.

The Meta-Game: The Hunger Mechanic (Updated)
With the Emperor active, the Hunger mechanic we discussed earlier is now tied to his influence.
The Emperor's Tithe:
You must perform a significant act of manipulation or cruelty every 3 sessions.
Success: You gain a "Favor of the Father" token. You can spend this to reroll a failed Force check (representing him lending you power).
Failure: You gain a "Gaze of the Dragon" point. If you accumulate 3, the Emperor physically summons you to the Throne Room for a "test" (a high-lethality encounter).
