Itachi moved with tireless efficiency, his **Nature Manipulation** guiding them through the dense wilderness, bypassing any lingering patrols or natural hazards effortlessly. Sasuke, fueled by a terrifying, re-forged resolve, matched his pace, his young face grimly set, his Sharingan occasionally flaring with a raw, unfocused power.

The journey to the hidden cave base was a test, subtle yet profound. Itachi observed Sasuke constantly, dissecting his brother's reactions to the harsh terrain, the prolonged silence, and the chilling realization that he was truly abandoning everything. Sasuke never wavered. His questions were few, sharp, and focused on vengeance: "When do we start training?" "How do we find Obito's loyalists?" "Will I be strong enough to kill him?" After three days of relentless, covert travel, they arrived. The roar of the waterfall, once a solitary comfort to Itachi, now greeted Sasuke, a new, unsettling home. Itachi led him into the cool, damp confines of the hidden cave. Sasuke's eyes, wide with a blend of awe and apprehension, scanned the cavern: the faint hum of the chakra siphon, the ancient scrolls, and the subtle, chilling aura of the sealed prison where Obito lay.

Sasuke nodded, his young body trembling slightly, partly from exhaustion, partly from the sheer weight of destiny now laid upon him. "Yes, Brother." He moved to the chamber, his loyalty now absolute.
