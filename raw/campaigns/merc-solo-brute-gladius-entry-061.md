The scent of cooking oats and the lingering warmth of the hearth wrapped around Gladius as he slipped into the Stonehand dwelling. Outside, the muffled voices of the burly man and his wife, drawn away by Gladius's deliberate chaos, faded as they investigated the ruined shed. Their simple curiosity was a pathetic distraction, a testament to their profound vulnerability. A cold, precise satisfaction settled in Gladius's gut; the raw, immediate pleasure of unchallenged entry, of taking possession of their most private space.

He moved with the fluid, silent grace of a predator, his Shadow-Step Boots whispering against the worn stone floor. His greatsword, a familiar weight on his back, felt eager. The main door, left ajar in their hasty departure, was Gladius's first focus. With a quiet grunt of effort, he swung the heavy oak slab shut, the resounding *thud* muted only by the dwelling's thick stone walls. He slid a sturdy wooden bar across its interior, the scrape of wood against stone a quiet, final click. No one was getting in or out that way.

His gaze swept the common room. A small, unlatched window near the hearth, meant for ventilation, was next. He reached it, his large hand surprisingly deft, and expertly secured its flimsy latch. The simple, domestic setting, filled with the warmth of a dying fire, felt almost mocking in its innocence. This was their perceived sanctuary, their safe haven. And now, it was his cage, sealed by his own hands. The thought brought a subtle tightening in his chest, a primal satisfaction in his control. He envisioned the family's return, their eventual realization of the trap, the dawning horror in their eyes. It would be a delicious moment, another layer to the sensory experience of their demise. The sun, a faint promise of orange and purple on the horizon, would soon reveal the full extent of his chilling work.
