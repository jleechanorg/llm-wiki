The heavy *thud* of the door slamming shut, followed by the metallic click of the bar, echoed in the Stonehand dwelling, a sound of absolute finality. The burly farmer and his wife froze, their faces contorted in dawning horror, their eyes wide and fixed on the limp, gagged form of their child. The air, thick with the scent of hearth smoke and their burgeoning terror, vibrated with unspoken fear.

Gladius emerged from the shadows beside the door, his immense frame filling the doorway, his splint mail a dark, menacing silhouette in the dim, pre-dawn light filtering through the grimy window. The Silent Strangler's Cord, a dark whisper of death, was coiled in his hand. His gaze, cold and unblinking, swept over their terrified faces, savoring the raw, visceral pleasure of their shattered composure. This was the ultimate experience of control, the true hedonism of power. Their simple strength, their boisterous defiance, was dissolving into pathetic, quivering fear.

"Quiet," Gladius rumbled, his voice a low, guttural growl that seemed to vibrate through the very stone of the house. He didn't shout; he didn't need to. The single word, heavy with implied menace, was enough. He took a slow, deliberate step towards them, then another, drawing their eyes to the unconscious child, then to his own impassive face. "Or I snap its neck in front of you," he stated, his voice flat, devoid of emotion, a chilling promise. His gaze flickered to the greatsword, propped subtly behind the door, then back to their faces, allowing the implied brutality to sink in. "Then you'll follow. Loudly, or quietly. Your choice."
