Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 11:20 AM.
Location: Sergeant Kaelan's Cell, Quiet Ward, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 2 (XP: 4730/2700), Gold: 10gp, HP: 10/10

The raw, unfiltered horror of Kaelan's memories still clawed at your mind, the metallic taste of blood a constant reminder of the psychic backlash. You had glimpsed it – the terrifying clarity of your mother's power, the chilling efficiency in unmaking – but the vision was chaotic, uncontrolled, and utterly devastating. Rowan continued his gentle murmurs beside the tormented Sergeant, while Gideon maintained his silent vigil, and Cressida watched with an intensity that missed nothing. Magister Kantos hovered by the door, pale and anxious. The air in the cell, thick with despair and the hum of Kaelan’s madness, felt heavy and suffocating.

You pushed yourself upright, swaying slightly, but your posture was rigid with a new, grim purpose. The time for individual exploration was over. You needed their minds, their skills, their unwavering loyalty. You needed a different approach.

You swept your gaze across your companions, meeting each of their eyes in turn. Your face, pale and streaked with fresh tears, was a stark testament to the horror you had just witnessed. Your voice, though raw and trembling, was firm, cutting through the spectral whispers of Kaelan’s madness. "We have a problem," you stated, your gaze fixed on Kaelan’s tormented form, then sweeping across their faces. "A problem far more complex than any of us anticipated. And a truth that will shatter everything you thought you knew about my mother."

You paused, drawing a ragged breath, the words tasting like ash. "Sergeant Kaelan," you explained, gesturing to the tormented figure, "is not merely mad. His mind was shattered by witnessing a truth too profound for mortal comprehension. My mother... Magister Arcanus... her public identity was a lie. She was not a Null. She possessed a terrifying, singular power: the Nullification Field. An ability that silenced all magic within its radius. And, impossibly, she could wield her *own* magic within that void."

You saw Gideon’s eyes widen behind his helm, a flicker of profound disbelief quickly replaced by a deeper, almost fearful respect. Cressida’s sharp gaze snapped to Kantos, demanding silent confirmation, which the old Magister provided with a trembling nod. Rowan, still tending to Kaelan, visibly flinched, his gentle face etched with a horror that mirrored your own.

"The Null Genesis journals," you continued, your voice dropping, "confirm this. They theorize this power, this chilling efficiency in unmaking, is a refined manifestation of the Empyrean Affliction itself—a perversion of celestial will, a lucid desire for absolute control. And it is linked to Lucifer's own essence. Kaelan witnessed her embrace this power, this... cold, terrifying precision in destruction. He saw her unmoved, while his comrades died believing she was a suffering hero sacrificing herself for them."

Your voice cracked with the raw pain of the betrayal. "He saw the ultimate lie. And his mind could not endure it. I tried to access his memories with `Comprehend Languages`. I glimpsed it. The Nullification. The sheer power. But the backlash was immense. His mind is a maelstrom of trauma and paradox. It’s walled off. We cannot simply force our way in without risking further harm to him, or shattering my own fragile composure."

You met their gazes, your voice now pleading, laced with a desperate urgency. "We need to access his memories. To understand. To find the blueprint of her power. But we cannot break him further. And I cannot risk breaking myself. How do we do this? How do we access a mind shattered by ultimate truth? I need your ideas. All of them."

The silence in the cell was heavy, filled with the echoes of your horrifying revelations and Kaelan's low, persistent moans. Your companions, now armed with the impossible truth of Alexiel's deception, looked from the tormented Sergeant to you, the fragile princess who had just revealed herself to be the unwitting heir to a terrifying, hidden power. The gravity of your words settled upon them, a crushing weight of cosmic secrets and devastating personal betrayal. They understood.

You turned back to Kaelan, ignoring the shocked faces of your companions for a moment. The fragments of his madness still echoed in your mind. You knew what you had to do. You needed to try again with `Comprehend Languages`, but this time, you would impose a filter. You would not seek the raw, overwhelming torrent of his trauma. You would seek only the specific, mechanical data: the precise visual of the Nullification Field, the nuanced expression on your mother's face, the precise *feel* of the "unmaking."

You extended your hand once more, hovering it inches above Kaelan's forehead. Your breath was ragged, but your will was a diamond-hard point of focus. You closed your eyes, pushing the fragile trickle of your nascent magic into the spell. You felt the familiar disorientation, the pull of his fractured mind. This time, as the torrent began, you consciously fought back, imposing a mental filter, a scholar’s precise demand for only the relevant data.

The feedback was still immense, but it was *sharper*. Less chaotic. You saw it: not a continuous loop, but a series of precise, repeating flashes. The sudden, agonizing *silence* as magic died. The raw, desperate struggle of the Apostates, their wings sputtering, their power *gone*. The frantic, futile grappling of the Starfall Guard, their faces contorted in suicidal devotion. And then, *her*. Your mother. Rising from her feigned collapse. Her four wings of light erupting. Her sword, burning white. And her eyes: wide, unblinking, filled with a cold, absolute clarity. There was no joy, no sadness, no fear. Only the terrifying, profound satisfaction of a perfect, elegant solution. The precise, devastating ruthlessness of a predator unleashing its final, unanswerable strike. You saw her *will* it, the very act of unmaking, precise and undeniable.

You gasped, tearing your hand away, a thin trickle of blood running from your nose. The psychic backlash still left you trembling, but this time, you did not collapse. You had glimpsed it. Not the full horror, but the chilling, precise mechanism. The power. The control. The cold, unblinking eyes of the truth.
