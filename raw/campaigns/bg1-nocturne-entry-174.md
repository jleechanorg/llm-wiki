The day of preparation passes in a blur of violence and discipline. The **Fortified Armory** becomes a hive of activity. **Roric** and **Kagain** turn the perimeter into a nightmare of tripwires and deadfalls, ensuring that any scavenger foolish enough to probe the ruins of the Bandit Camp will find only maiming steel. **Minsc** patrols the woods with **Boo**, returning with the carcasses of two wild boars—sustenance for the march.

But the true prize is found in the deep rot of the forest floor. Driven by the failure of the previous day, you stalk the undergrowth with a cold, surgical focus. This time, the *Hunger* does not make you hasty; it makes you perfect.

You locate a nest of Giant Centipedes beneath a fallen weirwood. You do not just kill them; you harvest them. With the precision of an alchemist, you extract the venom sacs intact, refining the toxin over a low fire into a concentrated paste. You secure **3 fresh doses** of Crawler Mucus. Your blade is now wet with death.

**Mirtul 11 concludes.** The base is sealed. The traps are set. The raven has flown to **Garrick** in Nashkel, summoning a garrison that will arrive long after you are gone. Somewhere to the south, **Oublek** is likely sleeping fitfully, counting the gold you didn't take, while **Silke**—if she still lives—plots her next move in the shadows of Baldur's Gate. **Sarevok** waits ahead, a mountain of iron and ambition. You have settled your debts with the past; now you look only to the future.

**Mirtul 12, Dawn.**

You march. The journey to the **Cloakwood** is a transition from the mundane danger of the bandit lands to something older and far more malevolent. The trees grow colossal, their branches weaving a canopy so thick it blots out the sun. The air grows heavy, tasting of copper and webs.

By midday, you reach the perimeter marked on Ender Sai's map. The forest here is draped in thick, sticky webbing that spans between the trees like the rigging of a ghost ship. The silence is profound, broken only by the chittering of things that have too many legs.

**Dynaheir** stops, her staff glowing faintly. "The Weave is tangled here," she whispers. "There are traps... not of metal, but of silk. And watchers."

**Xan** shudders, drawing his Moonblade. "Spiders. Why did it have to be spiders? We are marching into a larder."

A massive web blocks the path ahead. Through the translucent strands, you can see the shapes of **Giant Spiders** moving in the gloom, bloated and waiting. An **Ettercap** hangs from a branch above, watching you with alien, multi-faceted eyes.
