Location: Forest Edge, Near Man Tibbet's Farm, Early Evening

The cool night air settled around Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) as she found a secluded, well-hidden spot deep within the forest bordering Tibbet's farm. The six bundled forms of her captives lay nearby, silent and secured. The physical exertion of the past hours, while not debilitating, had drained her EP reserves and left her Channel Divinity expended. A strategist optimized their resources. Rest was not a luxury, but a tactical necessity.

She settled against the rough bark of an ancient tree, her Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate blending with the deep shadows. For the next hour, she allowed her mind to shift from active combat and infiltration to a more contemplative state, conserving energy. The sounds of the forest – the rustle of leaves, the distant hoot of an owl – became a soothing backdrop to her internal machinations. Her breath evened, her muscles relaxed, and a subtle hum of returning power resonated within her.

After precisely one hour, Nocturne felt her inner reserves replenish. Her EP flowed, filling the depleted pool, and the raw potential of her Channel Divinity returned, ready for use. She was restored, an apex predator fully primed for the next phase.

*Nocturne's Internal Monologue & Planning for Bargaining with Kaelen:*

*The contract was for 'peasants', 'easy pickings'. Kaelen's intelligence was critically flawed. This was a guarded outpost of House Durin's Folly, complete with professional guards, an arcane ward, and secret Shadowfell operations. My risk and effort were exponentially higher than anticipated. This translates directly to increased compensation. Kaelen, a nervous opportunist, will understand the language of risk and profit.*

*My leverage is substantial. I possess concrete evidence of Durin's Folly's clandestine activities: the deed, the ledger, the Orbs of Scrying, and most importantly, the ciphered journal and the Shadowfell maps. This information is far more valuable to House Valerius, and to any rival of Durin's Folly, than a mere handful of peasants. Kaelen will either comply with my demands, or I will use this intelligence to my own advantage, potentially disrupting his operation or going over his head directly to the Shadow Weavers, or even my father.*

*The negotiation will be precise. I will demand a significantly higher payment for the 'stock', citing the unforeseen and substantial security measures. I will imply, without directly stating, that I uncovered more than just farmers. I will also make it clear that for future contracts of this nature, **employer-provided transport and a detailed, accurate threat assessment are non-negotiable**. My efficiency and success come at a premium, and I am not a beast of burden.*

*I hold the power in this negotiation. Kaelen needs the 'stock' delivered cleanly; I delivered, despite his inadequate intelligence. The Shadow Weavers will value efficiency and discretion. I will present the facts of the increased risk, the neutralized security, and my successful acquisition, all while holding the true intel in reserve as ultimate leverage.*

*The night is still young. The captives are secured. Now, it is time to turn this risk into a greater reward.*
