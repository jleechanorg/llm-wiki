Location: Man Tibbet's Farm, Near the Barn, Early Evening

The cool night air was still, broken only by the distant chirping of crickets. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) stood, a dark, silent figure amidst the quiet farmland. Her message to Kaelen Flinn was sent, the new terms laid bare. While awaiting his response, her meticulous mind, ever seeking absolute control, turned to the final lingering defense of Tibbet's farm: the arcane ward near the barn. Her **Apex Senses** scan had revealed its subtle thrum, a detection mechanism linked to the now-neutralized guards. Leaving it active was an unnecessary loose end, a potential future complication.

She moved with a chilling grace, her Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate blending with the deep shadows cast by the barn. The arcane thrum grew more pronounced as she approached, a faint, almost imperceptible vibration in the air. Her expertise lay in strategy and combat, not direct arcane manipulation, but her formidable intellect (Intelligence 18) was her greatest tool. She intended to understand and dismantle this magical defense, not merely bypass it.

She knelt near the barn's foundation, her fingers probing the rough-hewn stones, seeking the subtle points where the ward's energy was woven. Her eyes, pools of intense concentration, traced the faint, almost invisible lines of arcane force shimmering in the air. It was a simple detection ward, designed for basic perimeter alerts, but expertly crafted to be subtle. She needed to unravel its magical logic, to find the flaw in its weave, or sever its connection.

A faint, almost imperceptible hum resonated through Nocturne's fingers as she touched a specific point on the barn's foundation. It was like feeling a taut string suddenly go slack. The subtle arcane pulse she had detected earlier, the one that had vibrated faintly beneath the ground, vanished. The air around the barn felt... normal again. The ward was gone, its magic unraveled, its purpose negated. It would no longer detect intrusions, nor would it alert anyone.

Nocturne stood, dusting off her hands, a cool satisfaction settling over her. The final piece of the farm's active defense was now dismantled. The operation was now completely clean, the site utterly compromised, its secrets extracted, its inhabitants subdued. All variables were managed. She had bought herself absolute control over the farm for the coming hours.

The night was still young, and the six bundled forms lay hidden in the forest, awaiting their fate. Kaelen's response, and the true future of the Durin's Folly intel, still hung in the balance. But for now, Nocturne had ensured that this particular chessboard square was entirely her own.
