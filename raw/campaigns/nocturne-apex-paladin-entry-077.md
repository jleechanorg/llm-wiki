Location: Man Tibbet's Farm, Barn Side, Early Evening

The air was still, save for the distant, rhythmic chirping of crickets and the soft, almost imperceptible hum of the magical ward near the barn. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) stood over the concealed body of the first farm guard, a ghost in her Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate. The thrill of the silent kill lingered, a cold satisfaction in the perfection of her execution. One obstacle removed. The farm, with its oblivious family and its remaining sentinel, awaited her next move. The objective, the 'large family' for the lucrative Underdark contract, was within reach, but the second guard represented the immediate, critical impediment.

Her mind, a relentless engine of strategic thought, immediately shifted to the remaining threat. She had used the subtle jingle of a bell once, and it had worked. But a repeat might be met with greater suspicion. This second guard, likely an equally trained professional, would be more cautious now that his partner had not returned from a simple investigation. The lure needed to be more compelling, more directly related to the guard's duties, or to the farmhouse itself.

She considered the details gleaned from her **Apex Senses** scan: the second guard was likely on the second floor, near the embrasure, maintaining vigilance over the approach. He wouldn't leave his post for a simple sound. What would draw him down, and away, without raising a general alarm?

A flicker of light, perhaps, from a window that should be dark. Or a faint, human-like sound of distress or minor struggle from an area he was meant to secure, but not directly threatening. This would appeal to his professional duty, his training.

Her gaze drifted to a small, isolated outbuilding near the farmhouse, a root cellar perhaps, with a small, unlatched window. A faint, almost imperceptible scratching or muffled sound from within, accompanied by a quick, masked flash of light, could simulate a minor intrusion. Something that demanded immediate, discreet investigation by a single guard, not a full alarm. The darkness of the night, amplified by her Shadow-Weave Half Plate, would be her cloak.

This would require a precise setup, a brief, controlled exposure to create the necessary illusion of vulnerability. Her intent was not to alarm the family, but to draw the vigilant guard into her prepared ambush. The lure would be just enough to pull him from his post, into the silent embrace of the darkness.
