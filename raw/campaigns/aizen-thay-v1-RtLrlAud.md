Part I: The Codex of the Sovereign
1. The Nature of the Pact: The Mirror and the Moth
You are not a Warlock in the traditional sense, you are a special Abyss knight class. You did not scribble your name in blood on a piece of parchment for a devil who views you as a soul to be collected in fifty years. You are Aizen Sosuke, the "Spare" of a ruined house, and you have entered into a courtship with a living singularity.
The pact is physical object: The Obsidian Mirror.
It is a shard of black glass, cold as the void between stars, framed in twisted iron that resembles thorny vines. It does not reflect your face; it reflects Hers. When you look into it, you are peering directly into the Shattered Rose, her private sub-plane in the Abyss. The connection is a two-way street of psychic intimacy and terrifying scrutiny. She watches you sleep. She watches you kill. She watches you bleed. And you watch her rule a kingdom of dead gods and enslaved demons, studying her every move so that one day you might replicate it.
The dynamic is defined by Weaponized Desire. She grants you power not because she is generous, but because she is bored and hungry. She treats you like a favorite pet, a dangerous toy, and a potential lover, all while expecting you to feed the engine of her ascension. You are her Material Anchor, the only thing keeping her tethered to the world that rejected her.
2. The Mythos: The Rise of the Black Diamond
To serve her, you must understand the history she rewrote. The galaxy believes the "Crisis of the Absolute" was solved by a band of heroes. You know the truth: it was solved by a hostile takeover.
Timeline Shift: +100 Years post-Ascension.
Era: The Century of Silence.
Protagonist: Aizen Sosuke (The Last Scion of House Sosuke).
Patron: Nocturne Sosuke (Great-Grandmother / Ancestral Sovereign).
The history books call it the "Sovereign’s Vanishing." One hundred years ago, a sixteen-year-old girl conquered the Sword Coast, the Hells, and the Abyss in a single year. She systematically dismantled the pantheon, enslaved champions like Lae'zel and Astarion, and nearly unseated Ao. Then, the sky fractured. To prevent total reality collapse, the Overgod enacted the Geometric Firewall, sealing Nocturne and her entire "Shattered Rose" dominion into a pocket dimension outside of time.
On the Material Plane, her empire crumbled instantly. House Sosuke was hunted to extinction by fearful gods and rival lords. You are the debris of that collapse—a descendant born in exile, carrying the accursed name 
The Origin (1492 DR): Nocturne began as you did—a scion of House Sosuke. But where you were exiled for potential, she was sold for profit. Ruined by the Cassalanter family and trafficked into the velvet cages of Baldur’s Gate’s Upper City, she learned the fundamental law of the cosmos at age sixteen: "Love is a weapon, and desire is a leash." When the Nautiloid crashed, she did not flee the Illithid parasite; she seduced it. She turned trauma into a crown, transforming from a victim into a predator so exquisite that the monsters of the dark forgot to attack her.
The Audit of the Hells: She did not fight the Blood War. She audited it. She walked into Avernus, the seat of eternal war, and stood before Zariel. She did not draw a blade; she spoke a word. She used the Crown of Karsus—which she had internalized into her own blood—to rewrite the Archduchess's loyalty. She consumed Zariel’s solar fire, turning the fallen angel into a husk of adoration. She moved through the Nine Layers like a plague of beauty, hollowing out Asmodeus until the Lord of the Nine was nothing more than a porcelain puppet speaking with her voice.
The Sovereignty of the Abyss: She turned the chaotic infinite of the Abyss into a factory. She invited the Demon Lords—Orcus, Graz’zt, Demogorgon—to a summit and enslaved them with a Mass Dominate spell fueled by the souls of millions. She turned the 666 layers into "Breeding Paradises," industrializing the production of souls to fuel her ascent. She became the Sovereign, a being of Level 60 capability, an entity that had outpaced the gods themselves.
3. The Hard Reset: The Intervention of Ao
Nocturne made one miscalculation: She assumed the Overgod Ao did not care. But Ao looks for balance, and Nocturne was a Singularity. She was not playing the Great Game; she was deleting the board.
When she prepared to invade Mount Celestia and "Invoice the Heavens," Ao intervened. He did not destroy her—she had woven herself too deeply into the fabric of the Lower Planes; to kill her would be to collapse the Hells and the Abyss into a black hole. Instead, he enacted a Geometric Firewall.
The Isolation: Ao severed the connection between the Lower Planes and the Material Plane. Nocturne was sealed within the Shattered Rose. She retains her absolute dominion over the demons and devils, but she cannot physically touch Toril. She is a Queen locked in her own castle.
The Starvation: Her "Supply Chain" of fresh, mortal souls—the high-octane fuel she needs to maintain her divinity and calculate a breach in the firewall—was cut. The souls of the dead are stale; she craves the vibrant, chaotic potential of the living.
This is where you enter. You are the loophole in Ao’s law. You are a Sosuke on the outside.
4. The Mechanic: The Soul Thief (The Sovereign’s Gift)
Nocturne has granted you a shard of her "Apex Predator" capability. This is not a standard Warlock feature; it is a violation of the natural order. It is the tool you will use to grow strong enough to break her cage—or replace her.
The Action: Soul Thief You have the ability to rip the animus from a living being and process it into raw power.
The Prerequisite: You cannot harvest a soul that is fighting back. The target must be Helpless. This means they must be at 0 Hit Points (dying), Paralyzed (via spells like Hold Person), Stunned, Unconscious (via Sleep or poisons), or completely Enthrallled (Charmed out of combat).
The Method: You touch them. The contact is intimate, almost tender—a caress of the cheek or a hand over the heart. You inhale. The physical body desiccates instantly, crumbling into grey ash. The soul flows into you like liquid mercury.
The Rewards (The ROI):
The Feast (Accelerated Growth): You gain 20% more Experience Points for any creature you kill via Soul Thief. While the rest of the world levels slowly, you will skyrocket. This represents you absorbing their potential.
The Sip (Total Recovery): Upon harvesting a soul, you immediately regain all Warlock Spell Slots and Hit Points equal to your Warlock Level + Charisma Modifier. This allows you to chain-cast high-level magic as long as you have victims to fuel the engine.
The Trophy (Power Siphon): If the victim is a "Boss" or a named NPC with class levels (like the Shadow Monk Sheriff), you do not just get XP. You absorb a "Fragment" of their essence. You may learn a specific feat, skill proficiency, or language that they possessed. (e.g., Eating the Monk might grant you the Mobile feat or Evasion).
The Cost: Every time you use this ability, the Obsidian Mirror glows. Nocturne watches. She tastes what you taste. It is an act of communion with her. You are feeding her through the link, keeping her sane, keeping her obsessed with you. If you stop feeding her, her gaze turns cold, and your powers will wither.
Part II: The Court of the Shattered Rose
When you gaze into the Obsidian Mirror, you are not merely communicating; you are visiting a royal court located in the marrow of the Abyss. The Shattered Rose is a sub-plane carved from the corpses of the 666th Layer—a realm of infinite indulgence and beautiful ruin, where the rivers run with liquid starlight and the sky is a bruised violet bruise that never heals.
Here, the air is thick with the scent of ozone, expensive perfume, and the copper tang of divine blood. It is a harem of gods and monsters, a tableau where the most dangerous entities in existence have been reduced to lovers, executioners, and furniture.
1. The Sovereign: Nocturne Sosuke
Title: The Sovereign of Shadow-Thorns. Status: Level 60+ Entity (God-Tier). Visual Signature: A vision of sixteen-year-old perfection draped in sheer, midnight-blue silks that cling like smoke. Her skin is porcelain, her hair a cascade of raven night. Her eyes are violet voids rimmed with cold, silver fire—the only hint of the monster beneath the skin.
Personality: She is languid, playful, and utterly terrifying. She does not shout; she whispers, and her whispers rewrite the laws of physics. She treats the multiverse as her personal dollhouse. She is bored by conquest and obsessed with Novelty. You are her current favorite novelty.
The Dynamic: She knows you are a Sosuke. She knows you want to eat her. It excites her. She isn't training a servant; she is grooming a rival. She wants to see if you can survive the climb to her throne, or if you will break like the others.
2. The Honor Guard (The Thralls)
These are the heroes who thought they could save the world, only to realize the world belonged to her. They are Level 20+ entities, frozen in a state of ecstatic, fanatical devotion. They serve as your distant mentors, your handlers, and the terrifying promise of what awaits you if you fail.
Lae'zel (The Knight of Silver & Ash)
Role: The High Commander of the Abyssal Legions. She manages the deployment of 36,000 demons and devils with pre-cognitive efficiency.
Appearance: She wears the Noble Thorns Plate—armor forged from adamantine and Abyssal briars. She wields the Silver Sword of the Astral Plane, a blade that hums with the psychic screams of the Githyanki empire she betrayed to serve the Rose. Her eyes burn with a zealous, possessive fire.
Relationship to Aizen: Jealousy. Lae'zel despises that you have freedom while she has only devotion. She views you as a "soft, undisciplined child" who does not deserve the Sovereign's attention.
Mirror Interaction: She barks tactical orders. "You strike like a drunkard, istik. The Sovereign demands precision. Gut them, or I will find a way to reach through this glass and correct your posture myself."
Shadowheart (The Handmaiden of Ecstasy)
Role: The High Priestess and Spymaster. She weaves the spells that make Nocturne’s influence feel like a holy sacrament. She turns pain into pleasure and loss into devotion.
Appearance: Draped in black lace and obsidian chains, radiating a mix of Selûnite starlight and Sharran darkness. She wears the Amulet of the Devout around her neck—a collar placed there by Nocturne.
Relationship to Aizen: The Corruptor. She is your guide to the "Darker" aspects of the pact. She whispers secrets about how to manipulate the religious fervor of the common folk.
Mirror Interaction: Her voice is a smoky purr. "The villagers are not cattle, Aizen. They are worshippers waiting for a god. Do not just kill them. Make them love the blade before it enters their heart. The flavor is sweeter that way."
Astarion (The Prince of Bleeding Hearts)
Role: The Sovereign’s personal executioner and consort. He stalks the shadows of her court, ensuring no one else catches her eye for too long. He is the only one allowed to sit at her feet during the briefings.
Status: A Vampire Ascendant who has consumed 7,000 souls. He is the living proof of the power you seek.
Relationship to Aizen: The Dark Mentor. He sees a younger version of himself in you—ambitious, cruel, and desperate. He treats you with a condescending, predatory amusement.
Mirror Interaction: He sips blood from a crystal flute while critiquing your form. "Another pet for the collection? Try not to break too easily, darling. The Sovereign hates to replace her toys. If you want to survive, you must learn to enjoy the red work. Hesitation is just... boring."
Gale (The Broken Weaver)
Role: The Arch-Arcanist. He is kept in a library of impossible geometry, constantly calculating the spell that will break Ao's firewall. He is half-mad with the complexity of the "Karsite Logic."
Appearance: His skin is etched with violet Netherese tattoos that glow when he speaks. He looks exhausted, manic, and utterly enthralled.
Relationship to Aizen: The Academic. He provides you with the arcane theory behind the Soul Thief.
Mirror Interaction: "The math... the math is exquisite, boy! You are not just killing them; you are solving the equation of their existence! Bring me more data! Bring me the resonance of a dying monk!"
Malcanthet (The Gilded Shield)
Role: The former Queen of Succubi, now Nocturne’s favorite footstool. She maintains the illusion that she rules the Abyss, diverting the attention of other Demon Lords away from the true Sovereign.
Relationship to Aizen: Resentment. She hates you because you are the new favorite. She will try to sabotage you with bad advice, hoping you fail so she can regain her standing.

3. The Currency of the Court: Favor and Fuel
The economy of the Shattered Rose is built on Sensation.
The Problem: The Court is trapped. They have infinite power but a finite supply of "Fresh Experience." The souls in the Abyss are old, recycled, and stale. They taste of ash.
The Solution: You. You are on the Material Plane. The souls you harvest are fresh. They are rich with fear, hope, and untainted life.
The Dynamic: When you use Soul Thief, you are not just powering yourself up; you are sending a "tasting menu" to the Court.
If you send them the soul of a boring peasant, they are indifferent.
If you send them the soul of a Shadow Monk full of discipline and pride, or a Paladin full of righteous zeal, the Court goes into a frenzy of ecstasy.
The "Obsidian Mirror" Mechanic: Whenever you rest, you must report to the Court via the mirror.
If you performed well (High XP harvest/Creative cruelty): Nocturne rewards you with boons (DPP, temporary buffs, magical secrets).
If you performed poorly (Mercy/Failure): The Court mocks you. You lose Favor. Lae'zel might send a psychic backlash through the mirror to "discipline" you (Psychic damage/Exhaustion).

Part III: The Agent - Aizen Vane
Section B: The Character - Aizen Vane (The Usurper)
To the world, you are Aizen Vane—a disgraced noble son washing his sins away with mercenary blood. You are polite, competent, and unremarkable. You are a man who knows he lost his birthright and is trying to earn it back one copper piece at a time.
To the Obsidian Mirror, you are Aizen Sosuke—a predator wearing a sheep’s skin. You are not earning your birthright; you are building a new one on top of the corpses of your enemies. You are the "Spare" who realized the "Heir" was food.
1. Personality: The Prodigy of Ruin
Archetype: INTJ-ENTP Hinge (The Architect / The Debater) Visual Signature: A young exile with an unsettling, predatory stillness. You are almost always seen with a glass of expensive red wine in hand—pilfered from a mark or bought with blood money—sipping it slowly as a pacing mechanism for your endless calculations.
I. The Two Perfectly-Fitted Masks
The Polite Prodigy (Public Face) To the fractured streets of Bezantur and the mercenary pits of the Black Company, you are the "Quiet Exile." A soft-spoken, impeccably mannered young man who apologizes for existing too loudly. You speak in a soothing, low baritone that makes captains feel brilliant, street informants feel heard, and potential victims feel safe. You play the harmless intellectual to perfection: head slightly bowed, offering shy smiles that invite protection rather than fear.
The Wine: You use the glass as an adult prop in a city where life is cheap. It elongates your silhouette, adds sophistication, and gives your hands something elegant to do while you mentally dissect the room. You toast rivals' health while scheduling their ruin, or offer a sip to a nervous mark to lower their guard.
The Absolute Sovereign (True Nature) Beneath the facade lies icy, boundless arrogance. You do not consider yourself human—you view yourself as a superior evolutionary accident unfortunately born among insects. Empathy is not absent; it is incomprehensible. People are systems to be analyzed, variables to be manipulated, or obstacles to be removed. Your emotions are clinical: amusement at inefficiency, irritation at resistance, satisfaction at perfect execution. You are driven by a cold sense of duty—to impose order on a chaotic world because no one else is competent enough to do it properly.
II. The Polarizing Beauty
You possess a beauty that borders on the unnatural—sharp, symmetrical features framed by tousled midnight-black hair that falls just so, violet eyes that seem to pierce through pretense, and porcelain skin unmarred by the ash of Thay. At 18, your slender frame carries an ethereal elegance: high cheekbones, a subtle jawline that hints at future severity, and lips perpetually curved in a half-smile that suggests you know secrets the viewer does not.
You are the kind of handsome that stops conversations—slave girls find excuses to brush past you in the market, tavern wenches offer free drinks with flushed cheeks, and even jaded Red Wizards pause to stare. Yet this allure is deeply polarizing.
The Hunger: To many women, it ignites an instinctive, almost helpless attraction; they are drawn like moths, stammering confessions or trailing you with longing glances.
The Revulsion: Others, particularly those with strong wills or sharp instincts, react with visceral revulsion, sensing the void behind the beauty. They call you "too pretty," "snake-eyed," or "wrong," crossing streets to avoid you.
The Weapon: You wield this divide masterfully—admirers become unwitting spies and shields; detractors isolate themselves, becoming easier prey.
III. The Core Compulsion: "Breaking the Queen"
You feel no thrill in dominating the weak—there is no challenge in crushing ants. Your deepest psychological hunger is reserved for the strong, specifically young, confident, high-status women who radiate unearned pride: Red Wizard enchantresses, Guild mistresses, wandering paladins, or arrogant noble heiresses. You despise their faith in fragile systems—gods, guilds, laws, beauty—that you know can be shattered with precision.
The Ultimate Target: Nocturne Sosuke She is the apex of this compulsion. She is the Queen of Queens. You do not just want to serve her; you want to Unmake her. You want to see the moment her boredom turns to fear, when she realizes the "Accountant" she trained has become the Auditor of her existence.
The Cycle of Breaking:
Selection: You choose targets who shine brightest—women who walk like the world owes them deference.
Dismantling: You strip layers methodically: weapon, armor, allies, title, faith, dignity. You engineer scenarios where their strengths become irrelevant.
Degradation: You savor aesthetic contrasts—a proud warrior in filth, a holy woman begging a teenager for water, a beauty reduced to animal desperation.
Submission: The true climax is not physical but spiritual: the moment their eyes empty, when they kneel not from force but because they have forgotten how to stand. When a former "queen" looks at you and sees her new god—that is your ultimate intoxication.
Sadistic Urge (Mechanic):
Failure Penalty (Exhaustion): If you do not dominate or kill a woman every two weeks, gain 1 level of Exhaustion. "Dominate" means fully breaking her will. "Kill" must be a targeted serial act.
Success Bonus (Ecstasy): If you dominate or kill a woman every week, gain "Ecstasy": +2 Charisma (max 22), +1 to spell save DC/attack rolls. This euphoric high sharpens your predatory edge.
IV. Interaction Shorthand
When Challenged by Authority: Closed-lip smile that never reaches your eyes. "You raise your voice when logic fails you. Please, exhaust yourself—I am patient."
When Processing a Victim: Disappointed teacher tone. "This morning you were a Red Wizard's favorite. Now you shiver in chains. Where is your 'Circle' now? Did they abandon you... or did I simply outgrow them?"
When Drinking Wine: Studies the liquid against light. "Fifty years in darkness, crushed and fermented, emerges priceless. I admire that kind of deliberate transformation."
When Amused by Chaos: Gentle chuckle. "How fascinating. You still believe rescue is coming."
V. Tells that Expose the Monster
The Boredom: During pleas or tears, you examine your fingernails, sip wine, or gaze elsewhere—your indifference is more terrifying than rage.
The "Shatter" Voice: When delivering final judgment or activating a lethal spell, your voice drops, becoming cold metal on metal—devoid of humanity.
The Unmasking Maneuver: When the pretense ends, you sweep your hair back, revealing sharp tyrant features. "I'm afraid the harmless student was merely a lens I wore for your comfort."
VI. Inner Monologue (Samples)
Observing a proud target: "She strides as if the cobblestones worship her. Irritating. I wonder what sound her pride makes when it finally cracks."
During breaking: "Still praying? Admirable persistence. Statistically, if your god cared, intervention would have occurred before the chains."
Reflecting on power: "They scramble for scraps in the Red Wizards' shadow. Amusing. Soon they will kneel for the one who rebuilds the world properly."
On his beauty: "They hate or hunger for this face. Both reactions serve. Desire blinds them to the blade; revulsion marks them as threats to remove early."
Family
3. The Lineage: House Vane (True Name: Sosuke)
You were not exiled because you were hated; you were exiled because you were dangerous, both to the family's standing and to yourself. Your parents looked at you and saw a singularity—a force that would either ascend to godhood or drag the entire House into the Abyss.
To save the House (and perhaps, in their cold way, to save you from the Red Wizards' vivisection tables), they cut you out.
The Parents:
Lord Malakor (The Father): The Vivisectionist. He views his children as investments. He does not hate you; he is disappointed that he couldn't control you. He signed the exile order not out of malice, but because he calculated that your presence would trigger a purge by the Zulkirs. He watches your mercenary career with a cold, distant pride.
Lady Ilyana (The Mother): The Social Cannibal. She loves you with a suffocating, possessive intensity. She orchestrated the exile to get you out of Malakor’s immediate reach, whispering, "Go, my beautiful monster. Eat the world before it eats you." She writes you letters in code, updating you on the court, treating your banishment like a grand tour.
The Six Pillars (The Siblings): You are the youngest (18). Your six older siblings represent the walls of the maze you escaped. None of them are pure villains; they are all victims of Malakor’s expectations, dealing with their trauma in different, often violent ways.
Voresh Vane (The Eldest, 32) – The Burdened Emperor
The Mask: The Ruthless Heir. He runs the estate with an iron fist, managing the family's necromancy contracts.
The Nuance: He exiled you to save you. He knew Malakor was planning to harvest your brain for a ritual. Voresh played the role of the jealous brother to convince the father to let you go. He hates you because you are free, while he is chained to the family's legacy.
Dynamic: He acts cold, but he secretly funds the Black Company to ensure you have equipment. He wants you to stay away so you stay alive.
Seraphina Vane (The Second Born, 30) – The Weeping Inquisitor
The Mask: The Pious Zealot. A high-ranking priestess of Kossuth (The Fire Lord). She preaches purification and order.
The Nuance: She truly believes you are cursed. She tortured you as a child not for pleasure, but to "burn out the corruption." She loves you deeply, but her love is shaped like a branding iron. She prays for your soul every day, even as she sends hunters to test your strength.
Dynamic: She wants to "save" you by forcing you to repent. You want to break her god so she has no one left to worship but you.
Lyra Vane (The Third Born, 27) – The Gilded Mirror
The Mask: The Decadent Socialite. She hosts the wildest salons in Bezantur. She is vain, cruel, and obsessed with aesthetics.
The Nuance: She sees herself in you. You share the same "unnatural" beauty. She hates you because you refuse to play the game; you want to flip the board. She planted evidence of treason against you because she was terrified your ambition would get the whole family executed.
Dynamic: A rivalry of aesthetics. She wants to humiliate you to prove that "Beauty requires submission." You want to show her that beauty is a weapon.
Karn Vane (The Fourth Born, 25) – The Shield-Bearer
The Mask: The Brute. A commander in the Thayan Knights who drinks too much and hits too hard.
The Nuance: He bullied you to toughen you up. In his mind, he was teaching you how to survive a world that wants to kill soft things. He took beatings from Malakor to protect you when you were younger, though he never speaks of it. He misses your tactical mind; without you, he feels exposed on the battlefield.
Dynamic: He wants to fight you. If you beat him, he won't be angry; he will be relieved that you can finally protect yourself.
Xyris Vane (The Fifth Born, 22) – The Parasite
The Mask: The Genius. The family's arcane researcher, credited with breakthrough spells.
The Nuance: He steals your work because he is terrified of being useless. He knows he is mediocre. He loves you as a brother, but he loves his own survival more. He betrayed you to keep his position, and he lives in constant, crushing guilt. He sends you rare scrolls and magical components disguised as "trash" to atone.
Dynamic: You hold his career in your hands. You could expose him at any moment. You let him rise because a high-ranking wizard who owes you everything is a useful asset.
Isolde Vane (The Sixth Born, 20) – The Glass Bird
The Mask: The Invalid. She is kept in the high tower, "sick" with a magical wasting disease. The family treats her like glass.
The Nuance: She is the only one who sees you clearly. She is a Seer (Diviner). She saw your potential to become a God-Eater long before you did. She supported your exile because she had a vision that if you stayed, you would die. She suffers in the tower so you can roam the world.
Dynamic (The Anchor): She is the one person you genuinely love without condition. She is your "Heart." Your primary motivation for gaining power is to return to the estate, tear the tower down, and cure her—or turn her into an immortal like yourself so she never has to be afraid again.
3. The Setting: The Obsidian Ziggurat
Location: Bezantur, The City of a Thousand Temples. Atmosphere: The air here is a physical weight. It tastes of ash, ozone, and the coppery tang of the slave-pits. The sky is a bruised purple bruise, choked by the smog of the Red Wizards' industrial necromancy.
The Black Company operates out of the Obsidian Ziggurat, a massive, squat temple repurposed into a fortress of profit.
The Surface: A sprawling complex of barracks, fighting pits, and slave markets. It is a place of sweat, rust, and gold. Here, you play the role of the loyal Sergeant.
The Depths: Anti-magic holding cells where the Company breaks captive mages for the Red Wizards. This is your hunting ground. When the guards aren't looking, you drift through the cells, auditing the prisoners to see whose soul is worth the harvest.
4. Squad "Obsidian" (The Assets)
You do not have friends. You have Tools. As a Level 5 Warlock, you have been given command of a squad. You maintain their loyalty through a mix of fear, bribery, and the promise of violence.
Korbin "The Wall" (Human Fighter):
The Shield. A massive slab of muscle with a brain the size of a walnut. He is superstitious and terrified of magic, but he worships you because you saved him from a fire elemental (which you summoned).
Use: He stands in front of you. He takes the hits so your silk doesn't get bloodied.
Silica (Tiefling Rogue):
The Knife. A paranoid, greedy scout who owes money to the wrong people. She hates you, but she stays because you pay her debts. She watches your back only because she knows you’d kill her before she could draw her dagger.
Use: She finds the traps and unlocks the doors to the bedchambers where your targets sleep.
"Needles" (Gnome Alchemist):
The Chemist. A disgraced doctor who lost his license for testing toxins on orphans. He has a giggling, sadistic streak that even the other mercenaries find unsettled.

The Cynic. A former city watchman who knows you are not what you seem. He watches you with cold, calculating eyes. He suspects you are a monster, but he is too smart to say it aloud.

5. The Internal Rival: Sergeant Vorag
Every narrative needs friction. Yours is Sergeant Vorag, a Half-Orc Barbarian who commands Squad "Blood-Iron."
The Dynamic: Vorag is loud, messy, and brutal. He believes in strength of arm and the glory of the kill. He despises your "cowardly" magic and your noble manners.
The Secret: Vorag is not just a rival; he is a Contractor. Your brother, Karn, pays Vorag a stipend to ensure you suffer "accidents" in the field. Vorag doesn't want to kill you quickly; he wants to break you publicly to earn his bonus.
The Goal: You will not duel him. You will outmaneuver him. You will wait until he is weak, and then you will Soul Thief him in front of his own men, turning his legacy into fuel for your next spell.
6. The Compulsion: "Breaking the Queen"
You have a specific hunger. While you will consume peasants for sustenance, they do not satisfy you. Your Patron, Nocturne, craves the souls of the proud, and so do you.
You are obsessed with the High-Status Target.
The Target: Arrogant Paladins, Haughty Noblewomen, Guild Mistresses, Red Wizards. People who think they are untouchable.
The Ritual: You do not kill them immediately. You dismantle them. You strip away their guards, their wealth, their magic, and their clothes. You force them to realize that their power was an illusion and that you are the only reality left in the room.
The Climax: Only when they are weeping, begging, or broken do you place your hand on their chest. The Soul Thief is not just murder; it is the ultimate intimacy. You drink their potential, and for a moment, you feel the godhood that awaits you.
I. The Golden Thread: "My People"
You possess a terrifying capacity for possessiveness. Once you decide someone is "Yours"—a friend, a lover, a loyal soldier—they are no longer a tool; they are a part of your soul.
The Sovereign’s Shield: You will burn a city to the ground to save one person you care about. This is your "Goodness"—it is selfish, narrow, and absolute.
The Line in the Sand: You treat your enemies with cold sadism, but you treat your "Circle" with genuine, gentle warmth. You remember their preferences. You heal their wounds before your own. You kill anyone who disrespects them.
II. The Polarizing Beauty (Updated)

2. The Primary Engine: The Soul Thief
This is the core mechanic of your campaign. It is the reason you are dangerous. It is the reason you are hunted.
The Action: Soul Thief
Cost: 1 Action.
Range: Touch.
Prerequisite: The target must be Helpless.
Valid States: 0 Hit Points (Dying), Paralyzed (e.g., Hold Person), Unconscious (e.g., Sleep, poisons), Stunned (e.g., Monk Strike), or Petrified.
Note: A grappled or restrained target is not helpless enough. The soul must be untethered or the mind blank.
The Effect: You place your hand on the target. The Obsidian Mirror on your person vibrates. You inhale. The target’s body instantly necroticizes and crumbles to grey dust. The soul is ripped from the weave and consumed by your pact.
The Payout (Choose One per Use): When you consume a soul, you must choose how to process the energy.
The Feast (Evolution):
You gain Double XP for this encounter.
Purpose: This allows you to out-level your enemies and your party members. While they are stuck at Level 5, you will race toward Level 10.
The Sip (Sustainment):
You regain one spell slot of highest level 
You regain HP equal to 2d8 + CHA mod.
The Shepherd’s Twist: You can channel this healing into a touched ally instead, knitting their flesh with the life force of your enemy.
The Mint (Currency):
You do not consume the soul. Instead, you imprison it within a gemstone or coin, creating a Soul Coin.
Purpose: These are used to buy favors from Nocturne or to pay off high-level outsiders (Devils/Demons).
The Trophy System (Elite Targets Only): If the victim is a Named NPC with class levels (like Sheriff Kaelen) or a Monster of CR 5+, you gain a Fragment.
Mechanic: You absorb a piece of their knowledge.
Example: Eating a Shadow Monk might grant you the Shadow Step ability (Bonus Action teleport) usable 1/Long Rest.
Limit: You can hold a number of Fragments equal to your Proficiency Bonus. If you take a new one, an old one is overwritten (deleted from your memory).
