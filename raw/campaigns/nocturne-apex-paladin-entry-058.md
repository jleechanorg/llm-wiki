Location: Oakhaven Outskirts, Moving to Tibbet's Farm, Early Evening

The secured form of Widow Elara lay silent and still on the cottage floor, a bound bundle in the flickering firelight. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) cast a final, dispassionate glance at her captive. One individual was secured, a precise acquisition. But the broader contract, the true accumulation of wealth she sought, demanded more. Man Tibbet's farm, with its promise of a "large family," was the logical next step. Leaving her single captive secured was a calculated risk, but the cottage's seclusion and the woman's abject terror made it a negligible one for now. Efficiency dictated swift action.

She slipped out of the cottage as silently as she had entered, the Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate absorbing the dim light, rendering her almost invisible against the deepening shadows of the thicket. Her path back out into the open felt fluid, every movement economical. Her mind, a strategic engine (Intelligence 18), was already plotting the fastest, most discreet route north, keeping the faint, methodical presence of the patrol on the main road in the back of her awareness. They were a variable, not an immediate threat, but their proximity to Tibbet's meant careful planning was essential.

The short journey north was a silent ballet of movement through moon-dappled fields and sparse woods. The air was cool, carrying the scent of damp earth and distant livestock. As she drew closer, the landscape opened up, revealing Man Tibbet's farm. It was indeed a larger operation than Elara's humble cottage, a cluster of buildings rather than a single dwelling. A stout farmhouse, a sizable barn, and several smaller outbuildings huddled together, their dark forms silhouetted against the bruised canvas of the sky. From within the farmhouse, the muffled sounds of multiple voices, perhaps even children, drifted faintly on the breeze. There was a faint glow of light from several windows, indicating more activity. This was the promised "large family."

As Nocturne reached the edge of a small copse overlooking the farm, her Apex Senses registered a distinct, low thrum beneath the ground near the barn. It wasn't natural. It felt... structured, almost mechanical, yet subtly concealed. And then, a glint of metal, barely visible in the deepening gloom, from the farmhouse's second-story window – a narrow slit, too regular to be a simple window, perhaps a subtle embrasure. This farm was not as undefended as the intimidated villager had implied. The "disciplined group" on the main road might be more than just a random patrol; they might be connected to a more robust, and hidden, defense of this particular farm.

The mission, as expected, was becoming more intricate.
