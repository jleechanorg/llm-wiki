The Blackwood cottage, now sealed and silent, breathed with the shallow rhythms of its sleeping occupants. Gladius stood within, the Silent Strangler’s Cord a familiar, ready weight in his hand. The scent of mildew and damp earth was strong here, a raw, unrefined smell that hinted at the simple, unvarnished lives he was about to extinguish. This was not the cloying sweetness of the Miller's home; this was something more primal, more fitting for the blunt force he was about to apply.

He moved with the fluid, silent grace of a seasoned predator, his Shadow-Step Boots absorbing every potential creak of the old floorboards. His heavy splint mail, a formidable barrier in battle, was now merely a muted whisper against the profound quiet of the house. His senses were alive, honed to the subtle cues of life: the faint warmth radiating from behind a thin wooden door, the almost imperceptible shift of a body on a straw mattress, the soft, rhythmic sighs of slumber. His low Intelligence, far from being a hindrance, allowed him to shed all extraneous thought, leaving only the pure, instinctive focus of the hunt. This wasn't about complex planning; it was about the exquisite, immediate sensation of control, the raw, experiential pleasure of an unfolding, brutal dance.

He found them quickly, two figures in a single, cramped room, their presence betrayed by the steady, heavy breathing that filled the space. An older couple, judging by the deeper cadences of their sleep. The room was sparse, almost barren, save for a rough-hewn bed and a small, scarred chest. Gladius's lip curled faintly in disdain. *More insignificance.* Their "fierce independence" had bought them nothing but a quiet, pathetic end. The satisfaction was not in malice, but in the efficiency, the undeniable proof of his own power to erase, to bend even stubborn life to his will. The night was still dark, the village oblivious, and the hunt continued.
