You continued to turn the fragile pages, the archaic script flowing into your mind like a cold current. The Seraphim's records grew increasingly desperate. They described attempts to 'train' these Joyful Predators, to 'channel' their destructive glee towards specific, approved targets. There were horrifying accounts of containment breaches where a Null, pushed to its limit, would unleash its 'resonant unmaking' in a burst of terrifying, silent chaos, leaving behind not bodies, but voids. The project, initially conceived as a way to create perfectly controlled anti-mages, spun wildly out of their creators' grasp. They couldn't understand the sheer, irrational *joy* some of these anomalies exhibited in the act of erasing. This emotional variable, so alien to the Seraphim's own cold logic, became their greatest impediment.

The journal hinted that while all Nulls possessed a dampening effect, only a terrifying few developed the full 'Joyful Predator' manifestation, often linked to the purity or specific lineage of the celestial 'seed' used in their creation. Your mother, born from the most potent and untamed of Lucifer's own essence, represented the terrifying culmination of this research—a perfect storm of inherent nullity and raw, surging divine power. Her unique lineage, you now understood, was not a flaw but a monstrous amplification.

You felt a dizzying surge of both fear and a chilling fascination. The text described how this 'joy' wasn't mere sadism, but the profound intellectual satisfaction of solving a complex problem through absolute control and overwhelming force. The same cold, analytical clarity that you felt when a star chart perfectly aligned, when a cryptographic puzzle yielded its secrets. The same feeling that had driven your mother to impossible victories. You closed the journal, its ancient leather suddenly feeling hot in your trembling hands. The weight of this inheritance, the potential for that terrifying 'joy' within your own blood, was a burden far heavier than any guilt you had carried before.
