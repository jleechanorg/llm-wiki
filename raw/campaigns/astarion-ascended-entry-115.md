2.  **Address Question - "Why would she lose capabilities?"**:
    *   **Vampire Spawn vs. Full Vampire**: In Dungeons & Dragons 5th Edition (and generally in Baldur's Gate 3 lore), a **vampire spawn** is a lesser form of vampire. When a humanoid is turned into a spawn, they lose much of their free will, intellect, and unique personality, becoming eternally bound to the will of their full vampire creator. They are typically simpler, more animalistic creatures, even if they retain some memories or skills from their mortal life. Their primary purpose is to serve their master.
    *   **Astarion's Unique Case**: Astarion's own conversion under Cazador was into a **vampire spawn**. He was indeed subject to Cazador's thrall and the diminishment of his free will. His unique arc in Baldur's Gate 3 involved him *breaking free* of Cazador's direct mental control *before* his ascension. His **ascension ritual** then transformed him into a **full, Ascended Vampire Lord**, a process that transcended the limitations of being a mere spawn. It fundamentally rebuilt his being, making him a creature of immense power and self-determination, overcoming typical vampiric weaknesses (like sunlight sensitivity) and amplifying his inherent abilities. He did not merely *become* a spawn; he *escaped* spawnhood and achieved a higher form of vampirism.
    *   **Morwen's Situation**: Morwen, if converted, would be undergoing the standard **vampire spawn conversion**. This process inherently carries the risk of a diminishment of her more nuanced human intellect and tactical finesse, as her mind is subsumed by the primal urges of the spawn and the absolute loyalty to her creator (Astarion). While Xykon's compelled knowledge might *mitigate* this (as per the "Controlled Transformation" option), it doesn't entirely eliminate the inherent nature of becoming a spawn. Her past intellect and skills are not guaranteed to transfer perfectly to the more primal, obedient nature of a spawn, especially when compared to Astarion's unique ascended state.
