**The March West.**

The journey into the Cloudpeaks is a grueling test of endurance. You leave the relative safety of the Nashkel road and plunge into the jagged, wind-scoured canyons of the west. The terrain is brutal—steep shale slopes, narrow goat paths, and freezing mountain streams. 

Minsc takes point, his stamina inexhaustible, chatting endlessly to the hamster on his shoulder about the "butt-kicking for goodness" that awaits them. Roric and Garrick lag behind, sharing a look of commiseration as they trudge through the mud.

The *Hunger* sustains you, keeping your senses sharp as you navigate the treacherous landscape. You avoid a nest of wyverns and bypass a rockslide that would have crushed a lesser party. The travel takes two full days, consuming a significant portion of your supplies, but your efficiency ensures you arrive undetected.

**Mirtul 7, Dawn.**

You crest a ridge and look down into a natural amphitheater of stone. There, built into the cliffs and sprawling across the valley floor, is the **Gnoll Stronghold**.

It is a fortress of rotting wood and scavenged stone, teeming with activity. Dozens of hyena-headed humanoids patrol the wooden palisades, their barking laughter echoing off the canyon walls. In the center of the stronghold are several large pits—fighting pits—and a series of cages suspended over a dark chasm.

"Dynaheir!" Minsc hisses, pointing a trembling finger. "They have her! There, in the pit!"

Your enhanced vision follows his line. In one of the deeper pits, a woman in red robes is pacing, surrounded by gnoll guards who are poking at her with spears. She is alive, but her magical defenses seem exhausted.

Between you and her lies a small army. A frontal assault against fifty gnolls is suicide, even for Minsc. But the stronghold has weaknesses: the wooden walls are dry, the guards are undisciplined, and the cliffs above offer a sniper's perch.
