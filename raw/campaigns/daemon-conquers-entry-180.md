One afternoon, a local reeve, a portly man named Master Helmer, arrived, visibly flustered. Daemon's gaze, cold and unwavering, fixed on Helmer. "There are no 'shortfalls' in my domain, Master Reeve," he rumbled. "Only incompetence, or defiance." He let the silence stretch, the weight of his presence crushing. Helmer immediately stammered out a confession: a small, customary portion had been set aside for "local festivals," a tradition his family had upheld for generations.

Isolde stepped forward. "The traditions of the past are over, Master Helmer," she stated, her voice sharp and clear, echoing Daemon's ruthlessness. "The grain belongs to Lord Daemon. Deliver it, or face his justice." Helmer blanched, bowing deeply before scurrying away. The incident, small as it was, solidified Daemon's authority and Isolde's commitment to his rule.
