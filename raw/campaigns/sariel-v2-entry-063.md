Game Master:
**--- THINK ---**
The sight of Kaelan is devastating, a raw testament to the truth. My earlier attempt to force Mother's memory left me bleeding and drained. This is a fragile moment. I need to be precise, methodical.

*   **Approach Kaelan directly:** Risky. I don't fully understand his madness. A sudden, unmediated contact could trigger a violent outburst from him, or another psychic backlash for me. I need more information about his specific trauma and how his mind is walled off. Without knowing how his madness manifests, direct contact is a gamble.
    *   *Success Rate for useful contact:* Low (High risk of negative outcome: further trauma for him, psychic backlash for me).

*   **Instruct Rowan on memory access:** Rowan is a physical healer, a balm for the body and the immediate emotions. He can ease Kaelan's physical torment, but accessing or facilitating the access of deeply fractured memories is beyond his expertise. My Mirror Magic requires direct observation or mental connection. Rowan can't initiate *that*.
    *   *Success Rate for accessing memories via Rowan:* Very Low. (Success rate for aiding Kaelan physically/emotionally: High).

*   **Cast a spell:** I have `Light` active, but `Detect Magic`, `Identify`, `Comprehend Languages`, `Magic Missile`, `Shield`, `Silent Image`.
    *   `Comprehend Languages`: This might allow me to understand his "fractured thoughts," to decipher the whispers. It won't soothe him, but it will give me vital information about the nature of his torment. It's an intellectual approach, low risk.
    *   `Silent Image`: Could create a calming illusion, but Kaelan's eyes are unseeing, lost in his own internal horror. It's unlikely to register meaningfully.
    *   I have no direct healing spells, so I can't physically heal him.
    *   *Success Rate for gaining understanding via spell:* Moderate (`Comprehend Languages`). *Success Rate for direct effect via spell:* Low.

*   **Explain Truth to Team:** This is a crucial strategic step, but not the *immediate* next one. It requires time, clarity, and an environment conducive to absorbing horrifying truths. Right now, the priority is Kaelan.
    *   *Success Rate for accessing memories:* Zero (indirect). *Success Rate for team understanding:* High (but not relevant to immediate goal).

*   **Direct Contact (Mirror Magic):** This is the ultimate goal, but my Mirror Magic is *latent* and *uncontrolled*. My last attempt resulted in a psychic backlash. Forcing direct physical contact and trying to pull *his* memories from a shattered psyche without more understanding of his specific madness or a more controlled method is extremely dangerous. It could destroy his mind further, shatter my own, or cause another uncontrolled magical flare. High risk, unknown payoff.
    *   *Success Rate for controlled access:* Very Low.

The most logical and safest immediate next step is to **observe Kaelan more closely**. This allows Rowan to continue his gentle work, perhaps providing a pathway to Kaelan's awareness. It gives me time to assess the precise nature of Kaelan's psychic torment, to filter his moans and whispers, and to identify the *exact* moments or triggers of his most vivid memories. It also allows me to conserve my strength and energy for when I finally attempt a more direct interaction. This isn't inaction; it's calculated reconnaissance before a plunge into the unknown.
**--- END THINK ---**

You rested for a few moments, the cold stone against your back a grounding presence, allowing the frantic hammering of your heart to slow, the psychic static in your mind to recede to a dull hum. The quiet competence of your companions—Rowan's gentle murmurs as he tended to Kaelan, Gideon's methodical checks of the cell's integrity, Cressida's sharp, analytical observation—was a fragile anchor in the horrifying silence of the Quiet Ward. You had gained a sliver of composure, enough to face the ordeal ahead.

With a deep, steadying breath, you pushed yourself away from the wall. The small orb of `Light` still glowed faintly on your fingertip, casting long, dancing shadows. You walked slowly towards the center of the chamber, towards Sergeant Kaelan, your footsteps echoing softly in the oppressive stillness. Every step felt heavy, burdened by the weight of the truths you had uncovered.

As you approached, the full horror of his condition became chillingly clear. Sergeant Kaelan (Level ?, Starfall Guard, Age ?) lay utterly motionless on the stone slab, his gaunt frame barely filling the tattered remnants of his Starfall Guard uniform. His skin was stretched taut over prominent bones, his features locked in a mask of perpetual, silent agony, as if he were screaming without sound. His eyes, wide and unblinking, were not empty, but filled with a profound, unseeing terror, fixed on some unseen horror in the darkness beyond the ceiling.

It was a low, rhythmic drone of fractured thoughts, a maddening loop of fragmented words, half-formed pleas, and the chilling echo of what you now knew to be the "unmaking." You knelt beside Rowan, who was gently cleaning Kaelan's emaciated face with a damp cloth, his own features etched with profound sorrow.

"His physical state is... precarious, my Lady," Rowan whispered, his voice thick with empathy. "The years of neglect, the constant torment... but his mind, it is pure chaos. A maelstrom of terror and broken thoughts. I can ease his pain, but I cannot touch his memories. They are... walled off."

You looked at Kaelan's unblinking eyes, knowing they held the final, terrifying piece of your mother's truth. The sight of his utter brokenness, of the madness born from witnessing the impossible, spurred a desperate resolve within you. You had to reach him. You maintained your distance for a moment longer, your gaze sweeping over his tormented features, trying to discern a pattern in the chaos. His eyes, wide and fixed, seemed to replay the same loop of horror, his lips occasionally forming silent, trembling words before dissolving into whimpers. The air around him shimmered faintly, a residual psychic distortion of the kind you had experienced yourself. You needed to understand the specific nature of his trauma before attempting to penetrate his shattered mind.
