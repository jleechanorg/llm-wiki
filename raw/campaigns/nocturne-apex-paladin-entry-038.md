Location: Outskirts of Oakhaven, Near the Barn, Early Evening

The air crackled with the lingering fear of the villagers, their wide, terrified eyes fixed on Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16). The burly man had delivered his information, a frantic torrent of details about Oakhaven: the winding path, the clusters of homes, the isolated farmhouses of Man Tibbet and Widow Elara. Her recent **Apex Senses** scan (Intelligence 18, INTJ) had provided even more crucial data: the quiet creek leading to Elara's, and the unexpected, disciplined patrol lurking on the main road, a quarter-mile back towards Baldur's Gate.

Her mind, ever the calculating engine, rapidly processed the confluence of new and old information. The initial element of surprise was gone, replaced by a need for calculated, efficient action. The barking had alerted *something*, and time was now a critical factor. The immediate threat of the villagers was negligible; they were effectively frozen. The larger concern was the professional patrol.

***

**Nocturne's Internal Monologue & Planning:**

*The objective is clear: acquire the 'stock' with maximum efficiency and minimal collateral. The initial phase of discretion has been compromised by the dogs' barks, but the situation is still manageable. The key is to leverage the fear I've instilled and the intelligence I've gained.*

*   **Target Assessment: Man Tibbet's Farm vs. Widow Elara's Cottage.**
    *   **Man Tibbet's Farm:** "Large family," "almost a mile north," "no close neighbors."
        *   *Pros:* Higher yield of 'stock'. Potentially more valuable due to sheer numbers.
        *   *Cons:* "North" implies proximity to the main road, where the patrol was detected. A large family means more individuals to subdue, increasing the risk of noise or prolonged struggle. More variables.
        *   *Initial Confidence:* Moderate yield, but higher risk of detection by the organized patrol. Less 'clean'.
    *   **Widow Elara's Cottage:** "To the west," "secluded by a small stream."
        *   *Pros:* "Secluded" and a "quiet ingress point" via the creek (Apex Senses) suggests much easier, quieter infiltration and extraction. Fewer individuals implies faster neutralization. Minimizes risk from the road patrol.
        *   *Cons:* Likely lower yield of 'stock' (a "Widow" implies fewer individuals).
        *   *Initial Confidence:* Lower yield, but significantly lower risk and higher probability of silent, efficient execution. More 'clean'.

*The most efficient path to wealth is the one that guarantees success and minimizes unforeseen variables. While Tibbet's offers a higher quantity, Elara's promises a cleaner, faster operation, preserving my resources and limiting exposure to the unknown patrol. I will prioritize the more certain, discreet path first. Additional stock can be acquired later, or from Tibbet's, if the initial extraction proves seamless.*

*The villagers must be dealt with, but not permanently. Their fear is a useful tool. Sending them back will temporarily clear the area and disperse any immediate threat of them calling a wider alarm beyond the initial, now-silenced barks.*

***

"Go," Nocturne commanded, her voice cutting through the twilight. Her finger, still resting on her longsword's pommel, gestured towards the hamlet. "Return to your homes. Remain inside. Do not speak of what you have seen, or heard, tonight. Your silence will ensure your continued... well-being." Her words were a thinly veiled threat, a promise of swift retribution if disobeyed.

The burly man, barely needing a second command, scrambled to his feet, grabbing his dropped pitchfork more for comfort than defense. He stumbled backward, pulling the whimpering young woman and the terrified old man with him. They didn't run, exactly; it was more of a panicked, shuffling retreat, their eyes never leaving Nocturne until the shadows of the trees swallowed them. They would spread vague fear, not concrete details. Good.

Nocturne watched them go, her gaze already sweeping the treeline, dismissing the now-empty space. The initial noise was a contained ripple. Her attention shifted, following the mental map of Oakhaven she had just constructed. The creek. A quiet, natural pathway. She moved with renewed purpose, her Fine Plate Armor a surprisingly muted presence as she slipped into the deeper shadows of the copse, heading west towards Widow Elara's secluded cottage. The distant, methodical movements of the patrol on the main road remained a cold, calculated awareness in the back of her mind, a variable to be monitored, not yet directly engaged. The night's harvest had begun.
