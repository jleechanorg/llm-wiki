The faint sound of Magister Kantos’s hushed instructions echoing through the speaking tube faded into the oppressive silence of the vault. You sagged against the rough-hewn stone wall, clutching your bandaged ear, the relentless throb a dull counterpoint to the searing ache behind your eyes. The metallic taste of blood still lingered in your mouth, a visceral reminder of your uncontrolled power, a terrifying echo of the monster your mother had become.

You closed your eyes, desperate for a moment of reprieve, a sliver of calm amidst the storm of revelations. The words of the ancient journals, the chilling designation "Joyful Predator," and the horrifying realization that this was merely the refined essence of the Empyrean Affliction, swirled in your mind. You saw your mother, not as the suffering martyr, but as the "unmoved" architect of her own terrible masterpiece. And the potential for that same chilling joy, that cold, precise unmaking, now thrummed beneath your own skin, an unwelcome inheritance that both terrified and fascinated you.

You focused on your breathing, trying to calm the frantic hammering of your heart. *Rest. Regain control.* You attempted to push the traumatic memories away, to build a temporary wall against the overwhelming data, but your mind, ever analytical, kept dissecting the raw experience. The flash of green light, the silent void, the sheer, unimaginable force of magic being ripped from the world – it had been chaotic, yes, and agonizingly painful, but beneath it all, a sliver of terrifying perfection. A cold, elegant beauty in absolute control.

Your body still trembled, a faint, uncontrollable tremor running through your limbs, and the psychic wound behind your eyes pulsed with every beat of your heart. You were utterly drained, physically and emotionally, but the very act of attempting to master your own internal chaos, to impose order on the rampant pain, brought a strange, grim satisfaction. You needed Kaelan. You needed to understand what you had just touched, to learn how to wield it without being consumed. The silence of the vault, once a comfort, now felt like a taunting void, waiting for your next attempt. Every passing second felt like a tick of the clock, counting down to the arrival of your companions, and the inevitable confrontation with the horrors of the Quiet Ward.
