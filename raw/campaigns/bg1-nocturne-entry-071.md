Game Master:
You lower your bow, the tension in the string easing. The *Hunger* demands the kill, but your mind—the mind of the Serpent Queen—demands survival. The calculation is cold and absolute: Chieftain Gnarl is a Tier 2 threat, fortified in a keep, surrounded by a returning army. Engaging him now, with half your party wounded and your spells depleted, is not bravery. It is suicide.

"Abort," you whisper, the command cutting through the adrenaline of the moment. "We have the witch. We have the gold. We leave."

**Minsc** looks at the keep, his greatsword twitching. "But... the evil! It is right there! Boo can smell its stink!"

"And if we stay, Boo will be eating your corpse," **Kagain** grunts, dragging **Dynaheir** away from the edge of the pit. "The girl is right, giant. The fire on the wall is dying down. The whole damn hive is about to swarm back here. We grab the win and we walk."

**Roric** nods, already melting back toward the cliff face. "Ghost protocol. I like it. Let's vanish before the rent comes due."

You execute the extraction with the same precision as the assault. Using the lingering smoke from the eastern fires as a screen, you guide the party up the shale slopes, bypassing the main gate entirely. You slip through a gap in the palisade where the fire has burned through the supports, stepping over the charred bodies of gnolls who died fighting the flames.

By the time the sun fully crests the peaks, the Gnoll Stronghold is a smudge of smoke in the distance. You push the pace, driving the party hard until the sounds of barking and war drums fade into the wind.

You have done the impossible: you walked into a fortress of monsters, stole their prize, slaughtered their elite, and walked out alive.

**Mirtul 8, Midday.**

You are camped in a high alpine meadow, miles from the stronghold. The air is crisp and clean. **Dynaheir** sits by the fire, mending her robes with a needle and thread Roric 'found'. She looks up as you approach.

"You possess a rare quality for a leader," the witch says, studying you. "Restraint. Rashemen warriors often die because they do not know when to sheath the blade. You... you calculate the cost of blood."

She gestures to the south, where the peaks give way to the valley of Nashkel. "My journal... my research into the Iron Crisis... it was lost in the capture. But the knowledge is in my head. The 'taint' is alchemical, as your bard suspected. But it is old magic. Abyssal."

**Minsc** is sparring with **Kagain** nearby, the clang of steel ringing out. **Garrick** is furiously scribbling in a notebook, muttering about "The Ballad of the Burning Keep."

You have a full party. You have the intel. The road leads back to Nashkel, and to the mines.

"We are a day's march from town," Roric says, chewing on a piece of dried meat. "We could head straight back to the Mayor and get that mine contract. Or... we could hunt. These mountains are crawling with bounties. **Prism** the sculptor is hiding somewhere near the emerald mines to the east. Oublek put a 300 gold price on his head for 'stealing' emeralds."
