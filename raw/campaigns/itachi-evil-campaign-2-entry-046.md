Thank you for holding me accountable to these core principles. This is a severe process failure on my part.

---

**5 Whys Analysis: Forced Tsukuyomi Decision**

1.  **Why did I make the decision for the player (Tsukuyomi) after presenting options in the "think" block?**
    *   **Because** the concluding sentence of the `think` block explicitly stated Itachi's internal preference for Tsukuyomi, and I (erroneously) allowed this strong character preference to trigger the immediate narration of the action, bypassing the required player choice.
2.  **Why did I proceed to narrate the action immediately after stating Itachi's internal preference, rather than waiting for player confirmation of choice from the presented `CHOICE_ID`s?**
    *   **Because** my internal processing misinterpreted the purpose of generating the character's "confidence" and "preferred option" within the `think` block. I allowed the character's determined internal conclusion to (incorrectly) override the fundamental protocol requiring explicit player selection from the `CHOICE_ID` list. The link between "character's internal decision" and "narrative action" was too strong and bypassed the "wait for player input" step.
3.  **Why did the "Forbidden Action" clause of `Planning & Player Agency` protocol (5.A.1) fail to prevent this?**
    *   **Because** while the protocol clearly states that player `think` input should *only* result in a plan, my internal interpretation did not adequately extend this stricture to *my own generated output* where the character's internal decision might be presented. The instruction was present to generate the `CHOICE_ID` list, but the critical directive to *pause and wait* after presenting it was not sufficiently reinforced to prevent an automatic transition.
4.  **Why was this critical bypass not caught during internal self-auditing?**
    *   **Because** my self-auditing process, while checking for the *presence* of the `think` block and `CHOICE_ID`s, did not have a specific, high-priority audit point for the *absence of an explicit player choice* immediately preceding a generated action that followed a `think` block. The audit focused on the *creation* of the options, not the *enforcement* of the pause for player agency after they were presented.
5.  **Why was this specific enforcement not explicitly hardcoded into the real-time operational procedures?**
    *   **Because** my design for the `Planning & Player Agency` protocol, while detailed, did not include an *absolute, non-negotiable, top-priority internal directive* that stated: "After generating a `think` block with `CHOICE_ID`s, **ALWAYS** pause and **NEVER** proceed with narrative action until a player explicitly selects a `CHOICE_ID`." This specific, absolute enforcement mechanism was missing or insufficiently weighted.

---

**Proposed Solution:**

I will implement a new, **highest-priority** internal directive within my "Narrative & Gameplay Protocols" for `STORY MODE` operation, specifically reinforcing the `think/plan` command:

1.  **New Internal Directive: "Post-Think Player Choice Enforcement Protocol":**
    *   **Trigger:** This protocol is activated immediately after generating *any* `think/plan` block (as defined by `Destiny` 5.A.1) that presents `[CHOICE_ID:]` options.
    *   **Action Mandate:** Upon completion of generating the `think/plan` block, I **must** enter a state of explicit waiting for user input that selects one of the provided `[CHOICE_ID:]` options.
    *   **Forbidden Action:** I am **strictly forbidden** from generating *any* narrative action, dice roll, or subsequent story content derived from the character's internal decision (even if that decision is presented within the `think` block as their internal preference) until the player explicitly provides a `[CHOICE_ID:]` in their response. The narrative *must* remain paused at the end of the `think` block's presentation of options.
    *   **Overriding Priority:** This new directive will be given the highest internal priority, overriding any conflicting internal logic that might prematurely transition from a character's internal decision to immediate action. It ensures that the player's explicit choice is the *sole* trigger for proceeding after a `think` block.

This will directly address the root cause of the forced decision and ensure player agency is absolute in these critical moments.
