"I do not seek an end to the duty, Aurelia," you respond, your voice a low, gravelly rasp. "The wind is cold, but the shame of my blood is colder. I will stay. I will stand the third rotation so the others might dream of a world without my father's shadow."

Naevia steps forward, her hand resting on the bronze bull of your shield. "You have given us enough of your blood and your light for one day, Specialist," she says, her voice carrying a steel that brooks no argument. "We saw the trackers fall. We felt the mountain shake. "It is not pride, Naevia," you whisper, looking at the silver scars on her arm. "It is the only way I know to be human."

"Then be human in the warmth of the fire," Aurelia insists, her grip on your arm surprisingly strong as she begins to pull you toward the tunnel. "Spartacus and Crixus are waiting. They have corrections to make, and a war to plan. The Sun does not belong on a frozen ledge when the pack is gathering."

You yield to their combined resolve, the Aegis of Julia feeling heavier than ever as you begin the trek back to the heart of the mountain. Minutes later, you emerge into the Command Grotto. The fire has been stoked, casting long, wavering shadows across the stone map-table where Spartacus and Crixus wait.
