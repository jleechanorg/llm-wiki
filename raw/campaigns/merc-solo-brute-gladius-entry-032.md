The air inside the Miller farmhouse was thick with the scent of dried herbs, a cloying sweetness that was almost offensive to Gladius's senses. The distant, faint glow of the burning shed outside, meant to draw the attention of the oblivious villagers, was a silent testament to his foresight. He had slipped in like a ghost, his Shadow-Step Boots muting his heavy armor to a mere whisper. Now, the den must be sealed.

He moved with a hunter's methodical precision, his large frame surprisingly agile in the cramped, moonlit interior. Each step was deliberate, silent. He first approached the heavy wooden front door, testing its sturdy latch. With a quiet grunt of effort, he slid a thick, iron bar across its frame, the *thunk* barely audible even in the profound silence of the sleeping house. No one was getting in or out that way without considerable noise.

Next, the windows. He checked each one, ensuring the latches were firm. Some were old, their wooden frames warped. He applied a touch more force, listening for any tell-tale creak that might rouse the slumbering occupants. The glass, grimy with years of dust, offered no view of the outside world, effectively sealing the house in its own morbid cocoon. His disdain for their carelessness, their pathetic lack of preparedness, was a grim satisfaction. They were so utterly vulnerable, so unaware of the predator in their midst. His gaze, cold and analytical, swept across the dimly lit room, confirming the trap was set. The scent of herbs seemed to intensify, now laced with the faint, metallic tang of his own anticipation. The stage was set.
