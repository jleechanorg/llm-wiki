You return to the Nashkel General Store, the heavy sack of Oublek's gold clinking with every step. The shopkeeper looks up, annoyed at the intrusion so close to closing time, but the annoyance evaporates instantly when you drop the platinum and gold onto the counter.

"We require upgrades," you state, your voice cutting through the dusty air. "And we require them now."

You conduct the transaction with cold, mathematical precision, prioritizing the assets that yield the highest return on violence. The *Hunger* ignores sentimentality; it feeds the strongest.

**First, yourself.** You purchase a **Potion of Invisibility** and a **Potion of Heroism**. These are force multipliers, ensuring you can vanish or sustain a rage-like focus when the line breaks. You also clear his stock of **Antidotes** (10 vials); the mines are rumored to be infested with spiders, and poison is an inefficiency you cannot afford.

**Next, the Iron Shield.** You turn to **Kagain**. The dwarf is eyeing a suit of heavy **Splint Mail** on a mannequin. It is ugly, functional, and tough—just like him.
"Take it," you order. "And the heavy steel shield behind it."
Kagain grins, stripping off his battered chain. "Finally. Some proper steel. I'll be a walking fortress."

**Then, the Blade.** You gesture to **Roric**. The rogue has been eyeing a finely balanced **Masterwork Shortsword** in the display case. It isn't magical, but the edge is razor-keen.
"For the flank," you say.
Roric weighs the blade, slashing the air with a whistle. "Beautiful. I'll put it to good use."

**The others get scraps.** 
**Minsc** eyes a similar suit of Splint Mail hopefully. "Boo thinks heavy armor would protect his soft fur!"
You shake your head. "Too heavy for the terrain, Minsc. Speed is your armor today."
The berserker looks crestfallen but accepts the logic. You buy him nothing but a whetstone. **Dynaheir** and **Garrick** receive standard rations and water. They are backline assets; if they are taking damage, the plan has already failed.

You leave the store 1,050 gold pieces lighter, but infinitely more dangerous. Kagain clanks with new weight, Roric tests his edge, and you have the alchemical tools to control the battlefield.
