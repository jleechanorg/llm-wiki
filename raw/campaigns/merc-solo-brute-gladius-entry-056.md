The barren silence of the Blackwood cottage lay behind Gladius, a testament to two more lives meticulously erased. The lack of valuables brought a sneer to his lips – further proof of their pathetic insignificance. But the chill satisfaction of absolute control, of having bent two more existences to his brutal will, was a potent hum beneath his skin. The subtle flicker of his signal to Corvan, sent moments ago, was a memory of triumph, forcing the envoy to wallow in the grim aftermath of his master's arrogance. This was the true indulgence: the manipulation of the supposedly powerful.

He moved through the pre-dawn gloom of Greenmeadow, heading towards the village center where the Stonehand dwelling stood. The air grew colder, the damp chill of the marsh beginning to yield to the crispness of approaching dawn. His Shadow-Step Boots continued their silent dance against the dew-soaked earth, effortlessly muting the heavy clang of his splint mail to a mere whisper. The greatsword on his back felt like a loyal, eager companion, vibrating with unspoken anticipation.

Gladius recalled the lackey's words: the Stonehands were "strong-backed, but simple." He’d observed them during his re-scout – loud, boisterous, their laughter carrying on the breeze. Such open, unshielded lives were ripe for the crushing. He felt a surge of raw, experiential pleasure. This wasn’t the cold, surgical precision of the previous two kills; this was for the sheer, immediate thrill of it. Their simple strength, their predictable lives, would be a fragile thing against his true power. The first faint streaks of orange and grey painted the eastern sky, promising a new day. By the time the sun truly rose, Greenmeadow would be a quiet, empty canvas, and Lord Silvanus’s arrogance would have paid its full, bloody price.
