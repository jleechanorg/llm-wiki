**--- THINK ---**
The raw torrent of Kaelan’s memories, the sheer, unendurable horror he relived, still echoes in my mind. My head throbs, and the metallic taste of blood is a constant reminder of how close I came to breaking under that psychic assault. I glimpsed it – the terrifying clarity of Mother’s power, the chilling efficiency in unmaking – but it was a chaotic, uncontrolled vision. I need to access those memories, but not like this. Not blindly.

I look at the options before me, assessing each with a cold, desperate precision.

*   **Instruct Rowan (`CHOICE_ID: InstructRowan_1`):** Rowan is empathy and kindness, a balm for physical and emotional pain. He has no training in accessing or navigating shattered minds, no understanding of psychic trauma. Asking him to directly facilitate memory access would be pushing him far beyond his capabilities, potentially traumatizing him, and almost certainly failing to yield useful information. It might even harm Kaelan further. His role is to soothe, to stabilize, not to probe.
    *   *Success Rate for accessing memories via Rowan:* Very Low. *Risk of negative outcome (for Rowan/Kaelan):* Moderate.

*   **Explain Truth to Team (`CHOICE_ID: ExplainTruthToTeam_3`):** This is a crucial step, a strategic imperative. My companions deserve to know the horrifying truths I've uncovered about Mother, about the Null Genesis, about the Affliction. Their loyalty is absolute, but their understanding of the stakes and the true enemy is incomplete. Knowing this truth would deepen their commitment, prepare them for what we truly face, and allow for better collaboration. However, this is Kaelan's cell, filled with his psychic torment. It's not the ideal environment for such a profound and disturbing revelation. It won't directly help me access Kaelan's memories now.
    *   *Success Rate for team understanding:* High. *Risk of immediate mission impact:* Neutral (won't directly help Kaelan, but sets up future collaboration).

*   **Direct Contact (`CHOICE_ID: DirectContact_4`):** This is the ultimate objective, the direct plunge into Kaelan's mind using my latent Mirror Magic. I just experienced a severe psychic backlash from attempting to access my *own* memory. To directly touch Kaelan's shattered, constantly looping horror, without further understanding, is incredibly risky. His mind is a maelstrom. It could shatter my own nascent abilities, or worse, break *my* mind, trap *me* in his loop. The journals spoke of the dangers of uncontrolled Null phenomena. My own power is still untrained. This is the fastest route, but the most dangerous. I need a clearer path into his mind, not a head-on collision.
    *   *Success Rate for controlled access:* Very Low. *Risk of severe negative outcome (for Sariel):* Very High.

My immediate need is to refine my approach to Kaelan. I understand the *what* of his trauma (the Nullification Field, Mother's terrifying composure), but not the *how* of his current mental state, how his memories are "walled off." Rowan said his mind was "pure chaos," "walled off." My `Comprehend Languages` helped me understand his *words*, but not the *structure* of his broken mind. I need to understand his madness, not just his whispers. This requires a more nuanced approach than simply understanding language.

I need to understand the *nature* of Kaelan's madness itself. Is it a protective shell? A prison? A doorway? What kind of spell or mental technique would be able to even *perceive* the structure of a mind so profoundly broken? My current spells are `Light`, `Detect Magic`, `Magic Missile`, `Shield`, `Silent Image`, `Identify`, `Comprehend Languages`. None of these seem perfectly suited to mapping the internal landscape of madness. I need a way to scan his mind's defenses, not just his words.

You knelt beside Rowan, the full horror of Kaelan's condition laid bare before you. His moans and whispers, once just a disorienting cacophony, now felt like a direct assault on your own fragile composure. Rowan’s gentle murmurs, while a comfort, could not pierce the wall of madness that imprisoned Sergeant Kaelan. You needed a different kind of understanding, a window into the specific torment that held him captive.

Your mind, now operating with a cold, precise focus, turned to your meager magical repertoire. `Comprehend Languages`. It was a simple spell, but one that might offer the crucial insight you needed. It wouldn't heal him, but it might translate the chaos, might turn the unintelligible whispers into a horrifying, yet decipherable, truth.

You extended your free hand, palm open, hovering inches above Kaelan's forehead. You closed your eyes, pushing a controlled trickle of your nascent magical energy into the cantrip. The subtle thrum of the Weave responded, your untrained magic still struggling faintly, but holding. You felt the familiar pull, the brief disorientation, as the spell wove its way through your mind, preparing to interpret the fractured thoughts of another.

As you opened your eyes, the moans and whispers from Kaelan's lips did not cease, but they *changed*. The chaotic cacophony resolved into a torrent of fragmented, yet horrifically clear, words. You heard snippets of battle cries, the clang of steel, the whoosh of immense wings. But beneath it all, a chilling, repeating refrain: *"...silence...the field...gone...impossible...unmoved...her eyes...cold...joy...unmaking...Lucifer...gone..."*

His words, now intelligible, painted a devastating picture. He was reliving the Starfall Gambit, trapped in the moment of your mother's ultimate act. He described the sudden, impossible void of the Nullification Field, the look of disbelief on the Apostates' faces as their magic died. And then, the horrifying climax: *"...she rose...the light...wings...so many...not a Null...she was...unmoved...Lucifer...his face...broken...joy...cold...eyes..."* The last words were a raw, desperate gasp of pure, unendurable terror, the sound of a universe shattering in his mind. The image of Alexiel, triumphant and terrifyingly composed amidst the unmaking, burned in his consciousness, forever scarring him.

You gasped, tearing your hand away, the metallic taste of blood returning to your mouth as the psychic strain momentarily overwhelmed you. The direct, unfiltered torrent of Kaelan's broken memories, the raw horror of his witnessed truth, was far more devastating than any abstract scholarly text. You had glimpsed it through his eyes, the terrifying clarity of your mother's power, and the chilling, elegant ruthlessness that accompanied it.
