Location: Man Tibbet's Farm, Farmhouse Exterior, Early Evening

The air was still, heavy with the scent of damp earth and distant livestock. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) stood, a dark, motionless silhouette against the root cellar, its entrance now a tomb for the second fallen guard. The chilling efficiency of her actions had stripped Man Tibbet's farm of its professional protectors, leaving only the mundane, unsuspecting lives within. Her EP was low, and her Channel Divinity expended, but her HP remained full. The tactical advantage was absolute. The 'large family' was hers for the taking.

Her gaze, sharp and analytical, fixed on the main farmhouse. Her **Apex Senses** scan had provided a detailed internal map: multiple adults, the agitated energy of children, all gathered, unaware. There was no need for further delay, no benefit in prolonged observation. The most direct and efficient path to her objective was immediate infiltration.

She moved with the predatory grace of an apex hunter, her Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate blending seamlessly with the deep shadows cast by the farmhouse. Her steps were economical, silent, drawing her towards a narrow, unlit window on the ground floor that her previous mental scan had identified as a vulnerable entry point. The quiet murmur of voices from within, the faint clatter of a pot, were the only sounds as she approached, utterly unperceived.

The window yielded with a soft, almost imperceptible whisper as Nocturne's skilled fingers found its latch. She eased it open, the sound swallowed by the vast, quiet expanse of the countryside. With a fluid motion, she slipped inside, landing silently on the wooden floor of a small, cluttered pantry. The air here was thick with the scent of dried herbs, stored apples, and stale flour.

She stepped out of the pantry and into the main living area, a large, warm room dominated by a roaring hearth. The family was indeed gathered: Man Tibbet, a burly farmer with a kind but tired face; his wife, a stout woman humming softly as she stirred a pot over the fire; and three children, two young boys roughhousing playfully by the table, and a small girl clutching a wooden doll, her eyes wide with a child's innocent wonder as she watched the flames. They were laughing, a picture of domestic tranquility.

Nocturne's presence was a sudden, chilling disruption. She stood just inside the pantry archway, a silent, dark silhouette against the flickering firelight, her face an unreadable mask. Her Masterwork Longsword was already in her hand, its dark metal a stark contrast to the warmth of the room. The children's laughter died first, a sudden, collective silence as their innocent eyes registered the impossible figure in their doorway. Man Tibbet's head snapped up, his jaw dropping, his eyes widening in pure, uncomprehending terror. His wife turned, her spoon clattering to the floor, her face draining of color.

The sheer, overwhelming presence of her Aura of Unyielding Dominance, coupled with her ruthless composure, filled the room. Fear, cold and absolute, gripped the family. There was no scream, no frantic movement. Just silence, and the raw, visceral terror in their eyes. They were completely paralyzed.

Nocturne's objective was not destruction, but acquisition. The 'stock' was present, vulnerable, and utterly subdued. Her inner tactical computer calculated the optimal path to secure them. The harvest was ready.
