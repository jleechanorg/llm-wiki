Daemon (Level 7, Age 30) sat at the head of the polished oak table in Blackwood Keep's Great Hall, the flickering torchlight reflecting in his scaled armor. The feast laid before him was lavish, a testament to House Blackwood's famed fertile lands. He sampled the roasted boar, rich with herbs, and the sweet, dark wine. A subtle, almost imperceptible satisfaction crossed his features as he truly *tasted* the quality of the meal – a rare indulgence he allowed himself, a reward for the relentless grind of conquest. He took another deliberate bite of the tender meat, appreciating the skill of the Blackwood cooks.

Even as he savored the flavors, his sharp eyes missed nothing. He observed Lady Isolde's (Age: ~22) staff – older, some with years of ingrained loyalty to the Blackwood name, others clearly nervous opportunists. Their movements were practiced but lacked the brutal efficiency of his own hardened recruits. He noted the quiet tension among the household guards, their fear of him palpable, a useful tool but not a guarantee of loyalty. This was an ancient house, steeped in tradition, not the grim, efficient machines Daemon preferred.

Later, in the privacy of his chambers, Daemon turned to Kael (Level 6, Age 25), who had been equally observant. "Their lands are fertile, beyond doubt," Daemon stated, a grim satisfaction in his tone, his appreciation for the Blackwood produce evident. "A granary for our growing army. But the keep's defenses are antiquated, and their forces meager. They have relied on isolation, a luxury they no longer have under my banner. The risk is not open defiance, but the subtle, ingrained habits of old power. Are there any immediate threats to our control, or to Isolde, that your scouts unearthed?"

Kael nodded, his own assessment aligning. "Indeed, My Lord. Isolde is a calculating woman. She accepted for survival and ambition. She will need to be reminded, constantly, that her ascension is entirely dependent on your will, and that her loyalty must be absolute. The old retainers may present a passive resistance, siphoning resources or quietly undermining your authority. We must ensure their supply lines are fully integrated, and their wealth accounted for, immediately. As for immediate threats, My Lord, the scouts mentioned only the typical regional banditry, nothing organized enough to challenge House Blackwood, much less your presence. The only other whisper was of a minor lord, **Lord Valerick of the Western Marches**, who had previously made overtures to House Blackwood for an alliance through marriage, but was rebuffed. He is not a direct threat, but a potential source of irritation, perhaps even a minor proxy conflict if he feels slighted by this new union."
