Game Master:
You hand Roric one of the black-fletched arrows recovered from the perimeter patrol. "Make it count," you whisper. "Black Talon arrow. Hobgoblin throat. Don't be seen."

Roric grins, his teeth white in the gloom. "I love a good tragedy." He melts into the shadows, circling wide toward the cluster of tents bearing the Black Talon insignia.

You wait in the brush, the *Hunger* counting down the seconds. The camp is a powder keg of racial tension; the human mercenaries despise the hobgoblins, and the hobgoblins resent the humans for their better pay. All it needs is a spark.

**The Shot.**

From the darkness near the Talon latrines, a single arrow flies. It is not a masterwork shot aimed at a vital organ for a silent kill; it is a messy, brutal shot aimed at the neck of a **Chill Hobgoblin Sergeant** who is loudly berating a human cook.

The arrow strikes with a wet thud. The Sergeant gurgles, clutching the black-fletched shaft protruding from his throat, and collapses onto the cook's table, spraying blood over the stew.

For a second, there is silence. Then, a roar erupts.

"Talons!" a hobgoblin screams. "They killed Krog! Kill the pink-skins!"

**Chaos Roll: 93 (Catastrophic Success).**

The effect is electric. The simmering resentment boils over instantly. Hobgoblins draw swords and charge the nearest humans. The Black Talons, confused and defensive, draw steel to protect themselves. Within moments, the central bonfire is the site of a fifty-man brawl. Tents are knocked over, torches fall into dry grass, and the shouts of "Betrayal!" echo through the valley.

**The Distraction.**

At the Command Tent, the discipline of the **Black Talon Elites** cracks. They look from the tent flap to the rioting army. "Protect the camp!" one shouts, and two of the four guards abandon their post to rush toward the melee, intending to restore order.

Only two guards remain at the entrance. The Mage (**Venkt**) and **Tazok** are likely still inside, distracted by the sudden anarchy outside. The noise of the riot will cover the sounds of your assault.

"Now," you signal. "While they bleed each other."
