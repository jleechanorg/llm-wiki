--- BACKGROUND ---

The year is 1493 DR, and Baldur's Gate still bears the fresh wounds of the Absolute's onslaught. The Upper City remains locked behind barricades, the Patriar families clutching their wealth while the Lower City descends into controlled chaos. The Guild's influence has fractured, creating a power vacuum that the Zhentarim have ruthlessly exploited. Under the banner of "Pax Zhentarim," the Black Network has established themselves as the new authority - peace enforced at swordpoint and maintained through superior organization.

You are Nocturne, the Silent Blade, a weapon perfected through both noble refinement and brutal necessity. Disgraced from whatever house once claimed you, you found your true calling among the Zhentarim, where your particular talents for psychological warfare and lethal efficiency are not just tolerated - they're prized. At nineteen, you've already carved a reputation as someone who doesn't just eliminate threats, but dismantles them piece by piece, leaving scars on both bodies and reputations. Your methods walk the line between seduction and slaughter, each simply a different tool in your arsenal.

The Hunger gnaws at your edges - it has been five days since your last kill, and that familiar twitchiness begins to creep into your fingers. Tonight, you stand on the precipice of opportunity. Zarys, your Zhentarim handler, has summoned you to discuss the Ironhead Legion, twelve mercenaries from Amn who foolishly believe they can challenge Zhentarim dominance in the Outer City. They raided a convoy, killed Zhentarim operatives, and stole smokepowder. This isn't just business anymore - it's a message that must be sent in blood.

--- ACT I, SCENE 1: THE NEGOTIATION ---

The penthouse of the Elfsong Tavern has been transformed into a throne of ill-gotten luxury. Velvet drapes catch the candlelight, stolen masterpieces adorn the walls, and the air is thick with the scent of roasted boar and expensive wine - the spoils of conquest laid bare. Zarys stands by the balcony, her silhouette framed against the ruined cityscape below. She doesn't turn as you enter, letting the silence hang as a test of your patience.

"You're late," she says, though your arrival is precisely on time. The goblet in her hand once belonged to a Duke - now it's merely another trophy. "I suppose talent operates on its own schedule."

She moves to a heavy oaken table spread with tactical maps, the wine swirling in her glass. "We have a situation. The Ironhead Legion - a mercenary company from Amn with more ambition than sense. They raided one of our convoys yesterday. Three men dead, a crate of smokepowder taken. They're holed up in an old Flaming Fist outpost near Wyrm's Rock."

Her knuckles pale as she grips the table edge. "I don't want them arrested. I don't want them chased off. I want them erased. Twelve men, led by a half-orc brute named Korg who thinks he's already king of the Outer City."

A heavy sack of gold slides across the table, the clink of coins a familiar melody. "Five hundred gold. Standard fee for a wipeout."

But this isn't standard, and you both know it. This is an assault on a fortified position against a dozen experienced mercenaries. This is the kind of message that requires artistry in its brutality. Zarys has the budget for at least eight hundred, perhaps more if you offer to make it especially memorable.

You've heard of Korg - loud, arrogant, with a weakness for women and wine. A vulnerability to be exploited. Twelve souls waiting to be unmade, and the Hunger stirs at the prospect.

Zarys meets your gaze, her expression unreadable. "Well? Do we have an accord, Silent Blade? Or is the blood too rich for your taste?"
