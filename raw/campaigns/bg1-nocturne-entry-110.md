Game Master:
You stand amidst the ruins of Mulahey's command, the *Hunger* calculating the value of the spoils with cold, hierarchical logic. The party watches you, waiting for the scraps from the alpha's table.

"Priorities," you state, your voice echoing in the silent cavern.

You pick up the **Shortsword +1**. The blade is etched with jagged runes and hums with a faint, predatory vibration. It is a weapon for a killer, not a soldier.

"Roric," you call out. The rogue steps forward, wiping kobold blood from his leathers. You toss him the blade. "Replace that masterwork toy. If you're going to watch my flank, I want you cutting through bone, not just skin."

Roric catches the weapon, his eyes widening as he feels the enchantment. He slashes the air, the blade singing a high, lethal note. "Magic steel," he grins, sheathing it. "I like the weight. Consider your flank secure."

Next, you lift the **Potion of Absorption**. The liquid inside swirls like liquid heavy metal. It is a defensive masterpiece, capable of turning skin to stone against elemental fury.

"Kagain," you bark. The dwarf grunts, leaning on his axe. You throw him the vial. "You take the hits. This will make sure you survive them. Drink it if we face another mage like Tarnesh, or a dragon."

Kagain snatches the potion, uncorking it to sniff the contents. "Smells like ozone and rock," he rumbles, tucking it into his belt pouch. "Good. I hate burning."

**Minsc** steps forward hopefully, Boo squeaking on his shoulder. **Dynaheir** watches with a cool, expectant gaze. You ignore them both. They are useful tools, but they are not *yours* in the way the mercenaries are. They get nothing but the privilege of survival.

"We are done here," you announce, turning your back on the disappointment in Minsc's eyes. "To the surface."

***

**The Return.**

The ascent is grueling but unopposed. You leave the darkness of the mines behind, emerging into the blinding afternoon sun of the Cloudpeaks. The air tastes sweet after the sulfur of the pit.

The march back to Nashkel is a victory lap. The miners huddled at the entrance watch you pass with awe, seeing the blood on your armor and the grim satisfaction on Kagain's face. Word spreads faster than you walk.

By the time you reach the town square, the mood has shifted. The sullen fear is gone, replaced by a buzzing, nervous energy. You head straight for the **Temple of Helm**, where Mayor Ghastkill is waiting. He stands on the steps, wringing his hands, looking terrified that you might have failed—or perhaps more terrified that you succeeded and he now owes you a fortune.

You throw Mulahey's holy symbol at his feet. It clatters loudly against the white stone.

"The mines are clear," you say, the *Hunger* projecting an aura of absolute dominance. "The cleric is dead. The kobolds are scattered. Now, pay me."
