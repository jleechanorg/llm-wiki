### Explanation of 'Soul Burn' in The Pax Julia

In the context of Decimus’s speech and the metaphysics of this campaign, **'Soul Burn'** refers to the visible and spiritual manifestation of your **Auctoritas** (the weight of your command) and the **Fated Miasma** (your divine/cursed heritage) when pushed to an extreme level of intensity.

**1. Metaphysical Meaning (The Miasma):**
Because your character, Gaia, is a 'Singularity' in the timeline, her soul isn't just a battery for magic; it is a anchor for reality. When you push yourself—like leading a Speed March while suffering psychic strain—the Miasma doesn't just stay inside your mind. It 'leaks' out. Decimus is describing the gold-flecked fire in your eyes and the way your presence seems to physically warp the air around you. To him, your soul isn't just flickering; it is incinerating the limitations of a normal human to force a new reality into existence.

**2. Cultural/Historical Context (Numen & Majestas):**
- **Numen:** Romans believed in *numen*, a spiritual power or 'nod' of a divinity that inhabited places and people of great importance. Decimus, a superstitious veteran, recognizes your 'soul burn' as a raw, terrifyingly high concentration of *numen*.
- **Majestas:** This is the 'majesty' or 'greatness' of the Roman state. By saying your soul burns, Decimus is acknowledging that you possess the inherent right to rule (*Imperium*) not because of a law, but because of your essence. In his eyes, you have the same 'fire' that made Julius Caesar capable of crossing the Rubicon and challenging the gods.

**3. Mechanical Indicator:**
You just used your Miasma to create a 'fragor' (psychic static) and then pushed a cohort through a Speed March while exhausted. In game terms, this 'burn' is the narrative flavor for your high Charisma score and your **Oath of the Eagle** manifesting under pressure. It is the moment where 'Personality' becomes 'Power.'

**Summary:** To Decimus, 'Soul Burn' is the undeniable proof that you are more than a girl or a fugitive—you are a living catalyst of Fate. It is why he, a hardened veteran of the Tenth, finally fell to his knees.
