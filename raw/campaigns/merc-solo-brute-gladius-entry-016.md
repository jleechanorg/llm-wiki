`Location: Baldur's Gate Market District & Back Alleys, Lower City`

The thought of the greatsword resonated with Gladius, a pure, brutal symphony of overwhelming force. He turned away from the general market, heading towards the clang and smoke of the smithies in the lower district. The air grew thick with the scent of hot metal and coal. He found a burly dwarven smith, sweat-slicked and grim, whose eyes held a flicker of respect for Gladius’s sheer physical presence. The transaction was swift; Gladius laid fifty gold pieces on the scarred counter, picked the heaviest, most unadorned greatsword, and immediately felt its satisfying weight in his hands. It was an extension of his own destructive will, simple and perfect. He discarded his longsword without a second thought, the lighter blade feeling almost dainty now.

With the greatsword secured to his back, he then turned his attention to the shadows, seeking the kind of 'merchants' who dealt in tools more discreet than steel. He navigated winding alleyways, the stench of refuse replacing the tang of metal, until he found a grimy, unlit stall tucked away behind a fishmonger. A gaunt, suspicious-looking half-elf, draped in dark fabric, watched him approach.

"Looking for things that go unnoticed, eh?" the half-elf rasped, his voice like rustling dry leaves. His eyes, quick and darting, assessed Gladius’s imposing frame and the greatsword on his back. A predator recognizing another.

Gladius simply grunted, his eyes cold. "For a job. Clean. Silent." He let the words hang, conveying both his intent and the promise of payment.

The half-elf’s lips twitched in something that wasn't quite a smile. He produced two items from beneath his counter: a length of thin, almost invisible cord woven with dark fibers, and a pair of soft, leather boots soled with layers of padded felt. "Silent Strangler's Cord," he whispered, gesturing to the garrote. "And Shadow-Step Boots. For those who want to walk among ghosts." He named a price: "A hundred for the pair."

Gladius looked from the items to the half-elf’s eyes. The price was steep, but the tools were precisely what he needed to ensure Lord Silvanus’s 'no witnesses' demand was met with extreme prejudice. This wasn't about cost; it was about absolute control over the situation. He pulled another heavy pouch, counted out the hundred coins, and dropped them onto the counter. He took the cord and the boots, feeling the raw efficiency of them. The half-elf nodded, a flicker of genuine respect in his eyes now, for both the coin and the implied ruthlessness.

The sun was beginning to dip below the city spires as Gladius emerged from the alleys, heavier with purpose, lighter by a hundred and fifty gold, but armed with the means to turn the Greenmeadow contract into a chilling testament to his own burgeoning power. The thought of the 'clever' Lord Silvanus, who thought he could simply pay for a service, stirred a deeper, more satisfying ambition within him. The peasants were just practice.
