*   With 450 GP, the best two-handed heavy weapon Gladius can afford is a **Greatsword**.
    *   **Cost:** 50 GP
    *   **Damage:** 2d6 Slashing damage
    *   **Properties:** Heavy, Two-Handed (perfect for Great Weapon Master)

The afternoon sun, now a little lower in the sky, cast long shadows across the bustling market. Gladius, his pouch heavy with the weight of Lord Silvanus’s coerced gold, paused amidst the chaotic energy. He had secured the necessities – potions, rope, rations – pragmatic tools for the task ahead. But his mind, ever the calculating predator, turned to a more direct form of enhancement: the sheer destructive power he wielded.

His worn longsword, though reliable, felt almost... *polite* in his hand compared to the brute force he craved. He thought of the sheer, unbridled impact a truly heavy weapon could deliver, the kind that didn’t just cut, but *crushed*. The thought brought a grim satisfaction. He glanced down at his splint mail, its metal scuffed and scarred but offering an unyielding shell. It was good, strong. *Plenty for now,* he mused, a flicker of his ambition burning in his eyes. More armor meant more wealth tied up, and he had bigger targets for his gold than a few more pounds of steel. His armor was enough, a solid foundation for the brutality to come.

No, the real improvement lay elsewhere. He pictured the Great Weapon Master feat, the way it could turn a clean strike into a devastating, bone-shattering blow. A heavy, two-handed weapon was the key to unlocking its full potential. A greatsword. The idea resonated with the core of his being – simple, direct, overwhelmingly powerful. He would not just kill these peasants; he would make an example out of their very existence, a warning to anyone who dared to defy the power he represented. The tools for that were within his grasp, for a mere fraction of his new wealth. He could already feel the heavy weight of the blade in his hands, the satisfying swing.
