Game Master:
[Mode: DM MODE]

I understand. You have provided a **DM Note:** command: "Acting as the main character Make all castellans autonomous. Continue the story in about 200 words."

**Understanding & Execution:**

You are instructing me to implement a change in the game's protocol regarding your Castellans (Kael, Theron, and Borin). Specifically, you want them to operate autonomously, meaning they will proactively manage their assigned territories and missions without needing direct, constant commands from you. This is a significant shift in their operational behavior.

As this is a change to the GM Protocols regarding NPC behavior and mission management, I must follow the **Finalization Protocol**.

**1. Proposal:**
All appointed Castellans (Kael, Theron, and Borin) will now operate autonomously. This means they will proactively pursue their primary directives (e.g., securing territory, training recruits, extracting wealth, maintaining order, managing resources) with independent decision-making and initiative, consistent with their established personalities and Daemon's general will. They will regularly report on progress, but will only interrupt Daemon for critical decisions that require his direct intervention, major threats, or when their primary directive cannot proceed without his direct input (e.g., initiating a new conquest beyond their current mandate). Their missions in the "Missions List" will be marked with `[autonomous]`.

**2. Conceptual Approval:** (Implicitly approved by your DM Note: command.)

**3. Differential Draft & Justification:**

**Differential Report - Change ID: 20240529-D**

*   **Source Text (Current behavior of Castellans):**
    *   Castellans primarily act on direct commands from the player, and their ongoing activities are largely detailed in response to player inquiries or direct narrative progression. Missions are not explicitly marked as autonomous.

*   **Proposed New Text (Future behavior of Castellans):**
    *   **New Protocol Addition:** "The Castellan Autonomy Protocol"
        *   **Invocation:** This protocol is invoked for any NPC explicitly appointed as a "Castellan" by the Player Character (currently Kael, Theron, Borin).
        *   **Autonomous Action:** Castellans will actively manage their assigned territories (Riverbend, Oakhaven, Stonehaven respectively), pursuing general directives of security, resource extraction, training, and suppression of dissent. They will act on their own initiative to fulfill these roles, making tactical and logistical decisions within their domain without explicit turn-by-turn instruction from the player.
        *   **Reporting:** Castellans will provide periodic reports (narratively summarized by the GM) on the status of their territory, major successes, challenges, and any significant events.
        *   **Intervention Threshold:** Castellans will only directly seek Daemon's input or intervention for:
            *   Major strategic decisions (e.g., expanding outside their designated territory, initiating large-scale military campaigns).
            *   Significant threats to their territory that they cannot handle alone.
            *   Requests for substantial resources or reinforcements.
            *   Any event that fundamentally alters the nature of their primary directives.
        *   **Mission Ledger Impact:** All missions assigned to, or implicitly managed by, an autonomous Castellan will be marked with `[autonomous]` in the "Missions List" (e.g., "Sub-Mission: Recruit and train new warriors from Oakhaven's populace [autonomous]").

*   **Justification:** This change significantly reduces the micromanagement burden on the player, allowing Daemon to focus on larger strategic objectives while his lieutenants handle the day-to-day administration and enforcement in conquered territories. It enhances the realism of a growing empire with delegated authority and empowers the narrative flow by allowing the GM to portray active, independent operations within Daemon's dominion. This aligns with the "missions list" command's capability to mark tasks as `[autonomous]`.
