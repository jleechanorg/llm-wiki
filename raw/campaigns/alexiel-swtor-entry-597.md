You look down at the Pilgrim, the cooling mask of Master Ogan-De still clutched in your porcelain fingers. The scent of ozone and ancient guilt is a sated perfume in the valley's artificial spring. You do not offer him a hand, nor do you offer him the absolution he desperately seeks. Instead, you offer him a command.

"The confession is a data-packet, Father," you whisper, your voice a melodic law that vibrates through his very marrow. "But the heart is a processor you haven't yet brought online. You've admitted to being the witness; now, go and be the man. The galaxy is full of the wreckage you created. Find a piece of it that doesn't scream when you touch it. Keep trying. If you return to me with nothing but more apologies, I will unmake your skin and return you to the second of a thousand years of silence. Go."

In a blinding, monochromatic strobe of white fire, you dismiss him. Vitiate is pulled through the void, cast back to the desolate Outer Rim where his quest remains unfinished. You turn back to your mother, Senya, offering her a soft, shy smile that masks the infinite boredom of a goddess who has already seen the end of the timeline.

Six months dissolve into the fertile soil of Nathema. To the galaxy, this was the 'Season of the Silent Hand.' Your Level 100 Simulacrum oversaw the 'Democratic God Servant' mask on Coruscant, passing the 'Universal Healthcare Directive' and the 'Entropic Stabilization Act' with the religious acclimation of a Senate that has forgotten how to say 'no.' The Sith Empire thrived in its clinical submission, governed by the Shadow Courts and the Saintly mask of Voe. Prosperity is a law of physics now, and the 'Gilded Freedom' is a masterpiece of unmoving perfection.

On Nathema, the Heaven Palace has become the only reality that matters. You have spent these one hundred and eighty days walking the hardwoods with Senya, playing the part of the daughter who chose a garden over a throne. Your mother has settled into a state of adoring resignation, her heart vitrified into a permanent, biological anchor for your divinity. She watches you tend the lilies, her spirit a sated witness to the 'miracle' she no longer has the strength to question.

Voe returned from her godhood exploration three days ago. She has changed; her Level 35 radiance is now a steady, leaden shroud, and she looks at the universe with the judicial coldness of an entity that has tasted the vacuum and found it sated. She sits at the base of your throne, a golden consort whose devotion has passed the event-horizon into total self-obliteration.

But this morning, a ripple of genuine breakthrough tears through the Sovereign's Relay. Through the telepathic link, you find the Pilgrim on the world of Rakata Prime. He is no longer in the habitations or the mines. He is standing at the edge of the Infinite Sea, holding a dying Kel Dor researcher in his arms—not a child, but an old man whose lungs are failing in the salty air. You feel it: a resonance of pure, unedited sorrow. Vitiate is not siphoning the man's fear; he is crying. Not for himself, and not for the script. He is crying because he recognizes the specific frequency of a father's ending, and he has found the heart required to mourn it.
