Game Master:
[Mode: STORY MODE]
Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 10:08 AM.
Location: Deep Archives, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 1 (XP: 0/300), Gold: 10gp, HP: 8/8

You followed Magister Kantos deeper into the silent, ancient darkness of the Archives. The air grew heavier, colder, the faint scent of dust replaced by the metallic tang of old, contained magic. The polished marble gave way to rough-hewn stone, the passages narrowing until they felt like the throat of the earth itself. Kantos’s small lantern cast long, dancing shadows that seemed to swallow the light, forcing you to step carefully over uneven flagstones. The immense weight of the Spire, of millennia of secrets, pressed down from above.

Your mind, usually a fortress of calm analysis, was a whirlwind of raw, terrifying data. *She was unmoved.* The words echoed in your thoughts, stripping away the comforting illusion of your mother’s final sacrifice. The picture of agony, the one that had consumed you with guilt, was a meticulously crafted lie. She had not been broken; she had been a "Joyful Predator," thriving in the very act that claimed her life. The horror of this truth was immense, but so was the chilling, intellectual fascination.

You thought of her "flaw" as a Null, the perceived weakness that had earned her pity from the court. It was a mask. You thought of her quiet strength, her relentless discipline. It was a performance. And the "Joyful Predator"—that terrifying, unseen part of her soul—was the true engine of her power. A part she desperately tried to hide from you, her daughter. A path of blood and subtle, terrifying deceptions.

*Was that her love?* A bitter question formed in your mind. *To hide this darkness from me? To spare me this burden? Or to protect her lie, to keep me naive and controllable?* The familiar ache in your bandaged ear throbbed, a physical reminder of your own weakness, your failure to live up to the impossible legend. You clenched your jaw. You were no longer just seeking truth; you were seeking answers about yourself, about the terrible inheritance that might lie dormant in your own blood.

Kantos stopped before another heavy, unadorned iron door, even older and more heavily warded than the last. He mumbled a complex string of invocations, his voice barely audible over the low, ambient hum of the Spire’s deepest arcane workings. The ancient wards shimmered, then dissolved with a faint crackle of energy, and the heavy door slowly, reluctantly, ground open.

The air within the chamber was colder, drier, and so still it felt preserved. It was a smaller, more intimate vault, lined with shelves of black obsidian that seemed to absorb the light. Here, there were no grand scrolls or gilded tomes. Instead, the shelves held a chilling collection of bound journals and data-slates, many of them oddly mundane in appearance, yet radiating an undeniable aura of suppressed, dangerous power. These were the true heresies, the secrets too toxic for the public archives.

Kantos pointed to a specific shelf, his trembling finger indicating a set of three leather-bound journals, their covers plain and unadorned. "These, Princess," he rasped, his voice a dry whisper, "are the records of the 'Null Genesis Project.' Accounts of the first attempts by the Seraphim to create beings without a connection to the Weave. And," his gaze darted to your mother’s bloodline mark in your own Starlit Eyes, "and the failures, those like your mother, who defied their creators and found power in the void."

Your breath hitched. You walked towards the shelf as if in a trance, your fingers reaching for the journals. The covers were smooth, cool beneath your touch. This wasn't just a truth; it was your heritage, a hidden current flowing beneath the surface of your own lineage. The desperate hunger for knowledge, a hunger even more potent than your grief, consumed you. You pulled the first journal from the shelf, its weight surprisingly heavy in your hands. This was the raw, unedited chronicle of your mother's secret.
