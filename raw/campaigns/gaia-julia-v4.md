## Summary
A solo D&D 5e campaign set in 73 BCE alternate Rome. Gaia Julia Caesar, daughter of Dictator Perpetuo Julius Caesar, is a Level 12 Siren (Bard/Paladin Gestalt) disguised as "Livia of Capua," a captive noblegirl. Captured by Spartacus's rebel army at Villa del Sole, she uses pheromonal charm, Subtle Spell, and her fanatically loyal Lotus Ten to manipulate the rebellion from within. Core theme: the architecture of control through seduction, deception, and psychological warfare.

## Key Claims
- Gaia's Siren class has permanent Subtle Spell for all Enchantment/Illusion spells, making her magic undetectable
- The Miasma of Venus grants her gold eyes and a pheromonal presence that triggers protective instincts
- She carries 500 uncut diamonds sewn into her dress as hidden wealth; the Hairpin of Venus is a poisoned stiletto
- Her Lotus Ten cult (5 men, 5 women) includes Kael the assassin, Elara the alchemist, Nia the bard/spy, and others
- The Masquerade system (0-10 suspicion) tracks how close rebels are to discovering her true identity
- Spartacus protects her out of honor; Crixus wants her dead for being Roman
