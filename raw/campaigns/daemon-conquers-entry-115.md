Daemon (Level 6, Age 30) held the Duke of Riverbend aloft, the man's terrified whimpers a satisfying counterpoint to the distant shouts and pounding boots from deeper within the Keep. The conquest was not merely about taking a life; it was about seizing a domain, making it his own. The city was in chaos, the Keep alive with the sound of alarm. The question was not *if* he would take the Duke's place, but *how*. His mind, honed by battle and brutal necessity, discarded subtlety for direct power.

*His thoughts were a series of stark, practical considerations:*

"I have the Duke. He is the symbol. Breaking him breaks the city's will. The people here are fat, soft. They respect this Duke because he is 'fair'. They don't know true fear yet. It is time they learned."

**Option A: The Public Execution and Declaration**
*   **The Idea:** Drag the Duke to the highest point, the main square, or the city gates. Execute him publicly, and declare myself the new ruler. Demand immediate, absolute surrender from all who witness it, or face annihilation.
*   **Pros (Daemon's view):**
    *   **Decisive:** Ends all pretense of resistance quickly, establishes dominance through overwhelming fear.
    *   **Terrifying:** Will send a clear message to Riverbend and other nearby domains. People will submit to avoid the same fate.
    *   **Direct:** Uses my greatest strength – raw power and the spectacle of violence.
*   **Cons (Daemon's view):**
    *   **Risky if Resistance:** If the populace doesn't immediately break, it could spark a desperate, messy rebellion. My few warriors here are strong, but the city has many.
    *   **Exposure:** Bringing the Duke out means moving through the alerted city.
*   **Daemon's Confidence:** "High. The sight of their Duke's head on a pike will break them. I can feel their fear already." (Estimated Success Rate: 75-85% for initial submission, 60% for prolonged control without more forces)

**Option B: The Forced Proclamation and Imprisonment**
*   **The Idea:** Force the Duke to issue a public decree from the Keep's balcony, ordering all guards and citizens to lay down arms and accept me as their new lord. Then, imprison him, perhaps using his remaining 'influence' to ensure compliance while I consolidate.
*   **Pros (Daemon's view):**
    *   **Less Immediate Bloodshed:** Might save my warriors from fighting through an entire city.
    *   **Leverages Authority:** Uses the Duke's own standing to legitimize my claim, at least initially.
    *   **Control:** The Duke is a living hostage, a tool.
*   **Cons (Daemon's view):**
    *   **Weakness:** Relying on the Duke's words might be seen as hesitation. What if he tries to signal for help?
    *   **Unreliable:** The people might not obey a forced decree, or his loyalists might try to rescue him.
    *   **Slow:** Does not immediately establish my raw power in the same way.
*   **Daemon's Confidence:** "Moderate. It is less clean, more reliant on their weakness than my strength. But it might work." (Estimated Success Rate: 50-60% for initial submission, 40% for prolonged control)

**Option C: Secure the Keep and Call for Reinforcements**
*   **The Idea:** Ignore the city for a moment. Clear and hold the Keep completely. Send a runner to Kael and Borin, ordering them to gather all the Level 1 recruits and march on Riverbend, laying siege to the walls.
*   **Pros (Daemon's view):**
    *   **Overwhelming Force:** When the full army arrives, the city cannot possibly resist. Guarantees victory.
    *   **Safety:** My current force is small. Holding the Keep and waiting is the safest path.
    *   **Systematic:** Destroys all resistance with sheer numbers.
*   **Cons (Daemon's view):**
    *   **Slow:** Days, perhaps a week, for the army to arrive. The city could barricade, organize, or send for outside help.
    *   **Costly:** A full-scale siege will be bloody for the recruits. Time is money.
    *   **Public Knowledge:** Announces my full intentions to the entire region.
*   **Daemon's Confidence:** "High for ultimate victory, but low for speed. This is for when direct action is impossible. It is possible now." (Estimated Success Rate: 90% for ultimate victory, but with high time and resource cost)

"The Duke is a symbol," Daemon concluded, his grip tightening on the man. "Symbols are meant to be broken."
