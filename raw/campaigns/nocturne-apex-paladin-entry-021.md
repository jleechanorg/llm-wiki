Location: Baldur's Gate to Oakhaven Road, Early Evening

The ceaseless clamor of Baldur's Gate's Lower City slowly faded into the gentler sounds of the countryside as Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) walked, her concealing garb allowing her to blend seamlessly into the flow of travelers leaving the city gates. Her mind, an engine of cold, precise logic (Intelligence 18), had already dismissed the fleeting interaction with Kaelen. His nervousness was irrelevant; only the contract mattered. The thrill she felt wasn't from the act itself, but from the elegant design of its execution. Her low Agreeableness (1/5) ensured no moral qualms sullied her thoughts regarding the "easy pickings" in Oakhaven.

As twilight deepened, painting the sky in bruised purples and greys, Nocturne left the main road, veering onto a less-traveled path that wound towards Oakhaven. The small hamlet, marked by the distant, flickering lights of farmsteads, appeared as Kaelen had described: idyllic, unassuming. Perfect for an efficient, surgical extraction. Her senses, honed by her Wisdom (16) and battlefield experience, began to expand, taking in the rustle of leaves, the scent of damp earth, and the distant bleating of sheep.

She moved with a quiet grace surprising for someone in fine plate armor, even if concealed. Her "Anatomy & Weak Points" expertise tag, usually applied to combat, now repurposed itself to analyze the layout of the fields, the likely patrol routes, and the vulnerable points of the village infrastructure. Her Conscientiousness (4/5) demanded a thorough reconnaissance.

As she crept closer, navigating a thick copse of trees at the hamlet's edge, a low, guttural growl broke the rural silence. Not a wolf, but something larger, more deliberate. A pair of exceptionally large, well-fed Mastiffs, clearly trained and vigilant, emerged from the shadows near the main path leading into Oakhaven. Their presence was unexpected; Kaelen had implied minimal resistance. This was the complication. These were not mere farm dogs; their posture was alert, their snarls deep, indicating a degree of training and purpose beyond common livestock guardians. One turned its head, its keen nose testing the wind, directly towards her concealed position. They hadn't seen her, not yet, but they were searching.

Nocturne paused, her mind instantly recalibrating. The dogs represented an unforeseen, albeit minor, obstacle. A direct confrontation would create noise, defeating the purpose of a discreet infiltration. Her objective was to scout undetected. She needed to bypass or neutralize them silently. Her options narrowed, and a cold satisfaction settled over her; a challenge, however small, was always preferable to tedium.

She surveyed the immediate area, her gaze flicking between the dogs, the nearby barn, and the dense undergrowth. The wind was shifting slightly, carrying the scent of hay and, more dangerously, her own subtle presence towards the vigilant beasts.
