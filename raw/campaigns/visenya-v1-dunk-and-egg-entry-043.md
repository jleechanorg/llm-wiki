Midday (12:15:00) at the Roadside Inn near Ashford—the sun is at its zenith, baking the thatch of the inn and turning the common room into a sweltering box. Dunk stands by the door, squinting at the hauberk you’ve just laid across the bench. The orange bloom of rust has vanished from the shoulders, replaced by a dull, honest slate-grey. He runs a massive, calloused thumb over the links, his jaw dropping just a fraction. "I'll be a brother to a blind beggar," he mutters, the disbelief thick in his voice. "I've seen armorers in Flea Bottom work a whole day and not get the grit out like that. You've a strange way with iron, Egg. A lucky way."

He looks from the mail to you, his eyes lingering on your soot-stained hands. For a moment, the hedge knight looks genuinely impressed, as if he's realizing the scrawny lad he picked up might be worth more than a few coppers. Encouraged by his mood, you wipe a smear of grease from your forehead and gesture toward the heavy pack horses tethered outside. 

"Ser," you begin, your voice adopting that measured, logical cadence. "We're heading to a tourney, and Ashford will be crawling with merchants and smiths. You're carrying an extra set of cracked boiled leather and that rusted gorget in the second pack. They're just weight for Thunder to haul. If we sold them now, we'd have silver for proper oil for your mail, and maybe some grain that hasn't seen a weevil in this century."

Dunk’s expression shifts instantly, the warmth of his praise cooling into a stubborn, defensive frown. He looks at the bundle of gear you've mentioned—his master's old, battered spares. "Sell them?" he rumbles, his voice taking on a sharp, jagged edge. "That's Arlan's gear, lad. It's seen more battles than you've seen summers. A knight doesn't just hawk his history like a common peddler because the road gets a bit dusty. We've got our coppers, and we've got our pride. That'll have to be enough."

He turns away, his massive shoulders tensing as he begins to buckle the cleaned mail. The pragmatism of the Dragon Scholar has met the immovable wall of a hedge knight's sentimentality, and for now, the wall remains standing.
