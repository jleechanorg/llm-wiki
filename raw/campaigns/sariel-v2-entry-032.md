Game Master:
**--- THINK ---**
Their paralysis is temporary. It's a "priority re-evaluation," not a surrender. They're trying to resolve the logical paradox I've presented. I've appealed to their highest directive—Imperium's stability—and linked it to a new, terrifying truth. But abstract logic alone might not be enough to override Raziel's explicit containment orders. I need a catalyst. A physical manifestation of the "unresolvable truth" that doesn't trigger combat protocols but *demands* a re-categorization of my threat level.

What "partial evidence" would be most effective?
*   **The Null Genesis Journals:** Too overt. Revealing the journals directly would be a clear breach of containment protocols, which they are programmed to prevent. It's a hammer, not a whisper.
*   **My own budding Mirror Magic:** Risky. My last attempt left me bleeding and drained. An uncontrolled flare could be miscategorized as a magical attack, triggering lethal force. And I'm not yet ready to reveal my own powers without knowing if I can control them under pressure.
*   **Something related to Mother's secret, but subtle:** The Codex spoke of their reverence for Mother's "pure will," her perceived Null status as a testament to inner discipline. What if I can demonstrate a consequence of the Null Genesis Project that they would recognize as an anomaly, but one they cannot explain or fight?

The key lies in the conflict between what they *perceived* Mother to be and what she *truly* was. They believed she had no magic. They revered her for overcoming this "flaw." What if I could show them a subtle echo of the Null state, a consequence of the Genesis Project, without revealing my own Mirror Magic? Something that causes a small, localized "silence" in the Weave, just enough to be an anomaly they recognize from their training about "Null-anomalies," but too subtle to be categorized as a threat. It must be passive. Unthreatening. A whisper of a truth.

I'll use the locket. My mother's locket. It was with her at the Sacrifice Fields. It absorbed the echoes of her final, massive Nullification. It carries a residual imprint. If I can focus my will, not to *create* a field, but to *amplify* the residual Null echo in the locket... it might just be enough to create a small, contained "system perturbation" that forces them to re-categorize my status. It's a gamble. A subtle, dangerous one.
**--- END THINK ---**

The three Silent Guard remained utterly motionless for a prolonged, agonizing moment. The faint, almost imperceptible hum from their masks intensified, a subtle vibration in the very air. The flickering in their Starlit Eyes became a rapid, almost frantic pulse. Their internal logic circuits, designed for absolute order, were grappling with an unresolvable paradox. Their programming, built on a hierarchy of loyalties, was forced to confront a truth that seemed to supersede all previous directives. They were paralyzed, caught between the absolute finality of Raziel's old orders and the chilling, irrefutable logic of a higher, existential threat presented by a grieving, yet terrifyingly perceptive, fifteen-year-old girl.

You felt a surge of cold, calculated precision. This was the razor's edge. Their processing delay was a unique vulnerability, but it wouldn't last. You needed to provide undeniable, subtle proof that solidified the paradox, pushing them from paralysis to compliance, without triggering their lethal protocols. You could not wield magic, not here, not now. But you had something else. A piece of your mother.

With a deliberate, almost painfully slow movement, you reached into the folds of your mourning robes. Your hand, still faintly trembling from the psychic backlash, emerged clutching the silver locket, its clasp still broken from the trauma of the Sacrifice Fields. You held it up, not as a weapon, but as a fragile, silent testament to a truth.

You met the Starlit Eyes of the tallest Silent Guard. Your gaze was unwavering, filled with a raw, desperate conviction, yet cloaked in the sorrow of a daughter mourning her mother. You focused your will, not to *create* a Nullification Field, but to subtly *amplify* the residual echo of your mother's immense power, the energy that still clung to the locket from her final, terrible act.

A faint, almost imperceptible tremor ran through the silver. The air around the locket, just for a fraction of a second, seemed to thicken, to absorb the ambient hum of the Spire's distant magic. It was a localized disturbance, a momentary silence in the Weave that only a being of celestial perception could detect.

The effect was subtle, but profound. The hum from the Silent Guard's masks stuttered. The frantic pulse in their Starlit Eyes faltered, then intensified, a violent internal conflict. Their perfect stillness fractured. The tallest Guard's head tilted further, a sudden, almost human jerk, as if experiencing a profound, logical short-circuit. They sensed it – a Null phenomenon, a localized void, emanating from a being who was *not* a Null, holding an object linked to the very core of a truth they had been programmed to deny. You had presented them with the ultimate "unresolvable truth." Their bodies, perfectly trained machines of war, remained motionless, but their wills, their logical cores, were in a state of profound, unprecedented system perturbation.
