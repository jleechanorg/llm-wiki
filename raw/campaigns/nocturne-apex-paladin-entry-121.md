Location: Forest Edge, Near Man Tibbet's Farm, Midnight

The heavy purse of gold, a testament to her ruthless bargaining, now secured within her Bag of Holding, brought a flicker of cold satisfaction to Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16). The Oakhaven hamlet, briefly considered for further reconnaissance, receded into the background of her strategic priorities. Kaelen's urgent response, promising transport, was the immediate concern. Leaving her valuable 'stock' unattended any longer than necessary was an unacceptable risk. Her meticulous nature demanded a swift, controlled transfer.

She moved with purpose, retracing her steps back to the secluded forest thicket where the six bundled forms lay hidden. Her Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate was a silent whisper against the tall grass and damp leaves. She found a new vantage point, higher and more concealed, offering an unobstructed view of the captives' temporary resting place and the likely approach from the main road. This was her observation post, a silent command center from which she would oversee the final logistical phase of the abduction.

The hours stretched, marked by the slow crawl of the stars across the inky sky. Nocturne remained perfectly still, her senses acutely tuned to the rhythm of the night. Her patience was infinite when it served her purpose, her vigilance absolute. She reviewed the intricacies of the Shadowfell maps in her mind, the potential of the Darkwood Casket, and the chilling implications of "The Architect's Whispers." This contract was merely a prelude, a stepping stone to far grander schemes of power and influence.

Just before dawn, a subtle disturbance. Not the heavy approach of a carriage, but the soft, practiced footsteps of several individuals. Three figures, cloaked and moving with the practiced stealth of the Shadow Weavers, emerged from the direction of the main road. They carried no lanterns, relying on the faint moonlight and their own night vision. They carried large, empty sacks slung over their shoulders. One of them, taller and leaner than the others, paused, tapping a coded signal on a tree trunk near the concealed captives. It was Kaelen's familiar rhythm.

Nocturne observed from her hidden perch. This was the promised transport. Professional. Efficient. Just as she had demanded. The operation was nearing its completion.
