The green ward on the heavy iron door had flickered and died. The silent, unyielding figures of the Silent Guard had complied, their internal logic overriding their standing orders. The heavy door groaned inward, revealing a chamber plunged in absolute darkness, filled with the raw, unfiltered sounds of a broken mind. Whispers and low moans, laced with a profound, desolate despair, clawed at the stone, a chilling chorus of Kaelan’s madness.

You took a slow, deliberate breath, the lingering psychic ache in your head a dull counterpoint to the frantic hammering of your heart. Kaelan. The Starfall Patient. The living witness to your mother's terrifying truth. The key to unlocking a power that now thrummed, dormant and dangerous, within your own blood. This was the precipice.

You looked at the Silent Guard, who stood motionless, awaiting your next command. Their Starlit Eyes were fixed on the open doorway, their posture rigid. You chose your words with a cold, absolute precision. "Hold this perimeter," you commanded, your voice a soft, unwavering whisper that seemed to cut through the ethereal moans. "No one enters. No one leaves. Understood?" The tallest Guard gave a subtle, synchronized nod. Their compliance, born of logical paradox, remained absolute.

You turned to Kantos, whose ancient face was pale with terror. "Magister, remain with them. Maintain vigilance. We will speak again when I have the truth."

Without waiting for a reply, you stepped into the absolute darkness of the cell. The heavy iron door swung shut behind you with a deafening *CRUMP*, plunging you into a profound, suffocating blackness. The air was cold, stale, and thick with the overwhelming stench of despair and the metallic tang of dried blood. The whispers and moans, once distant, now surrounded you, a disorienting cacophony of madness. You were utterly alone. Your hand instinctively went to the broken locket at your belt, its cold metal a fragile anchor in the terrifying void.
