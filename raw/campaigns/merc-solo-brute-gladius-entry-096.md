The quiet, mundane rhythm of Greenmeadow faded behind Gladius as he strode away, the rising sun illuminating his departure. The contract was complete, a meticulous erasure of peasant lives that had served as a satisfying display of his absolute will. The lingering scent of fear, the memory of Corvan’s broken terror, and the exquisite unraveling of the Stonehand family’s defiance were potent sensations, a deep hum beneath his skin. This was the raw pleasure of dominance, the visceral proof of his power.

He moved deeper into Shadowfen Moor, away from any lingering eyes, until he found a secluded copse of gnarled, ancient trees whose roots snaked through the damp earth, offering a perfect, hidden bolthole. The air here was cool and still, damp earth and faint, sweet marsh gas replacing the stale scents of the village. He shed his greatsword, leaning it against a tree trunk, its heavy presence a silent sentinel. His splint mail, usually a rigid confinement, now felt like a second skin as he settled down, not for sleep, but for a deep, focused period of rest and mental calibration.

His body, though physically unburdened, sought a different kind of replenishment. He reveled in the stillness, the damp air on his face, the faint rustle of leaves – each a sensory input grounding him after the intense performance of the night. He stretched, feeling the satisfying give and pull of his powerful muscles, the subtle aches from hours of precise movement. This was his temple, his body the instrument of his will, and he would ensure it was perfectly attuned. His mind, unburdened by introspection, focused purely on the experience of recovery, the visceral sensation of strength returning, of energy (his EP now fully replenished) flowing through him. The grim satisfaction of the Greenmeadow contract resonated, a completed performance, but already his thoughts drifted. The thirst for new experiences, new targets for his domination, stirred within him. This insignificant village was done. There were other, richer pastures, other wills to break, other powerful individuals to bring low. The memory of Lord Silvanus's arrogance, now a tangible target, remained.

With his body and mind fully recalibrated, Gladius rose. He would not return to Baldur's Gate immediately. The immediate region, perhaps a neighboring village, might hold another opportunity, another unsuspecting arena for his brutal artistry. He craved the fresh thrill, the next stage of his ascent.
