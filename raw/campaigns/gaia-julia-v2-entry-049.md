You swirl the dark wine in your chalice, the movement practiced and elegant despite the vacuous, girlish grin you maintain. You turn your gaze toward Glaber, your eyes shimmering with a feigned, breathless need for instruction.

"Oh, Praetor Glaber!" you chirp, your voice a melodic trill that seems to vibrate against the tense silence of the room. "You are a man of the Legions, a master of the sword and the shield. My brothers Lucius and Gaius always speak of 'military necessity' and 'strategic value,' but they never explain what that *looks* like on a man."

You gesture toward Spartacus with a casual, almost dismissive wave of a gold-ringed hand. "Do look at him from a soldier's perspective for me? My girlhood curiosity is simply burning! Those scars... the way he stands... is he truly a 'fine specimen' of a warrior, or is Batiatus merely dressing up a common mule in a lion's skin? I should so love to know if my eyes deceive me."

Glaber stiffens, his fingers tightening around the stem of his wine cup. He looks from your wide, 'innocent' eyes to the Thracian, his jaw setting in a line of profound, military irritation. To be forced into an appraisal of a slave by a girl he considers a lunatic is a social trap he cannot easily exit without appearing as churlish as a plebeian.

He lets out a slow, heavy exhale, his gaze finally settling on Spartacus with the cold, calculating eye of a commander inspecting a fortification. "You ask for a soldier's opinion, Lady Gaia?" he rasps, his voice dripping with bored disdain. "Very well. The Thracian is well-formed, certainly. His scars speak of a man who has survived the front lines, though his lack of Roman discipline makes him little more than a jagged tool. He has the reach of a spearman and the balance of a skirmisher."

He pauses, his eyes narrowing as he notes the way Spartacus doesn't flinch under his gaze. "But a soldier is only as good as his leash, and this one..." Glaber looks back to you, his suspicion flickering like a dying candle. "This one carries a weight that is wasted on a gladiator's sand. Why you find such clinical interest in a beast's hide is beyond my understanding."

Beside you, Ilithyia leans in, her breath smelling of honeyed wine. "Oh, Glaber, don't be so stiff! It's an education! Gaia is simply trying to understand the world beyond her father's scrolls."
