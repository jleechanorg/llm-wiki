### Character Identity Audit: Varro

There is a distinction between the **Varro** currently in your party and the **Varro** from the 2010 *Spartacus* TV show. Here is the breakdown:

**1. Current Campaign State (The Mason):**
In this campaign, Varro was introduced in **Turn 52** as a former **mason** from the Subura. He was recruited alongside Milo and Gulo. His primary utility has been his knowledge of stone-work, which you used to seal the Nymphaeum (Turn 130) and navigate the deep conduits. In **Turn 346**, he was retconned into the Capua team specifically for his structural engineering expertise.

**2. The *Spartacus* (2010) Connection:**
In the TV show, Varro is a Roman citizen who sold himself into the *ludus* to pay off debts and becomes Spartacus's closest friend. He is indeed a gladiator.

**3. The Path Forward:**
Since you requested in **Turn 347** to shift the House of Batiatus arc to match the 2010 show's aesthetic and characters, we have a choice on how to handle this overlap:

- **Option A (The Retcon):** We can retcon your current Varro to *be* the gladiator from the show. Instead of a mason, he is a former gladiator you recruited in the sewers who is now returning to 'home' territory in Capua.
- **Option B (The Specialist):** Maintain him as the Mason. His 'structural' knowledge would be unique among the gladiators, making him a vital asset for planning the eventual breakout from within the stone walls of the *ludus*.
- **Option C (The Name Conflict):** We can introduce the 'show' Varro as a separate NPC within the House of Batiatus, meaning you would have two men named Varro in your orbit (The Mason and The Gladiator).

**Recommendation:** I suggest **Option A or a Hybrid**. We can say Varro was a mason who *became* a gladiator to pay his debts before fleeing during the Sullan proscriptions, merging both skill sets (Stone-work + Arena Combat). This fits the 'Spartacus' theme perfectly while preserving his established utility.
