The Miller farmhouse, now a silent, empty shell, was behind Gladius. The faint, distant glow of the shed fire was a dying ember on the horizon, its purpose served. He felt a deep, cold satisfaction; a clean kill, an efficient erasure. Corvan, the sniveling envoy, would soon be wallowing in the grim aftermath, a tangible extension of Gladius's will. The thought, a subtle hum beneath his skin, was more intoxicating than any drink, a pure, distilled essence of dominance.

He moved through the pre-dawn gloom of Greenmeadow, his Shadow-Step Boots whispering against the damp earth. The early morning mist clung to his splint mail, muting its usual clang to a barely audible rustle, a testament to the tools he now wielded. His greatsword, a cold weight on his back, hummed with a quiet anticipation. The village still slept, oblivious, its few scattered lights like pinpricks of vulnerability against the encroaching dawn.

The path to the Blackwood cottages was less direct, winding through a cluster of smaller, older dwellings near the village's western edge. These homes looked even more decrepit than the Millers', their windows dark, their chimneys cold. *More of the same,* Gladius mused, a faint sneer touching his lips. *More weakness to expose.* His mind, though simple in its directness, thrived on the sensory experience of the hunt: the damp chill on his face, the faint scent of stale wood smoke, the profound silence of sleeping lives waiting to be extinguished. It was a tangible, immediate pleasure, the reality of his power made manifest in every silent step. The Blackwoods, fiercely independent, as the lackey had called them, would learn that independence was a fleeting illusion against true force.
