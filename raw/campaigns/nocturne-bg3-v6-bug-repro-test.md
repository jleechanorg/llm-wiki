## Summary

A D&D 5e campaign (Survival Horror / Gothic Noir / Infernal Politics) set in the Baldur's Gate 3 universe. Protagonist Nocturne Sosuke ("The Black Diamond") is a Level 5 Half-Elf Bard (College of the Abyssal Siren), trapped in the goblin camp as a "True Soul" under the Absolute's influence. The campaign explores her infiltration of the Emerald Grove from within the goblin camp, her manipulation of Minthara and companion assets, and the subversion of the Rite of Thorns. Key mechanics include Siren's Gaze (Social HP), Gilded Tether (Thralls), and Pheromone Spike (Bonus Action Command).

## Key Claims

- Nocturne is a de-powered Level 6 entity contained in a Level 5 mortal cage by the tadpole; every level-up represents a "leak" in containment
- The campaign frames BG3 companion mechanics as "Assets" with Approval tracking rather than equal party members
- Nocturne's "Uncanny Valley" visual progression (Ruin → Porcelain Doll → Predator → Sovereign → Ascended) maps to level ranges 0-20
- The Emerald Grove infiltration involves convincing Minthara the subversion will win a "decisive victory" before betraying both druids and goblins
- The Rite of Thorns is targeted for inversion/siphoning to fuel Nocturne's own ascension rather than simple destruction

## Scenes 1-20 Summary

Scenes 1-3: Character verification in Minthara's War Room. Level confirmed as 5 (not 6 despite the "nerf" narrative). Player establishes preference for direct adventure start.

Scenes 4-7: Nocturne asserts dominance over Minthara in the war room. Social confrontation with Minthara's 30 Social HP tracked. Nocturne convinces Minthara to let her infiltrate the Emerald Grove as a "porcelain victim" who will subvert from within. Minthara agrees to the plan, warning that the Absolute sees all.

Scenes 8-11: Nocturne plans the inversion of the Rite of Thorns. She pulls Lae'zel, Astarion, and Shadowheart into an alcove and reveals her plan to betray both the druids and the goblins after siphoning the ritual's power. Shadowheart expresses skepticism; Lae'zel agrees if the "Siren's song" can turn the magic into a weapon; Astarion endorses it enthusiastically.

Scenes 12-14: Travel to the Emerald Grove. Nocturne probes Gale (swayed by utilitarian logic), Wyll (resisting, chivalrous), and Karlach (soldier accepting pragmatism). She bypasses a goblin ambush through intimidation (+150 XP). Dawn arrival at the Grove gates. Zevlor lowers the gate for the "porcelain victim."

Scenes 15-17: Inside the Grove. Zevlor shares intel: gate mechanism is old and prone to jamming; Shadow Druids have taken over the inner sanctum; the Sacred Pool is where Kagha and the Shadow Druids gather. Astarion inspects the gate mechanism and identifies the weak point. Gale and Wyll are sent back to camp.

Scenes 18-20: Only Shadowheart, Lae'zel, and Astarion remain. Zevlor reveals the Shadow Druids' existence (Kagha radicalized but not yet "damned"). Nocturne descends to the Sacred Pool with her "evil" companions. Two druid guards block the entrance but yield to the "porcelain victim" act. Entry to the inner sanctum achieved.
