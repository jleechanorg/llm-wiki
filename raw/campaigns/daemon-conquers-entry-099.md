Kael (Level 5, Age 25) stood before Daemon (Level 6, Age 30), the unspoken command for a plan heavy in the air. He knew Daemon valued brutal efficiency over clever words, but Riverbend was no Stonehaven.

"My Lord," Kael began, his voice steadying. "The scouts report Riverbend has thick walls and many guards, disciplined ones at that. A direct, open assault would be a slaughter of our new recruits, and even our veterans would bleed needlessly."

Daemon grunted, a low, impatient sound.

Kael continued, emboldened by the lack of immediate censure. "I see a few paths, my Lord, none without risk, but all better than simply throwing our men at their walls."

*   **Option 1: The Grinding Siege (Starvation and Discord)**
    *   **Kael's Proposal:** "We surround the city, choke its trade. Its people are used to comfort. Deny them food, deny them coin, and their 'loyalty' to this Duke will turn to desperate cries. We can use our new recruits to cut off supply lines, while our veterans ensure no one escapes or breaks ranks. The populace will turn on itself, or force the Duke's hand."
    *   **Pros (as Daemon would see them):** Saves our best warriors from fighting walls. Breaks the enemy from within. Fewer direct casualties for our force.
    *   **Cons (as Daemon would see them):** Slow. Requires patience. City might have hidden supplies. Other nobles might interfere if we linger.
    *   **Kael's Estimated Success (Obedience/Capitulation):** "Moderate to High, my Lord. Perhaps 60-70% likelihood of them breaking within a month or two, provided no outside intervention."

*   **Option 2: The Serpent in the Walls (Infiltration and Uprising)**
    *   **Kael's Proposal:** "Riverbend has a bustling populace. It would be possible to send agents, perhaps disguised recruits, to spread rumors, incite dissent, exaggerate our power, and weaken the Duke's hold from within. We could open a gate, or cause panic, then strike."
    *   **Pros (as Daemon would see them):** Subverts defenses without direct assault. Could be quick if successful.
    *   **Cons (as Daemon would see them):** Requires subtlety. Relies on the weak will of others. Risks agents being caught and revealing our intentions.
    *   **Kael's Estimated Success (Opening a Path/Weakening Defenses):** "Low to Moderate, my Lord. Perhaps 30-40% likelihood. It requires precise timing and fragile cooperation."

*   **Option 3: The Shadowed Approach (Covert Entry and Surprise)**
    *   **Kael's Proposal:** "Our scouts noted no weaknesses in the main gates, but a city that large, built around a river... there might be overlooked sewers, old forgotten tunnels, or even a smaller, less guarded access point along the river itself. We find a back door and send a small, lethal force to seize a key location, perhaps the Duke's chambers, before anyone knows we are there."
    *   **Pros (as Daemon would see them):** Decisive. Avoids large-scale battle. Could be swift.
    *   **Cons (as Daemon would see them):** High risk for the small force involved. Requires more detailed intelligence gathering. Could become a desperate fight in tight quarters.
    *   **Kael's Estimated Success (Breaching and Decapitating):** "Moderate, my Lord. Perhaps 50% likelihood, if such a hidden path exists and we can exploit it without immediate detection."

Kael finished, waiting for Daemon's judgment, the silence in the hall heavy with anticipation.
