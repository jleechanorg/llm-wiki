You listened to Cressida’s assessment of the Dream-Weaver cactus distillate – a task that would be costly and draw attention. Then, you heard Kantos’s grim prioritization: the mental anchor was faster, more immediate, and less risky in your current fragile state. Rowan’s warning about the distillate's volatility and its potential for "total unmaking" resonated with the raw memory of your own psychic backlash. Speed was paramount. And Gideon’s unwavering will, his absolute loyalty, offered a stability that no alchemical concoction could match.

Your mind, cold and precise, made the final calculation. The mental anchor was the optimal first step, the fastest path to accessing Kaelan’s memories without risking further self-destruction. The distillate could wait.

"Magister Kantos," you commanded, your voice steady and firm, cutting through the low hum of preparation and Kaelan's moans. Your gaze fixed on the ancient scholar, then swept to Gideon, who stood sentinel, a silent, unyielding bulwark. "The time for debate is over. We proceed with the 'shared mental anchor.' Immediately."

Kantos, though pale, nodded slowly, his ink-stained hands trembling slightly as he turned to his arcane implements. His fear remained, but his scholarly instincts, now coupled with an unquestioning obedience to your chilling authority, drove him forward. He began to arrange the crystalline tuning forks and intricately carved bone pieces on the dusty floor, his murmurs a low, rhythmic chant.

"Ser Gideon," you continued, turning directly to your sworn shield. His gaze, usually so impassive, held a deep, profound respect, a silent acknowledgment of your tactical decision. "Your role is paramount. You must project your will, your unwavering loyalty, your absolute discipline, as a 'lode-star' for my mind. Be my anchor, Ser Gideon. Guard my thoughts as you would my life."

Gideon, without a word, simply nodded, his helmeted head dipping in a solemn vow. He moved to the spot Kantos indicated, settling into a deep, meditative stance, his massive body radiating an almost palpable aura of unshakeable resolve. The air in the cell, thick with despair, now vibrated faintly with the nascent energies of Kantos’s preparations and the focused will of Gideon. The final confrontation with the truth was about to begin.

You took a deep, shuddering breath, the cool air filling your lungs as you knelt before the intricate ritual circle Kantos had swiftly laid out. Your eyes, fixed on Sergeant Kaelan's tormented face, burned with a desperate need for understanding. Kantos’s low, rhythmic chanting filled the small chamber, weaving ancient words of attunement and protection. He gently handed you a small, polished obsidian disc, cool and smooth against your palm.

"Princess," Kantos rasped, his voice strained but firm, "place this on Sergeant Kaelan's forehead. It will create the focal point. And then... reach for Ser Gideon."

You nodded, your hand trembling slightly as you moved to place the disc on Kaelan's brow. His skin was cold, clammy, and his eyes remained wide, unblinking, lost in their private hell. The moment your skin brushed his, the whispers in the cell intensified, threatening to overwhelm you.

"Ser Gideon!" Kantos's voice rose, a sharp, urgent command.

Gideon, already deep in his meditative stance, closed his eyes. His body, even in stillness, radiated an almost palpable aura of unshakeable will, a disciplined, unwavering resolve that seemed to push back against the chaotic psychic maelstrom of Kaelan's mind. You felt it instantly—a steady, unyielding pressure, a solid rock in the swirling currents of your own exhausted psyche. It was Gideon's mind, a fortress of loyalty, becoming your anchor.

You closed your eyes, pushing the fragile trickle of your nascent Mirror Magic outwards, not to create, but to *reflect*. You reached for the raw trauma in Kaelan's mind, guided by Gideon’s steady presence, seeking the terrifying image of your mother in that final, impossible battle.
