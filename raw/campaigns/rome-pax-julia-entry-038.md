The Great Junction groans, a symphony of shifting bronze and draining sludge. As the last of the oil vanishes into the lower deeps, you don't wait for the **Augurs** (Sulla's state-sanctioned seers) to narrow their psychic search. You signal to Cinnaminus, who is already prying at a jagged, calcified seam in the western masonry—the entrance to the **Rat-Run**.

"Move!" you command, ushering Decimus and his shell-shocked veterans toward the narrow gap. The Rat-Run is not a sewer; it is a claustrophobic maintenance vein, a **cuniculus** (underground gallery) used by the original Republic-era builders. It is so narrow that Voros must turn sideways to pass, his massive shoulders scraping the weeping stone, but it is invisible to the Red Cloak patrols above.

Varro leads from the front, his mason's instincts guiding you through the 'dead' zones of the masonry where the stone is thickest, shielding your heat-signature from the Augurs' cold pressure. Cinnaminus lingers at the rear, using a handful of silt and debris to perfectly mask the seam behind you. You move in a rhythmic, silent shuffle, the only sound the distant, muffled *thump* of fire-pots exploding in the Junction you just vacated. The air here is ancient and thin, but it is free of the acrid reek of pitch. You have bypassed the funeral pyre Sulla intended for you, slipping through the city's very marrow like a shadow.

**Roman Terminology Note:**
- **Cuniculus**: A narrow, hand-dug underground channel or gallery, often used for drainage or secret movement.
- **Augurs**: Official diviners in Rome who interpreted the will of the gods (or in this world, the Miasma) to guide state policy.
- **Cataracta**: The heavy sluice-gate you successfully triggered, a marvel of Roman hydraulic engineering.
