The Undefeated Gaul reaches out, his hand clasping your shoulder with a grip that would have crushed a lesser man’s bones. 

"The mountain took three hundred of my brothers in the pits," Crixus rumbles, his voice thick with a raw, uncharacteristic gratitude. "I have never seen one man walk into that many swords and walk back out. You are no noble, Aurelius. You are a titan. Naevia... she breathes because you chose to stay in the dark."

Naevia herself rises weakly from her pallet, her eyes wide as she looks at you. She doesn't speak, but she bows her head low, a gesture of profound thanks that carries more weight than any Roman title. 

Spartacus watches the exchange, his dual blades crossed over his back. He sees the way the gladiators look at you—Agron, Nasir, and the others whispering of the 'Solar Ghost' who broke a century and vanished. While the Thracian leader's mind remains a fortress of tactical caution, unable to fully fathom why a son of Rome would court death for a Gaul's heart, even he cannot deny the legend you have become this night. 

"You did what no man should have survived," Spartacus states, his voice carrying through the gallery. "For anyone else, that was a last stand. For you... it seems it was merely a Tuesday in the service of justice. The men will speak of the Sun on the Spires for years to come."

Aurelia stands at your side, her face radiant with a devotion that has crossed the threshold into worship. "They don't understand," she whispers so only you can hear. "They see a warrior. I see the heart that makes the steel possible."
