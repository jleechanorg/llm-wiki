You leave the frozen statue of the True Soul Leader under Vesper's watchful blade. "If he so much as blinks," you tell her, "carve him."

She spins a dagger, grinning in the rain. "He won't blink, Boss. You turned him into furniture."

Signaling **Silas** to maintain his overwatch from the ridge, you descend into the belly of the ship, flanking yourself with **Gant**'s bulk and **Nyx**'s arcane sight. The air below decks is thick, smelling less like bilge water and more like ozone and stale copper—the scent of high-grade magic preservation.

The hold doesn't look like a cargo bay; it looks like a vault. The entire forward section is sealed off by a bulkhead of dark, riveted iron, reinforced with bands of cold steel that hum with a low, dissonant vibration. There is no keyhole, only a speaking tube shaped like a listening ear, sculpted from obsidian.

You step forward, the **Manifest of Souls** burning a hole in your pocket. You speak the words extracted from the puppet upstairs.

"Protocol. Omega. Nine. Black."

The ship groans. The obsidian ear glows violet, and the heavy locking mechanism disengages with a sound like grinding bone. The bulkhead swings inward.

Inside, the darkness is lit by hundreds of faint, pulsing purple lights. The "Lead Wagon" wasn't a wagon at all; it was this entire reinforced chamber. Rows upon rows of tall, glass cylinders line the walls, floor to ceiling, each filled with a viscous, suspended shadow-fluid.

And inside each one, floating in suspended animation, are the tadpoles.

Thousands of them.

They aren't swimming; they are frozen in mid-writhe, their tiny, lamprey-like mouths open, their tentacles curled. The sheer psionic potential in the room makes the **Ring of Mind Shielding** on your finger heat up, working overtime to dampen the noise.

**Gant** recoils, his hand going to the hilt of his weapon. "Hells above... it looks like a hatchery. How many of these things are there?"

**Nyx** moves past him, her eyes wide, glowing with the reflection of the purple light. She taps a claw against the nearest glass cylinder. "It's not just a hatchery, Gant. Look at the runes etched into the glass."

"Netherese stasis geometry," she whispers, tracing a sigil. "Old magic. Shadow-weave adjacent. This isn't just keeping them alive; it's *pausing* their development. They're locked in the parasitic stage indefinitely until the field is dropped. If you put one of these in a brain now, it wouldn't ceremorphose immediately. It would just... wait. Listening."

You walk down the aisle of cylinders, your boots echoing on the metal grate. You count the rows. The density. The Manifest wasn't exaggerating. There is enough biological ammunition here to enslave the entire Parliament of Peers three times over.

"Omega-9-Black," you muse. "The code wasn't just a lock. It's the activation key. If we drop this field..."

"They wake up," Nyx finishes, looking at you with a mix of fear and admiration. "Hungry."

You place your hand on the glass. A single tadpole drifts inches from your palm, a sleeping conqueror. You don't feel disgust. You feel the weight of the city in your hand.

"Secure the door," you order. "We're not destroying this. We're taking it home."
