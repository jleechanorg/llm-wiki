**1. Are all the jōnin dead?**
No, not all Uchiha jōnin are dead. Itachi has currently eliminated three specific high-ranking jōnin: Captain Ryota, Jōnin Kaito, and Jōnin Ren. These were the most immediate, active threats he encountered in the police headquarters and its vicinity during his silent infiltration. The Uchiha clan, as a major shinobi clan, would have many more jōnin, but they might be scattered throughout the compound in their homes, on other duties outside the compound, or even on covert missions for the clan's planned coup. Itachi's actions so far have been surgical, not a full-scale public battle against all available forces.

**2. Why are there so few Uchiha jōnin?**
You've encountered a concentrated, but not exhaustive, force. The apparent "fewness" is a result of several factors:
*   **Time of Night:** It's late, pre-dawn. Most shinobi, even jōnin, would be at home resting, not actively patrolling the entire compound in full force. The Police Force Headquarters would have a skeleton crew for a night watch.
*   **Itachi's Efficiency:** Itachi's highly specialized stealth and assassination skills (now further enhanced by Level 3 benefits like Super-Advantage Stealth) allow him to bypass or quickly eliminate targets without raising a general alarm. He's been extremely effective at isolating and dispatching individuals or small groups before they can consolidate.
*   **Clan's Status:** The Uchiha clan was a powerful, but insular, force. While they had many skilled shinobi, their numbers were not infinite, and their internal focus (especially on the coup) might have meant fewer active, outward-facing duties for some. The most elite are likely within the Main House, or out of the compound entirely.

So, while Itachi has dealt with the immediate, high-level opposition in his path, many other Uchiha shinobi (including jōnin) are still alive, largely unaware of the extent of the massacre, or simply not yet encountered by Itachi. The climax, with the strongest, will likely be in the Main House.
