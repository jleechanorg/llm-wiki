Game Master:
[Mode: STORY MODE]
Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 10:05 AM.
Location: The Chamber of Whispers, Great Archives, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 1 (XP: 0/300), Gold: 10gp, HP: 8/8

Kantos’s voice, thick with a profound, sorrowful conviction, had just painted a terrifying portrait of your mother: a hidden prodigy, a masterful deceiver, and a "Joyful Predator" who embraced a dark truth to win. The weight of his words settled over you, chilling you to the bone. Your mother, the legend, the hero, was a far more complex and frightening being than you had ever imagined. The image of her suffering at the Sacrifice Fields, the one that had wracked you with guilt, now felt like the ultimate, cruellest trick.

The horror was immense, but it was quickly eclipsed by a surge of pure, intellectual demand. "Joyful Predator," you repeated, the phrase tasting foreign and dangerous on your tongue. "You speak of a secret magic, a hidden nature. This is not the stuff of legends, Magister, it is the stuff of knowledge. If this truth exists, it must be recorded. Show me the texts. Show me the records that explain this power, this 'Joyful Predator,' and how she wielded the Nullification Field without the cost." Your voice, though quiet, held the unyielding force of an Imperial command, tempered by the desperate hunger of a scholar on the verge of a world-shattering discovery.

Kantos stared, his ancient face a mask of profound distress. He wrung his hands, his gaze darting to your Starlit Eyes, then to the unlit corners of the Chamber of Whispers. "Princess, these are not... public records," he stammered, his voice thin and strained. "Such knowledge is buried for a reason. Its very existence is a heresy against the established order of magic. Raziel himself… he would have had them purged centuries ago, were it not for my… careful preservation." He swallowed hard, his Adam's apple bobbing. "To access such texts is to walk a path of ultimate danger, far more treacherous than any battlefield."

His fear was palpable, a cold wave in the oppressive silence. But your mind, now a relentless engine of inquiry, paid it no heed. "I do not care about the danger, Magister," you stated, your voice flat, devoid of emotion. "My mother lived a lie. She died a lie. And I am left with the burden of that legacy. I will know the truth. Guide me, or I will find it myself. And if I find it without your aid, I will ensure every soul in this Spire knows what you tried to hide."

Kantos visibly flinched. He closed his eyes, a deep, shuddering sigh escaping him, and when they opened, a profound resignation had settled over his features. "Very well, Princess," he rasped, his voice barely audible. "Follow me. But know that what you seek… once it is seen, it cannot be unseen." He turned, his small lantern casting long, dancing shadows as he moved deeper into the silent, ancient darkness of the Archives.

You followed, your footsteps a soft echo behind his shuffling gait. The air grew heavier, colder, the scent of dust replaced by the faint, metallic tang of ancient, contained magic. The polished marble gave way to rough-hewn stone, the passages narrowing until they felt like the throat of the earth itself. Kantos navigated a labyrinth of forgotten corridors, each turn leading deeper into the Spire's foundations, away from the orderly world above. You could feel the immense weight of the rock above you, the thousands of years of secrets pressing down.

Your mind hummed with a grim anticipation. Your mother, the celebrated Null, had not only wielded magic, but had concealed it with a deception so vast it defied belief. And now, you were about to uncover the very texts that spoke of this forbidden truth, texts that Raziel himself wished purged. Your stomach twisted with a mixture of fear and a chilling, intellectual hunger. This was not just history; this was the secret heart of your lineage, a truth that had forged your mother into a weapon, and now threatened to reshape your own understanding of reality.
