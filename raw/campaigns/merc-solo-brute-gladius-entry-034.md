The Silent Strangler's Cord felt almost warm in Gladius's hand, a living extension of his will. He stood before the door with the lighter, quicker breaths, the chamber where the Miller children lay oblivious. The thought of their helplessness, their inherent weakness, brought a familiar, cold surge of disdain mixed with a grim satisfaction. This was not about malice; it was about efficiency, about demonstrating absolute control. The distant, faint glow of the burning shed outside served as a reminder of the carefully orchestrated chaos, the distraction that sealed their fate.

He reached for the wooden door, his large hand surprisingly gentle. No creak, no tell-tale groan. The Shadow-Step Boots ensured his weight was distributed, his armor silent. The door swung inward on well-oiled hinges, revealing a small, cramped room. Moonlight, a pale sliver through a high, grimy window, illuminated two small, lumpish forms under thin blankets on straw pallets. The air here was lighter, perhaps a faint scent of milk and childish dreams, but it was quickly being subsumed by the grim reality Gladius carried with him.

One child, clearly younger, stirred, a soft whimper escaping their lips, but did not wake. The other lay still. Gladius's gaze, sharp and unblinking, took in the innocent faces, the fragile vulnerability. He felt no pity, only a cold, calculating focus. This was the raw material of his power, the proof that no one, not even the most innocent, was truly safe from the reach of those who dared to wield absolute will. His grim satisfaction deepened; this would be quick, clean, and utterly silent. A testament to his mastery.
