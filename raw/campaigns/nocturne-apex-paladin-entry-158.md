Location: House Valerius Estate, Lord Cassian's Private Study, Morning

The study air, usually a sanctuary of quiet authority, now thrummed with a palpable tension. Lord Cassian Valerius (Age 55) remained motionless behind his desk, his fingers a fraction tighter on the polished wood. His gaze, sharp and analytical, was a mirror of Nocturne's (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) own, betraying nothing but intense mental effort. He processed her words – "Shadowfell," "The Architect," "Convergence in three nights" – rapidly re-evaluating the geopolitical landscape of Baldur's Gate, the complex web of allegiances and rivalries he had so meticulously woven. He sought to dissect her intentions, to ascertain the depth and veracity of her audacious claim.

Nocturne allowed the silence to stretch, a strategic weapon in itself. She met his unwavering gaze, her own features an unreadable mask. She offered no further words, no additional details, no hint of emotion. She was not begging for his attention; she was demanding his engagement. He broke first, his voice still low, but now laced with a chilling undertone of steel. "You speak of Shadowfell incursions and Architects. Unprecedented. If this is true, the implications are... vast." He paused, his gaze boring into hers. "You returned with a fragment. Where is the rest? What evidence do you possess, beyond your word, that substantiates such a claim?"

Nocturne did not flinch, nor did she immediately produce the physical evidence. Her gaze, cold and unwavering, intensified. "My word, Father, is proven by the failure of your existing network to detect this. My methods are effective. The intelligence is irrefutable. And the 'Convergence' is in three nights. Time is a luxury we do not possess."

She took a deliberate step closer to the desk, her presence filling the already opulent space. "I require a position of direct authority in addressing this threat, independent of Julian's command. And I require immediate access to House Valerius's resources: agents, funds, arcane specialists – whatever is necessary to counter Durin's Folly's designs in the Underdark. This is not a request for forgiveness, Father. It is a strategic necessity. I possess the insight and the ruthlessness required to win this game. You would be foolish to deny me the tools." Her tone was absolute, unyielding, a chilling reflection of his own calculating nature. She offered him a clear path to managing the crisis, but on her terms.

Lord Cassian's expression remained unreadable, but a muscle ticked in his jaw. His eyes, fixed on her, seemed to hold a flicker of both a grudging admiration and a profound, dangerous assessment of her sheer audacity. He understood the demand; he understood the stakes.
