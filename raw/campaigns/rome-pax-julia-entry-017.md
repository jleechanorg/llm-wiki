You watch his back, trying to read the tension in his frame, but the Miasma chooses that moment to pulse—a sickening thrum of gold and grey in your mind's eye. You see the river gates, not as they are, but as a jagged line of iron teeth closing shut over a drowning eagle. Cinnaminus is telling the truth, or at least his version of it, but his fear is a physical thing that even the damp cold of the sewers cannot mask.
