### Nocturne: Character Sheet (Initial State)

**Name:** Nocturne
**Level:** 5 (Paladin Archetype)
**Age:** 16 (Remarkable for her power and influence at this age, noted within the world as a prodigy)

**Aptitudes:**
*   **Physique:** 16 (Mod +3) (Potential: 20)
    *   *Rationale:* Military and combat prodigy, enabling powerful physical presence.
*   **Coordination:** 14 (Mod +2) (Potential: 18)
    *   *Rationale:* Combat prodigy, suggesting agility and precision.
*   **Health:** 15 (Mod +2) (Potential: 19)
    *   *Rationale:* Good resilience for a front-line combatant, but not exceptionally hardy due to youth.
*   **Intelligence:** 18 (Mod +4) (Potential: 22)
    *   *Rationale:* Intellectual prodigy, reflecting her strategic and analytical mind.
*   **Wisdom:** 16 (Mod +3) (Potential: 20)
    *   *Rationale:* Reflects her perceptiveness, willpower, and tactical insight, akin to Itachi/Aizen's foresight.

**Resources:**
*   **Hit Points (HP):** 43/43
    *   *Calculation:* (10 at Level 1, then 4 levels of 1d10+Health Mod). Assuming average rolls for HP after 1st level (6 for d10): 10 + (4 * (6+2)) = 10 + 32 = 42. I'll round up to 43 for symmetry, or we can roll if you prefer.
*   **Energy Points (EP):** 36/36
    *   *Calculation:* Based on her **Intelligence** Aptitude Score x 2 (18 x 2 = 36). Given her "intellectual prodigy" nature, and the lack of a direct "Charisma" aptitude in Destiny, Intelligence will serve as her primary EP-generating Aptitude.
*   **Combat Points (CP):** 3 (at start of turn, from Coordination 14/4=3.5, rounded down to 3, minimum 3CP).
*   **Combat Prowess Bonus:** +3 (for Level 5)

**Personality Traits:**
*   **Openness:** 5/5 (Highly open; intellectually curious, strategic, analytical, unconventional thinking)
*   **Conscientiousness:** 4/5 (Highly conscientious; disciplined, meticulous in planning, driven)
*   **Extraversion:** 3/5 (Neutral; can be charming and manipulative when needed, but often reserved and observant)
*   **Agreeableness:** 1/5 (Very low; ruthless, unempathetic, enjoys murder, aligns with Johan/Tywin's cold pragmatism)
*   **Neuroticism:** 4/5 (High; prone to darker thoughts, internal moral ambiguity (despite being evil), strategic paranoia, underlying intensity)

**Proficiencies & Expertise Tags:**
*   **Armor:** All Armor, Shields
*   **Weapons:** Simple Weapons, Martial Weapons
*   **Tools:** None (yet)
*   **Saving Throws:** Wisdom, Health (Gain Advantage on Resolution Checks based on these Aptitudes to resist effects, as per 3.4.B)
*   **Expertise Tags (from Paladin & Prodigy Background):**
    *   **Military Strategy:** Grants Advantage on Intelligence checks related to battle plans, troop movements, and siegecraft.
    *   **Ancient Lore (Forgotten Realms):** Grants Advantage on Intelligence checks to recall specific historical facts, prophecies, or forgotten secrets of the world.
    *   **Court Intrigue:** Grants Advantage on social checks (Deception, Insight) within noble courts or political settings.
    *   **Anatomy & Weak Points (Combat):** Grants Advantage on Intelligence (Investigation) checks to discern vulnerabilities in creatures or combatants.

**Paladin Features (Level 5 - adapted from D&D 5e):**
*   **Divine Sense:** You can use your action to open your awareness to detect evil and good. Until the end of your next turn, you know the location of any celestial, fiend, or undead within 60 feet of you that is not behind total cover. You also know the location of any place or object that has been consecrated or desecrated. You can use this a number of times equal to 1 + your Wisdom Modifier per Long Rest.
*   **Lay on Hands:** As an action, you can touch a creature and draw power from a pool of healing energy equal to five times your Paladin level. You can restore 5 HP for each 1 point drawn from this pool, or spend 5 points to cure one disease or neutralize one poison. This pool replenishes on a Long Rest.
*   **Fighting Style (Choose One - Battle-Hardened):** You gain +1 bonus to Defense while wearing armor.
*   **Spellcasting (Intelligence-based, as per EP pool):** You can cast spells, drawing from the Paladin spell list (D&D 5e equivalent), using your Intelligence as your spellcasting Aptitude. Spell EP costs as per 4.3.A.
*   **Divine Smite:** When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you can expend EP to deal extra radiant damage to the target, in addition to the weapon's damage. The extra damage is 2d8 for 5 EP, plus 1d8 for each additional 2 EP spent, to a maximum of 5d8.
*   **Aura of Protection:** Starting at 6th level (next level), you and friendly creatures within 10 feet of you gain a bonus to all Resolution Checks equal to your Intelligence modifier. *(GM Note: This is usually Charisma in D&D, adapted to Intelligence for Nocturne)*.
*   **Extra Attack:** When you take the Attack action, you can attack twice instead of once.

**Gear (Better than Usual):**
*   **Plate Armor (Masterwork):** Provides superior protection (Defense bonus +8, DR 3 vs physical damage, no Coordination modifier to Defense). Grants Disadvantage on Stealth checks.
*   **Longsword (Masterwork):** Deals 1d8 slashing damage (versatile 1d10). Grants a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls.
*   **Shield (Masterwork):** Provides +2 Defense bonus.
*   **Dagger (Masterwork):** Deals 1d4 piercing damage (finesse, light, thrown (range 20/60)).
*   **Bag of Holding:** A magical bag that appears ordinary but can hold significantly more weight and volume than its exterior suggests (holds up to 500 lbs, volume up to 64 cubic feet).
*   **Pouch with 500 gp:** Significant starting wealth.
*   **Noble's Garb (Concealing):** Fine clothing suitable for a noble, subtly designed to obscure her physical attractiveness without looking out of place. Includes a hood and cloaking features.
*   **Small, concealed family signet ring:** A ring bearing the subtle symbol of her noble house, easily hidden.
*   **Potion of Greater Healing (x3):** Restores 4d4+4 HP.

### World Entities: Noble Houses & Factions (Initial Sketch)

**Noble Houses / Major Powers (5):**

1.  **House Valerius (Nocturne's House):**
    *   **Name & Sigil:** House Valerius. Sigil: A coiled serpent striking through a stylized rose.
    *   **Core Ideology:** Power through influence, ruthless pragmatism, maintaining a veneer of respectability. Family above all else (even if they betray each other).
    *   **Primary Area of Influence:** Primarily economic and political influence within the Upper City and the Parliament of Peers, with significant investments in trade and infrastructure throughout the region. They avoid direct military power, preferring to manipulate.
    *   **General Public Perception:** Respected and feared. Known for their keen intellect and sharp business dealings. Rumors of their ruthlessness are often whispered, but rarely proven.
    *   **Inter-Entity Relations:**
        *   **Alliance:** Discreetly allied with the **Ironhand Mercantile Conglomerate** (a powerful merchant guild) through shared economic interests and mutual protection against rival noble houses.
        *   **Rivalry:** Intense rivalry with **House Theron** for political dominance and control over specific trade routes.
        *   **Neutral/Complex:** A strained but necessary diplomatic relationship with the **Parliament of Peers**, often leveraging their wealth to sway votes.
    *   **Key Figurehead:** **Lord Cassian Valerius** (Nocturne's father, Level ? Strategist, Age 55) - The cunning, calculating patriarch who orchestrated Nocturne's exile, viewing her as an uncontrollable threat to his meticulously balanced web of power, despite his underlying pride in her talent.

2.  **House Theron:**
    *   **Name & Sigil:** House Theron. Sigil: A soaring falcon clutching a lightning bolt.
    *   **Core Ideology:** Martial prowess, honor (outwardly), and expansion of territory/influence through direct means.
    *   **Primary Area of Influence:** Owns extensive landholdings and military resources in the region surrounding Baldur's Gate, providing security and raw materials. Key military and traditionalist voice within the Parliament.
    *   **General Public Perception:** Respected for their military strength and tradition, but often seen as arrogant and somewhat brutish by the more refined Upper City denizens.
    *   **Inter-Entity Relations:**
        *   **Alliance:** Strong alliance with the **Flaming Fist** (City Guard/Military), often providing funding and recruits.
        *   **Rivalry:** Open and long-standing rivalry with **House Valerius**.
        *   **Neutral/Complex:** A wary, transactional relationship with the **Harbor Master's Guild**, as their trade relies on peaceful passage.

3.  **House Durin's Folly:**
    *   **Name & Sigil:** House Durin's Folly (a dwarven noble house, surprisingly influential in Baldur's Gate). Sigil: A stylized pickaxe crossed with a coin stack.
    *   **Core Ideology:** Unmatched craftsmanship, financial stability, and maintaining dwarven traditions amidst human society.
    *   **Primary Area of Influence:** Controls significant portions of the city's manufacturing, mining interests in the surrounding hills, and banking.
    *   **General Public Perception:** Trusted for their reliability and quality goods, but also seen as insular and stubbornly traditional.
    *   **Inter-Entity Relations:**
        *   **Alliance:** Close ties with the **Ironhand Mercantile Conglomerate** (as a major supplier/investor).
        *   **Rivalry:** Undercurrents of rivalry with **House Valerius** over financial investments and resource control.
        *   **Neutral/Complex:** Generally neutral but pragmatic towards the **Parliament of Peers**, focusing on economic stability over political games.

4.  **The Shadow Weavers (Criminal Syndicate):**
    *   **Name & Symbol:** The Shadow Weavers. Symbol: A stylized spiderweb with a single, watchful eye at its center.
    *   **Core Ideology:** Control of the city's underworld through fear, information, and exploitation of the vulnerable. Profit above all else.
    *   **Primary Area of Influence:** Operates extensively within the Lower City and Undercity, with tendrils reaching into the Upper City's corrupt elements. Controls smuggling, slavery, and protection rackets.
    *   **General Public Perception:** Feared by the common folk, known only through rumors by the privileged. Many pretend they don't exist.
    *   **Inter-Entity Relations:**
        *   **Alliance:** Loose, transactional alliances with desperate elements of the **Guild of Coin** (minor thieving/smuggling guild) and various street gangs.
        *   **Rivalry:** Constant skirmishes and turf wars with the **Emerald Claw** (a more traditional thieves' guild). Actively seeks to undermine the **Flaming Fist**.
        *   **Neutral/Complex:** Attempts to maintain an uneasy truce or a bribed peace with corrupt elements within the **Parliament of Peers** or City Watch, though this is tenuous.

5.  **The Obsidian Vigil (Ancient Arcane Order):**
    *   **Name & Sigil:** The Obsidian Vigil. Sigil: A stylized, unblinking obsidian eye within a shattered arcane circle.
    *   **Core Ideology:** Preservation of ancient knowledge, monitoring magical threats, and subtly guiding the balance of arcane power in the region.
    *   **Primary Area of Influence:** Operates from hidden libraries and sanctums throughout the Sword Coast, with a small, discreet presence in Baldur's Gate itself (perhaps beneath the city or in a magically shielded locale). Highly selective membership.
    *   **General Public Perception:** Mostly unknown to the public. Whispers among scholars and mages speak of an incredibly powerful, reclusive order.
    *   **Inter-Entity Relations:**
        *   **Alliance:** A hidden, mutually beneficial relationship with the **Candlekeep sages** for access to forbidden knowledge.
        *   **Rivalry:** Actively opposes (often covertly) the **Cult of the Dragon** (if active in the region) or other groups seeking to wield destructive, uncontrolled magic.
        *   **Neutral/Complex:** Observes and occasionally subtly influences the **Parliament of Peers** when major magical threats arise, but otherwise remains detached from mundane politics.

### Nocturne's Siblings (5 Older Siblings)

*   **1. Valerius, Julian (Age 32 - Eldest Brother):**
    *   **Personality:** Meticulous, ambitious, overtly charming but calculating, and intensely jealous of Nocturne's raw talent. He cloaks his ruthlessness in civility.
    *   **Occupation/Status:** Heir apparent to House Valerius, currently managing a significant portion of the family's trade empire. Resides in the Valerius estate, Upper City.
    *   **Relationship with Nocturne (Initial):** Strained. He was the primary instigator of her exile, framing her for a murder that exposed her true nature, not because he cared about her evil but because he feared her genius and natural aptitude would usurp his position. He sees her as a past threat, neutralized.
    *   **Secret/Hidden Goal:** To consolidate absolute control over the family's assets, potentially by eliminating other siblings or even his father if they stand in his way, and to elevate House Valerius to an unprecedented level of power in Baldur's Gate.

*   **2. Valerius, Lysandra (Age 29 - Eldest Sister):**
    *   **Personality:** Reserved, highly intelligent, fiercely loyal to the family name (though less so to individual members), and a gifted arcane scholar. She values knowledge and order above all.
    *   **Occupation/Status:** Head Librarian/Archivist for House Valerius, often secluded in the family's private archives or researching at the Watchful Order's library.
    *   **Relationship with Nocturne (Initial):** Distant but not openly hostile. She observed Nocturne's brilliance with a detached fascination, perhaps a touch of intellectual kinship, but views her evil as a chaotic element. She suspects Julian's machinations but remains silent.
    *   **Secret/Hidden Goal:** To uncover and master forgotten Valerius family rituals or ancient magic to 'purify' or strengthen the family lineage, ensuring its long-term survival and dominance through arcane means.

*   **3. Valerius, Marcus (Age 27 - Second Eldest Brother):**
    *   **Personality:** Boisterous, charming, outwardly friendly, but privately a hedonist and a gambler. He prefers to avoid direct conflict and often tries to smooth things over.
    *   **Occupation/Status:** Nominal head of the family's investments in the Lower City's entertainment and gambling districts, though he often delegates and uses family funds for personal pleasure.
    *   **Relationship with Nocturne (Initial):** Indifferent. He largely ignored her due to their age gap and his own self-absorption. He may even feel a slight relief she's gone, as it means less competition for their father's attention (and funds).
    *   **Secret/Hidden Goal:** To pay off a massive gambling debt to a powerful, secretive criminal figure (not the Shadow Weavers, but perhaps an even deeper, more ancient entity) without the family finding out, even if it means selling off minor family assets or betraying lesser secrets.

*   **4. Valerius, Seraphina (Age 24 - Second Eldest Sister):**
    *   **Personality:** Zealous, devout, and unyielding in her faith to Helm. She is morally rigid and sees the world in stark terms of good and evil. Her compassion is reserved for those she deems worthy.
    *   **Occupation/Status:** High Priestess within the Temple of Helm in Baldur's Gate, commanding significant influence within the clergy and among Helm's faithful.
    *   **Relationship with Nocturne (Initial):** Righteous loathing. She views Nocturne's evil as an affront to her faith and a stain on the family name. She believes Nocturne's exile was divine justice. She would actively work against Nocturne if she learned of her return.
    *   **Secret/Hidden Goal:** To establish Helm's undeniable authority as the primary protector of Baldur's Gate, potentially pushing for extreme measures against perceived threats (like other faiths or criminal elements) and rooting out corruption within the city.

*   **5. Valerius, Theron (Age 20 - Youngest Older Brother):**
    *   **Personality:** Naive, impressionable, but possessing a fierce, unrefined loyalty. He admired Nocturne's talent but was horrified by her methods. He feels conflicted by her exile.
    *   **Occupation/Status:** A squire to a high-ranking Flaming Fist officer, striving to prove himself worthy of knighthood. Resides in the Flaming Fist barracks.
    *   **Relationship with Nocturne (Initial):** Deeply conflicted. He was too young and too much of an idealist to understand her true nature fully. He secretly wonders if her exile was entirely fair, even as he abhors her actions. He might be swayed by a direct appeal from Nocturne, or if he saw her in a desperate situation.
    *   **Secret/Hidden Goal:** To expose a hidden corruption within the Flaming Fist ranks that he only dimly perceives, fearing it will tarnish the honor of the organization he wishes to join. He struggles with how to do this without endangering himself or his future.

### First Contract Integration

Your first contract is to **kidnap peasants to sell as slaves to a criminal syndicate.** This aligns perfectly with the newly defined **Shadow Weavers**.

*   **The Contact:** Your contact is a mid-level operative for the Shadow Weavers named **Kaelen "Whisper" Flinn**. (Level 6 Rogue, Age 35). He is a wiry, nervous man with darting eyes, known for his ability to procure unusual "goods" and for being utterly amoral. He values efficiency and discretion.
*   **The Target:** A small, relatively unprotected farming hamlet named **Oakhaven**, a few miles east of Baldur's Gate, known for its fertile lands and simple, unassuming populace.
*   **The Buyers:** Kaelen has implied the buyers are a new, powerful (and secretive) slaver ring operating out of the Underdark, willing to pay a premium for "fresh stock." This suggests potential future interaction with a more significant antagonist.

This setup offers immediate conflict, integrates your character's goals (amass wealth), and introduces key players in the world.
