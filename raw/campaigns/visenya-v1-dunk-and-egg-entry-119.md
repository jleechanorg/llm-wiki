The smith doesn't even look up from the red-hot link he's quenching. The hiss of the water is a sharp, dismissive sound. "You and a thousand others, giant. If you've no lord's mark, you wait behind the Tyrells. My steel's for knights, not for every ditch-sleeper with a rusty shirt."

"Gold?" he rasps, his voice dropping to a suspicious whisper. "A hedge knight and a soot-lad with a dragon's coin? I'm an honest man, giant.
