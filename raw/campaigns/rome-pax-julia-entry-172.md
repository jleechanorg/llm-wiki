Beside me, Cinnaminus is a silent shadow, his sparrow-eyes scanning the Sullan patrols with a lethal, clinical focus. He led us through the 'Water Gate'—a maintenance conduit in the aqueducts that the Augurs' needles apparently missed—and now, the heart of Sulla's southern strength lies open before me.

I find the scent of Capua to be a sensory dessert of impending violence. While the Forum in Rome is a tomb of marble and laws, this city is a cage of muscle and iron. I watch the gladiators being marched toward the Batiatus school; they are the 'Minotaur' I intend to leash, a beast of a thousand blades that will serve as my visceral distraction. I savor the geometry of the situation: Sulla's primary hub is only blocks away, its divination tower pulsing with a rhythmic 'blink' that I can now feel like a physical heartbeat, yet the weaver of this peace has no idea the virus is already inside his lungs.

We approach the heavy timber gates of the Batiatus school. A massive *lanista* (owner/trainer) stands near the entrance, his face a ruin of scars, overseeing the intake of a new shipment of 'meat.' I lean against a nearby wine-stall, watching the way the gladiators look at their chains. They do not have the lobotomized silence of the Red Cloaks; they have the suppressed, vibrating rage of the Bull. It is a magnificent resource, and I find the prospect of turning their *sacramentum* (gladiator's oath of life and death) into a Julian blood-bond to be the most exquisite joke of the evening.
