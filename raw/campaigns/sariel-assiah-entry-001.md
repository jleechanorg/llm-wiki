The padded sparring chamber, high in your family's wing of the Zenith Spire, is filled with the familiar sounds of your brothers' exertion. The sharp *thwack* of blunted wood on steel, the hiss of their breathing, the scrape of their boots on the mat.

Cassian, your elder twin by a mere seven minutes, is a whirlwind of heroic, passionate offense. He fights like the stories say your father, Artorius, once did, his every swing filled with righteous fire. Valerius, his counterpart, is a study in calm, defensive perfection. He moves with an economic grace that mirrors your mother's, his shield always in the perfect position, his eyes analyzing every move.

You, however, are not watching them. You sit at a small, polished table to the side, your focus entirely on the complex, three-dimensional star chart hovering in the air before you. With a delicate touch, you adjust the orbital path of a theoretical moon, watching how its gravitational pull affects the stability of the entire system. The clash of swords is merely background noise to the silent, more intricate dance of celestial mechanics.

"Enough."

Your mother's voice cuts cleanly through the chamber. It is not loud, but it carries an authority that is absolute. Your brothers immediately freeze, turning to her, panting and slick with sweat.

Alexiel Arcanus stands at the chamber entrance, a serene and beautiful figure in simple, elegant robes. At twenty-nine, the years of war have settled into a quiet, unshakeable wisdom in her Unseeing Eyes. She gives her sons a long, analytical look.

"Cassian," she says calmly. "Your fury makes you predictable. You telegraph your power-strikes." She then turns to her other son. "Valerius, a perfect defense wins no wars. It only delays defeat. You fail to see the openings your brother's recklessness gives you."

The twins nod, accepting her critique without complaint. It is the only kind of maternal affection they have ever known—the precise, demanding love of a legendary commander.

Your mother's gaze then falls on you. She walks to your table, her eyes taking in the complex chart you've constructed. "Sariel," she says, her voice softening almost imperceptibly. "Your assessment?"

You look up from your device, your own Starlit Eyes—a direct inheritance from the father you never knew—meeting her pale, porcelain-fractured ones. "Cassian's center of gravity is too high on his final swing," you say without hesitation. "And Valerius fails to exploit the .75 seconds of recovery time that follows. If this were a true battle, they would both be dead."

A faint, rare smile touches your mother's lips. She places a hand on your head, a brief, gentle touch. Then, with a final, approving nod to all three of her children, she turns and departs, leaving you alone with your brothers and your thoughts.

The Battle of the Sacrifice Fields
Alexiel, after a frantic, weeks-long search, finally located her daughter. She and her elite Starfall Guard found Mordan's army in a remote valley, arrayed around a massive, unholy altar. Upon it, a central mage was preparing a ritual to sacrifice hundreds of recently captured children.
Alexiel was prepared to unleash everything. Her plan was simple and direct: she and her guard would storm the enemy lines, cut through to the central mage, and kill him before the ritual could begin. For the first time, she was prepared to use all three layers of her hidden power—her martial skill, her secret magic, and if necessary, the Nullification Field—to prevent the atrocity.
The Impossible Choice

But at the moment she was poised to strike him down, Mordan played his final card. He emerged from a command tent, dragging a bound Sariel with him. Before Alexiel's horrified eyes, he drew a blade and sliced off Sariel's ear, holding it up to show he was serious.
Sariel's terrified, pain-filled scream for her mother shattered Alexiel's perfect strategic calm. In that instant, her Layer 3 (The Joyful Predator) mind, the cold strategist, was overwhelmed by a mother's primal rage. She abandoned her primary objective—the mage—and launched herself at Mordan.
The Hollow Victory
The fight was brutally short. Enraged and focused on a single target, Alexiel was an unstoppable force of nature. She easily killed Mordan and his personal guard, cutting them down before they could further harm her daughter. But as she scooped up her bleeding, weeping child, a wave of horrific green energy pulsed from the altar behind them. The ritual had begun. Children began to die. Her personal victory had cost her the strategic one.
Atonement
Holding her daughter, Alexiel surveyed the horror she had allowed to happen. In a voice filled with a terrible, newfound calm, she gave her final orders. She commanded her remaining Starfall Guard to abandon the fight with Mordan's leaderless army and save as many children from the ritual's spreading corruption as they could.
Then, as Sariel watched in horror, Alexiel unleashed her ultimate power. She poured every ounce of her will and life force into her Nullification Field, expanding it to a massive, unprecedented scale to encompass the entire valley and snuff out the profane magic.
This time, the agony was real. The lie she had maintained her entire life became the truth. The strain of wielding such immense power on that scale was too much for her mortal body. The field held just long enough for the ritual to collapse, but the effort burned her out from the inside. She collapsed, her life force extinguished, dying in the arms of the horrified daughter she had just rescued. Her legend was cemented as a hero who made the ultimate sacrifice, but the truth of her final moments—a mother's fateful, emotional choice—was a secret that would forever haunt her daughter.
