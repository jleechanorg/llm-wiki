Daemon (Level 8, Age 30) listened to Kael's (Level 7, Age 25) assessment of potential conquests, his gaze still sharp, but a new question formed in his mind. He glanced at the ledgers on the table, where the growing tally of his gold was recorded. Nearly forty thousand gold pieces, and ninety more flowing in every day. Such wealth.

"Kael," Daemon rumbled, his voice low and direct, "we have amassed a considerable sum. Tell me, should we use all of this gold? How can we best wield this wealth to forge my empire, to crush our enemies, and to ensure my dominance? What is its purpose now?"

Kael's eyes, always keen, flickered to the ledger, a subtle appreciation for the raw numbers. He understood Daemon's pragmatic approach to resources. "My Lord," Kael began, his voice measured, "to simply 'spend all' would be… inefficient. Gold is the sinews of war, yes, but also the blood of administration and the foundation of long-term power. It must be invested, not merely spent."

Kael swept a hand over the map, indicating the vastness of Daemon's growing domain. "We have options for wielding this wealth, My Lord, each with its own advantages and risks:

*   **Option 1: The Iron Fist (Military Investment)**
    *   **Kael's Proposal:** "We could invest heavily in outfitting our Level 5 recruits with proper steel armor and better weapons, transforming them into a formidable, professional army, rather than relying on salvaged gear. We could also hire a significant contingent of seasoned mercenaries, if a conquest requires overwhelming force or a quick, decisive strike against a well-defended target. This would dramatically increase our immediate military power."
    *   **Pros (Daemon's view):** Direct increase in combat effectiveness. Reduces casualties. Overwhelms resistance.
    *   **Cons (Daemon's view):** Significant one-time expense. Mercenaries require constant payment and can be unreliable.
    *   **Kael's Assessment:** "This would give us undeniable military superiority over any immediate regional foe. Costly, but decisive."

*   **Option 2: The Unseen Hand (Intelligence & Influence)**
    *   **Kael's Proposal:** "We could establish a dedicated spy network across the region, gathering intelligence on rival lords, their weaknesses, their internal conflicts, and their hidden resources. This would allow for more precise strikes, or even the sowing of dissent and sabotage from afar. We could also use bribes to subvert key figures in unaligned territories, opening gates or creating chaos from within."
    *   **Pros (Daemon's view):** Avoids direct bloodshed. Creates chaos for enemies. Provides critical information.
    *   **Cons (Daemon's view):** Slow. Requires subtlety. Relies on others' greed or weakness. Outcomes are less certain.
    *   **Kael's Assessment:** "A long-term investment, My Lord. It would be an invisible weapon, but a powerful one, allowing us to conquer without marching."

*   **Option 3: The Enduring Foundation (Infrastructure & Defense)**
    *   **Kael's Proposal:** "We could invest in fortifying our Keeps beyond their current state – reinforcing walls, building watchtowers, improving garrisons. We could also invest in the infrastructure of our territories, improving roads, expanding mines, or enhancing farmlands. This would make our existing domains more secure, more prosperous, and harder to challenge, increasing our daily income and long-term stability."
    *   **Pros (Daemon's view):** Secures conquered lands. Increases long-term wealth. Reduces need for constant military presence.
    *   **Cons (Daemon's view):** No immediate offensive gain. Takes time to see returns.
    *   **Kael's Assessment:** "A wise choice for securing the empire you have already built, My Lord, making it impregnable and self-sustaining."

Kael finished, awaiting Daemon's decision. The gold lay heavy in the coffers, a silent promise of power.
