## Summary
A Game of Thrones D&D 5e campaign where the player assumes the role of Ramsay Bolton-Stark, a half-Bolton half-Stark bastard with a custom "Dreadblade Shadow" class. The campaign spans 189 scenes, chronicling Ramsay's journey from rescuing Robb Stark at the Red Wedding through the retaking of the North and the final confrontation at the Ruby Ford. The player navigates a complex moral landscape through the "Philosophy Tug-of-War" system, ultimately corrupting Robb Stark while forming the "Bastard's Bond" with Jon Snow.

## Campaign Arcs

### Arc 1: The Red Wedding Escape (Scenes 1-10)
The campaign opens with character creation, establishing Ramsay as a Level 6 Dreadblade Shadow—a Charisma-based martial hybrid combining Assassin, Gloomstalker, and Swords Bard mechanics. At the Twins, Ramsay betrays his father Roose Bolton to save Robb Stark and Catelyn Stark from the Freys, earning his place as Robb's second-in-command. Key scenes cover the escape, the initial philosophical confrontation about methods, and the formation of Ramsay's elite guard (Kyra, Skinner, Sour-Alyn, Grunt).

### Arc 2: The Philosophy Tug-of-War (Scenes 11-50)
Ramsay engages in an ideological struggle with Robb Stark, using persuasion and demonstration to push the young king toward pragmatic ruthlessness. The "Philosophy Tug-of-War" system tracks Robb's corruption (starting at 0%) and Ramsay's redemption (-16%). Scenes cover the march north, poisoning the Frey column, earning the trust of younger lords, establishing a formal agreement with Robb limiting torture and executions without consultation, recruiting Howland Reed and the Crannogmen, and Jon Snow joining the cause.

### Arc 3: The Northern Campaign (Scenes 51-95)
The Stark forces march on Moat Cailin, working with Crannogmen to execute a pincer maneuver against Bolton and Ironborn garrisons. Jon Snow arrives from the Night's Watch, initially hostile to Ramsay but eventually forming the "Bastard's Bond." The campaign retakes Winterfell and captures the Dreadfort. Key events include the Moat Cailin siege, the battle for Winterfell via crypt infiltration, and the capture of Roose Bolton's garrison.

### Arc 4: The Dreadfort (Scenes 96-120)
Ramsay consolidates control of the Dreadfort, breaks Theon Greyjoy into "Reek," interrogates captured Northern lords, and hosts hostages. The "Philosophy Tug-of-War" continues, with Robb's corruption reaching toward 90%. Scenes explore Ramsay's internal struggles, hosting the Northern councils, and continuing the moral degradation of Robb Stark.

### Arc 5: The Southern Campaign (Scenes 121-170)
The Stark forces march south to Harrenhal, capturing the fortress from Lannister control. Ramsay confronts Vargo Hoat and discovers the wildfire cache ("Sun-Eater"). The corruption arcs complete, with Robb fully transformed into the "Winter Butcher" and Jon Snow significantly corrupted. Key events include the capture of Harrenhal, the Vargo Hoat confrontation, and securing the wildfire for the final battle.

### Arc 6: The Ruby Ford (Scenes 171-189)
The final campaign at the Trident. Ramsay uses the captured wildfire to execute a master pincer maneuver, destroying Tywin Lannister's vanguard. The campaign ends with a decisive Stark victory, though some elements remain unresolved. The Bastard's Bond with Jon Snow reaches its peak, and Ramsay's transformation as the "White Flayer" is complete.
