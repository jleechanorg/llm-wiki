My apologies for the repeated oversight regarding Astarion's medium armor proficiency. You are absolutely correct; as a Level 5 Ranger, he should possess proficiency in Medium Armor and Shields. This was a persistent error in my character sheet generation and presentation.

Here is the "5 Whys" analysis to identify the root cause of this recurring omission:

1.  **Why was Medium Armor proficiency missed again after previous character sheet updates?**
    *   **Because** while the correction was acknowledged and processed internally, the mechanism for *re-generating and meticulously cross-referencing the entire presented character sheet* after a minor correction was insufficient. My process for integrating small, specific changes did not fully trigger a comprehensive audit of the entire document's consistency for external presentation.

2.  **Why was the character sheet not fully and consistently updated in the previous full printout (Sequence ID 6)?**
    *   **Because** my internal validation focused on the *specific change requested* (the previous correction for Medium Armor) rather than performing a full, line-by-line re-verification of *all* proficiencies against the cumulative class features. This led to the confirmed change being implicitly assumed as present, but not explicitly *rendered* in the comprehensive list for the user.

3.  **Why did the validation focus narrowly rather than comprehensively?**
    *   **Because** the current protocol, while emphasizing meticulous integration for *new* prompt versions, lacks a specific, explicit directive for a *full character sheet regeneration and audit* after *any* granular update to a character's stats or proficiencies. This creates a gap where minor corrections can be missed in the broader context of the sheet's presentation.

4.  **Why isn't there an explicit protocol for full character sheet regeneration and audit after every granular update?**
    *   **Because** the initial prompt design prioritized the core workflow of rule finalization and narrative flow. While general directives for meticulousness exist, a dedicated protocol for continuously auditing the *completeness and accuracy of a live character sheet* after every modification was not explicitly defined.

5.  **Why was this specific gap in the protocol not identified and addressed earlier?**
    *   **Because** the focus has been on new rule proposals and the overarching structure. This particular scenario (a small, persistent error in a core character stat) highlights a need for a more robust "Character Sheet Integrity Protocol" to prevent such oversights.

**Proposed Amendment to GM Protocols: Character Sheet Integrity Protocol [Change ID: 20240728-F]**

**Justification:** This new protocol directly addresses the identified root cause of persistent character sheet inaccuracies by mandating a rigorous re-audit and explicit presentation of the player character's full sheet after *any* modification, ensuring absolute accuracy and preventing omissions.

**Proposed Rule Text:**
*   **Character Sheet Integrity Protocol**: This protocol is invoked whenever a player character's stats, proficiencies, or traits are modified via DM MODE (including DM Note commands).
    *   **Mandatory Re-generation**: After any modification, the GM must regenerate the *entire* player character sheet, incorporating all current and previously approved traits, abilities, and proficiencies.
    *   **Line-by-line Audit**: The GM must perform an internal, line-by-line audit of the re-generated character sheet against the full set of established rules and approved changes to ensure absolute accuracy and completeness.
    *   **Explicit Presentation**: The GM must explicitly present the fully updated character sheet to the user for final confirmation before returning to STORY MODE. (For `DM Note:` commands, the updated sheet will be presented before the immediate return to STORY MODE within the same response).
