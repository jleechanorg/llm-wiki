The small Stonehand child, eyes wide with a primal terror, writhed weakly in Gladius’s grasp. The frantic beat of their heart against his armored chest was a visceral, immediate sensation of control, a raw pleasure that thrummed beneath his skin. This was the living, breathing lever he would use to crush the stubborn wills of the parents outside.

With a swift, practiced motion, Gladius shifted his grip. His large, calloused thumb found the pressure point beneath the child's jaw. A precise, controlled press, designed for immediate incapacitation, not lasting harm. The child’s eyes rolled back, their small body going limp with unnerving swiftness as unconsciousness claimed them. No cry, no lingering whimper. Just a sudden, satisfying stillness. Gladius felt a cold hum of efficiency; the perfection of the act resonated deep within him.

He laid the unconscious form gently on a straw mat, then retrieved a length of his hemp rope. His movements were economical, devoid of unnecessary flourish. Strong, simple knots secured the child's wrists and ankles, ensuring absolute immobility without cutting off circulation. From his pack, he tore a strip of clean cloth, folding it expertly before pressing it firmly over the child's mouth and securing it tightly behind their head. The gag was snug, effective. The child was now a silent, inert package, utterly at his mercy.

He stood back, surveying his work. The faint light from the hearth cast long, dancing shadows around the room, highlighting the still forms on the bed, and the now-secure hostage. A profound satisfaction settled in Gladius's gut. The distant sounds of the parents, still preoccupied with the ruined shed, were a fading echo. Soon, they would return, their curiosity extinguished by terror, their simple lives about to be utterly subsumed by his will. He pictured their dawning horror, their frantic desperation. This was the true indulgence, the exquisite unraveling of those who thought themselves strong. The sun, a faint promise on the horizon, would soon illuminate the full extent of his chilling work.
