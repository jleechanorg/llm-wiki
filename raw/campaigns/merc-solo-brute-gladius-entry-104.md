The greatsword had cleaved through the lead spider’s leg with a sickening crunch, its shriek of agony a symphony to Gladius’s senses. The viscous, green fluid that sprayed into the air was a visceral testament to his power, the tangible proof of a Perfected Strike. Giant Spider 1, heavily wounded and crippled, skittered violently, thrown off balance. Giant Spider 2, still healthy, paused, its multiple eyes swiveling towards its injured companion, a flicker of confusion in its predatory gaze. The raw, immediate pleasure of the impact, the sheer, undeniable force of his will made manifest, surged through Gladius. This was the thrill of the hunt, the exquisite experience of dominance.

He watched the first spider writhe, a cold, precise hum beneath his skin. His mind, unburdened by empathy, focused purely on the efficiency of the kill. He had hit it hard, but not hard enough to cleave it in two, not enough to send it instantly to the realm of stillness. *Still breathing. Still moving.* His Great Weapon Master feat, a brutal extension of his own philosophy, was a tempting thought, the promise of another immediate, devastating attack. He considered the surge of strength, the additional swing. But the conditions for that bonus action required a critical hit, or for the creature to be reduced to nothing. Neither had happened. The spider still clung to its meager life.

A faint sneer touched Gladius’s lips. The feat, for all its power, demanded utter finality for a bonus. It would not simply grant him an extra swing for a mere wound, however grievous. No, that would be a waste of its potential. His disdain was not for the feat, but for the spider's stubborn clinging to life, its failure to immediately yield. The thrill of the chase, the experience of overwhelming force, was still his to savor. He still had his second attack, a brutal follow-up that would bring the first spider to its knees, or perhaps turn his attention to the second, still-uninjured threat. The night was young, and the performance had just begun.
