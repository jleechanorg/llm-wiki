Location: Forest Edge, Near Man Tibbet's Farm, Early Evening

The air was cool, the forest floor damp beneath Nocturne's (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) boots. Six bundled forms lay hidden in the undergrowth, silent and secured. The physical exertion of subduing and moving the Tibbet family and Widow Elara, even with her formidable physique, was undeniable. Her EP reserves were low (6/36), and her Channel Divinity remained unrefreshed. The immediate task was completed, but the nagging truth of its logistical inefficiency was a sharp, unwelcome thought.

Her mind, a relentless engine of strategic thought, processed the recent events. The mission had escalated, transforming from a simple abduction to an intelligence coup, revealing a deeper layer of political intrigue involving House Durin's Folly and their Shadowfell operations. This new intel was the true prize, far more valuable than the peasants themselves. Yet, the peasants were still currency, a contract to be fulfilled.

**Nocturne's Internal Monologue & Planning:**

*The 'stock' is secured, for now. But the process of acquisition and concealment, while effective, highlighted a critical logistical bottleneck. Transporting multiple, inert bodies manually is inefficient, physically taxing, and introduces unnecessary risk of exposure.*

*This is not sustainable for future, larger-scale operations. My ultimate goal is power and wealth, and that requires efficiency and scalability. Relying solely on my own capabilities for mass extraction will limit my capacity for grander endeavors. I am an Apex Predator, not a beast of burden.*

*Therefore, for future missions involving significant 'assets' or 'stock,' a refined extraction protocol is paramount. This will involve one of two primary approaches:*

*   *Developing my own agents or network:* Cultivating a discreet cadre of loyal subordinates capable of handling logistics, transport, and perhaps even additional field work. This would require investment of time and resources, but would greatly enhance my operational independence and reach. It aligns with my ambition to build my own power base, a personal army of shadows.
*   *Demanding employer-provided transport:* For sensitive or large-scale contracts, the client (in this case, the Shadow Weavers and their Underdark contacts) must be compelled to provide the necessary transport and extraction security. They are paying a premium; they should bear the logistical burden of their demanding requirements. This would shift the risk and resource expenditure away from me, allowing me to focus on infiltration, acquisition, and intelligence gathering. This will be a point of negotiation with Kaelen, leveraging the new intelligence I possess.

*This strategic refinement is crucial. I will remember this for future contract negotiations and for the development of my own network. The current captives are temporarily secure, but their long-term security requires immediate attention. My resources are depleted, and before returning to Baldur's Gate to leverage the intel, I must regain my full capabilities. Resting is now a calculated necessity, not a luxury.*

***

The silent bundles of the Tibbet family and Widow Elara lay hidden. Nocturne's mind, ever calculating, had already moved beyond their immediate fate, contemplating the grander chessboard of power and influence. But the immediate concerns of resource management and securing her position could not be ignored.
