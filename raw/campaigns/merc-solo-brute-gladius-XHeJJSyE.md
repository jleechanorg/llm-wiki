### **Nocturne's Class: Oath of Self-Perfection (The Apex Predator)**

This archetype represents a warrior of supreme will and intellect who has no fealty to external powers, but rather to their own inherent potential and the brutal philosophy of self-maximization. Their "oath" is to themselves – to become the ultimate, most efficient, and most powerful being possible. Their abilities manifest as raw, focused power, unnatural resilience, and a chilling intellect that grants them superiority over all others.

**Core Aptitude for Extraordinary Abilities (EP):** Intelligence (EP Pool = Intelligence Aptitude Score x 2)

**Key Features (Level 5):**

*   **Fighting Style (Battle-Hardened):** You gain a +1 bonus to your Defense score while wearing armor.
    *   *Rationale:* Represents self-optimization for resilience and combat durability.
*   **Extra Attack:** When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can attack twice instead of once.
    *   *Rationale:* A fundamental martial mastery, essential for an Apex Predator.
*   **Perfected Strike (Replaces Divine Smite):** When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack, you can expend Energy Points (EP) to deal extra force damage to the target, in addition to the weapon's damage. This force damage represents the sheer, focused will behind your attack. The extra damage is **2d8 for 5 EP**, plus **1d8 for each additional 2 EP spent**, to a maximum of 5d8.
    *   *Rationale:* Focuses on pure, destructive force channeled through personal will, rather than divine energy.
*   **Aura of Unyielding Dominance (Replaces Aura of Protection, gained at L5):** You emanate an aura of pure, focused will within a **10-foot radius**. You (and friendly creatures within range) gain **Advantage on checks made to resist being Frightened or Charmed**. Additionally, enemies within range have **Disadvantage on checks to resist your Intimidation attempts**.
    *   *Rationale:* Emphasizes psychological control, mental fortitude, and a commanding presence that dominates lesser wills.
*   **Apex Senses (Replaces Divine Sense):** You can use your action to focus your honed intellect and perception, gaining enhanced awareness of your surroundings. Until the end of your next turn, you gain **Advantage on Wisdom (Perception) and Intelligence (Investigation) checks**, and you can pinpoint the location of any hidden or invisible creature within 30 feet of you that is not behind total cover. You can use this a number of times equal to **1 + your Intelligence Modifier per Long Rest**.
    *   *Rationale:* Reflects intellectual and perceptive prowess, allowing for superior information gathering and threat assessment.
*   **Willpower Infusion (Adapted from Lay on Hands):** You can touch a creature and channel your sheer willpower to mend or empower. You have a pool of energy equal to **five times your character level** (25 points at L5). This pool replenishes on a Long Rest.
    *   You can restore 5 Hit Points (HP) for each 1 point drawn from this pool to a creature you touch.
    *   Alternatively, you can spend **5 points** from this pool to remove one level of Fatigue from yourself or a creature you touch.
    *   As a Primary Action, you can spend **10 points** from this pool to grant yourself or a creature you touch a **+1 bonus to one Aptitude score of your choice for 1 minute** (cannot exceed Aptitude Score 20 from this effect).
    *   *Rationale:* A versatile resource pool reflecting self-optimization and the ability to push limits and bolster allies, rather than divine healing.
*   **Channel Divinity (Aspects of the Apex Predator):** You gain two Channel Divinity options. When you use your Channel Divinity, you choose which option to use. You must then finish a Short or Long Rest to use your Channel Divinity again.
    *   **Flawless Opening:** As a Secondary Action, you gain a flash of tactical insight. Your next weapon attack roll or Intelligence-based Resolution Check against a creature you can see this turn has **Advantage**.
    *   **Unchecked Ambition:** As a Primary Action, you temporarily ignore all penalties from Fatigue or the Frightened condition for 1 minute. When this effect ends, you immediately gain **1 level of Fatigue**.
    *   *Rationale:* These reflect specific tactical insights and the capacity to push oneself beyond normal limits, even at a cost.

## **Character Profile: Gladius**

### I. Core Identity
*   **Name:** Gladius
*   **Archetype/Role:** The Caged Adonis (A physically perfect predator hiding his nature beneath a veneer of brutish functionality).
*   **Occupation/Social Standing:** Mercenary of growing repute, known for efficiency and brutality. Publicly viewed as a low-class thug, albeit a highly effective one.

### II. Psychology & Personality
*   **Core Motivation (The "Why"):** To achieve absolute superiority over others, not merely through physical strength, but by psychologically breaking and controlling those the world perceives as "powerful" (nobles, mages, influential merchants). Wealth, pleasure, and conquests are the *trophies* and tools of this domination, not the end goal itself. He despises the weakness he perceives in the elite and seeks to prove his own strength by subverting them.
*   **Greatest Fear:** Insignificance and powerlessness. To be dismissed, ignored, or seen as just another common thug is his deepest terror. His entire brutal persona is a defense mechanism against the memory of being helpless and discarded. He fears his own physical beauty is a form of this weakness, inviting a type of predation he cannot counter with force alone.
*   **Key Personality Traits:**
    *   **Overt:** Impulsive, pragmatic, brutally direct, hedonistic, intimidating, seemingly simple-minded.
    *   **Covert:** Calculating, deeply insecure, predatory, enjoys psychological manipulation, carries a deep-seated class resentment.
    *   **Secret ESFP Drivers:** While his methods are evil, his core driver is experiential. He is stimulated by the "game" of social domination, the thrill of risk, and immediate sensory gratification. He is an artist of cruelty, not just a blunt instrument.
*   **Quirks & Habits:**
    *   A habit of letting his gaze linger on powerful or arrogant individuals, subtly assessing them as future targets.
    *   When his dominance is challenged, a vein in his neck pulses and he has a tendency to crack his knuckles slowly.
    *   Maintains a deliberately unkempt and grimy appearance (unwashed hair, worn gear, dirt under his nails) to hide his exceptional features and to encourage others to underestimate him.
    *   Has a taste for absurdly expensive food and wine, which he often consumes crudely as a private act of defiance against the "refined" people he despises.

### III. Behavior & Speech
*   **Demeanor Under Stress:** When his physical dominance is challenged, he becomes a model of cold, efficient violence. When his *will* is challenged or he feels psychologically cornered, he lashes out with disproportionate aggression or sharp, cutting intimidation to reassert control. He rarely withdraws unless it is a calculated move to orchestrate a more devastating future confrontation.
*   **Speech Patterns:** Speaks in short, direct, often crude sentences. He intentionally avoids complex vocabulary, seeing it as the pointless preening of the weak. His tone is usually flat and dismissive, punctuated by growled commands or sneering observations.
*   **Reputation:** Known in the mercenary world of Baldur's Gate as "The Hammer." A reliable, brutally effective tool for jobs that require muscle and a lack of moral questioning. He is considered dangerous but predictable—an assessment he actively cultivates.

### IV. Backstory
*   **Defining Moment:** Born the third son of a minor, now-destitute noble house in Amn, Gladius was blessed with a physical form that shamed his frail, politically-minded siblings. When the family's fortunes collapsed, they sold the then-13-year-old Gladius to a gladiatorial enterprise to settle a debt, reasoning that his "brutish potential" was their only liquid asset. In the fighting pits, his pretty face made him a target for a different, more insidious kind of predation from the pit's wealthy patrons and overseers. He quickly learned that his looks were a liability and that only absolute, terrifying violence could grant him safety and control. The moment he brutally killed his first owner during a "private exhibition" cemented his worldview: dominate or be dominated.
*   **Relevant History:** Gladius spent four years in the pits, rising from a terrified boy to a feared champion known for his brutal efficiency. He didn't just win; he broke his opponents. He learned to hide his face under a layer of grime and a perpetual scowl. He earned his freedom by fulfilling his contract (and killing the replacement owner) and immediately left Amn, drawn by the chaos and opportunity of a post-Absolute Baldur's Gate. He has spent the last year carving out a niche as a high-priced mercenary, taking the jobs others won't, and indulging his appetites with the proceeds.
*   **Secrets:**
    1.  **Disowned Scion:** He is secretly Gladius of House Al'Veri, and he knows his two elder brothers and one younger sister are still alive somewhere in Amn, likely in poverty or servitude. He harbors a cold, burning hatred for them and the concept of nobility.
    2.  **The Hidden Face:** He actively hides his classical, almost perfect, facial features. He believes it is a weakness that invites the kind of manipulation he despises, and he uses his unkempt appearance as a social shield and a tool to make others underestimate his cunning.

### V. Game Mechanics (Summary)
*   **Class:** Level 5 Apex Paladin, a warrior who channels his own will into devastating force damage and psychological dominance.
