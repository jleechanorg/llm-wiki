You signal the halt. Under your direction, Minsc and Kagain drag the heavy iron throne of the dead Chieftain in front of the tunnel entrance leading back to the surface, creating a crude but effective barricade. The main chamber, now a tomb for the kobold pack, becomes your temporary sanctuary.

**Short Rest**

The hour passes in tense silence, broken only by the dripping of condensation and the occasional settling of the mountain overhead. 

**Kagain** sits on a crate of ore, stripping off his dented armor to tend to the deep bruising on his side. He grunts, popping the cork on a flask of dwarven spirits (for medicinal purposes, he claims) and binding his ribs. "Tough little bastards," he mutters, but the color returns to his face. By the time he re-straps his plate, he looks ready to hold the line again.

**Roric** spends the time flexing his fingers in the new **Gauntlets of Dexterity**. He picks up a pebble, tosses it, and snatches it out of the air with a blur of motion that makes Garrick blink. "It feels... frictionless," the rogue whispers, delighted. "Like my hands know what to do before I tell them."

**Dynaheir** meditates, her hands glowing softly as she recovers her arcane focus. **Minsc** feeds a cracker to Boo, whispering promises of justice to the rodent.

You sit apart, the *Hunger* quiet and watchful. You clean *Varscona*, the blade steaming slightly in the cool damp air. You are rested. You are ready.

**The Descent**

When the hour is up, you clear the barricade and move to the central shaft. The air drafting up from below is noticeably colder, carrying a metallic tang that tastes like old blood and ozone. This is not the musk of kobolds; this is the scent of the grave.

You descend the ladder first, slipping into the gloom of **Level 2**.

The architecture changes here. The rough-hewn tunnels of the kobolds give way to older, shored-up passages. The silence is absolute, heavy and oppressive. There is no yipping, no pickaxes striking stone.

Your **Umbral Sight** pierces the dark. The tunnel ahead opens into a large junction. The ground is littered with debris—shattered crates, rusted tools, and bones. Not kobold bones. Human bones. Gnawed and broken.

And standing sentinel at the far end of the junction, motionless as statues, are four figures. They wear the tattered remnants of Amnish guard uniforms, but the flesh has rotted away to reveal gleaming white bone. They hold rusted longswords and heavy shields. 

**Skeletons.**

But they are not alone. Behind them, lurking in the shadows of a side tunnel, you spot small, hunched shapes holding glass bottles that glow with a faint, sick green light. The "Green Bottles" the prisoner mentioned.

"Contact," you whisper. "Undead. And grenadiers."
