Aurelius shifts his gaze from the map of the Mediterranean to Spartacus, his voice gaining a firmer, more confident resonance as he dives into the logistical reality of their survival. "The East is a gilded trap, Spartacus. The Parthian Empire may hate Rome, but they are masters of the chain as much as my father's house. To seek refuge there is merely to trade one brand for another."

He traces a path upward, past the Alps and toward the wild, dark forests of the North. "We should go North. I know the tribes of the Suebi and the Cimbri; they are not an empire, but a collection of brothers who value the hunt more than the auction block. I have allies there—friendly tribes who remember the honor of the Julian line before it was corrupted by the Dictator. The non-combatants, the ones who cannot survive a siege, would find sanctuary in those forests where Roman horses cannot gallop and the fog is our only walls."

Aurelius looks at Crixus, whose jaw has tightened at the mention of more marching. "From the North, we can strike the Empire's throat. Surgical raids to bleed their supply lines and liberate the frontier outposts. We do not need to burn Rome to break it; we only need to show the world that the North is free, and the Sun no longer shines for the Emperor alone."

Spartacus leans over the map, his eyes tracing the jagged mountain passes. The logic of a safe haven for the weak combined with a tactical base for the strong strikes a chord. "The North is cold, Aurelius," Spartacus muses, "but the cold is an honest enemy. Better the snow than the Parthian silk and the slaver's smile."
