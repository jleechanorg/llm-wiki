Location: The Blade and Stars, Secluded Back Room, Afternoon

The air in the private room of "The Blade and Stars," a grimy tavern nestled deep in Baldur's Gate's Lower City, hung thick with the smell of stale ale and unspoken deals. Nocturne, a mere sixteen years of age but carrying the gravity of a seasoned predator, sat across from Kaelen "Whisper" Flinn (Level 6 Rogue, Age 35). Her fine plate armor was concealed beneath her Noble's Garb, its dark fabric subtly obscuring her form, a stark contrast to Kaelen's nervous, fidgeting energy. He was wiry, his darting eyes constantly sweeping the room, betraying the anxiety of a man perpetually on the edge of discovery.

"Right then, Nocturne," Kaelen began, his voice a low, hurried whisper, even in the isolation of the room. He slid a rolled parchment across the scarred wooden table. "The details. Oakhaven, just a few miles east. Quiet farming folk. Easy pickings. Buyers are impatient. They're paying top coin for… *fresh stock*." He glanced up, a flicker of something akin to a predatory smirk playing on his lips, though it vanished quickly under Nocturne's unwavering gaze.

Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) picked up the parchment, her fingers brushing the rough paper. Her mind, ever a calculating engine (Intelligence 18), processed the information with detached efficiency. "The terms are unchanged?" she asked, her voice a low, even tone that held no hint of the moral revulsion most would feel. Her Agreeableness of 1 ensured that the plight of the "farming folk" held no sway over her. This was a contract, a means to power and wealth.

Kaelen nodded eagerly. "Same as agreed. Payment upon delivery. The buyers… they're from below. Deep. Very particular, but they pay well. And they don't like delays." His eyes darted to the door again, then back to Nocturne. "No… *unnecessary* complications, if you catch my meaning." He clearly feared her reputation, or at least the rumors of her methods.

Nocturne merely inclined her head, a barely perceptible gesture that spoke volumes of her conscientiousness (4/5). Unnecessary complications were inefficient. "Understood. When should the delivery be expected?"

"Tomorrow night. The Moon will be thin. Gives you tonight to scout, if you wish." He pushed a small, leather pouch across the table – initial expenses, perhaps.

Nocturne took the pouch, weighing it in her hand. The gold felt insignificant, a mere prelude to the true wealth she sought. She noted Kaelen's eagerness to be rid of her, a common reaction (Neuroticism 4). She rose, her movements fluid and precise, utterly devoid of wasted effort. "Consider it done."

Stepping out into the bustling thoroughfare of the Lower City, Nocturne melted into the crowd, her concealing garb serving its purpose. The vibrant chaos of Baldur's Gate was a symphony of opportunity, each individual a potential pawn in her grand design. Oakhaven was a means to an end, a stepping stone. Her mind, already churning with military strategy and anatomy knowledge (Expertise Tags), began to devise the most efficient method for extraction. She would approach with utmost discretion, ensuring the harvest was bountiful and clean, for the Underdark buyers clearly had deeper pockets. The thrill was not in the cruelty, but in the perfect execution of her will.
