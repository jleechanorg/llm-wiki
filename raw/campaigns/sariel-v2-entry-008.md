Game Master:
**--- THINK ---**
Kantos’s words, "She was a singular gift... a cage one must be born into," still echoed, slamming the door shut on the idea of directly acquiring Mother's Nullification Field. My own magic, my Starlit Eyes, are different. My unique talent, the Mirror Magic, is about replication from observation, from memory. And the ultimate observation, the most terrifying memory, is burned into my very soul.

*The memory of Mother's ultimate act, that massive, valley-encompassing field at the Sacrifice Fields, is burned into my very soul. If I could access that memory, truly understand its mechanism, perhaps my Mirror Magic could copy its effect. It would be my own path to her power, a terrifying echo rather than a direct inheritance.*

Why do I need Kaelan? Why do I need to find his shattered memories if the core of that power, the chilling, silent moment of its unleashing, is already here, within me? I was *there*. I felt it. I saw it. It was the moment that killed her, and in doing so, imprinted itself onto my very being.

The idea is terrifying. To delve into that memory is to relive the screams, the mutilation, the sheer, paralyzing helplessness that led to her sacrifice. It is to confront my own failure directly. My Mirror Magic is latent, uncontrolled. This isn't a spell I've studied; it's a raw, traumatic echo. Trying to access it now, under this immense emotional weight, could overwhelm me. It could trigger something, some part of that terrifying "Joyful Predator" inheritance.

But the alternative is to remain helpless. To remain the "inefficient variable" my brothers see. To be the child who caused her mother's death. No. I must try. If this power truly lies within my memory, I must attempt to wield it. If I can understand the mechanism from my own mind, perhaps Kaelan's fragmented memories will then fill in the missing details, providing the objective truth I need to truly *master* it. It's a calculated risk, a desperate gambit to transform my deepest trauma into my greatest strength.
**--- END THINK ---**

Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 10:35 AM.
Location: Deep Archives, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 2 (XP: 1000/2700), Gold: 10gp, HP: 10/10

Kantos’s ancient words, heavy with sorrow, had distilled the truth into a terrifying, singular point: your mother was a unique horror, a "one-of-a-kind" manifestation of the Empyrean Affliction, forged from Lucifer’s very essence. The potential for that chilling, lucid "Joyful Predator" now thrummed beneath your own skin, an unwelcome inheritance from both your celestial bloodline and the father you never knew. The vault, once a sanctuary of knowledge, felt like a tomb for your own innocence.

You looked down at your trembling hands, at the faint, shimmering lines of your Starlit Eyes. The guilt for your mother's death, for your perceived weakness, twisted into a new, complex knot of fear. Had her lies, her distant love, her constant efforts to keep you separate from the horrors of her world, been a desperate attempt to protect you from this—from yourself? From the bloodlust your great-uncle Lucifer embodied, and your mother refined? The answer, now terrifyingly clear, weighed heavier than any scholarly tome.

Your mind, reeling from the personal implications, instinctively sought a point of comparison, a way to categorize this monstrous truth. Your mother had been known as a Null, the flawed clone. The journals spoke of Nulls and Joyful Predators as distinct, yet linked phenomena. Where did the truth lie? Could this terrifying capacity for unmaking exist in others?

You looked at Kantos, whose ancient face was still etched with the sorrow and fear of his revelations. "Nulls," you whispered, the word feeling colder, more ominous than before. Your voice was a low, strained question, filled with a desperate need to understand the social landscape your mother had so masterfully navigated. "You said my mother was not a Null as the world understood it. But the project... the 'Null Genesis Project'... it created others. Are there other Nulls, Magister? Real ones, like the world believes my mother to have been? And how are they treated in the Imperium, now that you understand her lie?"

Kantos visibly flinched at the word "Null," his gaze briefly flicking to your own Starlit Eyes, a subtle, almost imperceptible reminder of the prejudice Alexiel faced. Most Imperial nobles, obsessed with magical prowess, viewed true Nulls as pitiable, a genetic dead end, incapable of touching the Weave, and therefore, incapable of true power. Alexiel had used this perception as her most potent camouflage.

He sighed, a dry, papery sound. "Yes, Princess," he rasped, his voice filled with weariness. "True Nulls exist. They are rare, a distinct, barren branch of the Empyrean bloodline. They are indeed magic-inert, utterly incapable of wielding or sensing the Weave. They are... harmless, in that regard. Often scorned for their lack of magical talent, they are generally relegated to quiet, academic pursuits or administrative roles where their lack of power is not a liability. They are, at best, tolerated. At worst, seen as a genetic stain upon their lineage." He paused, his gaze meeting yours, the full weight of his sadness palpable. "Your mother's public identity, her celebrated 'flaw,' was the perfect shield for her true power. No one, absolutely no one, suspected she was anything more than a glorified sword, a blunt instrument of will in a world of magic."

Your mind, now a maelstrom of conflicting thoughts, barely registered his last words. The journals, the Null Genesis Project, the Joyful Predator, Kaelan's shattered mind, the impossible truth of your mother's magic—it all swirled around a single, central point: the memory. *Your* memory. The final, horrifying moments at the Sacrifice Fields. The massive, valley-encompassing Nullification Field. You had seen it. You had felt it. It was burned into your very soul. And your Mirror Magic... it was about replication from observation, from memory.

You closed your eyes, a fierce, desperate resolve hardening your expression. You wouldn't need Kaelan's broken mind yet. You had your own.

You ignored Kantos’s anxious gaze, ignored the chilling cold of the vault. You reached inward, pushing past the overwhelming grief and guilt, past the searing pain of your bandaged ear, seeking the raw, traumatic core of that memory. You focused on the sensation of the Nullification Field, the feeling of magic being *unmade*, of the world going silent. You tried to recall the precise visual of its expansion, the exact mechanism you had glimpsed in your terror.

A wave of raw, psychic feedback slammed into your mind. Your vision exploded into blinding green light, then absolute blackness. You felt the ripping, tearing sensation of magic being violently suppressed, not from the outside, but from *within* you. The agony of the ritual’s collapse, the children’s screams, Mordan’s cruel smile, your mother’s final, gentle collapse—it all flooded your senses at once, overwhelming your conscious thought. It was too much. Your nascent Mirror Magic, untrained and untamed, buckled under the immense, traumatic power of the memory.

You cried out, a sharp, choked gasp, and stumbled back from the dusty table, your hands flying to your head. A thin trickle of blood, warm and metallic, ran from your nose. You gasped for breath, your body trembling uncontrollably, the sterile cold of the vault replaced by a feverish clamminess. You had glimpsed it, yes. A flash of understanding, a fleeting echo of your mother’s immense power. But it was fleeting, chaotic, and utterly beyond your control. The attempt left you utterly drained, physically and emotionally, your mind a raw, screaming wound.

Kantos rushed to your side, his old hands hovering, uncertain whether to help or recoil. His face was a mask of profound fear and sorrow. "Princess! What have you done? You cannot simply... command such a memory!" He looked at your trembling body, at the blood. "You are not like her, Princess. You cannot simply master such a terrible power without cost."
