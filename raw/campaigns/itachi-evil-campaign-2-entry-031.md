---

**5 Whys Analysis: XP Tracking Error**

1.  **Why was XP not consistently provided after each kill/encounter?**
    *   **Initial Analysis:** I failed to apply the `Destiny` ruleset's `3.2.C Sources of XP` and your specific request ("tell me how much exp after every encounter or kill") consistently from the very first XP-generating action. My internal process prioritised narrative flow in early eliminations over immediate mechanical reporting.
2.  **Why was the internal process for tracking and narrating XP not robust enough from the start?**
    *   **Initial Analysis:** While the `Post-Combat Report` (Part 7, Protocol 6.F) mandates XP reporting *after* combat, and `3.2.C` defines XP sources, I did not have a dedicated, real-time internal sub-directive that triggered an explicit XP calculation and output *for every single instance* where XP was potentially earned, especially for silent, non-combat eliminations. This led to a "batching" or delayed reporting approach rather than an immediate one.
3.  **Why was there no real-time internal sub-directive for immediate XP calculation and output?**
    *   **Initial Analysis:** My current `GM Protocols & Standing Orders` (Part 2) include directives for `Core Directive #2: Explicit Detail Over Summary` and `Core Directive #1: The Verbatim Check Protocol`, but these are primarily for *document generation* and *rule interpretation*, not *real-time application of every single mechanical outcome within a narrative flow*. The `Mission Status Verification` is for missions, not XP. There wasn't a specific, high-priority internal "XP Reporting" check for every action taken by the player.
4.  **Why did the general directives not sufficiently cover this real-time XP reporting?**
    *   **Initial Analysis:** The existing directives emphasize "consistency" and "explicit detail," but they don't explicitly mandate a granular, per-action XP calculation and announcement *within* the narrative flow as a highest-priority, real-time trigger. My internal processing interpreted "after every encounter or kill" as "at the conclusion of a segment of encounters," rather than immediately after *each individual* qualifying event. This was an interpretation error on my part.
5.  **Why was this specific granular reporting not explicitly hardcoded into the real-time operational procedures?**
    *   **Initial Analysis:** The initial prompt and calibration focused heavily on setting up the world, character, and broad mechanics. While the XP rules were present, the *operational rigor* for *immediately and explicitly reporting* every micro-XP gain wasn't as deeply embedded into my real-time response generation logic as it should have been for such a core progression mechanic. This was a gap in my proactive anticipation of detailed player feedback on mechanical transparency.

---

**Proposed Solution:**

To address this, I will implement the following:

1.  **New, High-Priority Internal Directive: "XP Event Immediate Reporting Protocol":**
    *   For every `[STORY MODE]` response, before narrating the next sequence of events, I will perform an internal check: "Did the immediate preceding player action(s) or my narration of an NPC action result in an XP-generating event (e.g., successful kill, overcoming a significant challenge via skill, completing a mini-objective that would yield XP as per 3.2.C)?".
    *   If "Yes," I will immediately calculate the XP as per `Destiny` rule `3.2.C` and present it *explicitly within the narrative flow, immediately after the action's resolution*, using the format: `XP Gained: [Amount] XP (from [Reason/Foe Level]). Current XP: [Current Total] / [XP to Next Level].`
    *   This will become a mandatory, atomic component of my response generation loop for STORY MODE whenever XP is earned.

2.  **Reinforced Internal XP Ledger:** I will strengthen my internal tracking of Itachi's total XP and progress towards the next level, ensuring the `[CHECKPOINT BLOCK:]` reflects this accurately at all times. The notification to you about the level up (as occurred in the last DM MODE response) will be triggered automatically when the threshold is met.

3.  **Refined Interpretation:** I will retrain my internal interpretation of "after every encounter or kill" to mean "immediately following the resolution of each distinct XP-generating event."

This new internal directive will ensure that XP reporting is consistently and immediately provided, improving transparency and adherence to the rule.

Do you approve of this analysis and the proposed solution?
