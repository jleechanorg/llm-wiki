`Location: Outskirts of Greenmeadow Village, Shadowfen Moor`

The oppressive bustle of Baldur's Gate faded behind Gladius as he strode purposefully out of the Lower City, the setting sun casting long, distorted shadows before him. He didn't look back. The thought of lingering for frivolous pleasures was fleeting; the promise of a job, cleanly executed and well-paid, held a deeper, more brutal satisfaction. The peasants were merely an appetizer before the true feast of Lord Silvanus's eventual downfall.

The paved roads quickly gave way to a winding, mud-slicked track that stretched into the desolate expanse of Shadowfen Moor. The air grew colder, damp with the chill of the marsh, and the silence was broken only by the distant croaking of frogs and the soft, almost imperceptible thud of Gladius's Shadow-Step Boots on the damp earth. His splint mail, usually a clanking testament to his brute force, was surprisingly muted by the padded footwear, a testament to the half-elf's grim craftsmanship. It was enough.

Hours passed in a rhythmic march, the greatsword a comforting weight on his back. His mind, usually a cauldron of raw appetites, was focused, calculating. He thought of the Millers, the Blackwoods, the Stonehands – nine souls. Easy. No challenge. But the sheer audacity of Lord Silvanus, to hire him for such a trivial slight, to think he could control Gladius like a common dog… that was the true spark of his interest. The memory of the lackey's terrified face, his fumbling for more coin, brought a slow, grim smile to Gladius's lips. This job wasn't just about gold; it was about the exquisite pleasure of making the powerful grovel.

The moon, a sliver of silver in the ink-black sky, offered little light as the first faint outlines of Greenmeadow appeared through the mist. A cluster of dark, silent shapes against the horizon, unadorned and utterly vulnerable. A few scattered lights glimmered from within, perhaps a late-night candle or a dying hearth. The village slept, oblivious. The job was here.
