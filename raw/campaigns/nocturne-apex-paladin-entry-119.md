Location: Forest Edge, Near Man Tibbet's Farm, Night

The urgent signal sent, Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) retreated deeper into the shadows of the forest, finding a concealed observation point that offered a clear view of the dead drop tree and the approach from the main road. The six bundled forms of her captives lay silent and still in their temporary hiding spot, their presence a calculated risk she had accepted. Her patience, though seemingly absolute, was merely a veneer over a meticulous intellect that abhorred idleness. The night hours passed slowly, marked only by the hoot of owls and the distant, unseen movements of nocturnal creatures. Every rustle was analyzed, every shadow scrutinized.

It was nearly midnight when a faint, almost imperceptible disturbance rippled through the quiet. A single figure, cloaked and moving with a practiced discretion, slipped from the main road and approached the dead drop tree. It was Kaelen "Whisper" Flinn (Level 6 Rogue, Age 35) himself, his wiry frame hunched, his head swiveling, eyes darting nervously through the darkness. He moved with a greater urgency than before, a subtle tremor in his movements betraying his unease.

Kaelen quickly located the dead drop, his fingers fumbling slightly as he retrieved the rolled parchment. His brow furrowed as he read the concise message, his lips moving silently over the words. A visible shiver ran through him, despite the cool air. The urgency of Nocturne's second signal, coupled with the stark implications of "Target unexpected" and "Resistance neutralized," clearly unsettled him. He glanced wildly around, then his gaze, though it could not pierce the deep shadows, seemed to linger on the area where Nocturne was concealed. He clearly felt her presence, or at least the cold weight of her implied threat.

"Unforeseen complications," Kaelen muttered, his voice a low, raspy whisper, barely audible over the night sounds. "Doubled fee... transport... By the Nine Hells, what did you *do*?" His fear was palpable, but so was his understanding of the new terms. He knew Nocturne's reputation for ruthless efficiency. This was not a negotiation; it was a directive.

He moved quickly, pulling a small, heavy purse from his belt. He placed it carefully at the base of the tree, then tapped out a rapid, coded response on the bark: three short, a pause, one long, then two short – a confirmation of receipt, and a promise of imminent compliance. Then he vanished back into the night as silently as he had arrived, leaving behind the heavy purse and an unnerving sense of haste.

Nocturne waited, allowing a full minute to pass before moving. Kaelen was gone. The immediate phase of negotiation was complete. The gold awaited. The captives remained hidden. The path to the Underdark rendezvous, and deeper power, was clearer.
