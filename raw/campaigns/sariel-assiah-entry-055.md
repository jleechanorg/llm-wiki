Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 10:30 AM.
Location: Deep Archives, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 2 (XP: 1000/2700), Gold: 10gp, HP: 10/10

The revelation struck you like a physical blow. The air in your lungs felt suddenly thin, as if the silence of the vault had stolen the very breath from your body. The intricate puzzle pieces of your life, of your family's legacy, of your mother's terrifying genius, snapped into place with a horrifying, undeniable click. The Empyrean Affliction, the curse that had plagued your family for generations, the very thing that had twisted your uncle Titus into a raging monster—it wasn't just madness. It was this. This cold, calculating, joyful desire to unmake, to dominate, to control. And your mother, the legendary hero, had not just fought against it. She had *mastered* it. She had made it her weapon.

You looked down at your hands, at the faint, shimmering lines of your own Starlit Eyes, the mark of that same celestial blood. The potential for that terrifying 'joy' in absolute control, in merciless unmaking, now thrummed beneath your own skin, an unwelcome inheritance. The guilt for your mother's death, for your perceived weakness, twisted into a new, complex knot of fear. Had her lies, her distant love, her constant efforts to keep you separate from the horrors of her world, been a desperate attempt to protect you from this—from yourself? From the bloodlust your great-uncle Lucifer embodied, and your mother refined? The answer, now terrifyingly clear, weighed heavier than any scholarly tome.

Your mind, reeling from the personal implications, instinctively sought a point of comparison, a way to categorize this monstrous truth. Your mother had been known as a Null, the flawed clone. The journals spoke of Nulls and Joyful Predators as distinct, yet linked phenomena. Where did the truth lie? Could this terrifying capacity for unmaking exist in others?

You looked at Kantos, whose ancient face was still etched with the sorrow and fear of his revelations. "Nulls," you whispered, the word feeling colder, more ominous than before. "You said my mother was not a Null as the world understood it. But the project... the 'Null Genesis Project'... it created others. Are there other Nulls, Magister? Real ones, like the world believes my mother to have been? And do they... do they also possess this 'Joyful Predator' trait? This... thirst for control, for unmaking?"

Kantos visibly flinched at the word "Null," his gaze briefly flicking to your own Starlit Eyes, a subtle, almost imperceptible reminder of the prejudice Alexiel faced. Most Imperial nobles, obsessed with magical prowess, viewed true Nulls as pitiable, a genetic dead end, incapable of touching the Weave, and therefore, incapable of true power. Alexiel had used this perception as her most potent camouflage.

He sighed, a dry, papery sound. "Yes, Princess," he rasped, his voice filled with weariness. "True Nulls exist. They are rare, a distinct, barren branch of the Empyrean bloodline. They are indeed magic-inert, utterly incapable of wielding or sensing the Weave. They are... harmless, in that regard. Often scorned for their lack of magical talent." He paused, his gaze meeting yours. "But no, Princess. Most Nulls do not possess the 'Joyful Predator' trait. That... that is a far more terrifying manifestation. A perversion. It is tied to the primordial celestial essence woven into the very first experiments of the Null Genesis Project, specifically when powerful, singular bloodlines were used as a 'seed.' Your mother's unique power, her capacity for both Nullification and magic, and the terrifying 'joy' she found in it... that was due to the celestial essence of Lucifer himself. His blood, Princess, forged a monstrous amplification of the Null state. A one-of-a-kind horror. There are no others like her."
