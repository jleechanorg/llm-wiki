The Stonehand couple lay prone, bound and broken, their faces pressed against the cold stone floor, whimpering softly. Their raw, visceral terror was a potent scent in the dwelling, mingling with the hearth smoke and the subtle aroma of their child's fear. Gladius stood over them, the Silent Strangler's Cord a dark, ready presence in his hand. The ultimate satisfaction wasn't just in the kill, but in the complete, utter obliteration of their will.

He knelt, slowly, deliberately, bringing his face close to the farmer’s ear. The man flinched, his body tensing, expecting a blow. But Gladius merely spoke, his voice a low, gravelly current, chilling in its quiet menace. "Lord Silvanus," he began, the name a poisoned whisper, "thinks he bought this land with gold. He thinks he bought *me* with gold." He paused, letting the words sink in, watching the farmer’s ear redden. "But some things... cost more. Your 'honor.' Your stubbornness." He leaned closer, a predatory gleam in his eyes. "What else does your master, or this village, value? What else is here that he thinks *his* by right, but isn't?"

The farmer, clearly caught between terror and confusion, made a choked sound. "N-nothing, sir," he stammered, his voice muffled by the floor. "We have nothing but this land. Nothing of value."

Gladius let out a low, humorless chuckle. "Silence," he snarled, a quick, almost imperceptible shift in his body conveying the threat. He nudged the man's head with his armored boot, just enough to make him shudder. He then moved to the woman, repeating the grim posture. "Your child is silent now. You will be too. Give me something else. A name? A hidden path? Something that Lord Silvanus thinks is *his*." He looked into her terrified, tear-filled eyes, enjoying the exquisite agony of her helplessness. This wasn't about gold, not anymore. This was about stripping away every last shred of their perceived strength, about breaking the spirit before the body. The dawn, now painting vibrant colors across the eastern sky, felt like a cruel spotlight on their profound, intimate humiliation.
