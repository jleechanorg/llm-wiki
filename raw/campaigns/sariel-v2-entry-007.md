Game Master:
**--- THINK ---**
*Power up.* The words burned in my mind, a desperate, almost primal urge. Kantos’s revelations crashed over me like a cold wave: Mother wasn’t just a hero, she was a masterpiece of deception, a "Joyful Predator" who found perverse satisfaction in unmaking. And that terrifying potential, that capacity for absolute control and intelligent destruction, now thrummed in my own blood. My own celestial essence, the very thing that marked me with these Starlit Eyes, was the same twisted gift that Lucifer possessed, and that Mother had wielded.

The thought was a venomous lure. My helplessness at the Sacrifice Fields, the screams, my own weakness that led to Mother's death—it was a gaping wound. This "Joyful Predator" offered a way to never be helpless again. To never break. To find satisfaction, even joy, in dominating the threats that once broke me. To make *them* scream. The idea was chilling, and yet, a dark, insidious part of my intellect felt a terrifying familiarity, a recognition of a pattern, a solution. *This* is how she won. This is how she survived. This is how she was *unmoved*.

But the thought curdled my stomach. To embrace that "joy" in unmaking others, in subtle psychological ruin, in cold, precise violence... that was the Empyrean Affliction, refined into something truly monstrous. That was Lucifer. That was the path of blood and subtle, terrifying deceptions that could twist a soul. Had Mother truly become that monster? Or had she simply wielded it without succumbing? The line felt impossibly thin.

No. I cannot become *that*. Not completely. But the power... the Nullification Field. The ability to silence magic, to turn gods into mortals. Kantos said it was innate, that I couldn't be *born* into it. But my own Mirror Magic... my unique talent is replication. If I can't *be* a Null, can I *reflect* the Nullification Field? The memory of Mother's ultimate act, that massive, valley-encompassing field at the Sacrifice Fields, is burned into my very soul. If I could access *that* memory, truly understand its mechanism, perhaps my Mirror Magic could copy its effect. It would be my own path to her power, a terrifying echo rather than a direct inheritance.

The first step, then, is clear. Kaelan. He saw it. He witnessed her true face, her "unmoved" power. His memories, no matter how shattered, are the only way to unlock that visual truth. And if I can understand it, truly understand it, perhaps I can wield the effect without embracing the monstrous "joy" that powered it. A grim, desperate hope, but the only path forward that didn't lead to either helplessness or damnation. I need to get to Kaelan. And I need to do it without alerting Raziel.
**--- END THINK ---**

Kantos’s explanation settled into the silence of the vault, a final, chilling piece of the puzzle. The Imperium's disdain for Nulls, their dismissal of them as genetically flawed and powerless, was the perfect soil for your mother's lifelong deception to take root. She had hidden in plain sight, her terrifying power masked by the court's own arrogance and prejudice.

You finally understood the full scope of her genius. The journals had given you the 'how'—the horrifying science of her creation. But they could not give you the 'what'. They could not show you the look in her eyes during that final battle, the truth of the "Joyful Predator" unleashed. Only one person could.

Your mind, now sharp and clear, cut through the fog of grief and confusion. Your objective was no longer abstract; it was a person. Sergeant Kaelan. The Starfall Patient.

You looked at the old Magister, your Starlit Eyes burning with a new, unshakeable resolve. The time for academic discovery was over. It was time to find the witness.

"Magister," you stated, your voice devoid of its earlier hesitation, now holding the quiet, firm authority of a princess of the realm. "You have guarded these secrets for decades. You have done your duty. Now, I must do mine."

You met his gaze, leaving no room for argument. "Take me to the Quiet Ward. Take me to Sergeant Kaelan. I will see the truth for myself."

Kantos paled, his ancient hands trembling. He opened his mouth to protest, to warn you of the dangers, of Raziel's explicit orders, of the horrors that lay in the sanitarium's deepest levels. But he saw the look on your face—the same relentless, unyielding determination he had once seen in your mother's eyes. He knew, with a profound and weary certainty, that he could no more stop you than he could stop a rising tide.

He gave a slow, defeated nod, the weight of his decision settling upon him like a shroud. "As you wish, Princess," he rasped. "But we will need help. The Quiet Ward is not a library. It is a fortress. And you will need your shield."
