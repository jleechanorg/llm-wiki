The endless shelves of the Archives, usually a source of comfort, now felt like a labyrinth designed to hide the very truth you sought. Public records only offered the grand, simplified narratives of "The Mortal Queen." You needed something deeper, something beyond the curated legend. Your thoughts turned to the one person who might possess such forbidden knowledge: Magister Lucian Kantos.

You found him in his usual haunt, the central scriptorium, hunched over a massive, illuminated tome. Magister Lucian Kantos (Level 16 Runesmith, Age 200+) was a frail, ancient figure, his fingers stained black with ink, his spectacles thick enough to distort his sharp, intelligent eyes. The air around him hummed with the quiet rustle of parchment and the faint scent of old paper. He looked up as your shadow fell over his work, his fussy annoyance at the interruption quickly giving way to a weary deference as he recognized you. His gaze, however, held a flicker of something more—a deep, almost sorrowful weariness, as if he carried the weight of all the secrets buried in these shelves.

"Princess Sariel," he rasped, his voice a dry academic rustle, "an unexpected, if... somber, presence. I trust you are well, given the circumstances?" His eyes flickered to the bandage on your ear, a subtle acknowledgement of your recent trauma.

You clutched your robes, feeling the familiar prickle of shame, but forced yourself to speak. "Magister," your voice was quiet, hesitant, but held a new, fragile determination. "I... I am trying to understand my mother. Not the legend, but the woman. The truth of her campaigns, her strategies... particularly her final one. The Battle of the Sacrifice Fields." You saw a flicker of something in his eyes, a momentary hardening, then a quick, practiced mask of neutral professionalism. "The official reports are... incomplete. I need to know what truly happened, Magister. I need to know what led to her final act. What did she truly face in those last moments?"

Kantos slowly closed the tome before him, the *thump* echoing loudly in the silent scriptorium. He adjusted his spectacles, his gaze seeming to peer into your very soul. "The past, Princess," he said, his voice dropping to a near-whisper, filled with a caution that was almost palpable, "is often more dangerous than the present. Some truths are... better left undisturbed."
