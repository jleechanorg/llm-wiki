Midday (13:15:00). Outside the grotto, the relentless rhythmic thud of your stone-labor continues, a self-imposed penance that vibrates through the cave walls. Inside the cool, damp shadows of the Command Grotto, the air is thick with a silence that Naevia finally breaks.

She sits on a low stone shelf, her skin clear and healthy for the first time in years, looking at the two men who lead this rebellion. Crixus paces like a caged tiger, his hand white-knuckled on his sword hilt, while Spartacus stands motionless, watching the light dance in the dripping water.

"You speak of him as if he were the whip itself," Naevia says, her voice soft but carrying a steel forged in the salt-pits. "But I felt his hands. They did not shake. They did not take. They only... gave. Crixus, you would have died in that tunnel to save me. He stayed so you wouldn't have to."

Crixus stops, turning to her with a snarl that softens instantly into a look of pained adoration. "He is a Caesar, Naevia! The very name is a curse! He is the son of the man who laughed while we bled for the amusement of Rome! I look at him and I see every brother I lost in the pits. I see the face of our enemy!"

"Then you are blind," Aurelia interjects, stepping from the shadows near the entrance. Her amber eyes, so like your own, are fixed on Crixus with a challenging fire. "You see a name. I see a man who knelt in the mud of Capua for a woman he didn't know. I see a soldier who stood alone against three hundred Iron Hounds so a 'slave girl' could see the sky. If he is our enemy, then we have already lost, for he is the only one who has shown us what a true Roman should be."

Spartacus finally turns, his gaze moving between the two women and the frustrated Gaul. "The men are murmuring, Crixus. They see a hero hauling stone like a thief. They see the 'Sun of the Spires' being extinguished by the very leaders he saved. If we treat him as a monster, we prove the Romans right—that we are nothing but beasts who cannot recognize honor when it bleeds for us."

Crixus growls, a low, guttural sound of cognitive dissonance. "And if he is a wolf? If this is all a game to ensure we bow to a Julian instead of a Legate?"

"Then let him lead the vanguard at the Western Cleft," Spartacus decides, his voice a final, gravelly decree. "If he is a wolf, he will flee to his father's arms. If he is a brother... he will be the first to meet Glaber's steel. Naevia is whole because of him. We will not pay that debt with a stone-wall. Go, tell the men the noble stands the watch."
