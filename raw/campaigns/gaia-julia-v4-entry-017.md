The heavy cedar door creaks open, admitting a slice of flickering torchlight and the sharp, iron scent of the atrium. Elara enters, her face a mask of 'motherly' concern, her older, weathered features etched with the exhaustion of the night. She doesn't look at you with the submissive deference of a servant; she looks at you with the fierce, protective gaze of a woman who has raised you since you were a babe in the cradle. 

She turns back to the doorway, where the silhouettes of Spartacus and Crixus loom like twin pillars of storm and granite. 

"She is a good girl," Elara says, her voice trembling but firm, addressed to the men behind her. "I have held her since she was a toothless infant. I know her heart. She is not the monster her bloodline suggests. She is just... Livia. My daughter in all but name."

She steps further into the room, her hand resting on the bedpost as she looks back at the rebel leaders. "I have spoken with my sisters. The Petals. We share your hatred for the Eagles and the chains they forge. We will not fight—we are women of peace and medicine—but we will help. Logistics, mending, the tending of wounds... even Livia has agreed to work. She will do chores, scrub the floors of her own father's house if she must, to prove she is not the parasite you believe."

Elara's eyes narrow, a steel-cold pragmatism flashing beneath the grief. "But there is a price for our labor. If this rebellion fails—and we all know the Legions are coming—you must allow the Petals to take her to safety. If the blood begins to flow, she will not be in danger. We will serve you in the shadows of the camp, but you will give us your word as men that she is never to be used as a shield or left to the mercy of a Roman blade. Do we have an accord?"

Spartacus looks from the 'mother' to the 'daughter' lying broken on the silk. The logic is sound; the Petals are skilled, and a willing labor force is more valuable than eleven corpses. He nods slowly. "Agreed. She works. And she remains protected. If the day is lost, you have my word... she goes to the cliffs."
