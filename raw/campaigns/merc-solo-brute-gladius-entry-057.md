The weight of the silenced Miller and Blackwood families settled in Gladius's mind with a cold, precise satisfaction. The subtle hum of total control, a pleasure more potent than any indulgence, pulsed beneath his skin. The sun, a faint promise on the eastern horizon, was already beginning to paint the sky with streaks of orange and grey, threatening the cloak of darkness that had served him so well. It was time for the final family, the Stonehands.

He moved with the practiced stealth of a seasoned predator, his Shadow-Step Boots sighing softly against the dew-soaked ground, muting the heavy clang of his splint mail to a mere whisper. He approached the Stonehand dwelling, a squat, sturdy stone house that stood defiantly near the village center. Unlike the isolated Miller and Blackwood homes, this one was surrounded by other, smaller houses, their windows still dark but their proximity a new consideration.

His sharp eyes, absorbing every detail, noted the smoke curling lazily from the Stonehand chimney, a sign of early rising. The distinct clatter of a pot, then a low, gruff voice, drifted from within. *Strong-backed, but simple,* Gladius recalled the lackey's words. *Loud.* Their inherent lack of subtlety was evident even now, their lives an open book compared to his own concealed brutality.

There was no hidden shutter, no sagging doorframe here. The stone walls offered no easy ingress, and the sturdy wooden door seemed firmly barred. Attempting a silent approach through the front would be a fool's errand; any noise would carry easily in the pre-dawn quiet, likely rousing neighbors. The narrow alleyways between the houses offered minimal cover, and the rising sun would soon strip away the shadows he relied upon. The distraction fire, now a distant memory, would be of little help here.

A cold, calculating assessment settled in Gladius’s gut. This wouldn't be as clean, as *silent*, as the others. Their very predictability, their simple, unyielding nature, would force a more direct confrontation. It wasn’t his preferred method for 'no witnesses', but it would be a visceral display of force, a direct imposition of his will. The thrill of the impending, unavoidable violence began to simmer within him, a raw, immediate anticipation.
