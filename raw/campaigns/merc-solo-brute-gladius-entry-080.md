The muffled, increasingly frantic voices of the Stonehand couple drew closer, their steps heavy on the dew-soaked earth outside. Gladius, hidden in the deepest shadow beside the entrance, felt a cold, precise hum beneath his skin. The greatsword, propped silently behind the heavy door, was ready if needed, but his primary tool, the Silent Strangler’s Cord, was already coiled in his hand. The stage was set.

With calculated precision, Gladius moved the unconscious, gagged child. He positioned the small form just out of direct sight, ensuring that only a sliver of the child's form, perhaps a tiny hand or a lock of hair, would be visible from the doorway. It was enough. The bait was laid.

The heavy wooden door creaked open further. "It's nothing, Martha," the man's gruff voice rumbled, though it lacked conviction. "Just that old shed. Probably a fox or-" His voice cut off abruptly.

Gladius watched, his eyes unblinking, as the burly Stonehand farmer stepped into the common room, his wife close behind him. The faint light from the hearth illuminated their faces, etched with a mixture of weariness and lingering confusion. Their eyes, searching for the source of the noise, then for their missing family, swept across the room.

Then, they landed on the glimpse of their child. The man's face, already pale from the early morning chill, went ashen. "No... no!" he choked, his voice raw with sudden, desperate terror. His wife gasped, a hand flying to her mouth, her eyes wide with dawning horror. The sight of their child, limp and bound, instantly shattered any remaining composure. Their simple world, their predictable lives, had just collapsed. Their fear, raw and visceral, was a potent offering to Gladius’s senses, a deep, immediate pleasure that spread through him. This was the true taste of dominance, the exquisite unraveling of those who thought themselves strong.

They took another step, then another, drawn in by the sight, oblivious to the towering shadow beside the door. "Our child! What have you done?!" the woman whimpered, her voice a fragile thing.

Gladius let them pass fully over the threshold. Then, with a swift, powerful kick of his Shadow-Step Boot, he slammed the heavy wooden door shut. The thud resonated through the thick stone walls, a final, unyielding punctuation mark. The click of the bar, sliding into place, echoed in the now-sealed chamber. They were caged. The sun, painting the eastern sky with bold oranges and purples, promised a new day. But for the Stonehands, trapped within, it would only bring the cold, final darkness.
