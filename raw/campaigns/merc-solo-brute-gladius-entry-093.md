The Stonehand dwelling, now lit by the full, intrusive light of dawn, was a scene of grim efficiency. Corvan, cuffed and utterly broken, had begun his gruesome work, his disheveled silks brushing against the cold, stone floor. His ragged breaths and clumsy movements filled the silence, a testament to his profound degradation. Gladius watched for a moment, a subtle hum of cold satisfaction vibrating beneath his skin. This was the true indulgence: to witness the unraveling of the 'elite,' to force them to wallow in the very filth they paid others to manage.

Corvan fumbled with a body, his cuffed hands hindering his movements, his face contorted in despair. Gladius’s lips thinned into a faint sneer. The man was useless like this, a pathetic display. The cleanup needed to be swift, flawless, before the village fully woke. It wasn't about pity; it was about pragmatic efficiency, another layer of control.

"Oh, come now," Gladius rumbled, his voice a low, gravelly current, laced with a brutal, cutting mockery. He reached for Corvan's cuffed hands, his large fingers closing around the cold iron. Corvan flinched, his eyes wide with a fresh surge of terror, expecting further torment. But with a sharp, decisive click, Gladius unlatched the manacles. He let them fall with a faint *clink* to the stone floor. "Don't want to make it *too* easy for Lord Silvanus to replace you, do we? Consider it a moment of levity. Now move, before the sun makes your incompetence visible to the whole village." His gaze, cold and unblinking, bored into Corvan, stripping away any last shred of dignity. The implied threat, the chilling 'joke' at Corvan's expense, was far more potent than any physical restraint. Corvan stammered, then, released but no less terrified, merely nodded frantically, his movements now more desperate, if still clumsy.

With Corvan now fully immersed in his horrifying task, Gladius turned his back on the grim scene. The Stonehand family was erased, their final defiance crushed. But one loose end remained, a final confirmation of Lord Silvanus's petty corruption. The sheriff, Borin.

Gladius moved through the now-brightening village, his Shadow-Step Boots whispering against the dewy grass, his heavy armor surprisingly muted. The scent of woodsmoke and damp earth mingled with the faint aroma of freshly tilled soil. Villagers were beginning to emerge, some already leading oxen to fields, others kindling hearth fires. They offered polite, distracted nods, seeing only a heavily armored mercenary, a common sight in these troubled times. Gladius met their gazes with an impassive stare, his true purpose hidden behind the mask of his cultivated reputation. He found the sheriff's home easily, a slightly larger, well-kept cottage near the village well. A single, small barrel, smelling faintly of cheap ale, sat near the front door. A crude, but effective, signpost of Borin's vice. The thought of this man, meant to uphold order, utterly compromised by drink, brought a familiar, cold satisfaction. Another weak link in the chain of authority, so easily bent to his will.
