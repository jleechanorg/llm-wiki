Game Master:
[Mode: DM MODE]

Understood. You are asking for clarification on why the Silent Guard would not permit Sariel access to the Quiet Ward, given that Raziel is dead and their loyalty might implicitly lie with Alexiel's heirs. This is a crucial point for maintaining character and world consistency.

Here is the canonical explanation for the Silent Guard's stance regarding the Quiet Ward:

1.  **Absolute Protocol & Final Orders:** The Silent Guard are not merely personal bodyguards; they are Raziel's **sacred enforcers of absolute, deeply ingrained protocols and secrets**. The Quiet Ward is not a regular facility; it's a "tomb for secrets," a place of *permanent containment* for truths (and individuals) deemed too dangerous for the Imperium. Raziel's orders regarding its security and *non-access* would have been issued as a final, unyielding directive, designed to remain in effect even in his absence or death. Their training emphasizes strict adherence to the *last, highest-priority command* from their ultimate authority.

2.  **Protection of "Dangerous Truths":** The Silent Guard are aware of the catastrophic nature of the secrets held within the Quiet Ward (the patient's madness, the truth of Alexiel's power, the implications for the Imperium's stability). Their loyalty to the Imperium's stability, and by extension, Alexiel's legacy (which is built on a grand deception), would mandate that these truths remain buried. They genuinely believe that preventing access, even to Sariel, is a necessary act to protect the Imperium from chaos and to preserve the very legend Sariel's mother died to create.

3.  **Sariel's Current Status:** While Sariel is Alexiel's daughter and a princess, she is also currently a "disgraced pariah" ("The Architect of the Fall"). Her perceived vulnerability and current emotional state, combined with the extreme sensitivity of the Quiet Ward's contents, would make the Silent Guard view her request as a dangerous and unauthorized breach of protocol, rather than a legitimate command. They would require an override from a higher, established authority (like the entire Triumvirate) which they would not likely receive for such a sensitive matter.

4.  **The Silent Guard's Unique Loyalty:** While their direct obedience to Raziel is gone, their profound respect and admiration for Alexiel (earned at the Starfall Gambit) and their dedication to the Imperium's *long-term stability* means they would enforce protocols they believe are in the best interest of the Imperium and Alexiel's *true* legacy (the preservation of her lie). They would interpret their duty as guarding the secret, not enabling its exposure.

Therefore, the Silent Guard will act not out of malice, but out of an unyielding, programmed adherence to protocol and a perceived higher duty to protect the Imperium from the chaotic truths contained within the Quiet Ward. They will be an immovable object, acting with quiet, lethal force if Sariel attempts to breach their mandate.

This canonical explanation is now integrated.

We will now resume the story.

Location: The Chamber of Whispers, Deep Archives, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 2 (XP: 1000/2700), Gold: 10gp, HP: 10/10

You sagged against the rough-hewn stone wall, clutching your bandaged ear, the relentless throb a dull counterpoint to the searing ache behind your eyes. The metallic taste of blood still lingered in your mouth, a visceral reminder of your uncontrolled power, a terrifying echo of the monster your mother had become. The memory of the Nullification Field, the glimpse of its raw, unbridled force, burned like an afterimage in your mind. You had touched the truth, and it had almost unmade you.

Magister Kantos, seeing your lingering distress, hovered anxiously. His ancient face was etched with a profound weariness, the burden of his shared secrets heavy upon him. You pushed yourself upright, swaying slightly, but your posture was rigid with a new, grim purpose. You needed to understand the path ahead, the precise obstacles that lay between you and Sergeant Kaelan.

"The Quiet Ward," you stated, your voice still a little strained, but firm. "You said it was a fortress. Guarded by the Silent Guard. Tell me, Magister, what are its defenses? What do we face? And the Silent Guard... how do we get past them? They were utterly loyal to Raziel. They saw my mother as a Null. They would never have suspected."

Kantos winced, as if the very words caused him pain. He wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting to the massive, warded door that sealed the vault. "The Quiet Ward, Princess, is designed for permanent containment. Its entrance is warded against all known forms of magic, and its corridors are a labyrinth, designed to disorient even the keenest mind. And the Silent Guard..." He shuddered, a visible tremor running through his frail body. "They are Raziel's personal phantoms. Their loyalty, even after his death, is to his final orders, to the Imperium's stability, and to the sanctity of the secrets he buried. They are incorruptible."

He looked at you, his eyes filled with a desperate warning. "They viewed your mother as the pinnacle of martial prowess, yes, a testament to what a 'Null' could achieve through sheer will. They respected her, even revered her, for her victories. But they never saw her true power. They would perceive any attempt to access the Quiet Ward, especially from you, as a direct threat to the Lord Regent's *last standing orders* and to the Imperial safety. They will not speak. They will not warn. They will simply engage with lethal force."
