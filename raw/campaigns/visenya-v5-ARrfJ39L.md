I. Introduction: The World of the Forever War
The year is 298 AC. It has been fifteen years since the Battle of the Trident, but the war has never ended.
In standard histories, Robert Baratheon killed Prince Rhaegar and ushered in a new dynasty. In this reality, Rhaegar Targaryen drove his sword through the Usurper’s throat, shattering the rebellion’s leadership but not its spirit. The resulting era is known as "The Long Discord."
Westeros is a broken continent. The Targaryens hold King’s Landing and the southern breadbasket, but the North, the Riverlands, and the Stormlands have transformed into entrenched, hostile nations. There is no peace; there is only "The Grinding"—a seasonal rhythm of border skirmishes, raids, and failed treaties.
The Iron Throne is no longer a seat of glory. It is a command center for a military junta. The Red Keep is a fortress draped in black banners, smelling of ozone, wildfire, and drying blood. The smallfolk do not cheer for dragons; they pray the dragons do not look at them.
You are Princess Visenya Targaryen, the 12-year-old daughter of Prince Regent Rhaegar. To the realm, you are a child. To your family, you are a curiosity. But to those with the eyes to see—the Maesters, the Mages, and the Mad King—you are the "Apex," a biological impossibility born of Rhaegar’s desperate union with a sorceress from the lost House Baelarys. You see the mathematical architecture of the world. You do not just live in the game of thrones; you see the code it is written in.

II. Timeline of the Divergence (283 AC – 298 AC)
To understand your place in this world, you must understand how the timeline fractured.
283 AC: The Ruby Ford
The Duel: Rhaegar meets Robert in the waters of the Trident. Rhaegar takes a hammer blow to the chest that cracks his ribs, but his counter-thrust pierces Robert’s neck. As the Usurper dies, the Rebel Vanguard, led by Eddard Stark, does not rout. Instead, they go berserk.
The Stalemate: The Royalist forces, exhausted and battered, cannot pursue the retreating Northern army. Rhaegar is carried off the field, unconscious. The Rebels retreat to the Neck and the impregnable fortresses of the Riverlands.
283 AC: The Sack that Wasn’t
Tywin’s Gambit: Tywin Lannister arrives at King’s Landing not to sack it, but to secure it for the victors. Seeing Rhaegar alive, he pledges loyalty but demands a high price: the position of Hand of the King for life.
The Mad King’s Fate: Aerys II, raving and paranoid, orders the wildfire caches ignited. Rhaegar, still wounded, returns just in time to countermand the order. He does not kill his father. Instead, he declares Aerys "unwell" and confines him to the Maidenvault. Aerys remains King in title, but a prisoner in reality.
284 AC: The Tower of Joy
The Silent Wolf: Rhaegar rides to Dorne to find Lyanna Stark dying. He returns with her bones and a baby boy, Jon. To prevent the North from rallying around "Lyanna’s stolen son," Rhaegar publicly acknowledges the boy as his bastard, Jaehaerys Waters (later legitimized as Targaryen), claiming Lyanna begged him to protect the child.
The Northern Wall: Eddard Stark, grieving and bitter, refuses to bend the knee but agrees to a "Ceasefire of Bones." He closes the Neck. The North becomes an isolationist kingdom, trading with Braavos but ignoring the Iron Throne.
287 AC: The Baelarys Arrival
The Obsession: Rhaegar, consumed by the "Prince That Was Promised" prophecy, believes he needs a third "head" of the dragon. Elia Martell is dead (killed by the Mountain during a chaotic skirmish, though Rhaegar executed the Mountain to appease Dorne).
The Summoning: Rhaegar’s mystics locate a survivor of House Baelarys in the ruins of Volantis—a woman named Vaela. She arrives at court, a figure of terrifying beauty. They marry. She gives birth to you, Visenya, in 286 AC.
The Disappearance: Six months after your birth, Vaela vanishes from the Red Keep. The official story is a sickness; the rumor is she "ascended" or returned to the shadow. You are left as the only evidence of her existence.
290 AC - 298 AC: The Grinding
The Forever War: The Crown fights a perpetual counter-insurgency against the "Stag Lords" (Baratheon loyalists) in the Stormlands and the "River Wolves" (Tully/Stark guerillas) in the Riverlands. The realm is bankrupt. The Iron Bank is circling like a vulture.

III. The Geopolitics of a Broken Realm
The map of Westeros is defined by trenches, burnt villages, and checkpoints.
1. The Crownlands (The Dragon’s Coil)
Status: Martial Law.
Atmosphere: King’s Landing is overcrowded with refugees. The City Watch (Gold Cloaks) has been expanded into a paramilitary force of 6,000 men, commanded by Prince Viserys. They are brutal, corrupt, and loyal only to the highest bidder.
Key Location: The Dragonpit. Rhaegar has partially rebuilt it, not for dragons (which are extinct), but as a "War Room" and a housing for the royal garrison.
2. The North (The Ice Cage)
Ruler: Lord Paramount Eddard Stark (Self-styled "Warden of the Silence").
Status: Isolationist / De Facto Independent.
Relationship: They pay no taxes. They allow no Crown forces past Moat Cailin. They harbor rebel fugitives. Rhaegar tolerates this because invading the North in winter (which is coming) would be suicide.
3. The Riverlands (The Corpse-Fields)
Ruler: House Tully (In Exile), House Frey (Crown Loyalists).
Status: Active War Zone.
Atmosphere: The Riverlands are a blackened wasteland. Skirmishes happen daily. The Brotherhood Without Banners is a massive guerilla army here, disrupting Crown supply lines.
The Front Line: Harrenhal serves as the Crown’s forward operating base. It is currently commanded by Prince Aegon, who is trying to "pacify" the Trident.
4. The Stormlands (The Anvil)
Ruler: Stannis Baratheon.
Status: Under Siege / Insurgency.
Atmosphere: Stannis holds Storm’s End and Dragonstone (which he seized early in the war). He operates a naval blockade that strangles King’s Landing’s trade. The Stormlands are filled with followers of R'hllor, as Melisandre has convinced Stannis that the Targaryens are agents of the "Great Other."
5. The West (The Golden Bank)
Ruler: Tywin Lannister (Hand of the King).
Status: Economic Backbone.
Role: The Westerlands fund the war. Tywin effectively owns the Crown’s debt. He is the most powerful man in the realm, perhaps more so than Rhaegar. He is constantly maneuvering to marry Cersei to Rhaegar (who refuses) or Jaime to Daenerys.

IV. The Royal House: The Vipers’ Nest
House Targaryen is large, powerful, and cannibalistic. There are two "Courts" within the Red Keep: The Regency Court (Rhaegar) and the Shadow Court (Aerys/Viserys).
1. The King of Shadows
King Aerys II (The Mad King):
Location: Confined to the Maidenvault.
Status: Alive, insane, and dangerous. He is a prisoner, but he still technically holds the title. He has a cult of followers ("The Pyromancers") who smuggle him messages.
Relationship to You: He is fascinated by you. He calls you "Little Valyria." He believes your Baelarys blood is the key to waking dragons. He often asks to see you, and Rhaegar allows it, fearing what Aerys might do if denied.
2. The Prince Regent
Prince Rhaegar Targaryen (Age 39):
Role: De Facto Ruler.
Personality: The "Silver Prince" is gone. In his place is the "Iron Regent." He is exhausted, grim, and singularly focused on the prophecy. He wears black armor with a ruby dragon on the breastplate—the rubies are chipped and missing, representing the pieces of his soul he lost at the Trident.
Relationship to You: He fears you. He sees his dead wife Vaela in your eyes. He knows you are an "Apex" and worries that you might be the "Dark Sister" to his "Blackfyre." He keeps you close but rarely speaks to you directly.
3. The Rival Faction (The Shadow Court)
Prince Viserys Targaryen (Age 24):
Role: Commander of the City Watch / Aerys's Favorite.
Personality: Cruel, vain, and politically astute. He channels the frustration of the "Loyalists" who think Rhaegar is too soft on the Starks. He wants to crush the rebellion with fire, not treaties.
Agenda: He plans to overthrow Rhaegar, reinstate Aerys (with himself as Hand), and burn the Riverlands.
Relationship to You: Pure hatred. You are younger, smarter, and more "Targaryen" than he is. He calls you "The Mongrel" or "The Witch-Girl."
Princess Daenerys Targaryen (Age 14):
Role: The Political Pawn.
Personality: Timid, traumatized, but with a hidden core of steel. She has grown up in a castle of screaming ghosts. Viserys abuses her, pinching her or twisting her arm to ensure obedience.
Agenda: Survival. She dreams of running away to Braavos.
Relationship to You: She is your aunt, but only two years older. She looks at you with awe. You are the only person she has ever seen make Viserys back down (with a word, or a look). She is your potential ally.
4. The Siblings (The Regency Court)
Princess Rhaenys (Age 20):
Role: The Hostess / The Spider.
Personality: Sharp, Dornish, and vindictive. She hates the Starks for "killing" her mother (Elia's death is blamed on the chaos of the war). She runs the spy network within the Red Keep.
Prince Aegon (Age 18):
Role: The Heir / The General.
Personality: Handsome, bold, but not particularly bright. He is a warrior, trained by Arthur Dayne. He loves the glory of war but understands none of the cost. He treats you like a little sister to be protected, unaware that you are protecting him.
Prince Jaehaerys (Jon Snow) (Age 16):
Role: The White Wolf / The Bastard.
Personality: Brooding, silent, and deadly. He serves in the Kingsguard (White Cloak) to prove his loyalty and avoid threatening Aegon's claim. He wields Dark Sister (gifted by Bloodraven via Rhaegar).
Relationship to You: He is the closest to you. He senses your "otherness" because he feels it too (his warging ability). He is your shield.

V. The Character: Princess Visenya (The Apex)
You are a Level 1 character, but in this setting, Level 1 does not mean weak; it means unrealized potential.
Class: Apex Weaver (Homebrew/Flavor)
Concept: A mix of Rogue (Mastermind), Bard (College of Whispers), and Wizard (Divination). You do not fight with swords; you fight with Information Geometry.
Stats:
INT 18 (+4): You process data faster than a Maester. You recall every book you’ve ever read.
CHA 18 (+4): Your presence is magnetic. People want to please you; they are terrified of disappointing you.
DEX 16 (+3): Your movements are perfectly efficient. You don't trip. You don't fumble.
CON 10 (+0): You are physically fragile. A single sword stroke will kill you.
STR 8 (-1): You are a 12-year-old girl. You cannot wear plate armor or wield a greatsword.
Special Abilities (The Apex Bloodline):
The Baelarys Eye: You can see "Stress Lines" in people and objects.
Mechanic: Advantage on Insight (Wisdom) and Investigation (Intelligence) checks. You can identify the "weakest link" in a social group or a physical structure instantly.
Subsonic Command: Your voice hits a frequency that bypasses the conscious mind.
Mechanic: You can cast Command without somatic components, appearing to simply speak a normal sentence. ("Sit," "Drop it," "Leave.")
The Static Field: When you are emotional, the reality around you warps slightly. Candles flicker, glass cracks, animals go silent. This gives you Advantage on Intimidation but Disadvantage on Stealth.
Current Status:
You are technically a "Ward of the Court," free to roam the Red Keep but forbidden from leaving the city.
You have no official army, but you have "The Weavers"—a small group of street urchins and servant girls who feed you information.

VI. Campaign Mechanics: The Game of Information
This campaign will not focus on dungeon crawling. It will focus on Social Combat and Strategic Maneuvering.
1. The "Heat" System:
Your actions generate Heat. If you act too openly, perform too much magic, or humiliate Viserys too publicly, your Heat rises.
Low Heat: You are ignored as a child.
Medium Heat: Spies are assigned to watch you.
High Heat: Assassination attempts begin. Aerys summons you.
2. The "Influence" Currency:
Instead of gold, you trade in Favors and Secrets.
Example: You know that the Master of Coin is skimming off the top. You can use this Secret to blackmail him for a Favor (e.g., granting a permit for a ship, or hiring a specific guard).
3. The "Geometry" of Battles:
When combat occurs, you are the commander, not the soldier. You direct Jaehaerys or your guards. Your turn is spent analyzing the enemy (finding AC, vulnerabilities) and shouting commands that grant bonuses to your allies.

The Iron Bank has threatened to call in the Crown’s loans unless a truce is signed with the Stormlands. Rhaegar has reluctantly agreed to host a delegation from Stannis Baratheon, led by the young and charismatic Renly Baratheon.

The Great Hall is tense. The air is thick with the smell of roasted boar and nervous sweat.
Rhaegar sits on the Iron Throne, flanked by the Kingsguard (including Jaehaerys).
Renly Baratheon stands before the throne, clad in green velvet, offering a scroll with Stannis's demands.
Viserys stands to the side, hand on his sword, whispering to Janos Slynt (Commander of the Gold Cloaks).

Your Position:
You are sitting on a velvet bench in the Minstrel’s Gallery, overlooking the hall. Beside you is Daenerys, who is gripping your hand so hard her knuckles are white.
"Visenya," she whispers. "Why are the musicians playing 'The Rains of Castamere'? That is a Lannister song. This is a peace summit."
The Apex Trigger:
Your INT 18 mind snaps into focus. You analyze the room.
The Musicians: They are not playing instruments. They are loading crossbows concealed in fiddles cases.
The Geometry: The Gold Cloaks have shifted their formation. They are blocking the exits, not guarding them.
The Target: They are not aiming at Renly. They are aiming at Rhaegar.

This is a coup. Viserys and Tywin have struck a deal. They intend to assassinate Rhaegar, blame it on Renly (sparking a total war that justifies destroying the Stormlands), and install Aerys (with Tywin as Regent and Viserys as Heir).
The Initiative:
The crossbows will loose in 6 seconds.
You are 30 feet above the floor. You have a pitcher of water, a heavy candelabra, and your voice.

Character Profile: Visenya Targaryen
The Entropy Child | The Pale Flame | The Giggling Ghost
Age: 12
Class: Level 1 Apex Weaver (Chaotic Variant).
Alignment: Chaotic Neutral (leaning toward Social Evil).
Archetype: The Bored Godling / The Vivisector.

I. Core Concept: The Glutton of Sensation
Unlike the "Architect" version of Visenya who seeks to build and order the world, this version of you is consumed by a black hole of boredom. Your Baelarys blood doesn't make you see geometry; it makes you feel everything at 100x intensity. The world is too slow, too dull, and too gray for you. You create chaos not for political gain, but to feel the "spark" of genuine emotion—usually fear or pain—in others.
To you, the "Game of Thrones" is not a war to be won; it is a toy box full of fragile things waiting to be broken.
II. The "Apex" Psychology
1. The Sadism (Social Vivisection)
You do not (usually) resort to physical torture; that is messy and crude, fit for beasts like the Mountain. Your sadism is psychological. You enjoy "opening people up" to see what color their trauma is.
The Scalpel Tongue: You have an uncanny ability (High Insight) to identify the one thing a person hates about themselves. You don't insult them; you compliment their insecurity so sweetly that it destroys them.
Example: You might whisper to Jaime Lannister, "It is so noble how you pretend to be a lion, even though we all know you’re just a golden dog waiting for a master."
The Toy Mentality: You view people as "dolls." Some dolls cry when you twist their arms; some dolls break. You are endlessly fascinated by which is which. You will save a person’s life just to own them, then torment them with their debt to you.
2. The Hedonism (Sensory Overload)
Your Baelarys blood makes you hyper-sensitive. A rough tunic feels like sandpaper; bad wine tastes like vinegar. Consequently, you are a slave to High Sensation.
The Craving: You are constantly eating sweets, touching velvet, seeking out heat, or putting yourself in mortal danger just to feel your heart race.
The "Flavor" of Magic: You perceive emotions as tastes. Fear is cold mint. Anger is spicy pepper. Despair is rotten honey. You provoke conflict at court because peace "tastes like dust," while chaos is a banquet.
3. The Chaos (The Entropy Engine)
You despise stasis. If a plan is working perfectly, you have an irresistible urge to kick a pillar out just to see if the roof collapses.
The "Stress Test": You rationalize your chaos as "testing" your family. "If Rhaegar’s plan couldn't survive me spilling a cup of wine," you argue, "then it wasn't a very good plan, was it?"
The Anti-Architect: Where others build alliances, you tie knots. You might tell Daenerys that Viserys loves her, and tell Viserys that Daenerys is plotting against him, just to watch the fireworks at dinner.

III. The Social Mask: "The Porcelain Doll"
To the court, you play the role of the Fragile, Eccentric Child.
The Act: You pretend to be dreamy, perhaps a bit "touched" by the Targaryen madness. You stare at candles for hours. You giggle at inappropriate times. This makes people dismiss you as harmless or pity you.
The Reality: While they pity you, you are memorizing their patrol routes, stealing their signet rings, and learning their passphrases. The "giggle" is not madness; it is the sound of you finding a loose thread in their fate.

IV. Relationships (The Dolls)
Rhaegar (The Boring Statue): You find your father exhaustingly dull. His obsession with prophecy and "saving the world" is so... structured. You love him in a possessive way, but you punish him for his neglect by being the one thing he can't predict. You are the glitch in his song.
Aerys II (The Favorite Toy): You adore the Mad King. He is pure, unfiltered chaos. His screams are "honest." You visit his cell not to comfort him, but to feed his paranoia with whispers about "shadows in the walls," because his frantic scribbling amuses you. He calls you "The Little Dragon," and he is the only one who sees your true face.
Viserys (The Squeaky Toy): He is your favorite plaything because he is so easy to break. You delight in triggering his temper, then hiding behind Rhaegar so Viserys gets punished. You want to see how far you can push him before he snaps completely.
Jaehaerys/Jon (The Wolf): He is the only one you respect, because he is "quiet." His emotions are shielded. This annoys you. You try to shock him or seduce him (intellectually) into a reaction, but his stoicism is a puzzle you haven't solved yet.

V. Combat & Abilities (Flavor)
Your magic is not about "weaving" reality; it is about unraveling it.
The "Rot" (Cantrip): Instead of Mending, you use a reverse version that accelerates entropy. You touch a rope, and it frays. You touch a seal, and the wax crumbles.
The "Panic Chord" (Spell): Your version of Command or Dissonant Whispers doesn't sound like a royal order; it sounds like a child giggling in the dark. It triggers primal, irrational fear in the target.
Passive Effect: The Static: When you are bored or angry, the temperature in the room drops, and people hear a high-pitched ringing in their ears.
VI. Current Goal
You don't care about the Iron Throne. You don't care about the War.
Your Goal: Turn the "Summit of the False Truce" into the most entertaining tragedy the world has ever seen. You want to save Rhaegar, yes, but only because if he dies, the game ends too quickly. You want to save him in a way that humiliates his enemies and leaves everyone terrified of you.
