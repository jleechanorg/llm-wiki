The low, guttural cries of the Stonehand parents, their raw grief echoing Gladius’s chilling act, were a symphony to his ears. Their child, a limp, lifeless weight on the floor, was the ultimate proof of their shattered defiance. Their wills, once stubborn, were now utterly broken, leaving them exposed to his final, exquisite torment. A cold, profound satisfaction settled in Gladius’s gut, a deep hum of absolute dominance. This was the pinnacle of his art: the complete erasure of lives, preceded by the unraveling of their very spirit.

He moved with a fluid, unhurried grace, the Silent Strangler’s Cord a dark, ready presence in his hand. The parents, bound and helpless, could only watch, their tear-filled eyes wide with a terror that transcended the physical. Gladius knelt first beside the father, his imposing shadow falling over the man's contorted face. There was no hesitation, only precise, brutal efficiency. The cord slid over the throat, thin and precise. A single, powerful pull, a silent, desperate struggle quickly quelled. The body stiffened, then went limp, the breath halting, life extinguished. He held it for a moment, the warmth fading, ensuring absolute stillness before gently lowering the body back to the stone floor.

He then moved to the woman. Her eyes, already hollowed by grief, met his. A flicker of raw, primal despair, a silent scream of agony, passed between them. But it was too late. The cord tightened, swift and final. Her body spasmed, a last, futile thrashing against the inevitable, then sagged, utterly still. The silence that followed was absolute, broken only by the faint crackle of the dying hearth fire and the distant, increasingly active sounds of the waking village.

Gladius straightened, the cord still in his hand, its recent use a warm impression. Nine souls, all accounted for. The Stonehand family, now a silent, inert tableau of his chilling work. The dawn, painting vibrant oranges and purples on the eastern sky, illuminated their utter defeat. The contract was complete. And the exquisite, perverse pleasure of having brought these seemingly strong-backed, defiant people to such an abject, humiliating end, was a potent indulgence, a visceral reminder of his own unyielding power.
