The Legate is bound and slumped against a damp limestone wall, his expensive subarmalis matted with the dust of the highway. "The silk of your litter is a world away, Legate," you say, your voice a resonant, calm anchor that vibrates through the cave. You step into his personal space, your Subtle Lorica absorbing the dim torchlight as you loom over him. You do not invoke the name Caesar; instead, you let the heat of your divine blood radiate from your skin, a physical reminder of a status that sits leagues above his own. "You were a Tribune once. You know the weight of the blood that flows in my veins. You know that I am the high-born of a truth that predates your master's laws."

Glaber shudders, his breath hitching as he looks at the thirty Praetorians—his own elite guard—now standing as silent sentinels for you. The superstitious terror finally breaks his remaining Roman discipline. He bows his head as much as his bonds allow, his voice a dry, defeated rasp. "I... I saw the Sun on the peaks. I heard the thunder of a god. We were told you were a specialist, a noble of minor house seeking glory... but no minor house carries the fire of Venus in its marrow."

He looks up, his eyes watering. "The Audit Teams are already moving from Capua. They seek the signal that never came. My correspondence... the ledger you took... it contains the names of the Senators who fund the expansion. But Glaber is merely a hand, noble. The heart is in the City. Octavian has ordered your retrieval at any cost. He does not wish for your head; he wishes for your submission to the new Order."

You stand tall, the Aegis of Julius reflecting a faint, golden glow. The Legate has surrendered the clinical data you required, not because of the lash, but because he cannot fathom opposing a creature he believes to be favored by the gods. The hunter has truly become the prey, and the storm you've gathered is only beginning to swell.
