You turned from your companions, their expressions a mix of grim determination and unwavering loyalty. Your words, sharp and precise, had solidified their roles in securing the terrifying knowledge you now possessed. Magister Kantos had already begun to turn his attention to the journals, his hands, though still trembling, moving with a new, meticulous purpose.

"Magister," you commanded, your voice cutting through the low hum of preparation. Your gaze fixed on the forbidden journals, then swept to the pale, anxious face of Kantos. "The final step. Once their contents are fully copied, cataloged, and partitioned into their new carriers, these originals must be utterly, irrevocably *unmade*. No trace. No magical echo. No physical residue that could ever be found, even by Raziel's ghosts." You leaned forward, your Starlit Eyes burning with a cold, absolute resolve. "What are the most effective and undetectable methods for destroying knowledge so profoundly, that it ceases to exist? Not just burned, but *unmade*?"

Kantos visibly flinched at the chilling precision of your demand. He wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting from your unwavering face to the ancient journals on the table, as if their very essence protested the coming annihilation. He understood. This was not a request for a simple bonfire; it was a demand for conceptual erasure.

He sighed, a dry, papery sound, then began to speak, his scholarly instincts asserting themselves even over his profound fear. "Princess," he rasped, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper, "the truly absolute methods are... terrifying. The Whisper-Binders speak of a 'Silence-Flame,' a rare Sylvan alchemical fire that consumes not just matter, but its very memory from the Weave, leaving only cold ash. Or, the Glimmering Combine once theorized a 'Void-Sphere'—a contained implosion that unmakes matter itself, reducing it to nothingness, but such devices are unstable and exist only in theory." He paused, his gaze fixed on you with a profound, sorrowful understanding. "These are acts of unmaking, Princess. They leave no trace, but they are acts that belong to a… different kind of power. A power that tastes of oblivion."

You absorbed his words, your mind coldly assessing the grim possibilities. The "Silence-Flame" or the theoretical "Void-Sphere"—both terrifying, both absolute. This was the final unmaking, the ultimate act of control over a truth too dangerous to exist in its original form.

"Magister," you commanded, your voice steady and precise, cutting through the heavy silence of the study. You gestured to the ancient journals. "Your knowledge of these methods is paramount. Once the copying is complete, you will oversee their utter destruction. You will determine the safest, most undetectable method, whether it is the Silence-Flame or a prototype Void-Sphere, and you will initiate it."

Your gaze then swept to Ser Gideon Vance (Level 5 Fighter, Age 25), who stood sentinel, his unyielding presence a silent promise of protection. "Ser Gideon," you continued, "you will be the enforcer of this final act. Your absolute loyalty. Your unwavering discipline. You will secure the area. You will ensure no one interferes. And you will bear witness to its completion. This is a sacred duty. No trace. Nothing to find. Understood?"

Gideon, his face grim but resolute, gave a single, curt nod. "Understood, My Lady. It will be done."

Kantos, though pale, nodded slowly, his hands already moving towards the copying instruments on the table, his scholarly horror warring with the profound, unquestioning obedience to your chilling authority. The air in the study, thick with the scent of dust and fear, now hummed with the silent, terrible promise of a final, complete unmaking.
