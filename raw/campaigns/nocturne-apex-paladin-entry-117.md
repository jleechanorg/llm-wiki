The farmhouse lay silent behind her, its secrets now plundered, its inhabitants secured. Her message to Kaelen Flinn, demanding double payment and transport, was sent, its coded arrogance a gamble on his fear and his syndicate's desire for clean results. But waiting, idle, was anathema to her nature.

Her mind, a relentless engine of strategic thought, disdained passivity. While Kaelen formulated his response, valuable time could be utilized. The unexpected layers of security – the road patrol, the farm guards, the arcane ward – confirmed a broader network. She had neutralized what she found, but a true Apex Predator ensured all variables were accounted for. More reconnaissance was warranted. The silent landscape around Oakhaven beckoned, promising no more surprises.

She moved with a fluid, silent grace, her Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate blending with the deepening gloom. Her route took her in a wide circle around the perimeter of Oakhaven, extending further than her initial approach, encompassing the approaches from the south and north, away from the concealed captives. Her senses were acutely tuned, her intellect searching for any lingering signs of organized presence beyond the cleared path. The wind whispered through the fields, carrying only the scent of damp earth and distant livestock. No metallic glint, no disciplined footsteps, no arcane hum. The night was quiet, deceptively so.

The patrol roster she'd acquired suggested a shift change, a routine that she had now utterly disrupted. Any subsequent patrols would be delayed, likely confused. The area was, for now, truly clear. A cold satisfaction settled over her. The silence she commanded was absolute. The mission continued, every second a calculated move.
