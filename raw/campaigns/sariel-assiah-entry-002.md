The silence in your chambers is absolute. It is a heavy, suffocating thing, a stark contrast to the storm of memory and guilt raging in your mind. The events of yesterday—the frantic search, the valley of screaming children, your own pain-filled cry for help, and your mother… the sight of your mother collapsing, her light extinguished—it all plays on a continuous, brutal loop.

It was my fault. The thought is a cold, hard stone in your stomach. I broke. I told him her secret. He used it. She died because of me.

You sit on the edge of your bed, still in the simple, dark mourning robes they put you in last night. Your left ear throbs with a dull ache beneath its new bandages. It is a constant, physical reminder of your failure.

The heavy oak door to your suite opens without a knock.

It is Cassian. Your brother’s face is a thundercloud of grief and raw fury. He doesn’t look at you, his eyes sweeping the room as if searching for an enemy to fight. He is dressed in the black and silver of the Starfall Guard, his sword already at his hip.

"The Guard is being mobilized," he says, his voice tight and strained. "Uncle Titus is leading a punitive campaign against Mordan's entire clan. We will burn them from the history books."

He finally looks at you, and the grief in his eyes is eclipsed by a cold, sharp shard of accusation. "Mother died saving children. It was a hero's death. The *only* death she would have accepted."

He takes a step closer, his voice dropping to a low, dangerous whisper. "But she should never have been in that valley. She should never have had to make that choice. The greatest commander in the world should not have died because her daughter, a scholar who was supposed to be safe in a library, got herself captured."

He doesn't need to say the words. You are the reason she is dead.
