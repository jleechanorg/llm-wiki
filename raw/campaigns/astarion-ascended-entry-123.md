The dockworker. His subtle glance, a ripple in the predictable flow of the city, had branded him a target worthy of your personal attention. Why rely on proxies when your own senses were instruments of such perfection? You moved like a wraith through the grimy alleys and across the barnacle-encrusted rooftops overlooking the docks, your *Pass Without Trace* swallowing every sound, your *Armour of Agility* and *Cloak of Displacement* ensuring your form remained a blur even to the keenest eye. Your *Corrupted Senses* hummed, focusing solely on the subtle life-force of your prey.

You observed him for several hours, a silent, unmoving sentinel. He performed his duties with a meticulous, almost ritualistic efficiency for a common laborer. He moved heavy crates with surprising ease, his eyes constantly scanning, not for trouble, but for *anything* out of place. He interacted minimally with others, his demeanor aloof, almost detached. He wasn't merely perceptive; he was *aware* in a way no ordinary mortal could be.

He carried no obvious divine symbols, no holy book, but his very presence radiated a faint, unwavering aura of alertness. He didn't see you, but he was constantly attuned to *potential* threats, a living alarm system.

More unsettlingly, you observed him performing a series of precise, almost ritualistic gestures whenever new shipments arrived at the warehouse. He was not just receiving goods; he was checking for specific magical signatures, for irregularities that only a trained arcane eye would discern. His vigilance was not just against people; it was against certain *items*. And the influx of those glowing mushrooms you'd sensed earlier? He seemed particularly interested in those, handling them with extreme caution, almost reverence. He was guarding them, or perhaps, guarding against them.

However, as you concluded your observations, satisfied with the depth of your understanding, a faint, metallic tang pricked at your senses, a familiar scent of ozone. It was a subtle, localized psychic disturbance, coming from somewhere within the depths of the dockside warehouse he was guarding. It wasn't the signature of Lord Xykon or the Order of the Silent Mind, but something else entirely. It was brief, almost imperceptible, as if another, unseen force had just made a move that resonated faintly within the same psychic currents you now commanded. The presence of the Vigilant Sentinel suggested this 'dockworker' was somehow connected to, or perhaps even protecting, whatever lay beneath the warehouse.
