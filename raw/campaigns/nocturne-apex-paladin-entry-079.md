Location: Man Tibbet's Farm, Near Root Cellar, Early Evening

The air was still, a canvas of quiet domesticity stretched over the undercurrent of latent threat. Nocturne (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) moved with the deceptive grace of a shadow, her Masterwork Shadow-Weave Half Plate whispering faintly as she traversed the moonlit yard. The first guard's silent demise was a testament to her chilling efficiency, a variable now perfectly controlled. Her focus, sharp and unwavering, shifted to the second sentinel, an obstacle that demanded a more nuanced, yet equally decisive, removal. The initial bell lure, while effective, was a spent gambit. A fresh approach was required.

Her gaze, devoid of any sentiment, fixed on the small, isolated outbuilding – the root cellar – its unlatched window a tantalizing vulnerability. This was the perfect stage for her new lure. It had to be subtle, yet compelling; a whisper of intrusion, not an outright alarm. She needed to draw him out, into the waiting darkness, without disturbing the unaware family within the farmhouse.

Nocturne glided to the root cellar, her movements economical, her body a fluid extension of her intellect. She produced her masterwork dagger, its blade a sliver of darkness in the gloom. With surgical precision, she used the tip to create a faint, intermittent scratching sound against the rough wood of the window frame, just audible enough to pique a professional guard's ear. As she made the sound, she simultaneously, fleetingly, exposed the barest sliver of the small silver locket she had taken from Elara, allowing a glint of reflected moonlight to flash through the gap, mimicking a quick, furtive movement within. Her hands moved with unerring accuracy, a testament to her honed coordination.

The sound, a minute, almost imperceptible scritch-scritch, ceased. The light, a momentary phantom, vanished. Then silence. Nocturne waited, melting back into the deeper shadows of the outbuilding, her presence utterly concealed. Her mind, a tactical engine, mapped the farmhouse interior, anticipating the remaining guard's reaction. A tense minute passed, stretched taut by the thrumming arcane ward near the barn.

Then, a subtle shift. The faint light from a farmhouse window, previously steady, flickered slightly, a brief shadow passing before it. A muffled murmur, a low, questioning voice. Then, the almost imperceptible creak of the farmhouse door. Footsteps. Slow, deliberate, heading directly towards the root cellar.

The second internal guard was taking the bait.

Nocturne's eyes narrowed further, a cold satisfaction tightening her jaw. The hunt was nearing its climax. The efficiency of her planning was paying dividends. Now, only the final, precise strike remained.
