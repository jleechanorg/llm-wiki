He stops inches from you, his gaze searching yours with a clinical intensity. 'The sea cave is a masterstroke of observation. The pirates are a variable we can buy; the Thracians are a problem we can now solve. You have shown me that you understand the most important lesson of command: that the mind must always sharpen the blade before it strikes.'

He places a hand on your shoulder—a rare gesture of physical approval that feels both heavy and intoxicating. 'The slaves believe they have found a sanctuary in the dark. We shall ensure it becomes their final rest. Tell me, son... how would you have us proceed? The gold of Rome is at our disposal, as is the steel of my legions.'
