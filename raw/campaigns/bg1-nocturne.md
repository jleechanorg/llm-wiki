## Summary

A Baldur's Gate 1 prequel campaign featuring Nocturne, a Level 1 Ranger (Gloom Stalker archetype) who is actually a Bhaalspawn — a child of the dead god of murder. The campaign opens at Candlekeep library where Nocturne, a 16-year-old hiding her true predatory nature behind the mask of a dutiful ward, is ambushed by an assassin named Shank sent to kill her before she can leave the library's protection. After killing the assassin and satisfying her "Hunger" mechanic (a compulsion to kill sapient beings for stat bonuses), she escapes into the world with Gorion, only to be ambushed again on the Lion's Way by Sarevok and ogres — where Gorion is killed. Nocturne escapes alone and meets Roric, a pragmatic rogue, forming an alliance as they travel toward the Friendly Arm Inn. The campaign uses D&D 5e with a custom "Hunger" mechanic that grants +2 to all stats when satiated (kill) and -2 when in withdrawal.

## Key Claims

- Nocturne is a Bhaalspawn with a compulsion to kill sapient humanoids; the Hunger grants +2 stats for 7 days after a kill, -2 stats if denied for 14+ days
- Gorion is a Level 9 Wizard and Harper who knows about Nocturne's true nature and is trying to protect her from those who sent assassins
- Sarevok is the armored figure leading the ambush; he breaks Gorion like nothing, establishing him as far beyond Level 1 threat
- The Iron Crisis on the Sword Coast (grey Brittle iron from mines) is creating a power vacuum that Nocturne intends to exploit
- Roric is a pragmatic, competence-respecting rogue who travels with Nocturne toward the Friendly Arm Inn to find Jaheira and Khalid
