Consider these abilities Karsus wearing the Crown of Karsus should be an apocalyptic-tier threat in 5e, reflecting his status as the most powerful mortal arcanist in D&D history. The crown grants dominion over himself to remain his own entity apart from the Weave, allowing him to wield magic independently of Mystra's control.[1][2]

## Core Crown Abilities

**Weave Independence**: The crown makes Karsus immune to magical effects that would shut down his spellcasting (antimagic field, silence, counterspell). He can cast spells even in dead magic zones, functioning as his own conduit for magical power.[2][3][4]

**Legendary Resistance**: 5/day minimum, potentially unlimited for spells 5th level or lower. As CR 58 equivalent from 3.5e, he needs robust defenses against save-or-die effects.[1]

**Magical Knowledge Absorption**: Whenever Karsus sees a spell cast within 120 feet, he can use his reaction to learn it permanently. This should have limitations (perhaps once per spell, or require the spell to be on the wizard list) but makes him terrifyingly adaptive.[2]

**Enhanced Spellcasting**: The crown should grant several epic-tier benefits:
- Access to 10th and 11th level spell slots (2 and 1 respectively)[1]
- +3 to spell save DC and spell attack rolls[1]
- Advantage on concentration checks
- Can cast two leveled spells per turn as an action and bonus action[1]

## Combat Capabilities

**Actions**: Multiattack with two spells per turn, reflecting his mastery of counterspelling, shaping, and elements from his 3.5e archmage abilities. He should have lair actions every initiative count 20, and legendary actions (3-5 per round) allowing him to cast cantrips, counterspell, or teleport.[1]

**Epic Spell Access**: Karsus should have 5-7 uses of custom epic spells per day with devastating effects. Think reality-bending magic like mass disintegration, temporal manipulation, or creating pocket demiplanes mid-combat.[1]

**AC and HP**: AC should be 20-23 (robes, shield spell, defensive magic items) with 600-800 HP to reflect his epic-level constitution and magical enhancements. He used wish spells to increase all ability scores by +5 in 3.5e.[1]

## Weaknesses

The crown's power creates exploitable vulnerabilities. In BG3 lore, control gems can be used to dominate the crown's wearer, and the Karsite Weave it channels tries to consume all magic including the wielder. Consider giving players a quest to acquire these gems or find a way to sever his connection to the crown before the final confrontation.[3][5]
