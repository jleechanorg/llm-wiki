The soft lamplight cast long shadows across Sariel Arcanus's private study within the Zenith Spire. Scrolls on ancient history lay unfurled across her desk, intermingled with intricate star charts. Magister Lucian Kantos, frail and hunched, peered nearsightedly at a faded text, his ink-stained fingers tracing the archaic script. Lady Cressida Valeriana sat nearby, her usual sharp wit muted, observing the quiet scene with a rare stillness. Ser Gideon Vance, as ever, was a silent sentinel by the heavy oak door, his presence a constant, reassuring weight.

The fragile peace shattered with a sudden, sharp rap, followed by the door swinging inward before Gideon could even move. Prince Cassian Arcanus strode in, his face a thundercloud of raw, barely contained fury. Prince Darius val Artorius followed, his expression cold and analytical, his gaze sweeping over the study, lingering with subtle disdain on Lucian Kantos.

"Sister!" Cassian roared, his voice echoing off the high ceilings. "Are you mad? Or merely incompetent? Imperial protocol dictates that any Starfall Guard member placed in the Quiet Ward for observation is to remain under strict, solitary confinement! Yet, I have received reports that Sergeant Kaelan has been moved to a private suite, with daily visits from... this man!" He gestured accusingly at Lucian Kantos, his lip curling. "A known heretic! What is the meaning of this insubordination?"

Darius stepped forward, his tone lacking his brother's heat but carrying a chilling weight of authority. "Your actions create a profound security risk, Sariel. You are circumventing established procedures, expending Imperial resources, and exposing a sensitive asset to a questionable influence. Your grief is understood, but your judgment, it seems, remains... clouded."

Sariel felt the familiar tightening in her chest, the twin assault of Cassian's raging accusation and Darius's icy condemnation. Her hand instinctively went to the scar behind her ear, a physical reminder of the weakness they so readily perceived.

Cressida, ever quick, interjected with a polite smile that held a razor's edge. "My Princes, with all due respect, Lady Sariel is conducting a sensitive scholarly inquiry under the Lord Regent's broad mandate. Magister Kantos's expertise is invaluable, and Sergeant Kaelan's comfort directly aids his... recovery."

Gareth Ashfeld, who had been quietly organizing scrolls in a corner, flinched at the tension, his hand hovering over a stack of fragile parchment. He glanced at Sariel, his kind eyes filled with quiet worry.

Sariel, however, remained outwardly still, her face a mask of mild confusion, her gaze lowered to the floor. Inside, her mind was a whirlwind.
