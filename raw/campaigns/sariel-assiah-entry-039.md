You pulled the first leather-bound journal from the obsidian shelf. Its weight felt immense in your hands, not just from the aged parchment within, but from the centuries of suppressed truth it contained. The air in the deep vault was unnaturally cold, dry, and silent, amplifying the soft rustle of pages as you opened the tome. Magister Kantos stood by, his lantern casting long, dancing shadows, his ancient face a map of the fear you now understood.

The script was archaic, a dense, technical language from the Age of Dominion, but your mind, honed by years of deciphering forgotten lore, consumed it with chilling speed. This was not a history of heroes, but a scientific log. The Seraphim, the journal detailed, had attempted to engineer beings of pure order, immune to the chaotic fluctuations of the Weave. The "Null Genesis Project" was their answer – a grand, ambitious attempt to create life that could not wield magic, and thus could not be corrupted by it.

But the project had produced "failures." Beings, like your mother, who did not merely lack magic, but actively *silenced* it. You read of initial confusion, then horror, as these "Nulls" displayed an "adaptive resonance" – a terrifying capacity to turn their magical void into a source, a perfect, internal anti-magic field. It spoke of them as "anomalies," "unpredictable variables," and sometimes, in hushed, almost fearful tones, as "Joyful Predators" who thrived in the very act of unmaking.

Your breath hitched. The words blurred for a moment, then snapped back into focus with terrifying clarity. This wasn't just your mother's secret; it was the blueprint of a power, a nature, you never knew existed. A power born from a lie, a predator forged from a flaw. The cold, intellectual satisfaction of discovery warred with a profound, dawning horror. You gripped the journal tighter, your knuckles white, the silence of the vault broken only by the frantic hammering of your own heart.
