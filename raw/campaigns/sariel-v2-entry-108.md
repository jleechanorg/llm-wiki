Kaelan’s eyes, wide and unblinking, suddenly darted, not with terror, but with a flicker of something… distant. The frantic gibbering slowed, replaced by a low, almost normal groan. His body, previously rigid, seemed to relax by a fraction. A single, silent tear, hot and thick, traced a path down his gaunt cheek. He didn't speak, but the subtle tremor in his hand, clasped in yours, intensified. He was searching. For the first time, his tormented mind seemed to be trying to remember something *other* than the horror.

"Rowan!" you commanded, your voice sharp with urgency, cutting through the profound silence that had fallen over the cell. You didn't look away from Kaelan, your Starlit Eyes fixed on his, willing him to remember, to find that fragile piece of hope. "Now! He's searching. He's vulnerable. Focus your healing. Not just his body, Rowan, but his mind. Soothe the chaos, but keep the pathway open. Ease his pain, guide his thoughts. He needs to find that memory. We need him to remember."

Rowan Thorne (Level 3 Expert, Age 16), his gentle face etched with a mix of awe and profound concern, immediately knelt closer to Kaelan. His hands, already practiced at soothing physical wounds, now moved with an almost spiritual grace over the Sergeant's brow. He murmured softly, a low, comforting chant of simple kindness and reassurance, mixing his herbal poultices with whispered promises of peace. His touch was a gentle balm on Kaelan's exposed psyche, a counterpoint to the sharp, cold precision of your words. Gideon (Level 5 Fighter, Age 25) stood sentinel, his hand now hovering over his sword, his gaze fixed intently on Kaelan. He was ready for any sudden shift, but his own presence was an anchor, a silent promise of protection that permeated the cell. Cressida (Level 4 Expert, Age 16) watched, her sharp eyes taking in every detail, her expression a mix of awe at your audacity and a dawning understanding of the immense, subtle power you wielded. Magister Kantos, pale and trembling by the door, simply stared, witnessing a profound violation of both scholarly and natural law, a truth more terrifying than any he had read in his forbidden texts.

Kaelan’s eyes, fixed on the ceiling, suddenly seemed to… see. A flicker of something akin to recognition, or perhaps just a profound, overwhelming confusion, passed through their depths. His body relaxed further, the chains rattling softly as he ceased to thrash. The low moaning faded to a soft, almost imperceptible breath. And then, slowly, agonizingly, a single, hoarse word, heavy with pain and memory, escaped his lips:

"...Lady?"

The word, a fragile echo of sanity, brought a surge of desperate hope to your heart. It was a confirmation. She *had* visited him. You pressed your hand more firmly onto his, your voice dropping to a low, insistent whisper, a lifeline into the depths of his madness. "Yes, Sergeant. My Lady. Your commander. My mother. Alexiel."

You leaned in, your Starlit Eyes burning with conviction, willing him to remember. "She came to you, didn't she? After the battle. After the Plaza of the Silent Throne. When the world thought she was celebrating, when she was solidifying her legend... she came here. To mourn you. To mourn the Starfall Guard. She didn't abandon you, Kaelan. She couldn't." You poured every ounce of your will into the unspoken words, trying to re-contextualize his core trauma, to heal the wound of betrayal. "Try to remember, Sergeant. Remember her visit. Remember what she said. Remember her grief. She was not unmoved. She was heartbroken."

Kaelan’s breathing hitched. His eyes, wide and unblinking, suddenly filled with unshed tears, mirroring the one that had already fallen. A profound, shuddering sigh escaped him, a sound of immense, terrible sorrow, but also of a dawning, fragile peace. The tension in his body seemed to drain away completely, and his hand, clasped in yours, trembled no more. The moans ceased. He was silent. For the first time in years, Sergeant Kaelan was still. And then, a memory, fragile and luminous, seemed to form in the depths of his eyes.

You felt a surge of cold, grim satisfaction. This was it. The moment. His mind, soothed by Rowan’s gentle touch and your insistent words, was open, searching for a truth other than horror. You leaned closer, closing your eyes, pushing your nascent Mirror Magic outwards, not to impose, but to *reflect*. You sought the precise vision of that visit, the true encounter between your mother and the last witness to her deepest secret.

Your mind plunged into Kaelan’s consciousness, but this time, it was a profound, almost dreamlike experience. Gideon’s unwavering will was a solid, reassuring anchor, a beacon that allowed you to navigate the shifting landscape of Kaelan’s mind without being consumed. The raw horror of the Starfall Gambit, the chaos and the betrayal, still pulsed in the background, but now, it was a distant echo, like thunder behind a closed door. Your will pulled you past it, towards the fragile, luminous memory you had sought.

You *saw* it.

The cell was dark. Kaelan lay chained to his slab, his mind already beginning to fray, the spectral whispers of madness gathering at the edges of his sanity. The heavy iron door groaned open. And then, she entered. Your mother. Alexiel.

She was not in her armor, but in simple, dark traveling clothes. Her movements were slow, burdened. Her face, in the dim light of the single lantern she carried, was not the cold, terrifying mask of the "unmoved" predator. It was etched with a profound, raw grief. Her eyes, your mother's Unseeing Eyes, were not burning with cold clarity, but were red-rimmed and swollen from weeping. She walked to his slab, her footsteps heavy, and simply knelt.

She reached out, her hand hovering over his brow, a gesture of deep, agonizing sorrow. You saw her mourn. You felt her grief, a silent, crushing weight that echoed your own. She placed a trembling hand on his forehead, and whispered, her voice raw with a pain you had never heard from her before, "Sergeant. My brave, foolish Sergeant. You saw it all, didn't you? You saw the lie. You died for it."

You saw Kaelan’s eyes, wide and unblinking, attempting to focus on her. You saw the flicker of betrayal, the dawning horror of what he had witnessed. And you saw your mother flinch, as if his very gaze was a condemnation.

She pulled her hand away. Her expression hardened then, a familiar mask of cold composure settling over her features, but you saw the effort it took, the pain beneath it. You saw her fight the grief, force it down, sheath it in absolute control. You saw her remember the "Joyful Predator" within, not with glee, but with a chilling, tactical necessity. She had to be unmoved. She had to be that to win. She had to be that to survive.

You saw her rise, her posture straightening, the grief carefully locked away. She looked at Kaelan one last time, her eyes filled with a profound sorrow, a silent apology for the truth that had shattered him. Then she turned and walked from the cell, the heavy door closing behind her, leaving Kaelan to his madness and his burden. She had not abandoned him. She had chosen to bear his trauma as a silent, invisible weight, to ensure the lie held for the sake of the Imperium, and for her children. Her grief was real. Her mask was real. And the cost was absolute.

You tore your hand away from Kaelan’s, gasping. The psychic strain was immense, the metallic taste of blood returning to your mouth, but Gideon's anchor held. You swayed, almost collapsing, but Gideon's strong hand on your shoulder kept you upright. The raw horror of Kaelan's trauma, the crushing weight of your mother's hidden grief, and the agonizing contradiction of her "unmoved" persona, now all burned within you. You had seen it. You had the truth.

Kaelan lay still, his moans silenced, a profound peace settling over his tormented features. The single tear on his cheek was dry now. He had found his memory. He had found his answer. His mind was still broken, but the torment had ceased.
