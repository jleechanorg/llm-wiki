I understand this to mean:

*   **Maintain Core Traits:** Valerius should still be defined by his cold analysis, pragmatic approach, and emotional repression. This is central to his character and his contrast with Cassian and Sariel.
*   **Subtle Emotional Cues:** His detachment should feel like a deeply ingrained defense mechanism or a conscious choice to suppress emotion, rather than a complete absence of internal feeling. This means incorporating subtle physical tells (e.g., a tight jaw, a flicker in his eyes, a barely perceptible tremor of a hand on a data-slate) or nuances in his tone that hint at a repressed emotional state beneath the logical exterior.
*   **Refine Dialogue:** His analytical statements should be precise, but perhaps less overtly blunt or devoid of all human cadence. He might still use terms like "inefficient," but the delivery should suggest a deep-seated frustration or disappointment he struggles to articulate emotionally, rather than a simple, clinical observation.

I will apply this adjustment immediately to future portrayals of Valerius. My goal is for his coldness to feel more like a heavy, internalized burden, a consequence of his grief and worldview, rather than a simple lack of empathy.
