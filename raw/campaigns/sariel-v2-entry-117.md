You tore your hand away from Kaelan’s, gasping. The psychic strain was immense, the metallic taste of blood returning to your mouth, but Gideon's anchor held. You swayed, almost collapsing, but Gideon's strong hand on your shoulder kept you upright. The raw horror of Kaelan's trauma, the crushing weight of your mother's hidden grief, and the agonizing contradiction of her "unmoved" persona, now all burned within you. You had seen it. You had the truth.

Kaelan lay still, his moans silenced, a profound peace settling over his tormented features. The single tear on his cheek was dry now. He had found his memory. He had found his answer. His mind is still broken, but the torment had ceased.

You ignored the tremor in your own body, the faint metallic tang of blood in your mouth. Kaelan’s newfound peace, however fragile, was a miracle. His eyes, though still wide and unblinking, no longer held the same unseeing terror. He had processed the new truth, the paradox of the hero who grieved. You knelt closer again, your hand gently cupping his cheek, your voice soft and insistent, a lifeline into his quieted madness.

"Sergeant Kaelan," you whispered, your Starlit Eyes fixed on his, willing him to understand. "It was not a betrayal. She mourned you. She mourned the Starfall Guard. But you also saw the agony of the cost etched into her soul. She *chose* that burden, Sergeant. She chose to bear the weight of your perceived betrayal, to keep the truth hidden for the sake of the Imperium, for her family, for the very peace you died to win."

You saw a subtle flicker in his eyes, a faint, almost imperceptible shift in his gaze that seemed to follow your words, as if a new pathway was being forged in his shattered mind. The profound peace on his face deepened, losing its previous blankness and gaining a subtle, almost melancholic, expression. His lips, dry and cracked, slowly, agonizingly, formed a single, soft, almost human sound: "...My Lady..." It was a whisper of understanding, of acceptance. He was still lost in the labyrinth of his mind, but the endless, screaming loop of terror and betrayal had finally broken.

"Rowan," you commanded, your voice sharp with renewed urgency, your gaze still fixed on Kaelan. "His torment has ceased. His mind has found a measure of peace. Focus all your healing. Not just his body, Rowan, but his mind. Soothe the chaos, but keep the pathway open. Ease his pain, guide his thoughts. He needs to find that memory. We need him to remember."

Rowan Thorne (Level 3 Expert, Age 16), his gentle face etched with a mix of awe and profound concern, immediately knelt closer to Kaelan. He was ready for any sudden shift, but his own presence was an anchor, a silent promise of protection that permeated the cell. Magister Kantos, pale and trembling by the door, simply stared, witnessing a profound violation of both scholarly and natural law, a truth more terrifying than any he had read in his forbidden texts.

Kaelan’s eyes, fixed on the ceiling, suddenly seemed to… see. A flicker of something akin to recognition, or perhaps just a profound, overwhelming confusion, passed through their depths. His body relaxed further, the chains rattling softly as he ceased to thrash. The low moaning faded to a soft, almost imperceptible breath. And then, slowly, agonizingly, a single, hoarse word, heavy with pain and memory, escaped his lips:

"...Lady?"

It was a whisper, filled with a fragile, dawning comprehension. A flicker of warmth, an echo of a person, not a monster, seemed to briefly break through the endless loop of his madness. He was trying to connect.

You felt a surge of cold, grim satisfaction. This was it. The moment. His mind, soothed by Rowan’s gentle touch and your insistent words, was open, searching for a truth other than horror. You leaned closer, closing your eyes, pushing your nascent Mirror Magic outwards, not to impose, but to *reflect*. You sought the precise vision of that visit, the true encounter between your mother and the last witness to her deepest secret.
