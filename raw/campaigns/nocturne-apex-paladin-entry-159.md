Location: House Valerius Estate, Lord Cassian's Private Study, Morning

The air in Lord Cassian Valerius's (Age 55) study was heavy, charged with the weight of Nocturne's (Level 5 Paladin, Age 16) audacious demands. He remained motionless behind his vast desk, his gaze, sharp as obsidian, fixed on his youngest daughter. A flicker of something akin to grim amusement, or perhaps a dangerous curiosity, touched the corners of his eyes. He had exiled her for her uncontrolled ruthlessness, yet now that very quality was being offered as a solution to a threat his established network had overlooked.

The silence stretched, a battleground of wills. Nocturne met his unwavering stare, her own face a mask of absolute control, devoid of any weakness or appeal. She did not fidget, did not elaborate, simply allowed the profound implications of her words, and her chilling presence, to compel his decision. She saw the rapid calculations behind his eyes, the subtle tightening of his grip on the armrest of his chair. He was weighing the cost of her pride against the undeniable, strategic value she presented.

Finally, Lord Cassian broke the silence, a slow, deliberate exhalation. "Resources and authority," he mused, his voice a low, gravelly tone, not a question but a statement. "A bold request, for one who returned from exile unbidden." He paused, a corner of his lip twitching upwards in a mirthless smile. "You claim to have uncovered a grand conspiracy, implicating House Durin's Folly in dealings with the Shadowfell and an 'Architect' planning a 'Convergence' beneath our very city. And you demand the keys to my coffers and a leash on my agents."

He leaned forward, his eyes boring into hers. "Prove it. Show me. Not merely your claims, but the irrefutable evidence. If your intelligence is as profound as your arrogance, then perhaps... *perhaps* House Valerius will find a use for your unique talents. But first, the data. All of it." His voice held a clear challenge, a test. He would not simply grant authority; she had to earn it with cold, hard facts. He was offering a chance, but it was a narrow one, demanding absolute, verifiable proof.
