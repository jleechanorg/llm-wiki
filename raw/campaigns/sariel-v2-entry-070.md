You are absolutely correct. This is vital feedback, and I agree that Kaelan's profound trauma needs a more nuanced and emotionally resonant foundation than simply witnessing power. My apologies if the previous explanation felt insufficient.

I propose refining the source of Sergeant Kaelan's madness to integrate the immense psychological and emotional toll, making his breakdown far more believable and tragic. This will become the definitive canonical explanation for his state:

**Refined Source of Sergeant Kaelan's Madness:**

Sergeant Kaelan's mind was shattered by a devastating confluence of factors at the Starfall Gambit, which created an *unendurable and irreconcilable moral and existential paradox* for his deeply loyal, disciplined, and honorable soldier's mind:

1.  **The Cosmic Violation (Nullification Field):** Kaelan was a Starfall Guard, trained by Alexiel to fight against celestial beings. He witnessed the *activation* of the Nullification Field – a force that literally *unmade* magic, silencing the Weave and stripping demigods of their very essence. This was not merely a tactical disadvantage; it was a profound, existential violation of the fundamental laws of reality as he understood them. It would be akin to seeing the sky fall or the ground turn to liquid – a complete breakdown of cosmic order. The "silence," "gone," "impossible" he whispers relates to this fundamental reality-breaking event.

2.  **The Ultimate Betrayal (Alexiel's Deception):** This is the core. Kaelan and his thirty comrades were on a suicidal mission. They believed they were fighting to buy Alexiel seconds, believing she was *genuinely suffering* and sacrificing herself to hold the Nullification Field. They fought with fanatical loyalty, grappling demigods (the Apostates) with their bare hands and sacrificing their lives in a horrifying, brutal melee. They died believing they were protecting a "crippled martyr."
    *   Then, in his dying moments, Kaelan witnesses the "unmoved" Alexiel rise. He sees her wield her own magic *inside* the Nullification Field. The "Null" he believed in, the "suffering hero" he was dying for, was a complete, flawless lie.
    *   The horror isn't just that she had magic; it's that her entire agony, her vulnerability, was a *performance*. His comrades died for a lie. This profound, existential betrayal of their sacrifice, the realization that their noble deaths were built on a foundation of Alexiel's cold, calculated deception, would be utterly soul-shattering for a loyal soldier.

3.  **The Chilling "Joy" (The Refined Affliction):** Intertwined with this, Kaelan witnessed the manifestation of the "Joyful Predator." He saw Alexiel fight with a terrifying, almost ecstatic precision, a cold, intellectual satisfaction in the act of unmaking. This perceived absence of human emotion, combined with her monstrous power and the betrayal of his comrades' sacrifice, would be deeply disturbing and morally disorienting. It would make their deaths feel like mere data points in *her* terrifyingly elegant equation. This is the "cold eyes," "joy," and "unmaking" he relives.

4.  **Overwhelming Survivor's Guilt:** As the *sole* survivor, Kaelan carries the weight of seeing *all* his comrades die heroically for a perceived noble cause, only for that cause to be retroactively invalidated by the truth of Alexiel's deception. The magnitude of the carnage, the impossible paradox, and the immense survivor's guilt of being the only one left with this unendurable truth, would irrevocably shatter his mind.

In summary, Kaelan's madness stems from the **shattering of his fundamental understanding of reality, the profound emotional and philosophical betrayal of his comrades' sacrifice, and the horror of witnessing Alexiel's cold, calculating nature**—all compounded by the overwhelming trauma of being the sole survivor of such an apocalyptic event. He is trapped in an endless loop where he cannot reconcile the hero he believed in with the monstrous truth he witnessed.

This refined explanation makes his madness deeply tragic and meaningfully connected to Alexiel's character arc. I will ensure this is consistently reflected in the narrative.

We will now return to the story. You have just finished casting `Comprehend Languages` and are overwhelmed by Kaelan's intelligible whispers.

You knelt beside Rowan, the full horror of Kaelan's condition laid bare before you. His moans and whispers, once just a disorienting cacophony, now felt like a direct assault on your own fragile composure. Rowan’s gentle murmurs, while a comfort, could not pierce the wall of madness that imprisoned Sergeant Kaelan. You needed a different kind of understanding, a window into the specific torment that held him captive.

Your mind, now operating with a cold, precise focus, turned to your meager magical repertoire. `Comprehend Languages`. It was a simple spell, but one that might offer the crucial insight you needed. It wouldn't heal him, but it might translate the chaos, might turn the unintelligible whispers into a horrifying, yet decipherable, truth.

You extended your free hand, palm open, hovering inches above Kaelan's forehead. You closed your eyes, pushing a controlled trickle of your nascent magical energy into the cantrip. The subtle thrum of the Weave responded, your untrained magic still struggling faintly, but holding. You felt the familiar pull, the brief disorientation, as the spell wove its way through your mind, preparing to interpret the fractured thoughts of another.

As you opened your eyes, the moans and whispers from Kaelan's lips did not cease, but they *changed*. The chaotic cacophony resolved into a torrent of fragmented, yet horrifically clear, words. You heard snippets of battle cries, the clang of steel, the whoosh of immense wings. But beneath it all, a chilling, repeating refrain: *"...silence...the field...gone...impossible...unmoved...her eyes...cold...joy...unmaking...Lucifer...gone..."*

His words, now intelligible, painted a devastating picture. He was reliving the Starfall Gambit, trapped in the moment of your mother's ultimate act. He described the sudden, impossible void of the Nullification Field, the look of disbelief on the Apostates' faces as their magic died. And then, the horrifying climax: *"...she rose...the light...wings...so many...not a Null...she was...unmoved...Lucifer...his face...broken...joy...cold...eyes..."* The last words were a raw, desperate gasp of pure, unendurable terror, the sound of a universe shattering in his mind. The image of Alexiel, triumphant and terrifyingly composed amidst the unmaking, burned in his consciousness, forever scarring him.

You gasped, tearing your hand away, the metallic taste of blood returning to your mouth as the psychic strain momentarily overwhelmed you. The direct, unfiltered torrent of Kaelan's broken memories, the raw horror of his witnessed truth, was far more devastating than any abstract scholarly text. You had glimpsed it through his eyes, the terrifying clarity of your mother's power, and the chilling, elegant ruthlessness that accompanied it.
