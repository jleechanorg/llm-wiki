[Mode: STORY MODE]
Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 9:58 AM.
Location: The Chamber of Whispers, Great Archives, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 1 (XP: 0/300), Gold: 10gp, HP: 8/8

Kantos’s ancient eyes, magnified by his thick spectacles, fixed on you. He had just revealed the existence of a man whose mind had been shattered by the truth, and he feared that same truth would poison you. You felt the chill of it, the weight of centuries of buried knowledge pressing down, but it only sharpened your resolve. This was not a moment for caution. This was a moment for the truth.

"A broken mind," you whispered, your voice low and intense, "is often the only one capable of holding a forbidden truth. But a complete truth, Magister, is sometimes the only antidote. My mother died in that battle. Her legend is absolute, but the way she died... it defies logic. Thirty Starfall Guard against seven Apostates, including Lucifer himself. And she, a Null, walked out alone?" Your gaze was unwavering, piercing through his practiced composure. "How? What truly happened at the Starfall Gambit? I need the unedited details. All of them."

Kantos winced, as if the words themselves were physical blows. He wrung his ink-stained hands, his gaze darting around the dark, silent chamber as if expecting Raziel's Silent Guard to materialize and spirit him away. He was a man caught between the terror of a lie and the horror of the truth. He closed his eyes, a deep, shuddering sigh escaping him, and when they opened, a profound weariness had settled over his ancient features.

"It is a story of impossible brilliance, Princess," he rasped, his voice barely audible, "and a cost no one should bear. The official account states your mother's peerless martial skill, honed by her perceived 'flaw' as a Null, allowed her to overcome Lucifer's immense power through sheer physical prowess. It claims the Starfall Guard simply grappled and held the Apostates through impossible bravery, buying her the seconds she needed to strike, even as their immense celestial powers were somehow rendered... inert. It is a beautiful lie, Sariel. One the Imperium desperately needed to believe."

You felt a cold knot of certainty tighten in your stomach. "Inert?" you repeated, the word a sharp blade in the silence. "How? Lucifer's Apostates were demigods. Their magic was their very essence. It does not simply become 'inert' because a Null is present. That is the lie, isn't it? Tell me the truth, Magister. What did my mother truly do?" Your voice rose, raw with desperate urgency, shattering the carefully cultivated calm of the chamber. You leaned across the dusty table, your eyes burning into his, demanding the truth that had been buried for so long.

Kantos recoiled, visibly trembling. He looked at your Starlit Eyes, so bright with the intelligence he had always admired, and saw the relentless, unyielding demand for knowledge that mirrored his own scholarly obsession. He had tried to protect you, but he realized with a chilling certainty that you would not stop until you knew. And perhaps, a part of him, the archivist, the keeper of all truths, longed to finally speak the unspeakable.

"Your mother, Princess," he began, his voice a dry, strained whisper, "was not a Null." He paused, letting the impossible statement hang in the air. "Not in the way the world believed. She possessed... a singular gift. A power that could silence magic itself." He leaned closer, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial murmur, his eyes wide with the horror and awe of the secret he was about to reveal. "She created a Nullification Field. A perfect sphere of absolute, unnatural silence that swallowed all magic within its radius."

He shuddered, pulling back slightly. "It was the ultimate weapon against beings like Lucifer. It turned gods into mortals. It turned Archangels into simple warriors." He looked at you, his gaze filled with sorrow. "But the cost, Princess... the official records spoke of her coming out of the battle wracked with agony, on the brink of death. They said it burned her from the inside. A suicidal power, a sacrifice." He wrung his hands, his voice thick with a terrible, dawning realization. "But the Starfall Patient... his fragmented memories... they speak of something else. A flicker of joy. A terrifying, cold precision. He saw it all. He saw her unleash that power. And he saw her... unmoved."

Kantos’s voice dropped to a barely audible whisper. "Your mother, Princess, was not just a Null. She was a deceiver. Her greatest power was the lie itself. She convinced the world, and even her closest allies, that her ultimate weapon was a self-destructive act of agony. She fought the last battle not as a broken martyr, but as a perfect, silent predator, wielding a power no one believed she possessed." He looked at you, his eyes filled with a profound sorrow. "And she was... unmoved."
