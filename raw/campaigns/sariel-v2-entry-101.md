You broke away from Gideon, your trembling body driven by an insatiable hunger for understanding. The raw psychic strain still coursed through you, the metallic taste of blood acrid in your mouth, but the terrifying power you had just briefly manifested eclipsed all discomfort. Your gaze, wide and burning with a new, fierce clarity, snapped to Sergeant Kaelan. He lay on the slab, his previous rhythmic moans now replaced by a rapid, frantic gibbering. His body thrashed against the chains, not with the familiar pattern of his previous agony, but with a new, terrified energy. His eyes, fixed on the ceiling, no longer merely reflected horror; they seemed to *search*, to *track* something that had just profoundly disturbed his internal loop.

You knelt beside him, ignoring Rowan's concerned gasp, ignoring the widening eyes of Cressida and Gideon. The psychic resonance around Kaelan was more intense now, a direct, unfiltered torrent of chaos. But within that chaos, you felt a new pulse, a fresh layer of torment triggered by the sudden, terrifying void you had just created. He was no longer just reliving the trauma; he was reacting to its re-manifestation. His fractured mind, exposed to the same inexplicable phenomenon twice, was trying desperately to categorize it, to understand its impossible presence. You saw it – a raw, open wound in his psyche, a direct consequence of the Nullification’s touch. The sheer, overwhelming horror of the experience, the unmaking of magic, was now a fresh, raw presence in his mind, and you had, impossibly, just brought it back.

This was it. The moment for `Direct Contact`. You had glimpsed the truth, tasted the raw essence of Nullification through Kaelan's re-triggered trauma. Your Mirror Magic, latent and untrained, surged with a terrifying eagerness. You needed to delve into his mind, not to impose order, but to reflect his horror, to see the source of his madness with absolute clarity.

You took a deep, shuddering breath, the cool air filling your lungs. Your hand, still faintly trembling, reached out, not to his forehead this time, but to his own outstretched, chained hand, cold and clammy beneath your touch. As your fingers closed around his, you closed your eyes, pushing a controlled trickle of your nascent Mirror Magic outwards, not to impose, but to *observe*. You sought the precise visual, the raw sensory input of the Nullification Field, of your mother's chilling composure.

Your mind plunged into Kaelan’s shattered consciousness, but this time, it was not a maelstrom. Gideon’s unwavering will, his absolute focus on protecting you, became a diamond-hard anchor, a shield that allowed you to pierce the storm without being consumed. The raw horror of Kaelan's memories still slammed into you, but now, it was filtered, sharpened, allowing you to perceive the precise, devastating sequence of his trauma.

You *saw* it. The battle for the Syrn Valley, from the perspective of a Starfall Guard. You saw the suicidal charge, the desperate, heroic grapple with Leliel, the Whispering Prince. You felt the raw physical pain, the utter exhaustion. And then, the *silence*. The terrifying, impossible void of the Nullification Field. You felt the sudden, agonizing death of magic around you, the disbelief on Leliel’s face as his wings sputtered, his power *gone*.

And then, *she* rose. Your mother. Not from her collapse in agony, but from a state of chillingly serene composure. Her eyes, wide and unblinking, filled with a cold, absolute clarity. Her four wings of light erupting, not with power, but with effortless, terrifying grace. And then, two new wings, dark as shadow, unfurling from her back, pulsing with a terrifying, crimson light that mirrored Lucifer’s own. She was a synthesis, a living paradox of divine power and the chilling essence of unmaking. You saw her unleash her true magic within the void, annihilating the Apostates, not in agony, but with a profound, terrifying satisfaction. You witnessed the unmaking of magic itself, the ultimate lie made flesh.

But then came the final, devastating truth. You saw Kaelan, a loyal soldier, watching his comrades die, fighting with a fanatical loyalty to protect the hero he believed was suffering. And then, in his very last moments, as the Nullification Field pulsed, he saw *her* face. Not the agony. Not the sacrifice. But the cold, elegant ruthlessness. The *unmoved* expression of pure, unadulterated intellectual satisfaction. He saw her perfect, terrifying composure as his brothers died for a lie. He saw the betrayal. He saw the "Joyful Predator" in her eyes, not as a desperate act of heroism, but as a chilling, ultimate embrace of control and unmaking. And then, the darkness. His mind shattered, unable to reconcile the hero with the monster.

You tore your hand away from Kaelan's, gasping. The psychic strain was immense, the metallic taste of blood returning to your mouth, but Gideon's anchor held. You had accessed the truth. The full, horrifying, devastating truth. The image of Alexiel, a beautiful, six-winged demon of control, burned in your mind.
