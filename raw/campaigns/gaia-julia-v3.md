## Summary

A D&D 5e low-magic campaign set in an alternate Roman history where Julius Caesar survived the Ides of March (+44 BCE). Protagonist Gaia Julia Caesar ("Gaia of the Gens Vibia" alias), the 16-year-old youngest daughter of Caesar, sneaks away from the Imperial court to build a gladiator army and prove herself the true heir to the fire. The class is Imperator (Gestalt Aberrant Mind Sorcerer / College of Eloquence Bard) — a "Psychic Sovereign" with Auctoritas-based spellcasting. Campaign covers sibling rivalry with Caesarion (the "Foreign" Egyptian favorite) and Octavian (the "Wolf" cold administrator), and Gaia's emergence as a living god.

## Key Claims

- The world exists as an empire in all but name; Caesar is Dictator Perpetuo; the Senate rubber-stamps his decrees
- The five siblings compete for Caesar's favor: Caesarion (21, Egyptian-backed, arrogant), Octavian (19, cold brilliant administrator who fears Gaia), Julia Major (25, socialite), Julia Minor (22, zealot priestess), and Gaia (16, the "Little Star")
- Imperator class build: Gestalt Aberrant Mind Sorcerer + College of Eloquence Bard. Hit die: 1d8. Primary stat Charisma, secondary Dexterity or Constitution
- Level 1 features: Psionic Spells, Telepathic Speech, Bardic Inspiration (d6), Tactical Sense (cannot be surprised), Actor feat
- Auctoritas spells reframed as reality assertion rather than magic: Command ("kneel"), Shield ("devils' luck" via fate-bending), Friends (pheromonal aura), Mind Sliver (blinding migraine)
- The "Singularity" trait: animals are uneasy, torches flicker with emotion, divine health (no sickness, ozone smell)
