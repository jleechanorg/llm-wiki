You saw it – the Starfall Gambit, Lucifer’s final, arrogant advance, the desperate, suicidal charge of the Starfall Guard. And then, the impossible. Your mother. Rising from her feigned collapse. Her eyes, wide and unblinking, filled with a cold, absolute clarity. Her four wings of light erupting. And then, two new wings, dark as shadow, unfurling from her back, pulsing with a terrifying, crimson light that mirrored Lucifer’s own. She was a synthesis, a living paradox of divine power and the chilling essence of unmaking. You saw her unleash her power, not in agony, but with a profound, terrifying satisfaction. You witnessed the unmaking of magic itself, the ultimate lie made flesh, and the cold, elegant ruthlessness that accompanied it.

The psychic strain was immense, but Gideon's anchor held. You had accessed the truth.

You pulled your hand away from Kaelan's forehead, a gasp escaping your lips. The metallic taste of blood was back, sharp and acrid, but the psychic backlash was less overwhelming than before, thanks to Gideon. You staggered back a step, trembling, your Starlit Eyes burning with the afterimage of the vision. You had to try. Now. The memory was fresh. The terror was real. The power... it beckoned.

You closed your eyes, forcing yourself to ignore the lingering pain, to ignore Kaelan’s soft moans. You reached inward, pushing past the guilt, past the fear, past the profound horror of what you had just witnessed. You focused on the raw sensation of the Nullification Field, the absolute silence that swallowed all magic. You envisioned it, precisely, as you had seen it through Kaelan's eyes – the shimmering void, the impossible unmaking of the Weave. Your latent Mirror Magic surged, desperate to replicate the observed phenomenon.

A profound silence descended upon the cell, sudden and absolute. It wasn't the sterile quiet of the deep archives. It was a terrifying, suffocating void. The soft, rhythmic moans of Sergeant Kaelan cut off abruptly, his face momentarily relaxing from its mask of agony, only to then twist into a new, profound confusion. The small orb of `Light` on your finger flickered wildly, then pulsed, freezing into a static, dim glow, its radiant heat diminishing until it felt merely cold. Rowan’s gentle murmurs ceased, his hands hovering over Kaelan, utterly baffled. Gideon, standing sentinel, stiffened, his hand instinctively going to his sword, which now felt inexplicably dead in his grasp. Cressida gasped, her sharp eyes wide with stunned disbelief.

You felt it – a vast, cold emptiness expanding from your very core, swallowing all magic in the cell. It was uncontrolled, raw, and utterly terrifying. A chaotic, unbidden echo of your mother’s power. The psychic strain was immense, the cost of wielding something so vast, so fundamental. A thin trickle of blood, warm and metallic, ran from your nose. You swayed, almost collapsing, but Gideon’s unwavering presence, his silent, desperate projection of strength, held you upright, shielding your mind from the immediate, overwhelming backlash.

The effect lasted only for a heartbeat, a fleeting, terrifying moment of absolute silence. Then, with a shuddering gasp from you, the void collapsed. Magic rushed back into the cell, filling the air with its familiar hum. Kaelan’s moans returned, louder, more frantic than before, his torment now compounded by this sudden, inexplicable void. Your `Light` cantrip flared back to full brightness, almost blindingly so. Your body trembled, utterly drained, but your Starlit Eyes burned with a terrifying, new understanding. You had done it. You had replicated the unmaking. You had glimpsed the chilling, elegant ruthlessness of your mother’s power.

You broke away from Gideon, your trembling body driven by an insatiable hunger for understanding. The raw psychic strain still coursed through you, the metallic taste of blood acrid in your mouth, but the terrifying power you had just briefly manifested eclipsed all discomfort. Your gaze, wide and burning with a new, fierce clarity, snapped to Sergeant Kaelan. He lay on the slab, his previous rhythmic moans now replaced by a rapid, frantic gibbering. His body thrashed against the chains, not with the familiar pattern of his previous agony, but with a new, terrified energy. His eyes, fixed on the ceiling, no longer merely reflected horror; they seemed to *search*, to *track* something that had just profoundly disturbed his internal loop.

You knelt beside him, ignoring Rowan's concerned gasp, ignoring the widening eyes of Cressida and Gideon. The psychic resonance around Kaelan was more intense now, a direct, unfiltered torrent of chaos. But within that chaos, you felt a new pulse, a fresh layer of torment triggered by the sudden, terrifying void you had just created. He was no longer just reliving the trauma; he was reacting to its re-manifestation. His fractured mind, exposed to the same inexplicable phenomenon twice, was trying desperately to categorize it, to understand its impossible presence. You saw it – a raw, open wound in his psyche, a direct consequence of the Nullification’s touch. The sheer, overwhelming horror of the experience, the unmaking of magic, was now a fresh, raw presence in his mind, and you had, impossibly, just brought it back.
