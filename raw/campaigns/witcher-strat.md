## Summary

A D&D 5e campaign set during The Witcher 3: Baptism of Fire (Season 4 framing). Protagonist Nocturne (alias Sariel), a 16-year-old College of Swords Bard, travels with "The Rats" — a band of teen bandits. The campaign is a psychological horror study of a prodigy who sees the world as a flawed schematic needing editorial revision. Nocturne identifies Ciri ("Falka") as her "favorite project" — a living weapon of immense power and a key to a larger game. Three-act structure: psychological cat-and-mouse with Ciri, the Bonhart arc, and the throne-of-corpses endgame.

## Key Claims

- Nocturne operates under two "perfectly-fitted masks": the Quiet Rat (default disarming gentility) and the Dispassionate Strategist (win-win scenario architect)
- Her core drives are Personal Transcendence (forbidden knowledge, magical source, treasury — portable absolute power) and Surgical Domination (perfect frictionless execution of will, not rulership)
- The Hidden Compulsion: stress-testing volatile systems — specifically, the will of young idealistic people shaped by nascent power (Ciri/"Falka")
- D&D build: Variant Human (Spy background), College of Swords Bard, CHA 17, DEX 15, INT 12, Actor feat, Deception/Persuasion expertise
- The campaign explicitly does not follow the Witcher book plot exactly — inspiration is taken, events are reinterpreted

## Scenes 1-20 Summary

Scenes 1-6: Character creation process. Three options presented (AI Generated, Standard D&D, Custom Class). Player chooses "ai_generated." Character sheet built: Variant Human College of Swords Bard with Spy background. Stats: STR 8, DEX 15, CON 13, INT 12, WIS 10, CHA 17. Actor feat, Proficiency in Deception (+5), Insight (+2), Investigation (+1), Persuasion (+5), Stealth (+4). Spells: Vicious Mockery, Message, Charm Person, Healing Word, Faerie Fire, Disguise Self. Player requests 18 INT and enemies scaled accordingly.

Scenes 7-14: The Rats find Ciri ("Falka") in the rain. Nocturne recognizes Ciri's hidden power (chaotic magic bleeding from her pores) and sees her as a "favorite project." First Test: Nocturne subtly uses Bardic abilities to manipulate Ciri into killing in cold blood during a village raid. Mistle becomes possessively attached to Falka. A bounty hunter agent from Nocturne's past arrives — must be eliminated quietly without revealing Nocturne's true skill to the Rats.

Scenes 15-20: Act I Climax. The Rats are tracked by professional bounty hunters (agents of Baron Rieb). Giselher's plan is wrong. Nocturne takes strategic control using military prodigy intellect and Bardic magic. A dazzling, terrifying display of Blade Flourishes saves the group but reveals a fraction of Nocturne's true power for the first time. Falka is terrified of Nocturne.
