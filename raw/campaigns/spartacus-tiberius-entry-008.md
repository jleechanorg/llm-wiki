[CHARACTER CREATION - Personality Phase]

Tiberius is a man of sharp edges and cold shadows, shaped by the overwhelming presence of Marcus Crassus. His personality is a cocktail of Roman entitlement and deep-seated insecurity, manifesting as a desperate, often violent, need to prove himself worthy of the Crassus name. He doesn't just want to lead; he wants to be feared as his father is feared, and he lacks the patience and restraint that comes with age.

**Personality Traits:**
1. **Ruthless Ambition:** Every action is a calculated step toward a higher station. He views morality as a luxury for the weak and obstacles as things to be crushed, not negotiated with.
2. **Seeking Approval:** His cruelty often stems from a desire to impress his father. He mimics Crassus's coldness but often adds a layer of impulsive malice because he feels he is constantly being judged and found wanting.

**Ideals:**
- **Order & Dominance:** The world is right only when Rome is on top, and the Crassus family is at the head of Rome. Slaves are tools; rebels are broken tools that must be discarded.

**Bonds:**
- **The Shadow of the Father:** His life is defined by Marcus Crassus. He loves, fears, and eventually resents the man who gave him everything but demands perfection in return.
- **Legionary Pride:** He views the men under his command not as friends, but as extensions of his own will. Their discipline is his shield.

**Flaws:**
- **Hubris:** He often underestimates 'lesser' men, particularly Spartacus and his rebels, believing Roman blood and wealth make him invincible.
- **Cruelty as Control:** When he feels weak or ignored, he resorts to extreme violence to reassert his status, often creating more problems than he solves.

How do these traits sit with you? Should we lean harder into his **resentment of his father**, or perhaps his **hidden vulnerability** when away from the legions?
