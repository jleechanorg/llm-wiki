Daemon’s eyes, though heavy with fatigue and the lingering burn of dragonfire, fixed on the slain wyrm not as a foe, but as raw material. This was more than a kill; it was a resource to be exploited. Ignoring the still-throbbing pain, Daemon (Level 2, Age 30) set about his grim work with a brutal pragmatism. His greatsword became a crude cleaver, hacking and prying at the massive carcass. He ignored precision, focusing solely on function, selecting thick, obsidian-like plates of scale, still warm from the beast’s life. Using heavy leather straps from his pack, and the sheer force of his hands, he began to lash and integrate these scales onto his existing plate armor. It was not elegant, but it was robust, adding another layer of grim protection to his chest and shoulders.

From one of the large, curving teeth, he chipped and ground at the edges, using the cavern’s rough stone and the pommel of his greatsword as impromptu tools. Sweat mixed with dragon blood on his brow until, finally, it became a wicked, serrated dagger – a secondary weapon of brutal efficiency, too large to conceal easily but perfect for a close-quarters, desperate fight. Daemon grunted, inspecting his work. His armor now bore the undeniable mark of his victory, its defenses enhanced by the draconic plating. The dragon-tooth dagger felt cold and sharp in his grip. This was power, tangible and earned from the very beast that sought to bar his path.
