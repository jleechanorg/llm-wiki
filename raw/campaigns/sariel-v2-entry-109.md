You tore your hand away from Kaelan’s, gasping. The psychic strain was immense, the metallic taste of blood returning to your mouth, but Gideon's anchor held. You swayed, almost collapsing, but Gideon's strong hand on your shoulder kept you upright. The raw horror of Kaelan's trauma, the crushing weight of your mother's hidden grief, and the agonizing contradiction of her "unmoved" persona, now all burned within you. You had seen it. You had the truth.

Kaelan lay still, his moans silenced, a profound peace settling over his tormented features. The single tear on his cheek was dry now. He had found his memory. He had found his answer. His mind was still broken, but the torment had ceased.

You ignored the tremor in your own body, the faint metallic tang of blood in your mouth. Kaelan’s newfound peace, however fragile, was a miracle. His eyes, though still wide and unblinking, no longer held the same unseeing terror. He had processed the new truth, the paradox of the hero who grieved. You knelt closer again, your hand gently cupping his cheek, your voice soft and insistent, a lifeline into his quieted madness.

"Sergeant Kaelan," you whispered, your Starlit Eyes fixed on his, willing him to understand. "It was not a betrayal. She mourned you. She mourned the Starfall Guard. She was not untouched by your sacrifice. She carried your grief, your loyalty, your deaths... as a silent, terrible burden. You saw her strength, yes. You saw her ruthlessness. But you also saw the agony of the cost etched into her soul. She *chose* that burden, Sergeant. She chose to bear the weight of your perceived betrayal, to keep the truth hidden for the sake of the Imperium, for her family, for the very peace you died to win."

You saw a subtle flicker in his eyes, a faint, almost imperceptible shift in his gaze that seemed to follow your words, as if a new pathway was being forged in his shattered mind. The profound peace on his face deepened, losing its previous blankness and gaining a subtle, almost melancholic, expression. His lips, dry and cracked, slowly, agonizingly, formed a single, soft, almost human sound: "...My Lady..." It was a whisper of understanding, of acceptance. He was still lost in the labyrinth of his mind, but the endless, screaming loop of terror and betrayal had finally broken.

"Rowan," you commanded, your voice sharp with renewed urgency, your gaze still fixed on Kaelan. "His torment has ceased. His mind has found a measure of peace. Focus all your efforts on stabilizing this. Soothe him. Give him true rest, if you can. He needs to heal."

Rowan Thorne (Level 3 Expert, Age 16), his gentle face a mask of awe and profound relief, immediately redoubled his ministrations. His hands moved with an almost frantic grace, applying fresh poultices, murmuring ancient lullabies and healing chants learned from forgotten lore, willing Kaelan's shattered mind towards a deeper, more profound quiet. Gideon (Level 5 Fighter, Age 25) stood sentinel, his unyielding presence a silent anchor, his gaze fixed on Kaelan, then on you, a profound respect deepening in his eyes. Cressida (Level 4 Expert, Age 16) watched, her sharp intellect processing the profound psychological feat you had just accomplished, her admiration palpable. Magister Kantos, pale and trembling by the door, simply stared, witnessing a miracle he would never have believed possible.

Sergeant Kaelan's breathing deepened, his body relaxing further against the chains. The subtle tremor in his hand, clasped in yours, stilled completely. His unblinking eyes closed slowly, a profound, almost blissful peace settling over his tormented features. His lips, faintly parted, exhaled a long, shuddering sigh that seemed to carry the last vestiges of his agony. He was still. He was catatonic, but no longer tormented. He was at peace.
