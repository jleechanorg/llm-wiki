`Location: The Rusty Flagon Inn, Greenmeadow Village`

The coded phrase hung in the stale air of the common room. Corvan, a flicker of understanding in his eyes, had set down his bread, his posture stiffening, but he didn’t speak. He watched Gladius with a wary, professional gaze, less the sniveling coward of Baldur's Gate, more a calculating minor player. *Good,* Gladius thought, a grim satisfaction coiling in his gut. *A challenge, however small, is always more interesting.*

Gladius didn't sit. He merely stood over the table, a towering, silent presence. The greatsword on his back seemed to hum with unspoken menace, a blunt promise of destruction. He didn't move, didn't utter another word. Instead, he let his gaze fall, heavy and unblinking, upon Corvan. His eyes, usually veiled and flat, now held a chilling intensity, stripping away the man’s composure. The faint sounds of the waking village outside – a rooster’s distant crow, the creak of a cart wheel – only served to emphasize the profound, oppressive silence within the inn, a silence Gladius deliberately cultivated.

Corvan, for his part, tried to hold the gaze. His jaw tightened, a muscle jumping in his cheek, but his eyes eventually faltered, darting away to the side, then down to his half-eaten meal. He cleared his throat, a nervous, dry sound. The subtle vein in Gladius’s neck began to pulse, a minute tell of his impatience, a silent warning that this charade of 'professional caution' was wearing thin. Corvan’s fingers, which had been resting on the table, curled into a tight fist, then relaxed, then tightened again. He was beginning to break.

Finally, unable to bear the silent pressure, Corvan looked up, his face a mask of forced calm that cracked at the edges. His voice, when it came, was low, almost a murmur. "The sheriff, Borin," he began, "is... accounted for. He won't be a problem. He's been paid to overlook any... disturbances in the village tonight. You have my word. And Lord Silvanus's." He gestured vaguely to the gold Gladius carried, a silent acknowledgment of the exorbitant price already paid. "What else do you need, 'Hammer'?"
