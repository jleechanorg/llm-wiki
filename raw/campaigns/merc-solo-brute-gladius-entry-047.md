The Miller farmhouse was now a silent shell, emptied of life, waiting for Corvan to begin the grim work of erasure. Gladius, having sent his subtle signal, stood amidst the lingering scents of dried herbs and freshly extinguished lives. His thorough search had yielded nothing more of material value, confirming the pathetic insignificance of these peasants. Their defiance, their "insult," was barely a ripple in the grand scheme of Baldur's Gate power.

His thoughts drifted, cold and calculating. A *message*. Lord Silvanus wanted a "permanent example." The phrase itself was almost poetic in its brutality, but Gladius’s mind, fueled by the raw, experiential pleasure of control, always sought the most effective means. *Should I leave a head on the front door?* The image flashed through his mind – visceral, dramatic, undeniable. It would certainly send a message. A very loud, very bloody message.

But then, the thought twisted, a subtle sneer playing on his lips. *Too crude. Too obvious.* That was the way a common brute would operate, not a predator who understood the true art of dominance. Silvanus and his lackey had insisted on "no witnesses," on "nothing to trace back," on the villagers eventually *noticing nothing*. A severed head was an undeniable trace, a screaming witness. It would draw undue attention, complicate Corvan's cleanup, and potentially bring unwanted scrutiny from those who might actually *care* about such a public display, even if the local sheriff was a drunkard. That kind of chaos was inefficient.

No, the true message wasn't for the village to see. The message was for Lord Silvanus, and it was being delivered by Corvan, trembling with fear, now complicit in the bloody work. The silent, complete erasure of the Miller family, the chilling perfection of the "no witnesses" clause – that was the real power. It proved Gladius was not just a hammer, but a silent, untraceable force. His own satisfaction stemmed from breaking the powerful, from forcing their agents into the muck they thought themselves above. That psychological mastery, the subtle, insidious twisting of their will, was far more delicious than any vulgar display of carnage. He would leave no head, only an absence, a quiet, horrifying void.
