The cold satisfaction of Gladius's dominance permeated the Stonehand dwelling. Corvan, cuffed and utterly broken, had begun his grim work, his disheveled silks now brushing against the cold, stone floor. The raw scent of fear and damp earth hung heavy in the air, punctuated by Corvan's ragged breaths and the clumsy sounds of his struggle with the bodies. Gladius watched for a moment, a subtle hum of pleasure vibrating beneath his skin. This was the true indulgence: to witness the degradation of the 'elite,' to force them to wallow in the very filth they paid others to manage.

With Corvan now occupied, Gladius turned his attention back to the dwelling itself. He had found thirty gold earlier, a paltry sum, but his pragmatic mind dictated a truly meticulous sweep. "No witnesses" wasn't just about the bodies; it was about leaving no trace, ensuring every last possible crumb of value or information was absorbed by him. This was not just a cleanup; it was a final, complete erasure. He moved with the fluid, silent grace that belied his size, his Shadow-Step Boots whispering against the worn stone.

He ran his large, calloused hands along the rough-hewn shelves, sweeping aside simple ceramic plates and crude wooden utensils. He systematically opened every cupboard, every small, hidden niche in the stone walls, his eyes sharp, his touch precise. He overturned the straw pallets, prodded at loose floorboards, even ran his fingers through the cold ashes of the hearth. There was nothing more. No forgotten coin, no hidden trinkets, no overlooked documents. The house, like the lives within it, was utterly barren of anything of true worth.

A faint sneer touched Gladius’s lips. *Pathetic.* Their stubbornness, their defiance, had brought them to this. Their lives, and their meager possessions, were as empty and insignificant as the space they now occupied. The grim satisfaction of his thoroughness settled in his gut; the job was done, cleanly and completely. The sun, now rising fully, cast long, intrusive beams through the windows, illuminating Corvan's pathetic struggle. Gladius's work here was finished.
