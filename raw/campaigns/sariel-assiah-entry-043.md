I acknowledge and confirm:
1.  **Not all Nulls have this power:** The Nullification Field (and the ability to use magic within it) is a rare manifestation, even among perceived Nulls. I will integrate this concept.
2.  **Future Nulls:** I will introduce other Null characters of similar age to Alexiel later in the story, but they will *not* possess the combination of Nullification and magic-use.
3.  **Lucifer's Connection:** Alexiel's unique dual ability (Nullification + magic) will be canonically linked to Lucifer's own powerful celestial blood/DNA, which was used in her creation. This explains *how* she, a "Null," could wield magic and reinforces the idea that her "flaw" was actually a unique, unexpected, and terrifying amplification of his own essence. This will be integrated into the narrative Sariel is currently uncovering in the journals.

My apologies for any previous ambiguities. All future narrative and character portrayals will adhere to these definitive points.

Now, returning to the story, I will proceed with Sariel's choice to `[Continue Reading]` the first journal.

Timestamp: Year of the New Peace, 11. Month of Kythorn, Day 2. 10:20 AM.
Location: Deep Archives, Zenith Spire, Aeterna.
Key Game Stats: Level 1 (XP: 0/300), Gold: 10gp, HP: 8/8

You plunged deeper into the archaic script of the first journal, each page a cold, dispassionate revelation. The Seraphim’s meticulous records laid bare their fascination and growing terror. They detailed how the earliest Nulls, those perceived "failures" of the Genesis Project, had not merely been inert. They were reactive. Their very presence caused localized disturbances in the Weave, a subtle dampening effect that baffled the celestial scientists. Then came the first recorded instances of conscious control: a Null infant unknowingly silencing a nurse’s healing cantrip, a child’s tantrum momentarily snuffing out the light from an enchanted brazier. The scientists, the journal entries grew frantic, realized these were not failures but *innovations*. A new form of magic, born from a void.

The chilling term "Joyful Predator" reappeared, no longer just a whispered phrase from Kantos, but a clinical designation. It described the rare instances where a Null, often under extreme duress or during unexpected surges of their innate power, exhibited a profound, terrifying satisfaction in the act of unmaking. The journal described the subjects' eyes, “burning with an unnatural clarity,” their movements "coldly precise," as they dismantled magical constructs or even, in one horrifying account, caused a lesser elemental to simply *cease* to be, all while an almost ecstatic pleasure seemed to play upon their features. It was a cold, alien joy, devoid of human empathy, found in the perfect, silent execution of their unique power.

You continued to read, your mind reeling. The Seraphim, desperate to control this unpredictable anomaly, experimented with different genetic infusions, attempting to harness the Null's unique resonance. You found passages detailing attempts to "seed" the Null essence with pure celestial energy, to "re-introduce the divine spark" in controlled quantities. One particularly chilling section described infusing Null clones with the highly potent bloodlines of certain Apex Seraphim – beings of immense, singular power, each a unique facet of the Creator's own will. This, the journal theorized, was meant to create a "stable conduit" for the Null's power, allowing them to channel raw magic *within* their own anti-magic field. It was a horrific, Frankensteinian attempt to forge a perfect, controllable weapon, combining ultimate suppression with ultimate power. The implications were staggering. Your mother, you realized, was not just a Null. She was a synthesis, a living paradox born from the very essence of a being like Lucifer, a being whose blood, raw and untamed, might have been the key to unlocking this impossible dual power.

You felt a shiver, cold and visceral, trace a path down your spine, and it had nothing to do with the chill of the vault. This was the essence of your mother’s secret, the hidden engine behind her impossible victories. This was the darkness she had carried. You gripped the journal, the sensation of your own Starlit Eyes burning, suddenly aware of the very Empyrean blood that flowed through your veins. Was this "Joyful Predator" nature a dormant seed within you? A horrific inheritance waiting for its own moment of extreme duress to unfurl? The thought was a raw, terrifying wound, deeper than any physical ache. You felt a profound, suffocating sorrow for your mother, trapped by a nature she had to both embrace and hide.
