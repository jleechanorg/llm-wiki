There is a sharp, ringing *clack* as the pin seats itself perfectly, bridging the gap between the plates. The groaning of the timber ceases instantly as the door's weight shifts from Dunk's straining shoulders to the iron itself. The heavy oak hangs true, the gap even from top to bottom.

Dunk lets out a long, ragged exhale, his knees buckling slightly as he lets go of the frame. He stares at the door for a long heartbeat, then wipes a thick smear of grease and sweat from his jaw with the back of a shaking hand. The innkeeper pushes off from the doorframe, her expression shifting from boredom to a reluctant, sour approval. She kicks the door, and it swings with only a faint, well-greased hiss. "The giant stays," she barks, though she doesn't offer a smile. "One copper for the stew, and mind you don't track more muck in than you fixed."

Dunk claps a massive, grease-stained hand onto your shoulder, nearly knocking the straw hat from your head. "A sharp tongue and a bit of luck, eh lad? Maybe you're not as useless as you look."
