The unconscious Stonehand child lay secured on the straw mat, a silent, helpless lever in Gladius’s hands. Outside, the muffled, increasingly frantic voices of the parents drifted closer as they returned from their futile investigation of the ruined shed. Their simple fear, their predictable concern for a mere structure, had served its purpose. Now, the true performance could begin.

Gladius surveyed the main common room, his eyes scanning for optimal kill zones, for anything that could betray his presence or give the returning couple an advantage. The hearth still cast a faint, warm glow, but it was just enough to show the worn tools hanging on the walls, the simple dining table, the benches. He considered his greatsword, its immense weight a comforting presence on his back. For an open confrontation, it was supreme. But for this, for the exquisite pleasure of luring them into a cage and then springing the trap, a more subtle approach was needed. He carefully unstrapped the greatsword, leaning it silently against the wall behind the heavy, wooden door, ensuring it would be out of sight, yet easily accessible if needed.

His focus narrowed on the Silent Strangler’s Cord, already coiled in his hand. This was the instrument of choice for this particular act of dominance. He moved to the side of the door, pressing his broad frame against the cool stone wall, melting into the deepest shadow. He checked the sturdy wooden bar on the door once more, ensuring it would slide into place with a decisive, final thud once the parents were inside. He envisioned their entry, their eyes scanning for their child, their dawning horror. The anticipation was a cold, pure hum beneath his skin, a visceral delight that sharpened his senses. He could already taste the raw fear, the utter submission. This was the moment their strong-backed defiance would shatter. The sun was now a definite orange on the horizon, promising a new day that the Stonehands would not live to see. He was ready.
