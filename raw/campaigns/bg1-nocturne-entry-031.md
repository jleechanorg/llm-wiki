She asks no questions about the burns on your chest or the bruising on Roric's ribs; on the Sword Coast, violence is as common as the rain.

"A donation to the Glittering Gold helps the healing light shine," she says gently.

The rogue gasps, his back arching as bones knit and bruised flesh turns pink and healthy. Moments later, he stands unsupported, taking a deep, testing breath.

"Better," Roric says, rolling his shoulders and checking his range of motion. "Much better. I feel like I could climb a wall. Or fall off one."

With your companion restored (**Roric HP: 9/9**), you cross the courtyard to the main inn. He is a jovial man who wipes a glass with a rag that looks cleaner than most of his patrons.

"Supplies for the road?" Bentley asks, eyeing your travel-worn gear. "Jaheira mentioned you might be heading south. Dangerous country, Nashkel. You'll need more than good intentions."

He opens the heavy iron-banded cabinets behind the bar, revealing a well-stocked armory and general store. "Take a look. For friends of the Harpers, I offer the 'survival' rate."
