The wedding ceremony, held within Blackwood Keep's solemn Great Hall, was a stark affair, devoid of unnecessary pomp but heavy with the weight of political might. Daemon (Level 8, Age 30) stood tall, his dragon-scaled armor a brutal contrast to the silk and velvet of the Blackwood retainers. He exchanged vows with Lady Isolde (Age: ~22) with grim pragmatism, his voice a low rumble of command. Isolde, for her part, was a picture of composed ambition, her eyes meeting his with a calculating glint. The pact was sealed, witnessed by Kael (Level 7, Age 25), Joric (Level 7, Age 32), Theron (Level 6, Age 28), Gareth (Level 7, Age 29), and Ragnar (Level 7, Age 33) – his iron-willed vanguard – and Isolde's nervous, traditional court.

For the next two weeks, Blackwood Keep became a dual crucible. By day, the training grounds screamed with Daemon's relentless drills. He personally pushed the integrated Blackwood guards and his 90 Level 3 recruits, honing their formations, sharpening their attacks, and instilling the brutal discipline that flowed from his formidable Physique. Joric, Gareth, Ragnar, and Theron were tireless in their enforcement, transforming terrified peasants into hardened, if unwilling, soldiers. Borin (Level 7, Age 30) meanwhile continued to oversee the training in Silverport.

By night, Daemon turned his attention to the second, more intimate, aspect of their union. He lay with Isolde, fulfilling the terms of their marriage, seeking to produce an heir to his burgeoning empire. He approached it with the same grim determination he applied to conquest, sensing the strategic imperative. Yet, as the weeks wore on, a subtle frustration began to niggle at the edges of his formidable will.

Isolde, ever the pragmatist, eventually spoke of it in hushed, carefully chosen words, her face etched with a deeper, more personal anxiety. "My Lord," she confessed, her gaze falling, "this is... not new to my house. The Blackwood line has long been... troubled. Many attempts have been made. Heirs are often stillborn, or none come at all. It is a curse, some say. A blight on the bloodline." Her intelligent eyes, usually so sharp, were filled with a desperate, unspoken plea. The stark reality of "The Barren Heir" had presented itself.
