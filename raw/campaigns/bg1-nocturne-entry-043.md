You turn your gaze to Garrick. The bard is huddled on the grain sacks, his finery stained with the muck of the alley. He looks up as you approach, his eyes darting frantically between your cold stare and Kagain's axe.

"Please," Garrick whispers, his voice trembling. "I... I only did what she told me. Silke... she's powerful. I had no choice."

You crouch down, bringing your face level with his. You do not shout. You do not rage. You simply let the *Hunger* bleed into your expression—a look of absolute, predatory assessment.

"We all have choices, Garrick," you say softly. "Silke chose to leave you. The Harpers chose to die. Now you have a choice."

You draw your dagger—not the poisoned one, but the clean blade—and rest the flat of it against his cheek. "You can stay here with Kagain. He needs entertainment, and I suspect his definition of 'fun' involves sharp objects."

Kagain grunts in affirmation, sharpening his axe with a whetstone. *Scritch. Scritch.*

"Or," you continue, "you can make yourself useful. We are going to Nashkel. We need someone who knows the lore of the region. Someone who can identify magic items. Someone who can lie to guards with a smile. You join us, you serve me, and you live."

**Intimidation Check: 11 (Success vs DC 10)**

Garrick swallows hard, the sound audible in the quiet shop. He looks at the dwarf, then back at you. The terror in his eyes shifts into a desperate, frantic loyalty.

"I... I know Nashkel!" he babbles, nodding so hard he almost cuts himself on your blade. "And the Cloudpeaks! I know the legends of the Amnish border! I can identify potions! I can sing! I'll carry the packs! Just... please, don't leave me with him!"

You sheath the dagger and stand. "Then get up. You're hired."

Garrick scrambles to his feet, dusting off his tunic with shaking hands. "Thank you, mistress! You won't regret this! I am a chronicler of heroes! I will write a saga about this... about us!"

"Less talking," Roric mutters, tossing Garrick a waterskin. "More hiding."

**Garrick has joined the party.**

With your team assembled—a thief, a mercenary, and a bard—you settle in for the night. Kagain bars the door and extinguishes the lamps, plunging the shop into darkness. Outside, the sounds of the Flaming Fist patrols continue late into the night, boots tramping on cobblestones, searching for the Harper-killers. But in the cellar of the mercenary charter, you are a ghost in the machine.

You have turned a disaster into a recruitment drive. You have a tank, a scout, and a lore-master. The Nashkel Mines await.
