You step closer to the elf, the *Hunger* noting the way he flinches at your approach. Xan is adjusting the belt of his Moonblade, his movements graceful but laden with a heavy, existential fatigue.

"The Half-Orc," you state, your voice cutting through his murmuring. "Did he speak to anyone? Not his kobolds. Anyone... else. Through magic."

Xan sighs, a long, tragic exhalation that seems to deflate his entire frame. "We are all speaking to the void, are we not? And the void rarely answers. But... yes. Mulahey was not merely ranting at his minions."

The enchanter gestures vaguely at the altar of Cyric. "He received... sendings. Whispers in the dark. I heard him pleading with a wizard named **Tranzig**. Begging for more soldiers. Complaining that the kobolds were 'too squishy' to hold the line."

Xan's eyes darken. "But it was not Tranzig he feared. He spoke of another. 'The Armored One.' Mulahey was terrified. He claimed this figure could crush a man's skull with one hand and turn a city to ash with the other. He believed the Iron Throne was watching him through the very stone."

He looks at you, his expression bleak. "If Mulahey—a priest of the Prince of Lies—was shaking in his boots, what chance do we have? We have merely traded a dungeon for a grave under the sky."

**Minsc** pats Xan on the back, nearly knocking the elf over. "Do not despair, little elf! We have kicked the butt of the dungeon! Now we shall kick the butt of the sky-grave!"

**Dynaheir** studies the letters you found. "Tranzig. The letters name him as the contact in Beregost. If Mulahey was reporting to him, then Tranzig knows the next step of the plan. We must find him."

**Roric** kicks a piece of ore into the dark water. "Beregost is north. Nashkel is south. We have a reward to collect from the Mayor before we go hunting wizards. And I'd like to see the look on Ghastkill's face when we tell him we actually survived."
